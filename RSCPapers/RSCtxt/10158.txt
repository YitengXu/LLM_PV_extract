Birkett*Department of Chemistry and Materials, Manchester Metropolitan University, John Dalton Building, Chester Street, Manchester, UK M1 5GDFirst published on 2nd July 20021 IntroductionThis review follows on from last years report1 and is a collection of the highlights from over 1000 research papers, reviews and books revealed by BIDS, Chemical Abstract and publisher website searches for fullerene and carbon nanotube references. Furthermore, it is intended that the papers reviewed reflect the current research trends in the field. Books reviewing aspects of fullerene nanotechnology,2 radical reactions of fullerenes,3 and carbon nanotubes (softback edition)4 have been published. The majority of the papers published in the journal dedicated to fullerene related papers, fullerene nanotubes and carbon nanostructures,5 are not individually referenced within this review. In addition, some journals have published special editions6 and others such as the new journal Nano Lett.
frequently carry several fullerene related papers per edition.7 A draft document on fullerene nomenclature was made available on the worldwide web for public comment.8 Reviews of unimolecular gas phase ion chemistry,9 main group element chemistry,10 and the interplay between theory and experiment in solid state inorganic chemistry,11 each of which contain sections on fullerenes, have been published. In addition, the current status and prospects of the science and technology of nanomaterials have been reviewed.122 Synthesis, separation and properties of fullerenesThe presence of fullerenes in meteorite samples has been identified by mass spectroscopy.13 These results have, however, been disputed and the discussion as to whether fullerenes are genuinely to be found in meteorites continues.14 The formation of [60]fullerene by the cyclodehydrogenation of a polyarene containing all sixty carbon atoms has been shown to occur under mass spectroscopic conditions.15 A similar approach used by independent authors employing macrocyclic polyalkynes has led to the formation of [36]fullerene16 and [60]fullerene17 under gas phase conditions. Glow discharge reactors have been used to pyrolyse chloroform resulting in the formation of fullerenes and chlorinated carbon clusters.18 Moreover, the authors report that five isomers of [60]fullerene,
identified using HPLC and mass spectrometry, are formed under these reaction conditions.19 The density-functional based tight-binding method has been used to show that pentagon adjacencies are stabilised by functionalisation in small fullerenes such as [36]fullerene.20 The Hückel bond orders of [60]fullerene isomers give a reliable graph-theory based illustration of the bond order pattern for the majority of isomers although only a small subset have bond orders that correspond to a Kekulé structure.21 A procedure for assigning Cahn–Ingold–Prelog descriptors to stereocentres in spheroalkanes, the fully hydrogenated form of a trivalent, polyhedral carbon framework, a class of molecules which includes the fullerenes, has been developed.22 The predicted nucleus-independent chemical shifts (NICS) of BN-doped fullerenes
show that these species are more aromatic than [60]fullerene.23 Semi-empirical AM1 and MNDO methods have been used to examine the relative stabilities of several isomers of BN substituted fullerenes of the general formula C60−2x(BN)x.24A review of the bonding present in fullerenes and fullerides has been published.25 Similarly, a comprehensive account of the spherical aromaticity of fullerenes has been published and the conclusion that there is delocalisation of π-electrons in fullerenes, which provides some energetic stabilisation, drawn.26 Previous calculations of the binding energy, Ea, of C60+ have been critically re-examined and the average value of Ea found to be 10.0 ± 0.2 eV.27 A comprehensive set of solubility data of [60]fullerene has been treated by multivariate stepwise linear regression and increasing molar volume and solvent polarity found to decrease solubility, whilst increasing electron pair donation ability and polarisability were found to enhance solubility.28 HPLC employing a chiral stationary
phase, amylose tris(3,5-dimethylphenylcarbamate) bound to silica gel, has been used to resolve the enantiomers of [76]fullerene and CD spectra obtained.29 Density functional theory has been used to optimise the geometry of isolated pentagon rule (IPR) isomers of [82]fullerene and their 13C NMR spectra calculated, which were found to be in good agreement with the experimentally observed δ values for the C2 isomer.30 The same authors have applied the same technique to calculate the 13C NMR spectra of isomers of [84]fullerene.31 Arc produced soot obtained from dysprosium-doped graphite composite rods was found to contain a new isomer of C2 symmetric [84]fullerene isomer.32 Six isomers of [84]fullerene have been characterised
by electrochemistry, electron spin resonance spectroscopy and molecular energy level calculations.33 Whilst the first electrochemical reductions of thin films of [84]fullerene on electrodes have been reported and found to result in the generation of multi-charged anions.34 The dimerisation of [60]fullerene has been subjected to symmetry considerations with the result that [60]fullerene is predicted to be in equilibrium with the known stable [2 + 2] dimer at moderate temperatures but this situation is not observed in the gas phase for thermodynamic reasons.35 Fluorescent and triplet absorption spectra of the [60]fullerene trimer, C180, are similar to those of 1,2-mono-adducts of [60]fullerene whilst other excited state properties of C180 have a shorter fluorescence lifetime due to non-radiative processes.36 High pressure and high temperature treatment of [60]fullerene thin films on steel transforms the fullerene layer to a superhard and superelastic material.373 Endohedral fullerenesThe EPR spectrum previously assigned to the endohedral [60]fullerene species, Cs@(COT)–C602− (COT = cyclooctatriene)38 has now been corrected by the authors and is not due to an endohedral fullerene derivative but to a reaction of a complex formed with Cs+ after reaction between [60]fullerene and the solvent, hexamethylphosphoramide, used for the investigation.39 Ba@C74 has been produced for the first time using a radiofrequency furnace.40 The single crystal X-ray structure of a trimetallic nitride unit encapsulated in [78]fullerene, Sc3N@C78, has been obtained.41 Similarly a scandium carbide endohedral metallofullerene, (Sc2C2)@C84, has been obtained and characterised using 13C
NMR spectroscopy.42 A perfect pentagonal–dodecahedral charge density of La2+ has been observed in La2@C80 which is the result of a highly selective trajectory of the two La atoms hopping along the hexagonal rings of the [80]fullerene cage.43 Samarium-containing endohedral fullerenes have been prepared using an arc discharge methodology and soot enriched with these endohedral fullerenes obtained by successive extractions using carbon disulfide and pyridine.44 Scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) has been used to show that Tb@C82 endohedral fullerenes have great mobility on a [60]fullerene film and form a close packed layer after aggregating along the edges of terraces of [60]fullerene films.454 Organic chemistryThere has once again been a considerable number of papers investigating aspects of the organic chemistry of fullerenes, only a fraction of which are reviewed here. The unequivocal assignment of all of the carbon atoms of 10%
13C enriched 1,2-methano[60]fullerene 61,61-dicarboxylate by 2D inadequate NMR spectroscopy has been achieved.46 Both thermal first-order and photochemical zero-order kinetics have been observed in the rearrangement of [6,5] open fulleroids to [6,6] closed fullerenes, which proceeds via a biradical intermediate under unimolecular conditions.47 Semi-empirical and density functional methods reveal that the addition of azides to [60]fullerene proceeds to give a stable triazoline intermediate which subsequently undergoes stepwise decomposition.48 A series of poly-adducts, hexakis, heptakis and octakis, of [60]fullerene
have been prepared by exploitation of the bond specific reactivity of C2v-symmetrical pentakis-adducts of [60]fullerene using addition of diazomethane.49 In addition, high-level ab initio and density functional calculations are shown to provide strong evidence that a common mechanism, an eight-electron, orbital symmetry-controlled [2πs

proceed by a concerted mechanism via a symmetrical transition state.513He NMR spectroscopy has been used to show that there are one mono-adduct, six bis-adducts, eleven tris-adducts and ten tetrakis-adducts in the equilibrium mixture formed after reaction of [60]fullerene with 9,10-dimethylanthracene.52 Mono-adducts of [60]fullerene from reaction with several anthracenes have been prepared and their thermolytic decomposition in the solid state found to produce a mixture of the parent [60]fullerene and, regioselectively, the antipodal bis-adduct.53 By contrast, eight bis-regioisomers resulting from the cycloaddition of benzyne and [60]fullerene have been isolated and characterised.54 Solid state reaction of [60]fullerene with di(2-pyridyl)-1,2,4,5-tetrazine results in the formation of
a reactive mono-adduct which undergoes further reaction producing a dimeric fullerene incorporating a 2,3-diazobycyclo[2.2.2]oct-2-ene framework, further photochemical reaction of this product results in a [2 + 2] cycloaddition between the two [60]fullerene cages (Fig. 1).55 The same reactive intermediate has been prepared independently and found to undergo 1,4-hydrogenation and a rearrangement from the fused 4,5-dihydropyridazine into a 4,5-dihydropyrazole [60]fullerene adduct upon reaction with diprotic nucleophiles.56 Fig. 1  Scheme showing the reaction sequence resulting in the formation of a [60]fullerene adduct incorporating a 2,3-diazabicyclo[2.2.2]oct-2-ene framework (reproduced by permission from Chem. A review of new fullerene derivatives and ring opened fullerene molecules has been published.57 The solid state reaction of [60]fullerene with phthalazine results in a [60]fullerene dimer, by contrast the liquid phase reaction produces a [4 + 2] mono-adduct which undergoes two further spontaneous rearrangements resulting finally in a ring opened [60]fullerene derivative having an eight-membered ring as part of the fullerene cage structure.58 The foregoing ring opened adduct undergoes an additional cleavage under photochemical conditions in the presence of oxygen affording a fullerene derivative with enol and ketone groups situated within a twelve-membered ring orifice.59 Similarly, 4,6-dimethyl-1,2,3-triazine undergoes a sequence of cycloadditions with [60]fullerene which, after additional cleavage under photochemical conditions in the
presence of oxygen, affords a triketone with a twelve-membered ring orifice.60 A tetra-substituted bis[60]fulleroid has been shown to produce a diketone derivative with a twelve-membered ring embedded within the fullerene cage structure after photo-oxidation.61 A ring-opened [60]fullerene bislactam has been used to encapsulate either helium or molecular hydrogen and encapsulation confirmed by NMR studies.62Dimethylsulfonium α-formylalkylides react with [60]fullerene producing formyl-substituted methanofullerenes and the formyl group shown to undergo typical reactions with, for example, aromatic amines.63 Similarly synthesis of methano[60]fullerenes has been achieved using the fluoride ion mediated reaction with silylated nucleophiles.64 Thiocarbonyl ylides undergo 1,3-dipolar cycloadditions with [60]fullerene resulting in tetrahydrothiophene-fused derivatives which can be oxidized to the sulfoxide using meta-chloroperbenzoic acid.65 Only the C2v isomer of two [78]fullerene isomers, C2v and D3, react with disilaranes producing a mono-adduct.66 The [60]fullerene dimer formed as the result of a [2 + 2] cycloaddition has been subjected
to reaction under Bingel conditions resulting in the formation of five regioisomeric mono-adducts and several bis-adducts.67 Reductive electrolysis of mono-methano[60]fullerene adducts in THF produces good yields of bis-adducts with a unique regiochemistry.68 Tandem reductive ring opening of tethered bis-methano[60]fullerenes using sodium cyanoborohydride under protic solvent conditions leads to the formation of 1,2-dihydro[60]fullerylglycines.69Fullerene-based amino acids and peptides have been reviewed.70 In addition, their roles in materials and medicinal chemistry have been highlighted and several new molecules synthesised from amino-functionalised [60]fulleropyrrolidines.71 A hybrid fullerene–trimethoxyindole–oligonucleotide, which is designed to have specific DNA binding activity, has been prepared using the same synthetic methodology.72 Malonodiserinolamide [60]fullerenes, C60[C(COSer)2]n where n
= 4, 5 or 6 and Ser = 2-amino-1,3-propanediol, suitable for biological evaluation, having water solubility of >240 mg L−1, have been synthesised.73 Similarly α-D-mannosyl derivatives have been synthesised by azido addition to [60]fullerene
and are shown to decrease the activity for both aggregating erythrocytes and in binding to β-D-galactopyranoside specific lectin (RCA120).74 Steroid containing [60]fullerene adducts have also been prepared by the Diels–Alder reaction of siloxydienes with [60]fullerene and one of these derivatives shown to inhibit the reconstituted sarcoplastic reticulum (SR) Ca2+–ATPase in soybean phospholipid liposomes.75 A new fullerene transfection agent bearing multiple functional groups has been produced by diastereoselective double cycloadditions of a biscyclopropenone acetal and shown to efficiently, comparable to that of a nor-analogue, transfer extra-cellular DNA into mammalian cells.76 The same authors have used atomic force microscopy (AFM) to show that a tetra-amino[60]fullerene transfection
agent folds supercoiled DNA into a single molecule condensate through adhesion of DNA double strands.77 One of a series of methanophosphonate and methanophosphonic acid [60]fullerene derivatives, prepared using the Bingel methodology, has photo-induced cytotoxic properties, which the authors suggest implies that molecules of this type may be useful in photodynamic therapy.78 Similarly, polymeric fullerocyclodextrins, synthesised by reaction of diamines with [60]fullerene complexed with cyclodextrin, have efficient DNA-cleavage properties in the presence of visible light.79 Fullerene malonates have been shown to interact with nitric oxide synthase (NOS) distorting its structure, and in doing so, provide a unique mechanism for the isoform-selective inhibition of nNOS.80 Studies on other water soluble [60]fullerene malonates
indicate that this class of molecule is capable of eliminating both superoxide anion and H2O2 and are effective inhibitors of lipid peroxidation.81 The photophysical properties of several bis-pyrrolidine derivatives of [60]fullerene have been shown to be similar to the parent fullerene and that aggregation is avoided, which may be a useful property in future biological evaluation of fullerene-based molecules, by incorporation of chains and charged units into the pyrrolidine unit.82 Water soluble hexa-sulfonated [60]fullerene has been subjected to several biological studies and shown to have neuro- and cardio-protective effects.83The fluorination of fullerenes has been reviewed.84 A new fluorinated derivative, C60F20, which has a structure comprising of two dehydrocorannulene caps held together by a (CF)20 chain has been isolated.85 A facile migration of fluorine about the [60]fullerene cage has been identified during high temperature fluorination of [60]fullerene and this observation used to explain the formation of a limited number of regioisomers of C60F3686 and C60F48.86b An independent study has found that fluorination of [60]fullerene with binary metal fluorides is thermodynamically controlled and that C60F44 is formed by reaction with silver(II) fluoride.87 A mixture of two isomers
of C60F48 has been characterised by single crystal X-ray diffraction studies.88 Several binary lead fluorides and their complexes with alkali and alkaline earth metal fluorides have been investigated as [60]fullerene fluorinating agents89a and seven bis(oxahomo)[60]fullerene derivatives of C60F18 have been isolated by HPLC from the reaction of [60]fullerene with K2PtF6.89b Perfluorinated [70]fullerene, C70F56, has been synthesised using fluorine gas and [70]fullerene.90 Anthracene is reported to add readily to C60F18 resulting in the formation of two major regioisomeric mono-adducts of C60F18 in which all of the fluorine atoms remain intact; one of the two
isomers is unstable and decomposes to the more stable regioisomeric cycloadduct of C60F18 and the parent C60F18.91 By contrast, reaction of C60F18 under Bingel-like conditions results in the loss of three fluorine atoms from the fullerene cage and the formation of a [18]transannulene chromophore on the fullerene cage surface.92 Reaction of C60Cl6 with alcohols results in the formation of 1,4-dialkoxy[60]fullerene derivatives, whereas reaction with alcohols in the presence of sodium alkoxide results in the formation of C60(OR)5Cl (where R = alkyl group of the alcohol).93 Arylated [60]fullerenes, C60(Ph)5Cl, are found to undergo spontaneous oxidation94 whilst a cage-opened
bisepoxide ketone is one of the products formed after reaction of C60Cl6 with methyllithium followed by hydrolysis.95 A series of arylated [70]fullerene products has been isolated from the reaction of C70Cl10 with benzene96 and the electrochemical96 and photophysical properties of the derivatives reported.97 In addition, two polyarylated [70]fullerene diols, C70Ph8(OH)2, have been characterised.98 The [5,6]-open oxide of [60]fullerene has been isolated by the photolysis of C60O3, a closed [6,6] ozone [60]fullerene adduct.99 Hydrolysis of the products from the reaction of [60]- or [70]-fullerenes with RuO4
results in the formation of 1,2-diols of [60]- and [70]-fullerenes.100A review detailing the synthesis and characterisation of fullerene hydrides has been published.101 A novel gas phase chemical vapour modification (CVM) technique has been employed to produce the known C3v isomer of C60H18.102 C60H36 has been shown to start to decompose at about 200 °C and can reduce, for example, anthracene when nickel powder is used as a catalyst.103 The theoretical fullerene derivative C60H60 has been calculated by ab initio molecular orbital theory to be unstable, decomposing exothermically to C60H44.104 The chemically generated fulleride dianion, C602−, reacts with halo-containing derivatives to produce 1,2- and 1,4-adducts with long chain derivatives and 1,2-fused ring [60]fullerene
derivatives with 1,3-dihaloalkanes.105 Highly stabilised carbocations, such as tri-tert-butylcyclopropenylium ion, did not react with fulleride anions, instead leading to the formation of a salt, whereas effective C–C bond formation between the fullerene and carbocation is observed with more reactive carbocationic species.106 A review of the key role played by C59N+ in azaheterofullerene chemistry has been published107 and pentakis-methano adducts of the azafullerene, C59N, having an octahedral Cs symmetrical addition pattern, have been synthesised.1085 Materials chemistryThe formation of hexakis-adducts of [60]fullerene has been reviewed109 and several new adducts of this type, one of which was used to prepare a supramolecular [60]fullerene-based dimer, have been synthesised using the 9,10-dimethylanthracene template synthesis strategy.110 A highly symmetrical decakis-methano-adduct of the [2 + 2]
[60]fullerene dimmer, C120, has been synthesised.111 The uses of fullerene-containing molecules in organic–inorganic nanocomposites,112a nanocomposites112b and in conjugated polymer containing solar cells has been reviewed.113 Several thiophene and oligothiophene containing [60]fullerene derivatives have been synthesised by independent research groups,114a,b,d
their photophysical properties examined114a,b,c and their use in, for example, photodiodes investigated.114a In addition, self-assembled monolayers of oligothiophene–[60]fulleropyrrolidine dyads have been shown to be stable and electroactive.114d Several tetrathiafulvalene derivatives of [60]fullerene have also been synthesised using cycloaddition techniques.115 Unusual linear electrooptic effects (LEO) have been observed in some tetrathiafulvalene-containing [60]fullerenes.116 The same authors have also described an electrolytic electrospray ionisation mass spectrometry technique for the analysis and investigation of the gas phase reactivity of this type of molecule.117 A related [60]fullerene
dimer, bridged by a cyanine dye unit, has been synthesised using the cycloaddition of an azomethine ylide and examined using STM.118 Poly(phenylenevinylene)

[60]Fullerene containing rigid dendrimeric meta-substituted phenylenevinylenes bearing a variety of electron donating groups have been synthesised and the competitive versus sequential energy and electron transfer events shown to vary between the derivatives.120 Similar derivatives have been synthesised independently and shown to have improved electron affinity with respect to the parent [60]fullerene.121 Methano[60]fullerenes which exhibit

films shown to mimic natural photosynthetic processes but, to date, have low incident photon-to-current efficiency (IPCE).129a In addition, simple complexing of diazabicyclooctane (DABCO) by [60]fullerene triads leads to rigid assemblies that display prolonged charge-separated states.129bTetraphenylporphyrins and metal(II) and iron(III) tetraphenylporphyrins form complexes with [60]- and [70]-fullerenes which do not show any charge transfer in the ground state.130 Similarly, chiral, effectively D4 symmetric, porphyrins have been employed to form chiral non-covalent, non-polar complexes with [60]fullerene.131 Short distances have been observed in the crystal structure of the complex formed between [60]fullerene and the C4 symmetrical cobalt tetrakis(di-tert-butylphenyl)porphyrin.132 Saddle-shaped tetraazaannulates also form complexes with [60]fullerene in which, as 13C NMR studies have shown, the [60]fullerene molecules undergo rapid pseudo-isotropic rotation at room temperature.133
Extremely high association constants (108 M−1) have been observed in rhodium(III) porphyrin cyclic dimers in which it is likely there is a rhodium to fullerene charge transfer interaction.134 A non-covalently linked meso-tetraphenylporphyrinatozinc(II) and [60]fullero-pyrrolidine complex has been studied using single crystal X-ray diffraction studies.135 An independent study shows that there is efficient charge separation, with lifetimes of about 10 µs in THF and several hundreds of microseconds in benzonitrile at room temperature in the same complex.136 Self-assembled molecular layers of hexagonally ordered [60]fullerene and quadratically ordered copper phthalocyanine molecules co-exist on Au(111).137 Large aggregates of [60]fullerene molecules and gold nano-particles
