Abstract

A series of narrow band gap donorâ€“acceptor type conjugated copolymers based on 2,6-linked anthracene derivatives are synthesized via Suzuki copolymerization. The resulting copolymers typically exhibit dual absorption characteristics in both solution and as thin films with optical band gaps in the range of 1.85â€“2.13 eV. By varying the substitutions from alkyloxy to aromatic thienyl and phenyl groups in the 9,10-positions of the anthracene unit, the constructed two-dimensional 2,6-linked anthracene structures lead to broader absorption, lower-lying highest occupied molecular orbitals, as well as improved charge carrier mobilities of the resulting copolymers relative to the alkyloxy side chain substituted counterparts. Additionally, it was found that the sizes of the substitutions in the benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole acceptor also play an important role in the optoelectronic properties of these anthracene based conjugated copolymers. The best polymer solar cell device with a power conversion efficiency of 4.34% and a high open circuit voltage of 0.98 V was realized based on the resulting materials. Our results indicate that substantial optimization in the sizes and patterns of substitutions of both the 2,6-linked anthracene donor and benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole acceptor may potentially lead to high performance narrow band gap copolymers for solar cell applications.

Introduction

Recently, considerable attention has been devoted to polymer solar cells (PSCs) with bulk heterojunction (BHJ) structures due to their unique advantages of low cost, flexibility, tunable electronic properties and being solution processable for large-area devices.1â€“9 Although significant progress, with power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) over 9%, has been achieved, several issues still remain unsolved for the further improvement of device performance.10,11 One of the most challenging aspects is to develop electron-donating conjugated copolymers, which are supposed to exhibit ideal energy levels, a coplanar molecular structure, as well as good solution processability. Conjugated polymers with a donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) type of architecture, which typically have electron-rich (donor) and electron-deficient (acceptor) units alternating along the polymer main chain, have therefore emerged since the molecular frontier orbitals can be readily tuned by rational choice of the electron-donor and acceptor units.12,13 Specifically, it has been recognized that the electron-donating ability of the donor units should be relatively weak to ensure a low-lying highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) level of the resulting conjugated polymers, which is critical for the high open circuit voltages (Vocs) of the corresponding PSCs.14,15 Consequently, a variety of Dâ€“A type narrow band gap conjugated copolymers based on weak electron donors, such as fluorene,16,17 carbazole,18â€“20 silafluorene21 or benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene22,23 have been recently designed and PSCs with excellent performances have been realized based on these materials.

To acquire good charge transport characteristics of conjugated copolymers, it is necessary to enhance the close packing of the polymer backbone by increasing the molecular coplanarity and rigidity. Anthracene derivatives are relatively weak donor units with enlarged planarity that can lead to the enhanced structural ordering of the resulting copolymers. Moreover, the incorporation of anthracene derivatives into the polymer main chain through its 9,10-positions24â€“27 or 2,6-positions,28,29 offers a flexible way to fine tune the optoelectronic properties of the corresponding copolymers. Hence, anthracene derivatives have been widely used as building blocks for conjugated copolymers that can be applied in high performance organic thin-film field effect transistors and polymer light-emitting diodes,30â€“35 while the application of anthracene derivatives in PSC materials has been rarely reported. Sonar et al. reported the Dâ€“A type of copolymer based on 2,6-linked anthracene as the donor unit and difuranyl diketopyrrolopyrrole as the acceptor exhibited a PCE up to 2.5% of PSCs.29 During the course of this work, Lidzey and co-workers reported an additional case of semiconducting copolymers with alternating 2,6-linked anthracene units flanked by either thienyl or dithienyl groups as donor units, and the resulting PSCs exhibited an impressive PCE up to 4.17%.36

In comparison to the analogous molecular framework of benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene, which is one of the state of the art building blocks for high performance PSC materials,37â€“41 anthracene is essentially a weaker electron donor that would potentially lead to a higher Voc of the resulting polymers,14,15 and the 9,10-positions of the central phenyl ring can allow for molecular design by introducing a variety of substitutions including aromatic substitutions to construct two-dimensional conjugated structures. Even though the specific impact of substituted groups on the photophysical, electrochemical properties and film morphology is very difficult to predict, it is still worth trying several types of substituents to get access to optimal polymer structures that can lead to improved device performances. Addressing this point, we developed a series of Dâ€“A type narrow band gap conjugated copolymers based on 2,6-linked anthracene derivatives with various substitutions of dodecyloxyl (12OA), 2-dodecylthiophene (12TA) and 4-dodecylphenyl (12BA) appended through 9,10-positions of the central phenyl ring as donor units, and benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (BT) flanked with thienyl groups with dodecyloxy- or octyloxy-substitutions as the acceptor unit. It was found that the solubility, absorption, frontier molecular orbital energy levels, charge carrier mobility, as well as the miscibility with [6,6]-phenyl C71-butyric acid methyl ester (PC71BM) of the resulting copolymers can be significantly influenced by the substitutions. The resulting PSCs exhibited a maximum PCE of 4.34% with a Voc of 0.98 V based on copolymer PBA8OTBT with a 12BA side group in the anthracene donor unit and an octyloxy side group in the BT acceptor unit.

Device fabrication and characterization

Before fabrication of the device, the indium tin oxide (ITO)-coated substrates were cleaned by ultrasonic treatment in deionized water, acetone, isopropyl alcohol and then dried in a nitrogen stream, followed by an oxygen plasma treatment. Polymer solar cells with inverted structures were fabricated with the structure of ITO/PFN-OX/copolymer:PC71BM/MoO3/Al under conditions as follows: a 5 nm thick layer of PFN-OX48,49 (poly[9,9-bis(6â€²-(N,N-diethylamino)hexyl)-fluorene-alt-9,9-bis(3-ethyl(oxetane-3-ethyloxy)-hexyl)-fluorene]) was spin-coated on the ITO substrates and then the cross-linking process was done by heating the half-dried films under 150 Â°C for 20 minutes and fluorescence lamp illumination for 20 min. The copolymers and PC71BM were co-dissolved in 1,2-dichlorobenzene (ODCB) in the weight ratios of 1:4 and the typical concentration of the copolymers/PC71BM blending solution used in this study was 7 mg mLâˆ’1. All the active layers were then spin-coated from the prepared blending solutions with a thickness of 90 nm. To complete the device fabrication, 10 nm of MoO3 and 100 nm of Al were subsequently thermally evaporated onto the active layer to form the top electrode of the device. Finally, post-annealing was carried out at 100 Â°C for 15 minutes. The effective device area was measured to be 0.16 cm2.

The current densityâ€“voltage (Jâ€“V) characteristics were investigated with a Keithley 236 source meter. The spectral responses were measured with a commercial photomodulation spectroscopic setup (Oriel). The EQEs (external quantum efficiencies) of the devices were recorded on a Hypermonolight System (CROWNTECH-1000).

Electrochemical properties

The cyclic voltammetry (CV) measurements were carried out under an inert atmosphere by using tetra-n-butylammonium hexafluorophosphate (Bu4NPF6, 0.1 M in acetonitrile) as the supporting electrolyte with a platinum button working electrode, a platinum wire counter electrode and a Ag/Ag+ (0.1 M of AgNO3 in acetonitrile) reference electrode. The potential of the ferrocene/ferrocenium (Fc/Fc+) redox couple was measured as the standard. It is assumed that the redox potential of Fc/Fc+ has an absolute energy level of âˆ’4.8 eV to vacuum.51 Under the same experimental conditions, the half-wave potential of the Fc/Fc+ was measured to be 0.29 V related to the Ag/Ag+ reference electrode. Therefore, the HOMO energy levels of the copolymers were calculated according to the following equation,
HOMO = âˆ’e(EOX + 4.51) (eV)
where the EOX is the onset of the oxidation potential vs. Ag/Ag+. HOMO energy levels of copolymers were calculated to be in the range of âˆ’5.48 to âˆ’5.70 eV. The detailed electrochemical data is summarized in Table 2 and Fig. S3 in the ESI.â€ 

It was observed that the HOMO energy levels of ca. â€“5.52 to âˆ’5.70 eV for copolymers bearing aromatic side groups are essentially lower than that of the ca. â€“5.48 eV for copolymers POA12OTBT and POA8OTBT that have alkoxy side groups as the anthracene side chain. When considering that the Voc of PSCs correlated with the difference between the HOMO energy level of the donor materials and lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) level of the fullerene acceptors,52 a higher Voc can thus be anticipated for copolymers bearing aromatic side groups, that is, the anthracene moieties with two-dimensional structures. It should also be noted that the LUMO levels of all the polymers are more than 0.3 eV higher than that of the fullerene derivative of the PCBM acceptor, which can overcome the intrachain exciton binding energy and ensure the formation of a downhill driving force for the energetically favorable electron transfer.53,54

Conclusion

In conclusion, a series of donorâ€“acceptor type of narrow band gap conjugated copolymers based on 2,6-linked anthracene derivatives as donor units and benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (BT) derivatives as acceptor units were synthesized. It was recognized that the side groups in both the anthracene donor and BT acceptor units can significantly impact the photophysical, electrochemical, charge carrier transport, as well as the bulk-heterojunction film morphology, and eventually resulted in different solar cell efficiencies. The aromatic thienyl and phenyl substitutions appended on the 9,10-positions of anthracene unit lead to two-dimensional structures that can provide lower-lying HOMO energy levels, improved hole mobility, along with superior blend film morphology with respect to the alkyloxy substitutions counterparts. In combination with PC71BM, polymer solar cells based on copolymers bearing phenyl side groups in the anthracene unit attained the best performances, and which exhibited a power conversion efficiency of 4.34% with a high open circuit voltage of 0.98 V. Our observations suggested that substantial optimization in the sizes and patterns of substitutions of 2,6-linked anthracene based narrow band gap copolymer would provide superb candidates for high performance polymer solar cells.
