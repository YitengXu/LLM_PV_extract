Abstract

Among all types of photovoltaics, perovskite solar cells (PSCs) have received extensive attention because of their potential for achieving cheap, lightweight, and fast fabricated devices. Although PSCs exhibit a power conversion efficiency (PCE) of up to 23%, they face issues regarding commercialization such as short lifetime. Remarkably, the most important key factors that regulate the durability of the devices are the type and crystalline structure of perovskite (PSK) materials. Though CH3NH3PbX3 is the first and also the widely utilized material as a light absorber in PSCs, it suffers from undesirable moisture stability, structural phase alteration, and photo and thermal instabilities. In this regard, the PSK crystal structure has been studied as a key factor to prolong the device lifetime. This review presents the progress of the changes in the PSK structure from 3 dimensional CH3NH3PbX3 to mixed cation or halide based PSKs and finally to Ruddlesdenâ€“Popper PSK two dimensional (2D) homologous structures regarding the lifetime improvement. To obtain insight on a realistic path towards durable and commercially viable PSCs, how the crystal formation energy, hydrophobicity, interface of the grains, and structural phase strength have been tuned to overcome the lack of the durability in PSK devices and the current status has been studied.

1 Introduction

In recent years, the demand for sustainable and clean energy resources has led to intense growth in the development of solar cells that directly convert sun light into electricity. Photovoltaics is one of the most promising technologies for harvesting the sun's energy as the largest noncarbon-based natural source. In order to meet the goal of commercialization, photovoltaic technology should be efficient, stable, and of low cost. Silicon photovoltaic cells as first generation solar cells are stable, with a long lifetime around 25 years and power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) as high as 20%, but their fabrication process is too complex and expensive. Photovoltaic uptake has been growing to introduce efficient alternatives to conventional solar cells such as crystalline silicon solar cells.1,2 The least costly and simplest fabrication method for solar cells is solution processing which provides roll-to-roll printing as a beneficial method for large scale production.3 One of the most promising technologies utilized for decreasing the manufacturing costs of solar cells is the perovskite solar cell (PSC)4,5 which has received extensive attention because of its potential for achieving cheap, lightweight, and fast fabricated solar cells. Although the PCE of PSCs reached up to 22%, their short lifetime and fast degradation still remain as the main challenges. Up to now, a lot of research has been undertaken to study and investigate the degradation of PSCs. Generally, the degradation of a complete device is related to structure, all the components of the device, fabrication, and operating atmospheric conditions. In this work, after a brief review of the history of PSCs, a general overview of the stability challenge in these devices is presented. Then, this study focuses on the progress of the changes in the PSK crystalline structure beginning from the 3D ABX3 based on CH3NH3PbI3 (MAPbI3) to the mixed halide and cation PSKs and finally to low dimensional PSKs to improve the device performance. In addition, because of the crucial role of the device interfaces in determining the stability, interfacial engineering related to the PSK layer is also focused on.

2 History of PSCs

Organicâ€“inorganic hybrid PSKs (CH3NH3PbX3 (X = Br, I)) were first used as visible-light sensitizers for liquid-junction-sensitized solar cells in 2009.6 In these devices, the dye pigment in DSSCs was replaced by two organicâ€“inorganic hybrid halide-based PSKs, CH3NH3PbBr3 and CH3NH3PbI3. Extremely unstable devices were achieved with PCEs around 3% which was much lower than the PCEs of the devices based on Ru-based molecular dyes.6â€“8 The PCE of the PSK-sensitized photocells reached 6.5% by optimizing the thickness of the photoanode and modifying their interfaces.9 The main problem in the liquid-junction-sensitized devices was their short lifetime (a few minutes), because the PSK was easily dissolved in liquid electrolyte. This problem encouraged the researchers to concentrate on solid-state hole transporting materials (HTMs). A device with 9.7% PCE was generated by utilizing 2,2â€²,7,7â€²-tetrakis-(N,N-dimethoxyphenyl-amine-)-9,9â€²-spirobifluorene (spiroMeOTAD) as a solid-state HTM.10 At that time, a PSK structure based on CsSnI3 was also used as a solid state hole conductor in dye sensitized solar cells (DSSCs).11 A PCE of higher than 10.9% was delivered for a solid-state sensitized solar cell based on a PSK absorber and alumina as a microstructured insulating scaffold in 2012.12 After achieving these advances, hybrid organicâ€“inorganic PSKs were significantly studied by researchers. The PCE of PSCs showed a rapid ascending trend and reached up to 23% by introducing and optimizing two structures based on the mesoporous network and the planar heterojunction. Thus far, a PSC with a PCE of 23.3% has been reported as the record by the NREL. In contrast, the stability issue has still remained as a main challenge that has gained less attention (Fig. 1).

The structures of PSCs can be divided into three categories: PSK sensitized mesoporous structure, planar heterojunction structure, and inverted structure. In all PSK structures, after absorption of the incident photons by CH3NH3PbX3, excitons with a low binding energy are generated which dissociated into the free charge carrier without needing acceptors. But, to achieve efficient absorption, charge transport, and charge collection, different structures of PSCs were presented.2 The basic architecture of mesoporous devices is similar to that of DSSCs with a solid HTM. In these devices, halide PSK fills a mesoporous network and forms a thin capping layer on the top. The filled network is sandwiched between two contacts with different work functions. In order to prevent direct contact between the anode and cathode, a thick compact TiO2 layer was deposited under the mesoporous layer.13 Two types of mesoporous networks have been used in mesoporous devices, namely active and passive. Active networks such as TiO2 nanoparticle networks act as both a scaffold for the PSK material and an electron transporting layer (ETL). By utilizing the TiO2 network, a large interface area between the absorber material and ETL is provided. Passive networks such as alumina mesoporous networks play only the supporter role.14 In the planar architecture, a PSK absorber layer is sandwiched between the ETL and hole transporting layer (HTL) without a mesoporous scaffold. Two heterojunctions are provided which are the junction between the absorber and HTL, and the one between the absorber and ETL. Different types of materials such as Cu2O15 are used as the HTL, and a compact layer such as a TiO2 layer is usually used as the ETL. In the inverted planar architecture, photogenerated electrons are collected in the cathode and photogenerated holes are collected in the anode.2

3 Stability of PSCs regarding PSK structure

Generally, issues of PSK device longevity can be addressed by two methods, the intrinsic and extrinsic methods. The extrinsic method includes using stable layers such as stable and hydrophobic inorganic and organic HTLs15 and ETLs,16 engineering interfacial contacts,17 device encapsulation,18 and defect passivation, leading to the lifetime enhancement of PSCs. For instance, TiO2 as the ETL can reduce the stability of PSCs under light irradiation. Thus, Shin and co-workers used lanthanum (La)-doped BaSnO3 (LBSO) PSK as an alternative ETL. They used the superoxide colloidal solution route to prepare an LBSO electrode and achieved a PCE as high as 21.2%. The long term photostability of both devices was monitored by storing them in a N2-filled container at a temperature of 25 Â°C under AM 1.5 G illumination provided by a xenon lamp. The TiO2 based PSC showed an abrupt decrease in PCE upon initial illumination but the LBSO-based PSC retained 93% of its initial PCE after 1000 h of full Sun illumination.19

The intrinsic method involves developing new PSK materials which are stable against moisture, heat, and light via composition engineering and modifying the original chemistry, and is explored in this review.

3.1 Stability of 3D ABX3 PSK based PSCs

As mentioned, the typical structure of 3D PSKs has an ABX3 formula that can adapt different crystal structures depending on the size and interaction of the A cation and the corner-sharing BX6 octahedra.20 In principle, in this structure, A+ ions are mainly responsible for maintaining the electroneutrality and cohesion of the lattices, though the electronic band structures of organicâ€“inorganic PSKs are governed by the electronic orbitals of the Xâˆ’ and B+ ions.21 Therefore, the A+ cation plays a critical role in affecting the symmetry of the PSK lattice and the associated phase transition, which will also influence the resulting band structures and relevant optoelectronic properties.22 In organicâ€“inorganic halide PSKs that are employed as absorbers in PSCs, the A+ cation is usually a monovalent cation (e.g. Cs+, CH3NH3+, and HC(NH2)2+), B represents a divalent metal (e.g. Pb2+ and Sn2+), and the X site is occupied by a halogen ion (Iâˆ’, Clâˆ’, or Brâˆ’). The PSK stability is strongly influenced by the Madelung electrostatic potential, and the electrostatic stabilization of organicâ€“inorganic hybrid halide PSKs with a small lattice energy is remarkably lower than that of metal oxide PSKs.23

The PSK microstructure has also been considered as one of the main key factors in the device degradation process. Interestingly, Wang et al. observed good stability of PSK single crystals that were stored in ambient air for more than two years. As a result, they concluded that CH3NH3PbI3 PSK materials might not be intrinsically unstable. They suggested that the moisture-induced degradation of 3D PSK films initiates from the grain boundaries. Therefore, the faster degradation and lower stability of the polycrystalline PSK material were attributed to the grain boundaries or grain size. They showed an increase in moisture stability by increasing the grain size and argued that water molecules permeate through or react with the grain boundaries quicker than with the top surface of the grains. In their study, the fastest degradation was observed in the films with the lowest average grain size of 297 nm in which almost 85% of their total surface was degraded after storage in a moist environment (RH around 85% adjusted by N2 gas) for 7 h. However, the PSK film with an average grain size of 678 nm showed only 10% degradation under similar conditions. Further, it was indicated that degradation of the grains was along the in-plane direction and amorphous grain boundaries include a higher content of water compared with the grain interior. In addition, in the solution processed CH3NH3PbI3 film, impurity segregation at grain boundaries is accompanied with the formation of an amorphous intergranular film that can be attributed to non-stoichiometric excess residuals in the grain boundaries (Fig. 4b).35 Sun et al. similarly argued that in the non-uniform PSK layer, the degradation initiates from the grain boundaries and disperses towards the bulk of the crystal grains. Thus, degradation was significantly reduced in films containing larger and more uniform grains compared to that in the films with smaller grains.34 Gomez et al. also confirmed that under light and voltage stress, the PSK layer degradation initiates from the PSK grain boundaries. They used photoconductive atomic force microscopy (pcAFM) to scan the PSK films under illumination and voltage stress. According to the photocurrent maps and topography images of the PSK film during light exposure shown in Fig. 6a and b, the photocurrent is distributed uniformly inside the PSK grains with discontinuities matching the grain boundaries. It is clearly shown that the degradation propagated from the grains' edge towards their bulk. Remarkably, the effect at the grain boundaries was significantly accelerated when the layers were kept under a combination of voltage and light stress which is more similar to the operation conditions of the device (Fig. 6c). Under these conditions, the effect at the grain boundaries in 2 h resembles that observed for the PSK layer kept for 24 h under illumination but without bias. Because the experiment was performed in dry air and no significant change was observed in the roughness of the PSK layer, Gomez et al. disproved that the diffusion of water from the boundaries into the bulk PSK grains was the reason for the propagation of the grain boundary effect. Additionally, the grain boundaries were defined to be responsible for the non-reversible power loss in the PSCs. Thus, grain boundaries act as the active sites for degradation that need to be passivated or minimized to have a stable device.43 In contrast to the aforementioned studies, according to investigation of local photoconductivity performed by Chu et al., the degradation of the PSK film did not initiate from grain boundaries but began with the disintegration of grains rather than nucleation and propagation from visible boundaries between neighbouring grains.44

Some efforts have been taken to improve the long-term stability of MA based PSKs and subsequently the device lifetime. Choi et al. doped MAPbI3 with Cs+ to optimize the optical and electrical properties of the PSK. This doping improved the PCE from 5.51% for the MAPbI3 device to 7.68% for the Cs0.1MA0.9PbI3 device. However, their long-term stability tests indicated that under ambient conditions (temperature of 20 Â°C, 11% RH), both devices showed poor stability. This was attributed to the acidic nature of PEDOT:PSS and rapid oxidation of the Al electrode.26 Similar to the previous work, Niu et al. also used cesium doping in the MAPbI3 lattice to form CsxMA1âˆ’xPbI3 PSK for overcoming the stability challenge. A PCE as high as 18.1% was achieved for the CsxMA1âˆ’x PbI3 PSC with x = 0.09. Stability tests showed good thermal stability with x = 0.09 and 0.02 and on further increasing the amount of Cs, the stability and performance of the constructed solar cells rapidly decreased. In the case of high Cs content, the higher bandgap of Cs based PSK and segregation and phase change from black to yellow of CsPbI3, were known to be the reasons for PCE reduction and low stability in the CsxMA1âˆ’xPbI3 lattice. Stability investigations in different atmospheres comprising nitrogen and dry and humid air showed the critical effect of oxygen on the decomposition rate. Halide deficiencies caused due to the loss of halide in the form of MAI precursors through the annealing process led to the formation of unsaturated Pb atoms and thus created recombination states. By introducing Cs to the MAPbI3 films, due to the higher stability of Cs in comparison with MA, there would be less loss of halide which leads to less unsaturated Pb and better performance of the device. With x = 0.09, there was no additional trace of unsaturated lead. Final stability evaluation under air exposure to 85 Â°C showed that for x = 0 and x = 0.09 about 60% and 20% reduction of performance occurred after 60 min, respectively. The achieved improvement was attributed to the partial substitution of MA with Cs atoms which suppressed thermal loss and oxidation of MA through less MA occupation in the A site of the PSK crystal and compressed crystal structure.48

3.1.1.1 Formamidinium based 3D-PSKs; FAPbX3. To overcome some of the challenges of MA based PSKs including thermal instability and photo-instability49 and also to improve the photovoltaic performance of PSCs, formamidinium (FA)-based PSKs were introduced which have longer charge diffusion lengths and extended light harvesting capabilities than the MA ones.50 However, pure FAPbI3 lacks structural stability at room temperature and a general observation is that MA-based PSK materials are more stable than FA-based ones against humidity.46 The strong tendency of FA for dissociation to ammonia and sym-triazine can be one of the main reasons for its lower stability.51 FAPbI3 can crystallize into either a photoactive PSK Î±-phase (black trigonal PSK) or a photoinactive non-PSK hexagonal Î´-phase (yellow hexagonal non-PSK) depending on the temperature of heat treatment.46 The Î±-phase of FAPbI3 is more thermally stable than MAPbI3 because of the relatively larger FA cation compared to the volatile MA cation. Though the Î±-FAPbI3 is metastable at temperatures lower than 160 Â°C it tends to convert to the thermodynamically more stable non-PSK Î´-phase which is accelerated at high humidity.52

A FAPbI3 based device with 4.3% PCE was first fabricated by Koh et al. It was shown that the band gap of FAPbI3 is 1.42 eV which is closer to the optimum value and lower than the band gap of MAPbI3 (1.52 eV). Through the synthesis of FAPbI3, a black trigonal PSK polymorph and a yellow hexagonal non-PSK polymorph were formed. 3.2 Stability progress of 3D PSK based devices regarding the interfaces related to the PSK layer

As illustrated in the previous sections, PSCs comprise different layers and interfaces such as PSK/HTL, PSK/ETL, and ETL and HTL/contacts. In reality, the performance and stability of these devices are strongly dependent on the properties of all of these components and interfaces. One of the most efficient and important strategies studied to improve the PCE and prolong the device lifetime is interfacial engineering. In fact, the interfacial layers can efficiently prevent undesirable chemical reactions between the adjacent layers while they retain the possibility of charge transport at the interfaces.

PSK materials are mostly processed by low temperature solution-based methods2 that lead to the formation of polycrystalline grains with high surface areas and unavoidable defects and atomic coordination deficiencies. As specified, the boundaries of these grains are considered as the active sites for initiating the degradation process.35 Thus, in addition to the interfaces related to the junctions including PSK/HTM, PSK/ETM, and transporting layers/contacts, the interfaces of the grains should be considered as one of the main key parameters for achieving a stable device. Accordingly, the studies related to interfacial engineering in the PSCs can be divided into several categories such as the interface modification of PSK and transporting layers, transporting layers and contacts, and PSK grains. For instance, Arora et al. added a conductive reduced graphene oxide spacer layer between a CuSCN HTL and Au which resulted in a PCE of 20.4% as well as negligible hysteresis. Moreover, the engineered PSC retained more than 95% of its initial PCE after aging at MPP for 1000 h (under full solar intensity at 60 Â°C).90 Likewise, a thin layer of tantalum doped tungsten oxide (Ta-WOx) was inserted between a poly[5,5â€²-bis(2-butyloctyl)-(2,2â€²-bithiophene)-4,4â€²-dicarboxylate-alt-5,5â€²-2,2â€²-bithiophene] (PDCBT) HTL and Au contact that led to the achievement of a PCE of 21.2% for a planar PSC. Remarkably, the PDCBT/Ta-WOx based device retained almost 95% of its PCE after 1000 h of exposure to one sun illumination in a home-built chamber filled with N2.91

According to the main scope of this review, the studies that relate to the interfaces are mainly concentrated on the PSK grains although all the junctions are determinant in the device stability. In this regard, Li et al. engineered the interfaces of the PSK grains by a crosslinking method using alkylphosphonic acid Ï‰-ammonium cations. It was evidenced that the phosphonic acid groups can form hydrogen bonds with the halide anions of the PSK and crosslink the neighboring PSK grains (Fig. 17a). The chemical modification of the surface of PSK grains using butylphosphonic acid 4-ammonium chloride (4-ABPACl) facilitated the growth of PSK crystals in the TiO2 mesoporous network and formed a smooth capping layer, leading to an enhancement of the PCE from 8.8% to 16.7%. In addition to the enhancement of the PCE, the stability of the PSCs was significantly improved after crosslinking of the grains. Over the first week of storage in the dark and at a RH of 55%, the device modified by 4-ABPA retained more than 90% of its initial PCE, while the pristine device lost more than 80% of its initial PCE (Fig. 17b). Also, the encapsulated modified and unmodified devices were illuminated continuously under 10 mW cmâˆ’2 ultraviolet-filtered simulated sun light at 45 Â°C for one week. Stability tests under conditions of holding on a hot plate at 100 Â°C in 50% RH, revealed that the sample with 0.5mg mlâˆ’1 FAI post treatment maintained its dark black color and its absorbance after 900 min compared to the control one.111

Fullerene derivatives such as PCBM are favored organic molecules that were used as efficient interface modifiers.112 Highly efficient PSCs with high thermal stability and photostability were fabricated by passivating the PSK grain boundaries using Î±-bis-PCBM. In this regard, the device with a PCE of 20.8% retained more than 90% of its performance after 44 days of storage in ambient air with a RH of 40% at 65 Â°C and 96% after 600 h under continuous full-sun illumination and MPP tracking, respectively. In contrast, the control device lost around 50% of its original PCE under the first set of conditions and was completely degraded after being exposed to continuous illumination for 250 h.112 Similarly, Park et al. introduced PCBM molecules as additives in different amounts into precursor solutions to improve the thermal stability of the CH3NH3PbI3âˆ’xClx PSKs. The added molecules which accumulated at the grain boundaries chemically interacted with the halogen atoms in the PSK and bound them to their original state in the PSK. Subsequently, they caused the passivation of the defects by preventing halogen atoms from exiting the crystal lattice and migrating from grain boundaries. Moreover, it was concluded that the larger PSK grains with a small grain interface area exhibited higher thermal stabilities. This illustrated the critical role of the grain size and boundary through the decomposition process which can be slowed down by adding additive molecules such as PCBM.113 C60 is another member of the fullerene family that was employed to improve both the performance and stability of PSCs. As a result of adding C60 into the PSK layer, the surface of the PSK grains was passivated and the migration of I and Pb elements was prevented, and the best PCE of 19.8% was obtained. Interestingly, the encapsulated PSCs retained 70% of its initial value after 1000 h of continuous full sun illumination while the reference device lost more than 80% of its original PCE. It should be noted that in this work, PCBM was not as efficient as C60 in defect passivation.114

The introduction of graphdiyne (GDY) hydrophobic molecules into the FA0.85MA0.15 Pb(I0.85Br0.15) PSK layer of a planar PSC affected the stability of the device as well as its performance. According to the XRD patterns and absorbance spectra of the samples, the addition of GDY had no effect on the band structure and crystallinity of the PSK layer. Interaction of GDY with the PSK causes the formation of a PSK/GDY bulk heterojunction and gave the layer advantages of better charge extraction and higher carrier mobility. These are speculated to be the reasons for the better performance of the device which is demonstrated in the recorded PCEs of 20.54% and 19.6% for 0.2GDY/PSK and pristine PSK, respectively. As evidenced, amounts less than 0.5mg mlâˆ’1 of GDY can passivate the defects and yield a better interface area between the PSK and ETL layer while amounts exceeding 0.5mg mlâˆ’1 can act as recombination centers which lead to undesirable PCEs. Samples comprising GDY showed better stability toward induced moisture. This result emphasizes the role of the presence of a hydrophobic compound in the grain boundaries which in addition to passivating the defects and forming a bulk heterojunction, prevents water molecules from entering the lattice and causing the decomposition of the device. Devices were kept in the dark and without encapsulation for 140 days. The results indicated that the device with 0.2GDY/PSK retained 95% of its initial PCE while the PCE of the control sample fell from 19.6% to 16.6%.115

4 Stability progress of PSCs regarding 2D Ruddlesdenâ€“Popper PSKs

As reviewed in the previous sections, three-dimensional (3D) PSK materials, especially organic cation based ones, suffer from intrinsic instability and are prone to degradation during exposure to heat, oxygen, moisture, and light. One important strategy that has been studied significantly regarding the stability of the PSK materials is employing 2D PSKs. In fact, 2D PSKs have been introduced as credible photostable and moisture-resistant light absorber candidates. As mentioned, the low formation energy of 3D PSKs accounts for their low stability relative to their precursor phases.

2D multilayered halide PSKs generally have a structural formula of (R)2(CH3NH3)nâˆ’1MnX3n+1, where R is a primary aliphatic or aromatic alkylammonium cation, M is a divalent metal such as Pb, and X is a halide anion such as I and Cl. When n = âˆž, it corresponds to the cubic PSK compound CH3NH3PbI3 and n = 2, 3, â€¦ corresponds to bilayer and trilayer compounds.117 In comparison to the 3D PSKs, 2D PSKs show more flexible structures, with more relaxed limitations in the size of the organic cations. Further, 2D PSKs with remarkable variations of electrical and photophysical properties can be produced.118 Reducing the dimensionality of the inorganic components from the 3D structure to the 2D structure causes enhancement of the exciton binding energy and bandgap enlargement.119 As presented in Fig. 18a, the stability study of dimensionally-tuned PSKs using density functional theory (DFT) simulations revealed that the conventional 3D PSK is marginally stable and the formation energy increases by the reduction of n values. The intercalation of appropriate organic molecules such as phenylethylammonium between the vPSK layers introduces considerable van der Waals interactions that cause the increase of the formation energy.120

2D PSKs are synthesized by inserting bulky alkylammonium cations such as 2-phenylethylammonium in between the anionic layers.117 In this structure, the adjacent layers are held together by weak van der Waals forces guaranteeing the stability of the structure, and also the hydrophobic nature of the spacing cations prevents the diffusion of water molecules into the PSK lattice.121 The importance of the stability issue in PSCs encouraged researchers to focus more on low dimensional PSKs as more moisture, photo, and temperature resistant materials. Research related to 2D PSK based PSCs can be divided into two categorizes, (a) pure 2D PSK and mixed 2D/3D PSKs as absorber layers and (b) 2D PSKs as a capping layer, that are reviewed in the following sections.

4.1 2D PSKs as the absorber layer

Interestingly, layered PSKs were introduced into PSCs to benefit their higher and tunable stability and tunable properties. For the first time, Smith et al.119 used two-dimensional hybrid PSKs as an absorber in PSK devices. They synthesized the n = 3 (n = number of Pbâ€“I sheets in each inorganic layer) member of the series (PEA)2(MA)nâˆ’1[PbnI3n+1] by combining (PEA: C6H5(CH2)2NH3+)I, (MA)I, and PbI2. PEA is a large organic cation that possesses a large ionic radius which is incompatible with a 3D PSK structure and yields the formation of a two-dimensional layered structure. In their study, 2D PSKs were more resistant to moisture compared to MAPbI3, and provided the possibility of fabrication of devices under humid conditions. Upon humidity exposure (RH: 52%), 3D MA based PSKs started to decompose after just 4â€“5 days while 2D PSK films showed no significant changes over a period of 46 days. The fabricated device containing the mentioned 2D structure displayed a low PCE around 4.73% because of the inhibition of out-of-plane charge transport by the organic cations.122 Actually, PEA acts like an insulating spacing layer between the conducting inorganic slabs.119

Obtaining superior prolonged stability of PSCs by employing 2D PSKs instead of 3D ones encouraged researchers to continue their studies; however, hereafter a mixture of 2D/3D PSKs was studied to overcome the challenge of the low PCE achieved in the first report. The Yao group118 deposited a mixture of 2D/3D PSK via a two-step method that formed compact PSK films by spin-coating a mixture of PbI2 and polyethylenimine hydriodide (PEI HI), followed by coating of a CH3NH3I layer. Among the devices containing different amounts of 2D PSK (PEI)2PbI4 incorporated into MAPbI3 â€“ that is, (MAPbI3)1âˆ’x[(PEI)2PbI4]x with the mole ratio x ranging between 0 and 4% â€“ the highest PCE of over 15% was achieved at x = 2% with higher reproducibility and moisture stability than MAPbI3 ones. In contrast to the MAPbI3 device which lost its performance completely after 120 h of exposure to ambient conditions with a RH of 50%, the PCEs of the (MAPbI3)1âˆ’x[(PEI)2PbI4]x (x = 2%) and (MAPbI3)1âˆ’x[(PEI)2PbI4]x (x = 4%) devices dropped by 16% and 8% on the 14th day, respectively. Similar to other studies, decreasing the possibility of the direct contact of PSK with water molecules led to enhanced stability.123

The moisture-resistant property of the 2D PSKs can be strongly attributed to the hydrophobicity of the cations used for the layered structure formation. n-Butylamine (BA) is another small molecule that has been used to form layered PSKs. Cao et al.124 fabricated 2D (CH3(CH2)3NH3)2(CH3NH3)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = 1, 2, 3, and 4) PSK thin films from a stoichiometric reaction between PbI2, MAI, and BA that combines the structural features of the simple 2D (n = 1) and the 3D (n = âˆž) PSKs. In fact, they used CH3(CH2)3NH3+ instead of PEA employed in the previous work.119 A comparison of the stability behavior of MAPbI3 and (BA)2(MA)2Pb3I10 showed that MAPbI3 gradually disintegrated to yellow PbI2 after a short time in a humid atmosphere, while (BA)2(MA)2Pb3I10 remained unchanged after 2 months of exposure under 40% humidity conditions. But the PCE achieved for the device involving 2D (BA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1PSKs was lower than 5% due to the low absorption coefficient and poor carrier transport.124 Quan et al. tuned the dimensionality of PSK by adding the phenylethylammonium iodide (PEAI) organic molecule that yielded compounds with different layer (n) values in the series PEA2(CH3NH3)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1. In both experimental and numerical investigations, the highest stability was achieved for the device with n = 6 while it showed the lowest performance due to its lower mobility (Fig. 18b). Moisture and thermal stabilities of the low dimensional devices were improved significantly compared to those of the 3D ones. In this regard, the PCE of the 3D device in humid air (55% RH) decreased from 16.47% to 4.2% in 3 days, while the PCEs of n = 60 and n = 40 devices decreased from 17.21% and 17.10% to 12.8% and 13.14% after 14 days, respectively.120

Regarding the effect of the type and size of cation molecules used for the synthesis of layered PSKs, Safdari et al. fabricated PSCs containing just pure 2D PSKs based on diammonium cations with different organic chain lengths. The 2D PSKs resulting from the reaction between PbI2 and the di-cations of 1,4-diaminobutane, 1,6-diaminohexane, and 1,8-diaminooctane were [NH3(CH2)4NH3]PbI4 (BdAPbI4), [NH3(CH2)6NH3]PbI4 (HdAPbI4), and [NH3(CH2)8NH3]PbI4 (OdAPbI4). It was concluded that the bulkier cation is the less suitable 2D PSK for PSCs, while an impressive thermal stability was observed for BdAPbI4 and HdAPbI4 up to 200 Â°C without any phase transition. However, a low PCE of 1.082% was achieved for the BdAPbI4 based device; it was comparable with the PCE of 2.1% for MAPbI3 fabricated under 55.6% humidity conditions.126 One important challenge in this structure is the insulating nature of organic spacer cations that hinders charge transport. By introducing a bulkier cation into the MAPbI3 materials which decreased the material dimensionality and enhanced the distance between Pbâ€“I structural units, the electrical conductivity was reduced.126,127 The conductivity of 3D MAPbI3 was reported to be around 1.1 Ã— 10âˆ’4 S cmâˆ’1, while a conductivity of 1.3 Ã— 10âˆ’6 S cmâˆ’1 was reported for the 1D (CH3CH2NH3)PbI3.126 To overcome the inefficient charge transport in 2D structures, Tsai et al. focused on the quality enhancement of the orientation toward single-crystalline. Uniform Ruddlesdenâ€“Popper phasesâ€”layered two-dimensional PSK films with crystalline planes aligned along the out-of-plane orientation, which facilitates efficient charge transport along the PSK planesâ€”were formed by a hot casting process. Compared to the 3D PSK device, (BA)2(MA)3Pb4I13 showed remarkably higher photo- and moisture stability both under encapsulated and unencapsulated conditions. Unencapsulated 2D PSK devices retained 80% of their initial PCE after 200 h and slowly degraded to about 70% of the initial PCE over 2050 h under one-sun illumination. In contrast, the 3D PSK device retained just 40% of its initial PCE within the first 24 h, followed by slow degradation to <10% of its initial PCE over 2250 h. Polymer cations have also been introduced as alternatives for small-molecule cations to form 2D PSKs. Polymer cations can improve the film quality as well as prevent moisture diffusion into the layer. Further, charge transport within the device can be improved compared to that of 2D PSKs employing small-molecule bulky ammonium because of the induced tight stacking of the separated inorganic unit layers. In this regard, Yao et al. used polyethylenimine (PEI) cations to form a 2D (PEI)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = 3, 5, 7) PSK. They argued that hydrophobic polymeric-ammonium can offer stronger steric hindrance, providing PSK crystals with improved moisture stability, and also more compact PSK films can be formed that prevent the diffusion of water molecules into the device. The device based on (PEI)2(MA)6Pb7I22 resulted in a maximum PCE of 10.08% which is much better than the photovoltaic parameters achieved for the (BA)2(MA)6Pb7I22 PSK device. Actually, fabricating the hybrid structure using polymeric-ammonium cations to covalently link adjacent PSK layers provided better charge transport properties. Another important result was that the performance loss observed for the large area 2D device was significantly lower than that of the large area 3D PSK device. Although the 3D MAPbI3 started to decompose immediately after exposure to a 50% humid atmosphere, no obvious changes were observed for (PEI)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 even after one month. Wang et al. showed that by engineering heterostructures between 2D and 3D PSK phases, both the performance and stability of solar cells can be achieved. 4.2 2D PSKs as the capping layer

The importance of the stability issue in PSCs encouraged researchers to focus more on low dimensional PSKs as more moisture resistant materials, though low PCEs were achieved in the early studies. The low PCE achieved in the primary study guided researchers to use 2D PSK materials at the interface of the PSK layer and HTMs. It should be noted that 2D PSKs have not only been used as absorber layers with higher stability than 3D ones, but they have also been used as capping layers or interfacial layers to protect the PSK absorber from moisture, leading to enhanced stability. It was argued that the 2D interfacial layer can act as a protective layer on the PSK layer by shielding it from atmospheric moisture and also improving charge injection through energy level matching. In this regard, for the first time, branched polyethylenimine hydriodide (PEI HI) was employed to in situ form layered PSK (PEI)2[PbI4] as a protective layer for the PSK film. The incorporation of a great moisture-resistant 2D PSK at the interface of HTM and MAPbI3xClx and MAPbI3 PSKs yielded PCEs of 14.79 and 13.09%, respectively, with long lifetime. In this study, both of these devices showed no significant changes after 10 days under illumination at 50% humidity, although the MAPbI3 based PSC was more stable.118 Using this strategy, both reasonable PCE and lifetime were obtained.

Hybrid PSK based on CA2PbI4/MAPbIxCl3âˆ’x is another mixed 2D/3D structure that was used to enhance the stability and performance of PSCs. 2D PSK (CA2PbI4) was synthesized in situ by a reaction of CAI (cyclopropylammonium iodide) with excess PbI2 on the top surface of 3D PSK. In contrast to the 3D PSK which showed rapid degradation over 8 days of storage under a RH of 63%, the mixed PSK exhibited no change in its absorbance for up to 40 days. The stability tests revealed that the device containing more hydrophobic 2D/3D PSK was appropriately stable and retained a majority of its initial PCE after 110 h of storage under ambient conditions with 63% RH. In contrast, 3D PSK devices degraded completely after only 50 h. The best PCE obtained for the mixed 2D/3D devices was 13.86% while the pure 3D device showed a PCE of 13.12%.139 Hu et al. reported employing a MAPbI3/layered PSK heterojunction in the device that combined the efficient light absorption of MAPbI3 and moisture resistance properties of layered PSK. Self-organized layered PSK incorporating methylammonium and long-chain phenylethylammonium (PEA+ = C6H5C2H4NH3+) or n-butylammonium (BA+ = C4H9NH3+) on top of MAPbI3 improved the PCE due to reduced recombination losses, as well as improved moisture stability in the devices. A device stability test was performed at 75% RH in air at room temperature for 19 days. Under these conditions, the PCE of MAPbI3 based devices decreased from 10% to 7%, while the PCE of the heterojunction devices decreased from 12.5% to 11.5%.140 In another study, an ultrathin 2D PSK (5-AVA)2PbI4 (5-AVA = 5-ammoniumvaleric acid) passivation layer was incorporated at the interface between (FAPbI3)0.88(CsPbBr3)0.12 and the hole transporting CuSCN, yielding a 16.75% PCE device with enhanced photo and moisture stability (Fig. 22).141

5 Summary and future outlook

The research on PSCs is undergoing rapid development; however, the majority of attempts are focused on achieving highly efficient devices. Although their PCE reached up to 23.3%, the short lifetime and fast degradation have still remained as the main challenges that have gained less attention. Thus far, lifetimes from a few minutes to several months were achieved in PSCs. As stated, issues of PSK device longevity can be addressed by two methods, the intrinsic and extrinsic methods. Particularly, the intrinsic method involves the development of new PSK materials stable against moisture, heat, and light via composition engineering and modifying the original chemistry. In fact, in this tremendous playground, a lot of effective parameters are present. Among all the effective parameters, PSK materials must display long-term stability under ambient conditions and at the upper end of the operational temperature range. The long term durability of organicâ€“inorganic PSK necessitates a stable crystalline structure against environmental stresses. This review summarized the progress of the changes in the PSK crystalline structure beginning from the 3D ABX3 structures based on MAPbX3 to the mixed halide and cation PSKs and finally to the low dimensional PSKs to prolong the device performance. In addition to the stability issues related to MAPbX3 as the first 3D structure used in PSCs, the degradation mechanisms were also illustrated. Then, other 3D PSKs engineered by the cooperation of organic and inorganic cations, various metals and halides were reviewed regarding the stability challenges. In general, 3D PSKs suffer from intrinsic instability over heat, light, and moisture which hindered their widespread commercialization. The origins of these instabilities are mainly attributed to the high hydrophilicity of organic cations in the PSK structure and the weak interaction between organic cations and the surrounding halide anions. In brief, MA-based PSKs are not thermo- and photo-stable absorbers and suffer from halide segregation instabilities. Although FA-based PSKs showed higher thermal stability, they suffer from high moisture sensitivity. In the case of CsPbI3, the perovskite phase can be formed though this photo-active phase is only stable at temperatures above 300 Â°C. It seems that FA-based PSKs mixed with other smaller cations can deliver the best balance between thermal stability and structural stability. Moreover, although some stability challenges were overcome by employing a mixture of halides, more study is needed to understand the exact mechanisms of the desired effects. Thus, cooperation of these PSKs to obtain durable mixed PSK structures still has enormous potential to prolong the lifetime of PSCs. In the case of metal sites, many efforts have been taken to substitute the toxic lead with Sn, though more significant stability challenges have emerged. As illustrated, the grain boundaries in the PSK films were defined as the most active sites in the degradation process and have been commonly passivated using organic small molecules and polymer chains. On the other hand, 2D and 2D/3D Ruddlesdenâ€“Popper PSK materials have provided desired stability for PSCs. Though intrinsic approaches such as lowering the dimensionality have significantly improved the thermal and moisture stability of PSCs, the devices based on the above new hydrophobic PSKs showed much lower PCE in comparison with 3D PSKs based devices. Thus far, very promising features were delivered by mixing 3D/2D structures, where a mixture of two structures was employed as the active layer or layered PSKs as capping layers. Remarkably, the 2D PSK family has enormous potential for achieving durable PSCs and still awaits further investigation.
