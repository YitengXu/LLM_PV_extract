Non-contact thermometer behavior of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+,Er3+ solid solution

Abstract

Oxides are physically and chemically stable. Non-contact thermometerYb3+Er3+ ions co-doped solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3, is prepared by the regular solid method. The structural results obtained by XRD indicate that a pure phase solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 has been obtained. The solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 has a similar crystal structure, especially Y2O3 and In2O3 with the same space group (Ia). Green emission from 500 to 600 nm is due to Er3+ 4f4f transitions: 4S3/2 4I15/2 at 567 nm and 2H11/2 4I15/2 at 528 nm. Red emissions from 630 to 720 nm are attributed to Er3+: 4F9/2 4I15/2. UC luminescence changes greatly with laser diode power and Er3+ and Yb3+ content. Furthermore, the two-photon process is confirmed to be dominant between Yb3+ and Er3+ in oxide solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3. Optical temperature sensitivity is also investigated systematically in order to explore the application of the oxide solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3. The temperature-dependent green fluorescence at 528 and 567 nm was investigated with the range of 313573 K. 0.316% K1 is the maximum absolute sensitivity at 503 K, which is higher than most Yb3+/Er3+ co-doped systems. In addition, the solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+,Er3+ has better thermal stability and stronger UC emission than a simple substance with excellent temperature sensing performance. It indicates that Yb3+Er3+ ions co-doped (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 solid solution is a good candidate for optical temperature sensing.

1. Introduction

Converting long wavelength light into short wavelength light, up-conversion (UC) luminescence involves the unique anti Stokes physical process.15 UC materials have several significant applications including bioimaging,6 solar cells,7 optical sensing8 and temperature measurements.9 In recent years, non-contact temperature measurements using rare-earth luminescent materials have attracted considerable attention.10 Rare earth doped up-conversion materials have great promise for optical sensing due to their non-contact property, fast response to light and temperature, high immunity to electromagnetic interference and high spatial resolution of temperature measurement. By thermal coupling energy levels (TCLs) of chosen pairs and tracking their fluorescence intensity at different temperatures, fluorescence intensity ratio (FIR) technology can be used to obtain the corresponding temperature. NaYF4 is currently the most efficient fluoride up-conversion luminescence material, such as NaYF4:Yb3+,Er3+, in which Yb3+ is used as a sensitizer and Er3+ is used as an activator.11 However, fluorides are generally unstable in air and are toxic, therefore it is crucial to limit use.12 However, oxides usually have excellent physical and chemical stability, and are particularly non-toxic and environmentally friendly. Therefore, in recent years, systematic studies of up-conversion oxides with an increased degree have been carried out.13,14 According to Planck's law, Ceramic Y2O3:Er3+,Yb3+ have excellent luminescence characteristics over a wide temperature range.34 In up-conversion luminescence, due to Yb3+ high near-infrared absorption cross section at 980 nm, it is usually used as a sensitizer and then energy transfer (ET) to other Ln3+, such as Er3+, Eu3+ and Cr3+.3537 As Er3+ has two suitable TCLs, 4S3/2 and 2H11/2, with a 770 cm1 energy gap, it is a widely used activator. Due to high and broad absorption and efficient ET to Er3+, the Yb3+/Er3+ co-doped hosts can significantly amplify the UC efficiency.1517 The studies on the up-conversion properties of UC solid solutions co-doped with Yb3+Er3+ ions, especially physically and chemically stable oxides, are still ongoing. The effective energy transfer process from Yb3+ to Er3+ is confirmed in In2O3.38 We suppose (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+,Er3+ has higher sensitivity. Herein, a UC phosphor (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+ solid solution has been successfully obtained via a traditional solid-state method. The photoluminescence properties were systematically studied, including UC luminescence, ET mechanism between Yb3+ and Er3+, and optical temperature sensitivity. When the UC solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+ was exposed to a 980 nm continuous laser, a bright orange output is visible, consisting of strong red radiation Er3+:4F9/2 4I15/2 and weak green radiation of Er3+:4S3/2 4I15/2, 2H11/2 4I15/2. From FIR technology, optical temperature sensitivity is probable with good approximation. In the range 313573 K, 0.316% K1 is the maximum sensitivity at 503 K, which is higher than that of most systems. These results demonstrate that (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+ can be used as an optical temperature sensor.

2. Experimental

2.1 Synthesis of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+Er3+

(Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+Er3+ solid solution phosphors were manufactured using a traditional solid phase process. Y2O3 (Analytical Reagent, A.R.), In2O3 (A.R.), Yb2O3 (4N) and Er2O3 (4N) are the raw materials. All reagents were weighed stoichiometrically and milled for 60 minutes. Then, the powder was sintered at 1400 C for 6 hours. The final product was obtained by further milling at room temperature for about 20 minutes.

2.2 Characterization

Bruker D8 diffractometer with Cu K1 radiation was used to detect the structure of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+Er3+. Data were collected across the range 10 2 80 with 0.02 increments. The microscopic morphology was characterized using a JSM-7800F scanning electron microscope (SEM). An energy dispersive spectrometery (EDS) was conducted on a ThermoFisher Scientific Talos F200S, Netherlands for sample composition analysis. A 980 nm cw laser was selected as excitation light for the UC luminescence. Visible radiation was collected using a Hitachi F-4600 Fluorescence spectrophotometer. An Orient-KOJI TAP-02 high-temperature fluorescence device was used for temperature-dependent luminescence behavior.

3. Results and discussion

3.1 Structure of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+

Fig. 1 shows the X-ray diffraction pattern of the synthesized (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+. The diffraction patterns of Yb3+Er3+ ions co-doped (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 obey well the data JCPDS No: 00-025-1097, standard (Y0.5In0.5)2O3, and no extra peaks are observed. The powder materials obtained in this way are purity phase and have no obvious lattice changes after Yb3+ and Er3+ doping into (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 solid solution. In accordance with activator's electrovalence and ionic radii, the ionic radii of Yb3+ and Er3+ are 11.6 and10.3 , respectively, which are near that of Y3+ (10.4 ) and In3+ (10.6 ) in the host lattice.18 Therefore, it was suggested that Er3+/Yb3+ occupy In/Y positions in (Y0.5In0.5)2O3. The X-ray diffraction diagram does not reveal the second phase, indicating that Yb3+/Er3+ ions are completely dissolved in (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 due to the substitution of In3+/Y3+ ions. Sharp diffraction peaks indicate that the sample is highly crystallized, which always increases luminescence intensity.

3.2 UC luminescent properties

UC spectra of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:xYb3+, 0.05Er3+ and (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:0.14Yb3+, yEr3+ under a 0.5 W cw 980 nm laser excitation are shown in Fig. 4 and 5. UC luminescence of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+,Er3+ contains the green and red emissions. Green emission in the range 500 to 600 nm is comprised of four peaks that belong to Er3+ 4f 4f transitions, 2H11/2 4I15/2 at 528 nm and Er3+: 4S3/2 4I15/2 at 567 nm.19 The red luminescence band from 620 to 730 nm is attributed to Er3+ transition 4F9/2 4I15/2. The emission intensity is sensitive to changes of Yb3+/Er3+ concentration in (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+,Er3+. When Er3+ is 5 mol% and Yb3+ is below 14 mol%, UC emission intensity for (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:xYb3+,0.05Er3+ increases first and then decreases (Fig. 4). The optimal Yb3+ content is determined as 14 mol% for (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:xYb3+,0.05Er3+. When Yb3+ exceeds 14mol%, UC emission gradually descends. The main reason may be energy back transfer (EBT) between Yb3+ and Er3+.10 When Yb3+ is 14 mol%, UC emission for (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:0.14Yb3+,yEr3+ also changes with the Er3+ concentration and the UC emission gets to its maximum at Er3+ 5 mol%, shown in Fig. 5. When the Er3+ concentration exceeds 5 mol%, the high doping concentration results in lower emission intensity for (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:0.14Yb3+,yEr3+ owning to concentration quenching effects caused by cross relaxation and nonradiative energy transfer between Er3+.2023 The Red emission of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:0.14Yb3+,0.05Er3+ is 9 times higher than that of green light, resulting in a bright orange color visible with the naked eye.

Fig. 8 is the schematic energy level diagram illustrating the UC mechanism of (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Er3+/Yb3+ solid solution ions. When Yb3+ absorb a 980 nm infrared photon, the electron is elevated from 2F7/2 to 2F5/2. Due to energy syntonic of Yb3+:2F5/2 and Er3+:4I11/2, ET from Yb3+ to Er3+ causes Er3+:4I15/2 4I11/2.25 Subsequently, electrons partially relax from 4I11/2 to 4I13/2 during nonradiative relaxation. After absorbing another photon, the population of 4I11/2 and 4I13/2 is stimulated to the 4F7/2 and 2F9/2 level. Thereafter, red emission (4F9/2 4I15/2) appears. In addition, 2H11/2 and 4S3/2 would be occupied by electrons from the 4F7/2 level because of nonradiative relaxation. The population on 4S3/2 partially relaxes to 4F9/2, generating 4F9/2 4I15/2 with red radiation. Meanwhile, the green emission, indicates transitions from 2H11/2 and 3S3/2 to 4I15/2.26 However, 4F7/2(Er3+) + 4I11/2(Er3+) 4F9/2(Er3+) + 4F9/2(Er3+), indicates that cross relaxation (CR) plays a dominant role instead of nonradiative relaxation.20,21 Meanwhile, there is an EBT, 4S3/2(Er3+) + 2F7/2(Yb3+) 4I13/2(Er3+) + 2F5/2(Yb3+), as Yb3+ ions concentration is high, which increases red radiation and weakens green emission.27,28

4. Conclusions

The up-conversion solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+/Er3+ was successfully synthesized and high UC emission was achieved under 980 nm excitation. The weak green radiation (Er3+:4F9/2 4I15/2 transition) and strong red radiation (Er3+:2H11/2 4I15/2 and 4S3/2 4I15/2 transition) were detected. As FIR optical thermometer, thermally coupled electronic levels 2H11/2/4S3/2, corresponding to green radiation of 528 nm/567 nm, depending on temperature in the range 313573 K, were systemically investigated. Maximum absolute sensitivity of the solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3 is 0.316% K1 at 503 K. All results make the solid solution (Y0.5In0.5)2O3:Yb3+Er3+ a promising physically and chemically stable optical temperature sensor.
