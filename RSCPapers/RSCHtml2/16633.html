Abstract

A novel monomer alkoxybiphenyl-substituted benzodithiophene (BDTBP) was synthesized and used as the donor to construct a donorâ€“acceptor copolymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT with 4,7-di(4-(2-ethylhexyl)-2-thienyl)-5,6-difluoro-2,1,3-benzothiadiazole (DTffBT) as the acceptor unit. Compared with the corresponding polymer PBDTPâ€“DTffBT based on alkoxyphenyl-substituted BDT, PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT exhibits a red-shifted UV-vis absorption spectra due to its extended Ï€-conjugation structure. The maximum power conversion efficiency (PCE) of PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT based polymer solar cells (PSCs) is 6.70% with Voc = 0.85 V, Jsc = 12.72 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF = 62.14%, and the hole mobility is 3.45 Ã— 10âˆ’4 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 while the maximum PCE of PSCs based on PBDTPâ€“DTffBT is only 4.91% and the hole mobility is 1.70 Ã— 10âˆ’5 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1. These findings demonstrate that BDTBP unit is a promising building block for constructing a copolymer with high hole mobility, and extending the Ï€-conjugation length on the alkoxyphenyl-substituted BDT is helpful to improve the photovoltaic properties.

1 Introduction

Polymer solar cells (PSCs) have developed into a research hotspot because of their low cost and flexibility, and great progress have been made in bulk heterojunction (BHJ) PSCs in recent years.1â€“6,7 The power conversion efficiency (PCE) for single and tandem junction PSCs based on polymer and fullerene derivatives has reached over 10%.8â€“12 The PCE of PSCs is determined by the open-circuit voltage (Voc), short-circuit current (Jsc), and fill factor (FF),13,14 therefore, many strategies have been used to tune the Voc, Jsc and FF for achieving higher PCE.15â€“18 One of the most efficient strategies is to use a D (donor)â€“A (acceptor) copolymer14,19,20,27 with an electron-rich unit as the donor and electron-deficient unit as the acceptor. Thus, choosing appropriate donor and acceptor units for Dâ€“A copolymers is an efficient route to achieve high PCE of PSCs devices.21

In conjugated polymers, electrons are transported along the conjugated backbone via inter-molecular Ï€â€“Ï€ interactions, therefore, enhancing the inter-molecular Ï€â€“Ï€ interaction is an efficient method to improve charge transport properties. Using a two-dimensional (2-D) conjugated structure is an efficient strategy to achieve improved photovoltaic properties by enhancing absorption in visible region and increasing hole mobility,33 which is widely used in the structure design of the benzodithiophene (BDT) unit.34â€“38 The 2-D BDT22â€“26 is constructed by introducing thiophene (BDTT),28,29,39 benzene (BDTP)31,40 or other conjugated units as side groups to the BDT core. The BDTT is the most widely used 2D conjugated structure based on BDT. The photovoltaic performances can be improved by extending the Ï€-conjugation length, thus improving the inter-molecular Ï€â€“Ï€ interaction further.41 Several extended Ï€-conjugation structures based on BDTT were synthesized by joining a conjugated unit onto the conjugated side chain9,42â€“47 (for example, introducing bithienyl or phenylthiophene units as the side chains to the BDT core instead of the thiophene group).

An alkylphenyl group30 instead of an alkylthienyl group attached to the BDT core was first synthesized by Yangâ€™s group in 2012. The polymer based on alkylphenyl-substituted BDT exhibited a deeper HOMO than the alkylthienyl-substituted BDT based polymer with the same acceptor. So a polymer based on extending the conjugated side chains on BDTP may be expected to improve the photovoltaic performances and keep the deep HOMO. It is a pity that there is almost no relevant work to investigate the extended Ï€-conjugation on the BDTP unit. Whatâ€™s more, the monomer based on extending conjugated side chain on BDTP exhibits more symmetric side chains than does this approach on BDTT due to the structure differences between benzene and thiophene unit. So adjusting the side chain based on BDTP for optimizing the optoelectronic properties of PSCs may be easier than that for BDTT.

Our group32 synthesized the polymer PBDTPâ€“DTffBT with alkoxyphenyl-substituted BDT and 4,7-di((4-(2-ethylhexyl)-2-thienyl)-5,6-difluoro-2,1,3-benzothiadiazole (DTffBT). The maximum PCE of PBDTPâ€“DTffBT was 4.91% with a Voc = 0.84 V, a Jsc = 11.81 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF = 49.43%. Inspired by the extended Ï€-conjugation strategy, a new monomer BDTBP was synthesized by introducing an alkoxybiphenyl group instead of an alkoxyphenyl group onto the BDT core (Chart 1). We synthesized a polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT with BDTBP and DTffBT by a Stille coupling reaction with Pd2(dba)3 and P(o-tol)3 as catalysts. The maximum PCE of PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT was 6.70% with Voc = 0.85 V, Jsc = 12.72 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF = 62.14% after thermal annealing, and the band gap of PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT was 1.71 eV calculated by the absorption onset. In comparison with PBDTPâ€“DTffBT, the PSCs devices based on the polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT exhibited higher PCE and Jsc, which may be attributed to its red-shifted absorption spectrum and higher hole mobility. This work demonstrated that extending the Ï€-conjugation length can work well on BDTP to improve its photovoltaic performance.

2.4 Theoretical calculations

In order to further research the optoelectronic properties of the polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT, density functional theory (DFT) calculations were performed by a Gaussian 09 program in the gas phase. These calculations with a model of B3LYP/6-31G were simplified by some strategies, such as replacing the long alkyl chains with methyl units and choosing one repeating unit as the object of calculation. When calculating vibrational frequencies at the same level, the minimum energy conformation was defined as the optimized molecular geometry. The optimized molecular geometries and energy levels are shown in Fig. 3, and detailed HOMO, LUMO energy levels and bandgap are displayed in Table 1. The LUMO was mainly delocalized in the DTffBT unit, while the HOMO was located both in the donor and acceptor unit. The simulated energy levels from the DFT calculations could coincide well with the experimental data measured by cyclic voltammograms.
Fig. 3 Optimized molecular geometries and frontier molecular orbitals for PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT obtained by DFT calculations.

As shown in Fig. 4, the dihedral angles of PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT between the side chain and the backbone is 57.67Â° and the dihedral angle between the two benzene units is 36.47Â°, which are larger than the dihedral angles of bithophene attached to the BDT. The increased dihedral angles between side chains and backbone may induce the decreased Ï€-orbital overlap between neighboring aromatic units and the DTffBT unit, while the HOMO was located both in the donor unit and the acceptor unit, which may lead to changes in the HOMO energy levels. Moreover, the thiophene unit exhibits stronger electron-donating than does the benzene ring. So the HOMO of the BDTBP based polymer may be lower than that of BDTTT, which is consistent with reported works40 (Fig. S2 and S3, ESIâ€ ).

2.6 Morphology characterization

Thermal annealing plays an important role in morphology control in PSCs devices. Atomic force microscopy (AFM) was carried out to investigate the influence of thermal annealing on the morphology of the polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT:PC71BM blend film. Processed with the thermal annealing, the polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT exhibited better miscibility with PC71BM in the active layer. 3 Conclusion

To summarize, we have synthesized a novel extended-conjugation BDT structure (BDTBP) by introducing the biphenyl unit to the BDT core. It was as the electron-rich donor for constructing the Dâ€“A copolymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT with DTffBT as the acceptor. Compared with the corresponding polymer PBDTPâ€“DTffBT, the polymer PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT exhibited an extended conjugated length, red-shifted absorption spectra and higher hole mobility. As a result, the photovoltaic properties of PBDTBPâ€“DTffBT were improved and the maximum PCE of then PSCs was 6.70%.
