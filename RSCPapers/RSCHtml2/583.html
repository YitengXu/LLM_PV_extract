Abstract

A novel star-shaped molecule based on triphenylamine as the core and 5,5-bibenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole as arms (S(TPA-BBT)) was synthesized and investigated as an electron acceptor in solution-processed organic solar cells. The compound S(TPA-BBT) shows excellent thermal stability with a decomposition temperature of 353 Â°C. S(TPA-BBT) in chloroform solution exhibits two absorption peaks at 326 and 450 nm, which red shifts to 334 and 482 nm in thin film, respectively. The HOMO and LUMO energies are estimated to be âˆ’5.48 and âˆ’3.10 eV, respectively, from electrochemistry, and match with those of poly(3-hexylthiophene) (P3HT). Solution-processed solar cells based on P3HT:S(TPA-BBT) after annealing at 150 Â°C for 10 min yield power conversion efficiencies up to 0.81%.

1. Introduction

Organic solar cells (OSCs) that can be fabricated by simple solution processing techniques are under intense investigation in academic and industrial laboratories because of their potential to enable mass production of flexible, light-weight and cost-effective devices.1 During the past decade, solution-processed bulk heterojunction (BHJ) OSCs have attracted much attention with encouraging power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) over 9%.2 Fullerenes and their derivatives have been the dominant electron acceptor materials in BHJ OSCs due to their large electron affinity, isotropy of charge transport and high electron mobility.3 However, fullerenes have a few disadvantages, such as restricted electronic tuning and weak absorption in the visible region. Furthermore, in a typical BHJ system of poly(3-hexylthiophene):[6,6]-phenyl C61 butyric acid methyl ester (P3HT:PC61BM), energy level mismatching between the donor and the acceptor results in low open-circuit voltage (VOC) and energy loss. To solve these problems, new electron acceptor materials with strong and broad absorption spectra and appropriate energy levels are desirable. Recently, non-fullerene small molecule acceptors have been developed;4 however, very few reports on solution-processed fullerene-free OSCs have shown PCEs approaching or exceeding 2%.5 High anisotropy in light absorption and charge transport is of critical concern for linear non-fullerene acceptor materials. To our knowledge, there have been very few reports on multi-branched or star-shaped electron acceptors so far.6

5,5-Bibenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (BBT) is a 5,5-connected dimer of a widely used electron deficient building block benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (BT). Due to several attractive properties of the BBT unit, such as easy molecular structure tailoring and facile electronic structure manipulation, we have firstly synthesized BBT based organic semiconductors, and demonstrated their application in organic electronics, and so far BBT based organic semiconductors have been used in organic field-effect transistors,7 phototransistors,8 solar cells9 and light-emitting diodes.9b Recently, several BT based molecules have shown promising photovoltaic performance in OSCs as electron acceptors with the PCE up to 2.54%,5e,i which is among the highest values reported for small molecule non-fullerene acceptors. Relative to BT, BBT has stronger electron-accepting ability due to two BT units, therefore BBT could also be a promising building block for the synthesis of electron acceptors.

Triphenylamine (TPA) has a special propeller starburst molecular structure due to the sp3 hybrid orbital of the nitrogen atom.10 As a result, amorphous materials with isotropic optical and charge-transporting properties could be expected when combining TPA with Ï€-conjugated systems. In the field of designing star-shaped TPA-based molecules for OSCs, Roncali and coworkers have carried out systematic research.1c,11 We also reported several solution-processed star-shaped donors and acceptors with TPA as the core for promising OSCs.6,12

Here we design and synthesize a novel star-shaped small molecule based on TPA as the core and BBT as arms (S(TPA-BBT), Scheme 1) and demonstrate its application in OSCs as the first example of BBT-based non-fullerene electron acceptors. S(TPA-BBT) exhibited excellent thermal stability and appropriate energy levels matching with P3HT. Solution-processed BHJ OSCs based on P3HT:S(TPA-BBT) showed PCEs of 0.81%.

2. Results and discussion

Synthesis and characterization

A synthetic route towards S(TPA-BBT) is shown in Scheme 1. BBT was brominated by bromine in hydrobromic acid to yield compound 1. The intermediate compound 2 was synthesized through Stille coupling reaction between compound 1 and tributyl(4-dodecylthiophen-2-yl)stannane using Pd(PPh3)4 as the catalyst. Compound 2 was brominated by N-bromosuccinimide to afford compound 3. Finally, S(TPA-BBT) was synthesized by a Stille coupling reaction between compound 3 and tris(4-(tributylstannyl)phenyl)amine. S(TPA-BBT) was fully characterized by MALDI-TOF MS, 1H NMR, 13C NMR, and elemental analysis. S(TPA-BBT) is readily soluble in common organic solvents such as dichloromethane, chloroform and dichlorobenzene at room temperature, owing to three solubilizing n-dodecyl substituents. The thermal properties of S(TPA-BBT) were investigated by thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) and differential scanning calorimetry (DSC). This star-shaped molecule exhibits excellent thermal stability with a decomposition temperature (5% weight loss) of 353 Â°C in a nitrogen atmosphere (Fig. 1a). The DSC traces for this compound show no melting peak from room temperature to 250 Â°C (Fig. 1b), suggesting that it could be amorphous.

Optical and electrochemical properties

Fig. 1c shows the normalized spectra of optical absorption of S(TPA-BBT) in chloroform solution (10âˆ’6 M) and in thin solid film. S(TPA-BBT) in solution exhibits two absorption peaks at 326 nm and 450 nm, which exhibits red shift to 334 and 482 nm in thin film, respectively. The optical band gap (Eg) estimated from the absorption edge (568 nm) of the thin film is 2.18 eV, similar to that of the widely used electron acceptor PC61BM.3

The electrochemical property of S(TPA-BBT) was investigated by a cyclic voltammetry (CV) method in film on a glassy carbon working electrode in 0.1 M [nBu4N]+[PF6]âˆ’ CH3CN solution at a potential scan rate of 100 mV sâˆ’1. As shown in Fig. 1d, S(TPA-BBT) exhibits quasi-reversible reduction and irreversible oxidation waves. The onset oxidation and reduction potentials versus FeCp2+/0 are 0.68 and âˆ’1.70 V, respectively. The HOMO and LUMO energies were estimated to be âˆ’5.48 and âˆ’3.10 eV from the onset oxidation and reduction potentials, respectively, assuming the absolute energy level of FeCp2+/0 to be 4.8 eV below vacuum. The HOMO and LUMO energies are lower than those (âˆ’4.76 and âˆ’2.74 eV) of P3HT, respectively.13 The LUMO gap (0.36 eV) and HOMO gap (0.82 eV) between P3HT and S(TPA-BBT) are large enough to guarantee efficient exciton dissociation.14 The difference between the LUMO of S(TPA-BBT) and the HOMO of P3HT is as large as 1.66 eV, which could result in high VOC of solar cells.

3. Conclusions

In summary, a novel star-shaped non-fullerene acceptor (S(TPA-BBT)) with TPA as the core and BBT as arms was synthesized and applied in BHJ OSCs as the first example of BBT-based non-fullerene acceptors. S(TPA-BBT) has excellent thermal stability and appropriate energy levels matching with P3HT. The BHJ OSCs based on the P3HT:S(TPA-BBT) blend after annealing at 150 Â°C for 10 min gave a PCE of 0.81%. This preliminary work demonstrates that BBT unit may be a promising building block of non-fullerene acceptors for OSCs.
