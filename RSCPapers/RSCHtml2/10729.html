Abstract

The acceptorâ€“donorâ€“acceptor structured fused-ring electron acceptors (FREAs) have piqued interest for organic solar cells. We herein employ time-dependent density functional theory to evaluate the effect of Hartreeâ€“Fock exact exchange (HFX) on the performance of 16 global hybrid functionals for computing the maximum absorption wavelengths (Î»ver-theo) and the vertical excitation energies (Ever-theo) of 34 molecules. We customize the HFX ratio in the functionals used to perform an in-depth analysis of its impact on the Ever-theo values. The computed Î»ver-theo values strictly follow an inverse proportionality to the HFX percentage. The performance of the methods with the same ratio of HFX is almost identical, such as B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE containing 20% HFX. The performance enhances with a relatively higher HFX ratio of 21% in X3LYP, B971, B972, and 22% in B98 giving smaller deviations. APF and APFD containing 23% HFX provide the smallest deviations for all compounds, with a mean signed error limited to 0.02 eV and a mean absolute error (MAE) of 0.06 eV. The performance drops using M06 and M05 with comparatively higher HFX ratios providing MAE values of 0.07 eV and 0.1 eV, respectively. M06-2X with 54% HFX provides the largest MAE value of 0.35 eV. The lowest obtained MAE is 0.06 eV at 23 to 25% HFX in most of the functionals considered in this study, suggesting that these are the optimal values for the prediction of excitation energies of FREAs. It has also been found that global hybrids seem to be more efficient for larger-sized molecules with a smaller bandgap.

1 Introduction

Organic solar cells (OSCs) with the bulk-heterojunction blend of non-fullerene acceptors (NFAs) and donor polymers as active layers have been the foundation of significant inventions because the NFA-based devices outperformed the fullerene counterparts in terms of efficiency, flexibility, stability, light weight, and easy synthesis and solution processing.1â€“3 The donor:acceptor blend crammed between two electrodes absorbs solar photons to produce excitons, which subsequently divide into free electrons and holes at the acceptor:donor interface and then drift in opposite directions towards the corresponding electrodes. Among the NFA family, the acceptorâ€“donorâ€“acceptor (Aâ€“Dâ€“A) structured fused-ring electron acceptors (FREAs) have made marvelous development due to their versatile structure alteration to attain excellent absorption of sunlight and tunable energy levels.4,5 The magnificent design and continuing progress of Aâ€“Dâ€“A FREAs have pushed the power conversion efficiencies for OSCs up to 19%.6,7

FREAs have low reorganization energy and robust electron accepting properties for a single electron reduction.8 They are the lower bandgap components in the active layer of OSCs to assist in capturing solar photons in the visible and near-infrared (NIR) wavelengths, whereas the fullerene acceptors have a broader bandgap. The strong NIR absorption, lower exciton binding energy and boosted optical cavity effects hence endow FREAs with high extinction coefficients and refractive indices.9,10 Due to superior NIR sunlight harvesting, FREAs generate higher open-circuit voltage values than fullerene-based acceptors. Extensive experimental and theoretical efforts are being made to push the OSC performance limit to those of solar cells based on inorganic semiconductors. Compared to the experimental trial and error procedures, simulation saves time and resources in the design and fine-tuning of the optoelectronic characteristics of photovoltaic materials to achieve the optimal efficiency in OSCs. Therefore, the performance of the theoretical approach adopted for specific features of certain materials is crucial.

Calculations employing density functional theory (DFT) and linear-response time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) yield transition energies at comparatively low computing costs. TD-DFT has thus become an established approach for exploring the electronic excited states of big organic molecules. The excited state interpretation assists in studying the exciton and charge generation, transport, and charge-recombination processes in OSCs. Importantly, the TD-DFT framework can take the solvent effects into account for the excited state simulations of organic photovoltaic (OPV) materials. Numerous DFT functionals have been formulated and categorized into different sets, such as local-spin-density approximation (LSDA), generalized-gradient approximation (GGA), meta-GGAs (mGGAs), global hybrids (GHs), range-separated hybrids (RSHs), and double-hybrid functionals.11,12 However, various functionals provide a poor depiction of Rydberg and charge transfer excitations, dropping the accuracy of computed transition energies for large molecules. Based on the comparatively better performance of GHs, i.e., B3LYP13â€“15 and PBE0 (PBE1PBE)16,17 than LC-Ï‰PBE, CAM-B3LYP, and PBE for the nature and energies of the excited states of Aâ€“Dâ€“A FREAs in our previous studies,18,19 we further explore the performance of GHs for FREAs by extending the functional set with a varying ratio of non-local Hartreeâ€“Fock exchange (HFX). The PBE0 technique outperformed the B3LYP technique when they were mutually compared in our previous reported studies.16,17 The GHs are widely used to optimize the chemical structures and calculate the optoelectronic properties of organic materials.

Fig. 1 and 2 present the chemical structures of molecules considered in this study. Based on the number of fused rings in the electron-donating unit (D), FREAs are classified as tri, penta, hepta, and nonacyclic molecules in the literature.20 F(DPP)2B2,21 Flu-RH,22 and FRd223 are tricyclic FREAs with fluorene as their D core. Cz-RH22 and ITDI24 have carbazole and indenothiophene as D cores, respectively. The electron accepting units (A) in the tricyclic FREAs studied here mainly include diketopyrrolopyrrole, (dicyanomethylidene)-indan-1-one, and 3-ethylrhodanine. Most of the pentacyclic FREAs adopt indacenodithiophene as the D unit, such as IDT-BOC6,25 ATT-1,26 DC-IDT2T,27 IEICO,28 IEICO-4F,29 ATT-2,30 and ITOIC-2F.31 Cyanoindanone, 1,1-dicyanomethylene-3-indanone, and 2-(1,1-dicyanomethylene) rhodanine are the most commonly used A units in pentacyclic molecules.

The accurate modeling of optoelectronic properties using ab initio quantum chemistry methods is a vibrant determination of the researchers addressing the electronic structure theory.39 Numerous TD-DFT benchmark studies have compared the vertical excitation and 0â€“0 energies produced using DFT functionals with the experimentally measured data and the energies computed using correlated ab initio approaches. Dierksen and Grimme40 studied the excited-state geometries of biological chromophores and organic dyes using numerous functionals. The theoretical 0â€“0 energies indicated that the vibronic structure was rigorously governed by the HFX ratio in a hybrid functional. Nguyen and co-workers41 studied the vertical excited states of 7-aminocoumarins using the GHs and RSHs. They concluded that the former functionals provided more accurate results than the latter. Jacquemin and co-workers42 investigated the Ï€ to Ï€* excitation of 15 dye molecules and found that the errors were extensive for RSHs but with smaller statistical dispersion, permitting additional expedient chemical intuitions. Jacquemin and co-workers43 also performed a TD-DFT study using different functionals and the Hartreeâ€“Fock theory for vertical absorption of more than 100 organic dyes. They found that PBE0 outperformed B3LYP and CAM-B3LYP with the smallest mean absolute error (MAE) of 0.14 eV. Leang and co-workers44 used 24 functionals to compute the transition energies of 14 small organic molecules. Compared to the experimental data, they found that the PBE0 and M06-2X methods had the lowest MAE of 0.28 eV and 0.26 eV, respectively. CAM-B3LYP gave a smaller MAE of 0.3 eV for singlet excited-states but a larger MAE of 1.1 eV for triplet excited-state transition energies. Isegawa and co-workers45 computed transition energies using 30 DFT functionals comprising mGGAs and RSHs. They found that the Ï‰B97X-D and M06-2X methods produced the lowest MAE of 0.30 eV. The CAM-B3LYP method gave a MAE of 0.32 eV. Charaf-Eddin and co-workers46 reproduced the emission and absorption spectra of 20 representative conjugated molecules using six hybrid density functionals. They concluded that the B3LYP method had the minimum MAEs for the emission and absorption maxima. The M06-2X method gave more correct values of transition energy for challenging materials. Fang and co-workers47 used TD-DFT, second-order coupled cluster (CC2), and configuration interaction singles (CIS) to calculate the vertical, adiabatic, and 0â€“0 transition energies of a benchmark set of 79 molecules covering both organic and inorganic systems. The 0â€“0 energy values calculated with the CC2 level of theory were mostly found to be in agreement with the experimental ones. PBE0, Ï‰B97X-D, B3LYP, CAMB3LYP, and M06-2X methods also produced values that mostly agreed with the experimental values, whereas higher MAEs were noticed with M06-HF, BP86, and CIS. Jacquemin and co-workers48 presented a study on the excited states of 80 medium and large conjugated molecules using five wave function approaches and TD-DFT. Suellen and co-workers49 presented an investigation into the 41 excited-states of organic molecules using wave function methods and TD-DFT. They concluded that TD-DFT was more accurate for the absorption energies as compared to the adiabatic and fluorescence energies. Shao and co-workers50 concluded in a recent study for biochromophores using TD-DFT that the vertical transition energies strongly depended on the amount of HFX in GHs.

In this report, we, for the first time, accurately evaluate the impact of the HFX on the TD-DFT performance when computing the vertical excited-state energies (Ever-theo) of a comprehensive set of 34 FREAs employing 16 GHs, including GGAs and mGGAs. As can be seen in Fig. 1 and 2, the major types of FREAs contain tri, penta, hepta, and nonacyclic fused-ring structures. The performances of the GHs for calculation of the excited-state energies can fluctuate in accordance with the benchmark set of molecules considered. For instance, in our previous TD-DFT benchmark study for a set of 50 FREAs, the B3LYP and PBE0 methods provided MAEs of 0.108 eV and 0.06 eV, respectively.18 In another study, we reported that on evaluating the nature of the vertical excited-states of a different set of 12 FREAs, the B3LYP and PBE0 methods produced MAEs of 0.06 eV and 0.05 eV, respectively.19 However, the benchmark set of 34 representative FREAs and 16 GH functionals fulfills the requirements to achieve the objectives of this study. The performance of the GHs was assessed using the conventional functionals before varying the HFX fraction to locate the optimal value. This study provides the performance of a wide range of GHs for the Ever-theo of large molecules. It also uncovers how the performance of a particular functional fluctuates with the molecular size changes and the customized optimal value of the HFX in the functional employed. This study paves the way for developing more efficient DFT functionals for larger organic molecules.

2 Theory and computational methodology

We aimed to evaluate the performance of the GH DFT functionals, including the GGAs and the mGGAs, with various HFX ratios in the TD-DFT framework for the vertical excited states of FREAs. The primary motivations for this research are twofold: (i) how the GH functionals used in this work ensure their relevance for calculating the Ever-theo of this efficient class of OPV materials, and (ii) what influences the HFX percentage has on their performance. The accuracy of the GH functionals for a specific property and material is related to the fraction of the non-local HFX. The local-spin-density gradient along with 20 to 25% HFX incorporated into the global hybrid GGA functionals for molecules corrects the significant over-binding given by the LSDA.51 The amount of HFX in the global hybrid GGAs intensely influences the Ever-theo.50,52 In our prior research work,18,19 the B3LYP13â€“15 method with a 20% HFX produced a larger mean signed error (MSE) and a MAE for Ever-theo values of FREAs, when compared with the PBE016,17 method with a 25% HFX. This indicates that the GH accuracy was related to the HFX ratio for the Ever-theo of FREAs, hence, the performance improves when PBE0 with a larger HFX percentage is employed. This motivated us to dig further into this relationship to find the GH with an optimal HFX ratio for the vertical excited states of FREAs. Hence, we picked a group of 16 distinct GHs with HFX ranging from 20% to 54% and a benchmark set of 34 representative FREAs. Table 1 lists all of the functionals along with their HFX percentages.

M05 eliminates the self-correlation effects by balancing the kinetic-energy density in the exchangeâ€“correlation functionals. In principle, theoretical 0â€“0 transition energies should be compared to the experimental 0â€“0 transition energies. Since there is no easy way to calculate the former for these large molecules and the latter are typically unavailable, the Ever-theo values are compared to the transition energies (Emax-exp) calculated from the experimental maximum wavelength (Î»max) values. All molecular geometries already optimized using the PBE0 method and a double-Î¶ basis set 6-31G(d,p) are adopted from our previous work.18 The alkyl chains of all molecules have been simplified into methyl groups before optimization in order to save simulation time, which is the same strategy used in the literature with no change in the optical properties of molecules.48,62 The 6-31G(d,p) basis set is also used to do the excited-state calculations, as it produces the reliable electronic trends between the theoretically anticipated and the existing experimental data for the OSC.62â€“64 The PBE0 method is acknowledged as a reliable method for producing the optimized ground-state molecular geometries for most of the organic molecules.65â€“67 All of the TD-DFT computations are conducted using the Gaussian 09 software.68 Narsaria and co-workers69 found that the transition energies in solution predicted through TD-DFT were more systematic and accurate than those anticipated in the gas phase when the experimental transition energies measured in solution were available. The polar solvent chloroform helps to stabilize the increased dipole moment caused by the electronic transition from D to A in the excited state.70 Hence, all the TD-DFT simulations are only accomplished in chloroform, and the experimental Î»max values recorded in the same solvent are taken from the literature. The polarizable continuum model (PCM)71 is a widely used model to account for solvent effects in the excited-state simulations of the OPV materials.48 Consequently, the solution cavity effects are taken into account using the PCM approach to give an actual TD-DFT performance for FREAs. The standard non-equilibrium TD-DFT protocol, specially developed for excited states, is nominated.72â€“75 The vibronic couplings are required for an ideal match between simulation and experimental spectra. The calculation of the Frankâ€“Condon factors for molecules makes the perfect match possible between the simulation and the experimental results.76â€“78 However, this analysis is not computationally viable for such an extended set of large-size molecules. Hence, the vertical approximation is adopted in this study.

Simple linear regression analysis (LRA) is performed on the Ever-theo and Î»ver-theo values obtained using TD-DFT. Correlations are developed between the experimental and TD-DFT data by obtaining the linear polynomials for all functionals. These polynomials are used to obtain the linearly-corrected Ever-theo and Î»ver-theo values for the whole set of functionals. MAE and RMSE values are obtained from the linearly-corrected values of Ever-theo and Î»ver-theo and experimental data. The MAE and RMSE values obtained using the TD-DFT actual data are, respectively, compared with those obtained from the linearly-corrected data.

A straight comparison of the computed Ever-theo values with the experimentally recorded spectrum is challenging. The spectrum peaks are widened by the vibrational and dynamical effects, mainly due to intensity scrounging for weak excitations. Hence, we adopt the standard conventional method to compare the values of Ever-theo to those of Emax-exp.50 The Î»ver-theo values obtained through B3LYP and PBE0 are taken from our previous study.18 To evaluate the TD-DFT performance for regenerating the experimental UV-visible spectra of 34 FREAs using all 15 GH functionals, the determination coefficient (R2), MAE, MSE, and the root-mean-square error (RMSE) are computed from the experimental data and compared. The Ever-theo values for all molecules are calculated with various HFX ratios in an individual functional to understand the impact of HFX on the excitation energies.

To uncover the effect of HFX in GH functionals for through-space charge transfer (CT), we qualitatively calculate the CT magnitude for a subset of 10 representative FREAs using Le Bahersâ€™ approach.79,80 This method is based on the computed electron density difference between the ground and excited states of pushâ€“pull molecules. It permits description of the spatial extent and electron transferred fraction associated to a given charge transfer transition. It is based on the definition of centroids of charges associated to the electronic-density rise and reduction regions upon transition. It gives a qualitative and easy way to visualize the measure of the spatial extent of the acceptor and the donor units of a molecule. An index is developed that allows defining the presence of eventually pathologic CT excitations for TD-DFT treatment in combination with GGA or GH functionals. Three functionals with an HFX amount of 20, 23, and 25% are selected for the as-mentioned purpose. The amount of CT for 10 FREAs is computed using APF, and the CT values given by PBE0 and B3LYP are taken from previous research.18 The electronic density maps are also obtained for PBE0 and B3LYP.

3.1 Comparison with experimental data

The Î»ver-theo and Ever-theo values of 34 FREAs obtained by employing B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE agree with the experimental data (Fig. 3, Table 2 and Tables S1, S2, ESIâ€ ). These functionals almost give similar results for the whole set of molecules, with slight deviations for B3LYP compared with the results presented for the other two methods. These methods have a non-local HFX of 20%. Compared to B3PW91 and mPW3PBE, the largest variation in the whole set of FREAs is 9 nm for IPIC-4Cl with B3LYP (Table S2, ESIâ€ ). To know the performance of the B3LYP method for FREAs in detail, one can read our previous study.18,19 The other two functionals behave similarly to the B3LYP method, although with somewhat smaller Î»ver-theo values for all molecules. It might be explained by the fact that all three GHs have the same HFX ratio. The B3PW91 and mPW3PBE methods almost give identical values of Î»ver-theo for all the molecules. In general, the Î»ver-theo value for an individual compound shows a smaller deviation from the experimental Î»max value. For instance, B3LYP gives a maximum absolute deviation of 78 nm for FRd2, whereas B3PW91 and mPW3PBE yield 75 nm for the same molecule. It demonstrates that the magnitudes of the deviations given by B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE follow a similar trend for an individual compound.

B3PW91 and mPW3PBE give equal MSE, MAE, and RMSE, which are 0.07, 0.09, and 0.11 eV, respectively (Table 3). The corresponding values for B3LYP are 0.08, 0.09, and 0.12 eV, as shown in Table 3. Against the same Î»max value (500 nm), each functional gives a larger value of Î»ver-theo for Flu-RH than that for CzRH (Tables S2 and S3, ESIâ€ ).

The Ever-theo values calculated using X3LYP, B971, B972 and B98 (first three with 21% HFX and B98 with 22% HFX) for any individual molecule are larger than those given by any of the methods of B3LYP, B3PW91 and mPW3PBE (Table 2 and Table S1, ESIâ€ ). This means that for FREAs, the Ever-theo values obtained with the GHs usually follow the trend of a larger HFX ratio producing larger Ever-theo values (smaller Î»ver-theo values). However, the tendency among some GHs is sometimes an exception. For instance, the Ever-theo values for Cz-RH computed through B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE are 2.27, 2.28, and 2.28 eV, respectively. It increases to 2.30 eV for X3LYP and B971 and 2.31 eV for B972. Despite having a larger HFX ratio of 22%, the B98 method gives a smaller Ever-theo value of 2.29 eV when compared with GHs comprising 21% HFX (Table 2 and Table S1, ESIâ€ ).

The MSEs obtained through X3LYP, B971, B972, and B98 are 0.05, 0.06, 0.05, and 0.04 eV, respectively, and the corresponding MAEs are 0.07, 0.08, 0.07, and 0.07 eV (Table 3). The lower MAE values indicate that these four hybrid methods show better performance than B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE. The Ever-theo values for an individual molecule and the MAEs and MSEs for the whole set show that the X3LYP and B972 methods perform nicely and give the same results. The maximum absolute deviation for all four methods is not more than 71 nm (for B971 in Table S2, ESIâ€ ). In comparison, B98 offers remarkably less deviation (MSE and MAE of 0.04 and 0.07 eV, respectively) for the current set of FREAs. The MSE provided by B98 is half that of B3LYP, and the same improvement in MAE (in eV) given by B98 is 22%. The 2% increase in the HFX amount from B3LYP to B98 delivers a considerable improvement in the TD-DFT performance for FREAs. X3LYP and B972 systematically produce smaller errors for most of the molecules compared to those given by the B971 and B98 methods, as presented in Table S4 (ESIâ€ ). The APF functional is an optimized combination of 58.9% PBE0 (PBE1PBE) and 41.1% B3PW91 that efficiently eradicates all spurious interactions, i.e., the long-range repulsive or attractive interactions found in most of the DFT models.

It serves as a solid foundation for adding an empirical dispersion correction term derived from a spherical atom model to form APFD. APF and its APFD variant have an HFX of 23%. Both of them generate equal Ever-theo values for each of the FREAs and hence exhibit unerringly the same and exceptional prediction performance across all the methods evaluated in this study. For simplicity, we will refer to APF throughout the text, and the conclusions are the same for APFD.

The deviations are smallest (i.e., âˆ’0.02, 0, or 0.02 eV) for ITOIC-F, ITOIC-2F, m-ITIC, IT-DM, IT-M, ITCPTC, ITIC2, INIC, INIC2, INIC1, and INIC3 (Table S5, ESIâ€ ). A vigilant analysis of the errors (Tables S4 and S5, ESIâ€ ) reveals that the deviations shift from positive to negative for most molecules going from B98 to APF. Hence, APF is the most efficient functional for the present set of FREAs and may be used to evaluate the nature of the excited states of new FREAs for high-performance OSCs.

M05 and M06 produce larger errors for the tricyclic compounds than for others (see Table S5 in the ESIâ€ ). This switching indicates that underestimation for small molecules transforms to overestimation for larger molecules (Table S5, ESIâ€ ). For hepta and nonacyclic FREAs, the M05 method produces negative deviations larger than those of M06 (Table S5, ESIâ€ ), indicating that M06 is more efficient for smaller band-gap FREAs. M06 has bigger positive and smaller negative deviations for the whole set of FREAs, whereas M05 does the opposite. The plots presented in Fig. 3 compare the Ever-theo values against the Emax-exp values for all functionals, which show a good agreement between the experimental and TD-DFT data. 4 Conclusions

We investigated the impact of the Hartreeâ€“Fock exact exchange (HFX) on the performance of global hybrid (GH) functionals using TD-DFT for computing the vertical excitation energies (Ever-theo) of Aâ€“Dâ€“A structured fused-ring electron acceptors (FREAs). The computed Ever-theo values strictly follow a direct proportionality to the HFX fraction. The performance of the GHs with the same HFX ratio is almost identical. All three methods i.e., B3LYP, B3PW91, and mPW3PBE with 20% HFX provide a mean absolute error (MAE) of 0.09 eV. The performance improves with 21% HFX ratio in X3LYP, B971, and B972, and 22% in B98 giving 0.07, 0.08, 0.07, and 0.07 MAE values, respectively. For the benchmark set of FREAs, the APF and APFD with 23% HFX give the smallest deviations, with the MSE and MAE limited to 0.02 eV and 0.06 eV, respectively. The APF and APFD tend to control the overestimation and the underestimation of the excitation energies. The performance drops with M06 and M05 with higher HFX ratios of 27 and 28%, respectively. M06 and M05 provide MAEs of 0.07 and 0.10 eV, respectively. M06-2X with 54% HFX provides the largest MAE value of 0.35 eV.

By varying the HFX ratio, the smallest MAE value of 0.06 eV was obtained at 23 to 25% HFX fraction in most of the GH functionals considered in this study. This suggests that 23, 24, and 25% HFX ratios are the optimal values for the best prediction of the FREA excitation energies. It is also found that the GHs seem to be more efficient for the larger-sized FREAs with smaller Ever-theo values, applying the vertical approximation. This research can provide accurate assessments of the excitation energies for new FREA designs for organic solar cells.
