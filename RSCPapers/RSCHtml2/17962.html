Abstract

New DâAâ²âÏâA type (D = electron donor, Aâ² = auxiliary acceptor, Ï = Ï-conjugated bridge, and A = electron acceptor/anchor) sensitizers with two bis(alkoxy)phenyl substituents incorporating thieno[3,4-b]pyrazines (TP) or a benzo[3,4-b]pyrazine (BP) entity as the auxiliary acceptor have been synthesized for application in dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs). 1 Introduction

In recent years, the drastic improvement in the efficiency1 of metal-free dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs)2 has paved a path leading to their commercialization parallel to conventional Si-based solar cells. Compared with Si-based solar cells, DSSCs have immense potential owing to their flexibility in molecular design, easy device fabrication, environmental friendliness and low cost of production.3 A high power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 13% has been achieved with metal-free dyes1b and Zn-porphyrin dyes.1c Record PCEs have also been achieved for a co-dyed single cell (14.3%)1e and a tandem cell (14.64%),1j using a cobalt-based redox mediator. These values are very close to the threshold required for commercialization (15%). In order to further boost the performance of DSSCs under 1 sun, sensitizers with absorption extending to the near-infrared (NIR) region (â¼700â1400 nm) are very important as NIR light (700â2500 nm) constitutes â¼50% of the solar spectrum. Among NIR sensitizers, the most representative dyes are porphyrins and phthalocyanines.4 Though Si solar cells (absorbing in the range of 350â1000 nm) have excellent efficiencies under 1 sun illumination, they are outperformed by DSSCs under dim light conditions because of two reasons: (1) indoor light sources such as fluorescent lamps and white LED lights cover the visible region mainly from 400 to 650 nm; (2) being an indirect band gap material, Si solar cells need intense light illumination for efficient light harvesting. Currently DSSCs are deemed promising for indoor applications such as powering portable electronics (sensors, wireless transmitters, etc.) used in the Internet of Things (IoTs).3a,5 However, there are only limited reports on dye-sensitized photovoltaics under indoor lighting.6

In this research, we intended to develop metal-free sensitizers for both indoor and outdoor DSSCs. We figured that dyes with panchromatic absorption in the visible region up to â¼650 nm could cover the spectrum of T5 or a white LED light source. In order to achieve this goal, we set out to develop DâAâ²âÏâA (D = electron donor, Aâ² = auxiliary acceptor, Ï = Ï-conjugated bridge, and A = electron acceptor/anchor) type sensitizers.7 Incorporation of an electron deficient entity as the auxiliary acceptor in the conjugated spacer of DâÏâA dyes is beneficial for red shifting the absorption spectra due to deepened LUMO and improving the photostability of sensitizers. We reckon that Aâ² is like a double-edged sword: on one hand, it helps with red shifting the absorption spectra; on the other hand, it deteriorates electron injection of the excited dye molecule due to charge trapping.8 Therefore, proper design of conjugated spacers with Aâ² is needed for the good performance of DSSCs. For example, replacing benzothiadiazole (BT)9 with more electron deficient pyrido[2,1,3]thiadiazole (PyT)10 resulted in lower cell performance in spite of the longer wavelength ICT band. In comparison, the cell performance was improved by one order when PyT was replaced with less electron deficient 2H-[1,2,3]triazolo[4,5-c]pyridine (PT).11 Similarly, replacing pyridoimidazole (PIm) with 1H-benzo[d]imidazole (BIm) led to better cell efficiency.12 We therefore set out to develop DâAâ²âÏâA type sensitizers with benzo[3,4-b]pyrazine (BP), an electron-withdrawing entity popularly used for small band gap DâA type polymeric materials,13 as the auxiliary acceptor (Aâ²). BP was selected as its moderate electron deficiency7b may avoid spectral over red shift of the dye and charge trapping of the excited dye molecule. A more electron deficient entity, thieno[3,4-b]pyrazine (TP), was also chosen as the electron-withdrawing entity for comparison. Incorporation of alkyl or aryl substituents in the pyrazine ring of BP or TP allows for tuning the solubility and optical properties of dyes.14 Herein we incorporate a 3,4-dihexyloxyphenyl moiety at the 2- and the 3-site of BP and TP to alleviate serious dye aggregation. To our knowledge, DâAâ²âÏâA dyes incorporating a dialkoxyphenyl unit at the auxiliary acceptor have not been reported so far, though there are examples of dyes with a 2,4-dialkoxyphenyl moiety at the donor.15 In addition to ambient light, the DSSCs fabricated from the new dyes were also explored under 1 sun. The results show that these novel dyes perform well under both 1 sun irradiation and 300â6000 lux T5 fluorescent light, providing viable materials for realizing the practical use of DSSCs.

2.4 Computational details

All the calculations have been performed using the Gaussian 09 program package.17 Geometrical optimization was performed in vacuo using the B3LYP exchangeâcorrelation functional and 6-311G (d,p)18 basis set. The optimized geometries were then used to obtain frontier molecular orbitals (FMOs). To simulate the optical spectra, the lowest spin allowed singletâsinglet transitions were computed on the ground state geometry. The TDDFT calculations of the lowest singletâsinglet excitations were performed in THF solution on the optimized geometries using the CAM-B3LYP/6-311G (d,p) level of theory. The integral equation formalism polarizable continuum model (PCM)19 within self-consistent reaction field (SCRF) theory has been used to describe the solvation of the dyes. The TDDFT calculations were also performed using CAM-B3LYP with the 6-311G (d,p) basis set. GaussSum 2.2.5 software was utilized to simulate the major portion of the absorption spectrum and to analyze the nature of transitions.20

Cyclic voltammetry (CV) measurements generally provide information on the feasibility of electron injection and dye regeneration. The CV measurements of the MD dyes were carried out in THF using tetra-n-butylammonium hexafluorophosphate (TBAPF6) as the supporting electrolyte. Electrochemical impedance spectra (EIS) taken in the dark were used to analyze the interfacial electron transfer properties in the DSSCs. Fig. 6a shows the Nyquist plots for the DSSCs based on the MD4âMD7 dyes. 4 Conclusions

We have developed four new DâAâ²âÏâA type sensitizers (MD4âMD7) with thieno[3,4-b]pyrazines (TP) and benzo[3,4-b]pyrazine (BP) as the auxiliary acceptor. Incorporation of the 3,4-dihexyloxyphenyl moiety at the 2- and 3-sites of TP and BP improves the solubility of the dyes in organic solvents and helps with suppression of dark current in the DSSCs. The best efficiency of 8.21% for MD7 reaches 92% of that of the N719-based standard cell. Addition of 1 mM CDCA as a co-adsorbent helps with alleviation of dye aggregation and improvement of cell performance. Consequently, MD7 has an efficiency of 9.03%, which surpassed that of the N719-based cell. The DSSCs based on MD5 and MD7 have cell efficiencies >15% under 300 lux irradiance (T5 fluorescent light source). The cell efficiency of MD7 reaches 18.95% under 300 lux and 27.17% under 6000 lux irradiance.
