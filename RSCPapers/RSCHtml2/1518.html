Abstract

Organic upconversion is a unique process in which low-energy light (usually NIR light) is converted to high-energy light through either the two-photon absorption (TPA) mechanism or the tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (TTA) mechanism. Both TPA upconversion (TPA-UC) and TTA upconversion (TTA-UC) have been actively investigated in recent years due to their intriguing applications in optics, biophotonics, and solar energy utilization. Although they show some similarity (i.e., belonging to the nonlinear two-quantum process and needing focused excitation light), TPA-UC and TTA-UC are very different, such as in mechanism, characteristics involved, molecular design and potential applications. Here, we collectively reviewed these two kinds of upconversion processes and compared their respective characteristics and merits. We also present recent advances made in the areas of TPA- and TTA-UC, the remaining challenges and opportunities, with particular emphasis on molecular engineering of these two active upconversion materials.

1. Introduction

Organic molecular photon-upconversion (UC) can convert two low-energy photons into one higher-energy photon by either the two-photon absorption (TPA) mechanism1 or the tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (TTA) mechanism.2 The two-photon absorption (TPA) can be described by the two-state model3 or the three-state model4,5 that a molecule can be excited from the ground state (S0) to the first excited singlet state (S1) or to the second excited singlet state (S2) by simultaneous absorption of two photons, via a virtual state. It is the virtual state (see Fig. 1a) that makes two-photon absorption require high excitation power density as large as more than MW cmâˆ’2, which was only achieved by an ultrafast laser. After two photons are absorbed to produce an excited state, the relaxation of an electron would give rise to the emission of a photon with frequency greater that those of the absorbed photons, that is, TPA upconversion (TPA-UC). Importantly, the TPA shows quadratic intensity dependence and possesses the overwhelming advantages of high spatial resolution and deep medium penetration, which can bring about numerous potential applications in biophotonics (fluorescence bioimaging6â€“9 and photodynamic therapy10), optics (two-photon data storage11 and frequency upconverted lasing12,13) and nano-micro fabrication.14

The other organic upconversion is tripletâ€“triplet annihilation upconversion (TTA-UC) (see Fig. 1b). This TTA-UC needs a bimolecular system containing a triplet sensitizer and a triplet acceptor (i.e., emitter).15 The sensitizer firstly absorbs low-power light, then undergoes intersystem crossing (ISC) and further transfers its triplet energy to the triplet state of the acceptor (TTT). Subsequently two nearby triplet acceptors undergo tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (TTA), producing a frequency-upconverted fluorescence (i.e. TTA upconversion).15 Since TTA-UC undergoes the inter-molecular two respective one-photon process that is transition allowed and just needs low excitation intensity (less than 100 mW cmâˆ’2, solar energy is enough).16 Thus, attractive solar energy applications, including photovoltaics,17â€“19 photosynthesis20 and photocatalysis,21 would be possible.

Although TPA-UC and TTA-UC possess different mechanisms, both belong to a nonlinear two-quantum process and need focused excitation light. The former involves a one-step intra-molecular two-photon excitation process through a virtual state which imposes certain symmetry restrictions. It also requires high excitation intensity thus necessitating the use of short pulse lasers with high peak intensities. In contrast, tripletâ€“triplet annihilation upconversion utilizes an intermolecular process in which two neighboring molecules in triplet excited states produced by respective one photon excitation, interacting to lead one molecule to the emitting higher energy state and the other dropping to the ground state. Thus even though the annihilation behaves like a nonlinear process, it actually involves coupling of two linear excitations, which can be generated by using an intensity CW laser or even an incoherent lamp. There is no symmetry requirement for TTA.

Currently, there are several reviews already existed, which deal with these two upconversion processes independently, none have described them collectively. This review described comparatively these two kinds of photon upconversion (UC) concluding their characteristics, measurements and the active materials as well as notable applications.

Lastly, the S1 state decays from the excited state to the ground state and irradiates the upconversion fluorescence. Theoretically, two triplet acceptors (3A1*) undergo the TTA process that are governed by the spin statistic law (eqn (16)).94 That is, when two excited triplets (3A1*) interact, nine encounter-pair spin states are produced with equal probability which are composed of three distinct sublevels: i.e., five of which are quintet (5[AA]2*), three of which are triplet (3[AA]1*) and one of which is singlet (1[AA]0*). 3.2 Polymer-based upconversion

Since the solution-based upconverted material is sensitive to oxygen, solid-state upconversion materials are much more attractive, which is convenient for real-world applications without solution toxicity and solvent evaporation. In 2011, Baluschev and Landfester reported the new nanocapsules by encapsulating perylene and PdOEP in polymeric matrixes (Fig. 19a).126 Such nanocapsules show efficient UC emission at 450 nm when dispersed in an aqueous environment and present potential application in the tissue and cells.126 Later, Monguzzi's group successfully obtained PtOEP/DPA-doped polymer nanoparticles (NPs) in aqueous suspension and drop-cast films (Fig. 19b).121 The up-conversion efficiency of the NPs was comparable to that of the corresponding dye solution and the excitation-power threshold (Ith) was as low as a few mW cmâˆ’2 (approaching solar irradiance).121 At the same time, Li et al. also prepared the water-soluble upconversion nanoparticles by coloading PtOEP/DPA into silica nanoparticles with the help of surfactants (Fig. 19c).123 These nanoparticles exhibit high upconversion efficiency at 4.5% (260 mW cmâˆ’2) in pure water, which have shown low cytotoxicity and successfully used to label living cells with high signal-to-noise ratio.123 Also, Kim et al. obtained the TTA-upconversion (UC) materials achieved in the aqueous phase by encapsulating the solvent phase (hexadecane, HD) containing PtOEP/DPA in a rigid polymer shell (polyisobutylene, PIB) in the form of aqueous dispersible microcapsules (Fig. 19d).20 The green-to-blue upconversion efficiency of the PtOEP/DPA system in HD/PIB was at 10% (at 100 mW cmâˆ’2) and 28% (2000 mW cmâˆ’2), respectively. These upconverted microcapsules eliminated the need for the deoxygenation step that is essential for existing TTA-based UC processes and enabled stable TTA-UC to occur even after a month of exposure to the ambient environment.

In seeking for the active TPA-UC and TTA-UC chromophores, physical scientists, chemists and material scientists are required to play the critical roles in the investigation of materials and photophysical rules, because the design of new and efficient chromophores is dependent on molecular engineering and optical operation. Recent TPA upconversion advance134 shows that the metalâ€“organic framework materials can effectively boost their multiphoton absorption cross-sections and enhanced upconversion efficiency based on FÃ¶rster resonance energy transfer. Within hostâ€“guest metalâ€“organic frameworks consisting of encapsulated high quantum yielding guest molecules, the multi-photon upconversion can be effectively enhanced at 800, 1200, and 1500 nm femtosecond pulsed laser excitation.134 The multi-photon upconversion is much more beneficial owing to the cubic or biquadratic dependence of the input light intensity and the use of a much longer excitation wavelength. Also, multi-photon excitation would present better spatial confinement of the excitation volume and thus higher resolution. Furthermore, the multi-photon lasing can result in the largest anti-Stokes emission so far.12 An other recent progress135 in developing TPA materials showed that an organic single crystal of an oligo(phenylene vinylene) can exhibit strong two-photon excited fluorescence and stimulated emission, with large TPA cross sections spanning a wide wavelength range at 700â€“800 nm. As for TTA-UC molecule engineering, sensitizers with intense absorption of visible light in the red and near-IR range, accompanied by long triplet lifetime (Ï„P) is aspired. Meanwhile developing acceptors with high fluorescence quantum yield (Î¦F) is also important. Moreover, the energy levels between the sensitizer and acceptor should be matched, that is, ET1(D) > ET1(A) and 2ET1(A) > ES1(A) > ES1(D) (i.e., the excited triplet state of the sensitizer (ET1(D)) is higher in energy than the triplet state of the acceptor (ET1(A)), the singlet excited state of the acceptor (ES1(A)) should be higher in energy than that of the singlet excited state of the sensitizer (ES1(D)) and lower than two times the triplet state energy of the acceptor itself).136 Recent TTA upconversion advance137â€“139 reveals that developing the sensitizers with a high triplet energy level (ET) and the acceptors with a small singletâ€“triplet difference (Î”EST) is beneficial to tripletâ€“triplet energy transfer efficiency (Î¦TTT) and especially to promoting tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (Î¦TTA).137â€“139 In addition, considering that the spin multiplicities of the other 5/9 and 3/9 of spin multiplicities can also be annihilated via the singlet pathway.140 How to use these the spin multiplicities is worth the whistle.

Finally, it should be pointed out that the key to increase the upconversion efficiency is to enhance the intramolecular charge-transfer for TPA-UC and the intermolecular energy-transfer for TTA-UC. Probably, in the framework of the hostâ€“guest super-molecular system, the combination of the FÃ¶rster energy-transfer for TPA-UC and Dexter electron-transfer for TTA-UC is a new strategy of molecule engineering. The investigation of the relationship between TPA-UC and TTA-UC is challenging and especially worth pursuing.
