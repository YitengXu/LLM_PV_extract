Abstract

We have performed a theoretical investigation using a combination of DFT/TDDFT and molecular dynamics simulations to explain the relationship between bulky donor groups and higher efficiencies for a recent indoline sensitizer YA422 derived from its counterparts IQ4 and YA421, which features an extended donor subunit for use in dye-sensitizer solar cells (DSSCs). Firstly, the absorption and fluorescence properties indicate that the FÃ¶rster resonance energy transfer occurs only in YA422, where the Î»ems of the donor group D2 matches with the Î»abs of the whole molecule around the band at 533 nm. Secondly, the simulated heterogeneity between the sensitizer and (TiO2)124 before and after the co-adsorption of the additive CDCA shows that the nearest position of every monomer in YA422 is separated by a row of Ti atoms due to the steric hindrance introduced by the extended donor group, which not only forms the ordered alignment but also prevents aggregation. Meanwhile, the separated position decreases the self-decay which is shown by the complete intramolecular charge transfer in the aggregate structure of (TiO2)124. Furthermore, using a combined NewnsâAnderson approach and the Marcus equation, a faster electron injection rate kinject of YA422 (2.27 Ã 1015 sâ1) is obtained compared with those of IQ4 and YA421. We confirm that the higher conversion efficiency achieved by YA422 is caused by its bulky donor group which enhances the electron-donation and transfer. Finally, based on the above insights, we designed a novel sensitizer DW1 which is expected to be a promising candidate due to its enhanced absorption and larger kinject compared with those of YA422.

1. Introduction

Dye sensitized-solar cells have aroused great interest in academia and engineering during recent decades due to the urgency of searching for sustainable and clean energy resources all over the world inspired by the first report by the pioneer group.1 Since then, considerable efforts have been made to further improve the conversion efficiency by optimizing the components such as sensitizers,2â8 electrolytes,9â11 photoanodes12â14 and counter electrodes.15,16 Meanwhile, the most critical mechanisms involved in photoinduced electron injection and recombination are still receiving much attention because a deeper understanding of the process of electron transfer can guide the synthesis of potential candidates before the real device is assembled.

In the recent yearsâ efforts, however, the conversion efficiencies have not improved obviously, especially in pure organic sensitizer-based devices because the ÏâÏ stacking in their own solutions17 and on the semiconductor surface (commonly TiO2)18 can lead to fast fluorescence decay from the excited state to the ground state, and deteriorates the electron injection efficiency.19â21 Thus experimental works have focused on co-sensitization via more than one kind of sensitizer22â27 to complement the absorption of each other28,29 which not only covers the panchromatic window extraordinarily but also produces a better short-circuit density Jsc, or co-adsorption with an anti-aggregate additive (commonly chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA))30,31 which aims to separate the sensitizers one by one and achieves a higher open-circuit voltage Voc.

For the process of co-sensitization, the inter-type FÃ¶rster resonance energy transfer (FRET)32â38 from the energy donor to the acceptor (the former features a shorter-wavelength absorption and is coded as a âblueâ or energy relay dye, while the latter possesses a longer-wavelength response and is named as a âredâ dye) occurs through nonradiative dipoleâdipole coupling. This strategy not only decreases the usage of every kind of sensitizer but also improves the overall efficiencies at the compatible ratio.29,34,39,40 However, an observation of the detrimental inter-type FRET via experiment41 says that the weaker dipoleâdipole coupling which features the relative vertical orientation can enhance the electron injection in a âblue dyeâ, while the more parallel orientation strengthens the intermolecular FRET at the expense of the degradation of its electron injection. Alternatively, this inter-type FRET can be transferred to intra-type FRET38,42,43 by end-capping the energy donor in the energy acceptor directly, similarly to an âantennaâ where the energy can be transferred without lessening the injection efficiency of the energy donor and this can be embraced.

The representative case is YA422 (Fig. 1),44 an indoline sensitizer derivatized from IQ4 (ref. 44 and 54) and YA421 (ref. 44) (Fig. 1), which achieves an excellent conversion efficiency of Î· = 10.65% among indoline sensitizer-based devices.45â53 It has been shown that the conversion efficiency is enhanced in the order IQ4, YA421 and YA422 from 7.57% to 8.84% and then to 9.60% in the [Co(bpy)3]2+/3+ redox, and finally, a further improvement to 10.65% sensitized by YA422 is obtained using gold-doped graphene as the counter electrode.44 Although the enhanced conversion efficiency of YA422 benefits from the extended donor group which can prevent the [Co(bpy)3]2+/3+ redox close to the TiO2 surface, the structureâproperty relationship, photoinduced energy transfer and electron injection rate still need to be understood deeply.

Therefore, it is the aim of this paper to explore the relationship between the extended donor part and the final efficiency from the viewpoints of the intra-type FRET, the capacity of anti-aggregation and the electron injection rate. Additionally, the co-adsorption along with chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA) is also considered based on experiments to explain whether it is necessary in the bulky YA421 and YA422.

And then the results and discussion analyze (1) the intra-type FÃ¶rster resonance energy transfer in our defined donor groups (D1(D1â²)/D2(D2â²), Fig. 1) in YA421/YA422, respectively (2) the influence of the extended donor group on the adsorption mode and the role of CDCA on the anti-aggregation in YA421 and YA422 as well as (3) the electron injection rate of these three sensitizers and (4) the properties of a newly designed sensitizer DW1 (Fig. 1). The final section concludes with a summary.

2. Methodology

2.1 Electronic structure computations

For the optimization of isolated sensitizers in the ground and oxidized states, the density functional theory (DFT) B3LYP55 functional with the 6-31G(d)56/LANL2DZ57 basis set for C, H, O, N, S/Ti atoms was used. The subsequent simulation of frequency based on the optimized geometries was carried out to determine that these geometries were indeed the minima on the potential energy surface. For the intermolecular interaction of the dimeric structure on the TiO2 substrate, the B3LYP-D3 (ref. 58) method was utilized. It is known that the calculated vertical excitation energies of different types of molecules depend on the employed functionals. Test calculations have been carried out for IQ4 with several usually employed functionals including BHandHLYP, CAM-B3LYP, PBE0, and MPW1K, where MPW1K shows the best performance with a discrepancy of 0.01 eV with respect to the experimental data (see Table S1 in ESIâ ). Thus, the optical properties of isolated and dimeric dyes are studied using the time-dependent DFT (TDDFT)-MPW1K/6-31+G(d) method in dichloromethane solvent (modeled by C-PCM59).

To explain the intra-type FÃ¶rster resonance energy transfer, the geometric optimization of the D1(D1â²)/D2(D2â²) subunits in their excited state (S1) was carried out in CH2Cl2 solution using the MPW1K/6-31+G(d) method. The maximum emission Î»ems from S1 to S0 was then calculated because the Î»ems is very important in the process of FRET and the emission spectra could not be obtained because the frequencies of the excited state are not available. All the above mentioned calculations were performed using the D.01 version of the Gaussian 09 program.60

To model the heterogeneous interface of the sensitizer and CDCA adsorbed on the anatase TiO2 (101) surface, a neutral, stoichiometric (TiO2)124 cluster was selected. For the aggregate pattern, the nomenclature is illustrated in Fig. 2, the first monomer located in the (0, 0) position is fixed and another one is labeled by (x, y) coordinates. For aggregate structures of IQ4/YA421/YA422, the interaction positions of (â1, 1), (1, 1), (â3, 1), (3, 1)/(1, 1), (0, 2), (2, 2)/(0, 2), (2, 2) were chosen due to the steric hindrance introduced by the extended donor group. After the optimization of the heterogeneous interface, a molecular dynamics simulation for a total time span of 10 ps was performed to detect the instantaneous variation of distances, such as H-bonds and Nâ¯N distances which were both located on the donor groups. The above mentioned static optimization and dynamic simulation were carried out using the DFTB+ program package61,62 which has shown adequate accuracy with low computational cost in this field.63â66

3.2 Influence of the extended donor on the adsorption mode

In general, a sensitizer with a co-planar backbone is inclined to aggregate on the TiO2 substrate and this aggregation contributes to a disordered alignment whereas this can be hindered via a co-adsorbent additive chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA).48 Thus, the sensitizers adsorbed on the TiO2 substrate have been simulated before and after the CDCA co-adsorption to explore the relationship between the bulky donor group and the capacity of anti-aggregation. According to experiments,74â76 the adsorption mode of sensitizers on a TiO2 anatase (101) surface is commonly found in three patterns: monodentate, bidentate chelating and bidentate bridging77â79 and the latter one has been shown to be more stable in sensitizers with 2-cyanoacrylic acid and cyanoacrylate as anchor groups. Our previous paper63 has reported that sensitizer C258 with cyanoacrylate as the anchor group can adsorb on a TiO2 anatase (101) surface in monodentate and bidentate bridging modes and the latter one is the most stable. Although the other bidentate bridging mode has been observed where the chemisorption by two TiâO bonds transfers to the TiâNC and TiâO bonds and is stabilized by hydrogen-bonding,80,81 we only use the common bidentate bridging mode in this paper. Notably, for the discussion of the aggregate pattern on the substrate, we define the first monomer located in the (0, 0) position as fragment F1 and the second monomer in another position as fragment F2 for simplification based on Fig. 2.82 Additionally, the oxygen atoms belonging to cyanoacrylic acid in the sensitizer and substrate are labeled as OS1/S2 and OTi, and the titanium atoms bonded to OS1/S2 are denoted as Ti1/2 (Fig. 5) to make a clear distinction in the following analysis.

4. Conclusions

In this study, inspired by the prominent conversion efficiency of the YA422-based solar cell, DFT/TDDFT combined with molecular dynamics simulations were performed to explore the relationship between the extended donor group and the higher efficiency compared with IQ4 and YA421-based cells. It is found that the intra-type FRET only occurs in YA422 where the energy transfers to the AâÏâA backbone via the luminescence of the D2 subunit around the band at 533 nm and the absorption strength fabs of this band is enhanced compared to that of IQ4 and YA421 with the smaller donor size. Meanwhile, the tendency towards aggregation in YA422 is lessened compared with IQ4 and YA421 due to the steric hindrance introduced by the extended donor group, as a result, the self-decay in the aggregate structure is weakened as evidenced by the fact that the mixed intra- and intermolecular transition in IQ4 transfers to the complete intramolecular transition in YA422. Additionally, the combined NewnsâAnderson approach and Marcus equation verify that a faster kinject is achieved by YA422 compared to IQ4 and YA421. Our research provides fundamental insights on the fact that the extension of the donor part by a suitably electron donating group can potentially enhance the conversion efficiency, and this has been applied to design a potential candidate DW1 that possesses the donor D3â² which not only strengthens the absorption but also achieves a larger electron injection rate.
