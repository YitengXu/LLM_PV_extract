Abstract

Precious metal based electrocatalysts are considered as the most efficient ones to drive kinetically sluggish oxygen evolution/reduction reactions (OER/ORR) for metalâ€“air batteries and fuel-cells. However, their monofunctionality in addition to their exorbitant cost has stimulated the quest for economically viable bifunctional electrocatalysts for use in next generation electrochemical energy devices. Here, we report Feâ€“Fe3C encapsulated in Feâ€“Nx enriched spheres of N-doped carbon nanotubes (FCMR, M = 3, 10, 25, 40 represents the ramping rate of temperature during synthesis) as a potentially enduring, cost effective, highly efficient bifunctional electrocatalyst for total oxygen electrochemistry (ORR and OER) and a comprehensive study to elucidate the role of various Fe moieties. In addition to the improved OER/ORR activities as evident from the better onset potential, lower Tafel slopes and high current densities over commercially available RuO2/Ptâ€“C electrocatalysts and several recently reported state-of-the-art bi-functional electrocatalysts, FC10R shows a current retention value of âˆ¼93 and âˆ¼98% after the accelerated cyclic stability test for the OER and ORR, respectively. The preferable 4eâˆ’ pathways and suppressed peroxide generation in the ORR by FC10R further ensure maximum electrochemical energy harvesting. Remarkably, the complete oxygen electrochemistry of FC10R in alkaline medium as evaluated from Î”E (=Ej(OER) = 10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) = 0.758 V) is significantly lower than that of commercially available/recently reported electrocatalysts and advocates the minimum cyclic loss. The overall study elucidates the synergistic effect of Feâ€“Nx coordination and Fe3C moieties on oxygen electrochemistry, and FC10R has shown its potential to serve as a non-precious metal based bifunctional electrocatalyst for next generation electrochemical energy conversion and storage devices. Finally, a prototype Alâ€“air battery arrangement using FC10R as an air-cathode for powering a green light emitting diode has been demonstrated.

Introduction

The electrochemical oxygen evolution/reduction reaction (OER/ORR) plays a decisive role in the performance evaluation of various electrochemical energy conversion and storage devices like fuel cells, metalâ€“air batteries, electrolyzers, etc.1â€“3 The OER and ORR reactions are sluggish in nature due to multiple electron transfer and are catalyst driven.1â€“3 Precious metal group (PMG) based catalysts are considered efficient for the ORR and have been studied widely.4â€“7 However, besides cost (which adds to âˆ¼49% of the device cost) and operational stability, PMG based catalysts are mainly known for their mono-functionality. Pt-based catalysts are promising for the ORR but show very poor activity/stability towards the OER.1,2 Similarly, Ru and Ir based oxides are well known for the OER, but show poor performance towards the ORR that restricts their use in metal air-batteries.1 Recent studies show that non-precious metal group based electrocatalysts (NPMGCs) can be promising alternatives for PMG based electrocatalysts as some of them warrant the bi-functional behaviour in addition to superior activity.1,8â€“22

NPMGCs enriched with Mâ€“Nx coordination (M = Fe, Co) offer excellent ORR as well as OER activities in alkaline medium.12,23â€“27 However, there exist divergent opinions regarding the direct or indirect involvement of nitrogen coordinated metals in oxygen electrochemistry.20 For rational engineering and designing of appropriate electrocatalysts, it is of paramount significance to identify the active centres responsible for electrochemical activity and stability.12 Further, it has also been suggested that the encapsulation of metalâ€“metal carbide nanoparticles in the graphitic layer activates the structure electrochemically towards the ORR by reducing the work function of the carbon layer, and in return, the carbon layer provides a protective coating and hence the stability.28,29 A recent study suggests that the presence of Feâ€“Fe3C moieties in electrocatalysts boosts the electrochemical activity.12,29â€“31 However, the stability and activity are inferior to those of benchmark state-of-the-art commercial catalysts.29,30 In order to evaluate the direct or indirect involvement of metal based active centres, poisoning tests have been suggested.20 Nevertheless, a comprehensive study, which can elucidate the impact of such poisoning on total oxygen electrochemistry and may shed some light on the importance of Mâ€“Nx/C coordination, is much needed as it may pave the way for designing NPMG based bifunctional electrocatalysts to serve as a catalyst for metalâ€“air batteries, in particular. Furthermore, it is essential to understand the role of each component associated in catalysis for the optimum utilization of electrocatalysts.

In this work, we synthesized Feâ€“Fe3C encapsulated N-doped carbon spheres and Feâ€“Fe3C encapsulated Feâ€“Nx enriched spheres of N-doped carbon nanotubes in an attempt to elucidate the importance of Feâ€“Fe3C and Feâ€“Nx based active centres for oxygen electrochemistry. Importantly, the study offers a facile approach to enhance the Feâ€“Nx coordination by simply optimizing the ramping rate of temperature during the synthesis. Four ramping rates, namely 3, 10, 25 and 40 Â°C minuteâˆ’1, has been taken in the present study, and the samples are named FC3R, FC10R, FC25R and FC40R, respectively. The electrochemical studies suggest the presence of Feâ€“Nx and Feâ€“Fe3C coordination centres in FC10R which offers remarkable improvement in the bi-functionality (ORR and OER) over FC3R, FC25R, FC40R (which mainly incorporates Feâ€“Fe3C nanoparticles in the N-doped carbon network), and various recently reported and commercially available state-of-the-art electrocatalysts. The poisoning study with NaSCN further reveals the direct involvement of coordinated metal (Feâ€“Nx) as electrochemically active centres for the ORR and OER. In addition to the electrochemical studies, we fabricated a simple prototype metalâ€“air battery using an aluminium wire (a common household object) as the anode material and FC10R coated carbon tape as the cathode electrocatalyst to power a light emitting diode for more than 150 h. Overall, we present a comprehensive analysis which is believed to be helpful in designing various Mâ€“Nx centre based bifunctional electrocatalysts for oxygen electrochemistry.

Poisoning study

Identification of the electrochemically active centres is helpful in designing future electrocatalysts. In an attempt to elucidate the involvement of nitrogen coordinated Fe (Feâ€“Nx) in oxygen electrochemistry, we carried out the poisoning test by repeating the electrochemical studies on FC10R in oxygen saturated 0.1 M NaOH + 50 mM NaSCN solution. The rationality behind the poisoning test is that the SCNâˆ’ ions interact with the Fe2+/3+ ions and mask the coordination sites which would reduce the activity.20 The substantial amount of decrease in the ORR current density (Fig. 7a and b) with a negative shift in the Eon-set and E1/2 (Fig. 7b), as evident from the CV and RRDE polarization curves of FC10R in 0.1 M NaOH and 0.1 M NaOH + 50 mM NaSCN (Fig. 7a and b), suggest the direct involvement of coordinated metal in the ORR process. The masking of Fe-based active centres has an adverse effect on the % HO2âˆ’ generation (calculated by eqn (6)) which has increased up to 25% (Fig. 7c). The decrease in the no. of electrons transferred has also been noted (Fig. 7d, calculated from eqn (5)). We also carried out the poisoning test on FC3R as the XPS study suggests mostly the presence of Câ€“Nx based moieties and carbon encapsulated Feâ€“Fe3C nanoparticles. Conclusions

In summary, Feâ€“Fe3C encapsulated N-doped carbon spheres and Feâ€“Fe3C encapsulated, Feâ€“Nx enriched spheres of N-doped carbon nanotubes (FCMR, M = 3, 10, 25, 40) have been synthesized as a noble metal-free bi-functional electrocatalyst for the oxygen evolution/reduction reactions (OER/ORR). The study suggests that though the nitrogen and Fe content in the present electrocatalyst is nearly the same, the one with enriched Feâ€“Nx coordination shows superior oxygen electrochemistry, and the Feâ€“Nx coordination mainly depends upon the ramping rate. As a consequence the surface defect density as evident from the ID/IG ratio in Raman spectra and the electrochemically calculated ESCA and roughness factor are much favourable in the Feâ€“Nx enriched nanostructure FC10R. In addition to the improved OER/ORR activities as evident from better onset potential, lower Tafel slopes and high current densities over commercially available RuO2/Ptâ€“C electrocatalysts and several recently reported state-of-the-art bi-functional electrocatalysts, FC10R shows a current retention value of âˆ¼93 and âˆ¼98% after 1500 cycles of the accelerated cyclic stability test for the OER and ORR, respectively. The preferable 4eâˆ’ ORR pathways and suppressed peroxide generation in the ORR on FC10R further ensure maximum electrochemical energy harvesting. Remarkably, the complete oxygen electrochemistry on FC10R in alkaline medium as evaluated from Î”E (=Ej(OER) = 10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) = 0.758 V) is significantly lower than that of commercially available electrocatalysts (RuO2/Ptâ€“C) and various metal-free/transition metal doped/co-doped carbon nanostructure based state-of-the-art bi-functional catalysts reported recently. Moreover, in an attempt to study the feasibility of FC10R as an air-cathode electrocatalyst, a prototype Alâ€“air battery system has been assembled to power a green light emitting diode. The OCV (âˆ¼2.59 V) even after 150 hours is nearly the same as its initial value (âˆ¼2.64 V). Importantly, the poisoning study on FC10R elucidates the direct involvement of nitrogen coordinated (Feâ€“Nx) metal based active centers in oxygen electrochemistry. Overall, the experimental outcome of the present study suggests that Feâ€“Nx/C enriched electrocatalysts have potential to serve as a promising bi-functional electro-catalyst for both the OER and ORR and therefore beneficial for next generation electrochemical energy conversion and storage devices.
