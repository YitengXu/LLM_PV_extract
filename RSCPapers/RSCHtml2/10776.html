Abstract

In this work, we demonstrate that the performance of c-Si/ZnO heterojunction ultrathin-film solar cells (SCs) is enhanced by an integrated structure of c-Si trapezoidal pyramids on the top of a c-Si active layer and Al pyramids in the active layer on the Al back electrode. The top c-Si trapezoidal pyramid (TTP) increases the absorption of short wavelengths by lengthening the propagation distance of incident light and coupling the incident light into photonic modes in the active layer. The bottom Al pyramid (BP) improves the overall optical absorption performance especially for the long wavelength band by forming the surface plasmon resonance (SPR) mode in the active layer. As a result, the average absorption in the entire wavelength range (300â1400 nm) reaches 93.16%. The optimized short-circuit current density (Jsc) and photoelectric conversion efficiency (PCE) of ultra-thin film c-Si/ZnO SCs are 41.94 mA cmâ2 and 18.97%, respectively. Moreover, the effect of different illumination angles on the optical absorption of the SCs was explored. The SCs have good absorption when the incident angles are in the range from 0 degrees to 60 degrees. Furthermore, the underlying mechanism for the enhancement of photon absorption in the SCs was discussed through careful analysis of the electric field intensity profile at different wavelengths. It was found that the electric field tends to concentrate around the bottom pyramids and top trapezoidal pyramids even for the long-wave band, which results in an excellent light-trapping performance.

1. Introduction

The rapid development of human society is increasingly becoming inseparable from the continuous requirement of electricity. Compared with traditional fossil energy, photovoltaic energy has attracted widespread attention due to its wide distribution, easy access and environmental sustainability.1â4 Due to extensive research, the efficiency of SCs is constantly being improved.5 At present, crystalline silicon (c-Si) solar cells account for a large proportion of the photovoltaic market. Although their cost has significantly reduced, the fabricating cost of c-Si is still high compared with fossil fuels. Besides, with the increasing consumption of silicon materials, silicon resources are quickly reducing.6â9 Researchers are thus increasingly committed to developing ultra-thin film SCs for the purpose of reducing the material cost.10,11 Ultra-thin film silicon SCs can greatly reduce the consumption of c-Si materials, thus significantly reducing the cost of SCs.

In recent years, the preparation of ultra-thin SCs has attracted widespread attention, and some progress has been made.12 Besides, there are more and more research studies on the performance enhancement of ultra-thin silicon SCs. Yan et al. found that when the recombination rate of silicon SCs is low, the thinner the silicon wafer is, and the greater the open circuit voltage is. Simultaneously, a shorter diffusion length is needed. The carriers can thus be easily collected using an electrode, which in turn can lower the requirement of excellent crystallinity for photovoltaic materials and make it easy to fabricate flexible batteries.13â15 But, the electrical property of ultra-thin c-Si SCs is poor compared with the traditional thick c-Si SCs. The main reason for the poor electrical performance of ultra-thin c-Si solar cells is its poor solar energy absorption in the near-infrared region.16 There are some challenges that need to be overcome, such as the limitation of light absorption, the high sensitivity of surface recombination as well as the complicated manufacturing processes brought about by reduced thickness, in order to use the ultra-thin SCs in practical applications.17,18 In order to increase the light capture ability of SCs, a variety of nanostructures applied to SCs have been reported in recent years, such as trapezoid-pyramidal,19 nanowires,20 nanocones,21 elliptical arrays,22 nanorings,23 and nanoholes.24 These nanostructures have excellent light-trapping and anti-reflection properties, which is a fantastic strategy to enhance the solar energy absorption for ultra-thin film SCs. However, because of the differences in materials and sizes, light-trapping of the nanostructures and the performance of anti-reflection are unstable.25 Besides, it is known that the SPR is an effective approach to enhance the optical absorption.26â29 The plasmonic structures can scatter and couple the incident light into SPR mode or active layer waveguide mode which is conducive to confining the light within the active layer, thereby improving the optical absorption.30â33 There have been many reports on ultra-thin c-Si SCs that utilize plasmons or nanostructures.34,35

Herein, we present c-Si/ZnO heterojunction ultrathin-film SCs based on the bottom pyramids and top trapezoidal pyramid double-layer nanostructures. The optical absorption of SCs with different structures beneath the illumination of incident light has been investigated, which shows that the SCs proposed by us can greatly improve the light capture ability. Moreover, in order to reveal the stability of optical absorption under different incident angles, the influence of different illumination angles of the SCs was explored. The short-circuit current density and photoelectric conversion efficiency of the optimized structure reach 41.94 mA cmâ2 and 18.97%, respectively. Furthermore, we attempt to reveal the possible mechanism for the strengthening of photon absorption in the SCs through careful analysis of the electric field intensity distribution. The results show that the electric field is prone to concentrate around the bottom pyramids and top trapezoidal pyramids even for the long-wave band. In addition, the absorption and electric characteristics for different c-Si active layer thicknesses and c-Si TTP top surface side length were compared and analyzed. Through the TTP structure, the propagation distance of incident light is lengthened and the incident light can be coupled into the photon mode in the active layer, which enhances the solar energy absorption of the short-wave band. The SPR is formed on the Al pyramids which can significantly enhance the optical absorption even for the long wavelength band. Overall, the structure greatly increases the solar energy absorption for the solar cells we presented, thereby reinforcing the electrical property of the solar cells. We demonstrate the potential of ultra-thin film c-Si/ZnO SCs combined with plasmon in one nanostructure, which provides a novel approach for improving the properties of ultra-thin film SCs. Compared with previous work, the conversion efficiency of the solar cells in this work is better compared to other solar cells of the same thickness.

2. Structure design and numerical model

The c-Si/ZnO ultrathin-film solar cell based on the bottom pyramid and top trapezoidal pyramid double-layer nanostructure is shown in Fig. 1. The bottom layer is an Al electrode. Periodic and neatly arranged Al pyramids are present at the interface in between the Al electrode and the crystalline silicon. The top surface of c-Si active layer is a c-Si trapezoidal pyramid which is arranged identically to the bottom Al pyramid. On top of the c-Si active layer is a zinc oxide (ZnO) electron transport layer, which is employed to reduce the surface carrier recombination rate brought by the reduction of the thickness of c-Si solar cells.36â38 The ZnO electron transport layer is coated on the c-Si silicon. The c-Si trapezoidal pyramid forms a conformal structure on it, and the upper glass has a light-gathering and antireflection effect.34Fig. 1(b) is a cross-sectional view in the xâz axis direction. As shown in the figure, a, b, c and d represent the thickness of the glass, ZnO, c-Si and Al electrode, respectively. The optimized parameters are a = 100 nm, b = 200 nm, c = 900 nm and d = 200 nm, respectively. The heights of the TTP and BP are both set as h = 200 nm, and w = 40 nm is the width of the top surface of the trapezoidal pyramid. P = 100 nm represents the period of the solar cell.

For the optical property, finite-difference time-domain (FDTD) simulation software is used to simulate the proposed solar cell. Light with a wavelength in the range of 300â1400 nm is incident directly above the Z axis. Periodic boundary conditions are used in both the X and Y axis directions, and perfectly matched layer (PML) boundary conditions are employed in the Z axis direction.40,41 PML absorption boundary conditions are designed to absorb incident light with minimal reflection. The PML boundary is basically implemented as an absorbing material, which also matches the impedance of the surrounding material to minimize reflection. In the meantime, the optimized geometric parameters of the solar cells are used. In addition, electric field diagrams at different wavelengths as well as the solar energy absorption under different incident angles are calculated. The optical generation rate calculated by FDTD will be used as the input of the Lumerical Charge Solver module. As a result, the short-circuit current density (Jsc), open circuit voltage (Voc), fill factor (FF) and power conversion efficiency (PCE) of the solar cell can be obtained.

3. Results and discussions

Because of the combination of the Al BP and the c-Si TTP, the optical and electrical performances of the ultra-thin c-Si/ZnO solar cell are improved. In the wavelength range from 300 nm to 1400 nm, the light absorption of these solar cells with comparative structures under the excitation of a plane wave (to simulate the sunlight) is shown in Fig. 3(a). In the full spectrum band (300 nm to 1400 nm), the average absorption of the âPlanarâ, âOnly c-Si TTPâ and âOnly Al BPâ structures are 65.31%, 74.51% and 79.05%, respectively. In contrast, the average absorption of the âc-Si TTP + Al BPâ structure is 93.16%. It is clearly seen that the absorption spectra can be divided into two absorption regions. In the short-wave band (300â500 nm), the âOnly c-Si TTPâ structure and âc-Si TTP + Al BPâ structure show almost identical absorption characteristics, and their average absorptions are 91.91% and 91.98%, respectively. For the âPlanarâ structure and âOnly Al BPâ structure, they also show a nearly identical absorption property in this region, and the average absorption is 80.92% and 81%, respectively. The short-wave band absorption is improved, which is probably ascribed to the extended propagation distance of incident light in silicon with a c-Si trapezoidal pyramid structure. For the long-wave band (500â1200 nm), the average absorption of the âOnly c-Si TTPâ structure and âPlanarâ structure are only 52.93% and 60.86%, respectively. However, with the embedding of the Al BP structure, the absorption of the âOnly Al BPâ structure and âc-Si TTP + Al BPâ structure is significantly improved. On the sub-wavelength regime, the incident light is localized around the surface of Al pyramids via electromagnetically coupled oscillation (the so-called Surface Plasmon Resonance, SPR), and thereby the long-wave band (>500 nm) absorption is enhanced by Al pyramids.47â49 The transmission is almost zero due to the existence of the Al layer. Therefore, we primarily discuss the reflection characteristics of solar cells with different structures. From Fig. 3(b), it can be seen that the reflection of incident light is greatly suppressed by the c-Si/ZnO heterojunction solar cell combined with microstructures of c-Si TTP and Al BP. Moreover, it can be seen that the solar absorption spectra of the âonly Al BPâ structure and âc-Si TTP + Al BPâ structure are more stable than other structures that have no Al pyramids, especially in the wavelength range of 300â1000 nm.

4 Conclusion

In conclusion, the solar energy absorption and electrical characteristics were improved by the structure of a solar cell with c-Si TTP and Al BP. The top c-Si trapezoidal pyramids enhance the absorption of the short-wave band (300 nm to 600 nm) by extending the propagation distance of incident light and coupling the reflected light into the active layer. The bottom Al pyramids primarily improve the absorption of a longer wavelength band by confining the electromagnetic energy around the Al pyramids by SPR. The average solar energy absorption of our proposed structure is above 92% under wavelengths ranging from 300 nm to 1400 nm. We have also explored the solar energy absorption characteristics under the AM 1.5 spectrum. The influence of different incident light angles on solar energy absorption was calculated, and the electric field intensity scanning diagram under different incident light angles was compared and analyzed. To further understand its electrical properties, we have calculated the electrical characteristics of different structures of the solar cells. The current density of the solar cell with c-Si TTP and Al BP structures was 41.94 mA cmâ2, which is consistent with the highest absorption under the AM 1.5 spectrum. Its energy conversion efficiency is as high as 18.97%. The underlying physical mechanism was also analyzed. Moreover, in order to explore the influence of different geometrical parameters on the electrical and optical properties of solar cells, the effects of different c-Si active layer thickness and c-Si TTP top side length on the light absorption and electrical properties of solar cells were compared and analyzed. Our work has made some progress in the development of ultra-thin crystalline silicon solar cells.
