Effects of different functional groups on the optical and charge transport properties of copolymers for polymer solar cells

Abstract

The ground state properties of polymers BSeTT, QTT, BDT-DTBTBPz (Pz), BDT-DTBTBQx (Qx) and their derivatives D1, D2, D3, including frontier molecular orbital, energy gaps, ionization potentials (IPs), electron affinities (EAs) and electron or hole reorganization energies, have been investigated with DFT-B3LYP/6-31G(d) method. Based on optimized structures, the absorption and fluorescence spectra of the seven oligomers are simulated with TD-DFT-Cam-B3LYP/6-31G(d) method. The external electric field is taken into account in view of the practical application. Moreover, developed visualized charge difference density is applied to present the charge density redistribution for the fullerene/polymer complexes. More deeply, the electronic coupling matrix, reorganization and Gibbs free energy are estimated to calculate the rates of the charge transfer and recombination for the fullerene/polymer complexes. Results show that the lengths of conjugated chains obviously affect the energy levels of HOMO and LUMO, and the ionization potentials and electron affinities. By evaluating the rates of the charge transfer and recombination for the fullerene/polymer complexes, reorganization energy and spectrum nature, it is found that the molecule BDT-DTBTBPz and designed molecule (D2) have the best optical and electronic properties among the investigated system, indicating that introducing appropriate functional group can effectively improve the photoelectric characteristics.

1. Introduction

In view of the continuous depletion of fossil fuels and the serious environmental pollution, a great deal of attention has been focused on the renewable resources. Solar energy is an abundant, renewable and clean energy source that is one of the most promising energy sources and has recently become virtually practical.14 Although commercially available solar cells are currently based on inorganic silicon semiconductors, their large-scale application has been limited due to the expensive, heavy, and inflexible shortcomings. Among all the renewable energy technologies, the polymer solar cells (PSCs) with a mixture of electron donor polymers and electron acceptor fullerene derivatives, are promising cost-effective solar cells because of their potentially low fabrication costs, environmentally friendly components, relatively high conversion efficiencies and flexibility in their manufacture and employment.57

In polymer solar cells, the photocurrent are generated after five process:8 (1) the active layer absorbs photons to form excitons; (2) excitons are transported to the donoracceptor (DA) interface; (3) excitons are separated at the donoracceptor interface and form free charges; (4) charges are transported under an electric field; (5) charges are collected by electrodes. There are several factors affecting the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of polymer solar cells: (a) unmatched spectra between the spectral response in polymers system and the sunlight radiation; (b) the lower carrier mobility; and (c) the lower collection efficiency of the carrier by electrode.914 The various approaches for the synthesis of highly efficient PSCs have been performed to enhance each process.1519 You and co-works20 designed and synthesized the two polymers, PCPDT-DFBT and PDTP-DFBT based on PCPDT-BT, in which PDTP-DFBT presented a narrow band gap (1.38 eV), high mobility and deep highest occupied molecular orbital. Moreover, a single-junction device based on PDTP-DFBT showed spectral response extends to 900 nm, with a power conversion efficiency of 7.9% and a certified power conversion efficiency of 10.6% under standard reporting conditions. Chen et al.21 had designed a triple-junction tandem solar cell to improve power conversion efficiency up to 11.5%. Liu et al.22 synthesized a novel two-dimensional (2D) conjugated copolymer 2D PTB7-DT by introducing the conjugated 5-alkylthiophene-2-yl side chain to substitute non-conjugated alkoxy side chain in 1D PTB7, finding that the single-junction PSCs based on 2D PTB7-DT exhibited a PCE of 10.12%. Moreover, some progress has been made in theoretical research.2331 Nora et al.25 investigated the electronic properties of fluorene-1,3,4-thiadiazole oligomers using DFT-B3LYP/6-31G(d) and demonstrated that the electronic properties were observed to be significantly enhanced as the number of monomeric units increased, which also lead to the decreasing gap energy from 3.51 eV in the oligomer with just one thiadiazole ring to 2.33 eV in the oligomer with 10 units. Ling and co-works27 studied the electronic structure of positive and negative radicals (polarons) of two functionalized DTTzTz materials using multi-frequency and multi-resonance electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) in combination with density functional theory (DFT), and this work gave in-depth information about the spread of the electron or hole in the DTTzTz molecules. Furthermore, Song and coworkers32 investigated the influence of the external electric field on the electronic coupling, the reorganization energy and free energy change of the electron transfer in bulk hetero-junction solar cells, which promoted a deeper understanding of the connection between the external electric field, chemical structure and optical and electronic properties of the donoracceptor system. In order to improve the power conversion efficiency of polymer solar cells, it is very important to select the appropriate photoactive layer molecules, and they are expected to possess the optical response in visible and near infrared region and obvious charge transfer ability in solar cells.

In this paper, we designed three novel molecules D1, D2, D3 based on the experiment reports.33,34 And the various properties, such as the ground state structures, the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO), the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO), energy gaps (HL, the difference between the HOMO and LUMO), ionization potential (IP), electron affinity (EA), electron and hole reorganization energy are calculated by the density functional theory (DFT). Moreover, the absorption and emission characteristics are investigated by the time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT). In addition, the external electric field is taken into account in consideration of the practical application. It is noteworthy that the aforesaid parameters were estimated through a linear extrapolation method.35,36 Visualized charge difference density analysis is used to present the charge density redistribution for fullerene/polymer complexes; the electronic coupling matrix, reorganization and Gibbs free energy are estimated to calculate the rates of charge transfer and recombination. Simulated data show that among the objective molecules, BDT-DTBTBPz molecule exhibits the best properties of the ground and excited states, and the designed molecule (D2) is the second most efficient configuration next to BDT-DTBTBPz. Our results contribute to understanding the structures, photophysical and photochemical properties of photosensitive molecules, and provide a reliable strategy for the design of efficient solar cells.

2. Computational details

The ground state geometries of BSeTT, QTT, BDT-DTBTBPz (Pz), BDT-DTBTBQx (Qx) and their derivatives have been fully optimized by using DFT37,38 method with B3LYP39 functional at the 6-31G(d) basis set. On the basis of optimized ground state structures, we obtained the bond length, dihedral angle, HOMO and LUMO energies, HOMOLUMO energy gaps, ionization potentials, and electron affinities. The ionization potential and electron affinity were calculated from the difference of neutral and cationic or anionic molecular energies. Furthermore, the absorption and emission spectra were calculated by using TD-DFT40,41 method with Cam-B3LYP42 functional at 6-31G(d) basis set on the basis of optimized ground state and excited state geometries, respectively. To calculate the charge transfer integral (electronic coupling matrix), the Generalized MullikenHush (GMH) model and the finite field method were performed on the donoracceptor hetero-junction.

Quantum chemical method has been used to study electro-optical and charge transport properties of organic molecules system.43,44 For clear understanding the charge density change, the three dimensional (3D) real-space representation was used to visualize charge transfer on the electronic transition. 3.7. Effect of external electric field

In the normal operation of solar cells, an electric field between two electrode plates will be produced since there is a current between the two electrode plates, which has an effect on the charge transfer characteristics of the active layer molecules. To get a rigorous research, the effect of external electric field on the photoelectric properties of the active layer molecules was investigated theoretically. The maximum absorption peaks and corresponding oscillator strengths for S1 excited state and the energy levels of the seven monomers under external electric field (F = 1 103, 2 103, 3 103 a.u.) were listed in Tables S2 and S3, respectively. Due to the limited computing power, the transition properties and energy levels of BSeTT, Qx (n = 2, 3) and D1 (n = 3) were not obtained. From Table S2, it can be seen that with the increasing electric field, the absorption peaks of QTT, Pz, D2 and D3 have a red-shift in vary degree. Comparing with the transition properties under no external electric field, the absorption peaks of the seven monomers under F = 1 103 a.u. have a red-shift about 2.15 nm, 2.61 nm, 33.82 nm, 19.72 nm, 4.63 nm, 1.31 nm and 3.36 nm for BSeTT, QTT, Pz, Qx, D1, D2 and D3, respectively, and the results imply that the electric field had a prominent effect on the photoelectric performance of Pz. It can be seen from Table S3 that the HOMO energy levels of the seven monomers are increased with the increasing external electric field. Moreover, except for D2, the LUMO energy levels of the other six monomers are decreased along with the increasing external electric field. The above results show that the external electric field has an effect on the optical and electrical properties of the active layer molecules.6366

4. Conclusion

In this paper, three molecules D1, D2, D3 were theoretically designed based on the BSeTT, QTT, BDT-DTBTBPz (Pz), BDT-DTBTBQx (Qx). Their frontier molecular orbitals, energy gaps, ionization potentials, electron affinities and the hole or electron reorganization energies have been calculated by using DFT-B3LYP/6-31G(d) method. Moreover, their absorption spectra and fluorescence properties were obtained by using TD-DFT-Cam-B3LYP/6-31G(d) method. Considering the practical application, the effect of external electric field on the optical and electronic properties of molecules was also taken into account. In addition, visualized charge difference density (CDD) analysis was applied to present the charge density redistribution for fullerene/polymer complexes. The electronic coupling matrix, reorganization and Gibbs free energy were estimated to calculate the rates of the charge transfer and recombination of fullerene/polymer complexes. The results show that: (a) with the increasing conjugated lengths, their HOMO energy levels are elevated, and LUMO energy levels are reduced, resulting in the narrow energy gaps; (b) the ionization potentials are gradually decreased, and electron affinities rose with the increasing conjugated units, and the hole or electron reorganization energy decreased; (c) absorption spectra make red-shifted. Charge transfer process are identified by CDD method, showing the charge moves from polymer to PC60BM with the larger rate of CT than CR. Comparison of seven molecules demonstrated that for original and designed molecules, BDT-DTBTBPz and D2 possess narrower band gaps, deeper HOMO energy levels, broader optical absorption spectra, smaller ionization potentials, larger electron affinities and lower reorganization energy. The results indicated the configurations of BDT-DTBTBPz and D2 have the best optical and electronic properties, and the present molecular modeling provided the reasonable theory basis for the design and synthesis of new photoactive layer in solar cells.
