Abstract

The mesoporous Al2O3â€“In2O3 composites with one-dimensional (1D) nanofibres (NFs) have been fabricated via a facile one-step synthesis of the electrospinning approach, followed by appropriate thermal treatment under ambient conditions. The composite nanomaterials display high dispersion of both In2O3 and Al2O3, forming a heterostructure and mesoporous tubular structure in a broad Al:In atomic ratio up to 1:4. It has been found that the Al2O3 composite tube-like nanostructures not only increase efficient sites for gas adsorption, but also possess higher donor densities. Moreover, the mesoporous structure also provides effective and fast channels for fast capturing and migration of electrons. The results show that the mesoporous Al2O3â€“In2O3 nanotubes exhibit excellent sensing properties to NOx down to a detection limit of 291 ppb at room temperature (RT), while containing 20 at% Al2O3 (labeled as meso-20AI NTs) with an average diameter of nanoparticles of about 10 nm. With respect to the 97 ppm NOx, the maximum response of 100 was determined, 7.3 times as high as the pure In2O3 NTs. Owing to its unique composite structure, 1D meso-20AI NTs may be promising for application in gas sensors.

Introduction

Gas sensors are very important in many fields, such as industrial emission, household security, vehicle emission control, environmental monitoring and public health. Among them, NO and NO2 (NOx) sensors have attracted great attention, since NOx are the most dangerous air pollutants affecting human health and the environment. Moreover, the exhaled concentration of NO has been identified as a biomarker for airway inflammation such as asthma and bronchiectasis. Generally, the exhaled NO level of the patients with asthma1 is about 34.7â€“51.1 ppb. Therefore, it is necessary to develop rugged and reliable NO gas sensors to detect very low gas concentrations. It is also highly demanded by public health and security applications.

Most NOx gas sensors are semiconductor oxides, such as SnO2, ZnO, In2O3,2etc. They usually require a certain working-temperature or doped with noble metals such as Pd.3 Xu et al. reported that high aspect ratio In2O3 nanowires are very sensitive to NO2 at 250 Â°C.2 Chen et al. reported that the SnO2 nanocrystalâ€“MWCNT gas sensor displayed a sensitivity of 1 for 200 ppm NO2 at RT.4 Marzbanrad and co-workers studied the sensors based on In2O3-nanoparticles for 500 ppb NO2 at 150 Â°C, 200 Â°C and 250 Â°C.5 Rao et al. studied the NO2 and N2O sensors of In2O3 nanowires at 150 Â°C and came to the limit of detection at 0.1 ppm.6 Nomani et al. reported a detection limit of 700 ppb for pure In2O3 thin films.7 Although the introduction of noble metals can effectively lower the operating temperature and increase sensitivity, the high price and the low stability still limit the appeal of NOx sensors. So, it is of importance to investigate low-cost and ambient operation NOx gas sensors with low gas concentrations and fast response. Operation of gas sensors at RT would be more suitable for future applications.

As is well known, semiconducting indium oxide has widely been studied from fundamental and technological levels and it has been widely applied in electronics/displays, catalysts, solar cells8 and gas sensors for the detection of HCHO,9 C2H5OH,10 NH3,11 CO (ref.12) and H2S.13 Lee et al. studied the sensing response of pure and additive (Sb, Cu, Nb, Pd, and Ni)-loaded In2O3 hollow spheres to NH3, H2S, H2, CO and CH4 at 371 Â°C and 440 Â°C.14

However, it is difficult for a single oxide based semiconductor gas sensor to satisfy all practical requests: high sensitivity, fast response, stability, and low energy consumption. To overcome these drawbacks, sensors based on two or more components have been explored for well-designed nanostructures, optimized components and well-fabricated heterojunction interactions between these components.15,16 The study by Ho et al. on NOx sensing with In2O3â€“ZnO nanostructures indicated that good sensitivity could be achieved only at elevated temperature (âˆ¼150 Â°C).1 Cui et al. detected NO2 down to 300 ppb in air using In2O3â€“SnO2 nanohybrids at RT,17 but the recovery process of the sensor was quite slow (âˆ¼600 s).

As mentioned above, the preparation of doped In2O3 shows great promise as a new class of materials for gas-sensors. However, they only exhibit higher sensitivity at desired working temperature. So far, reports on reliable, reproducible, and fast-responding/recovery NOx sensors with high sensitivity at RT remain rare. In order to solve the above problems, the identification of the most potential additive candidate is becoming a key problem to be solved as soon as possible.

To the best of our knowledge, easily available Al2O3 usually serves as a typical NOx storage and reduction catalyst support. It has been found to play an important role in a NOx storage and adsorption site.18 Previous studies showed that Al2O3 decorated TiO2 nanotubes templated by anodic aluminium oxide is a NOx gas sensor at RT.19 However, the synthesis includes many complicated steps, which restricts the use of Al2O3 in gas sensors. The Al-doped ZnO nanostructure has been proposed as a sensing material for CO detection at 150 Â°C,20 but the Al-doped sensors usually have high-temperature operation. Therefore, it is still a challenge to develop the Al2O3-doped sensors using a facile and low-cost synthetic method.

In this work, we targeted inexpensive Al2O3 as an additive of appropriate electrical conductivity to improve gas sensing of materials, and suggested a one-step method to fabricate 1D mesoporous Al2O3â€“In2O3 NFs. We have considered their different atomic ratios of directly annealing electrospuning composite fibers obtained from a polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP) solution. Heterostructures were constructed by controlling In2O3 and Al2O3 crystalline particles. This inhibits the grain growth and enhances NOx gas sensing performance. Meanwhile, the amount of Al2O3 incorporated into In2O3 NFs was changed. The effect of mesoporous structure on the gas sensing performance was investigated. The as-fabricated meso-20AI NTs with high surface-to-volume ratios exhibit high response, fast response and low detecting limit of NOx down to ppb levels at RT. Therefore, the Al2O3â€“In2O3 composites sensoring NOx display great potential for practical applications.

Experimental

Sample preparation

The Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs were synthesized by electrospinning PVP (MW = 1300000, Aldrich) solution (9 wt%). And then, an annealing process was performed. In a typical process, PVP was dissolved in N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) and ethanol. After that, 0.4 g of indium nitrate hydrate was added to all samples to obtain a transparent precursor solution. Different amounts of aluminium nitrate were added in the precursor solution to let the atomic ratios of In and Al reach 100:0, 100:15, 100:20 and 100:25, respectively. The mixed solution was stirred vigorously at 60 Â°C for at least 6 h and transferred into a 10 ml syringe with a capillary tip (0.8 mm in diameter) for spinning. A high voltage of 15 kV was applied at the spinneret by a direct-current power supply (BGG, Beijing Electro-mechanical Research Institute Supervoltage Technique Co., China). The solution was pushed out of the spinneret by a syringe pump (LSP02-1B, Baoding Lange Constant Flux Pump Co., China) at a rate of 0.25 ml hâˆ’1, and the NFs were collected using the aluminium foil at RT. After spinning, the Al2O3â€“In2O3 NFs with different atomic ratios were treated at 550 Â°C for 4 h in air with a heating rate of 1 Â°C minâˆ’1. The Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs with the atomic ratios of In and Al about 100:0, 100:15, 100:20 and 100:25 were labeled as pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs, meso-20AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs, respectively.

Material characterizations

The crystal phase of samples was characterized by X-ray powder diffraction (XRD, D/max-IIIB-40 kV, Japan, Cu KÎ± radiation, Î» = 1.5406 Ã…). The morphologies and structures of the Al2O3â€“In2O3 NFs were observed by scanning electron microscopy (SEM, HITACHIS-4800) and transmission electron microscopy (TEM, JEOL-2100). The Brunauerâ€“Emmettâ€“Teller (BET) surface area of the products was measured by N2 adsorptionâ€“desorption (Quantachrome Instruments, USA). The sample was dried for 10 h at 150 Â°C under vacuum before the measurement. X-Ray photoelectron spectra (XPS) were recorded with a AXIS Ultra DLD (Shimadzu Corporation) system equipped to analyse the adsorbed oxygen species of Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs.

Gas sensing tests

An alumina substrate (99.6%, 7 Ã— 5 Ã— 0.38 mm) with two interdigitated Au electrodes on its top surface was used. Each Au electrode contained 100 fingers which interleaved each other, and the distance between two fingers was 20 Î¼m. The sample materials were spin-coated onto the interdigital electrode to form a sensitive film and then dried at 70 Â°C for 5 h to obtain a gas sensor. The sensor was installed into a test chamber with an inlet and an outlet. The electrical resistance measurements of the sensor were carried out at RT and a relative humidity (RH) around 42%. The NOx gas concentration was controlled by injecting a volume of the gas and the chamber was purged with air to recover the sensor resistance. The gas response is defined as the ratio (Rg âˆ’ Ra)/Ra, where Ra and Rg are the resistances measured in air and the tested gas atmosphere.

Discussion of sensing mechanism

The sensing mechanism of the Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs to NOx was proposed based on its structure and composition. Considering the porosity of the 1D Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs and Al2O3 modification behavior, the discussion on the mechanism consists of: (i) the relationship among the pore structure, diffusion and the sorptionâ€“desorption of gas onto the surface of the Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs; (ii) the role of the Al2O3 additive on the sensor characteristics of the meso-20AI NTs.

It is well known that the working principle of gas sensing materials is built on the measurement of gas adsorption and the changes of the conductivity caused by the surface reaction process. Therefore, the increased surface area, chemisorbed oxygen and resistance can improve the response performance. Amongst the Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs, the meso-20AI NT gas sensor shows the high response of gas sensing property at RT. In order to explain the phenomenon, the pore structure, chemisorbed oxygen species and resistance of samples were studied, which is shown in Fig. 3f, S4,â€ 7â€“9.

The N2 sorptionâ€“desorption isotherms of the four samples are type IV isotherms. The four samples include pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs, meso-20AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs. Their BET specific surface areas are about 21.1, 37.4, 53.3, and 44.1 m2 gâˆ’1 with total pore volumes of 0.0966, 0.132, 0.151 and 0.122 cm3 gâˆ’1, respectively. According to the pore size distribution curve, all the four samples possess a lot of mesopores in the range of 2â€“50 nm. Pure In2O3 NTs have about 5.6 nm dominated mesopores; mesopores of meso-15AI NTs, meso-20AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs are about 3.8, 7.6 and 6.5 nm, respectively. Comparatively the meso-20AI NTs possess the largest surface area and total pore volumes. These are greatly advantageous for gas adsorptionâ€“desorption and gas molecular diffusion. And these structures provide numerous active sites for surface contact reactions.30

In addition, the meso-20AI NTs feature the unique mesoporous 1D hollow composite structure, allowing for adsorption of NOx gas not only on the outer/inner of the tubular surfaces but also throughout the mesoporous wall. The sensitivity of nanostructured metal-oxide gas sensors scales with the surface to volume ratio.31 Therefore, the fact that this ratio is twice as large for hollow fibers as for NRs of the same diameter can enable the NOx molecule adequately to utilize every In2O3 or Al2O3 nanocrystals. As a result, the meso-20AI NT sensor exhibits not only high response performance, but also fast response at RT to the NOx concentrations ranging from 0.291 to 97 ppm for detection.

Fig. 7 shows the typical response curves of the four sensors to NOx (97 ppm) at RT in air for one circle. The response of the pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs, meso-20AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs to 97 ppm NOx are 13.7, 18.3, 100, 29.1 and 247, 32, 28, 56 s, respectively. The response of meso-20AI NTs is 7.3, 5.5 and 3.4 times as high as those of pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs, respectively. The meso-20AI NT sensor has a better sensing performance than other sensors. Why is the gas sensing performance of the meso-20AI NT sensor so much better than those of the pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs or meso-25AI NRs? And what role does the Al2O3 additive play?

In general, the role and activity of catalytic additives on solid-state gas sensors are determined by the additive chemical state, aggregation form and interaction with the semiconductor oxide.32 Here, the Al2O3 additive can modify the microstructure of the base material, control the grain growth, introduce donor levels and change resistance. All these may also have an important effect on the NOx sensor characteristics.

As shown in Fig. 2, 3 and S1,â€  the size of the In2O3 particles on the meso-20AI NTs is much smaller than that of the pure In2O3 NTs (30â€“50 nm). This means that a large fraction of the atoms is present on the surface of the composite, leading to high surface-to-volume ratios and larger active surfaces.23 Consequently, the property of gas sensing will be enhanced. Also Fig. 3bâ€“d show that the overlapped parts of In2O3 grains contain a lot of defects. According to the UV-vis diffuse absorption spectra (Fig. S5â€ ), the estimated band gap of pure In2O3 NTs and the meso-20AI NTs by the Kubelkaâ€“Munk function is 2.8 and 2.6 eV, respectively. The band gap of the meso-20AI NTs is slightly smaller than that of pure In2O3 NTs, which can be attributed to surface defects. And the defects might be the vacancies of oxygen/defects. As a result, the surface-related defects are the best condition to the absorption of O2âˆ’ and Oâˆ’ for gas detection.33

The XPS results of Fig. 8 confirm the above speculation. The O 1s peak was fitted by three Gaussian simulation peaks. The peak centered at 529.2 Â± 0.1 eV (marked as Oc) is attributed to the O2âˆ’ ions in In2O3 and Al2O3. The Ob peak at 530 Â± 0.1 eV corresponds to the oxygen deficiency regions, which are the so-called oxygen vacancies. The peak at 531.4 Â± 0.1 eV, labeled as Oa, is usually assigned to the near-surface oxygen, the chemisorbed oxygen of O2âˆ’, Oâˆ’ and OHâˆ’etc.34

It can be seen that, all the chemisorbed oxygen peaks of the meso-15AI NT, meso-20AI NT and meso-25AI NR samples are more intense than the ones of the pure In2O3 NTs. In addition, the calculated oxygen vacancy/defect (Ob peaks area) of the four samples (the pure In2O3 NTs, meso-15AI NTs, meso-20AI NTs and meso-25AI NRs) are about 23.3, 23.4, 27.9 and 27.0%, respectively. In brief, the peak area of the oxygen vacancy/defect of the meso-20AI NTs is the largest, since the oxygen vacancies/defects are taken as shallow donors35 (see Scheme 1c and d). That means the more oxygen vacancies there are, the more donors exist in the meso-20AI NTs. This implies that adding Al2O3 could increase donors and chemisorbed oxygen. Chemisorbed oxygen is suggested to act as an electron donor, and can be defined as an n-type semiconductor.36 Hence, both oxygen vacancy and chemisorbed oxygen are helpful for good catalytic activity in gas sensors and improve the performance of NOx sensors.

Accordingly, we could conclude that the resistance of materials in the air plays a key role in their responses. Larger resistance in air induces higher response. Conclusion

The mesoporous Al2O3â€“In2O3 composite NFs were fabricated via a one-step synthesis of the electrospinning process. The morphology varies from NTs to NRs with increasing the added Al2O3 amount. Among them, the mesoporous Al2O3â€“In2O3 NTs containing 20 at% Al2O3 (meso-20AI NTs) exhibit excellent sensing properties to NOx detection at RT: high response of 100 to 97 ppm NOx, the detection limit of 291 ppb, and the high stability to 0.97â€“9.7 ppm NOx that remains constant in the response and response time for 35 days.

The enhanced gas sensing could be attributed to the synergistic effect between 1D mesoporous tubular structure and Al2O3 modification role: (1) the mesopores and unique 1D hollow structure with high surface-to-volume ratio can act as channels for gas adsorptionâ€“desorption and diffusion. And thus it provides more approachable active sites for the reaction of NOx with surface-adsorbed oxygen ions. Eventually, the favorable response to NOx at RT was effected. (2) The high response of the meso-20AI NTs may be attributed to the modification role of Al2O3. Namely, the Al2O3 additive can increase the oxygen vacancy/defect and donor densities, control the grain growth and resistivity and provide more activity sites (Oâˆ’, O2âˆ’). This results in fast capturing and migration of electrons from the conduction band or donor level.

In summary, the excellent gas sensing properties of the mesoporous tube-like structure of the meso-20AI NTs can be a reference for the preparation of other semiconductors for sensor applications, especially in the lower concentration range, less than 97 ppm (âˆ¼291 ppb) at RT.
