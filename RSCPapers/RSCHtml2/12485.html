Abstract

The influence of solubilizing substituents on the photovoltaic performance and thin-film blend morphology of new benzo[1,2-b:6,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (bBDT) based small molecule donor semiconductors is investigated. Solar cells based on bBDT(TDPP)2-PC71BM with two different types of side chains exhibit high power conversion efficiencies, up to 5.53%.

Small molecule semiconductors have attracted significant interest as electron donors for organic photovoltaic (OPV) cells, driven by several advantages vs. their polymeric counterparts, such as monodispersity, easier and more efficient purification, amenability to more characterization methods, and negligible batch-to-batch variations.1,2 Furthermore, small molecule-based solar cells now exhibit power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) as high as 7.8%.3â€“5 In this context, Ï€-acceptorâ€“donorâ€“acceptor (Aâ€“Dâ€“A) molecular donors using diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP) as the A unit and various thienoacenes such as benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT),6,7 naphtho[2,3-b:6,7-bâ€²]dithiophene (NDT),8 and naphtho[1,2-b:5,6-bâ€²]dithiophene (zNDT)9 as the D unit, have repeatedly proven successful (Fig. 1). However, to the best of our knowledge the benzo[1,2-b:6,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (bBDT) framework has never been used in molecular OPV donors, despite investigations in Dâ€“A OPV co-polymers10â€“13 and organic field effect transistors (OFETs).14,15 Nevertheless, DFT calculations on bBDT-centered molecules bearing two thiophene-capped DPP (TDPP) acceptor units reveal appealing frontier molecular orbital (FMO) energies and planar geometries (Fig. S1, ESIâ€ ), rendering the bBDT(TDPP)2 (1) core a promising OPV donor candidate. Considering the importance of solubilizing side chains for the self-assembly and performance of OPV donors,16 we investigated three bBDT(TDPP)2 derivatives, with systematically varied substitution pattern on the bBDT core and on the TDPP units. We report here preliminary findings on the synthesis, charge transport, and OPV properties of these new molecules and their corresponding blends with fullerenes.

The solid state morphology of 1c exhibits large PC71BM clusters even after 1% v/v DIO addition (Fig. 3C). However, close inspection of the images reveals areas with nanoscopically intermixed Dâ€“A domains, which reasonably explains the OPV performance enhancement (vide infra). Thus, 3,7-dimethyloctyl-substitution on molecular donors 1b and 1c prevents strong aggregation, large-scale phase separation, and the excessive crystallization observed for 1a as well as ensures intercalation of the fullerene acceptor when processed with DIO.20 The in-plane and out-of-plane line cuts of the GIWAXS spectra of films processed with DIO (Fig. S9 and Table S6, ESIâ€ ) show different lamellar spacing for the different substituents. Thus, 1a and 1c exhibit a singular lamellar peak with associated distances of 17.4 Ã… and 20.1 Ã…, respectively, consistent with the expected chain length dimensions. However, 1b shows two distinct lamellar peaks (17.2 Ã… and 20.4 Ã…), the first corresponding to the 2-ethylhexyl chains of 1a and the second associated with the 3,7-dimethyloctyl chain of 1c, indicating formation of two different phases and supposedly less ordered interactions between different types of side chains which may explain the reduced phase separation in the blend. Thus, the side chain mixing in 1b provides the optimum balance between solid-state ordering and donorâ€“acceptor-miscibility in this donor series.

OPVs with inverted ITO/ZnO/1:PC71BM/MoOx/Ag architectures were fabricated and optimized by varying the active layer composition, spin speed and solvent additives for the active layer deposition (Fig. 2C and D). The comparable electronic structures and film morphologies of molecular donors 1 and their BHJ blends deposited without DIO (vide supra; Fig. 2 and 3), result in similar illuminated current density vs. voltage (Jâ€“V) characteristics (Fig. S10 and Table S7, entries 1, 4, 9, ESIâ€ ). Despite the greatly enhanced crystallinity of the 1a domains in the blends, which should result in large intra-grain charge carrier mobilities and favorable charge extraction, the performance of the 1a-based blends remains modest with JSC âˆ¼ 4.6 mA cmâˆ’2, FF âˆ¼ 39% and a maximum PCE of 1.3%. Most importantly, due to the considerably increased phase interpenetration in the BHJ blend, OPV devices fabricated from 1b:PC71BM with DIO exhibit both dramatically increased JSC = 11.4 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF = 63.2% enhancing the overall PCEs to â‰¥5.5%. These values are among the highest reported for small molecule OPVs and significantly exceed those reported for bBDT-based polymers (PCEs = 1.1â€“5.2%).10â€“13

In conclusion, we report fine-tuning the self-organization properties of bBDT(TDPP)2 donor BHJ blends by varying the molecular core structure, the solubilizing side chains characteristics, and the blend processing additive yielding OPVs with PCEs as high as 5.53%. These results can be understood in terms of the particular blend morphologies, charge transport characteristics, and microstructures, again emphasizing the profound significance of side chain architecture in designing new OPV materials.
