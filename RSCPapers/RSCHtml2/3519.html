Abstract

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) are highly useful in various research fields such as materials science, dye chemistry, and solar cells. The relevance and properties of PAHs are manifold, and these species also possess interesting and tunable optical and electrochemical properties. Over the past few decades, numerous hetero-atom-containing planar and non-planar PAHs have been established through the Dielsâ€“Alder approach. Herein, we briefly summarize the versatility of Dielsâ€“Alder reactions of cyclopentadienones. This review is directed towards chemists interested in Dielsâ€“Alder reactions and concise routes to diverse hetero-atom bearing PAHs using cyclones as building blocks.

1. Introduction

Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) are highly conjugated sp2 carbons connected throughout a system.1 PAHs have been used in different fields such as organic electronics, dye chemistry, nanomaterials, and solar cells.2â€“5 Due to their extensive applications, chemists have shown interest in constructing these scaffolds and to diversify their synthetic scope. Heteroatom bearing-PAHs normally possess more prominent electronic properties than their respective carbocyclic counterparts due to the participation of lone pair electrons. There are several synthetic methods available for the synthesis of heterocyclic frameworks. Among them, the Dielsâ€“Alder (DA) protocol has been proved to be one of the most effective methods for assembling heterocyclic compounds.6a,b The DA reaction is also among the most fundamental and essential tools for the construction of Câ€“C bonds (in PAHs/hetero-PAHs) in an atom-economical manner. Notably, the DA methodology is potentially suitable to prepare highly branched phenylenes, as well as various annulated acenes/heteroacenes in a greater yield in comparison to other existing approaches.6câ€“g

Contingent on the electronic nature of the substrate, the DA reaction has been classified into two types, namely, normal DA and inverse electron demand Dielsâ€“Alder (IEDDA or DAINV) reactions. The DA reaction has a diene part that is electron-rich, and a dienophile part is electron-poor, but unlike the DA reaction, the IEDDA is a cycloaddition between an electron-rich dienophile and an electron-poor diene. Over the decades, the synthesis of the extensive variability of highly conjugated carbo-, and heterocyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (a PAH in which one or more of the skeletal carbon atoms have been replaced by a heteroatom), dendrimers, and polymers through DA reactions has been reported.7 Besides, in many natural product syntheses, the DA reactions have been effectively utilized as an elegant strategy for preparing crucial intermediates.8 Cyclopentadienones (CPDs) belong to the group of IEDDA dienes and have been frequently employed as a precursor to construct structurally complex PAHs.9

CPDs (versatile dienone) have been widely accessed by DA/nucleophilic-addition reactions for the production of carbo and also heterocycles. Initially, unsubstituted cyclopentadienone was utilized in the synthesis of carbonyl enclosed aromatic compounds as well as carbonyl bridged bicyclic compounds by the self-dimerization reaction.10 Cyclopentadienone is a unique substrate and has been consistently utilized as a diene in the IEDDA reaction for preparing annulated PAHs,11 and in some instances, tetraarylcyclopentadienone was used as an imperative precursor for making buckybowls.12 Furthermore, it has been used as a key step for the construction of some of the novel carbon allotropes such as nanographene, graphenes, graphene nanoribbons (GNR), and graphene-based nanomaterials.13 MÃ¼llen and co-workers have synthesized miscellaneous higher generation dendrimers, polymers and graphenes exploiting cyclopentadienones using the IEDDA protocol. Besides, MÃ¼llen et al. also published a number of review articles that focused explicitly on larger carbocyclic frameworks such as phenylene dendrimers, polymers, and nanographene.14 It is obvious that these existing review articles on IEDDA of CPDs are not focused on the synthesis of small carbocyclic and heterocyclic compounds. Herein, we covered the synthesis of heterocyclic frameworks achieved during the last 50 years through the IEDDA of CPDs.

Moreover, in recent years, non-planar PAHs have been extensively synthesized rather than their planar cousins owing to their enhanced solubility, reduced energy band gap, and higher photo-physical and electrochemical properties. Indeed, non-planar PAHs displayed greater material applications than planar PAHs because of their inherent nature.15 Besides, hetero-atom doped non-planar (heterocyclic) PAHs have shown enormous material applications compared to their respective parent non-planar carbocyclics.16 As a result of all the curious features, currently, the synthesis of innovative non-planar hetero-atom bearing PAHs is a significant topic in materials as well as synthetic organic chemistry. In that aspect, the present review outlines different strategies involved in preparing heterocyclic frameworks exploiting the Dielsâ€“Alder reactions of CPDs. Besides, this review also describes the incorporation of heteroatoms in PAHs at completely different positions (via the IEDDA reaction) compared to the classical heterocyclic system. Moreover, this review article also elucidates the diversity of cyclopentadienone frameworks towards the synthesis of heterocycles through a multi-directional approach (Fig. 1).

7. Conclusions

In this review article, we have outlined the synthesis of diverse hetero-atom bearing PAHs from simple heteroaromatic to larger graphene analogues, organometallic complexes, dendrimers, and polymers using cyclones. The adaptability of cyclopentadienones in the [4 + 2] cycloaddition reactions for making architecturally complicated heterocycles has been comprehensibly explained. The greater part of the synthesis involves hexagonal based planar hetero-frameworks; however, only narrow examples have been used for non-planar heterocycles. In contrast, scarce non-planar-like pentagonal (buckybowl), and heptagonal/octagonal (saddle) shaped carbocyclic compounds have been achieved using this strategy due to their good material applications. With the interesting material applications, there has been more attention devoted to designing novel non-planar (negative curvature) carbo/hetero-PAHs. Indeed, the reports are potentially valuable for further building up the negative curved carbo-PAHs materials, particularly, hetero doped highly twisted PAH skeletons.
