Abstract

Molybdenum selenide (MoSe2) nanosheets are prepared by a simple and facile sonochemical route. To optimize the synthesis process, the sonication is tested at three different time durations (15, 30 and 45 min) with a constant power of 500 W. In order to improve the electrochemical performance of the exfoliated MoSe2 nanosheets, we report the hybridization of 2D-MoSe2 with 2D-graphene by a simple solvothermal method. The exfoliated MoSe2 nanosheets are perpendicularly oriented on the surface of the graphene nanosheets. These MoSe2 nanosheet edges have a large number of electrochemically active sites, and the graphene sheets provide effective mass transportation of ions at the electrodeâ€“electrolyte interface. Cyclic voltammetry reveals the pseudocapacitive behaviour of a MoSe2/graphene nanohybrid based electrode. From galvanostatic chargeâ€“discharge studies, the specific capacitance is found to be 945 F gâˆ’1 at a current density of 1 A gâˆ’1. An asymmetric supercapacitor (ASC) device is fabricated, which delivers a specific capacitance of 75 F gâˆ’1 (@1 A gâˆ’1) with an energy density of 26.6 W h kgâˆ’1 and a power density of 0.8 kW kgâˆ’1, and it retains 88% of its capacitance even after 3000 cycles.

1. Introduction

Supercapacitors are the most promising sustainable electrical energy storage devices, offering long cycle life, high power density and fast charging/discharging times.1â€“3 Two conventional electrode materials have so far been used in supercapacitors but they have drawbacks: (1) carbon-based electrode materials, such as graphene or activated carbon from biological sources, suffer from low specific capacitance when used as non-faradic electrical double layer capacitors (EDLCs),4â€“6 and (2) metal oxide and conducting polymer based electrode materials such as MnO2, Co3O4, NiO, NiCo2O4, polyaniline, polythiophene etc., have high cost and are unstable over long cycles when used as faradic/redox type pseudocapacitors.7â€“13 Consequently, a newer electrode material with superior physicochemical properties is a stimulating and high priority task for researchers.

Inorganic graphene analogues, i.e. layered two dimensional (2D) transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDCs), have attracted great interest because of their excellent physical and chemical properties along with their unique 2D sheet-like structures.14â€“17 Layered TMDCs denoted as Aâ€“Mâ€“A sandwich structures (where A is a chalcogen [S, Se or Te] and M is any transition metal ion [group IV, V and VI]), such as MoS2, WS2, MoSe2, MoTe2etc., exhibit a wide range of applications in many fields such as hydrogen evolution, electrochemical energy storage, catalysis, bio-imaging, electronic devices and so on.18â€“22 Recently, researchers have focussed more on developing selenide rather than sulphide based electrode materials due to its lower electronegativity; this provides greater numbers of electroactive sites for the redox reaction. These properties indicate the potential for more research on selenide based electrode materials.

Among various layered TMDCs, molybdenum diselenide (MoSe2) is chosen because of its strong chemical bonds between Se and Mo within layers and its multi-layered structure held together with weak van der Waals forces, along with its superior properties like large interplanar distance, small band gap, low structure resistance etc. The properties of MoSe2 are mainly dependent on the layer thickness of MoSe2. Therefore, monolayers or small numbers of layers of MoSe2 have been used in a wide range of applications such as sensors, catalysis, dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSC), hydrogen evolution, electronics, Li-ion and Na-ion batteries and so on.23â€“30 To develop MoSe2 nanosheets (NS), different methods have been reported such as chemical vapour deposition (CVD), molecular beam epitaxy and micromechanical exfoliation,31â€“34 but the yield is very low. In the present work, we use sonochemical synthesis for the preparation of layered MoSe2 NS at room temperature with short reaction times to produce high quality and high yield NS. The advantage of sonochemical synthesis is the generation of acoustic cavitation in a solvent medium using ultrasound irradiation.35 This external driving force is used to exfoliate the precursor. Lei et al. reported the synthesis of ultra-thin MoSe2 NS through a simple modified liquid exfoliation process and used these for the hydrogen evolution reaction.36 However, only very few attempts have been made to obtain a few layers of MoSe2 NS. Moreover, the MoSe2 suffers from reduced electrochemical activity due to its limited electrical conductivity and slow rate of charge transfer which lead to significant loss of specific capacitance and continuous capacitance fading during prolonged cycling. To overcome these problems, MoSe2 hybrids with graphene, carbon nanotubes (CNT) or conductive carbon have been constructed, to improve the electrochemical performance and enhance the specific capacitance, rate capability and cyclic stability of the material. Balasingam et al. reported an MoSe2/reduced graphene oxide based electrode using H2SO4 as an electrolyte for supercapacitor application and achieved the specific capacitance of 211 F gâˆ’1.37 Ma et al. investigated the electrochemical performance of a MoSe2/C hybrid using 6 M KOH as the electrolyte.38 However, there is no report available on the asymmetric supercapacitor (ASC) device performance of a MoSe2/graphene (G) nanohybrid.

Hence, we report a facile and an effective approach via a sonochemical method for the preparation of a few layers of MoSe2 NS using H2O2 as an intercalating agent (oxidant) in isopropyl alcohol (solvent). Further, the prepared MoSe2 NS is hybridized with graphene via a one-pot solvothermal process. The physical characterization of the exfoliated MoSe2 NS and the MoSe2/G nanohybrid are studied through X-ray diffraction (XRD), Raman spectroscopy, atomic force microscopy (AFM), field emission scanning electron microscopy (FE-SEM) and high-resolution transmission electron microscopy (HR-TEM). The electrochemical properties of the MoSe2 NS and MoSe2/G nanohybrid in 6 M KOH are studied using cyclic voltammetry (CV), galvanostatic chargeâ€“discharge (GCD) curves and ac-impedance studies. Finally, an ASC device is fabricated and its electrochemical cell performances are studied in detail.

2. Experimental

2.1 Preparation of layered MoSe2 nanosheets

A simple and facile sonochemical assisted exfoliation method was used to exfoliate the bulk MoSe2 powder into MoSe2 NS. In this method, 1 g of bulk MoSe2 was dispersed in 100 mL of IPA with 2.5 vol% of H2O2. This resultant mixture was probe sonicated (SONIC VCX 500) by using a constant power of 500 W for various reaction times (15, 30 and 45 min) in ice-cold conditions, and due to the high shear force, the exfoliation of MoSe2 from its bulk powder was achieved. Once the reaction time ended, the dark grey colloidal suspension was centrifuged at 10000 rpm. The unreacted H2O2 was removed by decanting the supernatant to avoid the over-oxidation of MoSe2. The precipitate was collected and again dispersed in 100 mL of IPA and then the sonication was continued for 15 min and the supernatant was removed. The suspension of MoSe2 NS was collected and vacuum dried at 70 Â°C overnight to obtain a solid sample.

2.2 Preparation of the 2Dâ€“2D MoSe2/G nanohybrid

A 2Dâ€“2D MoSe2/G nanohybrid was prepared by a facile solvothermal method. A 1:0.5 weight ratio of exfoliated MoSe2 NS and GO was dispersed in 50 ml of deionized water by ultrasonication for 45 min to separate the stacked precursors. Under the stirring conditions, 15 ml of DMF was added dropwise to the above solution. After 15 min, the solution was transferred to a Teflon lined stainless steel autoclave and heated to 120 Â°C for 24 h, and then cooled to room temperature. Finally, the obtained product was washed several times with deionized water and dried in a vacuum at 50 Â°C for 12 h to obtain the 2Dâ€“2D MoSe2/G nanohybrid. If the mass ratio of GO to MoSe2 was fixed as 1, the electrical double layer capacitive (EDLC) behaviour of graphene was more dominant than the pseudocapacitive behaviour of MoSe2. Hence, the mass ratio of GO to MoSe2 was fixed as 0.5:1.0.39,40 The physical characterization, electrochemical measurement and fabrication of the ASC device is described in the ESI Section.â€

The inset in Fig. 9(a) shows an equivalent circuit, consisting of Rs, Rct, CF and ZW which are the solution resistance, charge-transfer resistance, faradic pseudocapacitance, and Warburg impedance, respectively.57 From the Nyquist plots, an equivalent series resistance (ESR) and Rct are calculated and are found to be 0.6 Î© and 1.5 Î©, respectively, for MoSe2 NS. Similarly, for the MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrode, ESR and Rct are found to be 0.25 Î© and 0.85 Î©, respectively. The presence of graphene in the MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrode offers a high specific surface for diffusion/migration of OHâˆ’ ions at the electrodeâ€“electrolyte interface, which reduces the internal resistance. Both the MoSe2 NS and MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrodes exhibit a knee frequency, i.e. a transition zone between the high-frequency semicircle and low-frequency straight line.58 In the high-frequency region, the semicircle can be attributed to the electrical charge transfer process and suggests lower internal resistance, while in the low-frequency region, the straight line represents the diffusion of the electrolyte ions inside the electrode material (mass transfer process). The cycle stability of the MoSe2 NS and MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrodes are assessed by GCD cycling at a constant current density of 5 A gâˆ’1 for 3000 cycles; results are shown in Fig. 9(b). The specific capacitance is maintained at nearly 100% for up to 1000 cycles for both the electrodes. The MoSe2 NS electrode exhibits a gradual decrease in specific capacitance after 1000 cycles and retains 84% of its capacitance at 3000 cycles. An XRD analysis of the MoSe2 electrode, carried out before and after the cycling test, is shown in Fig. S7.â€  The MoSe2 electrode is fully dipped in the KOH electrolyte in the three electrode system and hence during the electrochemical reaction, MoSe2 forms an oxide layer. The XRD pattern after the cycling test shows the appearance of some peaks at 34.8Â° and 53.5Â° after 3000 chargeâ€“discharge cycles which correspond to (111) and (211) planes indicating the formation of an MoO3 phase during the cycling process. Moreover, a gradual decrease in the specific capacitance is observed giving 84% after 3000 cycles, which is due to the formation of a MoO3 film that leads to poor electronic conductivity. In the case of the MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrode an excellent capacitance retention of 92% of its original capacitance is observed even after 3000 continuous chargeâ€“discharge cycles. Overall for 3000 cycles, we observe only a minimal 8% decay in capacitance which demonstrates the excellent electrochemical stability of the MoSe2/G nanohybrid electrode. This is due to the thin nanosheet-like morphology of MoSe2 and graphene which provide good strain relaxation for electrolyte species.

4 Conclusions

In conclusion, the present work reports the synthesis of MoSe2 NS that are a few layers thick, by a facile sonochemical exfoliation process. The optimum sonication time to obtain the MoSe2 NS is found to be 30 min. Further, a MoSe2/G nanohybrid is obtained by a simple solvothermal method. The pseudocapacitive nature of the MoSe2 NS, and both the pseudocapacitive and EDLC behaviours of the MoSe2/G nanohybrid are explored by cyclic voltammetry studies. The perpendicular arrangement of the few layers of MoSe2 on graphene gives a specific capacitance of 945 F gâˆ’1 at 1 A gâˆ’1. Finally, a MoSe2/Gâ€–AC ASC device is fabricated and obtains a high energy density of 26.6 W h kgâˆ’1 (@0.8 kW kgâˆ’1) with good long-term stability. This reveals that the MoSe2/G nanohybrid could be used as a promising positive electrode material for asymmetric supercapacitor applications.
