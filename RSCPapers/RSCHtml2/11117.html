Abstract

A novel triangular-spiral conjugation molecule of TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 using triphenylamine (TPA) as the donor core, diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP) as the acceptor arm and phenanthrene (PN) as the planar arene terminal, as well as its counterpart of TPAâ€“3DPP without the PN terminal, were prepared. Their UV-vis absorption, electrochemistry and thermal stability, as well as hole mobility were investigated. Significantly red-shifted UV-vis absorption profiles were observed for TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 instead of TPAâ€“3DPP in solution and solid state. A hole mobility of 1.67 Ã— 10âˆ’4 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 was obtained for the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM blended film, which is 2.1 times higher than that of the TPAâ€“3DPP/PC71BM blended film. Furthermore, the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM-based organic solar cells presented better photovoltaic property with a maximum power conversion efficiency of 3.67%, which is 1.9 times higher than that of TPAâ€“3DPP/PC71BM-based devices. The results confirm that appending planar PN terminals to TPAâ€“3DPP with a triangular-spiral shape is an efficient approach to improve the photovoltaic performance of its resulting molecules.

Introduction

Organic solar cells (OSCs) are considered to be a promising technology in the world's energy strategy due to their outstanding advantages such as low cost, easy fabrication, light weight, and compatibility with flexible substrates.1â€“5 Over the past few decades, most research efforts have been focused on solution-processed bulk hetero-junction (BHJ) polymer solar cells (PSCs) using a blend of p-type polymers and n-type soluble fullerene derivatives as the photoactive layer. A power conversion efficiency (PCE) of over 9% was demonstrated in PSCs.6â€“10 Recently, another branch of the OSCs, solution-processable small molecule organic solar cells (SM-OSCs) have gained considerable attention because of their simple purification, defined molecular weight, and superior batch-to-batch reproducibility.6,11â€“15 A PCE of over 8% was obtained in SM-OSCs, which is still lower than that in PSCs.8,16 Therefore, it remains a great challenge for us to develop new high-performance solution-processable small organic molecules and their SM-OSCs.

Among the developed small molecular donor materials, most of them are built by some electron donor (D) and electron acceptor (A) units, and they exhibit a lower band-gap owing to an increasing double-bond character among the D and A units.17â€“20 Furthermore, hybridization of the energy levels between the D and A units can decrease the highest occupied molecular orbit (HOMO) and the lowest unoccupied molecular orbit (LUMO) energy levels, which leads to an unusually small HOMOâ€“LUMO energy separation.21 Thus, the linear and star-shaped molecules with a Dâ€“A framework can act as light harvesting antennae and easily acquire enhanced photo-physical properties by tuning their molecular structures.22

As is well known, triphenylamines (TPA) and/or diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP) units have been widely used to construct Dâ€“A polymers and small molecules in OSCs, because TPA has good electron-donating and hole-transporting abilities,23â€“31 whereas DPP has strong light absorption and photochemical stability.32 As a result, some low band-gap molecules with the DPP unit were obtained, and these exhibited good photovoltaic performance in BHJ OSCs.33â€“39 Successful examples were reported for polymers with a PCE of 8.0% (ref. 36) and for small molecules with a PCE of 5.79% (ref. 38) in the devices. In order to completely utilize their advantages, both TPA and DPP units were recently employed to construct the low band-gap molecules due to the strong intramolecular Dâ€“A interaction. For example, Shi and Zhan reported some star-shaped Dâ€“A small molecules with a TPA core, three DPP arms and different substituted phenyl end-groups.40â€“43 The photovoltaic properties of these star-shaped molecules were observed to be improved by introducing different end-groups onto the DPP moiety. A maximum PCE of 2.13% was obtained in these OSCs without the addition of DIO.

As planar arenes have a favorable end-to-end Ï€â€“Ï€ interaction, which can improve the charge transport and interconnectivity,44â€“46 some planar arenes such as pyrene and phenanthrene were used as end groups to construct linear-shaped Dâ€“A small molecules. The planarity of these end-groups was demonstrated to significantly affect the device performances of their resulting small molecules. Considering that C2â€“phenanthrene has a greater degree of conjugation than C2â€“naphthalene and substituted benzene (phenyl, fluoro-phenyl and alkyl-phenyl) and can form smaller degree of planarity with other aromatic units than C9â€“anthracene, as shown in our previous work, we developed a type of the linear-shaped small molecules with Ï€-stacking planar phenanthrene terminals.47 The star-shaped small molecules have presented good solution-processability, high absorption coefficient, excellent purity and dispersibility.17,18 Therefore, in this study, in order to further study the influence of the star framework and planar terminals on the photophysical and photovoltaic properties, a new triangular-spiral small molecule of TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 has been designed and synthesized (Scheme 1), in which TPA, DPP and PN (phenanthrene) are used as the donor core, the acceptor arm and the end group, respectively. As expected, the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 molecule, which is appended additional PN terminals, exhibited broader and stronger absorption than the TPAâ€“3DPP counterpart without PN terminals. Furthermore, the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3-based devices demonstrated a better photovoltaic performance than the TPAâ€“3DPP-based devices. A maximum PCE of 3.67% with a short circuit current density (Jsc) of 8.95 mA cmâˆ’2, an open circuit voltage (Voc) of 0.80 V, and a fill factor (FF) of 51% was obtained in the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM based devices under the illumination of AM 1.5 G, 100 mW cmâˆ’2. The observed PCE, Jsc, and FF values are 1.9, 1.5 and 1.4 times higher than the corresponding values of the TPAâ€“3DPP based cells, respectively. In comparison to the reported star-shaped small molecular analogues with different substituted phenyl end groups, the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 molecule further displayed higher FF and PCE values in their OSCs.42 This work confirms that the photovoltaic performance of the triangular-spiral molecules can be improved by appending additional planar phenanthrene terminals.

Photovoltaic performances

The photovoltaic performances of the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 and TPAâ€“3DPP based OSCs were measured by the fabricated BHJ devices with a structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS(30 nm)/active layer (70 nm)/Ca (10 nm)/Al (100 nm). The active layer consists of a TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 (or TPAâ€“3DPP) donor and a PC61BM (or PC71BM) acceptor. In order to optimize the ratios between donors and acceptors, the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC61BM based devices in various ratios from 1:1 to 1:3 (w/w) were fabricated. Fig. S1â€  shows the resulting current densityâ€“voltage (Jâ€“V) characteristics of the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC61BM based cells, and the corresponding photovoltaic data are summarized in Table S1 (see ESIâ€ ) under AM 1.5 G illumination. A maximum PCE of 3.07% with a Voc of 0.81 V, Jsc of 8.12 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF of 46.6% was obtained in the cells under an optimized ratio of 1:2.5 (w/w) between TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 and PC61BM. It is worthy to note here that PCE and FF values for TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC61BM based cells are higher than the corresponding levels of its analogues/PC61BM based cells.42 This indicates that appending planar PN terminals is a better approach than appending substituted phenyl terminals in the star-shaped small molecules to improve the photovoltaic performances of its resulting molecules. In order to further improve the device performance, we took PC71BM instead of PC61BM as an acceptor to fabricate new BHJ-OSCs. The typical Jâ€“V characteristics are shown in Fig. 4 under illumination of AM 1.5 G, 100 mW cmâˆ’2 and the corresponding device parameters are summarized in Table 3. It is found that the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM based device presented the best photovoltaic performance with increasing PCE, Jsc and FF levels. A maximum PCE of 3.67% with Voc of 0.80 V, Jsc of 8.95 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF of 51% is obtained. To our knowledge, the PCE value here is the highest among the reported star-shaped small molecules with a TPAâ€“DPP or TPAâ€“3DPP framework in the single BHJ OSCs.40â€“42 In contrast, the TPAâ€“3DPP/PC71BM-based device displayed a lower PCE of 1.91% with Voc of 0.91 V, Jsc of 5.85 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF of 36%. The significantly increased PCE and Jsc for the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM-based devices can be attributed to the enhanced absorption intensity of TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3.

Conclusions

In summary, a novel triangular-spiral small molecule of TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 was pre-pared with additional PN end-capped units on the basis of the TPAâ€“3DPP counter-part. Its photovoltaic performance and the hole mobility of the TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3 based devices are significantly improved owing to the attachment of the Ï€-stacking planar PN terminals with the TPAâ€“3DPP parent. The optimized TPA(DPPâ€“PN)3/PC71BM based device presented a dramatically enhanced PCE of 3.67% with a Jsc of 8.95 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF of 51%, which is the highest among the star-shaped molecules with the same TPAâ€“3DPP framework and different appending terminals. The results indicate that the structural modifications by appending planar PN terminals into the star-shaped TPAâ€“3DPP parent could significantly enhance the device performances of its resulting molecules.
