Abstract

Electrode materials exhibiting nanostructural design, high surface area, tunable pore size, and efficient ion diffusion/transportation are essential for achieving improved electrochemical performance. In this study, we successfully prepared cobalt phosphide and cobalt nanoparticles embedded into nitrogen-doped nanoporous carbon (CoPâ€“CoNC/CC) using a simple precipitation method followed by pyrolysisâ€“phosphatization. Subsequently, we employed CoPâ€“CoNC/CC as the electrode for supercapacitor applications. Notably, the resultant CoPâ€“CoNC/CC displayed a high surface area with tunable porosity. Based on the benefits of the CoP in CoNC/CC, improved electrochemical performance was achieved with a specific capacitance of 975 F gâˆ’1 at 1 mA cmâˆ’2 in a 2 M KOH electrolyte. The assembled hybrid supercapacitor using CoPâ€“CoNC/CC (positive electrode) and activated carbon (AC) (negative electrode) exhibited a specific capacitance of 144 F gâˆ’1, a specific energy of 39.2 W h kgâˆ’1 at 1960 W kgâˆ’1 specific power, with better cyclic stability. The higher performance can be attributed to the synergetic effect between CoP, Co metal, and the nitrogen-doped nanoporous carbon in three-dimensional carbon cloth (CC). These excellent properties make CoPâ€“CoNC/CC a promising electrode for developing future energy-storage devices.

Introduction

Recently, environmental deterioration and energy shortage have attracted considerable attention for the identification of clean and renewable energy sources. Among various electrochemical energy technologies, supercapacitors (SC) with a superior power density (up to 105 kW kgâˆ’1), an extremely long cycle lifetime, and fast charge/discharge rates have attracted significant research attention from both industry and the academy.1â€“3 These merits are extensively used to argue for the replacement of conventional rechargeable batteries for practical applications, including portable electronic devices, back-up power systems, hybrid electric vehicles, and storage. But, the power density of the supercapacitors is somewhat poor when related to those of the batteries and fuel cells. Therefore, it is critical to find an appropriate electrode material to improve the corresponding electrochemical performance. To date, several electrodes, such as carbon materials, metal hydrides, metal oxides, conductive polymers, metal selenides, metal nitrides, and metal phosphides, have been studied for developing supercapacitors.4â€“11 Among these, metal phosphides have gathered considerable attention because of their superior redox activity, high chemical stability, and particularly high electrical conductivity.12,13

Metalâ€“organic frameworks (MOFs) have recently attracted significant attention owing to their easy synthesis, structural morphology, tunable pore size, unusually huge specific surface area, and metal ion-rich centers, which provide ample space for electrochemical reactions.14â€“20 Recent literature has revealed that MOFs have been extensively considered for energy and environmental applications such as batteries, solar cells, supercapacitors, and water splitting.21â€“29 However, the poor electrical conductivity and large steric hindrance effects minimize their applicability in electrochemical applications. Therefore, it is necessary to explore a new strategy for enhancing the MOF electrochemical performance. Generally, MOFs formed through intermolecular bonding or coordination bonding between the metal ions or clusters, with organic linkers providing exclusive 2D or 3D structural frameworks. MOF formed through various organic linkers, which offer different structural morphologies as well as electrochemical properties. Among various MOFs, the imidazole-based frameworks offer good metal ion coordination with the imidazole ligands, and its rich nitrogen, carbon, and metal ions provide good electrolyte ion diffusion pathways and improve the conductivity.30â€“34 Among the reported MOFs with various metal ions, cobalt-based MOFs possess good dualistic reversible redox pairs and extremely high stability in highly concentrated alkaline electrolyte media.

Recently, metal phosphide-based electrodes have shown excellent electrical conductivity and promise as energetic materials for energy storage applications. Among those electrode materials, CoP has been studied in various research fields such as electrocatalytic energy, electronics, photonics, etc.35â€“37 These widespread applications are due to submetallic alloy properties that arise from the combined properties of metalloid phosphides and metallic cobalt. It is the cobalt whose intrinsic higher conductivity promotes boosted the electro-kinetic rates, over-whelming the oxides. Cobalt phosphides offer rapid electron transfer kinetics among electrode and electrolyte interface, generating better power density in SCs and enhanced electrocatalytic performance for electrocatalysts. Additionally, in alkaline electrolyte media, sulfide-based electrodes tend to decompose, gradually but profoundly losing their initial capacity. Hence, the active reaction kinetics and robust strength of cobalt phosphides show promise for use in electrochemical and electrocatalytic reactions. Furthermore, the direct growth of these MOF nanostructures on the substrate without using binder or additives can assure improved electrical conductivity as well as mechanical strength. Among various substrates, carbon cloth (CC) is an economy and easily accessible texture with good electrical conductivity, robust corrosion resistance, excellent flexibility, and particularly high mechanical strength.38â€“41

Herein, we report the synthesis of polyhedral Co-MOF anchored onto CC by a simple precipitation method to yield Co-MOF/CC. Further pyrolysis of Co-MOF/CC resulted in Co metal nanoparticles embedded in nitrogen-containing nanoporous carbon (CoNC/CC). Electrochemical activity and electrical conductivity were further improved by partially converting Co metal into cobalt phosphide with a low percentage of phosphate precursor by pyrolysis and phosphatization under argon atmosphere resulting in CoP- and Co metal-embedded nanoporous carbon (CoPâ€“CoNC/CC). Supercapacitance performances of prepared Co-MOF/CC, CoNC/CC, and CoPâ€“CoNC/CC electrode materials were investigated by bi- and tri-electrode configurations. The experimental results illustrated that CoPâ€“CoNC/CC maintains extremely high specific and areal capacitance in 2 M KOH electrolyte, outstanding rate capability, also long cycling retention capability. Also, assembled hybrid SCs (CoPâ€“CoNC/CC//AC) demonstrated very high volumetric capacity, stability, and energy density.

Experimental section

Synthesis of Co-MOF/CC

Co-MOF was prepared according to a reported protocol.42 Specifically, 6.55 g of 2-methylimidazole (organic linker) was slowly added into 100 ml methanol and stirred well to obtain a homogeneous solution (Solution A). Pre-cleaned, dried CC was immersed into solution A. In another beaker, 2.9 g Co(NO3)2Â·6H2O was slowly added into 100 ml methanol under vigorous stirring until to obtain a homogeneous solution (Solution B). The obtained a homogeneous solution B was injected into solution A under vigorous stirring. And then, the final solution was kept undisturbed for 24 h at 25 Â°C. Finally, the CC with purple deposits was carefully collected, washed many times in ethanol solution, and dried under vacuum conditions at 80 Â°C to obtain pure Co-MOF/CC. The mass loading of Co-MOF on CC was 0.7 mg cmâˆ’2.

Preparation of CoNC/CC

CoNC/CC was obtained by high-temperature carbonization using a programmable furnace under Ar atmosphere. Per the typical method, 3 Ã— 3 cm2 Co-MOF/CC was carbonized up to 800 Â°C with a constant ramp rate of 2 Â°C minâˆ’1 and maintained for 3 h in Ar atmosphere to attain CoNC/CC. The mass loading of CoNC on CC was 0.58 mg cmâˆ’2.

Preparation of CoPâ€“CoNC/CC

To prepare CoPâ€“CoNC/CC samples, 3 Ã— 3 cm2 CoNC/CC and a low weight percentage of 10 mg sodium hypophosphite monohydrate (NaH2PO2Â·H2O) were kept at distinct positions in a ceramic boat, with the phosphate precursor located in upstream Ar purging. In this manner, the precursors were heated up to 300 Â°C with a contant ramp rate of 2 Â°C minâˆ’1 and maintained for 1 h to obtain CoPâ€“CoNC/CC. The mass loading of CoPâ€“CoNC on CC was 0.6 mg cmâˆ’2. The obtained samples were further subjected to physical and electrochemical analysis.

Material characterization

The chemical structure and surface morphological properties of the prepared Co-MOF/CC, CoNC/CC, and CoPâ€“CoNC/CC electrodes were characterized by X-ray diffraction analysis (XRD, Rigaku Ultima), Raman spectroscopy (LabRam ARIMIS IR2), X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS, ULVAC-PHI), high-resolution transmission electron microscopy (HR-TEM, JEOL; JEM-2010) with energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS-mapping) and field emission scanning electron microscope (FESEM, Quanta 25FEG). N2 adsorption and desorption isotherms were carried out by Micromeritics ASAP 2020 surface area analyzer.

Electrochemical measurements

Electrochemical analysis of the Co-MOF/CC, CoNC/CC, and CoPâ€“CoNC/CC electrodes were taken using bi- and tri-electrode configurations in aqueous electrolyte (ZIVE SP5). For tri-electrode configuration; working electrode (as-prepared Co-MOF/CC, CoNC/CC, and CoPâ€“CoNC/CC (1 Ã— 1 cm2)), reference electrode (Hg/HgO) and counter electrode (platinum wire). In the frequency range of 106 Hz to 0.01 Hz, electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) was performed with an AC amplitude of 10 mV. The two-electrode measurements were employed for a hybrid device configuration (CoPâ€“CoNC/CC//AC).
