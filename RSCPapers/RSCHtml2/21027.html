Abstract

Searching for adaptable, versatile, and highly performing substances or building blocks is the goal of materials science. Owing to their exceptional structural modifiability, capability for supramolecular assembly, distinctive optoelectronic specialty, and superior device performance, various pyridazine-based (PD-based) heteroaromatic hydrocarbons (HAHs), which are embedded with the adjacent sp2-N pair (NN/NN), have attracted increasing attention in the field of organic optoelectronic materials. In particular, organic field-effect transistors (OFETs), organic photovoltaic devices (OPVs), organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs), fluorescence probes, luminescence, chromism, catalysis, and supramolecular assembly have witnessed notable advancements in recent years. This review categorized different molecular species, outlined structural traits, personality traits, and device performance and analyzed connections between those elements, including PD-based polymers, pure organic small molecules, pure organic complexes, and ligandmetal complexes as host materials, thermally active delayed fluorescence materials, electrode materials, chromism materials, fluorescence probes, organic catalysts, and energy materials. The aza-quantity, substituent, regioisomerism, dihydrogen bond, and dual-coordination effect were all summarized and expanded. In addition, the trends, challenges, and future aspects of PD-based HAHs were discussed and prospected.

1. Introduction

Heterocyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (HAHs) have undergone thorough research and quickly developed as organic optoelectronic materials for various uses,16 including optoelectronic devices, sensing, information encryption, catalysis, gas storage, and energy storage. Significant efforts have been made to design and create new HAHs with specific optoelectronic properties to satisfy the aforementioned applications. This has increased the demand for HAH-related structural design, simple and affordable synthesis techniques, and efficient device performance optimization. Hence, a significant variety of HAHs based on various -conjugated core units, such as six-membered diazines,713 acridones,1417 quinoxalines,1821 pyrimidines,22,23 triazines,24 and five-membered imidazoles2527 and pyrroles,28,29 have emerged in the past decades. Among these, -conjugated pyridazine derivatives (PDs),2935 a type of HAH with PD-unit(s) in the conjugated skeleton, have drawn great attention because of their distinct properties, relating to the adjacent sp2-N pair, including excellent structural modifiability, unique supramolecular recognition and assembly capability, and potentially excellent optoelectronic properties in devices.3638 The conductivity of PDs has a long history that dates back to 1969. Hoare and Prat found that organic pyridazine-iodine (PD-I) complex30 had low resistivities ranging from 1 cm to 50 cm measured by the four-probe technique. In 1984, Satoh and collaborators found that the conductivity of doped and undoped polypyridazine (polyPD) (Scheme 1) films synthesized by electrochemical oxidation reached conductivities of 10 S cm139 and 6 S cm1, respectively.40 Yang and collaborators reported that the low electronic resistivity of the iodine-doped conjugation-interrupted poly(3-vinylpyridazine) (poly(3-vinylPD) (Scheme 1)) was measured to be 1000500 cm with the double probe method.41 According to a 1987 study, Kang and collaborators34 reported that the conductivities of PD-I complex approached 2 S cm1 for PD-I under room-temperature conditions. In 1990, Xi and collaborators42 reported that the measured conductivity of compressed powder of doped polyPD reached 1.56 S cm1. Similar to the building blocks of pyrimidine and pyrazine with farther sp2-N pairs,7 the electron-deficient PD unit is an outstanding electron-withdrawing unit to construct electron transfer materials with high electron mobilities29,36 and/or electron-pushpull molecular systems with well-separated and balanced electron/hole transfer capabilities.37,43 Particularly, the presence of adjacent N atoms facilitates the generation of pyridazinechelated (PDchelated) complexes,4446 dihydropyridazines (dihydroPDs),47 unique hydrogen bonds, and protonated PDs29,35 to construct new molecular systems for various applications from OLEDs, OFETs, OPVs, and sensing to catalysis. Owing to the outstanding biological features of PDs, such as bactericidal and anticancer actions (antitumor activity), their biological research and application in medical drugs and pesticides are very successful and have been reviewed.4852 Their progresses on materials have also been reviewed for many years.7,53 To demonstrate the structurepropertyperformance relationship of PDs, the accompanying principles, applications, challenges, and prospects, this review mainly focused on the classification of versatile PD-based materials and applications (Fig. 1a) in organic field effect transistors (OFETs), organic photovoltaic devices (OPVs), organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs), and also excellent fluorescence probes/indicators, chromism, catalysis, and energy storage/release.

2. PD-cored optoelectronic materials and their applications

2.1 PDs for OFETs

Electron-deficient and adjacent sp2-N pair-embedded PD-units have strong electron-withdrawing properties and tend to exhibit supramolecular effects with hydrogen, halogen, and coordination bonds, making them promising semiconductors for OFETs. These units aim for the terminal target of versatile and highly performing compounds or star building blocks. Moreover, owing to the rich substituent sites and the well-developed synthetic methods, the PD-unit is utilized as a universal electron-deficient building block to construct charge transfer materials with donoracceptor (DA) structures, including pyridazinethiophene (PDthiophene) polymers/oligomers and pyridazinecarbazole (PDcarbazole) small molecules via fused cyclization and CC, CN, or CO covalent bonding. Various PD-based organic semiconductors, including small molecules, polymers, and cocrystal charge transfer complexes, have been effectively used as channel semiconductors in OFETs by carefully manipulating the energy levels, charge mobilities, and stacking patterns of PDs with rational molecular design. Liu and collaborators5 found that pyrrolo[3,4-d]pyridazine (PPD) and diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP) unit-based copolymer PPPDDPP (Scheme 1) exhibited the ambipolar charge transfer features with 0.03 cm2 V1 s1 and 0.054 cm2 V1 s1 for electron and hole mobilities, respectively. Electron-rich tetrathiafulvalene (TTF) was successfully fused on PD-unit providing T-shaped small molecules T3a and T5b (Scheme 1)6 with mobilities of 4.5 105 cm2 V1 s1 and 4.5 105 cm2 V1 s1, respectively. The pyridazine-fused (PD-fused) thiophene polymers PDTTPTT, PDTTPBT, and PDTTPBDT (Scheme 1) have optical band-gaps ranging from 1.41 eV to 1.58 eV. PDTTPTT36 annealed for 1 h achieved the highest hole mobility of 0.05 cm2 V1 s1. After annealing at a high temperature (200 C), the hole mobility remains at 0.04 cm2 V1 s1 and the on/off ratio is still 105. This demonstrates that the PD-unit fused on the thiophene moiety is beneficial to the thermal stability of the polymer with alkyl side chains. The charge transfer capabilities for more star PD-units could be predicted using theoretical models. For example, Wang and collaborators55 predicted the maximum electron and hole mobilities of the large -extended pyridazino[4,5-d]pyridazine (diPD) derivative TAT (Scheme 1) to be 3.404 102 cm2 V1 s1 and 6.516 104 cm2 V1 s1 along the crystallographic a-axis, respectively. This indicates that single-crystal TAT is a potential n-type material. Furthermore, it was predicted that the polyhalogenated 4F-TAT, 4Cl-TAT, and polycyanated 4CN-TAT derivatives are potential n-type materials. A unique supramolecular system force-driven by hydrogen bonds can be built using the adjacent sp2-N pair on the PD-unit as hydrogen bonding acceptors. The PDcarbazole-based (DCPi) cocrystal DCPiTCNQ is coassembled with a 1:2.5 molar ratio of DCPi (Scheme 1), and TCNQ exhibits an electron mobility of 105 cm2 V1 s1.29 It is located between the values (106108, 8.3 103 cm2 V1 s1) of the other two complexes (1,4-di(9H-carbazol-9-yl)benzene (DCBz)-TCNQ and 2,5-di(9H-carbazol-9-yl)pyrazine (DCPa)-TCNQ), which were formed from donors and acceptors with a molar ratio of 1:2. This suggests that the adjacent sp2-N pair of the PD-unit can be used to control the charge transfer characteristics of organic semiconductor materials and, more crucially, to accomplish functional optimization through supramolecular interactions. For example, dibenzo[c,e]pyridazine (dibenzo[c,e]PD) (BCC) could be used to construct coassembled systems through HN hydrogen35 and CIN halogen bonds.34,56 Lu and collaborators57 designed and synthesized a series of coronal macrocyclic PDs (OCP-3a, OCP-3b, OCP-3c, OCP-4, and OCP-5) (Scheme 1) for the identification of organic ammonium and dinitrile compounds. The primary supramolecular driving force of the host guest aforementioned was the HN hydrogen bonds originated from the adjacent sp2-N pair on the PD-unit. This represents a significant advance that the coronal macrocyclic structures can be used to identify biological cations.

The adjacent sp2-N pair on the PD-unit promotes the formation of intramolecular dual SN contacts, intermolecular hydrogen bonds, and halogen bonds. It favors the optimization of the molecular configuration and increases device performance. For example, with an increase in the number of N atoms in the structures of the benzodithiophenepyridazine (benzodithiophenePD) copolymer system (MP0, MP1, and MP2) (Scheme 1),58 the dual supramolecular interactions between PD and thiophene units on both sides of the molecule are strengthened. Consequently, the conformational planarity of the main chain gradually increases (MP0 < MP1 < MP2), resulting in an absorption redshift and a decrease in the optical band-gap from 2.28 to 2.12 eV. Consequently, the conformational planarity of the main chain gradually increased (MP0, MP1, and MP2). The space charge-limited current (SCLC) mobilities of MP0, MP1, and MP2 polymer films improved from 2.33 106 to 3.78 105 cm2 V1 s1, providing one more clue for the existence of an aza effect on optimizing charge transfer ability by the embedment of adjacent sp2-N pair. Among various organic semiconductor materials, the electron or hole mobilities of the PDs (Fig. 2a) are kept at a comparatively low level. Consequently, systematic optimization must be used to continuously design and validate studies. The -conjugated units and the macroscopic properties are connected by the aza effect of multiheteroatoms,59 which has clear but not absolute patterns among the -conjugated backbones owing to their various distances, overlaps, group kinds, and sizes. Li and collaborators60 found that the electron-deficient pyridazinooxadiazole (PCCP5) (Scheme 1) unit contributes the majority of the polymer's highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO)/lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) energy levels (5.91 eV/4.30 eV) lower than that (5.79 eV/4.15 eV) of PD-based pyridazinothiadiazole (PCCP3), pyridine-based pyridooxadiazole (PCCP4), and pyridothiadiazole (PCCP2). Theoretically, PCCP5 (Scheme 1) with a pyridazinooxadiazole unit should have an electron mobility of 1.06 cm2 V1 s1. PCCP3 with pyridazinothiadiazole demonstrated the lowest mobility of 0.07 cm2 V1 s1, which is lower than that of PCCP2 with pyridothiadiazole. This provides clue for the potential synergy effect between four sp2-N atoms and a weakly electron-donating O atom. The latter has a distinctive contribution to the LUMO energy levels of pyridazine-containing (PD-containing) polymers in contrast to the former's strong electron-donating S atom.

Leclerc and collaborators61 found that the LUMO energy level (4.15 eV) of the pyridazinooxadiazole-cored polymer MRC-P1 (Scheme 2) was lower than those (3.88 eV and 3.99 eV) of MRC-P2 and MRC-P3, respectively. This showed that pyridazinooxadiazole, an electron-withdrawing compound, was very successful in regulating the LUMO energy levels of polymers. This further suggests that the molecular systems containing PD-unit exhibit the aza effect.62 However, compared to the fused oxadiazole system, the fused thiadiazole system has inferior optimization capabilities for energy levels and device performance.

2.2 PDs for OPVs

Through the synergy effect of supramolecular interactions and electronic structures, PD-units have been employed as electron-withdrawing acceptor units to control the energy levels, thermal stability, intermolecular packing modes, and polymeric configuration of OPV materials. The aforementioned pyridazinooxadiazole-based polymer60 not only exhibited obvious advantages in charge transfer ability but also possibly hint at potential advantages to enhance the performance of OPV materials. The structure-property-performance relationship of the polymer with pyridazinooxadiazole (Scheme 2, MRC-P1) clarified by Leclerc and collaborators61 is very important. The change trends of short-circuit current densities (Jsc) and power conversion efficiencies (PCE) (0.47%, 0.37%, and 0.13%) are compatible with those of the LUMO energy levels of MRC-P1, MRC-P3, and MRC-P2 (1.52, 0.86, and 0.55 mA cm1), respectively. This result reflects the potential optoelectronic properties and device performance of pyridazinooxadiazole-based polymers. In 2011, the empirical correlations discovered by Leclerc and collaborators63,64 in conjunction with density functional theory (DFT) and experimental information of model compounds allowed for fair estimations of the parameters, such as the HOMO energy level, LUMO energy level, and energy gap (Eg) of polymers. The expected values of the carbazolepyridazinothiophene copolymers (Scheme 2), including poly-CDTPZOx and poly-CDTPZQx64 (MRC-P1 and MRC-P3),61 as p-type materials in bulk heterojunction (BHJ) solar cells, are very close to the experimental data. The energy gaps of pyridazinooxadiazole-based poly-CDTPZOx are smaller than those of pyrazino[2,3-d]pyridazine-based (pyrazino[2,3-d]PD-based) poly-CDTPZQx, indicating the matched electronic structures of the PD-unit and oxadiazole unit. This accomplishment offers a significant and beneficial model for the development and exploration of high-performance PD-based OPV materials. The planarity of -conjugated systems,79 electron-withdrawing capability, substituent quantities,70 and ligands with various amounts and structures of PD-based complexes played key role in optimizing the performance of OLED devices. An orange light OLED device (560 nm) with a homoleptic Ir complex (IrNzPPya) (Scheme 3)79 as the dopant emitter containing pyridazinechelated ligands (PDligands) presented excellent device efficiencies (70.8 cd A1, 75.4 lm W1, and 30.8%). However, the overall performance of another homoleptic Ir complex (IrCzPPya)-based OLED device was relatively poor (46.1 cd A1, 40.1 lm W1, and 22.6%). In addition, white organic light-emitting diodes (WOLEDs) with a single emission layer (EML) composed of IrNzPPya and a blue emitter FIrpic exhibited excellent device efficiencies (49.9 cd A1, 55.9 lm W1, and 23.9%) and one of the best white light CIE coordinates (0.33, 0.46). Compared to Ir(BFPPya)3 (Scheme 3) with fewer F substitutions and Ir(BPPya)3 without F substitutions, Ir(BDFPPya)3 with more F substitutions70 has the highest photoluminescence quantum yield (PLQY) of 84%, the most blueshifted electroluminescence (500 nm), and the lowest thermal decomposition temperature (Td) (402 C). The corresponding efficiencies of Ir(BPPya)3 without F substitutions are still at low levels (30.2 cd A1, 31.8 lm W1, and 11.2%). The effective experience related to the WOLED of homoleptic Ir complex IrNzPPya with excellent performance79 provides a valuable alternative to solve the mismatch of high PLQY and low device performance of the polyfluorinated homoleptic Ir complex Ir(BDFPPya).70 In another set of Ir homo-/heteroleptic complex systems with multiple F substitutions,80 the performance of OLED devices prepared with heteroleptic Ir(mdfppya)2pic (Scheme 3) exhibited the highest efficiencies (41.0 cd A1, 33.9 lm W1, and 11.62%) compared to those of the other two. By utilizing a doping concentration adjustment approach, suitable PD-based ancillary ligands with well-matched energy levels are beneficial for increasing device efficiency. Wang and collaborators81 studied the performance of OLED devices with ICA-2 (Scheme 3) and ICA-4 from the perspective of steric hindrance. At a doping concentration of 2.5%, the comparison revealed that the device efficiencies of ICA-4 reached 50.5 cd A1 and 14.9%, which are better than those of ICA-2 (41.7 cd A1 and 10.5%). Under high lumens of 60000 cd m2, the device efficiency of ICA-4 rolled off by 64%, which is lower than that of ICA-2 (70%), indicating that the steric hindrance of the bridge ring could effectively inhibit the intermolecular interaction of ICA-4. Diphenylpyridazine-based (diphenylPD-based) homoleptic Ir complexes (Ir(PDPh)3, Ir(FHPDPh)3, Ir(HFPDPh)3, and Ir(FPDPh)3) (Scheme 3), developed by Tong and collaborators,82 were doped into polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) films at a concentration of 0.01 wt% releasing green or yellowish green phosphorescence emission in a relatively high PLQY range of 6476%. The device with non-CF3-substituted Ir(PDPh)382 (OE-1, Scheme 3)33 as the dopant phosphor gave 17.7% EQE and a high-efficiency roll-off value of 37.3%. These parameters are comparable to those of Ir(HFPDPh)3-doped (15%) devices82 and better than those of methyl-containing OE-2 and OE-3-based devices,33 indicating the potential inadequacy of steric methylated phenyls on optimizing device performance. In addition, they developed another group of regioisomeric homoleptic Ir complexes (Ir-1, Ir-2, and Ir-3 (Scheme 3)) containing disordered thieno[2,3-d] rings,31 representing regioisomerism. The device with Ir1 as the dopant phosphor gave obviously better performance parameters. The influence of the steric hindrance effect is expected from the control experiment between Ir1 and the minor product as the dopant phosphors of OLEDs.

3. Synthetic strategy and electronic structures

With many great achievements in the past decades, there are mainly two typical synthetic strategies (strategies I and II, Scheme 7) in the construction of PDs, including the cyclization of pyridazine-ring (PD-ring) (strategy I) and the modification of PD-ring (strategy II) from various precursory chemicals. On the one hand, the cyclization of PD-ring (strategy I) could be achieved by several methods, such as the Schiff-base condensation between hydrazine and diketones122/anhydrides73/aromatic ortho-diesters,6 oxidative NN coupling cyclization between hydroxylamine and dinitrogen tetroxide (N2O4),123 or t-BuOCl and aromatic diamines,78 DielsAlder cycloaddition between tetrazines and substituted acetylenes124126 or ethylenes,57,127 [3+n] cycloaddition from azomethine ylides,128 diazo-based cycloaddition48,104 or quarternizative cyclization,102 and photoactivation of pyridazine-N-Oxides (PD-N-Oxides).129 On the other hand, the modification of PD-ring (strategy II) could be achieved by a huge number of CC or CN coupling or cyclization methods, including Suzuki reaction, Stille reaction, nucleophilic substitution, Sonogashira reaction, Aldol condensation, CH activated direct arylation, Minisci reaction, Ullmann reaction, BuchwaldHartwig reaction, and aroylation via wet chemistry, thermochemistry, photochemistry, and/or electrochemistry, which have been significantly investigated and/or summarized.53,104,130

Ritchie and collaborators131 found that the replacement of CH by N (Fig. 1c) resulted in a slightly suppressed electron delocalization132 and gave the aromaticity in sequence by benzene > pyridine PD pyrazine > pyrimidine > s-triazine based on magnetisability. More specifically, in 2007, Sol reported that PD possesses prior aromaticity with the highest multicenter indices (MCI, Iring) of 0.044/0.0304 than 0.042/0.042 and 0.0304/0.0291 of pyrimidine/pyrazine, respectively, calculated at CCSD/6-311++G(d,p) level of theory.133 By comparing the ionization ability of these three isomers, the pka values (CH3CN/H2O) 10.07/2.33 of PD were measured higher than 8.72/1.3 of pyrimidine and 7.74/0.6 of pyrazine (illustrated in Table S1 with references, ESI). Unlike the other two, the molecular structure of PD has been proposed as three resonance hybrid forms, including two Kekul structures and one charge-distributed structure.134 The specialty of PD is ascribed to the large Pauli lone-pair repulsion in NN/NN bonds (Fig. 1d) and is influenced by the priority of the electrostatic and -orbital interactions of the CN bonds of PD-ring.135 It is well known that the NN/NN bond stores great energy, which is regarded as the primary source of PD's special electronic structure. In 2008, Benin summarized136 the high-energetic tetrazine, pentazines, and hexazine systems containing more than one NN/NN bond. In 2019, Dobrynin and collaborators developed a technique named nanosecond-pulsed spark discharge plasma in liquid N2 and contiguous N-fragment (N3) from NaN3 recombined and obtained neutral or ionic N6 as the polynitrogen material.137 In 2022, Wang reported the experimental and theoretical investigation of metallic luster and planar hexazine dianions in the form of N62, which is metastable when pressures decreased to 20 GPa and can be stabilized in K2N6 above 45GPa.138 Therefore, it is indicated that the structurevariability, reactionactivity, stimulussensitivity, and substitutionmodificability of PDs are strongly influenced by the unique NN/NN bonds, which are different from that of other diazines (Fig. 1c). Yu and collaborator139 systematically summarized the diazine-based TADF emitters, especially pyrazine- and pyrimidine-based ones. But PD-based TADF emitters are not so extensive owing to the potential intramolecular transformation of resonance hybrid forms in brackets (Fig. 1c) under various electronic pushpull environments. This self-regulative transformation might result in a neutralized tuning of the RISC process with a potentially large EST for naked PDs or common substituted PDs. Once strong electron pushpull groups are modified on PD-rings, the variability of intramolecular transformation from three resonance forms of PD-ring to monohydropyridazine (monohydroPDs) form or dihydroPD form (Fig. 1c) arises and makes the controllable structural optimization a multivariate puzzle. However, modification strategies on new PD-based TADF emitters can also be performed using proper bridge groups,77,78 steric ortho-substitution, expensive or cheap metal-complexation140143 with suppressed variables as ligands for PhOLED emitters.

4. Conclusion and outlook

In summary, PDs with adjacent sp2-N pair can be used to construct various compounds, including polymers, pure organic small molecules, pure organic complexes, ligandmetal complexes, and ionized quaternary ammonium salt derivatives, possessing structural or electronic characteristics of adjacent sp2-N pair, electron-deficiency, regioisomerism, acceptors of hydrogen bonds, and dual-coordination. These advantages enable them to be useful host materials, TADF materials, or key active ingredients for OFETs, OPVs, OLEDs, and other functional applications. Most interestingly, PDs, embedded with variable NN/NN moieties in their molecular structures, show excellent performance and great potential when used as stimulus-responsive materials for electrochromism, acidochromism, and chemochromism. In addition, PDs are beginning to make breakthroughs as organocatalysts and electrode materials for photocatalysis, energy storage/release. There are some difficulties and challenges. For example, the NIR or far IR emission spectra of organic PDs are not easy to achieve even for large -extended pyrrolopyridazine (pyrroloPD) (PP-1 and PP-2 (Scheme 6)),122 tetraazaperylenes (TAP-a and TAP-b (Scheme 6)),55,144 and -extended phthalazine (2QP (Scheme 6)),145 diazabenzo[ghi]perylene derivatives (DABP-1, DABP-2 (Scheme 6)).146 Moreover, the performance of organic thin film optoelectronic devices with PD-based active ingredients remains at relatively low levels, including the charge mobilities (Fig. 2a and Table 1), photovoltaic performance (Fig. 2b and Table 2), and NIR luminescence efficiencies (Fig. 2c and Table 3).

Based on the unique structures of PD-cored chemicals, more systematic top-down or bottom-up molecular design for highly performing PD-based molecular libraries and star molecular systems would be achieved under the great efforts of global experts. The relatively fewer kinds and numbers of PD-cored materials are mainly attributed to two reasons. (1) The first reason is that the potential self-transformation between NN and NN bonds determines the instability of the sp2-N pair containing the PD-ring with large Pauli lone-pair repulsion in NN/NN bonds and the three resonance hybrid forms. (2) In contrast, the stability of pyrimidine and pyrazine cores determines the relative predomination of the investigation as useful materials. Given time, there will be more specific achievements. This review calls for more efforts to enrich versatile PDs for high-performance materials beyond bioactive pharmaceutical drugs and pesticides.49,147

For the sake of more and better functional PD-ring-based materials, herein, the general strategy can be explored by combining the previous studies and interesting results with practical synthetic methodologies (Scheme 7),50,106,148156 theoretical calculations,129,157161 aza-quantity effect and aza-regioisomerism,159,160 and supramolecular strategies,29,162165 the serendipitously discovered or well-designed of PD-based materials possess huge application prospects in the fields of optoelectronics, chromism, fluorescence probes, and energy materials. The unceasing breakthroughs in catalysis,9,45,115,154,166,167 magnetic complexes,155,168 molecular rotators (Fig. 8b),162 and high strength/stiffness nanothreads32,169,170 are worthy of further investigation and devotion (Fig. 1b).
