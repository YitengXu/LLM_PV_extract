Abstract

Halide perovskite single crystal of cubic HC(NH2)2PbI3 (FAPbI3) having excellent optoelectronic properties, such as narrow bandgap, large absorption coefficient and superior thermal stability has caught a surge of attention as a promising material for production of high-performance optoelectronic devices. However, at room temperature, the self-transformation of cubic FAPbI3 perovskite phase to non-perovskite phase leaves a critical roadblock to its practical viability. Herein, a simple alloying strategy by mixing methylammonium with formamidinium is developed to stabilize the FAPbI3 perovskite phase, hence achieving a highly stable mixed cation MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 perovskite single crystal over a span of 14 months. The MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal exhibits exceptional optoelectronic properties like high carrier mobility of 271 Â± 60 cm2 sâˆ’1 Vâˆ’1 and long diffusion length up to 254 Î¼m, which are twice the values for sole MAPbI3 or FAPbI3 crystals. In addition, the photodetector based on MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal exhibits low detection limit of about 1 nW cmâˆ’2, high ONâ€“OFF ratio of âˆ¼1000, short response time of less than 200 Î¼s, and impressive stability under aging in dark for 4 months or continuous photo-switching test for 1000 s.

Introduction

Hybrid organicâ€“inorganic perovskites have been recognized as one of the most promising and stunning semiconductor materials by virtue of their remarkable optoelectronic properties, such as high absorption coefficient,1,2 tunable direct bandgap3 and long carrier diffusion length,4â€“7 which consequently thrust their extensive applications in solar cells,8â€“10 photocatalysis,11 photodetectors,12â€“15 lasers,16 light-emitting diodes,17 as well as field-effect transistors.18 Thus far, the overwhelming majority of perovskite devices are based upon polycrystalline films. However, they suffer immensely from a high density of traps and grain boundaries, implying an underlying roadblock to their further applications.4 In contrast, single crystals have been acknowledged to outperform polycrystalline films in yielding high-performance optoelectronic devices, attributing to their higher crystallinity, fewer defects, absence of grain boundaries and superior stability.13,19â€“21 Thus, fabricating perovskite single crystal is highly anticipated to realise the full potential of perovskite materials in building up high-performance optoelectronic devices.

Although CH3NH3PbI3 (MAPbI3) single crystal, possessing an exceptionally long carrier diffusion length over 175 Î¼m,4 has been the widely studied perovskite material, HC(NH2)2PbI3 (FAPbI3) is regarded as a more promising optoelectronic material for extended absorption spectrum and better thermal stability.22,23 The critical issue for FAPbI3 is that the perovskite structure of FAPbI3 (cubic phase, Î±-FAPbI3) is thermodynamically unstable at room temperature (RT), and thus spontaneously converts to a non-perovskite structure (hexagonal phase, Î´-FAPbI3) with low mobility and conductivity, particularly in the presence of polar solvent vapour such as water or Î³-butyrolactone.23,24 Hence, stabilizing the perovskite phase of FAPbI3 at RT leaves a burning question for contemporary studies. Recently, compositional engineering by incorporating smaller ions (such as CH3NH3+, Cs+, Rb+, Brâˆ’)25â€“31 into FAPbI3 structure has been verified to stabilize the cubic structure, which is attributed to the fine adjustment of Goldschmidt tolerance factor26 or the entropy increase induced by cation mixing.28 Accordingly, the mixed organic cation perovskite single crystals are highly expected to attain advanced optoelectronic performance since their polycrystalline thin films have outperformed pure MA or FA compounds in solar cells.31â€“35

Herein, the MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal was fabricated via a simple inverse temperature crystallization approach, presenting not only impressive long-term stability over a span of 14 months but also superior optoelectronic properties. Comparing with pure MAPbI3 and FAPbI3, higher mobility (271 Â± 60 cm2 sâˆ’1 Vâˆ’1), longer carrier lifetime (93 Î¼s) and ultra-long carrier diffusion length (254 Î¼m) were observed for the perovskite alloy single crystal, which are of critical importance with regard to highly efficient photodetectors. A desirable photodetector based on MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal was achieved, exhibiting short response time of less than 200 Î¼s, wide linear dynamic range of 136.3 dB, and exceptional stability after storage in an opaque desiccator for 4 months. In addition, the response spectrum of the single crystal photodetector at zero bias displayed a narrow single peak near the absorption edge while intriguingly changing to a broadband from visible to near-infrared at high bias, indicating a dual-functional photodetector.

Experimental

Synthesis of perovskite single crystals

The perovskite single crystals were grown via a modified inverse temperature crystallization method.36 In brief, 1.0 M MAPbI3/FAPbI3 perovskite precursor solutions were prepared by dissolving equimolar PbI2 and MAI/FAI in Î³-butyrolactone at 60 Â°C overnight, whereas the mixed cation perovskite precursor solution was blended with MAPbI3 and FAPbI3 precursor solutions in a ratio of 1:1. The solutions were filtered using PTFE filter with 0.2 Î¼m pore size. Seed crystals of 0.5â€“1 mm in size were first obtained by placing 2 mL precursor solutions at 160 Â°C for 30 min in an oil bath under ambient conditions. For MAPbI3 or mixed cation perovskite alloy single crystals, a corresponding seed crystal was placed into the fresh precursor solution that was heated at 120 Â°C for 3 hours to obtain crystal growth. To further increase the crystal size, the temperature was set to 125 Â°C for another hour. For FAPbI3 single crystals, similar procedure was implemented except the growth temperature, which was initially kept at 110 Â°C for 3 hours and then at 115 Â°C for another hour. The as-obtained large size single crystals were taken out from the hot precursor solutions, and then the residual solution was removed from their surfaces by clean room papers. Single crystals of âˆ¼8 mm in size were mechanically polished along the (100) direction to a thickness of âˆ¼1 mm and were further analysed for carrier transport and lifetime measurements, and used for the fabrication of photodetectors. All procedures were conducted under ambient conditions with humidity of 45â€“55%.

Characterization of perovskite single crystals

The powder XRD measurement was performed using a Rigaku MiniFlex X-ray diffractometer operated at room temperature. The temperature-dependent XRD data was collected using Rigaku SmartLab, comprising a high-temperature oven chamber (Anton Paar HTK 1200N), with a step size of 0.01Â° and a speed of 0.2 s per step. Data collection at each temperature was carried out 5 minutes after stabilizing at the corresponding temperature. The 1H NMR spectra were collected at 298 K using Bruker 600 AVANAC III spectrometer equipped with Bruker BBO multinuclear probe (BrukerBioSpin, Rheinstetten, Germany), and the spectra were analyzed using MNova software. 5 mg of the perovskite single crystals were dissolved in d6-DMSO, and the MA/FA ratio within each was calculated by comparison of the integrals of the CH3 group peak of MA+ (2.34, d) with the CH group peaks of FA+ (7.83, tt). Thermogravimetric analysis was performed on a TGA 209 (NETZSCH) system in an interval from 30 Â°C to 700 Â°C at a ramp rate of 10 Â°C minâˆ’1 under N2 gas with a flow rate of 20 mL minâˆ’1. The UV-vis absorption spectra were recorded on a Shimadzu UV-5600 UV-vis spectrometer with an integrated sphere in diffuse-reflectance mode. The steady and transient state photoluminescence was recorded using the Edinburgh Instruments FLS980 Spectrofluorometer with an excitation wavelength of 472.0 nm.

The carrier transport properties of single crystals were estimated by space-charge-limited current (SCLC) technique. Hole-only devices were fabricated by sputtering deposition of 80 nm gold electrodes on the top and bottom surfaces of âˆ¼1 mm-thick crystal plates. For transient photovoltaic analysis, a semi-transparent 25 nm-thick Au layer was deposited on âˆ¼1 mm-thick crystal plates, followed by attachment of the other side onto glass substrates using molten metallic Ga.

Single crystal photodetector fabrication and characterization

A semitransparent Au layer of 25 nm was deposited by sputtering on one (100) surface of the single crystal plates as the top electrode, and 60 nm Ti/80 nm Au was deposited successively on the opposite surface. The photocurrent response characteristics for the single crystal photodetectors were observed using a CHI660E electrochemical workstation in a shield box. The external quantum efficiency spectra were measured on a Keithley 2400 SourceMeter, as a function of wavelength from 400 nm to 1000 nm using a Spectral Products DK240 monochromator. The temporal response of the devices was measured under pulsed light from a function-generator-modulated 870 nm LED (âˆ¼1 mW cmâˆ’2) at different frequencies, and the transient photocurrent signal under âˆ’4 V bias was recorded using a digital oscilloscope (Tektronix TDS 2014C).

Results and discussion

Three species of perovskite single crystals (MAPbI3, Î±-FAPbI3 as well as mixed cation alloy) were grown in air via a modified inverse temperature crystallization method (see details in the Experimental section). To determine the precise MA+/FA+ ratio of the mixed cation perovskite crystals, 1H NMR spectra (seen in Fig. S1â€ ) were observed by comparing the integrals of the CH3 group peak of MA+ (2.34, d) with the CH peaks of FA+ (7.83, tt), yielding a MA+/FA+ ratio of 0.45:0.55, which was slightly deviated from the feed ratio, possibly attributed to the lower solubility of FAPbI3.37 Thus, the formula MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 is used hereafter to refer to the mixed-cation perovskite crystals. As shown in Fig. 1a, the as-prepared three single crystals of âˆ¼8 mm in size exhibited rhombo-hexagonal dodecahedron or rhombic dodecahedron shapes with similar metallic black colour. After exposure in air with 45â€“55% relative humidity (RH) for a span of 14 months, the MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal still maintained the original black colour. However, the Î±-FAPbI3 crystal turned yellow and MAPbI3 single crystal exhibited pale yellow spots on the surface (Fig. 1b), implying a superior long-term stability for mixed cation MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal compared to FAPbI3 and MAPbI3. Such a result was further validated by powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD) patterns described in Fig. 1c and d. Primarily, the MAPbI3 and FAPbI3 crystals displayed a satisfying match with tetragonal and cubic phase, without any impurity, respectively.38,39 Moreover, the MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 presented PXRD patterns similar to those of Î±-FAPbI3 with slightly enlarged diffraction angles caused by MA+ incorporation, indicating a desired cubic perovskite phase as that of Î±-FAPbI3. After being stored in air (RH 45â€“55%) for 14 months, the FAPbI3 single crystal had almost entirely converted from pristine Î±-phase to Î´-phase (yellow color) due to the thermodynamically unstable structure of Î±-FAPbI3. For MAPbI3, the detectable PbI2 impurity in PXRD pattern was derived from significant degradation on crystal surface, resulting from the hygroscopic nature of methylammonium. It is noteworthy that the structure of MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 had remained unchanged (Fig. 1d), proving the advanced long-term stability by alloying FA+ with MA+.

Conclusions

In summary, we demonstrated the fabrication of alloyed MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal and its utilization as a satisfying photodetector, achieving long-term stability and prominent carrier transport properties. Compared with pure MAPbI3 and FAPbI3 single crystals, the MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal maintained impressive stability over a span of 14 months in ambient air owing to its thermodynamically stable structure and reduced defect density. In addition, the mixed cation MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 perovskite single crystal has also been found to possess twice the carrier diffusion length (254 Î¼m) than pure MAPbI3 or FAPbI3 single crystal. Thus, by virtue of higher carrier mobility and lower trap density, the MA0.45FA0.55PbI3 single crystal photodetector achieved a short response time less than 200 Î¼s, a high ONâ€“OFF ratio of âˆ¼1000 and a low detection limit of 1 nW cmâˆ’2. Overall, given that fabricating mixed cation perovskite single crystal can evade the respective drawbacks of each pure one as well as improve the optoelectronic properties simultaneously, it is worthy to anticipate breakthroughs that are more significant and explore wider applications in future optoelectronic devices with alloyed perovskite single crystals.
