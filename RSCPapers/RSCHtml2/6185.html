Abstract

Nickel foam (NF) can be directly utilized as an electrochemical electrode because of its conductive, rigid and porous structure. Herein, a novel robust binder-free Feâ€“Ni hydroxide-loaded NF electrode is prepared by simple corrosion of NF in a home-made piranha solution with an Fe3+ precursor. The roughened NF surface observed after the sample treatment indicates the formation of active species associated with amorphous Fe-hydroxide and/or FeNi-hydroxide particles. The piranha solution-corroded NF electrode generates more hydroxide groups compared to the water-corroded NF electrode, and exhibits remarkable electrochemical oxygen evolution reaction activity in alkaline medium with a low overpotential of 245 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2. It also maintains low working potential below 267 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 over 500 h operation without any sign of degradation, indicating excellent long-term stability, which can be attributed to the robust binder-free electrode and the cooperative synergistic interaction between Fe-hydroxides and Ni-hydroxides in the FeNi hybrid composite of individually incompetent OER components, generated over the NF. The role of Fe doped in Ni(OH)2 is theoretically studied by DFT calculations, and it is found that the adsorption free energies of OH* and OOH* are significantly reduced by the addition of Fe into Ni(OH)2, accelerating the OER compared to bare Ni(OH)2. Furthermore, when the active electrode is equipped with a solar cell, a high current density of 23â€“24 mA cmâˆ’2 is observed for 10 h under AM 1.5G 1 sun irradiation, suggesting high possibility for the production of green hydrogen from renewable solar energy.

Introduction

With the rapid development of human society, various environmental problems like global warming,1 and air2 and water pollution3 have arisen, threatening our life and society. Many suggestions from the scientific community have been discussed, and use of renewable energy has been suggested as the most suitable solution to these problems.4â€“6 Regarding renewable energy, hydrogen has been widely considered as the most efficient, clean and renewable energy carrier for the future as it is a zero-emission fuel when burned with oxygen since water is the only product of the reaction. It can be used in internal combustion engines or electrochemical cells to power vehicles or to get electricity. Fuel cells using hydrogen as a fuel have been considered as the most promising alternative to fossil combustion engines because of high energy efficiency and negligible pollutant emission from the reaction.

Hydrogen gas can be obtained from petroleum cracking, bio-mass or electrochemical water splitting.7â€“12 The electrochemical water splitting is considered as the most clean and efficient way of generating hydrogen without production of other pollutants when combined with renewable energy sources like sun and wind power. The electrochemical water splitting comprises two reactions: one is the cathodic hydrogen evolution reaction (HER), and the other is the anodic oxygen evolution reaction (OER). These HER and OER for water splitting can be achieved in photocatalytic, electrocatalytic or photoelectrocatalytic systems.9,13â€“19 The OER is considered as the bottleneck for the overall reaction because of the high overpotential and the poor stability of the catalysts. These issues have encouraged a lot of researchers to develop efficient OER catalysts with low overpotential and long-lasting durability.20â€“29

Iridium oxide (IrO2) and ruthenium oxide (RuO2) in terms of electrochemical water splitting, have been known as reference state-of-the-art catalysts for the OER, but they are too expensive to be commercialized on an industrial scale.30â€“33 Recently, to overcome this issue, composites of non-precious metal elements consisting of Ni, Fe, and Co and their oxides, hydroxides or (oxy)hydroxides have been studied as promising low-cost OER catalysts. Xu et al. prepared porous NiCo@NiCoO2 micro arrays constructed on carbon fibers.34 NiCo@NiCoO2 was prepared by a hydrothermal reaction generating a metal organic framework (MOF) followed by carbonization. The overpotential recorded was 366 mV at 20 mA cmâˆ’2. Qiu et al. synthesized Ni0.6Co1.4P nanocages from Cu2O templates through metal oxide phosphidation.35 They used Pearson's hard soft acid base (HSAB) principle for making Ni0.6Co1.4(OH)2 and then phosphorized this to the alloy metal phosphide. The overpotential was 300 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2. Yu et al. synthesized ultrathin Niâ€“Fe layered double hydroxide (LDH) nanosheets from a solid nickel prism precursor with hydrolysis of Fe2+, which showed an overpotential of 267 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 and high stability in alkaline solution.36 However, most of the Feâ€“Ni hydroxide catalysts are prepared in powder form and thus require an extra interface with a binder (Nafion) on the electrode, resulting in the lack of direct electrical contact between catalysts and the substrate and hence poor electrical conductivity.20,22,37â€“40

Meanwhile, metals in the form of foams and foils have been directly used as binder-free water splitting electrodes with modification by a hydrothermal reaction or electrochemical deposition of other transition metals as well as chalcogenization using oxygen, sulfur and selenium.41â€“48 For example, Ren et al. reported Fe-doped Ni(OH)2 sheets on nickel foam (NF) by a simple hydrothermal reaction.49 It was used as a bifunctional electrode for the HER and OER. The overpotential of the cell was 440 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 for overall water splitting. Zhang et al. prepared NiFe-LDH on Ni2P@NiFe hydroxide.50 The catalyst was fabricated by phosphidation of NiFe-LDH grown on NF by a hydrothermal reaction, and showed an overpotential of 210 mV at a current density of 10 mA cmâˆ’2 in 1.0 M KOH.

Another promising approach is to use corrosion engineering for self-oxidation of the metal substrate followed by reaction with other active transition metals. Wei et al. reported that the binder-free open petal-like FeNiâ€“OH nanosheets over NF was prepared by simple immersion of NF into a solution mixture of FeCl3 and NiCl2.51 The as-prepared catalyst required an overpotential of 219 mV to achieve a current density of 100 mA cmâˆ’2. The excellent OER activity was attributed to the increased hydrophilicity induced by the FeNiâ€“OH structure generated on the NF. Liu et al. used corrosion engineering on an iron substrate with aqueous solution containing various metal ions (Ni2+, Co2+, Mn2+â€¦).52 The OER performance was well maintained for 6000 h below 300 mV at 500 mA cmâˆ’2 without any degradation. Yin et al. found that metallic Ni in NF was oxidized to Ni2+ in ferric nitrate aqueous solution which reacted with Fe2+ ions finally to generate active NiFexOHy layers on the surface for high OER activity.53

In this study, a binder-free hybrid electrode of Feâ€“Ni hydroxide composites is in situ prepared for the first time from NF with Fe(NO3)3 as an Fe precursor in a home-made piranha solution, which is a 3:1 mixture of sulfuric acid (H2SO4) and hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) as a reaction medium. Interestingly, in the presence of the piranha solution, the oxidation of NF is accelerated, while Fe3+ ions are transformed into Fe-hydroxide nanoparticles (NPs) on the NF. The identical synthesis is conducted in the presence of water instead of piranha solution to prepare a control sample. The final products are examined by various analyses and evaluated for the electrochemical OER. The sample prepared in piranha solution is found to show much better OER performance than ones prepared in water, H2SO4 and H2O2. The former shows a low overpotential of 245 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 and remarkable durability over 500 h.

OER catalytic performance

The electrochemical OER was carried out in 1.0 M KOH electrolyte solution, and the effect of reaction medium on the OER performance in the presence of Fe species was investigated in Fig. 4. The preparation method of the RuO2 reference catalyst is described in the Experimental section. Fig. 4a shows the linear sweep voltammetry (LSV) curves measured for different samples, where the pre-wave at 1.35â€“1.40 V indicates the oxidation of Ni2+ to Ni3+, and NFâ€“Feâ€“H2SO4 shows a higher pre-wave peak intensity than other samples probably due to the relatively high Ni2+ ratio and high N content (Table S1, ESIâ€ ) and relatively more formation of Mâ€“OH (Fig. S7dâ€ ). NFâ€“Feâ€“PS presents the best OER performance with an overpotential of 245 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 among the samples. It also displays a Tafel slope of 82.9 mV decâˆ’1 as shown in Fig. 4b. This catalytic performance is found to be much better than that of the state-of-the-art RuO2. NFâ€“Feâ€“H2SO4 and NFâ€“Feâ€“H2O2 display better OER performance than NFâ€“Feâ€“W. All the samples with Fe species also outperform RuO2, while NFâ€“PS, NFâ€“W, NFâ€“H2SO4 and NFâ€“H2O2 without Fe species reveal poor OER performance as shown in Fig. S10 (ESIâ€ ). Interestingly, the NF-based catalysts prepared in different reaction media without the Fe precursor exhibit similar poor OER activity, not much different from that of bare NF as shown in Fig. S10a (ESIâ€ ), indicating that the reaction medium has little effect on the OER activity in the absence of Fe. Furthermore, the Fe-treated catalysts show much lower Tafel slopes compared to the catalysts without Fe species as seen in Fig. S10b (ESIâ€ ). The catalysts without Fe species also display similar high Tafel slopes, not much different from that of the bare NF. This clearly indicates that Ni oxides or Ni hydroxides alone are not efficient without Fe species. Such a high OER performance of the NFâ€“Feâ€“PS sample is attributed to synergistic interactions between Fe-hydroxide particles and Fe-decorated Ni substrate or Fe-hydroxides and Ni-hydroxides in the FexNi(OH)y hybrids generated on the surface of NFâ€“Feâ€“PS. Moreover, the stabilization of Fe4+ by Ni(OH)2 was reported to be a key factor for high OER activity.60 It is also reported that FexNi(OH)y may be helpful for the stabilization of Fe4+.

NFâ€“Feâ€“PS after the stability test at different times was also analyzed by XPS to investigate the chemical structure changes. Fig. S16 (ESIâ€ ) shows the XPS spectra of the NFâ€“Feâ€“PS sample before and after the long-term test at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 as illustrated in Fig. 4c. During such morphology conversion from particles to thin layers, the rearrangement between Fe and Ni takes place, and excessive amount of Fe may be leached out. Finally, Ni hydroxide with a low doping level of Fe is formed as a stable and highly active catalyst. There has been debates on which metal species is the true active site in the Niâ€“Fe hydroxides for the OER. The Fe(OH)3 powder was prepared to investigate its activity in the OER. Conclusions

In summary, highly active and remarkably stable Niâ€“Fe based binder-free OER electrocatalysts were prepared in situ for the first time by simple corrosion of NF in home-made piranha solution with an Fe precursor. The NF was immersed in the piranha solution to react with ferric nitrate. Both sulfuric acid and hydrogen peroxide in the piranha solution played synergetic roles in the formation of active Fe hydroxide particles and Feâ€“Ni hydroxides on the Ni foam. The resulting NFâ€“Feâ€“PS catalyst showed excellent OER performance with very low overpotential (245 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2) and exhibited outstanding long-term durability with retention of low overpotential below 267 mV over 500 h. This remarkable OER performance can be ascribed to the cooperative synergistic Feâ€“Ni interaction in the form of hydroxide, low charge transfer resistance associated with the binder-free catalyst and high electrochemical surface area of the robust and porous NF-based electrode. Interestingly, although the active Fe species continued to decrease during the long-term test of NFâ€“Feâ€“PS, the OER activity was maintained without significant decay, which implied that a small amount of Fe was enough to play the key role for superior OER activity and stability. The DFT calculations illustrated that Fe4+ species played key roles to reduce the unfavourable RDS free energy of the OER. Finally, a more practical solar cell-equipped water electrolyzer has been set up with the as-prepared NFâ€“Feâ€“PS anode and showed a very stable current density of 23â€“24 mA cmâˆ’2 under 1 sun irradiation, enabling the production of clean hydrogen from renewable solar energy. The present work demonstrates an innovative synthesis approach of low-cost, highly active, and robust binder-free Feâ€“Ni based composite electrocatalysts of individually incompetent OER components for scalable water splitting though a simple new corrosion method using piranha solution and can be extended to the synthesis of other novel multi-component-based catalysts over NF.
