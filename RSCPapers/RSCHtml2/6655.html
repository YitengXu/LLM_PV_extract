Abstract

Two new donorâ€“acceptor copolymers, namely PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8, based on difluorinated quinoxaline (Qx) acceptor units and alkylthiothienyl benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT) donor units with different alkylthio side chains were designed and synthesized. The two copolymers exhibit a lower-lying HOMO energy level and closer interchain packing than their analogues with alkyl side chains attributed to the more polarizable S atom inducing stronger intermolecular interactions. Of these two polymers, PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 with linear alkylthio side chains shows closer molecular stacking with PC71BM than PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH with branched alkylthio side chains. As a result, the polymer solar cell based on the blend of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 and PC71BM exhibited a higher PCE of 8.51% than that based on PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PC71BM, which was only 7.02%. More importantly, both copolymers show higher photovoltaic performances in as-cast devices than in those treated by additive or thermal annealing because an appropriate bulk morphology was formed during the process of spin-casting due to the strong interchain interactions of the polymers, which eliminates the need for solvent additives or thermal annealing.

Introduction

Polymer solar cells (PSCs) have attracted increasing attention due to their potential advantages of low cost, light weight, capability to be fabricated into flexible devices and easily realized roll-to-roll manufacturing. Nowadays, owing to developments in molecule designing, device fabricating and principle studying, the power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) of PSCs are being well improved. The single-junction bulk-heterojunction (BHJ) PSCs have achieved PCEs over 10%.1â€“5 In many reported BHJ PSCs, post solvent and thermal annealing are important methods for improving the organic solar cell (OSC) performance. But the residual solvent additives in the BHJ films will act as hole traps.6 And additives, such as 1,8-diiodooctane (DIO), will significantly reduce the device stability.7â€“9 Therefore, after using additives the devices should be treated by a secondary process to remove the additives.7,8 However, considering manufacturing challenges for future commercialization of solar cells, it is necessary to remove extra processes such as adding solvent additives and thermal annealing facilitating the processing and reducing the cost of production. Thus, designing and synthesizing novel polymer materials that exhibit high solar cell performance using simpler processing methods are highly desired.

In designing molecules, a widely used strategy is to construct electron donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) types of alternating copolymers with narrow bandgaps. Through the combination of different electron donor (D) units and acceptor (A) units, the energy levels, absorption spectra and many other characteristics of the copolymers can be easily tuned. In addition, introduction of different side chains into the copolymers can further adjust precisely the physical characteristics of copolymers such as solubility, light absorption, molecular packing, energy level, charge transport, etc. To date, flexible alkyl groups have been widely used as side chains in tuning the basic properties of the copolymers. The polymers with branched alkyl side chains generally exhibit higher solubility while the polymer analogues with linear alkyl side chains show closer inter-molecular packing and higher charge mobilities.10â€“14 Also, recent studies demonstrated that alkylthio side chains offer prominent advantages over their alkyl analogues. This is due to the sulfur atom with higher polarizability than the carbon atom and formation of a pÏ€(C)â€“dÏ€(S) orbital overlap where a divalent sulfur atom accepts a Ï€-electron from the aromatic group into its empty 3d-orbitals.15,16 In that case, conjugated molecules substituted by alkylthio groups could exhibit unique optoelectronic properties. For example, Huo et al.17 replaced the alkyl side chains of poly(3-hexylthiophene) (P3HT) with alkylthio side chains, and the resulting poly[(3-hexylthio)thiophene] (P3HST) exhibited a red-shifted max. absorption peak and a slightly lower highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) energy level, therefore resulting in a higher open-circuit voltage (Voc). Li et al.18 and Price et al.19 found that the alkylthio side chain of the benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT) unit enhanced the Voc and PCE of the relative device compared to its alkyl analogue, although the HOMO level was almost unchanged. Also, in the work of Chen et al.,20,21 the HOMO and max. absorption peak of oligomers showed little change and Voc was not improved by introducing the alkylthio side chains, but both short-circuit current density (Jsc) and PCE increased because of a better morphology. The most exciting results for alkylthio groups were achieved when they were linked to the conjugated side chains of copolymers. For example, Li et al.16 introduced branched alkylthio substituents into the conjugated side chains of the BDT unit, and a high PCE of 8.42% was achieved for the device based on the resulting copolymer PBDTT-S-TT which is associated with its low-lying HOMO and extended absorption spectra. Based on the same copolymer, Ye et al.22 synthesized a copolymer PBDT-TS1 by replacing branched alkylthio side chains with linear ones, and the PCE of the relative device was further improved to 9.48%. These reports indicated that using alkylthio groups to replace alkyl side chains of the polymers would improve the photovoltaic performance, though the conclusion about the effects of the alkylthio side chains on the HOMO level, Jsc and Voc obtained in the literature studies was a little different.

In addition, Yang et al.23,24 found that some low-Eg Dâ€“A types of polymers, such as PBDTT-DPP, PDPP3T, and PDTP-DFBT, usually do not require DIO to achieve moderate cell performance, because these polymers can undergo a good nanoscale bicontinuous phase separation with PC71BM spontaneously. It is generally believed that these low-Eg polymers possess a stronger interchain interaction, thus an appropriate bulk BHJ morphology can be formed during the spin-casting or perhaps as early as in the solution state, which allows these polymers to achieve high device performance without having to use post-solvent and thermal annealing steps. This indicates that adjusting the interchain interaction to increase the aggregation propensity of the polymers would be an effective strategy to get rid of the additives or thermal annealing steps. Following this design concept, Yang et al.25 recently synthesized a polymer PBDTSe-TT through selenium substitution on the PTB7-polymer. PBDTSe-TT exhibits enhanced Ï€â€“Ï€ interactions between molecules brought by the selenium atom, therefore achieving a high performance of 8.8% PCE without any additives. At the same time, Yue et al.26 reported a 2,1,3-benzothiadiazole-5,6-dicarboxylic imide-based polymer, and Nielsen et al.27 reported thieno[3,2-b][1]benzothiophene isoindigo-based polymers which also demonstrated the same cell behaviors as PBDTSe-TT because of their strong intermolecular interactions.

Our group has been working toward developing new Dâ€“A conjugated photovoltaic polymers. Very recently, we reported a series of Dâ€“A copolymers based on the alkylthienyl BDT donor unit and difluorinated quinoxaline (Qx) acceptor unit, and found that PBDT-DFQX-TT (whose molecular structure is depicted in Scheme 1) demonstrated the best photovoltaic performance.28 More interestingly, these Qx-based polymers all exhibited better performances for additive- and annealing-free devices than those treated with solvent additives and thermal annealing. On the basis of these previous findings, here, we propose that if the alkyl side chains on PBDT-DFQX-TT can be replaced with alkylthio side chains to further enhance the interchain interaction, the corresponding polymers might demonstrate improved performance for additive- and annealing-free devices. Therefore, with the intention to further improve the photovoltaic properties as well as to investigate the effects of alkylthio side chains on the properties of the copolymers, we designed and synthesized two new polymers PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 (shown in Scheme 1, compared with the formerly reported PBDT-DFQX-TT in ref. 28) by replacing alkyl side chains with branched or linear alkylthio side chains on the thienyl BDT units of PBDT-DFQX-TT. The results showed that the two copolymers exhibit a lower-lying HOMO energy level and closer interchain packing than PBDT-DFQX-TT attributed to the more polarizable S atom inducing stronger intermolecular interactions. The BHJ PSC based on PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH achieves a high performance of 8.51% PCE without solvent additives or thermal annealing, whereas the highest PCE of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 device is 7.02%. As expected, both are higher than the devices treated with additives or annealing. These results demonstrate that introducing alkylthio substituents to enhance intermolecular interactions could be an effective strategy for developing new polymers with high performance for additive- and annealing-free devices.

Results and discussion

Synthesis and characterization

Scheme 2 shows the synthetic routes to the copolymers. Monomers (4,8-bis(5-((2-ethylhexyl)thio)thiophen-2-yl)benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene-2,6-diyl)bis(trimethylstannane) (M1), (4,8-bis(5-(octylthio)thiophen-2-yl)benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene-2,6-diyl)bis(trimethylstannane) (M2), and 5,8-bis(5-bromothiophen-2-yl)-2,3-bis(5-(2-ethylhexyl)thiophen-2-yl)-6,7-difluoroquinoxaline (M3) were synthesized according to previously reported procedures.16,22,28,29 All the compounds including 1â€“8 and M1â€“3 were analyzed by 1H-NMR and 13C-NMR. PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 were prepared by the Stille coupling reaction with Pd2(dba)3 and P(o-tol)3 as the catalyst and refluxed continuously under argon protection. The crude polymers were purified by continuous extractions with methanol, hexanes, chloroform and chlorobenzene. The polymers PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 were recovered from the chlorobenzene fraction. The polymerization conditions, including the types and dosages of the catalysts, reaction temperature, reaction times, etc., were controlled carefully to ensure that the acquired polymers have similar molecular weights. The molecular weights of the copolymers were measured by GPC in the trichlorobenzene eluent at 150 Â°C using polystyrene standards. From the GPC results (see ESI Fig. S1â€ ), one can see that the number-average molecular weights (Mn) of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 were 45.9 and 40.3 kDa, with a polydispersity (D) of 3.69 and 2.90, respectively. Obviously, differences of the two polymers in Mn and PDI are very small, which is beneficial for the comparative study on the effect of side chains. In addition, a high MW peak at a relatively short retention time can be observed from the GPC plots of the two polymers, which has been proved to be the residues of previous samples because the GPC testing involves continuous delivery of samples. Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) (Fig. S2â€ ) showed that PBDT-DFQx-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQx-TTSC8 were thermally stable with onset decomposition temperatures corresponding to 5% weight loss at 348 and 381 Â°C respectively. Obviously, both the polymers were stable enough for application in PSCs.

Optical properties

Fig. 1(a) and (b) show the absorption spectra of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 in chlorobenzene solution and as a thin film coated on quartz glass, and the relative optical data are summarized in Table 1, including the absorption peak wavelengths (Î»max), absorption edge wavelengths (Î»onset), and the optical band gaps (Eoptg). For the convenience of comparison, the absorption spectra of PBDT-DFQX-TT in chlorobenzene solution and in the thin film are also given in Fig. 1(a) and (b). It should be noted that all of the polymers have three characteristic absorption bands in the range of 300â€“700 nm as a solution or a film. Amongst them, absorption in the wavelength range of 450â€“550 nm arises from the Ï€â€“Ï€* localized transition, and absorption in 500â€“700 nm is attributed to intramolecular charge transfer (ICT) between the donor and acceptor units.30,31 In comparison with PBDT-DFQX-TT, PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 showed slight absorbance extension in both the solution state and solid state as a result of the pÏ€(C)â€“dÏ€(S) orbital overlap where a divalent sulfur atom accepts a Ï€ electron from the aromatic group into its empty 3d-orbitals.15,16 For PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8, the Î»max of both polymers in thin film absorption spectra appeared evidently red shifted compared with that in solution, indicating a stronger aggregation of the polymer main chain and a stronger Ï€â€“Ï€ intermolecular interaction in the solid state. Well defined vibronic shoulder absorption peaks in the long wavelength range (600â€“700 nm) of both polymers implied the existence of ordered aggregation and strong Ï€â€“Ï€ stacking.16 The peak at around 590 nm can be assigned as 0â€“1 vibronic transition and around 640 nm as 0â€“0. The dominant 0â€“0 vibronic peak is indicative of extended J-aggregate-like polymer chains that pack in ordered lamellae, whereas the 0â€“1 is of H-aggregate-like. The H-aggregate-like polymer has a side-by-side arrangement of chains while the J-aggregate-like polymer has a head-to-tail arrangement. PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TT have stronger 0â€“0 peaks indicating that the two polymers tend to form a J-aggregate arrangement whereas PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 has a stronger 0â€“1 peak and tends to form an H-aggregate arrangement. In general, polymers tend to form an H-aggregate-like arrangement which is beneficial for intermolecular charge mobility. The polymer possessing H-aggregate-like inter-chain packing would exhibit better charge mobilities, Jsc and PCEs for its devices.32â€“34Fig. 1(c) and (d) are the absorption spectra of the polymer:PC71BM = 1:1.2 (w/w) blending film. The absorptions in the short wavelength range of the thin films are improved evidently by blending with PC71BM, indicating that PC71BM can complement the absorptions of the two polymers. Obviously, the red-shifted spectra of the polymers in the solid state and the complementary absorption spectra with PC71BM in the blend films show desirable behaviours as donor materials for application in PSCs.

Cyclic voltammetry (CV) was performed to measure the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) energy levels of the conjugated polymers. The LUMO energy levels of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 are âˆ’3.46 and âˆ’3.47 eV, respectively. Compared to PBDT-DFQX-TT, the LUMO energy levels of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 are little up-shifted. Theoretical calculation

To further study the fundamentals of molecular architecture, theoretical calculations using the density functional theory (DFT) methods were carried out to optimize the molecular geometry and to investigate the frontier orbital and molecular electronic structure at the B3LYP/6-31 G(d,p) level with the Gaussian09 program package. The molecular main chain length was n = 1. The alkyl chains were replaced with methyl groups to save the computation time. The simplified molecules were referred to as PBDT-DFQX-TTS (for PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 because of the similar backbone) and PBDT-DFQX-TT. The frontier molecular orbitals of PBDT-DFQX-TTS and PBDT-DFQX-TT, the corresponding LUMO and HOMO levels, and the dipole moment of the chain elements are shown in Fig. 2. As can be observed, the HOMOs are delocalized along the whole backbone, while the LUMOs are mostly concentrated on the Qx acceptor units. These images provide clear evidence of the formation of a well-defined Dâ€“A structure and the ICT behaviours of the polymers (i.e., the HOMO to LUMO transition is a donor to acceptor intramolecular charge transfer). The calculated HOMO energy level of PBDT-DFQX-TTS is lower than that of PBDT-DFQX-TT, which is consistent with the results obtained from the CV measurements. The ground-state dipole moment magnitude (Î¼g) of PBDT-DFQX-TT and PBDT-DFQX-TTS was 5.95 and 6.41D. The higher Î¼g of PBDT-DFQX-TTS indicates that introducing a sulfur atom to the flexible side chain can improve the molecular polarizability. In addition, we noted that the HOMO energy level of PBDT-DFQX-TTS simulated by the DFT calculation seems significantly higher (by âˆ¼0.5 eV) than the experimental observation. The reason for such a deviation is that we adopted a monomer model (the number of repeat units is one) for simplifying the DFT calculation. But for the CV measurements, the experimental observation represented the real results of the whole polymer.

Photovoltaic properties

The device structures of the PSCs for characterizing the photovoltaic properties of the polymers were ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PC71BM/ZrAcac/Al. The polymer and PC71BM are dissolved in o-DCB together for spin-coating. The detailed device fabrication process is described in the Experimental section. The measurements of photovoltaic performances were carried out under an illumination of AM1.5G simulated solar light at 100 mW cmâˆ’2. The optimal performance data of the devices, including open-circuit voltage (Voc), short circuit current (Jsc), fill factor (FF), and power conversion efficiency (PCE) under the conditions of polymer:PC71BM = 1:1.2 (w/w) and different processing techniques are summarized in Table 2. Table S1â€  shows the device performance data of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH under other conditions not listed in Table 2. Fig. S5â€  shows the representative current densityâ€“voltage (Jâ€“V) curves of the best devices. Considering the fact that there were some literature studies reporting that the commercially available zirconium acetylacetonate (ZrAcac) as a cathode interfacial layer (CIL) can efficiently enhance the PCEs of the PSCs attributed to the well matching energy level of ZrAcac and the Al electrode, and benefiting to efficient charge extraction,35,36 here ZrAcac was also used as a CIL to fabricate the device by spin-coating methanol solution on its active layer at room temperature and without thermal annealing or any other post-treatment. As a result, the PCEs of the devices for PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 were 7.02 and 8.51%, respectively. In particular, the Jsc of the PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 device was remarkably higher than PBDT-DFQX-TT and PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH, rendering the devices rather higher PCEs. Further treatment of adding 3% (v/v) 1,8-diiodooctane (DIO) as an additive into the solution for device fabrication caused Jsc values of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 obviously increased but that of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH reduced. In particular, DIO treatment resulted in a dramatic decrease in Vocs and FFs of the two devices, therefore the PCEs were compromised by low Vocs and FFs. Relating to the AFM and GIXS results, the drop in the performance can be attributed to the fact that the DIO further promoted the PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 and PC71BM self-aggregation, therefore forming a larger improper phased domain and unfavorable blend film morphology. In addition, thermal annealing (TA) treatment on the devices also failed to improve their performance. The PCEs of the two devices were slightly decreased after thermal annealing (TA) treatment at 140 Â°C for 10 min. The device of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH showed a great drop in FF but a slight increase in Jsc, while that of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 demonstrated a little drop in both Voc and Jsc, which resulted in a decline in the PCEs of the two devices, indicating that thermal annealing changed the film morphologies to an undesirable status.

High performance solar cells depend on high hole mobilities. To investigate the charge transport properties of the polymer/PC71BM blends, a space charge limited current (SCLC) method was used to measure the hole mobilities of the polymers. The hole-only devices were measured with a device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/active layer/Au. The hole (Î¼h) mobilities of the devices with different treatments are summarized in Table 3. The DIO treatment devices of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 showed an increase in hole mobilities and accorded with the Jsc increase trend, while PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH showed a drop in Î¼h. However, the decreased Voc and FF, which were caused by the morphology change after treatment, suppressed the PCE increase after treatment with DIO. As Table 3 shows, the hole mobilities of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 were decreased after thermal annealing and the reduced mobilities led to lower Jsc and PCEs. In contrast, the hole mobilities of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH were increased after thermal annealing, in line with the Jsc increase. The hole mobilities of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH were in total lower than that of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8, which may be one of the reasons that the devices of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 show higher performance than that of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH fabricated under the optimized conditions. However, the hole mobility of PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 is nearly about one order of magnitude lower than the previously reported parent polymer PBDT-DFQX-TT;28 our experiments in the later section showed that the photovoltaic performance of the former is better than the latter. These results indicated that when the hole mobility of the polymers has reached as high as 10âˆ’4 to 10âˆ’3, the hole mobility will no longer be the main factor affecting their photovoltaic performance.

Charge recombination is also an important factor in determining the Jsc and FF of the PSCs. We used light-intensity dependent photocurrent measurements to study the charge recombination behaviour of polymers PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 in devices. In general, the relationship between the Jsc and illumination intensity (P) can be described as Jsc âˆ PÎ±, where the power-law exponent (Î±) should be approximated to 1 when charge recombination can be neglected.37,38 The measuring devices were fabricated with the structure ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PC71BM/ZrAcac/Al and without any other treatment. As we can see in Fig. 4, the Î± values for PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 are 0.958 and 1.000, respectively. High values of Î± suggested that bimolecular recombination was significantly suppressed. Higher Î± values of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 can be one of the reasons that PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 exhibited higher Jsc and FF than PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH.

The photon energy loss (Eloss) in PSCs is generally shown as Eloss = Eg, blend âˆ’ eVoc, where Eg, blend is the lowest optical band gap of the donor material and acceptor material in the blend film, and the empirical maximum value of Eloss is 0.6 eV.39,40 PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH under optimal conditions showed an Eloss = 0.88 eV while the Eloss of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 was 0.87 eV. The devices of PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 having lower Eloss, higher EQE and lower charge recombination than PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH should be prior factors to PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 devices achieving higher photovoltaic performance.

Conclusions

In this work, two new copolymers PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 with branched or linear alkylthio side chains, respectively, were designed and synthesized. Replacing alkyl with alkylthio side chains is shown to down-shift the HOMO energy levels of the polymers and slightly red-shift the absorption peaks, and to enhance the interchain interaction, therefore affecting the BHJ morphology with PC71BM greatly. The polymer PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH with branched alkylthio side chains possesses a lower HOMO energy level while PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 with linear alkylthio side chains exhibits higher hole mobilities. Optimization of the PSC fabrication conditions provided a maximal PCE of 7.02% for PBDT-DFQX-TTSEH and 8.51% for PBDT-DFQX-TTSC8 through a simplified processing procedure without any additives and thermal annealing. The high cell performance of the as-cast devices originates mainly from the fact that an appropriate bulk BHJ morphology has been formed during the spin-casting due to a strong interchain interaction. This is the first report on Qx-based polymers for additive- and annealing-free high-performance polymer solar cells. The results of this work provide a new approach for developing polymers with high cell performance without using extra post-treatment steps, which will enable the cost-effective fabrication of efficient polymer solar cells via high-throughput printing technologies.
