Abstract

Soluble perylene diimide-based copolymers with conjugated electronic pushâ€“pull substituent species (piperidinyl and 4-tert-butylphenoxyl) on the 1,7-bay position of the perylene core and a 5,5â€²-bis(4-aminophenyl)-2-2â€²-bifuryl as terminal group were synthesized and characterized in order to fabricate bulk heterojunction solar cells. The introduction of such electronic pushâ€“pull substituents, which results in relatively low-lying HOMO and high-lying LUMO energy levels, was achieved experimentally using absorption and cyclic voltammetric measurements and also by density functional theory. Successfully fine-tuned HOMO and LUMO energy levels translated into a high open circuit voltage (Voc > 1.0 V) in fabricated photovoltaic devices of type ITO/PEDOT:PSS/rr-P3HT:Perylene diimide-based copolymers/Ca/Al. However, a relatively poor fill factor and efficiency may be observed due to the large degree of aggregation domains, which are observed from atomic force microscopic studies.

Introduction

As the importance of renewable energy sources grows, the development of highly efficient organic photovoltaic cells is becoming of increasing relevance for electrical energy production.1,2 For the construction of solar energy conversion systems, many research efforts have been focused on the exploration of molecular photoelectronic devices for application in photochemical energy conversion, which requires the design of light-harvesting and electron transporting capacity bichromophoric and multichromophoric systems that undergo photoinduced intramolecular charge transfer processes.3 For this purpose, recent developments in the field of organic photovoltaics have boosted great interest in the development of a new class of photofunctional materials and multifunctional organic dyes or pigments exhibiting broad light-harvesting properties in the near-infrared (NIR) region, which is highly desirable to attain efficient solar energy conversion processes.1,4 In this regard, recent synthetic strategies have been developed for design and synthesis of efficient light harvesting materials based on electronic pushâ€“pull Ï€-conjugated systems combined with an electron donorâ€“acceptor pair, which lead to feasible photoinduced charge generation in bichromophoric and multichromophoric systems5 which exhibit broad absorption in the near-infrared (NIR) region as one of the key components to construct high performance solar cells.6â€“8 In addition to near-infrared (NIR) light-harvesting capability, high photostability and thermal durability,9 distinct redox properties,10 specific carrier injection-tuning properties,11 excellent electron acceptor ability,12 a large molar extinction coefficient in the wavelength range of 480â€“550 nm13 and unity fluorescence quantum yields14 are also key factors to attain efficient solar energy conversion processes. Among the different functional materials available,15 researchers have made efforts to modify perylene diimide based dyes with versatile functionalized electronic pushâ€“pull groups at bay-positions, as well as at imide positions, in order to utilize them as an active light-harvesting component to construct bulk heterojunction polymer solar cells.16 The main reason for introducing groups at bay positions of the perylene diimide (PDI) core is to use an electronic pushâ€“pull approach to enhance electron mobilities via strong Ï€â€“Ï€ stacking interactions between the planar PDI moieties,17 and to induce an ultrafast photoinduced charge-separated state which contributes to the slowing down of charge recombination.18 However, the electronic structures of perylene Ï€-systems are not significantly affected upon introduction of substituents at the imide position because the frontier molecular orbital nodes are available on the imide nitrogen atoms.10a,19,20

Recently, an alternating copolymer (PTCDI-PFDA) bearing a non-bay substituted perylene moiety combined with a Ï€-conjugated moiety (5,5â€²-bis(4-aminophenyl)-2-29-bifuryl; PFDA) moieties was prepared and utilized as an active electron acceptor material to fabricate bulk-heterojunction photovoltaic devices.21 As an extension of that work, here we prepare bay substituted perylene diimide-based copolymers (two-fold piperidinyl and two-fold 4-tert-butylphenoxyl moieties substituted at symmetrical bay positions (i.e., 1,7-bay positions) of the perylene central core as the electron donor part, and then a high degree of Ï€-conjugated PFDA moieties acting as an acceptor) to be used as a photoactive material for bulk heterojunction solar cells. The motivation for making use of the electron-rich piperidinyl and 4-tert-butylphenoxyl moieties at the 1,7-bay positions of the perylene core and the covalently linked PFDA moiety through Ï€-conjugated spacers is that they increase the acceptor character of the perylene core, thereby enhancing the intramolecular charge transfer processes and in turn, resulting in broad light absorption in the visible region of the solar spectrum. Furthermore, the results of DFT calculations revealed that judicious substitution of multiple strongly electron-donating substituents onto the symmetrical bay position of the perylene diimide core could induce a positive shift of the LUMO energy levels, but results in no significant change in the HOMO energy levels, which could lead to significant light-harvesting capability.

Therefore, the absorption properties of the two copolymers are highly suitable for harvesting sunlight.

From the computed results, it is clear that the entire Câ€“C bonds in these copolymer backbones are partial double bonds. These are good evidence for comprehensive Ï€-delocalization throughout the perylene core, as well as in the aromatic rings. The UV-vis absorption spectra and photoluminescence emission spectra were recorded on a double beam ultraviolet-visible spectrophotometer (T90+, PG Instruments Ltd., United Kingdom) and a Shimadzu spectrofluorometer (RF-5301 PC, Japan), respectively. Conclusions

In summary, soluble perylene diimide-based conjugated electronic pushâ€“pull copolymers with moderate molecular weights were synthesized by a one-step process. The as-synthesized copolymers showed extended broad light absorption from the visible-to-near infrared region and also showed higher absorption coefficients. The introduction of the electronic pushâ€“pull substituent species results in a relatively low-lying HOMO and high-lying LUMO energy levels were established well by experimental and theoretical calculations, which in turn translated into high open circuit voltages in the fabricated photovoltaic devices. However, the fabricated device based on 1,7-DBPDI-PFDA has a relatively poor fill factor and efficiency, which may be due to the large degree of aggregated domains found via AFM.
