Abstract

Novel small-molecule hole transporting materials (HTMs) with a linear Ï€-conjugated structure have been synthesized. The perovskite solar cell based on 2TPA-2-DP as the HTM exhibits an encouraging power conversion efficiency of 9.1% under AM 1.5 G (100 mW cmâˆ’2) illumination, which is the first demonstration of an effective perovskite solar cell using a linear structured HTM.

In recent years, there have been considerable interests in the development of amorphous small-molecule hole transporting materials (HTMs). These small-molecule HTMs exhibit many outstanding advantages over polymers, such as structural versatility and identical molecular weight as well as relatively easy purification by chromatography, crystallization or vacuum sublimation.1 They have been widely used in various electronic devices like emitting diodes, photovoltaic cells, photodiodes and field effect transistors.2

Solar cell is one of the most promising devices which convert sunlight directly into electricity. In order to relieve global warming, tremendous efforts have been devoted to develop low-cost and high-efficiency solar cells. Very recently, hybrid organicâ€“inorganic perovskites (CH3NH3PbX3, where X corresponds to halogens) have been introduced as light harvesters in low cost solar cells.3 Power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) of up to 15% have been reported using CH3NH3PbI3âˆ’xClx as the light absorber and 2,2â€²,7,7â€²-tetrakis(N,N-di-p-methoxyphenylamine)-9,9â€²-spirobifluorene (spiro-OMeTAD) as the HTM.4 Although it has been demonstrated that spiro-OMeTAD is the most effective small-molecule HTM for perovskite solar cells, the spiro-bifluorene core in the spiro-OMeTAD molecule is relatively expensive due to its tedious synthetic methodology. To develop a cheaper alternative to spiro-OMeTAD, Jeon and co-workers have developed novel pyrene arylamine derivatives as HTMs in fabricating mesoporous TiO2/CH3NH3PbI3/HTM/Au solar cells with a PCE of 12.4%, a short-circuit current density (Jsc) of 20.2 mA cmâˆ’2 and an open-current voltage (Voc) of 0.886 V.5 These pyrene arylamine derivatives also possess a bulky three-dimensional structure and their performance is comparable to the commonly used spiro-OMeTAD. To reveal the structureâ€“property relationships of the HTM for more fundamental chemical aspects behind the structural design and to bring perovskite solar cells one step closer to viable commercial applications, exploration of more cost-effective small-molecule HTMs with a diverse structure is highly desirable.

In this communication, we report the synthesis and characterization of novel small-molecule HTMs with a linear Ï€-conjugated structure, which is based on a diphenyl (DP) unit and a triphenylamine (TPA) unit linked with olefinic bonds (Scheme 1). The small molecular size of the HTMs could permit good pore filling of the TiO2 layer. The large linear Ï€-conjugated system makes the HTMs solution-processable, which enables fabrication of convenient devices in a wet process such as spin-coating and ink-jet printing. TPA usually serves as building blocks for construction of various HTM molecules with efficient hole mobilities.6 The propeller-like TPA located at the terminal of the linear conjugated molecule can be expected to prevent close contact between the perovskite and HTMs, which is in favor of reducing the electronic coupling and charge recombination. The perovskite solar cell, based on one of the TPA derivatives as a HTM, shows a PCE of 9.1% under AM 1.5 G (100 mW cmâˆ’2) illumination without optimization of the fabrication process and solar cell configuration. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first case for the adoption of small-molecule HTMs with a linear Ï€-conjugated structure in fabricating perovskite solar cells with an impressive performance. The UV-vis absorption spectra of 2TPA-n-DP (n = 2, 3, 4) in dilute THF solutions and thin films are depicted in Fig. 1. In dilute THF solution, all HTMs show absorption bands in the visible region, and the absorption maxima are located at 423, 436 and 451 nm for 2TPA-2-DP, 2TPA-3-DP and 2TPA-4-DP, respectively. This is due to inter-band transition between delocalized states, which can be derived from Ï€-orbitals interaction of triphenylamine and olefinic bonds.7 The UV-vis spectra of the thin films display a similar pattern as those of the solutions, which indicates that no significant aggregation or crystallization occurs in solid films.8 The absorption maximum present a bathochromic shift (33, 22 and 26 nm for 2TPA-2-DP, 2TPA-3-DP and 2TPA-4-DP, respectively) and slight broadening as a solid film, indicating enhanced intermolecular interactions in the solid state.9

To understand the charge-carrier transport properties of 2TPA-n-DP, their hole transporting mobilities were evaluated by time-of-flight (TOF) measurement (ESIâ€ ). Fig. 2 displays representative TOF transients for holes of 2TPA-2-DP. It could be observed that the transient photocurrent exhibits significant current decay, which reveals a typical dispersive charge transport behavior. The hole mobility (Î¼) is calculated according to the equation Î¼ = d2/VtÏ„, where d is the film thickness and V is the applied bias voltage.13 The transit time (tÏ„) is evaluated from the intersection point of two asymptotes in the double logarithmic representations (inset of Fig. 2). At room temperature, hole-drift mobilities of 2TPA-2-DP, 2TPA-3-DP and 2TPA-4-DP are 1.09 Ã— 10âˆ’4, 8.39 Ã— 10âˆ’5 and 6.79 Ã— 10âˆ’5 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1, respectively, at an electric field of 3.1 Ã— 105 V cmâˆ’1. These results indicate that the extension of the conjugated system could retard charge carrier transportation in an efficient manner.14 The hole mobilities of 2TPA-n-DP are close to that of spiro-OMeTAD (2 Ã— 10âˆ’4 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 determined using the same technique15), especially for 2TPA-2-DP, which suggests that these materials could be considered as promising candidates for HTMs in solar cells.

Considering its high hole mobility, 2TPA-2-DP was employed as the HTM to fabricate a FTO/TiO2/CH3NH3PbI3/HTM/Au solar cell without any dopant for the HTM. The photovoltaic performance of the perovskite solar cell is evaluated from its photocurrent densityâ€“voltage (Jâ€“V) and IPCE characteristics (Fig. 3). The FTO/TiO2/CH3NH3PbI3/2TPA-2-DP/Au solar cell exhibits an encouraging efficiency (Î·) of 9.1%, a remarkably high open-circuit voltage (Voc) of 0.94 V and a short-circuit current density (Jsc) of 16.3 mA cmâˆ’2, and a fill factor (FF) of 59.7% under AM 1.5 G (100 mW cmâˆ’2) illumination. These factors are comparable to that of the solar cell based on well-studied spiro-OMeTAD fabricated with the same process (Jsc of 16.8 mA cmâˆ’2, Voc of 0.91 V, FF of 67.1%, and Î· of 10.2%), which evidences that HTMs with linear Ï€-conjugated structures can also be suitable HTMs for high efficient perovskite solar cells. We believe that enhanced performance of the solar cell based on 2TPA-2-DP as the HTM could be reached after optimization of the device architecture and the fabrication process. A primary study on stability of the perovskite solar cell with 2TPA-2-DP as the HTM was also carried out. The unsealed cell was kept in dark at an ambient atmosphere and the Jâ€“V characteristics were tested every day during a week. It is found that the efficiency retained 91% of its initial value after 7 days, while the Jsc remained constant; the Voc and FF remain 97% and 94%, respectively. Further investigation about stability of the perovskite solar cell under working conditions is in process.

In summary, three novel small-molecule HTMs with a linear Ï€-conjugated structure, namely 2TPA-2-DP, 2TPA-3-DP and 2TPA-4-DP, were synthesized by a simple process with low cost and high yield. The 2TPA-n-DP exhibit good thermal stabilities, high hole-drift mobilities and appropriate HOMO levels, indicating that these materials can be promising HTMs in different types of electronic devices. The perovskite solar cell based on 2TPA-2-DP as the HTM exhibits an impressive conversion efficiency of 9.1%, which provide compelling evidence that small-molecule HTMs with a linear Ï€-conjugated structure have great potential for the application in perovskite solar cells. To achieve higher efficiency, further optimization of the fabrication process and the device architecture is under investigation and will be reported later.
