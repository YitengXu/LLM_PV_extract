Recent advances and future perspectives in MOF-derived single-atom catalysts and their application: a review

Abstract

Metalorganic-framework-derived single-atom catalysts are widely used in the catalysis field due to their special structure and superior performance. In general, there is a strong relationship between the precise control of the interaction between the carriers and metals to design efficient MOF-derived single-atom catalysts (SACs). In this paper, the synthesis of SACs from multiple MOF derivatives, the formation mechanism of various single-atom sites, and their application in the catalysis field were reviewed in detail. We concluded that the different kinds of MOF substrates were used to construct coordination environments with different anchoring conditions. Simultaneously, the related catalytic mechanisms through the introduction of metal active centers in different coordination environments are discussed in detail. Subsequently, the use of MOF-derived SACs in the fields of electrocatalysis, photocatalysis, and thermocatalysis is also introduced as well as some important characterization methods. Eventually, the fields future potential and challenges were also outlined. How to regulate accurately the coordination environment of the central atoms from many aspects is addressed, so as to improve the catalytic performance of the catalysts and build a reasonable catalytic mechanism. Herein, we believe that this review can provide a good foundation for the future development and application of single-atom catalysts.

1. Introduction

Single-atom catalysts (SACs), a class of materials prepared by anchoring single metal atoms on a carrier and prepared by various efficient methods, show excellent features in heterogeneous catalytic reactions because of their maximum atom utilization. In 2011, Zhang's team prepared a new catalytic material by limiting Pt single atoms (Pt-SAs) on an FeOx support,1 thus proposing the concept of single-atom catalysts, which caused a stir among global researchers and promoted a qualitative leap in the field of catalysis. In a short period of more than a decade, the preparation,2 characterization,3 and application4 of SACs have achieved remarkable results. Generally speaking, the synthesis methods of SACs mainly include the wet chemical method (impregnation method,5 co-precipitation method,6etc.), atomic layer deposition method (ALD),7,8 pyrolysis method, and mass-separation soft landing method.9 Currently, various metal-based SACs have been successfully developed, incorporating metals such as platinum,10 cobalt,11 iron,12 copper,13,14 bismuth,15 palladium,16 ruthenium,17 nickel,10 and silver,18 and their various applications are also introduced. For the characterization of SACs, it is tough to efficiently distinguish the existence of single metal atoms. Therefore, more advanced and precise characterization instruments were used, such as X-ray absorption fine spectroscopy (XAFS) and high-angle annular dark field scanning transmission electron microscopy (HAADF-STEM).19 Moreover, compared with traditional metal nanoparticles (Me NPs) and metal cluster catalysts, SACs can effectively improve the dispersity of metal atoms, so as to obtain the maximum atom utilization efficiency. Due to the existence of new characteristics such as unsaturated coordination environment, high surface free energy, and local electronic structure adjustment of single atoms,2022 they exhibit excellent selectivity, activity, durability, and other catalytic performances for various catalytic reactions. Of course, an efficient coordination environment is inseparable from the induction of carriers, such as metal oxides,2325 graphene,26,27 hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN),28,29 covalent organic frameworks (COFs),30 metalorganic polyhedra (MOP),31 and metalorganic frameworks (MOFs), which is essential for the local environmental regulation of metal single atoms (Me-SAs) in SACs. Besides, the successful synthesis of SACs also significantly reduces the cost of preparation. However, the problems and challenges faced by SACs are also need to be studied by the scholars all the time. On the one hand, in view of the high surface free energy and easy movement of single atoms (SAs), one of the difficulties in preparing highly active and stable SACs is how to firmly fix single atoms to prevent agglomeration or even sintering in subsequent different environments.32 On the other hand, with the rapid development of SACs, the preparation of catalysts with high loadings about single atoms has been put on the agenda. Therefore, to increase the content of single atoms, the design of SACs with strong anchoring ability, uniform atomic active centers, and high density of single atoms is the main research focus at present. In this paper, relatively new metalorganic framework derivatives will be introduced as the support materials, contributing to the research progress of SACs.33

Metalorganic frameworks (MOFs) have excellent properties of functional ligands, tunable pore size, and high porosity, which are widely used in catalysis,34 gas storage and separation,35,36 and energy storage-conversion technology.37 MOFs were created by the Xu team,38 and are composed of metal nodes and organic ligands, and it has been explored that MOF derivatives can inherit some properties of MOF precursors. In other words, MOF derivatives can improve the defects in some directions of pure MOFs and effectively expand the application field of MOF materials, especially under certain harsh conditions.39 With the rapid development of MOF materials, functional nanomaterials constructed by traditional methods could not meet the current catalytic direction. Previously, Zitolo et al.40 confirmed the availability of MOF derivatives in the preparation of SACs. Naturally, MOF-derived materials have emerged as ideal materials in the field of SACs. It is well known that the biggest difference between SACs and other catalysts lies in their extraordinary single-atom structure, which not only improves the efficiency of atom utilization but also improves the process of adsorption and activation of reactants.7 In view of the original properties that MOF carriers can retain, MOF-derived materials are mostly used to construct porous carbon materials, including carbides and phosphates.41 SACs are an emerging era, and SACs derived from MOFs have also become promising materials with great prospects in catalytic research.42 To date, multifunctional MOF-derived SACs have made significant progress, especially in electrocatalysis, and interested scholars may refer to these review papers.43,44

So far, the precise regulation of unsaturated coordination, size effects, and local electron density around metal atoms has been developed at the level of single atoms.4547 Therefore, the detection of single atoms cannot be outdone. Generally, high-resolution performance and advanced characterization technology are used for catalyst detection. HAADF-STEM could be utilized to prove the coexistence about single atoms and nanoparticles on the surface of catalysts.48,49 However, it is quite challenging to clearly express the catalytic mechanism and other laws at the atomic scale with existing characterization methods.3 At present, it is mainly based on the analysis of the site and then extended to the exploration of the mechanism. The active site, a place where catalytic reactions take place, is made up of a central metal and its surrounding coordination atoms. Notoriously, the catalytic performance of SACs is intimately associated with the active sites.50 Due to the controllability of the coordination environment of metal ions,51 it provides a feasible way to extensively design atomic-level studies. However, due to the highly complex and unpredictable growth process of MOF crystals, it is hard to control and determine the location of metal precursors, and it is still difficult to give a definitive answer to the catalytic mechanism of the active sites obtained by characterization. Naturally, the auxiliary method of theoretical calculation comes into being. The proposal of functional theories, such as quantum mechanics and so on,52 provided researchers with powerful theoretical calculation and simulation tools. Theoretical calculation can systematically study the active sites, such as revealing the catalytic reaction mechanism at the sites,53 calculating the energy barrier of transition states of single atoms,54 and it can also analyze the dispersion behavior of metal atoms.55 At the moment, there are many theoretical calculation methods, but density functional theory (DFT) is the main method adopted. Some groups mainly focus on purely theoretical calculations.56,57 With the help of these calculations, they can further understand the relationship between metal atoms and coordination atoms, and even the relationship between catalytic activity and selectivity. However, how to generate realistic theoretical models and establish a close relationship between catalytic performance and coordination states remains a major challenge. Therefore, basic information can be provided by means of a variety of characterization techniques, and theoretical analysis can be provided by means of computational simulation. The combination of theory and experiment is becoming the growing trend of single atoms.

In this paper, through various synthesis and treatment methods, we established a suitable coordination environment for metal ions from functionalized MOF materials and then introduced the connection between single-atom active sites and catalytic reaction mechanisms. Finally, the article summarized the research progress in electrocatalysis, photocatalysis, and thermocatalysis of MOF-derived SACs in recent years, and introduced the main characterization techniques of single-atom identification in SACs. Most importantly, the role of active sites of MOF-derived SACs in the catalytic pathway was introduced from coordination and electronic environment in combination with theoretical calculations. At the same time, it also puts forward the existing shortcomings and challenges and provided constructive suggestions for the future of SACs. The purpose of this review was to provide some practical directions for studying MOF-derived SACs, so as to facilitate the understanding of others' research results and the construction of innovative ideas.

2. The synthesis and coordination environment of SACs derived from MOF nanocages

Up to now, the preparation strategies for SACs have been rich and colorful. However, SACs are prone to aggregation during the process of preparation and use, which makes it difficult to provide maximum atom utilization and thus reduces catalytic performance. Therefore, it has become a hot topic to improve the stability and loading capacity of single atoms by changing preparation methods, carriers, and metal types. The emergence of MOFs and their derivatives have solved this difficulty for many research studies. Most of the MOF-derived SACs are obtained through pyrolysis, and in the pyrolysis process, the difference in internal and external pressure of MOF material promotes the appearance of the derived carbon and nitrogen structure, thus expanding the cavity of the MOFs.58 Moreover, as shown in Fig. 1, these catalysts showed excellent performance in various catalytic reactions, combined with the advantages of SACs and MOF derivatives.5961 On the one hand, the abundant pore structure in MOFs contributes to the exposure of more active sites. On the other hand, the pore structure can also promote the migration of substances,62 so that the active species can fully contact with active sites for a long time, thus improving the catalytic efficiency.63 Meanwhile, it is precisely because of the advantages of single metal sites, tunable functional ligands (e.g., organic ligands rich in nitrogen or oxygen species), and rich pore structures that MOF derivatives can boost SACs to the next level. Now, the main preparation methods of MOF-derived SACs are direct pyrolysis and post pyrolysis (secondary pyrolysis, acid etching, and top-down preparation from bulk) according to the methods of forming single atoms.

Besides, according to the structural classification of anchoring single atoms, the strategies for preparing MOF-derived SACs can be divided into the metal node strategy, organic link capture strategy, and encapsulation strategy. MOF derivatives can firmly control single atoms on the carriers to prevent agglomeration due to their unique properties of anchoring, which led to their widespread use in the preparation of SACs. To date, ZIF materials are the most popular MOF precursors.64,65 The original inorganic nodes of the materials can obtain single atoms by high-temperature pyrolysis or acid treatment, and it is easy to make the target metals replace part of zinc, so the metals will be evenly distributed on the MOF derivatives in the form of MNx. Importantly, the carbonnitrogen structures derived from MOFs can anchor target single atoms tightly to effectively avoid their migration and aggregation. In order to further explore the coordination environments provided by MOF precursors for single atoms and to achieve the anchoring operation of single atoms after pyrolysis, the following part would concentrate on introducing various SAC preparation strategies proposed in recent years based on various MOF substrates. This will help future researchers in this field by offering insightful information.

Zeolite-like imidazole frameworks (ZIFs) can be synthesized by the reaction of zinc or cobalt species with imidazole ligands.66,67 As ZIF materials have multiple modification methods, good specific surface area and other advantages, the SACs derived from such MOF materials have been extensively studied, as shown in Table 1. Therefore, the interaction between metal ions and the coordination environment was mainly introduced below from the direction of synthesis of Me-SAs by adding target metals to ZIF materials and their own metals.

Zn/Co species in ZIF materials can be used to prepare Me-SAs. Zhou and Yang et al.58 proposed the vacuum pyrolysis method to enlarge the MOF cavity to construct a 3D graphene framework so that the ligands could effectively bind cobalt ions and achieve atomic dispersion on the framework in the form of metal nodes (Fig. 2A and B). In addition, Yang et al.5 used the original zinc ions in ZIF materials and made them into Zn-SAC with a high load (11.3 wt%). They used the template method and pyrolysis method to prepare ZIF materials into hollow structures and combined them with N-rich ligands to promote the preparation of high-density active-sites catalysts as shown in Fig. 2C. Subsequently, a zinc single-atom catalyst was successfully prepared and it was obvious that the prepared spherical catalyst had hollow structure according to various electron microscope images (Fig. 2D). The inner voids of hollow structure can effectively reflect light,74 and then form photothermal effect, which significantly improves the catalytic performance of catalysts. Moreover, the construction of composite substrates also provided constructive assistance for the coordination environments of Me-SAs. Guo et al.75 also designed a Zn-SAC derived from ZIF materials to construct three-dimensional ordered large pores through the hard template-assisted strategy. They added a composite material SiO2-CCT (colloidal crystal template) into the zinc precursor solution, formed a ZIF-8 single crystal in the gap of hard template SiO2-CCT, and then pyrolyzed and carbonized in an inert atmosphere and removed the template to prepare the ZnN-HOPCPs catalyst (Fig. 2E). SEM and HAADF-STEM images (Fig. 2F and G) showed that the catalyst retained a hierarchical, interconnected three-dimensional macroporous structure, and was distributed on the support in the form of Zn-SAs. In fact, SiO2-CCT can play a physical isolation role, not only preventing material collapse but also effectively inhibiting the agglomeration of metal species during calcination.76 Therefore, with the isolation of SiO2-CCT, the catalyst inhibited the migration of zinc ions at high temperature, and with the help of the large specific surface area and templates of MOFs, more internal metal ions could be exposed, thus generating more single-atom active centers, creating a feasible path for designing other MOF-derived SACs with a hierarchical structure. Obviously, ZIF materials can be directly pyrolyzed to generate SACs by virtue of the effective combination of organic ligands and metal ions, that is, organic ligands will produce porous N-doped carbon (NC) frameworks22 with rich-nitrogen characteristics during pyrolysis and carbonization, and metal ions can be captured67 under high-temperature environment to build stable Me-SAs, so ZIF materials themselves can be used as an excellent precursor of MOF-derived SACs.

ZIF materials certainly have a special feature that zinc ions in ZIFs (boiling point 907 C) are easily vaporized at high temperatures, allowing additional target Me-SAs to replace the zinc metal sites and be distributed uniformly over the MOF-derived substances, and the remaining metal ions can be regarded as a barrier to widen the distance between target atoms. In this way, single-atoms aggregation can be effectively avoided.77 In 2020, a Co-SAC showed good formic acid dehydrogenation activity, suggesting that Co2+ of another target species could be isolated on ZIF materials with the help of Zn2+ (Fig. 3a) and that the target metal also replaced part of the Zn sites and formed single atoms through subsequent pyrolysis on account of the evaporation of Zn.78 Furthermore, Chen's group79 also designed a Fe-SAC that partially replaced zinc with iron (Fig. 3b). They added iron ions to stimulate the Kirkendall effect, resulting in a large number of large mesoporous structures that facilitated mass transfer and allowed adsorption to contact the active sites. SACs can also be designed by subsequent addition of the target metal and two-step pyrolysis (Fig. 3c and d).80 In conclusion, the substitution of Zn metal sites is of great significance for strengthening the stability of SAs. In other words, the capture of target metal ions by organic ligands is also important.

In addition, the particularity of ZIF materials is also applicable to the study of dual single-atom catalysts (DSACs). Wang et al.81 first doped copper into ZIF-8, and then calcined it at high temperature to make copper replace the position after zinc evaporation and coordinate with nitrogen in the carrier to obtain the C-ZIF-Cu material. Then, the C-ZIF-CuPt alloy catalyst was prepared by reducing platinum on C-ZIF-Cu with ascorbic acid. In the other case, Zhang, Li, and Jiang82 used other two kinds of non-precious metals to explore the ORR. They designed the Fe, Ni-SAs/DNSC catalyst with an Fe/Ni atom coordination environment synthesized on the basis of the ZIF structure (Fig. 4a and b). Moreover, the different solvents may also affect the coordination environment of MOFs and thus alter the catalytic activity. Gao and his colleagues83 designed Co-SACs with different micro morphologies (polyhedral, layered, accordion-like) using different solvents. The experimental proved that the change of ZIF morphology was mainly attributed to the fact that the aqueous solution of acetate (OAc) could effectively isolate metal ions in space and form large pores, so as to coordinate multiple ligands of Co ions, and the water formed hydrogen bonds with the amines in the ligands, so that Co ions cross-linked with the ligands, leading to the formation of layered structures. There is no doubt that the interaction between the target metal and the coordination environment also has a great influence on the morphology of the catalyst. In addition, an alternative is to confine the target metal to a cage of ZIF materials. Guo et al. used the pore structure of MOFs to fabricate SACs through a space-limiting strategy.84 They first encapsulated iron precursors into cages of ZIF-8 and then the ZIF material produced N-doped carbon material by calcination, and anchored single atoms in the cage by the metalN interaction to obtain catalytic materials with different iron loads. In addition, Han and Wang's team85 reported the DSAC (FeN4/PtN4@NC) based on ZIF materials. They first encased the target metal ions in the nanocages of MOFs, followed by pyrolytic carbonization to anchor single atoms in the MOF-derived framework through metal-N interactions (Fig. 4c and d). Understandably, the design of DSACs with special coordination environments may have unexpected surprises.

In fact, there are already a large number of ZIF-derived SACs, so how to stand out from the numerous materials is a problem that researchers need to consider. Surprisingly, metal ions can be anchored with the help of defects in the carriers. As previously mentioned in the DSAC,82 the ZIF material in this paper has been vulcanized and treated with hydrogen peroxide to produce a coordination environment rich in nitrogen, sulfur, and defects, which was conducive to the coordination of target metal ions and improved the stability of SAs. And SACs were also prepared by the pyrolysis strategy of salt-sealed precursor (ZIFs) with a nitrogen-doped three-dimensional interconnected structure that was rich in defects.86 In addition, Li and colleagues proposed a new strategy to prepare SACs by applying salt to MOF materials.87 With the introduction of iron phthalocyanine (FePc) molecules, Fe SAC (SA-Fe-NGM) was produced by the addition of potassium chloride/lithium chloride (KCL) to produce ultrathin 2D materials (NGM). It was clear that the salt-treated NGM material was rich in topological defects and could anchor FeNx maximally, achieving not only high power density (634 mW cm2) but also high metal loading (8.36 wt%) in H2/O2 fuel cell tests. Furthermore, Wu's team88 designed a ZIF-derived catalyst (CoZnO/NC) with a layered pyramid structure of Co single atoms (Co-SAs) interacting with ZnO through high-temperature pyrolysis. By adjusting the appropriate Zn/Co ratio, they achieved a high dispersion of ZnO nanoparticles on the NC material and anchored the target cobalt ions with the ZnO (100) facet, that is, ZIF derivatives achieved a high dispersion of Co SAs. The rhombic dodecahedral shape of the ZIF precursor was well preserved by electron microscopy (Fig. 5A and B). In this study, the preparation method was relatively simple, but the suitable Zn/Co ratio was the key for the synthesis of pyramid structure (Fig. 5C). It has been proved that metal ions can also be trapped in different coordination environments such as carriers' defects and oxide crystal planes. MOF-derived SACs were also synthesized by the supercritical CO2 assisted method (scCO2-FeC@FeNC) (Fig. 5D). Gao and Yang et al.89 added ZIF-8 and iron solution together into the supercritical reaction reactor, injected CO2 until the pressure and temperature reached a certain condition and continued to react for a period of time. Then, they released the gas to obtain scCO2-Fe@ZIF-8, and the precipitate was pyrolyzed and treated with acid to produce the required catalyst. Supercritical gas cut the carrier and formed new fragments to provide a large amount of exposure, and due to its low viscosity, high diffusivity, and near zero surface tension, the metal ions will be evenly distributed on the ZIF materials, creating enough anchoring sites.

The preparation of SACs from composite materials is also a focus of attention in the research field. Materials combined with electrospinning technology are a relatively new research area. Polyacrylonitrile (PAN) nanofibers prepared by electrospinning facilitated the formation of independent, conductive and porous N-doped carbon nanotubes (CNT). Gao and colleagues90 prepared ZIF-derived Co-SAC using PAN as a template and showed good activity in Fenton-like catalytic oxidation of peroxymonosulfate (PMS). They primarily prepared the starting template by electrostatic spinning, then added 2-methylimidazole and cobalt salt at intervals to make ZIF-67 grow evenly on the PAN film, and finally added dicyandiamide (DCDA) after pyrolysis and acid treatment to obtain Co-SAC (SAs-Co@NC) (Fig. 5E and F). Among them, DCDA was introduced to provide self-pyrolytic reduction gas to promote the formation of conductive CNT. In the catalytic process, SAs-Co@NC showed the highest degradation rate (81.6%) in a certain time, which was significantly higher than NPs-Co@NC (54.4%) and CoC (33.1%). However, it was difficult to guarantee the stability of the catalyst, so the subsequent research can start from the durability of the catalyst to effectively degrade organic pollutants. In addition, Wang et al.91 derived Fe-ISAs/H-CN catalyst from a mixture of polypyrrole (PPy) and MOF. They successively added iron solution (Fe(acac)3) and a certain amount of pyrrole (Py) to the ZIF-8 carrier and then pyrolyzed to obtain Fe-SAC with a cage structure (Fig. 5G and H). The iron precursor was favorable for initiating pyrrole polymerization to form the shell structure, which destroyed the ZIF-8 structure, enlarging the pore and promoting the formation of the hollow structure. The nanocages catalyst prepared with the help of pyrrole achieved the encapsulation of metal ions, thus limiting the migration of metal ions. It also showed higher half-wave potential (E1/2 = 0.87 V), high stability, and excellent tolerance to methanol. In addition, the target metal-doped ZIF material was pyrolyzed at high temperature and then aminated with ammonia to prepare a metal single-atom stable and uniformly dispersed SAC.92

Nowadays, MOF-derived SACs are mainly based on ZIFs. First of all, ZIF derivatives can effectively anchor metal ions and improve the stability of single atoms by virtue of their nitrogen-rich coordination environment to provide a basis for the coordination of metal ions. Second, the special coordination environment of the ZIF material also provides a guarantee for the fixation of metal ions, which can not only effectively block the migration and aggregation of metal ions, but also realize the preparation of polymetallic SACs. Moreover, the nanocages of MOF materials can also anchor atoms through spatially confined metal ions. Most importantly, the ZIF materials can be externally modified to achieve the anchoring of metal ions by carrier defects and crystal planes. Of course, ZIF materials are widely used in MOF-derived SACs due to their excellent properties, whether they have their own or additional target metal ions. How to propose more innovative preparation methods requires the redoubled efforts of the majority of researchers to extract the power from the ordinary, and find the unique from the common.

The octahedral isoreticular metalorganic frameworks (IRMOFs) composed of [Zn4O]6+ inorganic groups and a series of aromatic carboxylic acid organic ligands,93 which can also achieve anchoring of the target metal precursors. Zhang's team94 first impregnated Fe species into the IRMOF-3 carrier, then pyrolyzed in an inert atmosphere, and finally treated with acid to obtain the OFeNC catalyst, and also prepared a comparison sample (FeNC catalyst) (Fig. 6a). Obviously, the strategy of using the IRMOF-3 carrier, together with the proof of the density functional theory (DFT) calculation (Fig. 6b), had realized the heteroatom coordination at a specific position at the atomic level and through the heteroatom to adjust the coordination environment to destroy the original symmetric electronic structure, creating an axial coordination environment for gold ions and providing a new research path for the precursor of MOFs rich in heteroatoms. In addition, Li and his colleagues95 designed a Co-SAC with a high loading capacity (7.35 wt%) derived from MOF-74 as a carrier. They prepared Co-MOF-74 precursor, then added glucose, sulfur dopant (Na2S2O3), and Co precursor, and pyrolyzed the mixture in a molten salt system to obtain the CoS/C-SAC catalyst with a CoS4 coordination environment (Fig. 6c and d). The presence of Co atoms in the form of Co2+ was confirmed using EXAFS spectra (Fig. 6e), and the existence of CoS4 site structure was confirmed by various characterization methods. Through this study, it can be recognized that, compared with inorganic cobalt salt, Co-MOF-74 effectively alleviated the volatilization of Co species in the pyrolysis process to reduce the loss of metal content. There was one point that the addition of salt and sulfur dopants can improve the structure of the materials, prevent Me-SAs agglomeration, and accelerate the mass transfer capacity in the catalytic reaction process. It's been proven that the SACs significantly improved the catalytic activity and photoelectric conversion efficiency (PCE) of the reaction of I3 to I (IRR), and developed carbon-based SACs that catalyze IRR efficiently to achieve sustainable energy conversion.

And the addition of chelating elements can effectively improve the metal coordination environment, and the defect structure also contributes to anchor atoms. Yuan's team96 proposed a defective MOF-derived SAC (Co@DMOF). They synthesized a rod-like crystalline MOF (DMOF) carrier by adding triethylenediamine (DABCO) as a support on the basis of the preparation method of IRMOF. The catalyst was then prepared by introducing the Co species at high temperatures using a gas transport method that uses nitrogen purge to bring the Co-containing powder placed upstream of the tubular furnace downstream of the DMOF. The presence of a defective carrier can not only fully improve the defect degree but also optimize the coordination environment of transition metal (TM) sites, which can improve the catalytic reaction (Fig. 6f and g). Studies97 have shown that at a certain temperature, the carboxylic acid part of MOF dissociated from the aromatic ring and formed COx, which may lead to the appearance of defects and thus affect the coordination environment of the catalytic active metal ions. So the team explored and compared the difference in catalysts synthesized at different temperatures. It turned out that high temperatures did avail the formation of defects, but too high a temperature caused the gathering of single atoms. In conclusion, the carrier structure of IRMOF materials can be changed by simply substituting organic ligands, or they can even have some of their attributes altered to make them better for study. Crucially, adding heteroatoms to the material can increase conductivity and even expose more metal ion coordination environments by increasing the defect structure.

Generally, UiO materials formed by the combination of regular octahedral metal-containing material [M6(3O)4(3-OH)4] and terephthalic acid organic ligand have a three-dimensional octahedral cage structure.98,99 The material was pioneeringly discovered in the form of a zirconium-based MOF, and there are many other metal forms, such as UiO-66 (Ce).100 Most importantly, UiO materials have the advantages of strong photoactivity, defect generation, and high stability, which can improve the catalysts' capacity to absorb the light.101103 You can also adjust their chemical structures at the metal oxide node to fine-control interaction with their single atoms.100,104 For example, the functionalized UiO-66-NH2 material can adjust the electronic environment of metal ions.105 The target metal can also be anchored to the defect sites of the zirconium clusters in UiO-66 to control the agglomeration of Me-SAs,106 so as to improve the stability of single atoms. Obviously, the application of single-atom catalysts derived from UiO materials in photocatalysis is a major development trend.10,105 Of course, it is also involved in the field of electrocatalysis,13 but there are relatively few studies based on MOF-derived materials. It has previously been reported that direct anchoring could be used to make the UiO chelate ligand more receptive to the target metal ions, effectively inhibiting particle formation. Lin and his colleagues107 successfully prepared MOF-derived SACs (Fe SAC/NC) with FeN5 sites by virtue of the advantages of UiO (Fig. 7a). Subsequently, Chen et al.108 mixed tungsten and MOF precursors to prepare precipitates of UiO-66-NH2 coated tungsten species, which were then pyrolyzed in an inert atmosphere and treated with acid to obtain W-SAC (Fig. 7b). The characterization of HAADF-STEM and EXAFS specified the formation of W atoms (Fig. 7c and d), and the existence of amino coordination environment effectively dispersed metal ions and inhibited the aggregation of W species, thus presenting a good application of W-SACs in the HER. At present, it is not common for UiO derivatives to be used to prepare SACs. Composite materials109,110 may also be a way to expand the research on the derivatives of this material. It is worth mentioning that UiO materials easily produce defect structures and carry amino functional groups. It is worth introducing the research in the field of SACs derived from UiOs.

Materials of institute Lavoisier frameworks (MILs) refer to substances with certain flexibility synthesized using different transition metal elements and a series of dicarboxylic acid ligands.111 The pore of the materials can contract or expand, which facilitates the collection of the target species. MILs can be considered as a carbon source to anchor metal atoms.112 However, the low specific surface area after derivation is not beneficial to the uniform distribution of metal atoms, and then other carriers with rich surface area need to be often added.113 Xie and colleagues114 obtained the Fe SAC-MIL-101-1000 catalyst by using pyrolysis and acid treatment to obtain NH2-MIL-101(Al). The NH2-MIL-101 (Al) precursor was first pyrolyzed, followed by acid treatment to remove some of the aluminum, and then the iron complex was uniformly impregnated on the support (Fig. 8a). The catalyst was pyrolyzed again to produce a rod-like Fe-SAC-MIL101-T catalyst (T indicates the temperature of the first pyrolysis). The existence of Fe-SAs was confirmed by various characterization results, and the coordination of Fe atoms with four N atoms (FeN4) was obtained with the help of EXAFS fitting (Fig. 8b). NH2-MIL-101(Al)derived materials provided a site for the coordination of metal ions with their good mesoporous structure. According to previous reports,115 a micropore structure is conducive to loading the active sites, while a mesopore structure is conducive to the transfer of reaction molecules. Therefore, the catalyst adopts the characteristics of the MOF structure to introduce the mesopore structure and combines the advantages of micropores and mesopores to help the transfer of related species to the active sites and realize the utilization of iron single atoms. MIL materials with functional groups can also construct defective structures to prepare SACs. Li and colleagues116 prepared Cu-SACs with a defective structure by using carriers with functional groups. They synthesized the NH2-MIL-125 (Ti) carrier by a hydrothermal method, then added Cu solution (Cu(acac)2) and combined copper with an uncoordinated functional group (NH2) through the Mannich reaction, and finally formed the SAsCuTiO2/CN catalyst with copper loading of 1.1 wt% through high-temperature pyrolysis. And the catalysts without the NH2 functional group could not anchor the Cu atoms well, leading to the agglomeration of particles. Moreover, XAFS characterization technology was used to laterally prove that the NH2 functional group can effectively stabilize and uniformly disperse Cu single-atom metal ions on TiO2 defects. Nevertheless, in order to overcome the low surface derived from MIL materials, SACs with double MOF structure (ZIF/MIL-x-900) have also been prepared. Xu et al.113 synthesized ZIF-8 solid and MIL-101 (Fe) solid, and then mixed them and prepared a series of ZIF/MIL-x powders with different mass ratios (the mass ratio of ZIF to MIL is expressed as x) by a mechanical grinding method. They were then pyrolyzed in a N2 atmosphere and the solid acid finally was treated and vacuum dried to obtain the final product ZIF/MIL-x-900. It was evident from comparing ZIF/FeCl3 contrast samples (Fig. 8c) that the double MOF strategy was capable of capturing iron ions in the MIL material itself with the aid of N-doped carbon materials formed from ZIF materials, in addition to successfully improving the low surface of the carrier. More interestingly, the ORR activity of the ZIF/MIL-x-900 catalyst showed a volcanic distribution (Fig. 8d and e) with different mass ratios of ZIF/MIL-10-900 catalyst, in which ZIF/MIL-10-900 showed the highest E1/2 at 0.78 V, and the ORR of the ZIF/MIL-10-900 catalyst followed the four-electron path. In fact, the dual MOF strategy holds great promise for building inexpensive SACs. In general, MIL-derived materials can generate a variety of metal carriers. In addition to their mesoporous structures, functional groups, and the addition of additional materials, they can build a good coordination environment for metalcarrier interaction, providing a certain direction for the stability of single atoms.

Pocket-channel frameworks (PCNs) are a kind of three-dimensional porous material with cubic octahedral cage-channel topology in space,117 which are important materials to combine metal ions with organic connectors and generate single atoms. The materials are mainly represented by the zirconium (Zr)porphyrin MOF, which are made up of porphyrin ligands and Zr clusters, and can also provide rich nitrogen coordination for metal anchoring. Besides, they are easy to synthesize defective structures, and the metal ions can be stabilized in the porphyrin center,118 making this kind of MOF conducive to the preparation of SACs. For more information on organic connectome, please refer to the review paper by Xu and Zou's team.60 Using PCN-222 as a precursor, He and colleagues119 generated a series of metal single-atom catalysts (Me-SACs) and proposed a general synthesis strategy. In this design, a series of DPCN222(Co)-X (X represents the ratio of adding water) catalysts (Fig. 9) were prepared by mixing zirconium salts, compounds containing the target metal (Co), ligands, and precipitation with different proportions of water through pyrolysis and pickling. Co-SAs/NC-Y (Y represents the pyrolysis temperature) with different structures were obtained by changing the pyrolysis temperature. Finally, the comparison samples of different metal SACs were synthesized by changing the target metal species (Cu and Mn) (Fig. 10a). The reason was to explore a method that can regulate MOFs to adjust the distance between adjacent coordinated target metals, that is, to change the coordination environment of target metal ions with the help of ligands to reduce the aggregation of single atoms in the high-temperature process. The experiment well increased the yield of single metal atoms by nearly 70%, and various characterizations also proved the existence of Co single atoms and could still maintain a single atomic state after use. Similarly, Jiao and colleagues120 prepared a variety of Me-SACs based on PCN materials. They selected porphyrin MTV-MOFs (M = Co, etc.) synthesized from M-TCPP and H2-TCPP as precursors, and then prepared the required catalysts by pyrolysis (Fig. 10b and c). The diversified MOF matrix can control the pore structure and other microscopic factors of its derivatives, and it is helpful for the construction of high-density active sites suitable for the environment of the coordination of metal ions. Moreover, by comparing these samples, the Ni1NC catalyst showed the best CO2RR performance, which also contributed to the wide application of Ni-based SACs in the CO2RR.

It has been reported that silica (SiO2) can stabilize single atoms on the carrier by reducing the surface energy of Me-SAs, which has a protective effect on target metal ions.121 Therefore, Jiao, Zhang, and Wan122 synthesized Fe-SAC (FeSANC) catalysts by adding SiO2 into the porphyrin material (PCN-222(Fe)). They used trifluoroacetic acid (CF3COOH) instead of benzoic acid to improve the original preparation, in order to facilitate the introduction of ethyl orthosilicate (TEOS). Then, TEOS was added into the prepared PCN-222 (Fe), and TEOS was hydrolyzed in the hole by HCl steam treatment to obtain the SiO2@PCN-222(Fe) composite. Finally, SiO2@PCN-222(Fe) was pyrolyzed and treated with acid to synthesize FeSANC (Fig. 10d and e) with an Fe content of 3.46 wt%. According to characterization analysis, SiO2 was successfully confined into PCN-222(Fe) and the ORR half-wave potential of the catalyst was 0.8 V, far better than that of FeNPNC (0.67 V) and NC (0.51 V). It is obvious that SiO2 can be easily introduced into the pore structure of MOFs and interacts with metal ions, which is very beneficial for the construction of stable single-atom sites. However, the disadvantage is that the synthesis process of the catalyst is relatively complex, and the used hydrofluoric acid is more harmful, so the nano-casting strategy can be improved in an environment friendly way, which is also a direction of development. Generally, MOF materials are not only doped with SiO2 but also use polyaniline (PANI) to form a composite material with a strong protective function. Meng and his colleagues123 reported MOF-derived SAC (PANI@PCN-224(Fe)-900) wrapped PANI with PCN-224(Fe) (Fig. 11a). The synthesis diagram illustrated the preparation process of SAC in detail. It should be noted that the purpose of packaging PANI is to prevent the formation of impurities, and according to PXRD and EDS (Fig. 11bd), PANI can effectively inhibit the aggregation of Fe species during pyrolysis. It is clear that PANI acted as a protective barrier in the preparation, not only controlling the migration of metal species but also promoting electron transfer, which enabled PANI@PCN-224(Fe)-900 composite to have superior properties and high activity. PCNs are famous for their organic linkers with controllable coordination sites. In addition to the original sites in MOFs, they can also use post-synthesis to connect the linkers to the chelate groups, and design new organic linkers with a strong coordination environment to stabilize the target metal ions. However, at present, the SACs derived from PCNs mainly use porphyrin ligands, and other organic ligands need to be developed.

In general, the MOF material is a porous material and can provide an effective coordination environment to limit metal ions, thus achieving high stability of single atoms. Apparently, in the extraction of SACs from numerous MOF precursors, ZIF materials are the most prominent and gradually systematized application, no matter the direction of metal species or the addition of other materials. Pyrolysis is the most commonly used method to form single metal atoms from MOF precursors containing target metal species, although the methods are relatively simple. But it is usually accompanied by acid etching to remove metal NPs and ensure uniformity of single atoms. In addition, the mixed metal strategy and composite strategy show more favorable changes in the coordination environment of the target metal ions and facilitate the construction of high-density SACs. Research on composite materials is largely to protect metal ions to achieve the stability of single atoms at high temperature. Loading of various metal types can be studied in this direction to achieve high-density single atoms. Most noticeably, ZIFs have been extensively studied, and too much reliance on ZIF carriers is not conducive to systematically expanding the catalytic mechanism about MOF-derived SACs. There is no doubt that it is necessary to vigorously develop other types of MOF precursors. Among them, IRMOF-derived materials can provide a heteroatom coordination environment, MIL-derived materials can provide a carbon source, UiO materials can produce abundant defect sites, and PCN materials can realize coordination between different organic linkages and metal ions. All these materials can achieve MOF-derived SACs well. The optimization of MOF-derived SACs requires more attention from researchers, including how to increase metal loading and dispersion. Of course, the most important research is contributed to the catalytic mechanism research about MOF-derived SACs. Typically, the MOF derivatives will form the structure of carbon and nitrogen and strong interaction with the metal to build a stable MNx key. Therefore, proving MNx is the catalytic reaction of main active centers and how to promote the reaction mechanism are necessary. 3. The formation mechanism of SAC active sites via the pyrolysis of MOFs

With the further research of SACs, we have stepped into the stage of precise regulation of the coordination environment, electronic structure of metal atoms, and the existence of metal active sites is the mapping of the coordination environment of metal atoms. Usually, the metal active site consists of a target metal atom and multiple non-metal atoms around it; among them, the coordination configuration is critical to the reaction performance of the catalysts, even for the process of catalytic mechanism also can produce a very significant impact.125 Common types of active sites are shown in Fig. 12.45 The formation of these different site structures depends on the regulation of preparation methods. It is obvious that the exploration of active sites reflects the quality of catalyst preparation at the microscopic level. A study of Liu et al.126 thought in the center of the dual-core Ni metalorganic network that hydrogen adsorption could adjust and control the electronic structure, spin, and magnetic field of Ni on the surface confirmed the coordination environment of different impact on Me-SAs. In addition, He and his colleagues119 believed that introduced water would compete with certain ligands, thus promoting crystal nucleation and forming structural defects.127 Through the comparison of different Co atomic loads, it was confirmed that structural defects could isolate individual active sites, and a reasonable number of defects was conducive to expanding the average distance of the structure to increase the concentration of Me-SAs, but the excessive presence was not conducive to the anchoring of metal, resulting in the loss of a small part of the metal. This provides constructive suggestions for enhancing the load density of single atoms on the carrier. Moreover, the synergy between bimetallic nanoparticles can also be referenced in the study of SACs.128

3.1 Active sites of metal atoms with symmetrical structures (MN4)

Types of metal and ligand, atomic local electronic structure, and other factors on the catalytic performance of the active sites have a significant influence; it is easy to understand that the coordination environment of metals can be precisely regulated from the above directions to affect the site. MN4 is a common active site, mainly from N-rich organic ligands, and its symmetric electronic structure is conducive to single atom stability.

Up to now, MN4 symmetric structure and related types of SACs have been widely applied. For example, Wang and his colleagues132 reported a multi-scale structure of an FeNC catalyst containing an FeN4 site. They believed that the micro-scale skeletons, nano-scale ordered mesopores, and atomic-scale abundant carbon edge defects at the atomic level could increase the number of sites (FeN4) significantly. In particular, the abundance of edge defects favored anchoring a single Fe atom, resulting in the formation of dense FeN4 sites, as shown in Fig. 13a. Therefore, defects can also be used to construct high-density sites, thus enhancing the utilization rate of Fe single atoms. In addition, Xie et al.133 further studied the influence of ligands on single-atom sites. The introduction of MOF-5 support by a ligand-mediated method not only increased the single-atom loading capacity but also subtly increased the number of FeNx active sites, facilitating material transport. They further studied the electronic structure and local environment of Fe atoms in Fe SAC-MOF-5 by XAFS, and confirmed that Fe species are in a +3 valence state (Fig. 13b).134 EXAFS fitting calculation showed that Fe SAC-MOF-5 had FeN4 configuration in the central iron sites (Fig. 13c). There is no doubt that the Fe-SAC-MOF-5 catalyst had an E1/2 at 0.83 V (vs. RHE), which was far better than that of other Fe-SACs prepared by the same synthesis step, and significantly demonstrated high ORR activity.

Apart from the FeN4 symmetric type, Hou and colleagues135 demonstrated that the ORR satisfies the four-electron mechanism at the active FeN4C site. With theoretical calculation, the final electron transfer step was the rate-determining step (RDS) of the ORR at the FeN4C site, and the required endothermic energy was 0.57 eV. Obviously, with the help of the FeN4C site, the Fe/OES catalyst could change the RDS of the ORR, thus speeding up the reaction. Here, they also proposed that because of the over-hang-eave morphology of the catalyst, more three-phase interfaces were generated to rapidly transfer the substance to the active FeN4C site to achieve site utilization. Fe-based symmetric sites have been widely used in the study of ORR activity, so Liu and colleagues136 provided a detailed view of how the FeN4 site on the FeNC catalyst affected the ORR activity, theoretically revealing the relationship between sites and activity (Fig. 13eg). They constructed the FeN4 model and accurately described the association with ORR performance by means of DFT theoretical calculations. They suggested that it was because of the hybridization between orbitals that the ORR activity of the catalyst was triggered, which should be looked at by interested researchers for new insights.

Iron-based catalysts, however, dont necessarily guarantee better stability, so Luo and Zhang137 for the first time in 2019 designed an embedded cadmium single atom carbon Cr/N/C-950 catalyst with CrN4 interchange structural sites (Fig. 13h). It was found that CoN4-6r-c2 was the most favorable for the reaction. The combination of these defects reduced the adsorption-free energy of the intermediate *OOH at the site and accelerated the formation of products by the ORR (Fig. 14b). Besides, Zhou and Yang et al.58 proposed ORR electrocatalysts with special active sites (Co SAs/3D GFs) that were similar to the active site structure reported by Yuan et al.96 and by means of DFT, they simulated multiple sets of models, and finally determined a possible defect CoN4 structure model (CoN4-5r-c1) (Fig. 14c), and confirmed that the site structure had a lower overpotential, indicating that adjacent defects would affect Co-SA (Fig. 14d). Moreover, the coordination environment of single atoms could also be changed by adding dopants. Li and colleagues95 prepared Co-SACs with CoS4 sites, and they used a sulfur dopant (Na2S2O3) to add sulfur species for coordination, which not only helped anchor Co atoms but also regulated the surrounding coordination environment. The CoN4 site could also be used to explore the CO2RR mechanism,138 and compared with other transition metals (Mn, Fe, or Ni), it had a lower energy barrier for the formation of intermediate *COOH and the desorption process of intermediate * CO, which was more favorable for the reaction.

3.2 Active sites of metal atoms with asymmetric structures (MNx)

Asymmetric site structures (such as MNx, MNxC, and MNxO) are formed due to the use of different synthetic conditions including organic ligands and even the addition of additional heteroatoms. For instance, the MN4 site has a symmetrical planar structure, and it will be chemically inert to a certain extent.139 The unsaturated chemical environment can induce the spin redistribution of the site (such as FeN3, CoN3, MnN3), thus changing the adsorption activity of the intermediate.140 In addition, the doping of heteroatoms is a very favorable factor to regulate the catalytic active center and the coordination environment of the metal, so the design of an asymmetric active center with high doping or unsaturated coordination is effective to improve the catalytic performance.141

Previously, Chen et al.108 designed a W-SAC with an asymmetric structural site (W1N1C3) and explored the asymmetric site of MN1. Combined with theoretical calculations, they found that the most favorable catalytic activity center in the W-SAC catalyst is the coordinated C atom close to the N atom, as shown in Fig. 7d. Moreover, compared with other possible catalytic sites (W1NxCy), W1N1C3 had a low GH* value (0.033 eV), so it was more suitable for the study of the HER. Besides, the CoN3C structural site is also suitable for researching the catalytic mechanism of the nitrogen reduction reaction (NRR). Qin et al.145 explored the number of CoNx sites generated in different N coordination environments with the help of different Zn/Co mole ratios. 3.3 Bimetal M1M2 atomic interface sites

At present, relevant atomic-level studies mainly concentrate on regulating the local environment about the active sites, and few studies extend the exploration to the synergistic interactions between adjacent active sites.151 According to reports,152 the catalysts with multiple Me-SAs have been shown to provide better catalytic performance than corresponding SACs containing only one metal type, that is, synergies between the active sites of different Me-SAs contribute to catalytic reactions. In dual SACs, the synergistic effect of dimetal includes two types. On the one hand, the two metal sites promote each other in the catalytic process and act on different stages of the same reaction catalytic mechanism respectively. On the other hand, the two metal sites don't interfere with each other and produce different effects for different reactions.45 Obviously, the addition of another suitable metal can effectively improve the coordination environment and electronic structure of the original metal. With the help of bimetallic sites, the catalytic reaction can be orderly conducted. However, it is still a major challenge to study the synergistic effect of adjacent single-atom active sites, especially since there are few SACs based on two or more metal sites of MOF-derived SACs.153

Wang et al.154 constructed the diatomic active site (Fe2N6CO), in which the orbital coupling of diatoms would cause the orbital energy level drop and the delocalization of electronic states (Fig. 18a), thus promoting the desorption of *CO, and achieving excellent CO2RR performance. In addition to the construction of the same type of double single-atom sites, the CO selectivity of bimetallic single-atom electrocatalyst Fe1Ni1NC could also achieve 96.2% at 0.5 V by combining FeN4 and NiN4 sites.155 Through theoretical simulation, FeN4 mainly activated CO2 in the CO2RR process, and the formation of intermediate (COOH*) on FeN4 was lower (Fig. 18b and c), which proved that the Ni atom could activate the Fe atom on account of the proximity of the two active sites. In addition, XANES spectra (Fig. 18d) showed the energy transfer between iron and nickel, confirming the electron transfer phenomenon from Ni to Fe. This work proved that CO2RR performance could be obviously improved by adding an appropriate second metal and cooperating with each other. Han and Wang85 also used DFT to reveal the influence of PtN4 on FeN4 sites in DSAC. According to the analysis of electrons and orbitals in Fig. 19, it can be concluded that FeN4 was the main active center in the bimetallic site system, while PtN4 was treated as the electronic regulator of FeN4. The most important point was that the RDS at the diatomic site was the generation of the O* intermediate, which was different from the RDS on FeN4.

Now the active sites of MNx configuration have been popularized and show superior performance in the CO2RR, ORR, and other directions. Moreover, the catalytic mechanism of related reactions has been explained in detail from the aspects of reaction rate-limiting steps, energy barriers, and interactions. However, because the interaction between bimetals is difficult to explore, there are relatively few studies on the catalytic mechanism at the active site of M1M2, which is also the direction that researchers need to jointly research. On the other hand, the wide popularity of MOF-derived electrical SACs has led to a thorough study of its catalytic mechanism. However, based on thermal catalysis and photocatalysis, there are relatively few MOF-derived SACs. Therefore, great efforts should be made to expand the mechanism system of these two directions.

In short, symmetric (MN4) and asymmetric (MN4) active sites, as well as bimetallic (M1M2) active sites, play a critical role in controlling the orientation of the monatomic coordination environment in order to improve the catalytic activity of the catalyst. Of course, altering the type of metal active centers, the type of ligands, the type of solvents, or the morphology of the carriers to incorporate heteroatoms (O, S, or P) can improve the ability of MOF-derived materials to anchor single metal atoms. In addition, the electronic structure of the metal center can be effectively improved to achieve the construction of diversified active sites, allowing for the precise control of the coordination environment of single atoms to improve the activity of active sites. These are effective improvement measures. But it is still necessary to conduct in-depth research on the MOF-derived SACs to understand the complex relationship between the microstructure and reactivity of SACs and even to realize further systematic research on the mechanism because there are few research directions on the regulation of single-atom active sites at the atomic level.

4. The application of SACs derived from MOFs in the catalysis field

Up to now, MOF-derived SACs have been widely used in the field of catalysis. SACs not only make full use of the active metal centers at the single-atomic sites but also reduce the metal load, providing broad prospects for the stable and efficient development of industrial catalysis.158 Various preparation methods of MOF-derived SACs and the mechanisms of action of diverse metal active sites were introduced previously. Combined with their significant advantages, it is indisputable that such catalysts are prosperous. At present, the field of electrocatalysis has achieved relatively mature development, while thermocatalysis and photocatalysis still need to be carefully considered. In order to deepen the understanding of MOF-derived SACs and realize the optimization of SACs as soon as possible, the application of MOF-derived SACs in electrocatalysis, photocatalysis and thermocatalysis will be introduced in detail below.

4.1 Electrocatalysis

In clean energy, electrocatalysis has shown great application potential and made a great contribution to green development. In the direction of electrocatalysis, traditional metal nanoparticle catalysts, metal cluster catalysts, etc., also have excellent activity, but with the unique properties of single-atom catalysts being discovered, they have gradually become a new research area in electrocatalysis. The existence of MOFs and their derivatives provides strong support for the immobilization of single atoms, thus realizing the ideal single-atom electrocatalysts. Not only are the original advantages of MOF materials retained, but also this type of catalyst can make full use of the metal atoms on the carriers even under the conditions of low metal loading, thus improving the catalytic activity and excellent turnover frequency value. There has been much use of MOF-derived SACs in electrocatalysis, for example, precious metal-based (Pt, Ru) materials can effectively promote electrochemical reactions.159,160 However, because of the high cost, the research is turned to the invention of non-noble metal SACs, as shown in Table 2, which mainly summarizes the research progress of some non-noble metal SACs in recent years. In this module, we summarize the application of MOF-derived SACs in electrocatalysis, such as the electrochemical carbon dioxide reduction reaction (CO2RR), oxygen reduction reaction (ORR), oxygen evolution reaction (OER), hydrogen evolution reaction (HER), and nitrogen reduction reaction (NRR).
4.1.1 The electrochemical CO2 reduction reaction (CO2RR). The excessive consumption of fossil fuels leads to excessive generation of carbon dioxide (CO2), and the conversion of CO2 into a series of chemicals (carbon organic matter, etc.), so as to turn waste into treasure, is considered a green treatment method. With the development of the CO2RR, SACs have been developed due to their isolated metal sites and maximum atomic efficiency. Due to the relatively high price of precious metal (Pt, Ir, Ru) single-atom catalysts, various non-precious metal catalysts are emerging. Currently, non-noble metal elements mainly used for the preparation of single atom catalysts are mainly concentrated in transition metal elements (Mn, Fe, Co). But owing to the limitations of the reaction itself and the influence of the HER,167,168 developing long-term stability, low overpotential, and high-efficiency materials for the CO2RR as potential electrocatalysts remains a challenging task. In addition, the emergence of MOFs, a new material with high surface area, high porosity, and fully exposed active sites, improves the process of mass and energy transfer in the CO2RR. There is no doubt that various improvement measures frequently appear, and the research of MOF-derived SACs is a hot field at present. According to literature reports, carbon monoxide (CO), formic acid (HCOOH), methanol (CH3OH), methane (CH4), and ethanol (C2H5OH) are the main products of the CO2RR.26,45,169 Therefore, in the following section, we will focus on products to introduce the current research status of the electrocatalytic CO2RR.

Under electrification conditions, there are generally only three steps to convert CO2 into CO: (i) CO2 + H+ + e *COOH; (ii) *COOH + H+ + e *CO + H2O; (iii) *CO * + CO. H2 is a by-product accompanying production, so limiting the HER is also an important part of the design of CO2RR catalysts. Currently, some non-noble metal (Fe, Co, and Ni) catalysts can replace the precious metal catalyst well.170 More importantly, the emergence of MOF-derived SACs also provides a feasible research for reducing the cost of noble catalysts.

Zhang and colleagues94 designed Fe-SACs with a CO FE of 95.0%. They formed an FeN4 site structure with axial O coordination through FeN and FeO chelation, resulting in Fe-SACs with FeN4O active centers with a maximum CO FE of 95% at 0.50 V (Fig. 21a). DFT theoretical calculation showed that O doping not only reduced CO binding energy, but also effectively inhibited the HER (Fig. 21b). The high electronegativity of oxygen not only broke the electron symmetry but also enhanced the strength of the metalcarrier electron interaction (EMSI) at the MN4 position.171 Unfortunately, traditional calcination methods are difficult to accurately control the coordination configuration of oxygen atoms. It has been proved that the highly electronegative oxygen not only broke the electron symmetry but also enhanced the strength of EMSI on MN4.172 Therefore, the addition of heteroatoms to Fe for coordination is helpful to promote the performance of the catalysts.

4.1.2 The electrochemical oxygen reduction reaction (ORR). The ORR at an air cathode is a very important electrochemical reaction. Typically, in acidic media, the reaction pathways fall into two categories,181 the two-electron pathway of hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) and the four-electron pathway of water (H2O). Among them, the four-electron reduction path is preferable for the ORR, because harmful peroxides will be produced in the two-electron reduction process, which is not conducive to the stability of the battery.182 There is no doubt that the efficient control of the production of intermediates in the ORR is the key to accelerate the reduction of O2, especially the regulation of the adsorption energy of intermediates.183 Therefore, it is necessary to effectively adjust the electronic structure of the catalytic center to reduce the overpotential of the reaction and achieve rapid transformation. At present, MOF-derived precious metal-based (platinum, gold, iridium) SACs are relatively advanced electrocatalysts in the market. Liu et al.184 summarized the effects of active sites of precious metals (PMs) with different loads on the ORR. However, high cost and poor toxicity resistance inhibit the development, so there is an urgent need to develop O economical ORR catalysts, with strong tolerance, and high activity. In addition, in order to prepare high active sites for MOF-derived SACs, two aspects are mainly explored to change the metal or adjust the structure of the carriers. Therefore, the application of MOF-derived SACs to the ORR will be introduced from these two directions.

FeNC SACs are currently expected to be one of the substitutes for platinum-based catalysts. It is widely believed that the FeNx structure with uneven charge distribution plays a role in promoting oxygen adsorption and reducing the OO bond energy in the ORR.185 For example, Wang and colleagues132 designed a MOF-derived Fe-SAC with a three-dimensional structure. As shown in Fig. 24ac, FeNC with a 3D structure containing 1.70 wt% Fe had higher onset potential (Eonset) and half-wave potential (0.90 V and 0.82 V, respectively) under acidic conditions than the most advanced Pt/C. In addition, the ORR on 3D FeNC satisfied the four-electron reaction path, indicating that O2 is completely converted to H2O. After 10000 cycles, the E1/2 of 3D FeNC did not have a large shift. It was precisely because of their micro/nano structures that hydrophobic surfaces were constructed to facilitate the transport of reactants. Besides, Zheng and colleagues186 investigated the application of Fe-SACs with a two-dimensional lamellar structure to the ORR in acidic and alkaline electrolytes. By introducing g-C3N4 as a nitrogen source and Fe-SAs as the stabilizer, an Fe/NPCNS catalyst with rich active sites (FeN4) was prepared. The E1/2 in acidic and alkaline solutions was 0.86 V and 0.79 V, respectively, and was much larger than the contrast samples prepared. And their ORR durability was superior to that of Pt/C catalysts from the market (Fig. 24dg).

4.1.3 Electrochemical water splitting. Electrochemical water splitting is considered as a renewable hydrogen energy technology. The electrolysis process of water is mainly divided into two half reactions, the hydrogen evolution reaction (HER), and oxygen evolution reaction (OER). Electrocatalysts are needed for the hydrolysis reaction, and efficient catalysis can reduce overpotential and accelerate product generation.195 Traditionally, the Tafel slope is used to determine the kinetics of the HER and OER, which is ideal if you can get a small Tafel slope. At present, precious metals (platinum, ruthenium, and silver) are relatively efficient catalysts in the directions of the OER and HER,196,197 but their high cost and limited resources limit the development of hydrolysis. Therefore, suitable catalysts, with low cost and high activity, have been sought by a large number of scholars. MOF-based SACs have made a breakthrough in the direction of water cracking. This section mainly describes the progress related to MOF-derived SACs for water splitting and provides a thorough comprehension of the role of catalytic active centers.

The OER is a reaction occurring at the anode of the water cracking process and plays an important role in many electrochemical devices. Under acidic and alkaline conditions, O2 production has completely different mechanisms. Intermediates (O*, HO*, HOO*) that play a key role in catalytic activity are generated in the reaction process. The weak binding of oxygen makes the oxidation of HO* become the rate-determining step (RDS), while the strong binding of oxygen makes the formation of HOO* become the RDS,198 so it is difficult to control the oxygen binding process. Furthermore, SACs derived from MOFs mainly form carbon and nitrogen materials, which will fully combine with carbon, resulting in poor OER performance.199 Therefore, for the sake of improving the performance of OER catalysts, it is of practical meaning to regulate the formation of intermediates and effectively inhibit the binding of carbon, which means to control the coordination environment of the active centers, so as to accurately regulate the binding of oxygen and carbon.

For example, Wang and Wu et al.124 used Hofmann-type MOF materials to design a FeNiHF catalyst with 290 mV overpotential, which had the best OER performance compared with other samples. Compared with CoNiHF (65 mV dec1) and NiNiHF (60 mV dec1), the Tafel slope of FeNiHF (44 mV dec1) was the lowest in Fig. 28a, indicating that the catalytic OER step was the fastest. In addition, the charge transfer resistance of FeNiHF was 2.97 , showing the lowest resistance, as can be seen from the electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) diagrams (Fig. 28b). Through theoretical simulation, it was proved that the FeNi active center was more efficient for the formation of intermediates (OH* and O*). There is no doubt that with the help of MOF nanosheets, electron conduction was beneficial and electron transfer was promoted in the OER.

Above all, the most noteworthy is the preparation of SACs which can be applied to a variety of catalytic reactions. It is well known that in the air pole of a metalair secondary battery, the ORR occurs during discharge and the OER occurs during charge, and both have high overpotential and slow dynamics. Therefore, in order to meet the requirements of both reactions, the intrinsic and apparent activity must be improved.201 Obviously, the construction of bi-functional SACs is mainly realized from the microstructure morphology and electronic structure or it depends on the synergy between components, which also lays a foundation for the research and development of other advanced bi-functional materials.

Molecular hydrogen (H2) is a kind of renewable and clean fuel, which is helpful to solve the energy crisis. It can be directly generated by the HER, and the HER is the reaction occurring at the cathode in the process of water cracking, which is conducive to the production of high-purity hydrogen. There are two main reaction pathways in the HER, the VolmerHeyrovsky mechanism (H+ + e H*; H* + H+ + e H2 + *) and the VolmerTafel mechanism (H+ + e H*; 2H* H2 + 2*).33,202 Currently, Pt/C catalysts are mainly used in the market as high catalytic materials for the HER. Due to the high cost and other factors, other methods, such as the emergence of SACs, have been developed to replace expensive catalysts. Among them, MOF-derived SACs have made some progress as HER electrocatalysts.

Xing and Gao203 produced a MOF-derived electrocatalyst (Ru-ZIF-900) for the HER with atom-dispersed Ru sites using in situ domain limiting and showing better performance than a commercial Pt/C electrocatalyst in alkaline media. They confirmed the existence of Ru atomic sites using XAFS technology. Ru-ZIF-900 was shown to have higher TOF (9.38 H2 per s), lower overpotential (51.6 mV), larger ECSA (147 cm2), and excellent activity and stability (Fig. 29). Because the ruthenium atom site was located on a unique structure, the free energy of water dissociation in the HER step was significantly optimized, resulting in improved performance. In addition, W-SAC derived from the UiO material only needs to satisfy the overpotential (10) of 85 mV in HER to obtain a high current density. Compared with other comparison samples, Chen et al.108 also confirmed that the catalyst was more conducive to hydrogen evolution from the perspective of the Tafel slope (53 mV dec1), mainly because W-SAC had a faster charge transfer ability. After durability testing, W-SAC did not show significant activity decline, which was believed to be due to the strong interaction between W-SAs and the carrier with the help of unique active sites like W1N1C3, leading to high stability. Subsequently, they also carried out extended research and prepared SACs based on In and Sn under the same synthesis conditions. It has been proved that this project has good universality and provides unique insights for the development of electrochemical HER.

4.1.4 The electrochemical nitrogen reduction (NRR). Ammonia gas (NH3) is a reactant in all kinds of important industrial processes (fertilizer, nitric acid) and efficient NH3 output efficiency is the main goal now. At present, more than 90% of NH3 is derived from the HaberBosch reaction, but the reaction conditions require high temperature (573773 K) and high pressure (100200 atm). Therefore, scholars are working on an alternative method, such as the reduction of NH3 by fixing N2 under mild conditions. The emergence of electrochemistry has realized the desire of the NRR to generate NH3 under environmental conditions. The reduction of N2 to NH3 involves the transfer of multiple protons and electrons.204 In the MOF-derived SACs discussed now, single atoms mostly exist in the form of positive valence,205 which can effectively inhibit proton adsorption and improve N2 adsorption capacity. Therefore, combined with the advantages of porous materials, MOF-derived SACs can be a good candidate for catalysts required by the NRR.

In 2018, Geng's team206 achieved a record high yield of NH3 (120.9 gNH3 mgcat1 h1), which was a good indication of the future of NRR. Later, Qin et al.145 prepared catalysts for CoNxC by coprecipitation and pyrolysis. Although not as good as Geng's team, they also contributed to the development of the NRR. Surprisingly, the ammonia production rates of CoNxC at 0.9 V and 1.0 V are 37.6 g mg1 h1 and 33.2 g mg1 h1, respectively, which are higher than those of the Pt/C catalyst. The great properties depend on the CoN3C active site, which reduces the activation energy of the RDS of the NRR, favored N2 adsorption, and effectively controlled the occurrence of hydrogen evolution reaction, thereby promoting N2 reduction.

In summary, the electrochemical application of MOF-derived SACs includes the CO2RR, ORR, OER, HER, and NRR. The main research directions are the ORR and CO2RR, and reviews based on the two directions have been widely circulated.159,207 Fe, Co, and other non-noble metals are mostly used in the study of the ORR, while Ni-based SACs are mostly used in the study of the CO2RR, and the CO2RR electrocatalysts are mainly used in the study of the electrochemical reduction of CO2 to CO, while relatively little research has been done on other products. On the one hand, how to speed up the process of multi-electron transfer may be a difficult link to overcome. On the other hand, the formation of multi-carbon products depends on the affinity of CO, and the activation energy of the CC bond is high. The fracture of the CH bond and the CO bond is relatively dominant, which is not conducive to the coupling of CO. This greatly limits the application scope of SACs for the CO2RR. It should be noted that for the OER and HER in water cracking reaction, these metal SACs or DSACs are often used in alkaline media and are unstable under acidic conditions, which is also the reason why it is difficult to overcome at present. To solve this problem, the concept of non-carbon SACs has been proposed, but it has not been implemented in the experiment. For the NRR, higher overpotential and stronger NN bond both affect the performance efficiency of the NRR.208 In addition, there is serious competition between the HER and NRR.209 And the HER needs to be suppressed in the reduction process of N2. Therefore, the development of advanced catalysts that can improve the adsorption of reactants, suppress side reactions, and improve faradaic efficiency is the primary focus of the NRR at present. Comparatively speaking, it is easier to prepare MOF-derived SACs applied to the ORR and CO2RR, and it is also easier to build the corresponding catalytic mechanism system, which is convenient for future generations to learn.

4.2 Photocatalysis

Photocatalysis is a redox process in which the catalysts absorb photons to generate electrons and holes.210 Photocatalytic reactions mainly involve three steps: light collection, catalysis, and charge generation and separation. There are many SACs based on photoreaction, such as noble metal-based SACs for selective reduction of CO2 to methane.211,212 Based on MOF materials, especially UiO materials,10 as the carriers of photocatalysts, they are also materials with relatively great potential. After all, they can use themselves and their derivatives to restrict single atoms to prepare various SACs. Therefore, it is very feasible to use MOF derivatives to prepare single-atom photocatalysts.213 The synergy between organic ligands and inorganic metals in MOFs can provide ligand-to-metal charge transfer/metal-to-ligand charge transfer (LMCT/MLCT), reflecting their good light absorption properties.214 In addition, single-atom photocatalysts (SAPCs) with single metal atom active sites can collect shorter distance charge transfer carriers than catalysts synthesized by metal nanoparticles, thus improving the charge dynamics of the carriers. Moreover, the special coordination environment and electronic structure of single atoms can effectively change the metalcarrier interaction, thus regulating the interfacial species conversion via improving charge transfer properties.28

In general, stable metal ions can be reduced to atoms by photogenerated electrons in photocatalysis.215 Therefore, the introduction of SACs in photocatalysis combined with the interaction between Me-SAs and carriers can affect the three steps mentioned above. At present, SACs have made remarkable progress in electrocatalysis, but photocatalysis is still in the early stage. It is precisely because the MOF matrix is an ideal photosensitive material for photocatalysis. Due to the existence of organic ligands, it has a large range of light absorption, and the advantage of the porous structure of MOFs can further reduce the recombination probability of electronhole (eh) volume pair in the semiconductor, thus promoting the photocatalytic reaction. SACs based on MOFs are an ideal photocatalytic materials with a wide range of functions.216 If you are interested in single-atom photocatalytic materials based on original MOFs, please refer to the articles of Liang207 and Liu.217 SACs combine single atoms as electron acceptors and catalytically active centers with various MOFs as photosensitizers and stabilizers, which help to significantly enhance the photocatalytic reduction reaction.61,218 However, up to now, MOF-derived SAPCs are still in the process of gradual exploration, so it is worth exploring for researchers.

As we all know, due to a series of problems caused by excessive emissions of carbon dioxide (CO2),219 carbon neutrality has been strongly advocated and various emission reduction measures have been widely applied. From the perspective of green and sustainable, it is a wise choice to absorb solar energy to catalyze the conversion of CO2 into valuable chemicals, such as CH4 (ref. 220) and so on, referring to the photosynthesis of plants. However, the establishment of efficient catalytic systems needs to be put on the agenda. Recently, some scholars221 realized CO2 reduction and hydrogen production with SACs prepared from triazinyl covalent frameworks (CTFs), which has solved some difficulties for photocatalysis. Currently, MOF-derived SACs are considered as potential CO2RR photocatalysts due to their maximum atomic efficiency and the advantage of MOFs in collecting sunlight.222 Classically, photocatalysts can absorb light energy to activate CO2 and convert light energy to chemical energy.223 Therefore, mass transfer and light absorption efficiency have a great influence on photocatalysts, both of which depend on specific surface area and porous structure.224 Therefore, efficient CO2RR photocatalysts constructed for the complex reduction reaction of CO2 (ref. 207) can start from the carrier material. For example, Zhang's team225 used a ligand replacement method to prepare Co-MOLs which increased the specific surface area, and obtained a variety of g-C3N4-MOLs catalysts by mixed pyrolysis with urea. In particular, when g-C3N4-MOLs-30 was used in the CO2RR, 9.28 mol CO and 1.85 mol H2 were reduced and the CO selectivity was 83.4% (Fig. 30). The photocatalytic performance of g-C3N4-MOLs-30 was much better than that of many g-C3N4-based photocatalysts, thanks to the fact that the active site of Co(II) was fixed on g-C3N4, which improved its optical performance and promoted the transfer of photogenerated charge so that the CO2 molecules adsorbed on the surface gained electrons and reduced to CO. It is worth mentioning that this design opens up a new way to establish a simple and economical field of photochemical energy conversion.

In the direction of nitrogen fixation, people have studied many methods for N2 fixation, such as enzyme catalysis and so on. Not only electrocatalysis can generate NRR under ambient conditions, but the emergence of a photocatalytic N2 reduction system also provides a direction for finding a mild method,226,227 in particular photocatalytic nitrogen reduction (NRR) has achieved great progress in nitrogen fixation. Because the catalysts contain uniformly dispersed atoms, there are many clear active sites, which can greatly improve catalytic performance.24 Moreover, it has been reported228 that single atoms have a significant effect on electron transfer, which is crucial for molecular adsorption and activation, so it emphasizes the great potential of SACs in the field of photocatalytic NRR, especially the addition of MOF materials with light collection ability and porous structure in the catalyst. Generally, there are three reaction pathways for N2 reduction to NH3, including dissociation, enzyme promotion, and association.229 The electrocatalyzed NRR and HaberBosch reactionis are mostly dissociation and enzyme mechanism, while the photocatalytic NRR is the association mechanism.

Shang's team230 designed MOF-derived Al-PMOF(Fe) SACs using the porphyrin ligand, which was a photosensitive agent that efficiently traps light, and applied them to the NRR under light. The NH3 yield and formation rate of Al-PMOF(Fe) are 635 g1 and 127 g h1 g1, respectively, which are 82% and 50% higher than those of the iron-free single-atom Al-PMOF catalyst (Fig. 32a and b), respectively. Moreover, Al-PMOF(Fe) exhibited good hydrolysis stability and three photocatalytic NRR cycles had been carried out. The reason was that the presence of FeN active sites could effectively promote the absorption of N2 by Al-PMOF(Fe), and the key was to delay the electronhole recombination so that the SAC showed high catalytic performance. Meanwhile, the catalytic mechanism of the NRR reaction was explored in combination with DFT theory, as shown in Fig. 32c and d. They used associative alternation and distal pathways to explore the specific catalytic mechanism at the active site and confirmed that the RDS of the reaction was the activation of N2 and that the activation of N2 was significantly reduced with the help of FeN sites. During the reaction, the energy barrier of the alternating process was lower and the distal pathway showed higher free energy in subsequent steps. There is no doubt that the association alternating mechanism was the main reaction pathway of NRR under the action of the Al-PMOF(Fe) photocatalyst. In this work, they explored the efficient catalytic reduction of N2 by Fe-SACs derived from porphyrins under light conditions and adopted the method of combining experimental and theoretical calculation, which has guiding significance for constructing the reaction mechanism of the NRR.

In the field of hydrogen evolution, MOF-derived SACs are also involved. Zhang et al.231 developed a CuSATiO2 photocatalyst derived from MIL-125, which resulted in a H2 precipitation rate of 101.7 mmol g1 h1 and achieved a quantum efficiency of 56% at 365 nm. In another study,232 the same team mentioned that the coupling of Cu single atoms (Cu-SAs) with O atoms on the surface accelerated the transfer of photo-generated charge carriers from TiO2 to Cu-SAs, ultimately increasing the rate of hydrogen evolution. Of course, the excellent hydrogen evolution activity is mainly due to the existence of copper single atoms capable of in situ self-healing and MOF carriers with a high specific surface, which facilitates effective charge separation. Obviously, MOF-derived SACs can also be well applied to the CO2RR, NRR, HER, and other reactions in the field of photocatalysis, which opens the way for the design of advanced materials with excellent photocatalytic activity and durability.

4.3 Thermocatalysis

From the development of the current situation, MOF-derived SACs develop slowly in the direction of thermal and photocatalysis, but people still focus on the study of efficient MOF-derived thermal SACs, such as hydrogenation or dehydrogenation of some organic compounds. The following was mainly from the hydrogenation reaction and oxidation reaction corresponding to the MOF-derived SACs used in the direction of thermocatalysis.

Aniline derivatives derived from catalytic hydrogenation of nitroaromatic hydrocarbons are very important in organic synthesis and mainly involved in the production of dyes, drugs, pigments, and other chemicals.233 He's team119 synthesized a SAC named Co SAs/NC-800 for the nitro aromatics hydrogenation reaction. Under the condition of a certain amount of H2, the yield of aniline synthesized by the catalyst (Fig. 33ac) was as high as 99.8%, and the TOF was 18.2 h1, which was significantly different from the comparison sample. More importantly, Co SAs/NC-800 had higher catalytic activity than many previous non-noble metal catalysts. The catalytic activity of Co SAs/NC-800 was mainly attributed to the existence of the CoN site. Moreover, after 5 cycles, the conversion rate and selectivity remained basically unchanged. In addition, Co SAs/NC-800 could also catalyze the selective hydrogenation of nitroaromatic hydrocarbons with different substituents (yield > 90%). For one thing, the addition of Co SAs increased the specific surface area of contact, which was beneficial to the adsorption and dissociation of H2. For another, pyrolysis changed the coordination environment of the active sites to generate a larger porous structure and cause Co atoms to form CoNx coordination, thereby accelerating the electron transfer of Co to H2. In addition, Sun and his team234 also designed a Co-SAC based on the SiO2 template method and applied in the hydrogenation reaction of nitroaromatic hydrocarbons. They conducted comparative experiments under different conditions and revealed that the hydrogenation performance of Co@mesoNC catalyst (Fig. 33e and f) depended on H2 pressure and reaction temperature. In addition, Co@mesoNC showed excellent nitrobenzene conversion and aniline selectivity under certain conditions. In fact, the high Zn/Co mole ratio in BIMZIF precursors and the silica protection strategy were critical to prevent the formation of cobalt nanoparticles and to generate atomically dispersed CoNx sites that facilitated the rate of catalytic hydrogenation. MOF-derived SACs could also be applied to quinoline hydrogenation (QHR). Guo et al.235 prepared a Co-SAs@NHOPC thermocatalyst with an orderly structure and applied it to selective hydrogenation of quinoline by template method. Under certain circumstances, the selectivity of the catalyst remained above 99% and the conversion of quinoline was 100%. However, compared with Co-NPs@NHOPC (82%), Co-SAs@NC (66%), and Co-NPs@NC (42%), the Co-SAs@NHOPC catalytic reaction yielded 99%, showing a significant advantage (Fig. 33g and h). Experiments have shown that the size and distribution of metal particles had a great effect on the performance of catalysts. Moreover, Co-SAs@NHOPC had lower temperature and hydrogen pressure than other cobalt-based catalysts at the same yield. Moreover, the yield did not decrease significantly after 5 cycles, indicating that Co-SAC showed good stability and repeatability. The purpose of this work is to make SACs with a hierarchical structure more widely applied.

Naturally, SACs can also be used for the oxidation of some organic compounds. A Co-based catalyst for the dehydrogenation of formic acid (FA) has been reported previously, but due to the coexistence of single atoms and nanoparticles, no active species have been identified.236 Subsequently, Li and colleagues78 designed a Co-SAC with good FA dehydrogenation activity. Their work (Fig. 33d) illustrated that CoNC (SACs) had different hydrogen selectivity in different solvents, especially in propyl carbonate (PC), where the yield of CoNC(SACs) was increased by about 150% compared with CoNC(NPs) at 98 C. In addition, Co-SAC exhibited high acid resistance and activity at a low temperatures of 75 C compared to other inactive catalysts. In combination with various characterization techniques, it was suggested that CoIINx was the best active site for the FA dehydrogenation catalyst, and the stability of the catalyst was tested. Only slight inactivation of CoNC(SACs) was found after 120 h of catalytic reaction. However, excessive HCOOH at high temperature could cause the loss of CoNx, which was an inevitable problem. But in general, this work provides some insights for exploring the application of MOF-derived SACs in thermal catalysis. In addition, Sun and colleagues237 prepared an Fe1NC single-atom catalyst using ZIF material and used it in the reaction of oxidizing aromatic alcohol to nitrile. The reaction was carried out at a certain temperature. Compared with other inactive catalysts, Fe1NC showed more than 99% conversion and >99% yield after 24 h. Moreover, the catalytic oxidation of benzyl alcohol to benzaldehyde was the rate-determining step for the generation of nitrile.

In conclusion, MOF-derived SACs are now mostly applicable to a small number of reduction reactions (hydrogenation of nitroaromatic hydrocarbons and quinoline) and oxidation reactions in the realm of heat catalysis (dehydrogenation of formic acid and oxidation of aromatic alcohols to nitrile). As a result, the application of MOF-derived SACs in thermocatalysis still needs to be explored for a long time, and the development of MOF-derived thermal SACs is the top priority in the area of thermocatalysis.

5. The characterization techniques of SACs derived from MOFs

As research on SACs progresses, various advanced characterization techniques with high-resolution performance are gradually established and improved. To verify the relationship between microstructure and performance of MOF-derived SACs, we need powerful techniques, that is, it is necessary to carefully identify the structure of MOF-derived single-atom sites in order to further explore their catalytic performance. This will allow us to provide accurate phenomena of the catalytic process and reveal the reaction mechanism as much as possible. Currently, the study of single-atom active sites has expanded to include some other high-tech technologies in addition to traditional characterization techniques like transmission electron microscopy (TEM), X-ray diffraction (XRD), thermogravimetric (TG), X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), Raman spectroscopy, nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR), infrared spectroscopy (IR), etc., including the scanning tunneling microscopy (STM), high-resolution TEM (HRTEM), high angle ring dark field scanning transmission electron microscopy (HAADF-STEM), electron energy loss spectroscopy (EELS), single crystal X-ray diffraction (SCXRD), X-ray fine absorption structure (XAFS), surface-enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS), surface interrogation scanning electrochemical microscopy (SI-SECM), Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) and energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDX). Since there have been many evaluations of these ex situ and in situ operando techniques3,19,238 and because the structure of MOF-derived SACs varies during the reaction process, it is challenging for ex situ operando techniques to provide reliable real-time information. So the majority of references239,240 are about in situ operando characterization methods. In this section, we give a brief introduction to the use of HAADF-STEM, XAFS, and other identification methods in MOF-derived SACs, mainly dealing with the advantages and disadvantages of each characterization method and suggesting the necessity of a combination of characterization techniques to confirm the microstructure of SACs.

HAADF-STEM is able to visually represent the distribution of single atoms on the carriers.33 Compared with the images of TEM, the images obtained by HAADF-STEM are more sensitive to the atomic number but not to the thickness of the catalyst, making the heavy and light atoms show different bright and dark regions, which can be used to recognize the presence of single atoms on the carriers. For example, Sun et al.150 used the HAADF-STEM technique to identify a clear high density of isolated bright spots (Fe atoms) around Co nanoparticles, and it was also clear that Fe atoms were uniformly dispersed on the carbon layer by combining EDX. In another study, Zhang et al.231 confirmed the presence of Cu atoms only in Ti vacancies by the HAADF-STEM method and also clarified the stability of Cu single atoms located on TiO2 by comparing the HAADF-STEM of the catalyst after photocatalytic reaction, laterally responding to the porous nature of MIL-125 (Tiv) and the strong anchoring of Cu ions by unsaturated bonds. Furthermore, Ma et al.232 analyzed the results by using XPS, XRD, and HAADF-STEM to conclude that Cu SAs in MOF-derived SACs replaced certain Ti atoms and were anchored by O atoms to produce +2 and +1 valences. HAADF-STEM, however, has flaws as well and is only appropriate for the examination of a few chosen places rather than the overall analysis of the samples.

In addition, the implementation of SACs derived from MOFs likewise heavily relies on XAFS technology. A potent approach for verifying the presence of single atoms in catalysts is the introduction of XAFS in combination with HAADF-STEM. As is well known, XAFS can be divided into X-ray absorption near edge spectroscopy (XANES)45 and extended X-ray absorption fine structure spectroscopy (EXAFS).47 XANES can detect the oxidation states, coordination states, and electronic structures of Me-SAs, and EXAFS can simulate the quantitative structural information of metal coordination number and bond length. For instance, Wang et al.176 inferred the oxidation states of various metals in MNC materials by using the XANES measurement method, and confirmed the absence of metalmetal bonds with the help of EXAFS technology, and accomplished the anchoring of Me-SAs, most notably by fitting the results to show the formation of MN4 structures. The combination of XPS and XAFS results then clarified the presence of a single atom with four pyridine N atoms coordinated in the catalyst. In other examples, Han and his colleagues156 clarified the synthesis of diatomic CoNi coordination with the help of XAFS technology, and the resulting data were subjected to DFT calculations to construct a possible dual single-atom model. However, the data processing of XAFS is quite complicated, and a peak may be the superposition of multiple ligating atoms, and a peak at the same position in different samples does not necessarily represent the same coordination, thus limiting the repeatability of XAFS results.

In conclusion, this section mainly provided a brief introduction to HAADF-STEM and XAFS techniques, both of which played a crucial role in the identification of SAs. Of course, it was also necessary to fully study the microstructure of MOF-derived SACs in combination with a variety of characterization methods in order to correlate with the catalytic activity. For example, the EELS energy spectrum can not only be used to determine the types of light elements and measure the thickness of samples but also can be combined with HAADF-STEM images to achieve the analysis of element valence states, chemical bond, and other information.241 In addition, STM is applicable to the dispersion of SAs on the surface of catalysts.242 Most notably, in situ FTIR can provide relevant information about active sites, is highly sensitive to CO molecules adsorbed on single-atom sites, and can propose a reaction path of CO catalysis on SACs.240 Although the combination of these technologies can obtain more accurate conclusions, these cannot clarify the relationship between the properties of catalytic sites and structureproperties. Therefore, it is urgent to develop powerful characterization tools that can reveal the catalytic mechanisms at the active sites of SAs.

6. Summary and outlook

The MOF derivatives can maintain some characteristics of MOF precursors, coupled with the excellent properties of SACs. Therefore, MOF-derived SACs have shown great advantages in bridging the difference between homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysts. In this paper, it is first introduced that derivatives based on different MOF carriers can provide different coordination environments to effectively anchor metal ions, and then the subsequent introduction of different MOF-derived SACs can result in different active sites, which can affect the catalytic mechanism and the performance of catalysts. Specially, the application of electrocatalysis, photocatalysis, and thermocatalysis in recent years is reviewed and the important characterization methods for the identification of SACs are briefly introduced. MOF-derived SACs are still the focus of future development due to their excellent catalytic capacity. There are still some challenges in the future.

(1) It is not yet mature to precisely control the coordination environment of single metal atoms. At present, the theoretical modeling and calculation of catalysts can be used as a means to study the mechanism of catalytic reaction, but completely relying on theoretical means to explore the coordination environment of metal single atoms and the catalytic effect of electronic structure, can lead the researchers to reduce the exploration of the actual structure. Therefore, increasingly advanced characterization instruments should be fully utilized to provide detailed structural information to make the theory more plausible.

(2) At present, the types of MOF derivatives need to be expanded. Now, only a few MOF materials such as ZIFs, IRMOFs, UiOs, PCNs, and MILs are used as precursors for the construction of SACs. Expanding the species is conducive to the preparation of diverse MOF-derived SACs, and it is also possible to extensively study MOF derivatives doped with other species. For example, in the study of composite materials, the synergy between the metals and the carriers is used to change the binding site provided by the carrier to adjust the coordination environment of single atoms. MOF-derived catalysts with a variety of Me-SAs can also be studied. Each metal has different properties. For the complex system of multiphase catalysis, the characteristics of each metal species can be fully utilized, such that the presence of Ce species is conducive to the oxygen storage function of the catalysts, so as to realize the optimization of the catalysts. However, it is still necessary to consider the problem that a variety of single atoms are easy to aggregate or even deactivate, and the proportion of different single atoms is difficult to control. In addition, different MOF carriers have different coordination for different Me-SAs, which make the development of this type of catalyst more difficult. Therefore, the research on MOF-derived SACs still has a long way to go, and there is an urgent need to develop diversified and even multifunctional catalysts to contribute to the progress in this direction.

(3) High-density single-atom strategies are necessary to improve catalytic performance. However, studies have reported that the metal loading in SACs basically does not exceed 1.5 wt%. Of course, many researchers have designed SACs with high loadings, which also provide high-loading single-atom catalysts in a feasible direction. In addition, the preparation of high-load SACs should meet the actual demand of metal single atom and energy conversion, which requires the prevention of single-atom aggregation, and the coordination environment of metal sites should be regulated to meet the demand, so as to provide suggestions for improving the catalytic performance of SACs.

(4) The general system is not yet widespread. A universal method has been established for anchoring Me-SAs with MOF derivatives, including the application of single metal atoms or even multiple metal atoms, such as the domino reaction strategy, cascade anchoring strategy, and template-assisted strategy previously reported. These strategies can systematize the preparation methods of SACs, and also ensure that the prepared SACs have excellent activity, stability, selectivity, and high loading capacity, which can be used for reference to the manufacture of MOF-derived SACs to improve the general system.

In addition, most of the MOF-derived single-atom catalysts are currently used for electrocatalytic applications, while directions of photocatalytic and thermocatalytic are still in the preliminary research stage. The reasons for this are as follows: firstly, not all MOF materials are suitable for photocatalysis. At present, UiOs, MILs, and other materials are mainly used, and most of them need the help of other materials to produce composite materials better for photocatalytic reactions. Second, only a few MOF-derived SACs have been used in thermocatalytic studies, mainly involving evolution hydrogenation, dehydrogenation, and alcohol oxidation. For thermocatalysis, the density of single-atom active sites is obviously particularly important. However, due to the high density of metal nodes in MOFs, the metals are easily agglomerated, which leads to the reduction of metal single-atom active sites. Therefore, researchers still need to achieve precise regulation of single atoms. Furthermore, the mechanism of MOF-derived SACs on photo- and thermal catalysis is not well defined, which makes it difficult to carry out the reactions on a large scale. Therefore, it is relevant to explore the strategies applicable to light photo- and thermo-catalysis to broaden the direction of MOF-derived SACs. It is of practical significance to explore general strategies for photo- and thermal catalysts to broaden the direction of MOF-derived SACs.

In conclusion, the diversity of MOF structures leads to MOF-derived SACs with strong application prospects in the catalysis field. The relationship between the structure and performance of SACs derived from MOFs has been investigated in detail and strengthened the understanding of heterogeneous catalysis. More importantly, it is vitally significant to design and prepare more efficient catalytic materials, which can be suitable for large-scale production.
