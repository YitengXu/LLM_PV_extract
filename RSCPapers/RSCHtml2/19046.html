Abstract

Organic photovoltaics (OPVs) are a promising technology for indoor low-energy-consumption portable electronic equipment. However, the relatively large voltage loss (Vloss) of OPVs, typically exceeding 0.6 V, limits their application. Here, we develop a wide bandgap (WBG) two-dimensional (2D) linear polymer PE6 with a donorâ€“Ï€â€“acceptor (Dâ€“Ï€â€“A) backbone structure, where 4,8-bis(4-chloro-5-tripropylsilyl-thiophen-2-yl)benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT-T-SiCl), thienothiophene (TT) and benzo[d][1,2,3]triazole (BTA) are used as the D, Ï€ and A units, respectively. PE6 achieves a power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 15.09% when blended with a narrow-bandgap acceptor Y6 (Eg = 1.33 eV), which is one of the highest values in BTA-based photovoltaic polymers. In addition, using classic IT-M (a medium-bandgap acceptor, Eg = 1.60 eV) and BTA3 (a wide-bandgap acceptor, Eg = 1.82 eV) as acceptors, PE6-based OPVs exhibit a high VOC of 1.032 V and 1.292 V, respectively. Notably, the Vloss in the three combinations is in the range of 0.54âˆ¼0.57 V, and the non-radiative voltage loss in the PE6:BTA3 blend is as low as 0.158 V. Therefore, our results indicate that introducing both silicon and chlorine atoms simultaneously on the 2D conjugated side chain is effective to design photovoltaic polymers with decreasing Vloss. In addition, PE6 has the potential as a standard polymer to pair with different types of newly developed non-fullerene acceptors (NFAs).

Introduction

Bulk heterojunction (BHJ) organic photovoltaics (OPVs) are constructed with the device structure of an active layer sandwiched between two electrodes, where p-type and n-type semiconductors are used as the electron donor and acceptor respectively.1â€“5 OPVs have numerous advantages, such as the absorption range that can be finetuned to match different light sources, and the possibility to fabricate large-area, flexible and semi-transparent devices.6,7 These features make OPVs particularly suitable for incorporation into indoor low-energy-consumption portable electronic equipment.8â€“12 With the continuous development of new photovoltaic materials and device engineering, the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of single junction OPVs based on polymer:non-fullerene acceptors (NFAs) has exceeded 18%.13â€“16 In terms of the three important parameters of OPVs, the short-circuit current density (JSC) has reached 28 mA cmâˆ’2, and the fill factor (FF) exceeds 80%,17,18 but the open-circuit voltage (VOC) is usually limited to below 0.9 V. Compared with perovskite solar cells (PSCs), the VOC of OPVs is the least among the three parameters mainly because of the larger voltage loss (Vloss) of organic materials, typically exceeding 0.6 V.19â€“23

To meet the needs of different application scenarios and the rapid development of the Internet of Things (IoT), NFAs with different optoelectronic properties have been synthesized.24,25 Narrow-bandgap materials such as Y6, BTP-eC9, Y6-1O, etc. are widely used in high-efficiency devices,17,26â€“28 and medium/wide-bandgap NFAs represented by BTA3, F-BTA3, ITCC, IOC-Cl, etc. are commonly used in high-voltage systems.29â€“35 Thus, it is challenging to design universal donors that can realize a low Vloss in both high PCE and high VOC systems, which will help to understand the structureâ€“propertyâ€“Vloss relationship. In principle, this kind of polymer needs to possess suitable energy levels, an efficient driving force for charge separation, a high quantum efficiency of electroluminescence (EQEEL) for suppressed Vloss and suitable miscibility with a variety of acceptors.

Compared with homopolymer donors such as MEH-PPV and P3HT,36,37 the donorâ€“Ï€â€“acceptor (Dâ€“Ï€â€“A) type copolymers attracted more attention because of the strong intramolecular charge transfer, tunable absorption spectra and energy levels.38,39 Among numerous electron-accepting (A) units, benzo[d][1,2,3]triazole (BTA) is an important one and has been widely used to construct high performance p-type polymers, especially for J-series polymers containing benzo[1,2-b;4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT) as the D unit and thiophene as the Ï€ bridge respectively.40 Furthermore, with the introduction of heteroatoms, such as fluorine, chlorine, silicon and sulfur, on the thiophene conjugated side chain of the BDT unit, the energy levels can be effectively downshifted and the non-radiative recombination can be inhibited.22,27,41 Regarding the linker between D and A units, we found that by changing thiophene to thienothiophene (TT), the polymer backbone could be tuned from a curved to a linear structure.42,43 The change of Ï€ bridge type can increase the crystallinity and hole mobility of Dâ€“Ï€â€“A polymer, and thus largely improve the PCEs. As a result of these polymer modifications, BTA-based polymers have realized the highest PCEs beyond 14%.43â€“49

In this manuscript, we adopt a linear backbone, where 4,8-bis(4-chloro-5-tripropylsilyl-thiophen-2-yl)benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT-T-SiCl),50 hexylthienothiophene and BTA are used as the D, Ï€ and A units respectively. By simultaneously introducing silicon and chlorine atoms on the side chain, we finally got a wide-bandgap (1.98 eV) polymer PE6 with a low-lying highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) energy level of âˆ’5.53 eV. To verify the generalizability of decreasing the Vloss, PE6 is utilized to combine with three classic NFAs, narrow-bandgap Y6 (Eg = 1.33 eV), medium-bandgap IT-M (Eg = 1.60 eV) and wide-bandgap BTA3 (Eg = 1.82 eV) respectively. We obtained a stepwise increase in VOC in the order of 0.863, 1.032 and 1.292 V, with low voltage losses of 0.543, 0.570 and 0.540 V, respectively. Notably, the PE6:Y6 and PE6:BTA3 combinations achieved the highest PCE (15.09%) and the highest VOC (1.292 V) respectively of the reported BTA-based photovoltaic polymers (summarized in Fig. 1 and Table S1â€ ). Our results prove that the WBG polymer PE6 can be used as a standard polymer in a variety of NFA systems.

The maximum exciton generation rate Gmax and charge dissociation probability P(E,T) were also calculated to study the exciton behaviors by measuring Jph (photocurrent density) âˆ¼Veff (effective voltage) (Fig. 5c and d). The Gmax values of Y6, IT-M and BTA3-based devices are 1.29 Ã— 1027, 7.46 Ã— 1026 and 4.87 Ã— 1026 mâˆ’3 sâˆ’1, which is related to the broader photon absorbance range and high EQE response. Furthermore, the P(E,T) values calculated by Jph/Jsat are 96.6%, 83.1%, and 99.0% for the three blends, in which Jph is the current density under the short-circuit condition and Jsat is the saturated photo-generated current density which can directly reflect the number of absorbed photons. The highest Gmax and relatively high P(E,T) reveal an efficient exciton dissociation and collection process, and thus increased JSC in PE6:Y6. And the high P(E,T) value of 99% may be related to the high charge-transfer state (CT) in PE6:BTA3 to enhance the formation of exciton dissociated states and reduce geminate recombination. Compared with PE6:IT-M, the highest P(E,T) value of PE6:BTA3 also elucidated that PE6 could maintain JSC while further boosting VOC in PE6:BTA3 system. Indeed, the lowest exciton generation rate still inhibited the enhancement of JSC and the FF value in PE6:BTA3.

It is noteworthy that PE6:BTA3 exhibited a larger energy level offset Î”ECT (roughly defined as the difference between the Eg and ECT associated with the charge generation) (112 meV) than PE6:Y6 (59 meV) and PE6:IT-M (58 meV) with the PE6 donor polymer. A large number of NFA-based devices have already demonstrated that minimizing Î”ECT is a universal potential way to reduce non-radiative loss. Here, PE6:Y6, PE6:IT-M and PE6:BTA3 with negligible Î”ECT obtained a relatively low non-radiative loss, which is consistent with the observation by Nelson et al.60PE6:BTA3 exhibited a larger Î”ECT than the other two systems, but still showed larger EQEEL value. We attributed such a contrasting tendency to the too small Î”ECT (<100 meV) in the PE6:Y6 and PE6:IT-M systems. Two possible reasons for the lowest non-radiative loss of PE6:BTA3 systems are presented as follows: one possibility is that the electronic coupling between the CT state and the first excited singlet state in PE6:BTA3 is larger than that of PE6:Y6 and PE6:IT-M as demonstrated by Nelson et al.60; the other one is that there are long-lived species between the D/A interface in PE6:BTA3, which largely reduced the recombination loss.61 To explain the similar Î”ECT with different Î”V3 in the PE6:Y6/IT-M blends, the Urbach energy (EU) has been obtained by exponential fitting of the sensitive EQE spectra in the sub-gap region. As shown in Fig. S3,â€ PE6:Y6 exhibited lower energetic disorder with a EU value of 24.8 meV than that of PE6:IT-M (EU = 27.9 meV). Hence, the low energetic disorder dominated the reduced Vloss in a small Î”ECT system, as well as efficient charge generation.62

Conclusion

In summary, a linear wide-bandgap polymer PE6 was synthesized and used to study the charge transfer, exciton generation and dissociation, and molecular ordering when blended with three NFAs Y6, IT-M and BTA3 with different bandgaps. The highest PCE of 15.09% was achieved for narrow-bandgap Y6 due to the widest absorption range, highest charge mobilities, inhibited recombination and more ordered molecular stacking. When using medium-bandgap IT-M and wide-bandgap BTA3 as acceptors, PE6-based OPVs can realize a high VOC of 1.032 V and 1.292 V respectively, but relatively low PCEs of 8.61% and 7.88%. The Vloss in the three acceptors are 0.54, 0.57 and 0.54 V, respectively, and the Î”V3 in the PE6:BTA3 blend is as low as 0.158 V due to the strongest EQEEL. Compared to most PM6-based single-junction devices with Î”V3 in the range of 200â€“350 mV, our results indicated that the linear wide bandgap BTA-based polymer PE6 with trialkylsilyl and chlorine substitution on the conjugated side chain is a versatile material that can be blended with various acceptors for low voltage loss OPV devices. To achieve a higher PCE, Vloss must be reduced with Î”V3 being the key focus to decrease. How to design donor and acceptor systems to simultaneously enhance the VOC and PCE needs to be considered in the future.
