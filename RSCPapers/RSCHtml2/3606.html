Abstract

The combination of large energy density and high power density is the optimized model for supercapacitors. However, due to the sluggish charge transfer and ion diffusion, cobalt-aluminium hydrotalcite (CoAl LDHs) electrode materials currently deliver barely satisfactory performance. Oxygen vacancies have been reported to have positive facilitation to the electrochemical property of the hydrotalcite-like materials. In this work, the proportion of oxygen vacancies in CoAl LDHs was increased by NaBH4 treatment, which improved the electrochemical energy storage performance of pristine CoAl LDHs, while prolonged treatment will damage the internal structure of the material and is not conducive to the improvement of electrochemical performance. Consequently, the acquired CoAl LDHs-0.5 delivers a high specific capacity of 799.2 F gâˆ’1 at a current density of 1 A gâˆ’1 and maintains 81.1% capacity as the current density up to 20 A gâˆ’1, which exhibited higher performance than pristine CoAl LDHs and the most reported CoAl LDH-based materials. Besides, the asymmetric supercapacitor (ASC) was fabricated using CoAl LDHs-0.5 and commercial activated carbon (AC) as the positive and negative electrodes, respectively, which exhibits a maximum energy density of 28.9 W h kgâˆ’1 at a power density of 523.5 W kgâˆ’1 and retains 73.3% of the initial capacity after 5000 cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1. This work provides an ingenious strategy and builds confidence in the further application of CoAl LDHs in electrode materials of supercapacitors.

1. Introduction

In the past few years, the increased utilization of traditional energy and rapidly growing energy-related and environmental issues have prompted an urgent need for clean and sustainable energy. In this regard, energy conversion technologies, in particular electrochemical energy applications (e.g., rechargeable batteries, fuel cells and supercapacitors), have developed rapidly and made satisfactory progress.1â€“3 As one of the typical devices of electrochemical energy conversion, supercapacitors has been applied in a wide range of industries including power sectors, industries, electronics and electrified transportation.4 These practical applications are inseparable from the outstanding characteristics of supercapacitors, in particular the peculiarities of large power density, durable service life and low price, which are mainly determined by the physical energy storage mechanism.5,6 Unfortunately, the low energy density of supercapacitors, in particular the traditional carbon-based electrical double-layer capacitors (EDLCs), cannot meet the requirements of the rapidly developing industries. As one of the new functional materials emerging in recent years, pseudocapacitive materials offer both high energy density and power density simultaneously, exhibiting battery-like charge storage mechanism but with a charge storage velocity comparable to that of EDLC materials, which filled the gap between EDLCs and the batteries.7â€“10

As a kind of material that is usually expressed as [M2+1âˆ’xM3+x(OH)2]Anâˆ’x/nÂ·mH2O (M2+ represents bivalent metal cations, M3+ represents trivalent metal cations and Anâˆ’ represents an interlayer anion, respectively), layered double hydroxides (LDHs) have been adopted as active materials for supercapacitor electrodes thanks to their special structure and composition.11â€“13 Eminently, LDHs containing trivalent aluminium (Al3+) such as NiAl LDHs,14 CoAl LDHs15 and MgAl LDHs16 have been reported to possess stable structures and electrochemical properties. Although LDHs have great potential characteristics of energy storage according to the theoretical calculation, the actual performance is not up to expectations due to the considerably poor electrical conductivity of LDHs in themselves.17,18 Thus far, familiar strategies such as complexation with materials such as graphene,19 carbon materials,20 and conductive polymers21 that well conduct electricity, conversion into oxides,22 sulfides23 and phosphates,24 and changing the electronic structure of the bulk materials25 have been used to solve this conundrum.

Extensive research shows that any vacancy or defect has an effect on the output characteristics of electrode materials, such as boosting the quantity of redox or adsorption effective sites, accelerating the transmission of electrons and improving the intrinsic conductivity of the materials.26â€“28 For instance, Zou's group modulated the oxygen vacancies of NiMn LDHs with mild H2O2, which presented a supreme specific capacitance of 1183 C gâˆ’1 at a current density of 1 A gâˆ’1.29 Liang et al. synthesized an oxygen-vacancy-rich NiCo LDH nanowire array by reducing the pristine LDHs using NaBH4, and the obtained Vo-NiCo LDH showed 1.57 times of specific capacitance compared to pristine LDHs.30 Furthermore, compared with reduction in a hydrogen atmosphere, anoxic calcining and acid etching, the wet chemical reduction is a simple vacancy generation method, which can increase the content of oxygen vacancies without destroying the structure of materials subversively.30,31

Based on the aforementioned discussion, in this work, the oxygen vacancies in CoAl LDHs were further increased upon reduction of NaBH4. Thanks to the abundant oxygen vacancies and slight structural changes, the obtained CoAl LDHs-0.5 presents a maximum specific capacity of 799.2 F gâˆ’1 at a current density of 1 A gâˆ’1 (approximately 1.4 times to original CoAl LDHs) with a capacity retention of 81.1% with the promotion of current densities to 20 A gâˆ’1. The outstanding electrochemical expression is benefited by the promotion of electron and ion transfer by vacancies. Furthermore, the asymmetric supercapacitor packaged with CoAl LDHs-0.5 and commercial activated carbon delivers an energy density of 28.9 W h kgâˆ’1 at a power density of 523.5 W kgâˆ’1 and retains 73.3% of the initial capacity after 5000 consecutive cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1.

2.1. Synthesis of CoAl LDHs-0.5 material

Materials and chemicals. Synthesis of CoAl LDHs. Then, the multicomponent solution was put in a sealed autoclave and heated at 120 Â°C in a constant temperature oven for 16 h. After the reaction procedure, the cooled precipitate was washed 3 times with distilled water and ethanol, and the product was separated using a high-speed centrifugal separator. In order to increase the oxygen vacancy, 0.3 g prepared CoAl LDHs were soaked in 50 mL NaBH4 solution (0.8 M) under magnetic stirring for 0.5 h, 1 h, 1.5 h and 2 h, respectively. The final products were denoted CoAl LDHs-0.5, CoAl LDHs-1.0, CoAl LDHs-1.5 and CoAl LDHs-2.0, respectively.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Structures, morphologies and composition

Scheme 1 summarizes the diagrammatical steps used for synthesising CoAl LDHs-0.5, mainly involving a hydrothermal method and subsequent reduction treatment. First, the lamelliform CoAl LDHs were prepared by a one-step hydrothermal process, in which NaOH and Na2CO3 are the precipitants. 3.2. Electrochemical properties of the as-prepared CoAl LDH-based samples

The electrochemical performances of the synthesized materials were evaluated in a typical three-electrode system. Fig. 4a shows the CV curves of the pristine CoAl LDHs and the treated CoAl LDHs in the voltage window from 0 to 0.6 V at a scan rate of 20 mV sâˆ’1. Remarkably, all the five electrodes show a pair of redox peaks, and the CoAl LDHs-0.5 electrode expressly presents the highest peak current. Consequently, these five electrodes are all materials with pseudocapacitance properties and the CoAl LDHs-0.5 displays the highest specific capacitance. GCD measurement is another important method to quantitatively measure the specific capacitance. Fig. 4b presents the GCD results of the prepared five sample electrodes, from which it could be inferred that the specific capacity reduces with the prolonged processing time. However, the specific capacities of the samples treated with NaBH4 for half an hour and one hour were both higher than those of the untreated CoAl LDHs. In other words, appropriate oxygen vacancies can prolong the discharge time of CoAl LDHs, whereas excess oxygen vacancies reduce the discharge time. We speculate that appropriate vacancy is helpful to regulate the electronic structure of LDHs and facilitate the rapid transfer of charges, while excessive vacancy disturbs the arrangement structure of LDHs and hinders the redox reaction during the chargingâ€“discharging test. Thus, we can preliminarily consider that the treatment time of reduction has a dominant impact on the electrochemical expression of samples. In order to further verify this tendency, the EIS measurement that acted as a powerful tool to understand the intrinsic mechanism of storage charge was adopted.48Fig. 4c shows the EIS plots of five electrodes simulated with the equivalent circuit inserted in the figure. The value of the intersection point of the curve and the real axis represents the equivalent series resistance (Rs), and the diameter of the semicircle can be used to measure the internal resistance (Rct) of charge transfer of the electrode. For Rs in the high-frequency region, there is no obvious gap between five electrodes, while Rct in medium-frequency region has significant differences. Through comparison, we found that Rct of CoAl LDHs-0.5 is the minimum one, manifesting the best performance of charge transfer. Instead, there was a slight increase in Rct of other samples, which further confirms our conjecture in the results of GCD. It can be seen from the TEM images that the LDH nanosheets have a certain amount of fragmentation after reduction treatment. However, the promotion effect of the vacancy on charge transfer is dominant under short-time treatment. While the nano-sheet is broken seriously after long-time processing, which leads to the complexity of the charge transfer path and an increase in the transfer resistance. Based on the CV, GCD and EIS results, we can draw a conclusion that the appropriate time of reduction treatment improves the charge storage performance of CoAl LDHs, while the excessive treatment is not conducive to the energy storage performance of CoAl LDHs. Moreover, the detailed electrochemical properties of CoAl LDHs-0.5 were tested under different test parameters. Fig. 4d reveals the CV curves of CoAl LDHs-0.5 electrode at crescent scan velocities from 5 to 100 mV sâˆ’1. A pair of distinct anodic and cathodic CV peaks account for its pseudocapacitance, and the outstanding symmetry of the curves indicates its good reversibility. Besides, the comparison of the chargingâ€“discharging performance of CoAl LDs before and after treatment is shown in Fig. 4e. The discharge platform at about 0.35 V further demonstrates the pseudocapacitive characteristics, and the longer discharge time indicates the positive role of oxygen vacancy. At current densities of 1, 2, 5, 10, and 20 A gâˆ’1, the specific capacities of CoAl LDHs-0.5 were calculated to be 799.2, 784.6, 747.6, 706.2 and 648.5 F gâˆ’1, while for pristine CoAl LDHs, the values were 576.4, 557.6, 528.4, 506.2 and 473.1 F gâˆ’1, respectively. In comparison with original CoAl LDHs, the specific capacity of CoAl LDHs-0.5 is improved, while the rate performance is not decreased (both about 82%). Besides, after 5000 cycles of chargeâ€“discharge process, the CoAl LDHs-0.5 still retained a higher capacitance than CoAl LDHs (Fig. 4f). The minor difference in the final specific capacitance may be due to the recovery of unsaturated coordination oxygen by the long chargingâ€“discharging process, leading to the material returning to its pre-treated state. Finally, we made a comparison with the reported work listed in Table 2, which found that although the specific capacity of CoAl LDHs-0.5 is not as high as that of NiAl LDHs and NiCo LDHs, the rate capability is far more than NiAl LDHs and NiCo LDHs. In addition, the specific capacity is higher than that of other cobalt-based materials such as Co3O4/CoO, CoP, and CoS-20. Therefore, we believe that our CoAl LDHs-0.5 material has great advantages and competitiveness.38,45,49â€“61

4. Conclusion

In summary, the CoAl LDH electrode material has been referentially and successfully prepared by a one-step hydrothermal method. The subsequent reductive processing increased the oxygen vacancy contents of the original CoAl LDH material. Based on the comprehensive analysis of XRD, TEM, XPS and EPR characterization results, we believe that the increase in vacancies disrupts the crystal and microcosmic structure of the CoAl LDHs, enhances the quantities of REDOX active sites and changes the transport route of electrons. Consequently, the electrochemical properties of the as-designed CoAl LDHs-0.5 electrode were improved distinctly. The maximum specific capacity of CoAl LDHs-0.5 is 799.2 F gâˆ’1 at a current density of 1 A gâˆ’1, which is approximately 1.4 times to original CoAl LDHs. Moreover, there is 81.1% capacity retention as the current density increases to a high value of 20 A gâˆ’1. Furthermore, the asymmetric supercapacitor packaged with CoAl LDHs-0.5 and commercial activated carbon delivers an energy density of 28.9 W h kgâˆ’1 at a power density of 523.5 W kgâˆ’1, superior to most of the reported CoAl LDH-based ASC devices. In addition, the CoAl LDHs-0.5//AC ASC shows a good cycling stability with 73.3% of the initial capacitance after 5000 cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1. These electrochemical correlation results indicated that CoAl LDHs-0.5 is an impressive choice for supercapacitor cathodic materials.
