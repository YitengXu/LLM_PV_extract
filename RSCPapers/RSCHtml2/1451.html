2D layered group IIIA metal chalcogenides: synthesis, properties and applications in electronics and optoelectronics

Abstract

The remarkable properties of graphene have inspired great research interest in two-dimensional layered materials (2DLMs) with novel electronic and optical attributes. As a class of graphene-like 2DLMs, ultrathin group IIIA metal chalcogenides (GIIIAMCs, metal = In, Ga; chalcogen = S, Se, Te) have shown great potential in electronics and optoelectronics. In this review, we first introduce the crystal structures and their unique advantages in specific potential applications. Then, we summarize the progress in the fabrication of GIIIAMCs in the framework of top-down and bottom-up methods. Next, their recent achievements regarding enhanced field-effect transistor and photodetector applications are discussed in detail. In addition, the construction strategies of hybrid GIIIAMCs heterostructures and their applications in electronics and optoelectronics are presented. Finally, this review is concluded with some perspectives and an outlook for continued research.

1 Introduction

Efforts on exploring new kinds of materials for next-generation high-performance electronic and optoelectronic devices have never ceased. More and more electronic and optoelectronic devices, such as field-effect transistors (FETs)14 and photodetectors,513 based on low dimensional materials, especially two-dimensional layered materials (2DLMs), have demonstrated excellent properties, which are promising potential alternatives to the widely used semiconducting silicon.1417 Taking graphene as an example, the atomic thin carbon film has an ultrahigh carrier mobility (up to 105 cm2 V1 s1) at room temperature18 and a considerable wide-band spectral absorption for its zero bandgap, obviously superior to that of semiconducting silicon.1923 However, such a zero bandgap character of graphene results in extremely low on/off ratios in related FET devices because the off-state current in the device cannot be cut off and also causes undetectable photocurrents in related photodetectors.24,25 To overcome the disadvantages induced by the gapless energy band structure in graphene, many efforts have been attempted, like chemical doping, morphology control and applying an external electronic field,2630etc., which modify the bandgap of graphene in either a limited degree or some degree but at the expense of carrier mobility. Therefore, exploring other graphene-like 2DLMs attracted the great interest of researchers worldwide.

2DLMs are a class of graphene-like ultrathin film structures with strong in-plane bonds and weak van der Waals interactions between adjacent layers, which could thus be exfoliated into single or a few layers from their bulk materials by physical or chemical methods. Different from gapless graphene, many 2DLMs are found to be semiconductors with wide-range bandgaps,3136 which have obvious advantages over graphene in electronic and optoelectronic applications. Until now, a variety of 2DLMs with high crystalline quality and stable unique electrical, optical, thermal and mechanical properties have been successfully fabricated, including the insulator h-BN,37 topological insulators (e.g., Bi2Se3, Bi2Te3, and Sb2Te3),38,39 semiconducting transition-metal dichalcogenides (TMDs, e.g., MoS2 and WS2),31,40,41etc. Among them, group IIIA metal chalcogenide (GIIIAMC) 2DLMs have attracted great interest recently due to their novel properties and promising applications in next-generation high-performance electronic and optoelectronic devices. For example, unlike the striking indirect-to-direct bandgap transition generally occurring in TMDs, such as MoS2,42 InSe exhibits a complete contrary layer-dependent property, that is, a direct-to-indirect bandgap transition occurs during a decrease in sample thickness.4345 Accordingly, the indirect bandgap of few-layer InSe also results in a different photoluminescence (PL) performance from MoS2. The quenching of the PL signal showing a blueshift up to 200 meV was observed when the thickness of InSe was reduced to 6 nm.46 What's more, InSe has a lighter electron effective mass (m* = 0.143 m0)47 than MoS2 (m* = 0.45m0),42 and as a result, InSe-based FET has exhibited surprising electronic properties such as a high electron mobility of ~103 cm2 V1 s1 and a high on/off ratio of 108.48,49 In addition, other GIIIAMC 2DLMs also exhibited excellent optoelectronic properties in their corresponding ultrathin films. For example, mechanically exfoliated In2Se3 nanosheets have an extraordinary high photoresponsivity of 3.95 102 A W1 at 300 nm with an external quantum efficiency (EQE) greater than 1.63 105% at 5 V bias.55 In short, there is plenty of room for the application of GIIIAMC 2DLMs in high-performance electronic and optoelectronic devices.

Fabrication of materials is an endless subject for materials science. Generally, two major fabrication strategies have been employed to obtain single- or few-layer GIIIAMC 2DLMs: the top-down method and the bottom-up method. The top-down methods, including mechanical exfoliation by using adhesive tape5659 and chemical exfoliation via liquid molecule (or lithium ion) intercalation,6063 can achieve high-quality and micrometre-sized monolayers. The bottom-up methods, including chemical/physical vapor deposition and wet chemical synthesis,6467 have advantages in control of the type and number of layers, edge morphologies, and the degree of crystallinity. To date, many GIIIAMC 2DLMs have been synthesized successfully by both bottom-up and top-down methods.

Besides the preparation of individual GIIIAMC 2DLMs, vertical or lateral 2D heterostructures have also received growing attention in the past few years.14,6878 To take a deep insight into the physical mechanism, a few GIIIAMC 2D heterostructures have been investigated, such as pn heterojunction7983 and graphene-based GIIIAMC 2D heterostructures.8486 Further, combining thin layer materials with zero-dimensional (0D) quantum dots or one-dimensional (1D) nanowires, semiconductors can generate interfaces with properties significantly different from that of each component.87,88 For example, GaTe/Sn:CdS nanoflake/nanowire heterostructures have an excellent photoresponsivity, which is higher than those from previous reports of single GaTe nanosheets and Sn:CdS nanowires.87 Compared with studies on individual 2DLMs, research on 2D heterostructures is just beginning.

In this review, first, we present a brief summary of the crystal structure and electronic properties of some typical GIIIAMC 2DLMs. Then, we provide the methods for preparing these materials based on top-down and bottom-up methods. Next, their recent achievements regarding the enhanced FET and photodetector applications are discussed in detail, especially in electronic and optoelectronic applications based on their heterostructures. Finally, this review is concluded with some perspectives and an outlook for continued research.

3 Synthesis

Reliable synthesis of individual GIIIAMCs is a precondition for practical application in electronics and optoelectronics. In general, it can be categorized as top-down and bottom-up methods, such as mechanical or chemical exfoliation,24,153155 vapor deposition,63,156,157 solvothermal method,158162 pulsed laser deposition163 and so on. In the following part, we will give a brief review about these synthesis methods of GIIIAMC 2DLMs.

3.1 Top-down methods

The individual layers in 2DLMs are held together vertically via weak van der Waals force, which enables the facile exfoliation of these layers. The most common approaches for obtaining single- and few-layer 2DLMs include mechanical exfoliation, chemical exfoliation, and molecule/atom intercalation in the liquid phase.

The mechanical exfoliation method developed from the first generation of monolayer graphene24 has been used to fabricate and study the properties of other 2DLMs. It is well known that single- or few-layer 2DLMs produced by the mechanical exfoliation method exhibit high-quality, clean and micrometre-sized flakes on a variety of substrates,164,165 which are suitable for fundamental research and the demonstration of high-performance devices.64,166,167 To date, most of the reports about the synthesis of GIIIAMC 2DLMs have been focused on mechanical exfoliation, such as InSe, GaS, GaSe, GaTe and In2Se3. For example, Mudd et al.46 demonstrated strong quantum confinement effects in InSe flakes fabricated by the mechanical exfoliation method, as shown in Fig. 2a (AFM image) and Fig. 2b (optical image). From micro-PL (PL) maps (Fig.2c and d) and the corresponding PL spectra of representative flakes (Fig. 2e), the PL emission exhibited a strong photon energy blue-shift up to 200 meV with decreasing thickness down to 6 nm, indicating the direct-to-indirect transition in InSe resulting from quantum confinement effects. Hu et al.93 also reported the mechanically exfoliated GaSe-based photodetector exhibiting a high photoresponsivity of 2.8 A W1 and EQE of 1367% at 254 nm. Further, they published another work on a GaS nanosheet photodetector made by the same method on rigid and flexible substrates, which exhibited a photoresponsivity of 4.2 A W1 and 19.2 A W1, respectively.92 After that, Yang et al.51 proved that mechanically exfoliated layered GaS nanosheets showed a high photoresponsivity of 64.43 A W1 and EQE of 12621% in ammonia environment. Recently, the production of 2D GaTe and In2Se3 nanosheets via mechanical exfoliation has also been reported, which suggested that they are promising candidates for future electronic and optoelectronic device applications.5355,168 However, mechanical exfoliation is not suitable for large-scale production due to the absence of controllability in sample morphology and yield.

Recently, Harvey et al.152 reported the production of large quantities of GaS nanosheets by liquid exfoliation used as hydrogen evolution catalysts, as shown in Fig. 2fh. As is well known, layer number controllability and large-area uniformity are essential requirements for practical application in electronic and optoelectronic devices. Top-down exfoliation methods are obviously unsuitable for such goals. In contrast, bottom-up methods have been employed to prepare single- or few-layer 2DLMs for large scale production. In the following section, we will focus on these methods.

3.2 Bottom-up methods

The bottom-up methods include chemical vapour deposition (CVD), physical vapour deposition (PVD), pulsed laser deposition (PLD) and wet chemical methods.

The CVD method has shown great promise to generate high-quality TMDs with a scalable size, controllable thickness and excellent electronic properties. CVD-grown graphene on copper has been a major breakthrough that has enabled the preparation of large-area graphene.171,172 Recently, the synthesis of large-area 2DLMs using CVD has been demonstrated in many works and most of them are focused on MoS2.160162,173176 To date, there are a few reports addressing monolayer or few-layer GIIIAMC 2DLMs by the CVD method. One of these is the synthesis of GaSe by using GaSe and Ga2Se3 (molar ratio: ~50:1) as the raw materials reported by Li et al.,169 as shown in Fig. 3a. The small amount of Ga2Se3 powder played an important role during the synthesis, which provided sufficient Se for growing regularly shaped triangle monolayer flakes. They found that the size, density, shape, thickness, and uniformity of the 2D GaSe crystals were shown to be controllable by growth duration, growth region, growth temperature, and argon carrier gas flow rate. Importantly, the 2D GaSe crystals showed p-type semiconductor characteristics and high photoresponsivity of 1.7 A W1 under white light illumination, which was comparable to that of exfoliated few-layer GaSe nanosheets (2.8 A W1).93 By the same method, they found that bilayer GaSe crystals could be selectively grown.177 More recently, Wang et al.106 have synthesized high-quality, single-crystalline GaTe nanosheets by employing a facile CVD method, as shown in Fig. 3b. Two different growth models were observed on SiO2/Si and mica substrates. Planar GaTe nanosheets could be obtained by using mica as the growth substrate, while vertical GaTe arrays were observed on the SiO2/Si substrate.

PVD stands as an appealing, versatile synthetic strategy.178 However, the development of a controlled synthetic method of GIIIAMC 2DLMs requires a better understanding of the fundamentals involved. Recently, the production of 2D In2Se3 and GaSe nanosheets via PVD has been reported.107,143,179 Lei et al.128 have developed a PVD growth method for the synthesis of large-area few-layer GaSe crystals on SiO2/Si substrate by using GaSe powder as an evaporation source, as shown in Fig. 3c. The source and substrate were heated up to 750 and 720 C, respectively. Owing to their special quartz tube design, the mean free path was large enough for mass transfer. As a result, they found that the shape of the flakes transformed from triangle to truncated triangle and finally lost the geometric symmetry with increasing distance between source and substrate. Very recently, Zhou et al.143 exploited a controlled synthesis of high-quality monolayer -In2Se3 synthesized on SiO2/Si substrate via the PVD method (seen in Fig. 3d). In2Se3 powder was evaporated at 850 C under atmospheric pressure, and the collecting substrates were placed downstream about 1015 cm away. They found that the geometries and thicknesses of the -In2Se3 atomic layers could be controlled by the growth time. More importantly, the In2Se3 FETs exhibited p-type semiconducting behaviors with mobility of up to 2.5 cm2 V1 s1. By the same method, high-quality 2D GaSe crystals were synthesized on flexible and transparent mica substrates by using polycrystalline GaSe powder as the source material.179

PLD offers a facile method to synthesize high-quality 2DLMs with precise thicknesses. Recently, Mahjouri-Samani et al.163 published two reports on fabricating GaSe nanosheets by stoichiometric transfer of laser-vaporized material from bulk GaSe targets based on the PLD method. They obtained individual 13 layer GaSe triangular nanosheets with a domain size of ~200 nm under low laser pulses, then got GaSe nanosheet networks by increasing the laser pulses up to 100. The results showed that GaSe nanosheet networks exhibited a p-type behavior with mobilities up to 0.1 cm2 V1 s 1. To improve the high nucleation density and average size, they combined the PLD method and the vapor transport growth (VTG) system to grow large and crystalline 2D GaSe and MoSe2 nanosheets with the size of 100 m by precisely defining the mass and location of the source materials,170 as shown in Fig. 3e. Firstly, a stoichiometric amount of nanoparticles was deposited uniformly and precisely onto a substrate by PLD at room temperature; secondly, this source substrate was then covered with a receiver substrate to form a confined VTG system. This PLD-VTG synthesis and processing method offered a unique approach for the controlled growth of large-area metal chalcogenides with a specific number of layers in patterned growth locations for optoelectronics and energy-related applications.

4 Applications

Due to their unique properties and remarkable advantages, such as strong electronhole confinement, extreme bendability, high transparency, scalability, low defectivity and perfect interface, 2DLMs have become a new class of emerging materials to meet the needs of new electronic and optoelectronic devices. In the following section, we will discuss the recent achievements regarding the enhanced FET and photodetector applications of GIIIAMC 2DLMs.

4.1 Electronics

One of the most important applications in the field of electronics is the FET device. A basic 2DLMs-based FET structure is composed of three parts: sourcedrain metal contacts, 2DLM semiconducting channel, and dielectric layer (gate electrode). The current flowing between the source and drain electrodes is controlled by the gate electrode to modulate the conductivity of the channel. During operation, high charge carrier mobilities, high on/off ratio and effective switching are the key points to obtain a good FET performance, which is strongly affected by the metal contacts of the source and drain, the properties of 2D layered channel materials (thickness,180,181 length,182,183 doping and defects53,184187), dielectric layer materials1,188190 and so on. In this section, we will discuss the effects of these factors on the newest FET reports on GIIIAMC 2DLMs.
4.1.1 Dielectric materials. The performance of 2DLMs FETs can be significantly affected by the interfaces on both sides of the semiconductors due to the various carrier scattering centers and charge traps.191 For the unencapsulated monolayer MoS2 FETs, the carrier mobility under vacuum conditions is remarkably different from that in an ambient environment, which indicates charge scattering caused by atmospheric adsorbates.192 Similarly, large enhanced mobilities have been observed in a bare sample with the aid of top or back dielectric encapsulated devices.1,17,188,193199 High dielectrics, such as Al2O3, HfO2, etc., are proven to be beneficial for the performance of FETs because of the dielectric screening of coulomb scattering on charged impurities. For example, multilayer MoS2 on Al2O3 substrate showed improved mobility >100 cm2 V1 s1 and good sub-threshold swings.200 Moreover, Kis et al.1 reported that the HfO2 top-gated monolayer MoS2 transistor showed an excellent on/off current ratio (~108), room-temperature mobility >200 cm2 V1 s1 and subthreshold swing of 74 mV per decade. However, these oxidized dielectric substrates have a lot of hydroxyl groups and absorbed water molecules at their surface, which will reduce the carrier mobility due to the interfacial coulomb impurities or surface polar phonon scattering. To alleviate or avoid such scatterings, the general treatment method is to coat the dielectric surface with a thin layer of polymer. For instance, with PMMA/Al2O3 bilayer dielectric, the mobility of multilayer MoS2 FETs is increased to 500 cm2 V1 s1.196

In the case of mechanically exfoliated -InSe FETs with PMMA/Al2O3 bilayer dielectric,49 the mobilities of InSe FETs are up to 1055 cm2 V1 s1, as shown in Fig. 4(ac). This improved ultrahigh carrier mobility resulted from the suppression of interfacial coulomb impurity scattering or surface polar phonon scattering at the interface of the oxidized dielectric substrate after coating with PMMA. This transistor showed high current on/off ratios of 1 108, low standby power dissipation and robust current saturation in a broad voltage range. They also found that this carrier scattering showed a thickness-dependent impact on the mobilities of multilayer InSe FETs and a stronger impact on the mobilities of thinner InSe FETs. All these fascinating results made multilayer InSe an attractive candidate material for high-performance electronic devices.

4.1.2 Metal contacts. The contacts between metal/semiconductors in FETs play a very important role in determining device performance since they control the charge injection into the channel materials. Thus, in order to realize the practical applications of 2DLMs FETs, the role of the metal contact and the interface with the 2DLMs is a crucial scientific issue, which involves the choice of appropriate metals, configuration of the contact, and an understanding of the interactions in the metal/semiconductor channels. Generally, the relationship between the work functions of metals and the band edge positions of 2DLMs determines the hole or electron conduction of FETs.201 If the work function of the metal is high, it favors hole conduction by pinning the chemical potential closer to the valence bands; on the contrary, it prefers electron conduction. Therefore, the choice of metal contacts with appropriate work functions can improve the performance of FETs by reducing the contact barriers.202 Walia et al.203 studied the characteristics of four metal contacts, including aluminum, tungsten, gold, and platinum, on 2D MoS2 flake transistors. It was observed that lower work functions of the contact metals led to a lower Schottky barrier and thus higher charge carrier injection speed through the contacts. Systematic studies of various metal contacts in ultrathin semiconducting TMD transistors have also been performed theoretically and experimentally. These studies found that low work function metals form lower resistance contacts than do high work function metals, leading to higher drain currents. For example, Das et al.204 demonstrated a high-performance MoS2 transistor with an extremely high mobility of 700 cm2 V1 s1, which was attributed to the lower work function of Sc strongly pinning the Fermi level close to the conduction band of MoS2.

Despite these efforts, a full understanding of the nature of the interface between the metal and the 2DLM remains unknown, especially as there is still a lack of solid evidence to reveal the exact nature of the interaction between the metal and the 2DLM. Feng et al.50 presented an experimental study of the contacts of multilayer -InSe with aluminum (Al), titanium (Ti), chromium (Cr), and indium (In) using back-gated FETs, shown in Fig. 4(dg). From Fig. 4d and e, they expected that the InSe FET with an Al contact had the highest performance because it had the smallest work function among the selected metal contacts. But in contrast, the InInSe FET exhibited the best performance with an electron mobility of 162 cm2 V1 s1, high current on/off ratio of 108 and the lowest contact resistance of 1.9 103 m1 according to the experimental results (Fig. 4f). By investigating Raman spectra and AFM, they found that the contact resistance of metal/InSe was not controlled by the work function of the metals but the nature of the metal/InSe contact interface. More importantly, the surface morphology of the In films deposited on multilayer InSe with the lowest Rms roughness of 0.32 nm (Fig. 4g) and its d-orbitals were beneficial for electron injection and for forming a lower contact resistance. All these results provided general guidelines and rules on how best to design low-resistance contacts for high-performance FETs based on thin-film layered semiconductors.

4.2 Optoelectronics

Optoelectronic devices, including photodetectors, solar cells or light-emitting devices can generate, detect, interact with or control light.154 The electronic band structures of semiconductors directly influence their ability to absorb and emit light. Due to an atomically thin and large active area, strong lightmatter interaction and novel electrical properties, optoelectronic devices based on 2DLMs have attracted much interest. Recently, some high-performance optoelectronic devices have been reported using several types of GIIIAMC 2DLMs (e.g., InSe,112,205207 GaS,51,92,208 GaSe,93,128,163,179 GaTe53,106,130,168 and In2Se3 (ref. 55, 107, 143 and 144)), prepared by mechanical exfoliation or vapor deposition. This section will review these photodetectors in detail.
4.2.1 Photodetectors based on controllable synthesized GIIIAMC 2DLMs. MoS2 has become an interesting research topic nowadays due to its high electron mobility, excellent on/off ratio, and high quantum luminescence efficiency.1,200 Photodetectors based on monolayer MoS2 have shown a considerably high photoresponsivity of up to 880 A W1 but with a relatively slow response time (~4 s) that hinders its practical application.5 The monolayer MoS2 mentioned above was fabricated by the mechanical exfoliation method, which is obviously not suitable for scalable applications. In contrast, controllable CVD synthesis provides a solution. Fig. 5a shows the optical image of a 2D GaSe device with patterned electrodes. This controlled synthesis of multilayer GaSe crystals was realized by growing the crystals on a SiO2/Si substrate through a vapor-phase growth method from a previous report (Fig. 3a).169Fig. 5b shows the IdsVds characteristic of the device in the dark (black curve) and under white light illumination (red curve); the IdsVds curves were linear (inset of Fig. 5b), which indicated ohmic contacts. GaSe is a p-type semiconductor according to the behavior shown in Fig. 5c in the dark state. Under a fixed Vds of 10 V, the responsivity of the 2D GaSe crystal was 1.7 A W1, which was comparable to that of exfoliated few-layer GaSe nanosheets (2.8 A W1)93 and higher than previously reported vapor-phase grown 2D GaSe (17 mA W1).128 These 2D GaSe crystals with controllable size, density, shape, thickness, and uniformity are potentially useful for next-generation electronic and optoelectronic devices.

4.2.2 High-performance flexible photodetectors. One of the most promising near-term applications of semiconducting 2DLMs is in high-performance flexible electronics. GaS is considered as a promising material for near-blue light emitting devices because of its indirect bandgap of 3.05 eV. The responsivity of 19.2 A W1, EQE of 9371% and response time of less than 30 ms were achieved for few-layer GaS on a flexible PET substrate.92 Compared with GaS, photodetectors based on GaSe have received more attention. Zhou et al.179 reported flexible GaSe photodetector fabricated on mica substrate, exhibiting efficient photoresponse even after repeated bending with different radii. For practical applications, a more effective, controllable, uniform material preparation method is highly desired. GaTe has a direct bandgap of around 1.7 eV in both the single layer and bulk forms at room temperature.129131 Taking advantage of the direct band gap of GaTe, Wang et al.106 presented a high-performance flexible 2D layered GaTe photodetector through the CVD method, as shown in Fig. 5(df). Fig. 5d shows a typical optical image of a flexible GaTe device constructed on a PET substrate. According to IV curves before and after bending 100 and 200 times (Fig. 5e), this device was in excellent agreement with the original data under both illumination and dark tests, indicating the high stability of such a flexible GaTe-based photodetector. To further confirm its high photoresponse stability to the mechanical bend, time evolutions of the photoresponse before and after bending 100 times and 200 times were measured at a fixed bias voltage of 5 V, as shown in Fig. 5f. The current before and after bending showed a small difference that is estimated to be below 4%. These results make GaTe nanosheets a strong candidate for constructing novel rigid and flexible optoelectronic devices.
4.2.3 Wide spectral response photodetectors. High-performance broadband photodetector responses from the ultraviolet (UV)-visible (vis) to the near-infrared (NIR) wavelength have attracted much interest. It would be greatly advantageous to have a broadband photodetector system with high sensitivity, fast speed, wide spectral response from UV to NIR, and high detectivities at room temperature.6,62,92,93,153,209212 Recently, 2DLMs such as MoS2, WS2, GaS, GaSe, etc. have demonstrated attractive capabilities for optoelectronics due to their ultrathin thickness, atomic flatness and facile manipulation as well as their tunable optical properties controlled by the thickness. UV-vis photodetectors based on few-layer GaSe93 and GaS92 nanosheets exhibited responsivities of 2.8 A W1 and 19.2 A W1, respectively. UV-NIR photodetectors based on multilayer InSe205 and In2Se3 (ref. 55) showed high responsivities of 12.3 A W1 and 395 A W1, respectively. Also, multilayer GaTe photodetectors have demonstrated a very high photoresponsivity of 104 A W1 to UV-vis light.130 It is still challenging to make phototransistors with both ultrahigh photoresponsivity and detectivity and having a broadband spectral response. Feng et al.206 demonstrated broadband, ultrahigh responsivity, multilayer InSe nanosheet phototransistors, which have a wide spectral response ranging from UV to NIR light. In particular, they adopted a multilayer configuration, air/InSe/SiO2/Si (as shown in Fig. 5g), to improve the performance of the multilayer InSe photodetector. In their theoretical simulation, photon absorption is thought to be very important to the performance of the photodetectors, which is determined by multiple reflection interference at the interfaces and in the interlayers and the complex refractive indices of each medium in the multilayer configuration. According to their experimental results, the InSe nanosheet phototransistors showed high photoresponsivity and detectivity over a broad spectral range from UV to NIR, as shown in Fig. 5h. Furthermore, these InSe devices exhibited a fast response time of ~5 ms, as shown in Fig. 5i. Such a superior performance made multilayer InSe nanosheet photodetectors very attractive for various applications, including touch sensor panels, image sensors and solar cells.

4.3 Electronics and optoelectronics based on heterostructures

Combining the electronic properties of different 2DLMs in hybrid heterostructures has attracted significant interest both for fundamental research and in practical applications. With the advent of GIIIAMC 2DLMs, electronic and optoelectronic devices based on their heterostructures have been attracting more and more attention, especially in applications of pn junctions. Furthermore, due to the high carrier mobilities of graphene, a lot of effort has also been focused on hybrid heterostructures based on it.71,213215 Thus, in the following section, first we will review the applications of GIIIAMC 2DLMs in the field of pn heterostructures, then, we will discuss the enhanced performance of heterostructures based on GIIIAMC 2DLM stacking on graphene.
4.3.1 pn heterojunctions. p-n heterojunctions are the foundation of the modern semiconductor industry. When p- and n-type semiconductors come in contact, free charge carriers will deplete while leaving the fixed charges behind and then form the so-called space charge regions and built-in potentials at the interfaces. When connected to an electric circuit, under the effect of a built-in potential, a rectification characteristic will appear. Due to their direct bandgap, strong photoluminescence and large exciton binding energy, most reports are focused on the IIIV semiconductor heterostructures, such as lateral heterostructures MoSe2/WSe2,216,217 WSe2/WS2,218 WS2/MoS2,219 BP/MoS2,220etc., and vertical heterostructures WSe2/MoS2,221,222 MoS2/Si,223etc.

Generally, two ways are usually used to fabricate 2DLMs heterostructures: the in situ growth method216219 and the mechanical transfer method.34,224 One of the in situ growth methods, molecular beam epitaxy (MBA) is an outstanding method to easily control the thickness of films with wafer-scale homogeneity and atomically sharp interference, which limits the range of possible heterojunctions to those materials that possess closely matched lattice parameters.75 Xiu and co-workers8082 have recently published three works on the MBA growth of pn heterojunctions based on GIIIAMC 2DLMs. At first, highly efficient photodetector arrays were fabricated based on few-layer GaSe80 and GaTe81 on 3-inch Si wafers; these pn heterojunctions showed high photoresponsivity, external quantum efficiency up to 23.6% and 62%, and photoresponse time of 60 s and 22 s, respectively. Importantly, these devices showed no sign of degradation after over 1 million cycles of operation. Further, they reported the controllable synthesis of wafer-scale and few-layer GaTexSe1x/Si heterostructures made by MBA,82 as shown in Fig. 6a. Fig. 6b reveals the IV curve of the GaTe0.64Se0.36/Si pn heterojunction, showing a typical rectification characteristic. By increasing the incident power, they found that the photocurrent increased initially with the negative bias-voltage and then reached saturation (Fig. 6c). They attributed these results to the fact that an increased bias-voltage could separate electronhole pairs more efficiently, then lead to a larger photocurrent, whereas it gradually saturated as the voltage went beyond a certain value due to the limited number of carriers generated by the fixed incident power.

Another kind of pn heterojunction based on 2DLMs and 0D quantum dots or 1D nanowire hybrid structures have drawn much attention, which combine their advantages into a single device. For example, Zhou et al.87 reported the fabrication and optoelectronic properties of GaTe/Sn:CdS nanoflake/nanowire pn heterojunction photodetectors. The GaTe nanoflakes were mechanically exfoliated on SiO2/Si substrate, and then, as-prepared Sn:CdS nanowire suspension dispersed in ethanol was added dropwise to form the GaTe/Sn:CdS pn heterojunction. By comparing the IdsVds curves of a single Sn:CdS nanowire, GaTe nanoflake and GaTe/Sn:CdS heterostructure under dark condition as well as illumination with different wavelengths (Fig. 6d and e), they found that the heterostructure photodetector showed a good photoresponsivity of 607 A W1 and fast response time of less than 260 ms, which was higher than those of previous reports of single GaTe or CdS nanostructures.

Note that the abovementioned is applicable only for vertical van der Waals structures. For 2D pn heterostructures with lateral configuration, due to a space large enough for carrier deletion, the phenomenon can be understood by the traditional built-in potential theory. For instance, Feng et al.79 demonstrated the fabrication of a lateral pn heterojunction of a thin-film InSe/CuInSe2 nanosheet by a simple solid-state reaction, as shown in Fig. 6(gi). Multilayer InSe possesses a direct bandgap, low effective mass, and high electron mobility. CuInSe2 is an important solar energy material, which exhibits an effective light-absorbing capability due to its direct bandgap of 1.1 eV and high optical absorption coefficient. The layered InSe could be transformed into nonlayered CuInSe2 with the aid of copper film via a solid-state reaction: 2InSe + Cu = CuInSe2 + In. Fig. 6g and h shows the 3D schematic and optical image of InSe/CuInSe2 pn heterojunction, respectively. As shown in Fig. 6i, this lateral InSe/CuInSe2 exhibited a robust rectifying behavior, which allows it to pave the way for practical optoelectronic and electronic applications.

4.3.2 Graphene-based heterostructures. Since the first report of graphene and its electric field effect, graphene has been one of the hottest research topics in electronic device applications due to its exceptionally high carrier mobility. However, the lack of bandgap limits the on/off ratio in graphene transistors. Alternatively, heterostructures based on graphene and other 2DLMs provide opportunities to design novel device structures for electronic and optoelectronic applications. Heterostructures based on vertical graphene/MoS2 stacks have shown an enhanced performance.14,76,88,225 Until now, most of previous research attention has been paid to heterostructures based on graphene and IIIV semiconductors, while more and more attention has been focused on hybrid heterostructures constructed with GIIIAMC 2DLMs and graphene. For example, Li et al.86 reported the synthesis and preferred orientations of 2D GaSe atomic layers on graphene by van der Waals epitaxy growth. Unfortunately, they didn't report any data about the applications of these heterostructures. Alternatively, Chen et al.84 fabricated a graphene/few-layer InSe heterostructure photodetector by transferring graphene on top of a mechanically exfoliated ultra-thin InSe, as shown in Fig. 7a and b. By changing the illuminated power, they found that this hybrid device showed a four orders of magnitude enhancement in photoresponsivity and EQE with respect to a simple few-layer InSe device, as shown in Fig. 7c. They thought these enhanced results may be attributed to the combination of absorption and photoelectron generation characteristics of InSe and efficient charge transport in graphene, which was also seen in previously reported TMD-based heterostructures. Mudd et al.85 reported lateral and vertical van der Waals graphene/InSe heterostructures formed by mechanical exfoliation. They exploited the broadband transparency of graphene and the favorable band line up of graphene with InSe to create high-performance photodetectors. Particularly, two types of vertical devices, A (Fig. 7d) and B (Fig. 7e), made by different processing steps, exhibited a stable and reproducible photoresponsivity of up to 105 A W1 at = 633 nm and response to light at powers P as low as 1014 W over an area of the InSe flake of 16 m2 for device A (see photocurrent map in the inset of Fig. 7f), which has not yet been achieved in other 2DLMs, and with a spectral response that extended from the NIR to the visible spectrum. They attributed the enhanced photoresponsivity and detectivity of these devices to the favorable alignment of the bands at the graphene/InSe interface. In summary, these heterostructures provide innovative device architectures that enable access to fast electron speeds and high broad-band spectral response.

Conclusions

2DLMs have been a rising new star in materials science. In this review, we first summarized the crystal structures of GIIIAMC 2DLMs and then discussed recent achievements in GIIIAMC 2DLMs, including preparation methods and applications in electronics and optoelectronics, and finally presented the construction of hybrid GIIIAMC 2DLMs heterostructures and their applications in electronics and optoelectronics. Mechanical exfoliation is the most used method for lab research, while bottom-up methods are seen as the most promising method for controllable and scalable synthesis of high-quality GIIIAMC 2DLMs. A lot of efforts have been focused on FETs, where the underlying charge transport and scattering mechanisms have been identified and the effects of dielectric materials and metal contacts have been delineated. For optoelectronics, high-performance, flexible and wide spectral response photodetectors were introduced. Further, either the lateral or the vertical pn heterojunction can be realized by delicately tuning the composition and stacking sequences among the GIIIAMC 2DLMs in the present study. In particular, intensive efforts have been devoted to graphene-based heterostructures.

In the next few years, new synthesis methods are still highly desired for obtaining high-quality GIIIAMC 2DLMs with large area, thickness controllability, high crystallinity and considerable uniformity. With access to high-quality GIIIAMC 2DLMs, further effort is required to obtain high-performance FET and photodetector devices. Wide spectral response, flexibility and transparency are also desirable characteristics for next-generation electronic and optoelectronic devices. Furthermore, investigating artificial heterostructures with lateral or vertical configurations and exploring the fundamental theory will be the next hot topics for novel 2D devices. In a word, 2DLMs are a relatively new but exciting research area and it is really worth anticipating what they will bring to us in the future.
