Dual functional hetero-anthracene based single component organic ionic conductors as redox mediator cum light harvester for solid state photoelectrochemical cells

Abstract

We have synthesized a novel solid organic ionic conductor (SOIC) that acts as a redox mediator and a light absorbing material at the same time. Such dual function of SOICs has not been reported before. It was achieved by substituting N,N-dimethyl benzimidazolium iodide (BIMI) with hetero-anthracene (phenoxazine (POZ)/phenothiazine (PTZ)) which are labeled as SOIC-1 and SOIC-2, respectively. These substitutions caused the absorption spectrum of BIMI to be extended over 100 nm into the red spectrum and at the same time exhibit excellent redox capability and ionic conductivity. In addition, these synthesized SOICs also enhance the total electron injection into the TiO2 matrix with the metal-free SK3 dye sensitizer. Density functional theory (DFT) was used to optimize the structure and geometrical arrangement of the SOICs. To evaluate the role of PTZ/POZ substitution on BIMI and how pore filling can affect the device performance, solid-state DSSC (ss-DSSC) devices were prepared with two different thickness of TiO2 (8 m and 12 m) photoanode. The 8 m thick TiO2 photoanode with SOIC-1 has an overall PCE of 7.9% which is about 72% higher than the unmodified BIMI (PCE of 4.4%) under AM1.5 illumination. Last but not least, the synthesized SOICs have high thermal stability up to 120 C which is way beyond the operating temperatures of solar cells which make them ideal for real-world applications.

Introduction

Dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSC) have attracted tremendous attention due to their simple fabrication process, high photoconversion efficiency, feasibility to assemble on a flexible/rigid substrates and low manufacturing cost since they were reported in 1991 by M. Gratzel and B. O'Regan.1 Briefly, DSSC is made up of light absorbing dye, nanocrystalline TiO2 film, a catalytic Pt-counter electrode and an I/I3 redox couple electrolyte. One of the key components is the electrolyte, which plays a pivotal role in the charge transfer process and regeneration of dye. The electrolyte directly affects the performance and stability of DSSC. The first generation of DSSC that used liquid I/I3 electrolyte exhibit a power conversion-efficiency (PCE) of up to 13%,2,3 but they suffer from numerous drawbacks like dye desorption and electrolyte leakage due to the volatile solvents. These resulted in low stability and device sealing problems.46 Therefore, to avoid the above problems, the DSSC community has developed solid state electrolytes to replace liquid electrolytes.6,7 The quasi solid8,9 and polymeric redox electrolytes10 are potential alternatives to liquid electrolytes due to their excellent ionic conductivity, but they have poor pore filling ability and interfacial compatibility with TiO2 or Pt-electrodes surface. This has therefore spurred scientists to synthesize small molecule hole transporting materials11 and solid metalligand complex12 based electrolytes. However, they suffer from low conductivity, poor hole mobility and stability issues.6,13 All these factors have hindered DSSC in real-world applications.

Solid organic ionic conductors (SOICs) are well known for their excellent performance in photoelectrochemical cells due to their low volatility, high conductivity and facile synthesis procedure.14,15 The most promising class of solid ionic conductors for the fabrication of high efficiency ss-DSSCs is based on imidazole iodide (IMI) salts. A huge amount of efforts have been carried out to increase the performance of the IMI based electrolyte by incorporation of bulkier moieties or substitution of alkyl chains. For instance, Z. S. Wang and co-workers have reported the substitution of an ester group on IMI to enhance the function of the electrolytes.1618 They also reported high conducting propargyl-functionalized single component ionic conductors used in ss-DSSCs and reported a PCE of 6.3%.19 Yan et al. has developed cyanobiphenyl-functionalized and nitrogen doped imidazolium-based ionic crystals and achieved a PCE of 5.11%.20,21 Midya et al. has reported that the ionic hole transport materials of carbazole substituted on IMI with different anions to achieve 3.0% PCE.22 Recently, we have reported the POZ(phenoxazine)/PTZ(phenothiazine) substituted IMI based SOICs, which exhibit excellent conductivity, hole mobility and thermal stability.14 Ss-DSSCs fabricated with POZ/PTZ substituted IMI based SOICs has a PCE of 5.2%, where substituent donor moieties contributed in enhancing electron transport properties and short-circuit current density (Jsc). Increasing ionic sites on the moiety improves ion diffusion which leads to better conductivity, i.e. the di-cationic SOICs based on pyrrolidinium and IMI salts have achieved a remarkable performance of more than 6.0% efficiency.23,24 These promising results suggest that more works are needed in this field to develop more efficient SOICs with an effective substituent in conventional champion moieties, which can provide better hole mobility and light harvesting ability along with redox mediating capability.

Herein, we have introduced di-cationic SOICs based on POZ and PTZ substituted BIMI electrolytes that were linked by octyl chain between the twin donor moieties through the 10-H position of POZ(SOIC-1)/PTZ(SOIC-2) for the first time. Their general structures are shown in Fig. 1. The bulkier photosensitive substitution on BIMI resulted in photo-generation ability, enhancement of the conductivity through the bulkier cations. These SOICs absorb visible region spectrum and support the enhancement in photo-generation for champion metal free sensitizer SK3 (Fig. 1)25 along with their excellent iodide diffusion and hole carrying function, they perform dual role in ss-DSSC. Due to this, it directly broadens the light absorption spectrum and decreases electron recombination as compared to bare BIMI. DFT calculations were also carried out to find the possible arrangement of twin moieties between the octyl linkage and cationic effect on the distance of iodide atom. Ss-DSSCs were fabricated using the SOIC-1 and SOIC-2 with various thickness (8 m and 12 m) of TiO2 photoanode for better insight into the pore filling ability. The optimized thickness of TiO2 photoanode (8 m) with SOICs shows highest photoconversion efficiencies (PCEs) of 7.9% and 6.8% for SOIC-1 and SOIC-2, respectively. The presence of multi-components in the electrolyte generate complexity, therefore we have also introduced SOICs in DSSC without mixing any additive such as I2, TBP, GSCN, etc. Single component SOICs were used in ss-DSSCs and achieved highest PCEs of 6.0% (SOIC-1) and 5.60% (SOIC-2) under 100 mW cm2 illumination. We also investigated the IV responses of the ss-DSSCs under different light intensities ranging from 0.2 to 1 Sun.

Experimental

Materials and reagents

Phenothiazine (>98%), phenoxazine (97%), 1,8-dibromooctane (98%), ortho-phenylene diamine (98.5%), benzimidazole (99.9%), methyl iodide stabilized (98%), TiO2 (P25), ethyl cellulose (99%), alfa-terpineol (99%), and titanium isopropoxide (97%), were purchased from Sigma Aldrich and used as received without purification. FTO (fluorine doped tin oxide, 12 ) and sealant materials were purchased from Solaronix, SA, Switzerland. Dimethylformamide (DMF), ethanol, dichloromethane, potassium bicarbonate, sodium metabisulfite were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich. The metal-free organic dye (SK3) was synthesized according to our previous work.25

Characterizations

Structural characterizations of SOICs were carried out with 1H and 13C NMR spectroscopy (Bruker 400 MHz) in DMSO-d6 solvent. Thermogravimetric (TG) analysis was performed by thermal analyzer TGA-Q500 system with nitrogen as purge gas (60 mL min1) at a heating rate of 10 C min1 in a temperature range of 25500 C. The UV-vis absorption spectra experiments were done with UV-vis spectrophotometer (UV-160A). Cyclic voltammetry (CV) was performed on Autolab (FRA 32) electrochemical workstation with Ag/AgCl, Pt-wire and glassy carbon as the reference, counter and working electrode, respectively at a 25 mV s1 scan rate. The similar electrodes setup (working electrode used SK3 dye sensitized TiO2 film) used for MottSchottky analysis. Theoretical calculations were obtained by TD-DFT, using Gaussian 09 software with B3LYP, LanL2Dz basis set. Raman spectra were carried out by using Horiba Jobin Yvon modular Raman spectrometer with a green laser (514 nm, Stellar Pro Argon-ion laser). Steady-state voltammetry was measured on a conventional photo-electrochemical cell with platinum UME as the working electrode and Pt-disk and Ag/AgCl as the counter and reference respectively. The conductivity of the SOICs was measured by impedance spectroscopy with two symmetric conducting electrodes (area 0.22 cm2), on an Autolab electrochemical workstation. The thickness of TiO2 photoanode film was measured by Alpha-Step IQ Surface Profiler.

Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measurements were measured by an Autolab (FRA32) workstation with the frequency ranging from 0.1 Hz to 120 kHz and AC amplitude 10 mV. During the measurement, the device was kept in dark under the application of 0.70 V DC bias. Photocurrentvoltage measurements were performed by using Keithley-2450 source meter and apply a potential bias to the ss-DSSCs under 1 Sun illumination (solar simulator, Newport, Oriel) with a power density of 100 mW cm2 (AM1.5G). The active area of the device was maintained at 0.2 cm2. Open circuit voltage decay (OCVD) was obtained by same autolab instrument and the electron lifetime (e) of SOICs based ss-DSSCs was calculated.

Fabrication of photoelectrochemical cells

The preparation nanocrystalline TiO2 photoanode could be found in our previous works.26,27 Fluorine doped tin oxide (FTO) conducting glass was cleaned with a surfactant, deionized water, and alcohol in an ultrasonic bath for 15 min. A thin buffer layer of TiO2 (titanium isopropoxide dissolved in isopropyl alcohol) was spin coated (2000 rpm, 60 s) onto the FTO followed by annealing at 450 C. Upon annealing, pieces of adhesive tape were fixed along the side of the conducting glass and kept 0.2 cm2 area uncovered. The prepared TiO2 paste was deposited onto the FTO substrate by doctor blade method and calcinated at 450 C for 30 min. The porous TiO2 electrode was then cooled down to 50 C and was kept in 30 mM of SK3 dye (dissolved in ethanol:dichloromethane) solution at room temperature (kept in dark environment) for overnight. The counter electrode was prepared by spin coating 50 mM H2PtCl6 (in IPA) solution on another FTO at 2000 rpm for 60 s. The FTO coated with H2PtCl6 was then heated up to 400 C for 15 min. The solid-state DSSCs had a sandwich structure. The organic ionic conductors were filled into the space between the SK3 sensitized TiO2 photoanode and counter electrode.

The electrolyte consists of SOICs or BIMI, I2, TBP and GSCN at a molar ratio of 0.1:0.01:0.1:0.02 dissolved in acetonitrile solvent. The device was then kept at 40 C to evaporate the organic solvent. The procedure was repeated two to three times to make a compact layer between TiO2 photoanode and Pt-counter electrode.

Synthesis process of SOICs

1,8-di(10H-Phenoxazine-10-yl)octane (1a) and 1,8-di(10H-phenothiazine-10-yl)octane (1b) were synthesized according to the literature procedure.28,29
General procedure for synthesis of 10,10-(octane-1,8-diyl)bis(10H-phenoxazine-3-carbaldehyde) (2a) and 10,10-(octane-1,8-diyl)bis(10H-phenothiazine-3-carbaldehyde) (2b). The compound (1a) (1 g, 2.0 mM) and dry dimethylformamide (520 mg, 7.11 mM) in 1,2 dichloroethane (5 ml), POCl3 (1.1 g, 7.17 mM) was added slowly at 05 C in 50 mL three neck round bottom flask on an ice bath. Then reaction mixture was heated under stirring at 90 C for 24 h and after completion of reaction, the mixture was quenched with DI water and extracted with organic solvent (CHCl3). These organic fractions were washed with brine and dried over Na2SO4. The solvent is removed under reduced pressure and the residue was purified by column chromatography on a silica gel column using dichloromethane and hexane as the eluent (1:4), to give a light pink colour product (compound 2a yield 75%). For synthesising compound (2b), the same protocol as that of (2a) except starting material was taken (1b) (compound 2b yield 70%).

The product was extracted by chloroform from the aqueous phase and purified by column chromatography (compound 3a yield 73%). For synthesis compound (3b), the same protocol was adopted using starting material from compound (2b) (compound yield 72%).

Results and discussion

Electrochemical properties

The chemical structure of SOICs is given in Fig. 1 along with the metal-free sensitizer and general synthesis protocol is presented in Scheme 1. In brief, substitution of the hetero-anthracene donor was done with octyl alkyl chain using sodium hydride. Resultant compounds were formylated using VilsmeierHaack synthesis. The formylated product was coupled with o-phenylenediamine to produce substituted benzimidazolium intermediate. Salt formation was done using methyl iodide following substitution of methyl. The structure and purity of the synthesized SOICs compounds were confirmed by 1H and 13C NMR spectroscopy. Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) was carried out to investigate the thermal properties of the SOICs.

Theoretical study

DFT calculation helps in better understanding the SOICs anion and cation interaction, as well as which types of bonding, are available in electrolytes.

Geometry optimization of SOIC-1, SOIC-2 and BIMI were carried out by using B3LYP functional and LanL2Dz basis set. B3LYP functional is Becke's three parameters hybrid exchange functional couple with nonlocal correlation functional of Lee, Yang, and Parr.37,38 Usually, low molecular weight atoms (i.e. carbon, nitrogen, hydrogen, sulfur, phosphorus, etc.) optimization occur by 6-31G(d,p) basis set, while higher atomic number present atom optimized using LanL2Dz higher basis set required.39 Because of large atomic number of I2 atom, 6-31G (d,p) basis set is incapable of predicting the accurate structure and optical spectrum of SOICs. Energy minimization, absorbance spectrum and time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) were calculated at the B3LYP set. The optimized SOIC-1, SOIC-2 and BIMI are determined at B3LYP/LanL2Dz (SK3 dye optimized by 6-31G (d,p)) level of computational are shown in (Fig. 3(ad)). Same side BIMI cation arrangement is more stable and its optimization energy is depicted in Table 1.

Photovoltaic and photophysical discussion

To study the photovoltaic properties of the SOICs, they were used to fabricate ss-DSSCs devices in combination with the metal-free carbazole based SK3 dye. The photocurrent voltage characteristic was performed under 100 mW cm2, AM 1.5G sunlight illumination and dark condition are presented in Fig. 4(ab). We have examined the effect of solid electrolyte pore filling in different TiO2 photoanode thickness (8 and 12 m) corresponding to bare BIMI and SOICs on ss-DSSCs performance.

Conclusion

We have designed and synthesized hetero-anthracene (PTZ/POZ) substituted BIMI based solid organic ionic conductors (SOICs) which have been used as an electrolyte in ss-DSSCs. The influence of PTZ/POZ donor moiety substituted with BIMI, has increased the cationic effect, ionic conductivity and thermal stability. SOICs possesses higher conductivity compared to the unsubstituted BIMI. The SOICs that consist of substituted hetero-anthracene donor moiety and BIMI ionic salt act as both light harvester and redox mediator. In order to confirm the dual nature of SOICs, the photo-physical and electrochemistry based techniques were carried out. SOICs chemical structure optimization and optical spectra influence are calculated by density functional theory (DFT) and to support the experimental data. The optimized thickness of TiO2 photoanode has improved solid electrolyte pore filling and PCE. The SOIC-1 electrolyte based ss-DSSCs gave the highest PCE of 7.86% with the 8 m thick TiO2 film. Here, reported PCE is quite comparable with conventional liquid electrolytes. Single component (without additive) electrolytes achieved highest PCE is 6.0% for POZ based SOIC-1. We have also shown that the ss-DSSCs photo-response with different light intensity 0.2 to 1 Sun. The synthesized dual function organic ionic conductors open a new way to develop single component solid electrolytes for the ss-DSSCs.
