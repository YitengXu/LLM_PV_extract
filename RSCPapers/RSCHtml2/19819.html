Abstract

Based on the crystal structure prediction, we propose a new allotrope of phosphorene, Ïˆ-phosphorene (Ïˆ-P), with a porous structure, which is both thermally and dynamically stable in comparison with the previously reported allotropes. Due to its unique atom configuration, Ïˆ-P has highly orientation-dependent mechanical properties and excellent flexibility. Calculations using the HSE functional predict that Ïˆ-P is semiconducting with an indirect band gap of 1.57 eV and possesses anisotropic transport properties. Particularly, the electron mobility along the x-direction is up to 1.3 Ã— 104 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1, which is comparable with that of black phosphorene. Considering its intrinsic porous structure, the performance of monolayer Ïˆ-P as a gas purification membrane was investigated. The calculation demonstrates that Ïˆ-P could be used for hydrogen purification from the mixture of CH4, CO2, N2, CO, and H2 with high selectivity. Furthermore, combining a suitable band gap with high carrier mobility, a MoSe2/Ïˆ-P van der Waals heterojunction is predicted to be a good solar cell material, whose power conversion efficiency is estimated up to 20.26%. Finally, we demonstrated that the Au(110) surface could be a suitable substrate for the synthesis of Ïˆ-P.

1. Introduction

As a new member of the two dimensional (2D) materials family, black phosphorene (Î±-P) possesses extraordinary electronic,1â€“4 thermal,5,6 and mechanical properties.4,7â€“10 With a direct band gap, Î±-P has been applied to field effect transistors displaying attractive characteristics,3,11 including a room-temperature on/off ratio of 105 and a peak field effect mobility near 1000 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1, which makes Î±-P a promising candidate for electronic and optoelectronic devices. From the theoretical aspect, first-principles simulations demonstrate that Î±-P could sustain tensile strain10 up to 27% and 30% along the zigzag and armchair directions, respectively. This enormous strain limit of Î±-P results from its unique puckered structure. Compared with graphene, Î±-P displays superior flexibility with a smaller magnitude order of Young's modulus and a highly anisotropic rippling pattern.12 Another attractive property of Î±-P is that it has a negative Poisson's ratio9 along the out-of-plane direction, which has recently been experimentally demonstrated.13

Except for Î±-P, elemental phosphorus can exist in several allotropes, among which the most common are white and red solids.14,15 In addition, other allotropes, including Î²-P,7 Î³-P, Î´-P,16 Î±-P6, and Î²-P6,17 have been proposed. According to the theoretical studies, these allotropes display a variety of electronic, optical, and mechanical properties, which open a window for designing new nanodevices. For example, Î²-P has been successfully synthesized on a Au(111) surface18 and a twisted bilayer Î²-P19 was found to be a promising candidate as a solar cell or photodetection device. Î±-P6 and Î²-P6 are expected to emerge in the epitaxial growth of Î²-P.17 With a high electron mobility and good optical absorption in the visible light region, these two phosphorus sheets may find applications in solar energy conversion.17 In addition, our previous study predicts that Î´-P has a strong auxetic effect, and the negative Poisson's ratio (NPR) is much higher than that in other previously reported 2D materials.20

Although the above allotropes have been proposed, all of those structures are built from chemical or physical intuition, which is incomprehensive and biased. In this study, we perform an unbiased state-of-the-art theoretical crystal structure search based on the particle swarm optimization (PSO) algorithm as implemented in CALYPSO.21,22 Except for those proposed structures, a new thermally and dynamically stable phosphorene allotrope, named Ïˆ-P, was found. Ïˆ-P has a formation energy comparable to previously predicted allotropes such as Î²-P6,17 Î´-P16 and Î¶-P,23 and may be synthesized on a Au(110) surface by means of chemical vapor deposition (CVD) or molecular beam epitaxy (MBE). The intrinsic porosity makes Ïˆ-P applicable in hydrogen purification with a high selectivity from the mixture of CH4, CO2, N2, CO and H2. Furthermore, MoSe2/Ïˆ-P is predicted to be a promising candidate for constructing highly efficient solar cell devices because of its suitable band gap and high electron mobility.

2. Computational methods

Systematic 2D structure searches were performed using the recently developed CALYPSO21,22 code with the particle swarm optimization algorithm containing 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18 and 20 phosphorus atoms per unit cell. The first-principles calculations were carried out by applying the Vienna ab initio simulation package (VASP).24 Structural relaxations and electronic property calculations were simulated by adopting the generalized gradient approximation (GGA) with the Perdewâ€“Burkeâ€“Ernzerhof (PBE)25 exchangeâ€“correlation functional. We also used the Heydâ€“Scuseriaâ€“Ernzerhof (HSE06) hybrid functional26 to correct the band gap errors. Electronic wave functions were expanded in a plane wave basis set with the kinetic energy cutoff of 450 eV. The convergence criteria for electronic SCF iterations and ionic step iterations were set to be 1.0 Ã— 10âˆ’6 eV and âˆ’5.0 Ã— 10âˆ’3 eV Ã…âˆ’1, respectively. A vacuum layer of at least 20 Ã… was chosen to eliminate the interaction between the images resulting from the periodic boundary condition. The reciprocal space was set to be 0.03 Ã— 2Ï€ Ã…âˆ’1 for ionic iterations and 0.02 Ã— 2Ï€ Ã…âˆ’1 for electronic SCF iterations using Monkhorstâ€“Pack meshes centered at the Î“ point. The van der Waals27â€“29 (vdW) correction proposed by Grimme (DFT-D2) was chosen due to its good description of the long-range vdW interactions (more details see the ESIâ€ ).

3. Results and discussion

Geometric structure and stability

We conducted the PSO search several times with different unit cells (Pn, n = 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18 and 20) and the locally optimized structures were selected by comparing their energies. During the search, we obtained the most stable allotrope, Î±-P, and some previously reported structures,3 which demonstrates the validity of this calculation. Apart from familiar phosphorene allotropes, a new structure with 10 atoms per unit cell was gained, namely Ïˆ-phosphorene (Ïˆ-P). From the top view [see in Fig. 1(a)], Ïˆ-P is composed of 4, 6 and 10 membered rings, which is similar to the previously studied metastable structure of phagraphene.30 The optimized lattice parameters are a1 = 6.14 Ã… and a2 = 7.78 Ã…. The stability can be approximately estimated by the â€œoctet ruleâ€ as in other non-planar structures: each phosphorus atom adopts sp3 hybridization, and is bonded to three other phosphorus atoms, ensuring eight electrons in the sp shell. To quantitatively compare the relative stabilities of Ïˆ-P with previously predicted structures, the average energy of different allotropes was calculated and is shown in Fig. 1(b). Ïˆ-P is about 0.09 eV per atom higher than that of Î±-P, which may arise from the 4-membered ring with large ring tension. However, the energy difference among Ïˆ-P, Î²-P6,17 Î´-P16 and Î¶-P23 is very small, implying that these structures have similar stability.

To check the dynamic stability of the Ïˆ-P phase, the phonon spectra was calculated. As shown in Fig. 1(c), Ïˆ-P is dynamically stable because all of the vibrational modes are real in the first Brillouin zone. In addition, ab initio molecular dynamics (AIMD) simulations under constant temperature and volume (NVT) were also performed to check the thermal stability. The system was simulated at T = 300 K, 500 K and 800 K for 5.0 ps with a time-step of 1.0 fs using a 3 Ã— 3 Ã— 1 supercell. To visualize the evolution during the AIMD, we display the snapshot of the crystal configuration in Fig. S1 (see the ESIâ€ ). The results indicate that the geometries at 300 K and 500 K remaining nearly intact at the end of AIMD simulation. However, at 800 K, there is a noticeable structure distortion, illustrating a phase transition at this temperature, which is approximately the melting point of red phosphorus (863 K).31 By contrast, the sublimation temperature of 2D Î±-P (673 K)32 is lower than this temperature, indicating Ïˆ-P is more stable than Î±-P under high temperature.

Electronic properties

We calculated the band structure and projected density of states for Ïˆ-P. As can be seen from Fig. 3(a), Ïˆ-P was predicted to be an indirect semiconductor with a band gap of 0.83 eV, since the valence band maximum (VBM) was located at the Î“, whereas the conduction band minimum (CBM) lies between the Y and Î“ in the Brillouin zone. Its corresponding density of states (DOS) is shown in Fig. 3(b). Based on the DOS, the VBM is mainly contributed by 3pz orbitals. However, unlike VBM, the CBM is a hybridized state of 3s and 3p. This was confirmed by the charge density distribution of VBM and CBM, as shown in Fig. 3(c). These features of VBM and CBM will directly affect the strain engineering of band gaps and transport properties. Since the PBE functional systematically underestimates the band gap, the HSE06 functional was also used to correct the band gap. The calculated band gap was 1.57 eV.

Potential applications

Given that Ïˆ-P possesses superior mechanical and electronic properties, an exploration of its potential applications was desirable. In this work, we investigated its potential applications as a purification membrane and as a solar cell material.
Hydrogen purification membrane. Hydrogen purification from other gases is of particular importance for energy applications.47 Taking the advantages of easy operation and low energy cost, membrane separation has been widely used in hydrogen purification.48 Since a natural porous structure exists in Ïˆ-P, we explored the possibility of Ïˆ-P as a candidate hydrogen separation membrane. The stability of molecule adsorption on Ïˆ-P, including CH4, CO2, N2, CO and H2, was firstly evaluated. As summarized in Table 2, the equilibrium distance between Ïˆ-P and gas molecules ranges from 2.77 to 3.56 Ã…. The adsorption energy of H2 was about 0.04 eV and the largest adsorption energy was about 0.12 eV for the CO molecule. Therefore, the conclusion was that porous Ïˆ-P was chemically inert to these gas molecules, and gas molecules interacted with Ïˆ-P via weak vdW interactions.

According to the Arrhenius equation,50 the selectivity of H2 relative to other gas molecules through the hole of Ïˆ-P can be evaluated by eqn (S10) (see the ESIâ€ ). Assuming that the diffusion pre-factors of these gas molecules are identical (A = 1011 sâˆ’1),51 we calculated the temperature-dependent diffusion rate and selectivity [Fig. 5(b) and (c)]. The diffusion rates of H2 molecules were much higher than those of CH4, CO2, N2 and CO, consistent with the calculated diffusion energy barriers. We found a high selectivity on the order of about 1019, 1015, 1012 and 1011 for H2 over CH4, CO2, N2 and CO, respectively (Table S3, ESIâ€ ). Compared with previously reported silica-based52,53 and graphene-based50 membranes, Ïˆ-P exhibits high selectivity for H2 over CH4, CO2, N2 and CO gas molecules. Considering the high selectivity for H2 over CO [3.16 Ã— 1011 (298 K) to 4.08 Ã— 108 (398 K)], Ïˆ-P could be used as a promising purification membrane to reduce the CO concentration in reformate H2 from 1000â€“10000 ppm to the several ppm level, where CO in reformate H2 is known to degrade the performance of a proton exchange membrane fuel cell (PEMFC) by poisoning the anode active sites.54 All in all, the special geometric structure makes Ïˆ-P a potential hydrogen purification membrane.

Solar cell. A previous study55 has indicated that Î±-P was a promising material for fast and broadband photo detection and solar cell application56 due to its high carrier mobility and suitable band gap (1.53 eV). Noting that the band gap of Ïˆ-P is close to the value of Î±-P, to see whether Ïˆ-P was also a good candidate semiconductor for solar cells, we calculated the frequency dependent absorption coefficients. As a comparison, the absorption coefficients of Î±-P were also plotted in Fig. 5(d). Although Ïˆ-P is an indirect semiconductor, it still exhibits comparable sunlight absorption with Î±-P, which manifests that Ïˆ-P can be a good donor material if an appropriate acceptor material can be found. For Ïˆ-P, the computed VBM and CBM were âˆ’5.39 eV and âˆ’3.82 eV, respectively; while for MoSe2,57 the VBM and CBM levels are âˆ’5.59 eV and âˆ’3.91 eV, respectively. The latter energy levels match those of Ïˆ-P very well, giving a type-II alignment [see Fig. 5(e)]. Thus, a potential solar-cell device consisting of MoSe2/Ïˆ-P was constructed. Furthermore, in order to evaluate the performance of the MoSe2/Ïˆ-P system, we calculated the power conversion efficiency56,58â€“60 (PCE) according to eqn (S8) (ESIâ€ ). As displayed in Fig. 5(f), the MoSe2/Ïˆ-P hetero-bilayer could yield a PCE of 20.26%, which is higher than that of PCBM/CBN (12â€“20%)58 and MoS2/Î±-P systems (16â€“18%).56 Noting that the direct band at the Î“ point is only 0.19 eV higher than the band gap of Ïˆ-P, the real PCE should be close to the theoretical value.

Potential CVD or MBE substrate

Although 2D Ïˆ-P shows intriguing structural and electronic properties for potential applications as a hydrogen purification membrane and in solar cells, the synthesis of this material is a critical issue. Through careful searching, we found the Au(110) surface, with the 2 Ã— 2 supercell (a = 5.77 Ã…, b = 8.16 Ã…), may be used to match the lattice of Ïˆ-P, whose mismatch is smaller than the previously reported blue phosphorene on Au(111) surface.18 Moreover, the total energy of Ïˆ-P, with lattice parameters a = 5.77 Ã… and b = 8.1,6 is only 0.0636 eV per atom higher in energy than that of free standing Ïˆ-P. Therefore, the Au(110) surface [see Fig. S4(a) and (b) in ESIâ€ ] will be a potential substrate for the synthesis of the Ïˆ-P monolayer using prevailing techniques such as CVD or MBE. To help identify possible Ïˆ-P in future experiments, we also calculated the STM images of Ïˆ-P on a Au substrate [see Fig. S4(c) in ESIâ€ ].

4. Conclusions

In summary, on the basis of an unbiased PSO searching algorithm and DFT calculations, we present a new thermally and dynamically stable allotrope of phosphorene, namely Ïˆ-P. The Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio and strain under constant in-plane stress are highly orientation-dependent. The calculation shows that Ïˆ-P is a semiconductor with a band gap of 1.57 eV. Moreover, the number of layers and external stress can be used to tune the band gaps, which will broaden its applications for different semiconductor devices in the future. Due to its porous structure, single layered Ïˆ-P is demonstrated to be a promising membrane for hydrogen purification from gas mixtures of CH4, CO2, N2 and CO with high selectivity. Besides its outstanding mechanical, electronic and structural properties, Ïˆ-P also has good transport properties and optical properties, which makes Ïˆ-P a potential solar cell donor material. Combining with a suitable acceptor material, the MoSe2/Ïˆ-P van der Waals heterojunction was predicted to be a good candidate for a solar cell with a theoretical PCE up to 20.26%. Finally, we propose that Ïˆ-P may be synthesized on the Au(110) surface using the CVD or MBE method due to the relatively small lattice mismatch between the Ïˆ-P monolayer and the Au substrate. We hope our study will stimulate further experimental efforts on this subject.
