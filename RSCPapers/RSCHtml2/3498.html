Abstract

The low photocatalytic activity of single semiconductor photocatalysts mainly originates from their fast recombination of photogenerated electronâhole pairs. Constructing nanosheet-based composite photocatalysts is an effective way to enhance the charge separation efficiency of photogenerated electronâhole pairs. In this work, AgI-decorated Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheet heterostructured photocatalysts were prepared by a facile method. The as-obtained AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 heterostructures exhibited enhanced visible-light-driven photocatalytic activity towards the degradation of methyl orange (MO) and tetracycline hydrochloride (TC) in aqueous solution. The optimum photocatalytic activity of 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 for the degradation of MO was about 4.1 and 6.2 times higher than that of AgI and Î²-Bi2O3. The photocatalytic activity enhancement of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 heterostructures could be ascribed to the formation of a Z-scheme system, which results in the efficient space separation of photo-induced charge carriers.

1. Introduction

Searching and optimizing visible-light-driven photocatalysts with high performance is of great importance for their potential application in solar energy conversion and environmental purification since visible light accounts for about 43% of the total radiation emitted by the solar spectrum.1â5 Bismuth oxide, a p-type semiconductor, appears to be a promising alternative due to its small band gap (â¼2.8 eV), high oxidation power of valence holes, and non-toxic properties.6â9 Bi2O3 has many polymorphs, including the Î±- (monoclinic), Î²- (tetragonal), Î³- (body-centered cubic), Î´-(face-centered cubic), and Ï- (triclinic) phases.10 Among these, tetragonal Î²-Bi2O3 was reported to have better photocatalytic performance due to its smaller band gap, which could induce stronger absorption in the visible light region.11,12 However, like many other semiconductors, the fast recombination rate of photogenerated electrons and holes is one of the main drawbacks that restricts the photocatalytic efficiency of pure Î²-Bi2O3. In order to further improve the photocatalytic efficiency of Bi2O3, various methods have been developed, such as construction of heterojunctions with other semiconductors, morphology and surface modification of Bi2O3.13â15 Especially, constructing heterojunctions between Bi2O3 and other semiconductors with matched band-edges is highly desirable because of their ability to enhance the charge separation efficiency. In this regard, a large number of Bi2O3-based heterojunctions, such as Bi2O3/Bi2WO6,16â18 Bi2O3/BiVO4,19,20 Bi2O3/Co3O4,21 Bi2O3/TiO2,22 Bi2O3/BaTiO3,23 BiOCl/Bi2O3,24 Fe3O4/Bi2O325 and Bi2O3/ZnO,26 have been developed to improve photocatalytic activity of Bi2O3. Recently, silver halide-based nanoparticles have been demonstrated as a class of highly efficient promising visible-light-response photocatalysts for organic pollutants.27â30 They also act as excellent sensitizers to modify wide-gap semiconductors to become visible-light responsive.31,32

Recently, two-dimensional (2D) inorganic nanosheets have drawn substantial research interest in photocatalysis due to their unique 2D geometry with outstanding electronic properties and high specific surface area, which could provide a large amount of reaction sites and enable rapid charge generation and transport.33â36 Various semiconducting metal oxide photocatalysts, such as layered metal oxides, double hydroxides, and transition metal chalcogenide nanosheets, are being extensively investigated.37â42 For example, Hu and co-workers recently reported the synthesis of 2D Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets enclosed by the {001} facets with enhanced photocatalytic activity.43 By introducing the upconversion element erbium, the Bi2O3 nanosheets exhibited an enhanced photoactivity. However, much less attention has been paid to constructing Bi2O3 nanosheet-based heterojunction photocatalysts, which could provide abundant coupling interfaces due to their large surface area and structural features of 2D Bi2O3 nanosheets. Construction of a novel heterojunction with superior activity by using 2D Bi2O3 nanosheets as the support for the dispersed silver halide nanoparticles is desirable for achieving efficient visible-light-induced photocatalysts.

In this work, we constructed a novel AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 nanocomposite by the facile precipitation method at room temperature. The photocatalytic activity of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 nanocomposites was evaluated by the photodegradation of methyl orange (MO) dye and tetracycline hydrochloride (TC) under visible light irradiation (Î» > 400 nm). The as-prepared AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 nanocomposite exhibited enhanced photocatalytic activities for MO and TC degradation. Furthermore, we demonstrated that these improved photocatalytic performances should be attributed to the fabrication of a direct Z-scheme system of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3, which can result in a more efficient separation of photoinduced charge carriers. This work will provide new insights into the design of high-performance Bi2O3 nanosheet-based Z-scheme photocatalysts for efficient degradation of organic pollutants.

2. Experimental section

2.1 Preparation of Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets

Bismuth nitrate hexahydrate (Bi(NO3)3Â·5H2O, AR), silver nitrate (AgNO3, AR), potassium iodide (KI, AR), acetic acid and absolute ethanol were used as received without any further purification. 3.4 Mechanism of MO photocatalytic degradation

It has been reported that the ËO2â, h+, and ËOH are the major reactive species for the photocatalytic oxidation of organic dyes.48â50 In order to distinguish the roles of the reactive species, the scavenger investigation was performed over 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 by adding different scavengers, such as tert-butanol (t-BuOH), ammonium oxalate (AO) and 1,4-benzoquinone (BQ), for the detection of the hydroxyl radical (ËOH) scavenger, hole (h+) scavenger and superoxide (ËO2â), respectively. As shown in Fig. 8, with 1 mmol of tert-butyl alcohol (t-BuOH) as a ËOH scavenger added to the solution, the degradation rate of MO over the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 sample is not obviously decreased, indicating the absence of ËOH radical species in the reaction system. When 1 mmol of AO as a scavenger for h+ radical species was added, the degradation of MO was significantly depressed, indicating that the h+ was involved in the degradation of MO. Notably, with the addition of 1 mmol of ËO2â scavenger BQ, the degradation of MO was also significantly depressed, suggesting that the ËO2â pathways have a crucial role in the process of MO oxidation. To further confirm this result, a N2 purging experiment was conducted compared with air-equilibrated conditions. As shown in Fig. 8, the removal efficiency of MO is obviously inhibited by conducting N2 (0 scavenger, N2); the degradation rate of MO was remarkably decreased compared with that under air-equilibrated conditions (0 scavenger), which shows that dissolved oxygen which can act as a photogenerated electron scavenger to give ËO2â radical species is pivotal for the MO degradation. 3.5 Mechanism of enhanced photocatalytic activity

It is generally accepted that photocatalytic activity is mainly governed by light-absorption ability, surface properties (including adsorption property, specific surface area, etc.), and photogenerated charge-separation efficiency. To investigate the effect of specific surface area of the samples on the photocatalytic activity, we tested the specific surface areas of the two typical samples: pure Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets and 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composites. As shown in Fig. 9, both the isotherms for these two samples exhibit a type IV with a H3 hysteresis loop according to the IUPAC classification. The BET surface area of pure Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets and 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 is 8.85 and 8.16 m2 gâ1, indicating that the BET surface area was only slightly reduced by the introduction of AgI nanoparticles. However, the apparent reaction rate constant of the MO degradation reaction over the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 is 6.2 times that of Î²-Bi2O3. Therefore, it can be concluded that the enhanced photocatalytic activity of the samples has no direct relationship with the BET surface areas. As mentioned above, the AgI nanoparticle loading is incapable of enhancing the visible light absorption of Î²-Bi2O3, indicating that the light-adsorption property is also not crucial for the photocatalytic activity of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composites. Therefore, the enhanced photocatalytic activities of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composites might be mainly attributed to the synergic effect between AgI and Î²-Bi2O3, which can greatly accelerate the separation of photogenerated carriers.

The photo-generated electrons in AgI migrate to the surface and lead to the reduction reaction. Meanwhile, the electrons can activate molecular oxygen, e.g., the formation of ËO2â, which together with photo-generated holes in the valence band (VB) of Î²-Bi2O3 can drive the oxidation reaction. The holes from AgI and electrons from Î²-Bi2O3 recombine at the interface contact, completing the relay of the âZ-schemeâ system. This Z-scheme model has been also demonstrated in the previously reported work. For example, Tian et al. reported a Z-scheme BiVO4/g-C3N4 hybrid photocatalyst with enhanced photocatalytic ability due to the efficient separation of the photogenerated electrons and holes.52 Liu and co-workers found that the nanotree-like CdS/ZnO nanocomposites act as a âZ-schemeâ system to drive the photocatalytic processes and improve the photocatalytic activity.53 Chen et al. synthesized the highly active Z-scheme NaNbO3/WO3 photocatalysts via the hydrothermal method and the resulting photocatalysts showed enhanced photocatalytic activity under visible light irradiation.54

3.6 Regeneration and reusability

The reusability and stability of the photocatalysts are vital for the practical application. To evaluate the reusability and stability of the AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composite photocatalysts, recycling reactions are performed for the photodegradation of MO over the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composite under visible light irradiation (Fig. 12a). After four consecutive cycles, no apparent deactivation of the photocatalysts is observed, which implies the high stability of the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 hybrid material. The XRD and TEM patterns of the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composite were also investigated after four recycling runs for photodegradation of MO. As shown in Fig. 12b and S3,â  the XRD patterns and TEM image of the 20%-AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 composite before and after reaction reveal that the phase and structure remain unchanged. These results indicate that the AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 heterostructure photocatalyst is sufficiently stable during the photodegradation of the organic dye.

4. Conclusions

In summary, 2D Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets were synthesized and decorated with AgI nanoparticles to construct nanosheet-based heterojunction photocatalysts. The as-obtained AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 heterostructures exhibited enhanced photocatalytic activity compared to the pure AgI and Î²-Bi2O3 in the degradation of MO and TC under visible light irradiation. It was found that the photodegradation of MO molecules is mainly attributed to the oxidation action of the generated h+ and ËO2â. The photocatalytic activity enhancement of AgI/Î²-Bi2O3 heterostructures could be ascribed to the fabrication of Z-scheme AgI/Î²-Bi2O3, which can result in the more efficient separation and largely reduced recombination probability of photoexcited electronâhole pairs. This work not only furthered the understanding of Z-scheme photocatalysis, but also provides an opportunity for future development of high-performance photocatalysts based on 2D Î²-Bi2O3 nanosheets.
