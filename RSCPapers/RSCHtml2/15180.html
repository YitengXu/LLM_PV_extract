Abstract

1. Introduction

Small molecule organic semiconductors have received considerable attention owing to their unique physical properties that make them promising candidates in functional organic device applications.1 Particularly, pushâ€“pull molecules in the recent years have emerged as a sub-class of Ï€-conjugated organic semiconductors for various optoelectronic applications.2 Among the different forms of pushâ€“pull configurations,3 the simplest is the linear arrangement of electron donor (D) and acceptor (A) fragments end-capped on a Ï€-conjugated spacer (Ï€).4 Compared to the symmetric systems (Dâ€“Ï€â€“D and Aâ€“Ï€â€“A), the asymmetric pushâ€“pull arrangement (Dâ€“Ï€â€“A) accounts for divergent optoelectronic properties because of the facile intramolecular charge transfer (ICT) from the HOMO of donor to the LUMO of acceptor.5 Also, because of their non-centrosymmetric nature, they tend to exhibit electric dipole moment whereas the centrosymmetric Aâ€“Ï€â€“A and Dâ€“Ï€â€“D molecules typically show electric quadrupole moment (Fig. 1).6 For instance, Misra's group has explored the influence of symmetrical and unsymmetrical pushâ€“pull effect on the optoelectronic properties of various twisted ICT chromophores.7 Attias et al. compared the non-linear optical properties of symmetric and asymmetric conjugated 3,3â€²-bipyridine chromophores.8 The impact of asymmetric effect on the panchromatic absorbance in organic dyes was disclosed by Evans et al.9 The combined chiroptical properties and computational analysis of the symmetrical as well as the non-symmetrical chromophores was reported by Sergeyev et al.10 BÃ¤uerle's team established the effect of structureâ€“property relationship of symmetrically and unsymmetrically substituted S,N-heteropentacenes on the optoelectronic and photovoltaic performance.11 In a similar way to these literature precedents, the aim of our investigation is to glean the molecular structureâ€“physical property relationship between the linear pushâ€“pull chromophore of the type Dâ€“Ï€â€“D, Aâ€“Ï€â€“A (symmetric), and Dâ€“Ï€â€“A (asymmetric). Due to the advantage of good optoelectronic properties coupled with their photovoltaic application, we used fluoreno-thiophene template as Ï€-spacer12 in conjunction with diphenylaminostyryl and dicyanovinyl as D and A sub-units, respectively.

As part of our research interest on molecular chemistry for materials and biological applications,13 we herein disclose the synthesis and optoelectronic investigation of three new pushâ€“pull chromophores (TPA-TPA, CN-CN, and TPA-CN). To gain insight on the effect of D and A units on the optical and electrochemical properties, we carried out systematic variation on the Ï€-spacer core by incorporating (i) two D groups (Dâ€“Ï€â€“D, TPA-TPA), (ii) two A groups (Aâ€“Ï€â€“A, CN-CN), and (iii) one D and one A group (Dâ€“Ï€â€“A, TPA-CN). The effect of such substituent alterations on the optoelectronic properties was assessed by photophysical/electrochemical studies and correlated with theoretical calculations. Finally, organic photovoltaic devices were constructed for each of the aforementioned chromophores to understand the impact of the symmetric as well as the asymmetric pushâ€“pull transition on the cell performance and all the results are discussed in this paper.

2. Results and discussion

2.1 Chemical synthesis and structural characterization

As depicted in Scheme 1, our chemical synthesis commenced with the diformylation of commercially available 2,7-bis(thien-2-yl)-9,9-dioctylfluorene under Vilsmeierâ€“Haack conditions to obtain the common dialdehyde precursor CHO-CHO. In fact, this preparative method slightly differs from the one devised by Chen et al. as there is a reduction in the stoichiometry of POCl3 (2.0 eq.) and DMF (3.5 eq.) in order to improvise the yield of CHO-CHO (96%).12a The obtained CHO-CHO intermediate upon Knoevenagel condensation with 1.0 and 2.2 equivalents of malononitrile using basic alumina affords CN-CHO and CN-CN, respectively, in good yields. Likewise, CHO-CHO and CN-CHO were subjected to Wittig olefination with 2.2 and 1.1 equivalents of triphenylamine phosphonium ylide13a to form the desired TPA-TPA and TPA-CN, respectively. All the chromophores displayed excellent solubility in most of the commonly used organic solvents such as CH2Cl2, CHCl3, EtOAc, THF, and o-dichlorobenzene, which signifies their likely use in solution-processed organic solar cell fabrication. The chemical structure of all the chromophores was established by spectral data (FTIR, 1H NMR, 13C NMR, DEPT-135, MS) and elemental analyses. In the FT-IR spectra, the newly formed CC bond in TPA-TPA, CN-CN, and TPA-CN was confirmed by a characteristic stretching peak at 1589, 1559, and 1574 cmâˆ’1, respectively. In addition, a distinct peak at 2217 and 2218 cmâˆ’1 corresponds to the nitrile functionality in CN-CN and TPA-CN, respectively. In the 1H NMR spectrum of TPA-TPA, a doublet at 6.82 ppm with a coupling constant (J) of 15.0 Hz suggests the presence of olefinic hydrogens in the trans-configuration. For the same compound, two peaks appeared at 119.7 and 120.1 ppm in the 13C NMR, which indicates the presence of two olefinic carbon atoms. In the case of CN-CN, the appearance of a sharp singlet in the 1H NMR spectrum at 7.75 ppm reveals the presence of two CH(CN)2 protons. In the 13C NMR spectrum, a peak at 156.9 ppm suggests the presence of olefinic carbon (CH) and two peaks at 113.4 and 114.2 ppm correspond to the presence of nitrile carbons. Moreover, DEPT-135 spectra of all the compounds were recorded to confirm the number of carbons in different environments such as primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary. The HRMS data of TPA-TPA, CN-CN, and TPA-CN exhibited a molecular ion [M+] peak corresponding to m/z = 1092.5445, 706.3157, and 899.4301 [M+], which unambiguously confirm the product formation.

The images of frontier Ï€-molecular orbitals as predicted by DFT calculations are depicted in Fig. 4, left panel. In the case of TPA-TPA, the HOMO is delocalized over the electron rich triphenylamine, thienyl, and fluorenyl portions, whilst the LUMO is concentrated more on the thienyl and fluorenyl units. In the chromophore CN-CN, the HOMO is contributed mostly by the electron rich aromatic units such as thienyl and fluorenyl. 2.4 Redox properties

To evaluate the redox properties and orbital energy levels of the synthesized chromophores, cyclic voltammetry (CV) was performed in chloroform (2.0 Ã— 10âˆ’3 M) by utilizing nBu4NPF6 (0.1 M) as the supporting electrolyte at room temperature (Fig. 7). Platinum foil, glassy carbon, and silver wire were used as the counter, working, and reference electrodes, respectively. A standard ferrocenium/ferrocene redox couple (Fc+/Fc) showing a half wave potential (E1/2) of 0.49 V vs. Ag wire was used for calibration. At the outset, analysis of the CV results indicates that the anodic potential (Eox) of all the compounds is irreversible. This observation suggests that the in situ formation of radical-cationic species is either unstable or the species might engage in subsequent complex reactions spurred by electron transfer. A quick glance at the CV data confirmed that the Eox value downshifts progressively with every increase in the number of triphenylamine moiety (TPA-TPAvs.TPA-CN). This can understandably be attributed to the easy oxidation of the systems containing a greater number of electron-rich triphenylamine groups (Table 4).26 For instance, TPA-TPA showed Eox and Ered at 0.65 and âˆ’0.92 V, respectively, whereas the redox potential was found to be at 0.68 and âˆ’0.82 V, respectively, for TPA-CN. In contrast, CN-CN displayed two oxidation waves (Eox) at 1.45 and 1.67 V, and one reduction wave (Ered) at âˆ’0.78 V. For CN-CN, the first oxidation potential is shifted more positively compared to other chromophores, which could be attributed to the deep location of the HOMO brought by the strong electron withdrawing effect of the two CH(CN)2 groups. It is also to be remembered that when the potential window was slightly extended, a second oxidation occurs immediately, which allows no reversibility of the redox process. Since, only the onset of the oxidation potential is required to calculate the HOMO, the first oxidation potential was immediately reversed. By applying the Pommerehne empirical relation,27 the frontier molecular orbital energies (EHOMO and ELUMO) were calculated from the respective Eox and Ered onset values. To this end, the HOMO/LUMO energies for TPA-TPA, CN-CN, and TPA-CN were estimated to be âˆ’4.96/âˆ’3.39 eV, âˆ’5.76/âˆ’3.53 eV, and âˆ’4.99/âˆ’3.48 eV, respectively. Although, the electrochemical energy level correlates with the theoretical calculations in merely a reasonable capacity (vide supra), the observed trend was found to be in good agreement. Likewise, the electrochemical band gap for CN-CN (ECVg = 2.23 eV) correlates well with its optical band gap (Eoptg = 2.37 eV). For TPA-TPA and TPA-CN, the ECVg (1.57 and 1.51 eV) is lesser compared to their Eoptg (2.52 and 2.21 eV). This discrepancy is probably attributed to the presence of poor electron carriers such as the triphenylamine group, hence, the value of the obtained reduction potential may not be reliable. Nonetheless, the orbital energies derived from the computational and electrochemical studies for asymmetric pushâ€“pull TPA-CN was found to be in between those of the symmetric TPA-TPA and CN-CN, as expected.

It is noteworthy to mention that the cell performance got increased only after solvent vacuum annealing (SVA) treatment. 3. Conclusions

In summary, three new chromophores (TPA-TPA, CN-CN, and TPA-CN) were constructed on the fluoreno-thiophene backbone. These chromophores were differentiated by varying the lateral groups such as diphenylaminostyryl and dicyanovinyl to form one asymmetric (TPA-CN) and two symmetric (TPA-TPA, CN-CN) structures. The influence of this structural variance on the physical properties was assessed by photo-absorption studies and correlated with theoretical investigation. These results revealed a reduced band gap for the dipolar chromophore TPA-CN compared to its quadrupolar counterparts. As far as the relative position of the frontier orbitals is concerned, both cyclic voltammetry and DFT calculations exposed a deep HOMOâ€“LUMO location for CN-CN. TGA analysis showed a remarkable thermal stability for TPA-CN compared to the other compounds. The photovoltaic performance in the prototype devices revealed a power conversion efficiency of 2.01% for TPA-CN, which is better than the symmetric TPA-TPA (1.07%) and CN-CN (1.47%). The better efficiency for TPA-CN was supported by its smooth film forming ability and crystallinity, as confirmed by AFM and XRD respectively. On a whole, the present work unravels the impact of symmetric and asymmetric pushâ€“pull effect in modulating the molecular properties of Ï€-conjugated semiconductors, which in turn is essential for the desired optoelectronic applications.
