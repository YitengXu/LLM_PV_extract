Abstract

This paper reports for the first time the preparation of Co3O4 nanorods by simple hydrothermal and annealing methods, which are doped with polyoxometalates that act as an intermediary in electronic transmission to accelerate electron transport. The designed H3PW12O40(PW12)/Co3O4â€“Cu2S composite film was prepared by the screen printing method to boost its efficiency as the counter electrode for quantum dot-sensitized solar cells, where the preparation process is simple and inexpensive. PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S (Jsc = 16.60 mA cmâˆ’2, Voc = 0.636 V, Rct = 0.32 Î© and FF = 0.436) shows better electrocatalytic performance compared to the original Co3O4 and Cu2S. Accordingly, the photoelectric conversion efficiency of PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S is 4.67%, which is 10%, 46%, 55.6%, and 72% higher than those of Co3O4â€“Cu2S (4.2%), Cu2S (2.54%), PW12/Co3O4 (2.07%), and Co3O4 (1.30%), respectively. Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy and Tafel measurements performed on two CE symmetric virtual cells proved the excellent electrocatalytic activity of PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S. Therefore, this composite film can be used as an alternative option for QDSSC counter electrodes.

Introduction

The large consumption of fossil energy has resulted in the global energy crisis as well as environmental pollution. To solve this issue, it is urgent for us to search for low-cost and high-performance solar devices at present.1,2 Solar cells are devices that convert solar energy directly into electrical energy based on the photoelectric effect, and have attracted extensive attention.3â€“8 Quantum-dot-sensitized solar cells (QDSSCs) are considered as promising third-generation cells due to their facilely tunable band gaps7 through size or composition control, high molar extinction coefficient,9 and multiple exciton generation (MEG) possibility, which could make QD-based solar cells to overcome the Shockleyâ€“Queisser efficiency limit of 32.9%.10â€“12 To date, 12.75% is the highest known conversion efficiency achieved by QDSSCs consisting of ZnCuInSe co-sensitized with CdSe photoanodes and titanium mesh supported mesoporous carbon as the counter electrode, which was reported by Zhong's group.13 The theoretical PCE value of QDSSCs could reach 44%, nonetheless, the actual PCE value is still far behind. To further enhance the conversion efficiency, the performance of solar cells should be continuously improved by further optimization. During recent decades, prior studies have largely focused on the research on photoanodes, while there are only a few reports on counter electrodes. It is well known that CEs are responsible for collecting electrons from the external circuit and catalyzing the oxidation of the polysulfide electrolyte at the electrolyte/CE interface. The counter electrode should have a large specific surface area, good electrical conductivity, chemical durability and effective catalytic activity to ensure outstanding catalytic performance.14,15 To date, CEs are based primarily on the following materials: metals, metallic compounds, carbon derivatives, conducting polymers, and composite materials.12 The surfaces of Pt CEs can adsorb sulfur atoms in electrolytes, leading to poor conductivity and low catalytic activity. To address this limitation, in the recent literature Cu2S, MoS2, PbS, NiS, and CoS have been considered as counter electrodes in QDSSCs due to their good catalytic activity in polysulfide electrolytes; however, they suffered continual corrosion and ultimately mechanical instability under operating conditions.6,15â€“18 Accordingly, various composite CEs comprising TiN NPs/CNTs-RGO, Fe2S/Ni foam, CuS/CoS, CoS/NiS, NiS/PbS, PbS/carbon and CuS NPs/carbon nanofibers were extensively researched to further increase their electrocatalytic properties in QDSSCs.15,19â€“24 The QDSSCs consisting of the CdS/CdSe photoanodes and composite CoS/NiS CEs obtained a higher PCE of 3.40%, compared with NiS (2.61%), CoS (2.53%), and Pt (1.47%) CEs. The QDSSCs fabricated by incorporating composite Cu2Sâ€“SiW12/MoS2 CEs and CdS photoanodes, according to our previous work, show PCEs of 4.28% (Cu2Sâ€“SiW12/MoS2), 2.90% (Cu2Sâ€“MoS2), 2.45% (Cu2S), 2.18% (SiW12/MoS2) and 1.25% (MoS2).20,25

Co3O4 is a widely studied metal oxide, which is a p-type semiconductor found in abundance.26 Co3O4 has a spinel crystal structure resulting from the cubic closed packing array, in which the Co(II) ions and Co(III) ions occupy the 8a position of the tetrahedron and the 16d position of the octahedron, respectively.27â€“31 Co3O4 is also a superior catalyst, which has been applied in supercapacitors, magnetic lithium ion batteries, CO gas sensors and so on.32â€“35

Polyoxometalates (POM) are a group of multinuclear transition metal oxygen cluster compounds. POMs with strong oxidizing properties can be used to receive electrons and transmit photo-generated electrons. Therefore, selecting a suitable polyoxometalate to compound it with a semiconductor material could promote electronâ€“hole separation and improve the efficiency of electron transport, thereby optimizing the cell performance. The lower Rs and Rct suggest that the addition of PW12 can facilitate the transport of charge carriers, which decreases the electronâ€“hole recombination.36â€“40

Our group previously added POMs to MoS2 to improve the photovoltaic performance, and also obtained significant performance enhancement.25 In this work, QDSSCs based on CdS/TiO2 as photoanodes and the composite film of PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S as CEs were assembled. Here, Co3O4 nanorods doped with polyoxometalates were used for the first time to accelerate electron transfer and a composite film with Cu2S was fabricated to further improve the efficiency and stability of the cell. The composite CEs showed good electrocatalytic activity in agreement with EIS and Tafel test results. Specifically, under standard light intensity (AM 1.5G, 100 mW cmâˆ’2), the power efficiency of the QDSSCs fabricated with the PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S counter electrode can reach 4.67%, exceeding those of other QDSSCs fabricated with Co3O4â€“Cu2S (4.20%), bare Cu2S (2.54%), PW12/Co3O4 (2.07%) and Co3O4 (1.30%) counter electrodes. This enhancement is mainly caused by POM's preventing holes from recombining with electrons at the counter electrode interface and accelerating electron transfer on the counter electrode, accompanied by the synergistic effect of other components on the device and the good band gap matching of Co3O4, PW12 and Cu2S.

Electrochemical properties of the counter electrodes

We depended on electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measurements to investigate the electrocatalytic activity and charge transfer process of the as-prepared counter electrodes (CEs). Using Z-view software, the Nyquist plots fitted curves are shown in Fig. 5a and b. From the Nyquist diagram, the first starting point represents Rs that is the series resistance of the cell. The first circle is a high-frequency semicircle named Rct, which can estimate the charge transfer resistance, that is the charge transfer process between the counter electrode and the electrolyte. The Rw is the diffusion resistance in the low frequency region, which is provided by the electrolyte. Zw is a Warburg diffusion resistance provided by a polysulfide electrolyte in the low frequency region. The Rs also influences the electron transport between the FTO and the catalytic film, a lower Rs indicates a better electron transport. As the Rs of the PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S CE (1.629 Î©) is the lowest compared to other counter electrodes, the electron transfer should be the fastest. As shown in Table 1, it can be observed that the PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S CE has a lower Rct value (0.32 Î©), which is much smaller than those of Co3O4â€“Cu2S, Cu2S, PW12/Co3O4 and Co3O4 CEs (0.39, 1,923, 3.225, and 4.332 Î©, respectively). The good band gap matching of Co3O4 (âˆ’4.7 eV, AVS), PW12 (âˆ’4.72 eV, AVS)46 and Cu2S (âˆ’4.44 eV, AVS) are conducive to the transmission of electrons. In addition, PW12 serving as one of the polyoxometalates can improve device performance and effectively block holes, thereby preventing holes from recombining with electrons at the counter electrode interface and accelerating the transfer of electrons on the counter electrode. Remarkably, PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S exhibits outstanding electrocatalytic performance because PW12 has good electron transport capabilities, accompanied by QDSSCs having an excellent photovoltaic performance. In order to further explore the electro-catalytic performance of different CEs, Tafel polarization measurements were conducted on the same symmetrical batteries in EIS. A Tafel curve is theoretically divided into three major regions, as displayed in Fig. 6. The first region is the polarization zone where the voltage is small. The second region is called the Tafel zone at the median potential, which has a higher slope value. The Tafel curve can also supply two important parameters: the exchange current density (J0) and the limiting current density (Jlim). Conclusions

In summary, Co3O4 prepared by simple hydrothermal and air calcination methods was fabricated into the PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S composite film via screen printing. The improvement of the photovoltaic performance should be related to the band matching of the PW12 (4.72 eV, AVS), Co3O4 (4.7 eV, AVS), and Cu2S (âˆ’4.44 eV, AVS) with PW12 serving as POM, which act as an intermediary in the electronic transmission. Experimental results reveal that the composite film PW12/Co3O4â€“Cu2S has excellent electrocatalytic performance and high photoelectric conversion efficiency reaching 4.67%, which is 46%, 55.6%, and 72%, respectively, higher than those of Cu2S, PW12/Co3O4 and Co3O4 CEs. The above results show that this composite film can be used as an alternative option for QDSSC counter electrodes.
