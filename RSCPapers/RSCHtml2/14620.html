First published on 30th June 2006

Abstract

This review highlights the year 2005 in fullerene chemistry and includes the elegant multi-step organic synthesis of H2@C60 from an opened cage aza-thia fullerene derivative. Carbon nanotube research has continued to advance rapidly. Highlights include the impressive use of single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWNT) as delivery agents for short interfering RNA (siRNA) which resulted in gene silencing in mammalian cells. Similarly, effective, specific delivery of boron to, and retention in, tumour cells using substituted carborane-appended SWNT has been achieved which highlights the potential for the future development of new carbon nanotube-based targeted anti-cancer therapies.

1 Introduction

This review complements previous reports and is a collection of the highlights revealed by database searches for the year 2005.1

2 Synthesis, separation and properties of fullerenes

A book reviewing the chemistry and reactions of fullerenes, which includes SWNT, has been published.2 Solid films, 2D islands and 3D pyramid-like structures, of covalently bound C58 dimers or larger oligomers have been deposited on highly oriented pyrolytic graphite (HOPG) using a mass selected low-energy ion beam of C58 cations.3 Hydrogenation of [60]fullerene at elevated temperature and pressure for up to 3000 min. results in the formation of hydrogenated carbon molecules of general formula C59Hx or C58Hx (where x is not less than 28) in which the fullerene cages have either one or two carbon atoms fewer than the parent fullerene.4 Pyrolysis of [76]-, [78]- and [84]-fullerenes at about 1000 C results in the loss of carbon atoms and the formation of stable [60]fullerene.5 Multicyclic cyclophanes containing cyclobutene rings when subjected to laser irradiation produce [78]fullerene anions which were identified by their characteristic mass spectrum fragmentation patterns.6 The aromaticity of fullerenes with fewer than sixty carbon atoms and single walled carbon nanotubes (SWNT) has been reviewed.7 Calculations show that the current due to the movement of the electrons on the inside and outside surface of the fullerene cage in a magnetic field in the theoretical polycation of [60]fullerene, C6010+, is unidirectional which leads to the presence of a strong induced electric current around the molecule.8 Aqueous suspensions of [60]fullerene have no acute or subacute toxicity and protect the liver against radical damage.9 [60]- and [70]-Fullerenes and multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWNT) have proved useful sorbents in the speciation of organometallic compounds in environmental samples.10

3 Endohedral fullerenes

A general rule for the stabilisation of fullerene cages containing trimetallic nitride templates (TNT) based on the electronic structure of the free fullerene cages has been developed.11 Gd3N@C80 has been isolated and contains the largest caged TNT cluster to date.12 Gd3N@C80 produces a bis-adduct upon a Diels-Alder cycloaddition with 6,7-dimethoxy-3-isochromanone.13 A new family of TNT containing fullerenes, Tm3N@C2n (30 n 43) have been prepared.14 Cyclic voltmametry, FTIR, Raman and X-ray photoemission spectroscopy show that thulium has a ground state of +3 in Tm3N@C80.15 The differences between the first electrochemical oxidation potentials of two Sc3N@C80 isomers have been measured and used as a method for their separation.16 Lanthanide TNT endohedral fullerenes have been separated from the as prepared soot by means of a reversible Diels-Alder reaction which is reported to be higher yielding and faster than purification using HPLC.17 Pyrrolidine derivatives of Y3N@C8018 and Sc3N@C8019 have been prepared. Sc3N@C80 has a lower thermal reactivity towards cycloaddition with disilaranes than the isoelectronic metallo-endohedral fullerene La2@C80.20 A TNT endohedral fullerene, Sc3N@C68 is proposed to contain adjacent pentagons on the basis of relative energy, geometrical data and 13C NMR spectroscopic data.21 A new model in which strong ligand fields within the TNT cluster act on the ferromagnetically exchange-coupled moments of the metal has been developed to explain the net magnetic moments of the endohedral TNT in Ho3N@C80 and Tb3N@C80.22

Dichlorophenyl radicals have been used to solubilise the previously insoluble endohedral fullerene La@C74 which has enabled the separation of this endohedral fullerene for the first time.23 HPLC has been used to separate Sr@C74, two isomers of Sr@C76 and Ca@C80.24 Single crystal X-ray diffraction studies of the adamantylidene carbene derivative of Sc3@C82 have shown that the material is in fact Sc3C2@C80; the new structure being further corroborated by the 13C NMR data.25 The foregoing discovery calls into question the efficacy of the maximum entropy method/Rietveld analysis of synchotron X-ray powder diffraction data when applied to determining the structures of endohedral metallofullerenes. Similarly density functional calculations predict that the material previously described as Ti2@C80 is in fact an endohedral titanium carbide Ti2C2@C78.26 2D INADEQUATE NMR spectroscopy has been shown to be a useful alternative method for the structural characterisation of endohedral metallofullerenes.27 Quantum chemical density functional theory has been employed to show that the position and stability of a molecule inside a [60]fullerene cage can be modified by derivatising the fullerene cage.28 Disilarane functionalisation of Ce2@C80 results in the two Ce atoms being fixed at specific locations within the fullerene cage.29 An attempted cyclopropanation of La@C82 using diethyl bromomalonate under standard fullerene reaction conditions does not follow the usual reaction sequence and instead results in the formation of the singly bonded derivative, La@C82CBr(CO2Et)2.30 Cycloaddition of La@C82 and cyclopentadiene is reported to be reversible and regioselective.31 Y@C82 has been functionalised with trifluoromethyl groups and the possible structures, based upon NMR data and density functional theory (DFT) calculations, of the two isolated isomeric pentakis-addition products Y@C82(CF3)5 predicted.32 The chemical reduction or oxidation of endohedral metallofullerenes, M@C82, (M = Y, La and Ce) has proved to be useful in controlling reactions with either nucleophiles or electrophiles.33 Addition of phosphate to water soluble aggregates of Gd@C60(OH)x and Gd@C60[C(CO2H)]10 is particularly effective in disrupting the order of the aggregates34 and has important implications for the development of this type of fullerene derivative as MRI contrast agents.35 Pyrrolidine derivatives of the TNT endohedral fullerenes, A3N@C80 where A = Sc or Er, have been prepared via azomethine ylide cycloaddition.36

A methodology has now been developed for closing the enlarged thirteen-membered orifice of an aza-thia fullerene derivative which has enabled the controlled synthesis of the endohedral fullerene, H2@C60 in an overall yield of 22%.37 The reaction sequence requires that the sulfur atom present in the cage opening as a sulphide unit is oxidised to the sulfoxide, which can then be eliminated from the cage by a photochemical reaction. The two carbonyl groups present in the resultant product were reduced using Ti(0) and then heating of the reduction product produced the parent endohedral fullerene, H2@C60. A similar fullerene derivative with a sixteen-membered cage opening has also been loaded with hydrogen molecules.38 Single crystal X-ray diffraction studies of an endohedral hydrogen-containing fullerene derivative shows that the hydrogen molecule is situated at the centre of the fullerene cage.39 Helium has also been inserted into the aza-thia open cage fullerene derivative.40

4 Chemistry of the fullerenes

Organic Chemistry

Solid state reactions of [60]fullerene with a range of aromatic diazoketones results in the formation of fulleroids in which the methine proton is located over a six-membered ring as the major product.41 Methanofullerenes containing alkyl and poly(oxyethylene) chains terminated with primary ammonium functional groups have been prepared using the Bingel reaction.42 The Curtius rearrangement of azidocarbonylmethano[60]fullerene in the presence of an alcohol results in the in situ formation of the carbamate which, when treated with trifluoromethanesulfonic acid, was converted to the insoluble salt of aminomethano[60]fullerene.43 [60]Fullerene has been used to trap a new S-heterocyclic carbene via formation of the methanofullerene.44 Usefully functionalized cyclopentenone derivatives of [60]fullerene have been prepared by hydrolysis of the [60]fullerene product obtained from cycloaddition in the presence of isonitriles and dimethyl acetylene dicarboxylate.45 Copper acetate has been used to promote radical reactions of [60]fullerene with -keto esters and -diketones.46 The addition of N-(diphenylmethylene) glycinate esters with [60]fullerene results in the formation of [60]fullerenyldihydropyrroles and not methanofullerenes as previously reported.47 The diastereoselective synthesis of fulleropyrrolidine derivatives with diastereomerically pure susbtituted cyclobutanes has been achieved.48 The high stereoselectivity of the reaction is attributed to the steric hindrance caused by the bulky organic functional group present in the 1,3-dipole.49 Picolylamines have been used to generate azomethine ylides which undergo the usual cycloaddition with [60]fullerene resulting in the formation of pyridyl-substituted fulleropyrrolidines.50 A study of the restricted rotation in phenyl substituted fulleropyrrolidine derivatives shows that only one of the two possible atropisomers is obtained.51 The Merrifield solid-phase synthesis strategy has been used to synthesise fulleropyrrolidine containing peptides.52

[2 + 2] Photocycloaddition of dienes or alkenes with [60]fullerene proceed via a biradical intermediate and not a dipolar species.53 Tertiary amines undergo photoreactions with [60]fullerene resulting in the formation of pyrrolofullerenes, 1-alkylated 1,2-dihydro-fullerenes or tetrahydroquinoline addended [60]fullerenes each in relatively low yield.54 By contrast, photochemical reactions of [60]fullerene and substituted piperazines proceed smoothly resulting in the formation of the substituted piperazine.55 Surprisingly, 1-aryl 1,2-dihydro[60]fullerenes are reported to result from the reaction between phenylhydrazines and [60]fullerene.56 Secondary phosphines, phosphine oxides, phosphinates and phosphonates react with [60]fullerene to produce 1,2-hydrophosphorylated [60]fullerene derivatives.57 Bridged [60]fullerene dimers, HC60-C(PO3Et2)2-C60H, have been prepared by the reaction of an excess of aminomethylenebis(phosphonate) anions with [60]fullerene.58 The regioselective oxygenative tetraamination of [60]fullerene with secondary amines in dimethyl sulfoxide proceeds via ground state single electron transfer.59 Attempted polyamine addition to [60]fullerene produces C3v C60H18.60 The same regioisomer of C60H18 has been produced by hydrogenation of [60]fullerene under 100 bar H2 pressure at 673 K for 10 hours.61 The high pressure treatment of [60]fullerene with hydrogen at high temperatures is reported to lead to the formation of covalently linked (C60Hx)2 dimers where x > 15.62

Cyclo-[n]-alkylbis- and cyclo-[n]-alkyltris-malonates, trismalonates linked by an aryl moiety, and bismalonates linked by tetraphenylporphyrin and calix-[4]-arenes have been used to produce regio- and stereoselective bis-and tris-cyclopropanations of [60]fullerene.6365 Similarly, bis-malonates attached to a Trger base have been used to prepare trans-1, trans-2, trans-3 and trans-4 bisadducts of [60]fullerene and the absolute configuration of the trans-2 and trans-3 bisadducts identified by CD spectra.66 The formation of an equatorial (e)-edge methanodihydropyrrolofullerene results from reaction of [60]fullerene with a tethered N-(diphenylmethylene)glycinatemalonate.67 The absolute configuration of a cis-3 bisadduct of [60]fullerene has been determined by single crystal X-ray diffraction and circular dichroism studies.68 The complete series of bis-fulleropyrrolidine derivatives and nine tris-fulleropyrrolidines have been prepared, isolated and unambiguously characterized.69 In addition, UV-Vis spectroscopy is suggested as a useful and reliable tool for identifying the addition pattern present in tris-[60]fullerene adducts.69

An overview of fluorofullerenes a host materials for intercalation chemistry has been published.70 Fluorinated derivatives, C58F18 and C58F17(CF3), of the new [58]fullerene, which has a seven membered ring in the fullerene cage structure, have been isolated.71 A detailed theoretical study of the addition patterns observed in fluorination of [60]fullerene has been published.72 New fluorinated [70]fullerene derivatives characterized include C173 and C274 regioisomers of C70F38, whilst C2 isomers of C84F4075 and C84F4475 have also been isolated and characterized. The common feature of most of these fluorinated fullerene structures is the development of aromatic regions, such as benzenoid rings, on the cage surface. The regioisomers 1,7- and 1,9-C60F(CF3) have been isolated and the rotation of the CF3 group in the latter found to be hindered.76 A C1 isomer of C70(CF3)10 with a previously unknown ten fold addition pattern has been isolated and characterized by single crystal X-ray analysis.77,78 The cyclic voltammograms of several regioisomers of trifluoromethylated [60]fullerene have been isolated and one radical 3 anion of C60(CF3)10 was found to have a half-life of about 7 minutes in solution at 25 C.79trans-2-[3-{4-tert-butylphenyl}-2-methyl-2-propenylidene]malononitrile (DCTB) was found to be the best matrix for MALDI generation of fluorofullerene anions resulting in, for example, enhanced parent molecular ion yield and reduced fragmentation.80 By contrast, a separate mass spectroscopic study of methanofullerenes found that trans-4-tert-butyl-4-nitrostilbene (TBNS) was the best matrix for the positive-ion mode.81 The synthesis of trannulenes, which have a 18 electron trannulene belt, from C60F18 has been reviewed82 and their photophysical properties examined.83 C60[C(CCH3)(CO2-t-Bu)2]6, which has an unusual double-triphenylene interlinked trannulene chromophore embedded within the fullerene cage structure, has been prepared from the 6-anion of [60]fullerene and characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction studies.84

The synthesis and structures of brominated and chlorinated fullerenes have been reviewed.85 A modified synthesis of C60Cl6 from [60]fullerene and iodine monochloride (ICl) has been published.86 New chlorinated fullerenes prepared and characterised during the year include Th C60Cl24,87C1 C60Cl28,88C2-C60Cl30,88D3d-C60Cl30,89C1 C70Cl1690 and C1, C2 and C2 regioisomers of C70Cl28.91 [5,6]- and [6,6]-Oxahomofullerenes have been prepared from [60]fullerene mixed peroxide adducts, C60O(OOtBu)4, via ring opening of the epoxide moiety by Fe(III) chloride or cleavage of the OO bond of one peroxide group by Al(III) chloride.92 Four isomers of C70(OOtBu)2, two isomers of C70(OOtBu)4, two isomers of C70(OOtBu)6, and one isomer of each of C70(OOtBu)8 and C70(OOtBu)10 have been isolated from the reaction mixture resulting from the addition, which was found to proceed by both equatorial and cyclopentadienyl modes, of tert-butylperoxy radicals to [60]fullerene.93

Advanced materials

Hydrogen-bonded fullerene ensembles94 and self-organized fullerene-containing structures95 have been reviewed. Supramolecular structures with up to ten [60]fullerene units comprising of hydrogen-bonded [60]fullerodendrimers have been prepared.96 Hydrogen bonding has also been used to assemble a novel guanine containing[60]fullerene derivative and porphyrin dyad with a long, 2.02 s, lived charge separated state.97 Similarly flavin-functionalised [60]fullerene forms electrochemically-tuneable H-bonds with 2,6-diethylamidopyridine.98 Columnar discotic liquid crystals containing methanofullerene moieties99 and a liquid crystalline [60]fullerene dyad functionalized with tetrathiafulvalene (TTF) and a bis-mesogenic fragment100 have been prepared. 1D-Nanotubules are formed by the self-organisation of a fulleropyrrolidine salt functionalized with a phthalocyanine which have ultrafast charge separation, 1012 s1 and ultraslow charge recombination, 103 s1.103 A convenient method employing (4-formylcatecholato)-2,(3)-(tetra-tert-butylphthalocyanoto)titanium(IV) as the aldehyde in azomethine ylide synthesis has lead to a facile synthetic route to axially substituted phthalocyanine-[60]fulleropyrrolidine dyads.104 Axial coordination and ammonium cation-crown ether complexation has been used to assemble a porphyrin-fullerene conjugate.105 The lifetimes of the radical ion-pair in the foregoing supramolecular complex were found to be longer when pyridine was added to the supramolecular dyad.106 Addition of pyridine or diazabicyclooctane lengthens the lifetimes of charge-separated states in Zn-porphyrin [60]fullerene dyads.107 Pyridyl-substituted fulleropyrazolines and fulleropyrrolidines have also been prepared, the former by cycloaddition of [60]fullerene with the hydrazone, and ruthenium containing complexes prepared.108 The complementarity between a Zn(II) porphyrin receptor and a pyridine containing fullerene ligand leads to the self-assembly of a stable non-covalent complex.109 A similar strategy has been employed to prepare supramolecular complexes comprising of bisammonium or mono-ammonium [60]fullerene derivatives and ditopic crown ethers.110,111 Fused subphthalocyanine dimers, in which three diiminoisoindole units are N-bonded to a boron atom, incorporating aldehyde units have been used to prepare bis-susbtituted [60]fullerenepyrrolidine dyads.112 Similar strategies have been used to incorporate thioxanthene,113 resulting in molecules which are two -electron donors, and cyanine114 into [60]fullerenepyrrolidine dyads. Several fullerene-based dendrimers have been prepared which are liquid crystals having smetic A phases.115

Biologically active fullerenes

The disulfate salt of a fullerene diol derivative has been employed as one of the counteranions in a study looking at the modulation of the cellular uptake and anion carrier activity of polyarginines.152,153 The stable human serum albumin-C3 tris malonic acid [60]fullerene complex has been prepared and characterised.154 A [60]fullerene-based slow release system of paclitaxel for liposome aerosol delivery to the lungs has useful efficacy in vivo.155 Hydroxylated [60]fullerene produces insoluble metal-fullerene cross linked polymers which reduces the mobility of the fullerene molecules in an aqueous environment.156 The interaction between biotin and streptavidin has been used to prepare biotinylated fullerenes in which the enzyme is immobilised but still retains it reactivity after recovery.157 Stable self-assembled structures, similar to bilayer vesicles and liposomes, of bis(mannopyransyl) [60]fullerene adducts envelope metal ions and may be used in the development of new types of magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) reagents.158 Adamantyl containing methano[60]fullerenes act as noncompetitive antagonists for the N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor.159 Aggregation and partition measurements and retention factors in reverse phase chromatography are useful descriptors for the reversible transition from monomeric to aggregated forms of amphiphilic fullerene derivatives.160 A fullerene amphiphile has been synthesised in which the supramolecular structure can be modified by changing the pH.161 A fullerene exchange method using the fullerene cyclodextrin complex has been used to prepare cationic lipid membrane-incorporated [60]fullerene complexes which have efficient DNA photocleaving properties.162 Arrays of [60]fullerene derivatives have been assembled on DNA scaffolds.163 Fullerene mediated assembly of metal nanoparticles into 3D architectures has been achieved which offers the possibility of controlled drug delivery applications.164
