Abstract

BaCO3 is primarily demonstrated as an excellent synergistic catalyst for efficient electro-reduction of H2O/CO2 to H2/CO at the state-of-the-art Niâ€“YSZ (yttria-stabilized zirconia) cathodes for solid oxide electrolysis cells (SOECs) with YSZ electrolytes and strontium doped lanthanum manganite oxygen electrodes. BaCO3 nanoparticles, which are deposited onto porous Niâ€“YSZ using the infiltration process, can significantly improve the SOEC performance such as increasing the electrode interfacial polarization conductivity over 50% and promoting the electrolysis current density by a factor of up to 2 for the electrolysis of H2O to H2, CO2 to CO, and co-electrolysis of H2Oâ€“CO2 to H2â€“CO at 800 Â°C. In addition, it seems that BaCO3 can improve the SOEC durability for the co-electrolysis of H2Oâ€“CO2. The improvement is associated with an enhanced surface charge transfer process, which is the rate-determining step for the reduction reaction, as shown by analyzing the AC impedance spectrum measured with a three-electrode configuration. The highest improvement is achieved with CO2 reduction possibly due to the additional enhancement in the adsorption/dissociation process by BaCO3 as revealed with temperature programmed CO2 desorption analysis.

Introduction

The reduction reaction of H2O/CO2 to H2/CO is the fundamental process to mitigate the problems associated with heavy dependence on finite fossil fuels. The reduction is a promising solution to transform greenhouse gas into syngas, which is an important feedstock for hydrocarbon synthesis through the Fischerâ€“Tropsch (Fâ€“T) process.1,2 The reaction makes it possible to achieve a carbon neutral energy cycle using renewable energy sources such as solar, wind and geothermal heat. H2O/CO2 reduction is often conducted in electrolysers such as solid oxide electrolysis cells (SOECs) because of their controllable reaction temperature, minimal energy consumption and nonconsumption of nonrenewable fossil fuels.3,4 High-temperature SOECs are more advantageous than low-temperature proton exchange membrane electrolysers and alkaline electrolysers because SOECs can produce H2/CO at a higher electrochemical reaction rate with a lower electrical energy requirement and have thus received much attention in recent years.5,6

The reduction reaction takes place at a SOEC cathode, which supports gas diffusion and provides active sites for the reaction. Porous Niâ€“YSZ (yttria-stabilized zirconia) is the state-of-the-art cathode material for SOECs, in which Ni is the electrochemical catalyst to induce H2O and CO2 reduction and is responsible for electron transport while YSZ is the electrolyte which offers oxygen ion transport paths to extend the reaction sites, the three-phase boundaries (3PBs) where the pore, the electrocatalyst and the electrolyte meet.7,8 The length of the 3PBs, where the reduction reaction takes place, should be maximum to decrease the activation polarization resistance because of the inverse relationship between the 3PB length and the cathode interfacial polarization resistance.9 The 3PB length can be effectively increased by decreasing the sizes of Ni and YSZ particles, increasing the porosity for a given particle size, and modifying the Ni/YSZ ratio.10,11 To achieve a significant drop in the activation polarization and improvement in the electrical efficiency, in addition to maximizing the 3PB length, it is necessary to ensure that 3PBs are connected three dimensionally and only these 3PBs will be electrochemically active. Both experimental and modeling studies suggest that there is an optimum compositional Niâ€“YSZ electrode value to balance the 3PB density and porosity.12,13 So, the electrochemical reduction reaction properties of Ni/YSZ electrodes are strongly influenced by the composition and microstructure of the composites. Consequently, the electrode performance is greatly affected by the SOEC fabrication process, such as, dry pressing, tape casting and gel casting.12,14,15 In addition, microstructure optimization can promote the electrode performance by introducing an active fuel electrode layer to restrain Ni mobility.16

This work reports the promotion of the H2O/CO2 reduction reaction by depositing BaCO3 nanoparticles onto Niâ€“YSZ cathodes for SOECs. It is primarily found that BaCO3 has synergistic catalytic effects on H2O/CO2 reduction when it is infiltrated into the porous Niâ€“YSZ cathode although it is not an electronic or ionic conductor, nor an electrocatalyst. It is noted that BaCO3 has been demonstrated to be very effective in improving the oxygen reduction reaction on electrocatalysts such as La0.8Sr0.2MnO3âˆ’Î´, La0.8Sr0.2FeO3âˆ’Î´ and La0.6Sr0.4Co0.2Fe0.8O3âˆ’Î´ for solid oxide fuel cells, the reverse reaction of SOECs.17,18

In order to understand the BaCO3 effect on the electrolysis process, the impedance spectrum is transferred into the corresponding distribution of relaxation time (DRT) spectra, Fig. 7.37,38 Each plot contains three peaks, P1, P2 and P3, each one representing different electrode reaction processes.39â€“41 The low-frequency P1 registered near 10 Hz is related to the reactions on the gas/electrode surface such as adsorption and dissociation. The middle-frequency P2 near 103 Hz is associated with the surface charge transfer reaction. And the high-frequency P3 near 104 Hz represents the oxygen ion transport reaction. The area for each peak is the resistance associated with the corresponding reaction. Obviously, the process for P2 dominates the whole reaction since the area of P2 is much higher than the sum of P1 and P3, and contributes at least 69% of the total electrode resistance. The area of P3 does not change much with BaCO3 addition, suggesting that BaCO3 has almost no effects on the oxygen ion transportation process for the electrochemical reduction. The area for P2 is significantly reduced by infiltrating BaCO3 particles, from 3.71 to 2.63 Î© cm2 for H2O electrolysis, from 5.41 to 3.52 Î© cm2 for CO2 electrolysis and from 5.39 to 2.86 Î© cm2 for H2Oâ€“CO2 co-electrolysis. Thus, BaCO3 particles can obviously accelerate the surface charge transfer reaction process, which is also the rate-limiting step for the electrolysis reaction, thus significantly reducing the total electrode polarization resistance. The area for P1 for H2O electrolysis is almost not changed by BaCO3, inferring that steam adsorption/dissociation is not affected by BaCO3. Conclusion

BaCO3 was demonstrated to be an excellent synergistic catalyst for the H2O/CO2 reduction reaction at the state-of-the-art Niâ€“YSZ fuel electrode for SOECs. Improved H2O/CO2 electro-reduction activity was obtained for single cells with YSZ electrolytes and LSMâ€“YSZ oxygen electrodes when 8.9 wt% BaCO3 was deposited onto Niâ€“YSZ fuel electrodes although BaCO3 had a negative effect on the H2 electro-oxidation reaction and the RWGS reaction. The current density at 1.3 V and 800 Â°C was increased by 53%, 103% and 72%, meanwhile, the total electrode interfacial polarization conductivity 1/Rp under open-circuit conditions was increased by 58%, 94% and 69% for the electrolysis of H2O, CO2 and H2Oâ€“CO2, respectively. Compared with the report by Kim-Lohsoontorn, it is very likely that BaCO3 can improve the durability of the Niâ€“YSZ cathode. DRT analysis of the electrochemical impedance spectra measured with a three-electrode configuration revealed that BaCO3 particles could significantly reduce the interfacial polarization resistance for the surface charge transfer reaction, which was the rate limiting step for the H2O/CO2 reduction. The CO2-TPD results revealed that BaCO3 enhanced the CO2 adsorption/dissociation process, which also enhanced the CO2 reduction reactions.
