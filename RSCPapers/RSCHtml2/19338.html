Internally functionalized multifaceted organochalcogen compounds

Abstract

Two families of internally functionalized multifaceted organochalcogen compounds based on flexible and rigid backbones, viz., N,N-dimethylaminoalkyl and pyridyl/pryrimidyl groups, have been designed and developed in the author's group. These compounds exhibit remarkable ligand chemistry and often show unprecedented reactivity leading to the formation of several serendipitous products. The presence of both soft (chalcogen) and hard (nitrogen) donor atoms makes them hemilabile ligands which facilitates the design of palladium based homogeneous catalysts for CC coupling and other related reactions. Pyridyl and pyrimidyl based chalcogenolato complexes of Group 1015 metals have been successfully employed as molecular precursors for the preparation of metal chalcogenide nano-crystals and for deposition of thin films. By making a suitable choice of chalcogenolate ligands and reaction conditions, a single phasic material can be isolated conveniently. 2-Pyridyl and 2-pyrimidyl ring systems, being important constituents of several biological molecules, several selenium based 2-pyridyl and 2-pyrimidyl compounds show promising GPx mimicking activity. The substituents at the C-3 position play a key role in governing the molecular structure as well as GPx activity.

Introduction

The chemistry of organochalcogen compounds is making great strides in diverse areas ranging from organic synthesis, ligands in coordination chemistry, materials science and biology, etc.14 Such versatility can be realized by a proper design of these compounds. More than a quarter century back when our group was exploring the chemistry of thiolato complexes, two serious problems were encountered, viz., (i) solubility of the products hampering their characterization and (ii) a direct tool to monitor the reactions. Fortunately, both selenium and tellurium possess NMR active isotopes (both I = 1/2; natural abundance: 77Se = 7.63%; 125Te = 7.07%). Their reactions and the compounds can readily be monitored/characterized by 77Se and 125Te NMR spectroscopy. For solubility, a hard donor in addition to the softer chalcogen atom was also desirable so as to introduce hemilability in the system. Accordingly, two families of internally functionalized multifaceted organochalcogen compounds were conceptualized. Initially, compounds based on flexible backbones, viz., N,N-dimethylaminoalkyl, followed by a small-bite rigid framework based on N-heteroaryl groups (pyridyl/pryrimidyl), were developed (Scheme 1).57

Diorgano dichalcogenides

N,N-Dimethylaminoalkyl dichalcogenides are conveniently prepared by the reactions of sodium dichalcogenides with an appropriate N,N-dimethylaminoalkyl chloride in organic solvents (Scheme 2).812 The reaction of Me2NCH2CH(Me)Cl, however, gives a mixture of [Me2NCH(Me)CH2E]2 (E-1) and [Me2NCH2CH(Me)E]2 (E-2), the relative ratio of which depends on the nucleophilic character of chalcogen atoms.11 The formation of E-2 decreases upon going from S to Se through Te (the ratio of E-1 and E-2 is 1:2 (S), 1:1 (Se) and 5:1 (Te)). A cyclic aziridium (CH-1) species as an intermediate has been found in these reactions. The chalcogenides, [Me2N(CH2)nE]2, display only one set of resonances, but bis(2-dimethylamino)propyl dichalcogenides exhibit two sets of signals in the 1H and 13C NMR spectra. The 77Se NMR spectrum (Fig. 1) displays 8 lines which can be assigned to RR/SS and meso forms of E-1 and E-2. The intra-molecular interactions between Se and N may lead to a magnetic non-equivalence of Se atoms in each molecule.11

The heavier analogue of 2-mercaptopyridine, viz selenide/telluride, remained elusive for a long time possibly due to the absence of convenient synthetic methods. Mautner et al.13 described the synthesis of 2,2-dipyridyldiselenide by reacting 2-bromopyridine with H2Se which was generated by the addition of dilute HCl to aluminium selenide. Subsequently efforts were made to develop safe and convenient methods1416 with improved yields.17,18 Similarly, preparation of the corresponding 2,2-dipyridylditelluride encountered several difficulties specially with regard to not only low yields but also non-reproducibility of the synthesis.19 Subsequently other methods,2022 in particular a reaction involving sodium borohydride reduction of tellurium powder in alkaline 2-ethoxyethanol followed by treatment with 2-bromopyridine and areal oxidation,23 have been developed for the synthesis of 2,2-dipyridyl ditelluride. Later, Bhasin et al., described a convenient high yield synthesis of 2,2-dipyridyl ditellurides.24 This method involves the treatment of pyridylmagnesium chloride, generated in situ by bromine-magnesium exchange reaction between 2-bromopyridine and PriMgCl in THF, with elemental tellurium followed by areal oxidation.

Several dipyridyl diselenides/ditellurides have been structurally characterized by single crystal X-ray diffraction analyses.6 The SeSe and TeTe distances in these compounds vary in the range of 2.282.35 and 2.682.72 , respectively, while the CE distances lie at 1.92 (CSe) and 2.12 (CTe). The two pyridyl groups can adopt the synsyn, synanti, and antianti conformation with respect to the EE bond in the solid state. There could be weak EN secondary interactions which can influence the CEEC torsion angle and EE bond distance.

Ligands and reactivity

The reactions of Na2PdCl4 with diorgano dichalcogenides in methanol yield sparingly soluble/insoluble oligomeric complexes of empirical composition [PdCl(EN)]n (1), the structure of which depends on the nature of the EN ligand (Scheme 3). A trimeric structure is formed with alkyl ligands where E and N are separated by two carbon atoms (e.g.8 [PdCl(SeCH2CH2NMe2)]3), whereas a dimeric structure is adopted (e.g., [PdCl(SeCH2CH2CH2NMe2)]2) when E and N are three carbons apart.10,12 In the case of N-heteroaryl ligands, 1 is isolated as an insoluble powder.25,26 The formation of 1 in these reactions is intriguing in terms of the oxidation of dichalcogenide (R2E2) to chalcogenolate ion (RE), gaining an electron per chalcogen atom, without any isolable reduction product. Having examined the reactivity of numerous dichalcogenides differing in denticity and steric demands, it was proposed that initially dichalcogenide oxidatively adds to Pd2+ to give an unstable Pd4+ species [PdCl2(ER)2] which undergoes reductive elimination of RECl with the concomitant formation of [PdCl(ER)]n.27 Interestingly, the reaction between Na2PdCl4 with (4-pyE)2 in methanol yields a brown insoluble product, [PdCl2{4-py2E2}] (E = Se or Te).28,29 The telluro derivative when treated with two equivalents of PPh3 affords a tellurolato bridged complex, [Pd2Cl2(-TeC5H4N-4)2(PPh3)2], which is formed as a mixture of cis and trans isomers.29

The chemistry of palladium and platinum chalcogenolato complexes with phosphines as auxiliary ligands has been investigated. Thiolate and selenolate ligands, in general, afforded desired complexes, but the tellurolate, due to a weaker TeC bond, often resulted in several unprecedented derivatives. The neutral donor atoms (P and N) invariably occupy trans positions. Metallophilic interactions in coinage metal complexes

Attractive interactions between metal centres with a closed shell electronic configuration (d10) in silver(I) and gold(I) complexes have been known for a long time and are referred to as argentophilic44 and aurophilic45 interactions, respectively. The term metallophilicity was first introduced by Pyykko et al. in 1994 to describe such attractive interactions collectively.46 Relativistic effects, which reach maximum at gold, have been attributed to this phenomenon. The metalmetal distance from crystal structural analysis has been considered as a diagnostic tool for metallophilic interactions.

Metallophilic interactions in coinage metal complexes derived from N-heteroaryl chalcogenolate ligands have been investigated. Homoleptic copper(I) complexes have been isolated as tetra- (e.g. [Cu{EC5H3(3-R)N}]4 (E = Se or Te, R = H or Me)/hexa- nuclear (e.g. [Cu{SeC4H(4,6-Me)2N2}]6) species with CuCu contacts of 2.662.81 .47,48 In each case copper adopts a distorted square pyramidal configuration defined by pyridyl/pyrimidyl nitrogen and two bridging chalcogen atoms and two adjacent copper atoms. The tetranuclear complexes have a distorted tetrahedral core of copper atoms while the hexanuclear derivative comprises an octahedral core of copper atoms. The selenolato complexes with short Cu-Cu interactions are tetranuclear (e.g. [Cu{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}]4 with CuCu = 2.66 ) while a longer separation of CuCu contacts results in a hexanuclear species (e.g. [Cu{SeC4H(4,6-Me)2N2}]6 with CuCu = 2.742.82 ).47

The evolution of argentophilic interactions has been mapped in the reactions of AgCl(PPh3) with thio/seleno pyridine ligands.48,49 Sequential release of the PPh3 ligand from the mononuclear complexes [Ag(Epy)(PPh3)3] leads to the emergence of AgAg interactions (Scheme 12). With the extrusion of the PPh3 ligand, binuclear [Ag(-Epy)(PPh3)2]2 (no argentophilic interactions, AgAg = 4.07 ) and [Ag(-Epy)(PPh3)]2 (weak argentophilic interactions, AgAg = 3.42 ) complexes, a trinuclear complex [Ag3(-Sepy)3(PPh3)4] (silver atoms with single PPh3 exhibits AgAg = 3.27 ) and finally homoleptic hexanuclear complexes, [Ag(Epy)]6 (AgAg = 2.81 ), have been isolated as crystalline products. The trinuclear complex [Ag3(-Sepy)3(PPh3)4] represents an interesting example containing a six-membered Ag3Se3 ring in which argentophilic interactions are dictated by the number of coordinated PPh3 ligands to the metal centre. Silver atoms in these complexes adopt either a distorted tetrahedral or distorted trigonal configuration depending on the number of the ligating atoms.

Catalysis

Among various transition metal complexes, palladium complexes have played a pivotal role in metal catalysed organic transformations. Catalyst design has remained of paramount importance not only for understanding various catalytic processes but also for improving the activity of the catalytic system. A myriad of organic ligands, in particular phosphines, have been employed to develop numerous catalysts. Sulfur ligands, despite their rich transition metal chemistry, remained dormant as these ligands were believed to be catalyst poison. Thus, the palladium complexes derived from internally functionalized chalcogenolate ligands were considered as these were thought to serve as hemilabile ligands during the catalytic processes. Accordingly, different catalytic reactions using such complexes have been examined.

Among different catalytic reactions, the carboncarbon cross-coupling of aryl halides with arylboronic acids, commonly known as SuzukiMiura coupling reactions, has been investigated. Several bi- and tri-nuclear palladium complexes containing N,N-dimethylaminoalkyl chalcogenolate groups show catalytic activity in the CC cross coupling reaction between aryl halides and phenyl boronic acid. Under optimized conditions, electron deficient aryl bromides give higher yield of the coupled product. Binuclear complexes show better catalytic activity than the trinuclear derivatives. Within a series, acetate complexes are more active than the corresponding halides (Table 2).51 Similarly, palladium complexes with internally functionalized chalcogenoethers, [PdCl2{RE(CH2)nNMe2}], have also been used in Suzuki coupling reactions.52 Better catalytic activity is noted upon increasing the chain length from a strongly chelating Me2NCH2CH2ER to a flexible Me2NCH2CH2CH2ER system.52

The former complexes are seldom isolated. The acetate group in palladium(II) complexes (e.g. [{Pd(OAc)2}]3 and Pd(OAc)2L2] etc.) facilitates the formation of such species. Molecular precursors for metal chalcogenides

Metal chalcogenide semiconductors represent an important family of inorganic materials and have made an everlasting impact on the progress and growth of opto-electronic and photovoltaic technologies. Metal chalcogenides are delicate materials needing a softer approach for their synthesis. A single source molecular approach has emerged as a versatile strategy for the synthesis of these materials and even for the isolation of metastable phases. Internally functionalized organochalcogenolate metal complexes have been successfully employed as molecular precursors for the synthesis of both binary (nano-crystals and thin films) and ternary chalcogenide materials.57

Coinage metal chalcogenides (IVI materials)

The coinage metal chalcogenides are an interesting family of inorganic materials exhibiting a wide compositional and stoichiometric versatility. Temperature dependent phase transitions as well as composition variations are frequently encountered. They find numerous applications in photovoltaic, thermoelectric devices, photo-rechargeable batteries, etc.58 Homoleptic copper(I) chalcogenolates, [Cu{EC5H3(3-R)N}]4 and [Cu{SeC4H(4,6-Me)2N2}]6 (E = Se or Te; R = H or Me), have been used for the preparation of copper chalcogenide nano-crystals and for deposition of thin films.

The thermolysis of [Cu{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}]447 and [Cu{SeC4H(4,6-Me2)N2}]648 in coordinating solvents like HDA/TOPO (HAD = hexadecylamine; TOPO = tri-n-octylphosphine oxide) and dodecanethiol (DDT), respectively yields a cubic phase of Cu7Se4 (Fig. 6). These precursors have been successfully used for the deposition of thin films of copper selenide by AACVD (aerosol assisted chemical vapour deposition). The former complex ([Cu{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}]4) produces films of orthorhombic Cu5Se4 in the temperature range of 360400 C (Fig. 7),47 while the latter ([Cu{SeC4H(4,6-Me)2N2}]6) gives films of cubic Cu7Se4 at 400 C.48 The films show continuous grains that are overlapping on the substrate. Zinc, cadmium and mercury chalcogenides (IIVI materials)

A variety of complexes of zinc triad have been designed which undergo clean decomposition. They have been used as single source molecular precursors for metal sulfide,59 selenide6065 and telluride66,67 nano-materials. A number of precursors, based on either internally functionalized selenolate ligands or selenocarboxylates, have been developed for metal selenide nanoparticles.6065 The facile cleavage of the CSe linkage in selenocarboxylates offers an attractive potential for MSe synthesis. The zinc and cadmium complexes [M(SeCOAr)2(tmeda)] (tmeda = N,N,N,N-tetramethyl ethylenediamine) undergo a three-step decomposition (by TGA) leading to the formation of polycrystalline metal selenide nano-crystals. The corresponding mercury complex could not be isolated but during the synthesis gave a black powder identified as cubic HgSe. The pyrolysis of [Cd(SeCOtol)2(tmeda)] in HDA at 230 C yields red coloured cubic CdSe nanoparticles of average diameter of 15 nm.61

The monomeric complexes, [M(Se(CH2)nNMe2)2] (M = Zn, Cd; n = 2 or 3), undergo a one- or two- (overlapping) step decomposition to give exclusively MSe.60,62,63 Both hexagonal and cubic CdSe could be isolated by the pyrolysis of [Cd(SeCH2CH2NMe2)2] in a vacuum and flowing inert gas, respectively.63 Thermolysis in the HDA-TOPO mixture affords HDA capped cubic CdSe nanoparticles, the emission of which is size dependent (Fig. 9).63 Attempts to prepare an analogous mercury complex, [Hg(SeCH2CH2NMe2)2], however, result in the formation of a black powder identified as cubic HgSe.63 In contrast, the complexes [Hg(TeCH2CH2NMe2)2]66 and [Hg(SeCH2CH2CH2NMe2)2]62 could be isolated in pure form which upon pyrolysis under flowing argon in a furnace gives polycrystalline cubic HgE (E = Se or Te).

Indium selenide

Tris selenolate of indium, [In(SeC5H3(3-Me)N}3], prepared by the reaction of InCl3 with NaSeC5H3(3-Me)N in a benzenemethanol mixture, upon thermolysis in HDA, TOPO or OA at 330 C, yields agglomerated spheres (in HDA), a spherical morphology (in TOPO) and sheet like structures (in OA) of In2Se3. The average diameter and length of the rods obtained from HDA are 5776 nm and 400 nm, respectively.68 The complex, [Me2In(SeC5H4N-4)]n, a one-dimensional polymer, in the solid state undergoes a two-step decomposition under an argon atmosphere to yield In2Se3, whereas in oleylamine at 250 C it gives nano-crystals (diameter 50 nm) of hexagonal of In2Se3.69

Tin and lead chalcogenides

Tin forms two narrow direct-band semiconductor selenides, viz. orthorhombic SnSe (band gap 1.0 eV) and hexagonal SnSe2 (band gap 0.9 eV). The photoelectric properties of the former have been used for solar cell applications while the layer structure of the latter has been utilized in lithium ion batteries. Diorganotin(IV) 2-pyridyl and 2-primidyl chalcogenolates, [R2Sn(Epy/Epym)2] and [R2Sn(Epy/Epym)(Cl)], have been developed as single source molecular precursors for tin selenides.7072 The former has a distorted skew trapezoidal six coordinate tin (Fig. 11a) while the latter acquires a trigonal bipyramidal configuration. Depending on the nature of the selenolate ligand, both SnSe and SnSe2 nanostructures have been prepared. The complexes, [But2Sn(Sepy-2)2]70 and [But2Sn{SeC4H(4,6-Me)2N2}2]72, have also been employed for the deposition of phase pure orthorhombic SnSe (at 490 C) and hexagonal SnSe2 (at 375 C) films, respectively on a p-type silicon(100) substrate. The SnSe films show continuous grain boundaries that overlap on the substrate. These films showed photo response on a Si/SnSe/Au configuration with a 20% increase in the current.70

Ternary metal chalcogenides

Besides binary chalcogenide materials, ternary chalcogenides, such as IIIIVI and IIVVI, represent yet another interesting family of inorganic materials that show remarkable photovoltaic and thermoelectric properties. Copper based chalcogenides with diamond like structures are promising thermoelectric materials due to their low thermal conductivity and tunable transport properties. Their synthesis is, quite often, a challenging task due to the existence of not only different compositions but also diverse phases (e.g. Cu2SnSe3 can be isolated in monoclinic, cubic, tetragonal or orthorhombic phases).57

Molecular precursors have been successfully employed to isolate monophasic materials. The thermolysis of a 1:1 mixture of [Cu{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}]4 and [In{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}3] in HDA, TOPO or oleylamine at 300 C yields nearly spherical particles (400 nm diameter) of tetragonal CuInSe2 (Fig. 14).68 Similarly, the thermolysis of a 2:1 molar ratio of [Cu{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}]4 and [Me2Sn{SeC5H3(3-Me)N}2] in oleylamine at 300 C yields nanostructures (11 1 nm) of phase pure cubic Cu2SnSe3 which show photo response in a photo-electrochemical cell with a power conversion efficiency of 1.7% (Fig. 15).76

Organoselenium compounds in biology

Selenium is an essential micronutrient for animals and humans. It exists in the form of selenocysteine at the active site of several selenoenzymes (e.g., glutathione peroxidase, GPx) which are believed to be responsible for most of the biological effects of selenium.77 Its deficiency is linked to various diseases. Inspired by the importance of natural selenoenzymes, considerable efforts have been made by several groups to develop synthetic organoselenium compounds which can imitate functions exhibited by such enzymes. Different families of organoselenium compounds, such as ebselen,78 DSePA,79etc., have been designed and synthesized which show GPx like activities.7881 Attempts have also been made to synthesize organoselenium compounds by the incorporation of selenium in biologically relevant molecules such as nicotinic acid (vitamin B3), pyridoxine (vitamin B6), nicotinamide, etc., with the hope that the resulting compound would exhibit better biological activities. Accordingly, selenium substituted pyridine and pyrimidine ring systems have been designed and developed by us8288 and others.8991 In contrast to the vast exploration on organoselenium compounds in biological systems, particularly as an antioxidant,1 organotellurium compounds are scarcely investigated.9294

In vitro antioxidant activity of several pyridine and pyrimidine based organoselenium compounds has been evaluated by different methods.82,83,89 Their GPx like activity falls in the following order: py2Se2 > pym2Se2 > Ar2Se2 (Ar = C6H4X; X = H, 4-F, 4-Cl, 4-MeO, or 3-CF3).83,89 2,2-Dipyridyl diselenide shows much better antioxidant activity than other diselenides. This is attributed to the ring nitrogen atom which facilitates radical formation and is supported by the fact that the compound exhibits radical (ABTS) (ABTS = 2,2-azinobis(3-ethylbenzthiazoline-6-sulfonic acid)-scavenging activity at low concentrations (5 M). Interestingly ABTS radical-scavenging activity of py2Se2 is superior to ascorbic acid (10 M) whereas other diselenides hardly show any such activity.89 The antioxidant activity of these compounds depends on various functional groups attached to selenium and also on the overall electron density at selenium which is manifested from 77Se NMR chemical shifts. From the 77Se NMR chemical shifts, the electron density at the selenium atom increases in the following order: py2Se2 > Ph2Se2 > pym2Se2 (Fig. 16).83 The GPx activity is in accord with this trend. However, the reverse trend is noted for Ph2Se2 and pym2Se2, although both of them show poor activity. This is attributed to the ability of pym2Se2 to form selenenic acid stabilized by SeN interactions while such interactions will be absent for the phenyl derivative.83 Monoselenides, both symmetrical and asymmetrical, show much lower or hardly any antioxidant activity.83

Polymorphism is one of the important factors that affect the efficacy of pharmaceutical drugs. Polymorphism of active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) remains a challenge in drug development. More than 50% APIs are known to exist in multiple polymorphs and are responsible for differences in many properties.95,96 Polymorphism in some dipyridyl diselenides has been noted and therefore was considered for structureproperty correlations. Two polymorphs, orthorhombic97 and monoclinic,18 of 2,2-dipryridyl diselenide have been isolated and structurally characterized. 2,2-Diselenobis(3-pyridinol) ({N(3-HO)C5H3Se}2) upon recrystallization from methanolwater and methanolchloroform crystallizes in the monoclinic space group with different cell parameters and crystal morphology.87 The molecules in both forms are associated through strong and distinctly directed hydrogen bonds. In the case of the former, the molecules are interlocked through intermolecular OHN hydrogen bonds forming 3-D sheets, whereas in the latter, helical chains of two different orientations are formed by both intra- and inter-molecular OHN hydrogen bonds (Fig. 17).87 The two polymorphs differ with a very small energy barrier. 2,2-Diseleno bis(3-nicotinamide) upon recrystallization from DMSO and DMF gives monoclinic88 and orthorhombic88/triclinic98 forms. Conclusions

In this perspective, it has been demonstrated that versatility and utility of organochalcogen compounds in diverse areas can be realized by a proper design of these compounds. Internal functionalization introduced through the hard donor centre in these compounds serves as a tool to design metal complexes with diverse structural motifs. Unprecedented reactivity of heteroaryl organochalcogen compounds arises due to a weaker CE bond and results in the formation of several serendipitous products. Such organochalcogen compounds may lead to the exploration of a rich chemistry of metal chalcogenolates. Their metal complexes, owing to a clean cleavage of the CE bond as well as their solubility in organic solvents, have emerged as successful precursors for the preparation of technologically important and synthetically challenging ternary semiconducting metal chalcogenides.

The dogma associated with chalcogen compounds as catalyst poison turns out to be an illusion. In fact, the palladium complexes with chalcogen ligands, discussed here and by several other researchers in the recent past, not only show good catalytic activity but also provide an opportunity to identify low-valent, low-coordinated catalytic intermediates. Such species are often believed to be active catalytic species responsible for the organic transformation catalysed by palladium complexes.

In these unprecedented times due to Covid-19, organoselenium compounds show promise to emerge as lead drug molecules. Ebselen, a synthetic organoselenium compound investigated for the treatment of multiple diseases, has been shown to inhibit Covid-19 virus replication in cell-based assays.99 In short, internally functionalized organochalcogen compounds would offer new challenges and opportunities in different domains of science and technology.
