Abstract

A two-dimensional (2D) Dionâ€“Jacobson (DJ) lead-free semiconductive hybrid double perovskite (HDP) [(3AMPY)2AgBiI8Â·H2O] (3AMPY = 3-(aminomethyl) pyridinium) (1) has been reported, which shows an attractive narrow band gap of 1.86 eV. Importantly, 1 is the first 2D DJ lead-free environmental friendly HDP constructed by an aromatic diammonium cation with high stability in humid conditions.

The toxicity issue of lead halide perovskites limits their applications in numerous fields.1â€“4 Low toxicity and narrow band gap of hybrid perovskites have always been the hot pursuit in the field of optoelectronics. From the perspective of environmental friendliness and wide absorption spectrum, increasing number of researchers have paid attention to the lead-free narrow gap hybrid double perovskites (HDPs) because of their non-toxicity and phase stability.5â€“7 Compared with three-dimensional (3D) hybrid perovskite materials, low dimensional organicâ€“inorganic hybrid-layered materials can provide more diversity and environmental stability in the selection of various organic cations.8,9 During the past few years, a series of lead-free two-dimensional (2D) HDPs have been successfully synthesized, such as (C3H9NI)4AgBiI8,5 (n-propylammonium)2CsAgBiBr7,7 and (n-butylammonium)2CsAgBiBr7.8 However, the above-mentioned lead-free HDPs all have the Ruddlesdenâ€“Popper (RP) type structure constructed by chain-like organic amines. Compared with the RP-type lead-free HDPs, the Dionâ€“Jacobson (DJ) type lead-free HDPs constructed by diammonium cations can shorten the distance and eliminate the van der Waals gap between the adjacent inorganic layers. Simultaneously, the neighboring inorganic slabs can be connected via hydrogen-bonding interactions with the diammonium groups, which may effectively promote the charge transport. These advantages make the 2D DJ type hybrid perovskites popular in numerous fields. It is worth noting that even in the lead hybrid perovskites, due to the lack of suitable structural characterization, the kinds of DJ type lead hybrid perovskites are still not abundant and various.10â€“15 Moreover, most of the cations used to construct 2D DJ type lead hybrid perovskites are linear alkyl diammonium and cyclic diammonium, such as 2-methyl-1,5-diaminopentane,10 1,4-butanediamine,14 and 1,6-hexamethylenediammonium.15

It is well known that the types of organic cations have great influence on the properties of hybrid perovskite materials. Recently, it has been reported that aromatic diammonium cations with delocalized Ï€-electrons show larger dielectric constants and smaller exciton binding energy, which are helpful to the charge transport.16 To the best of our knowledge, there are few 2D DJ type organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskites constructed by aromatic diammonium cations, and all of them are lead hybrid materials. For example, Kanatzidis et al. reported a series of 2D DJ lead halide perovskites, (xAMPY)(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (x = 3 or 4, n = 1â€“4). The cations were 3AMPY and 4-(aminomethyl) pyridinium (4AMPY).16 GrÃ¤tzel et al. reported a phenyl-based 2D DJ hybrid perovskite (PDMA)FA2Pb3I10, (PDMA = 1,4-phenylenedimethanammonium), which shows excellent stability in a humid environment.17 Recently, Liu et al. reported the thiophene-based high-quality 2D DJ (ThDMA)(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = 5), which exhibits high power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 15.75%.18 Compared with the 2D DJ type hybrid perovskites constructed by aliphatic diammonium,19,20 these 2D DJ type hybrid perovskites based on aromatic diammonium cations have been greatly improved in stability and numerous other properties, such as electron transfer capacity between the layers,16 light absorption ability and film forming ability.21 Unfortunately, these 2D DJ type hybrid perovskites all contain the toxic metal lead, and so far, there is no case of lead-free HDP materials based on aromatic diammonium cations. Moreover, the reported band gap of these 2D DJ lead hybrid perovskites constructed by aromatic diammonium is still relatively large, such as (3AMPY)PbI4 (Eg = 2.34 eV), (4AMPY)(MA) Pb2I7 (Eg = 2.15 eV) and (PDMA) PbI4 (Eg = 2.42 eV). Recently, Zheng et al. reported (C6H16N2)2-AgBiI8Â·H2O,22 (C6H16N2)2-CuBiI8Â·0.5H2O,22 (4-aminomethylpiperidine)4[BiAgI8]2Â·H2O23 and (4-aminopiperidine)4 [BiAgI8]2Â·H2O.23 These lead-free HDPs are all constructed by cyclic diammonium cations. Therefore, it is urgent to study the lead-free HDPs with narrow band gaps constructed by aromatic diammonium cations, which have potential applications in solar cells and optoelectronic devices (X-ray detectors, photo-detectors), photo-catalyst, ferroelectric photovoltaics and storage devices.22â€“25

Herein, we successfully synthesized the first 2D DJ type lead-free HDPs constructed by aromatic diammonium cations (3AMPY = 3-(aminomethyl) pyridinium), [(3AMPY)2AgBiI8Â·H2O] (1). The Ï€â€“Ï€ interactions between pyridine molecules and the strong hydrogen bonding between inorganic layers synergistically affected the band gap of the material. Fortunately, 1 exhibits an attractive narrow band gap of 1.86 eV comparable to those of 3D hybrid perovskites. Kanatzidis reports that there is a relationship between the band gap and inorganic interlayer spacing: stronger the interactions between inorganic interlayers, narrower the band gap.26 Our conclusion is consistent with the theoretical results previously reported. Our study provides some reliable detailed research for the design and synthesis of lead-free HDPs based on aromatic diammonium cations.

The replacement of toxic Pb in the rich Pb-halide perovskite hybrids with Bi3+ and Ag+ can not only be beneficial to the development of environment-friendly HDP materials but also endow the acquired lead-free HDPs with strong light absorption abilities of the Pb hybrids. Fig. 2a shows the optical absorption and the energy band gap of 1, which shows a wide absorption range and a stepwise absorption feature.
