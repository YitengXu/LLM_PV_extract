Abstract

The photoelectrochemical (PEC) water oxidation performance of Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes is jointly restricted by their high charge recombination and sluggish water oxidation kinetics. Herein, through coupling Ag and CdS nanoparticles with an Î±-Fe2O3 nanorod film, a new kind of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction film was constructed and investigated as a photoanode for solar water oxidation. The resultant Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode can take full advantages of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunction and the properties of Ag nanoparticles. Specifically, the formation of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunction improves the charge separation and transfer efficiency of the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode through band potential differences. Meanwhile, the presence of Ag nanoparticles enlarges the absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and decreases the electronâhole recombination through their surface plasmon resonance (SPR) effect. In addition, the electrocatalytic properties of Ag nanoparticles could initiate the water oxidation on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode jointly. As a result, a higher photocurrent density of 3.25 mA cmâ2 at 1.23 V vs. RHE was achieved on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode during water oxidation, which is 2.5 times higher relative to the photocurrent of the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode. Furthermore, the onset potential of water oxidation on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode was negatively shifted about 220 mV relative to the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode due to its faster kinetics. With the protection of the amorphous TiO2 layer, the water oxidation activity of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode can be steadily maintained even after 10 h of reaction at 1.23 V vs. RHE. The present work provides an efficient strategy for the development of Î±-Fe2O3-based ternary heterojunction photoanodes for solar water oxidation.

Introduction

Using semiconductor photoelectrodes, solar energy can be converted into hydrogen fuel by photoelectrochemical (PEC) water splitting.1,2 On the whole, PEC water splitting involves two half reactions: the four-hole reaction of water oxidation into oxygen on a photoanode as well as the two-electron reaction of proton reduction into hydrogen on a photocathode.3 Since the kinetics of water oxidation on a photoanode is more sluggish than that of proton reduction on a photocathode, development of suitable photoanode materials for efficient water oxidation is the key direction of PEC water splitting research.4 Due to the superiorities in the band gap and position, reaction stability, and preparation cost, hematite (Î±-Fe2O3) has been considered as a promising photoanode material for solar water oxidation.5â7 The band gap of Î±-Fe2O3 is about 2.1 eV and its valence band edge is around 2.5 V vs. RHE, which suggest â¼15% solar energy can be absorbed and potentially used by an Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode for the water splitting reaction.8,9 In addition, Î±-Fe2O3 has good stability in neutral and alkaline solutions, and raw materials for Î±-Fe2O3 preparation are cheap and nontoxic. But it is regrettable that the severe recombination of electronâhole pairs and sluggish kinetics of water oxidation jointly limit the PEC water oxidation activity of Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes.10â12 To achieve efficient water oxidation on Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes, the modifications of Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes have to be concentrated on the above activity obstacles.

As is well known, the oxidation of water into oxygen on photoanodes is a typical heterogeneous catalytic process. The recombination of electronâhole pairs of photoanodes during water oxidation is mainly related to their thermodynamic properties,13 while the kinetics of water oxidation on photoanodes is affected by their kinetic properties.14 In the past few years, the construction of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst heterojunction photoanodes has been demonstrated to be an effective strategy to address the activity limitations of Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes.15â17 In principle, the band edge difference between Î±-Fe2O3 and the coupled photocatalyst can drive the transfer and separation of charge directionally through the heterojunction. Furthermore, Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst photoanodes can integrate the advantages of Î±-Fe2O3 and the coupled photocatalyst, which may promote the water oxidation kinetics of Î±-Fe2O3 if the coupled photocatalyst has electrocatalytic properties. Accordingly, a series of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst heterojunction photoanodes (such as Si/Î±-Fe2O3, Co3O4/Fe2O3, Fe2O3/Fe2TiO5) display superior PEC water oxidation activity relative to the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode.18â20 It is noteworthy that the absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst heterojunction photoanodes of solar spectral absorption is strictly determined by the band gap of Î±-Fe2O3 and the coupled photocatalyst.21â24 Since the charge transfer and separation of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst photoanodes are not as high as expected, the enlargement of the solar absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst photoanodes is a significant approach to improve their water oxidation activity. Based on the surface plasmon resonance effect of metal nanoparticles, the solar absorption edge and catalytic activity of photocatalysts can be obviously expanded through coupling with suitable metal nanoparticles.25â30 Inspired by the construction of Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst and photocatalyst/metal nanoparticle heterojunctions to achieve efficient photocatalytic activity, it can be inferred that Î±-Fe2O3/photocatalyst/metal nanoparticle ternary heterojunctions may have better function of utilizing solar energy to initiate PEC water oxidation.

Herein, we constructed an Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction photoanode through the coupling of Ag and CdS nanoparticles with an Î±-Fe2O3 nanorod film. Compared with the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanodes, the resultant Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode shows higher water oxidation activity as well as faster kinetics by taking full advantage of its ternary heterojunction. Firstly, the formation of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunction can improve the charge transfer and separation efficiency of Î±-Fe2O3 photoanodes. Secondly, the presence of Ag nanoparticles enlarges the absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and decreases the electronâhole recombination through the surface plasmon resonance (SPR) effect. Finally, the electrocatalytic properties of Ag nanoparticles could initiate the water oxidation on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode jointly. Consequently, a higher photocurrent density of 3.25 mA cmâ2 at 1.23 V vs. RHE was achieved on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode during water oxidation, and its onset potential for PEC water oxidation was negatively shifted about 220 mV relative to the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode owing to faster kinetics. The water oxidation activity of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode can be steadily maintained with the protection of the amorphous TiO2 layer.

Results and discussion

The typical morphology and microstructure of the as-prepared Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS samples are shown in Fig. 1aâf. From the SEM and TEM images of the Î±-Fe2O3 sample (Fig. 1a and b), we can observe that the Î±-Fe2O3 film consisted of nanorod arrays, and the diameter of the nanorod is about 80 nm. For the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS sample, its main structure is the Î±-Fe2O3 nanorod arrays, while the Ag and CdS nanoparticles were coupled on the Î±-Fe2O3 nanorods evenly (Fig. 1d and e). The interface in contact between Î±-Fe2O3 nanorods and Ag/CdS nanoparticles would form heterojunctions naturally. The cross-sectional SEM image is shown in Fig. S2,â  and the thickness of the film is about 500 nm. The microstructure of Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS was further observed from their HR-TEM image. As shown in Fig. 1c, Î±-Fe2O3 has a lattice fringe of 0.25 nm, which well corresponds to the (110) crystal plane of Î±-Fe2O3. In the HR-TEM image of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS (Fig. 1f), its heterojunction feature is demonstrated clearly. The larger lattice fringes are the Î±-Fe2O3 (110) crystal planes, the smaller lattice fringes are the CdS (200) crystal planes (0.28 nm), and the particle lattice fringe regions are the Ag (200) crystal planes (0.20 nm). It should be pointed out that the tight interfacial connection between Î±-Fe2O3/CdS/Ag should have influence on its PEC reaction relative to bare Î±-Fe2O3. The energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDX) analysis further confirmed the heterojunction feature of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS (Fig. 1g). In the EDX mapping image, Fe and O elements are observed on the whole scale with uniform distribution, and the contents of Ag, Cd and S elements are smaller, indicating that a spot of Ag/CdS nanoparticles coupled on the Î±-Fe2O3 nanorods uniformly.

In other words, the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has the highest ABPE and IPCE, which is consistent with the photocurrent investigation results in Fig. 3a and b. Although there are inevitable deviations in utilization of MottâSchottky equation, which is established for dense films not nanostructured films.35 According to the MottâSchottky equation,37 the donor density of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is calculated to be 4.64 Ã 1020 cmâ3, which is higher than that of Î±-Fe2O3 (6.72 Ã 1019 cmâ3) and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS (3.85 Ã 1020 cmâ3) photoanodes. To further confirm the enhanced water oxidation performance on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode, the charge separation and injection efficiency of the Î±-Fe2O3, Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanodes were contrastively investigated. As shown in Fig. 4a and b, the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has highest charge separation and injection efficiency among the Î±-Fe2O3, Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanodes. It is generally accepted that photoanode with higher charge separation and injection efficiency exhibits better water oxidation activity and kinetics, respectively.38,39 Accordingly, we could know that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has higher water oxidation activity and faster kinetics than the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanode. In the further electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measurements, a direct finding about faster water oxidation kinetics on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode was obtained. As shown in Fig. 4c and d, the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has the smallest semicircle arc in the Nyquist EIS plots, indicating its fastest water oxidation kinetics. The fitted equivalent circuit for the three photoanodes is shown in the inset of Fig. 4c and d, and the fitted component values are summarized in Table 1. In the equivalent circuit, Rs represents the electrolyte resistance, R1 represents the water oxidation resistance on the photoanode, and R2 reflects the charge transfer resistance at the photoanode/electrolyte interface. The R1 of the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode is 214.0 Î©, while the R1 of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is 43.4 Î©, indicating that the water oxidation kinetics on the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode is significantly boosted after the coupling with Ag and CdS nanoparticles. The R2 of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is 20.8 Î©, which is almost half of the R2 of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanode (38.37 Î©), revealing that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction is better than the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS binary heterojunction for the charge transfer at the photoanode/electrolyte interface. In addition, CPE1 represents the capacitance of the space charge region of the photoanode and CPE2 is the transmission capacitance between the photoanode and electrolyte.40 As can be seen from Table 1, the CPE1 value of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is higher than that of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanode, but the CPE2 value of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is lower. The capacitance of the photoanode represents its performance to hold charge (C = Q/V, C is the capacitance, Q represents the charge and V is the potential). The higher CPE1 value of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode means its higher space charge region, and its lower CPE2 originates from the change of voltage or charge distribution caused by the relatively high Ag/CdS overlayer.41 Therefore, the EIS result is consistent with the other PEC results that confirm the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction photoanode having higher water oxidation performance. From the above PEC investigation results, it is certain that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction photoanode has higher water oxidation performance than the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode and the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS binary heterojunction photoanode.

To understand the origin of improved water oxidation performance on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode, the UV-vis harvesting properties of Î±-Fe2O3, Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS films were investigated. Fig. 5a shows the UV-vis absorption spectra of the three films, in which the lowest absorption intensity can be observed on the Î±-Fe2O3 film. By contrast, the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS films have enhanced absorption intensity, particularly the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film. Additionally, the absorption band edge of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film is extended to 620 nm, while the absorption band edge of the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS film is around 580 and 600 nm, respectively. This observation suggests that Î±-Fe2O3 coupling with CdS and Ag enhances its light absorption intensity, which can be attributed to the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunction effect and the surface plasmon resonance (SPR) effect of the Ag nanoparticles.42,43 In the UV-vis absorption spectrum of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film, a detailed feature is worthy of attention. Namely, a small bulge of absorption appeared between 500 and 550 nm, which could be attributed to the typical SPR effect of the Ag nanoparticles on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film.44 Based on the UV-vis absorbance spectra, the band gap of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS, Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3 films is found to be 2.04 eV, 2.08 eV and 2.06 eV, respectively (Fig. 5b). The band gap difference between these Î±-Fe2O3-based films is very tiny. Fig. 5c shows the difference in the open-circuit potential (OCP) of Î±-Fe2O3, Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanodes under dark and under illumination conditions. The OCP variation of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode under dark and illumination conditions is 200 mV, while that of the Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode is 90 mV and that of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanode is 170 mV. It is apparent that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has the highest value of OCP variation, indicating that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode has the highest efficiency in charge separation and transfer due to the presence of the ternary heterojunction. A similar result was also observed from the photoluminescence (PL) spectral investigation of the three films. As shown in Fig. 5d, the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film exhibits lower PL intensity than the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS films, suggesting its lower radiative recombination of carriers. In principle, the water oxidation on the photoanode involves kinetic processes in addition to the above light harvesting as well as charge separation and transfer processes. For further understanding the kinetic difference on Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS, the electrochemical water oxidation of the three film electrodes was investigated under dark conditions. As shown in Fig. 5e, the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS electrode in 0.5 M Na2SO4 shows the highest activity and lowest overpotential during electrochemical water oxidation compared to the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS electrodes. The Tafel slope of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS electrode is 60.1 mV decâ1, significantly lower than Î±-Fe2O3/CdS (91.0 mV decâ1) and Î±-Fe2O3 (95.7 mV decâ1) (Fig. 5f), indicating the highest water oxidation activity. The above findings reveal that the presence of Ag nanoparticles on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS film could boost its electrocatalytic kinetics for water oxidation, which should be an important factor that results in the faster PEC water oxidation kinetics on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode. To further reveal the role of Ag in the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction photoanode, we tested the charge separation efficiency of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag photoanode (Fig. S5â ). A higher charge separation efficiency was observed on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag photoanode relative to the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3 photoanode. This observation indicates that the presence of Ag nanoparticles can effectively improve the chargeâhole separation of Î±-Fe2O3. Further UV-vis absorbance investigation shows that the presence of Ag nanoparticles can enhance the light absorption intensity of the Î±-Fe2O3 film. Accordingly, it could be known that the presence of Ag nanoparticles enlarges the absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and improves the electronâhole separation through their surface plasmon resonance (SPR) effect.

Summarizing all of the above investigations, the better water oxidation activity and kinetics of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode could be ascribed to the following reasons. Firstly, due to the presence of the SPR effect from Ag nanoparticles, the production of photogenerated electrons and holes in the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is more than those in Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanodes. Furthermore, the formation of the Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunction could drive the photogenerated electron and hole separation and transfer directionally through their favorable band potential differences (see Fig. 5g). Since the valence band potential of Î±-Fe2O3 (â¼2.48 V vs. NHE) is higher than that of CdS (â¼1.88 V vs. NHE),45â47 the photogenerated holes of Î±-Fe2O3 can be transferred to CdS. In contrast, the photogenerated electrons of CdS can be transferred to Î±-Fe2O3 owing to their conduction band potential difference. As a result, higher water oxidation activity can be achieved on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode. Meanwhile, the Ag nanoparticles exhibit electrocatalytic activity for water oxidation, which could jointly initiate the water oxidation on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode, and thus results in its faster kinetics.

The water oxidation stability of the resultant Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode can be well maintained with the protection of the amorphous TiO2 layer. The amorphous titanium oxide layer was proved to be a useful protective layer to avoid the photocorrosion of the photoanodes as much as possible.48,49Fig. 6a shows the photocurrent density changes of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS and Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 photoanodes at 1.23 V vs. RHE within 10 h under AM 1.5G irradiation in 1.0 M NaOH. In general, better stability was observed for the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 photoanode. The initial photocurrent density for the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode is 3.25 mA cmâ2, but only 59% activity remained after 10 h of reaction due to the photocorrosion of CdS. With the protection of the amorphous TiO2 layer, the photocurrent density of Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 maintains 80% of its initial photocurrent density after 10 h testing (Fig. S6â ). The comparative SEM, HRTEM and XPS investigations for the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 photoanode before and after stability testing are shown in Fig. S7 and S8.â  The morphology of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 photoanode did not change significantly after 10 h stability testing. In addition, HRTEM investigation indicated that the amorphous TiO2 layer also did not change. A similar result was found in the XPS investigations that the chemical states of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS/TiO2 photoanode after stability testing were almost the same as before testing. The above observations solidly proved that the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode with an amorphous TiO2 layer has good stability for solar water splitting.

Conclusions

In summary, an Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS ternary heterojunction film was designed and investigated as a photoanode for solar water oxidation. Since the resultant Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode takes full advantage of its ternary heterojunction, higher water oxidation activity and faster kinetics can be achieved on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode relative to the Î±-Fe2O3 and Î±-Fe2O3/CdS photoanode. Specifically, the formation of Î±-Fe2O3/CdS heterojunctions improves the charge transfer and separation of Î±-Fe2O3 through their favorable band potential differences. Meanwhile, the presence of Ag nanoparticles enlarges the absorption edge of Î±-Fe2O3/CdS and decreases the electronâhole recombination through the surface plasmon resonance effect. In addition, the electrocatalytic properties of Ag nanoparticles can initiate the water oxidation on the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode jointly. With the protection of the amorphous TiO2 layer, the water oxidation activity of the Î±-Fe2O3/Ag/CdS photoanode can be steadily maintained even after 10 h of reaction at 1.23 V vs. RHE.
