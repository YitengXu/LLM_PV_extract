Abstract

Two-dimensional group III chalcogenides have gained global interest on account of their excellent prospects for applications in multifunctional energy conversion. Herein, via density functional theory calculations, we systematically unravel the electronic structures of In2XY2 (X, Y = S, Se, and Te; X â‰  Y) monolayers for their applications in energy conversion areas. Interestingly, the characteristics of the efficient spatial separation of photo-generated charge carriers, suitable band edge positions, and outstanding light-harvesting abilities indicate that In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers are desirable photocatalysts for the overall water splitting. It is found that In2SSe2 and In2STe2 act as donor materials, while In2SeS2 and In2SeTe2 act as acceptor materials in solar cells made of In2SeS2/In2SSe2 and In2STe2/In2SeTe2 heterostructures. It is highlighted that the theoretical power conversion efficiency reached 15.5% and 22.5% for In2SeS2/In2SSe2 and In2STe2/In2SeTe2 heterostructures, respectively. In particular, the ZT value of the In2STe2 monolayer reaches 3.2 at 500 K, indicating its potential as an up-and-coming candidate for next generation thermoelectric materials. The present research will pave the way for the practical applications of In2XY2 monolayers in the field of multifunctional energy conversion.

Introduction

The development of photocatalytic, photovoltaic, and thermoelectric materials has been given much attention due to the energy crisis and environmental degradation in the past few decades.1â€“3 Inspired by the successful synthesis of graphene,4 many two-dimensional (2D) materials have been reported experimentally or theoretically, providing unimagined possibilities for the development of clean energy conversion materials. For example, g-C3N4,5 black phosphorene (BP),6,7 MXenes,8,9 transition metal di-chalcogenides (TMDs),10 and group III chalcogenides (MX and M2X3)11â€“14 are all able to achieve high performance in the fields of photocatalysis and photovoltaic cells. Moreover, these 2D materials also show better performance in the field of thermoelectric devices due to their outstanding thermal transport properties which can be traced back to the quantum confinement effect.15 However, the discoveries and investigations of these novel 2D materials applied to the multiple energy fields are still ongoing and of great importance to realize the environmentally friendly, sustainable, and efficient energy conversion.

Fortunately, as one of the typical members of the group III chalcogenide family, the Î±-In2Se3 monolayer has been successfully fabricated using the physical vapor deposition (PVD)16 and molecular beam epitaxy (MBE)17 method. The out-of-plane and in-plane ferroelectric18 and piezoelectric19 properties induced by the asymmetric structure of Î±-In2Se3 suggest its promising applications in data storage devices, optical devices, and electronic devices. In addition, Î±-In2Se3 has been theoretically revealed to be a promising photocatalyst14 and thermoelectric20 material. However, its indirect bandgap characteristic feature significantly hinders its further applications.13 Currently, 18 artificial asymmetric indium-based tri-chalcogenide monolayers are theoretically constructed.21 Interestingly, half of them exhibit the direct bandgap semiconductor characteristics, which are beneficial to the transport of photo-generated charge carriers.21 Herein, similar to Janus 2D materials,22,23 indium based tri-chalcogenide monolayers with the stacking configuration of Y-In-X-In-Y (short for In2XY2, X, Y = S, Se, and Te; X â‰  Y) represent tunable electronic properties related to the chemical composition, which show the presence of the same chalcogen Y on the top and bottom surfaces, and another different chalcogen X in the internal layer. It is worth noting that the suitable and direct bandgap characteristics of In2XY2 monolayers indicate their great potential applications in multifunctional sustainable energy conversion fields. Therefore, the evaluation of In2XY2 monolayers for energy conversion applications is anticipated and of great interest.

In the present studies, systematic investigations have been carried out on the dynamic electronic properties and structures of In2XY2 (X, Y = S, Se, and Te; X â‰  Y) monolayers using the density functional theory calculations. The phonon spectrum calculations and AIMD simulations confirm the dynamic and thermal stabilities of In2XY2 monolayers, respectively. The present calculations show that In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 are very promising photocatalysts for the overall water splitting. The spatial separation of photo-induced charge carriers and outstanding light-harvesting ability further ensure a high efficiency in the overall water splitting. Additionally, the In2SeS2/In2SSe2 and In2STe2/In2SeTe2 heterostructures have been utilized as the solar cells and their corresponding theoretical power conversion efficiencies are predicted to reach 15.5% and 22.5%, respectively. Moreover, the In2STe2 monolayer exhibits outstanding phonon transport properties, which lead to high thermoelectric performance.

To check the dynamic stabilities of the In2XY2 monolayers, firstly, the calculations on the phonon dispersion curves for the lattice dynamic stability evaluations were carried out. The corresponding results are shown in Fig. 2. Except for the fact that the In2TeS2 and In2TeSe2 monolayers possess a very small imaginary frequency (âˆ’0.02 THz and âˆ’0.09 THz, respectively), no imaginary frequency is observed in other monolayers, which confirmed the lattice dynamic stabilities of the In2XY2 systems. Moreover, the thermal stabilities of In2XY2 monolayers were evaluated by performing AIMD simulations. As shown in Fig. S1 (ESIâ€ ), after annealing for 10 ps at 300 K, the total energy of the individual In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers is found to fluctuate in a narrow window and the corresponding structure is found to have no significant distortion. However, for the In2TeS2 and In2TeSe2 monolayer, as shown in Fig. S1(e and f) (ESIâ€ ), the total energy gradually decreases and the structure is severely changed and damaged. This phenomenon is mainly due to the existing imaginary frequency in the phonon dispersion curves, leading to the relative structural instability of In2TeS2 and In2TeSe2 monolayers. These results clearly indicate that the In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers possess high dynamic and thermal stabilities.

To unravel the electronic structures of the In2XY2 monolayers, their band structures were investigated and the atomic contributions are shown in Fig. 3. As shown in Fig. 3(c), the conduction band minimum (CBM) of the In2SeS2 monolayer is situated at the Î“ point, while the valence band maximum (VBM) is located in the middle between Î“ and M points. Interestingly, except for the In2SeS2 monolayer, all the other materials are found to be direct bandgap semiconductors. Compared with the indirect bandgap features of the Î±-In2X3 monolayers, the direct bandgap natures of the In2XY2 monolayers are more beneficial to the optical transition to induce the photo-generated charge carriers, which create favorable conditions in the photocatalysis and photovoltaic devices.40 The calculated band gaps of the In2XY2 monolayers are found to be within the range of 0.05 and 1.60 eV (as listed in Table 1). Moreover, from the investigated projected band structures of the In2XY2 monolayers, the contributions of the VBM are found to be mainly classified into the following two types: In type-I, the VBM is primarily occupied by the upper surface Y atoms (In2SSe2, In2STe2, and In2SeTe2), while in type-II, the VBM is mainly contributed by the inner X atoms (In2SeS2, In2TeS2, and In2TeSe2). As for the CBM, in all the cases, the CBM is mainly contributed by the lower surface Y atoms and inner In atoms. The spatial separation of the VBM and CBM makes a significant influence on the efficiency of the charge separation of the photo-generated electronâ€“hole pairs, the larger separation of the VBM and CBM leads to prolonging the recombination time of photo-generated electrons and holes, being beneficial to the photocatalytic overall splitting of water.41,42

Since the light absorption capacity of photocatalysts is an essential factor to affect the photocatalytic efficiency, the optical absorption coefficients of the In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers were investigated and their results are shown in Fig. 5 to evaluate the light-harvesting abilities. As shown in Fig. 5, the optical absorption peaks of the In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers are found in the visible light regions at around 410â€“440 nm, while that of the In2SSe2 monolayer is found in the UV-light area at around 330 nm. These features indicate that the In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers utilize sunlight more efficiently than the In2SSe2 monolayer. However, it was found that despite this drawback of the In2SSe2 monolayer, its high absorption coefficient (âˆ¼105 cmâˆ’1) in the visible light region guarantees its high visible light-harvesting ability. These results further demonstrate the high potential of the In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeS2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers for application to photovoltaic devices.

As shown in Fig. S2 (ESIâ€ ), six different stacking configurations were considered to understand the stacking pattern's effect on the stability of the heterostructures. These six stacking configurations are divided into two categories: In category-I, the upper surface Y atom in one layer is combined with another one's upper surface Y atoms at the interface (stacking-I, stacking-II, and stacking-III), while, in category-II, the upper surface Y atoms combine with lower surface Y atoms (stacking-IV, stacking-V, and stacking-VI). By considering their practical applications as solar cells, the stacking configurations in category-I (stacking-I, stacking-II, and stacking-III) with larger bandgaps were investigated.

Conclusions

In summary, based on density functional theory calculations, systematic studies of the structural, electronic, and optical properties of In2XY2 (X, Y = S, Se, and Te; X â‰  Y) monolayers as well as their corresponding applications in the field of photocatalysis, photovoltaics, and thermoelectrics have been carried out. The stabilities of the In2XY2 monolayers were confirmed from the lattice dynamics and thermodynamics point of view. Except for the In2SeS2 monolayer, all In2XY2 monolayers exhibited direct semiconducting features with the bandgap energies in the ranges between 0.05 and 1.60 eV. The In2SSe2, In2STe2, In2SeTe2, and In2SeTe2 monolayers were demonstrated to be very promising candidates for the photocatalytic overall water splitting to produce hydrogen and oxygen because of their thermodynamically suitable band edges, efficient separation of the photo-generated charge carriers, and excellent optical absorption abilities. Due to the appropriate bandgaps and lattice mismatches, the In2SeS2/In2SSe2 and In2STe2/In2SeTe2 vdW heterostructures exhibited a high power conversion efficiency of 15.5% and 22.5%, respectively. In particular, the excellent optical absorption coefficients and high photocurrents of these two heterostructures suggest their potential applications in solar cell devices. Furthermore, the In2STe2 monolayer exhibited outstanding thermoelectric properties owing to its low lattice thermal conductivity and high power factor, where the ZT value reached 3.22 at 500 K with n-type doping. These present findings have clearly indicated that the In2XY2 monolayers are promising materials for clean energy production and also pave the way for the development of new types of Î±-In2X3-based monolayers.
