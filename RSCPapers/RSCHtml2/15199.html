Cobalt-tungsten diselenide-supported nickel foam as a battery-type positive electrode for an asymmetric supercapacitor device: comparison with various MWSe2 (M = Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn) on the structural and capacitance characteristics

Abstract

New exploration in nanomaterial research has been greatly encouraged so as to discover active electrode materials with extraordinary properties and performances. In this report, we demonstrated the synthesis of different transition metal-incorporated MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn) and studied them using various characterization techniques. Subsequently, the proposed bimetallic chalcogenides were successfully applied as the active electrode materials for pseudocapacitor applications. The results of the electrochemical studies showed that CoWSe2 exhibited a higher specific capacitance of 3309.58 F g1 at a constant applied current density of 1.35 A g1, which is 1.07, 1.76, 2.04, 8.7, and 12.28-fold higher than that of NiWSe2, CuWSe2, ZnWSe2, MnWSe2, and pristine WSe2, respectively. The interconnected nanosheet structure with voids facilitates rich active sites for efficient electrolyte uptake and superior charge transfer during the faradaic redox reaction. In addition, the cycle stability of CoWSe2/NF was studied and the retention capacitance of about 82.1% was recorded, which is higher than that of NiWSe2 (60.4%), CuWSe2 (50.12%), ZnWSe2 (46.44%), MnWSe2 (40.12%), and pristine WSe2 (31.2%). Owing to the higher specific capacitance and cycle stability, CoWSe2 was proposed as a battery-type electrode material for the fabrication of an asymmetric device. The fabricated CoWSe2//AC device provided excellent energy density and power density of 182.54 W h kg1 and 2810.81 W kg1, respectively, at 3.51 A g1. Based on these properties, the proposed research and studies can provide a way for the profound development of 2D-layered metal chalcogenides for energy storage applications.

1. Introduction

Recent research has concentrated on the exploration of new types of methods for supplying energy and satisfying the demands of the world's population. The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) has predicted that energy usage might increase by nearly 50% in 2050, especially in Asian countries. In the energy cycle, energy resources and technologies for energy conversion/storage play a crucial role.1,2 For decades, fossil fuels have been listed as the world's important energy resources. The energy consumption report of EIA-2019 mentioned that fossil fuels are the foremost energy source, contributing to about 35% of the total energy generation in the U.S. However, the concerns associated with fossil fuels, including their depletion and emission of CO2, are the major reasons for the exploration and use of alternative clean and sustainable energy sources.3,4 With the extensive development of renewable energy sources and zero pollution effects, solar and wind energy technologies have been considered as favourable and alternative options. However, the energy from these intermittent resources must be stored and integrated into the power grid for efficient energy distribution.5 Owing to the elevated power density, superior chargedischarge, storage capacity, and long cycle stability, electrochemical supercapacitors have been focused on widespread applications.6 Supercapacitors and rechargeable batteries have similar components including positive/negative electrodes and separators in the electrolyte. Besides, in terms of development, supercapacitors are considered to be between rechargeable batteries and conventional capacitors with higher power density and energy density, respectively.7,8 Globally, the supercapacitor market was valued at 685 million USD at the end of 2018; it can be expected to be at 2187 million USD after 6 years. Thus, many types of research have been executed for enhancing the performance of supercapacitors so as to replace or use them along with rechargeable batteries.9 According to this perspective, many new types of electrode-active materials with proficient physical and electrochemical properties have been innovated. The major interdisciplinary attention in new research is the synthesis and development of 2D-layered metal chalcogenides because of their tunable physical and electrochemical properties as well as the graphene family of nanomaterials. The layered metal chalcogenides, especially MX2 (M = Mo and W), are considered as ideal electrode materials and are quite suitable to explore the exciting electrochemical performances for supercapacitors owing to their layered crystal nature, tunable bandgap, superior charge transfer mobility, higher stability, high electron density in the d subshell, and their contribution to the high electronic conductivity and electrocatalytic activity.10,11 For example, M. Pumera et al. synthesized MX2 (M = Mo, W and X = S, Se) and studied the effect of the exfoliation technique on their capacitance, wherein the W-based chalcogenides showed better capacitance than that of Mo-based chalcogenides.12 Also, M. Habib et al. demonstrated the synthesis of WX2 (X = S and Se) and achieved a higher specific capacitance for the WSe2-based electrode with higher cycle stability and 99% capacitance retention owing to the higher metallic conductivity and electrocatalytic activity of Se than that of S.13 Although WSe2 exhibits an indistinguishable layered atomic structure and intrinsic electronic properties as good as those of MoSe2, only a few research studies have been reported on WSe2-based energy storage devices. Therefore, it is necessary to discover a suitable method to improve the performance of WSe2 and to develop its application towards energy storage devices. Recently, the intrinsic electronic conductivity and electrocatalytic activity of metal chalcogenides have been adjusted and optimized using various engineering methods including heterogeneous metal doping, integration of carbon allotropes, and modification/preparation of mono-metal chalcogenides into bimetallic chalcogenides.1416 Among these, the preparation of bimetallic chalcogenides is considered as a simple and effective way to improve the electrochemical characteristics of mono-metal chalcogenides, wherein the integrated heterogeneous transition metals combine their individual intrinsic properties together with mono-metal chalcogenides. It facilitates additional redox behavior and enhances the charge transfer conductivity. For example, S. J. Kim et al. reported a supercapacitor based on copper-tungsten sulfide and achieved a high specific capacitance of about 2666.6 F g1 at 10 mA with high energy/power density.17 W. An et al. prepared nickel-cobalt selenide and compared its performance with mono-metal cobalt selenide, wherein the bimetallic nickel-cobalt selenide showed higher specific capacitance and lower charge transfer resistance than that of the mono-metal cobalt selenide.18 Besides, our previous research works have strongly demonstrated the better performance of Ni and Co-doped MoSe2 towards supercapacitor, biofuel cell, and electrochemical sensor applications and their improvement compared to that of pristine MoSe2.19,20 Thus, the synthesis of bimetallic WSe2 can be an interesting research topic for the development of layered materials in energy storage applications. Inspired by the above-mentioned reports, we synthesized various transition metal-incorporated (Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn) WSe2 (CoWSe2, NiWSe2, CuWSe2, ZnWSe2, and MnWSe2) and studied their electrochemical properties towards the realization of an electrochemical supercapacitor. In this work, the proposed bimetallic chalcogenides were directly coated on nickel foam (NF) by following the hydrothermal method. The direct growth of active materials on NF offers several benefits such as the direct/effective binding of active material with NF and prevented the detrimental effect of the polymer binder. In addition, the three-dimensional structure of NF facilitates a large number of active sites to the electrolyte for creating abundant interfaces for electrochemical reaction. The results of the electrochemical studies showed that CoWSe2/NF exhibited a superior specific capacitance of 3309.58 F g1 at a constant applied current density of 1.35 A g1, which is comparatively higher than that of NiWSe2 (1598.52 F g1), CuWSe2 (1196.56 F g1), ZnWSe2 (1085.99 F g1), MnWSe2 (339.80 F g1), and pristine WSe2 (249.14 F g1). In addition, the cycle stability of CoWSe2/NF was studied and the retention capacitance of about 82.1% was recorded, which is 0.35, 0.63, 0.76, 1.04, and 1.63-fold higher than that of NiWSe2 (60.4%), CuWSe2 (50.12%), ZnWSe2 (46.44%), MnWSe2 (40.12%), and pristine WSe2 (31.2%). The obtained higher specific capacitance of CoWSe2 can be related to various factors as follows: (i) the electrocatalytic promotional effect of Co is comparatively higher than other transition metals, as reported in the literature;21 (ii) the local bond order of Se in WSe2 can change due to the incorporation of Co, which introduces unsaturated Se edges22 and is favourable for higher intrinsic electronic conductivity and electrocatalytic activity; (iii) the Co atoms replace the W in WSe2 and introduce intermediate bands within the band gap, which contributes to higher conductivity;23,24 (iv) the unpaired electron with low spin state of Co supports its stronger binding with W and Se atoms; which promotes faster electron transfer;25 (v) also, Co shares its individual electronic conductivity and redox properties with WSe2.26 The obtained higher specific capacitance, lower charge transfer resistance, and larger cycle stability are the reasons for the development of the CoWSe2//AC asymmetric supercapacitor, which delivered superior energy density (182.54 W h kg1) and power density (2810.81 W kg1) at 3.51 A g1. Thus, we determined that the incorporation of transition metals into pristine WSe2 provides a new idea and platform to achieve superior layered materials for the development of next-level flexible supercapacitor technology.

2. Experimental section

2.1. Materials and reagents

Nickel foam (NF) with 7598% porosity and 98% hole rate, which was used as the substrate, was purchased from Xiamen Technology, Fujian, China. Sodium tungstate dihydrate (Na2WO42H2O), nickel nitrate hexahydrate (Ni(NO3)26H2O, 98.5%), cobalt nitrate hexahydrate (Co(NO3)26H2O, 98%), copper nitrate hexahydrate (Cu(NO3)26H2O, 98%), zinc nitrate hexahydrate (Zn(NO3)26H2O, 98%), manganese nitrate hexahydrate (Mn(NO3)26H2O, 98%), selenium powder (Se, 99.99%), urea (NH2CONH2, 98%), N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone (NMP, C5H9NO, 99.5%), poly(1,1-difluoroethylene) (PVDF, (CH2CF2)n, M.W. 530000), diazane (NH2NH2H2O, 7882%), activated carbon (AC, large surface area of 1000 m2 g1), acetylene black (>99.95%, particle size of 45 5 m, pore volume of 0.5 cm2 g1, average pore diameter of 100 10 ), poly(ethanol) (PVA, [CH2CHOH]n, M.W. 8900098000), ethyl hydroxide (EtOH, 99.5%), and pottasium hydrate (KOH, 85%) were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich. All the reagents were of analytical grade and used without further purification.

2.2 Synthesis of MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn)

The proposed bimetallic metal chalcogenides were prepared by using a two-step hydrothermal synthetic method. In this method, MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn) were directly coated on the NF substrate. Before the hydrothermal reaction, NF was washed gently with 1 M HCl by using the ultrasonication process. For CoWSe2/NF, 0.01 mol of Na2WO42H2O and 0.01 mol of Co(NO3)26H2O were mixed into 80 mL of H2O/EtOH (1:1 vol ratio) under magnetic stirring for 20 min. Consequently, 0.1 mol of NH2CONH2 was mixed into the above solution and continuously stirred for 30 min. The final mixture solution was poured into a 100 mL autoclave and the washed NFs were kept into the solution. Then, the hydrothermal reaction was carried out at a temperature of 180 C for 6 h. After the hydrothermal reaction, the autoclave was cooled down, and the NFs were washed using H2O/EtOH (1:1 vol ratio) and kept in an oven at 110 C for 5 h. For the selenization process, the obtained NFs were again mixed into 50 mL of Se (0.02 mol) containing the NH2NH2/EtOH (1:1 vol ratio) solution and the hydrothermal reaction was performed at 140 C for 12 h. At the end of the reaction, the obtained CoWSe2-coated NFs were washed using H2O/EtOH and kept at 110 C for 6 h. NiWSe2, CuWSe2, ZnWSe2, MnWSe2, and pristine WSe2-coated NFs were obtained by following the similar experimental conditions mentioned above. 2.3 Characterization techniques

Field emission scanning electron microscopy (FESEM, Nova NanoSEM230, USA) and transmission electron microscopy (JEOL 2100F) were performed to analyze the morphology and structural information of MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Mn, and Zn) and pristine WSe2. X-ray diffraction (XRD, XPERT-3 diffractometer with Cu K radiation (K = 1.54 )) was carried out to understand the crystalline nature and the formation of the prepared samples. The electronic state of the elements in the prepared samples was estimated by using X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS, Thermo Scientific Theta Probe, East Grinstead, UK) analysis. The surface area and pore size distribution of the prepared samples were estimated using BrunauerEmmettTeller (BET, Micro metrics ASAP 2020 M instrument, USA) analysis. The electrochemical properties of the prepared MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Mn, and Zn) and pristine WSe2-coated NF were studied by using cyclic voltammetry (CV) and galvanostatic chargedischarge (CD) technique (CHI440 electrochemical analyzer). The interfacial charge transfer resistance between the electroactive material and the electrolyte was analyzed by using electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS, IM6ex ZAHNER impedance measurement unit).

2.4 Electrochemical measurements

2.4.1 Fabrication of three- and two-electrode cells. The electrochemical properties of bimetallic MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Mn, and Zn) were studied and compared with those of pristine WSe2 using various electrochemical analyses including CV, GCD, and EIS. For these electrochemical studies, the proposed bimetallic chalcogenides and pristine WSe2-coated NFs were used as the working electrodes; meanwhile, Hg/HgO (1.0 M KOH), Pt wire, and aqueous 1.0 M KOH were used as the reference electrode, counter electrode, and electrolyte, respectively. In the CV experiment, the set of working potential was fixed from 0.4 to 0.6 V at varying scan rates of 10, 20, 30, 40, and 50 mV s1. On the other hand, the GCD experiment was carried out in a potential window from 0 to 0.55 V at various current densities of 1.35, 1.89, 2.43, 2.97, 3.51, and 4.05 A g1. The EIS study was performed in the frequency range from 0.1 Hz to 100 kHz with a fixed potential of 10 mV. The quantitative result of Rct at the electrode/electrolyte interface was obtained from the resultant Nyquist plot. For the two-electrode asymmetric cells, CoWSe2 and AC electrodes were used as the anodic and cathodic electrodes, respectively. In beginning, the AC electrode was fabricated by preparing a homogeneous slurry containing AC (80 wt%), carbon black (15 wt%), PVDF (5 wt%), and NMP (0.4 mL). Then, the prepared slurry was gently coated on the NF (1 1 cm2) and kept overnight in a vacuum oven at 100 C. Before the fabrication, the PVA/1.0 M KOH gel electrolyte was prepared by dissolving 1.0 g of PVA in 50 mL of water under constant magnetic stirring at 100 C for 6 h. After stirring, 1.0 M KOH (10 mL) was slowly added into the above polymer solution and the stirring process was continued until the mixture turned into a gel-like substance. After the reaction, the obtained colloidal PVA gel was applied in between the anodic and cathodic electrodes and sandwiched together. Then, the sandwiched two-electrode cell was directly used for the electrochemical studies.

3.2 Electrochemical properties of different working electrodes

3.2.1 Cyclic voltammetry and galvanostatic chargedischarge studies for three-electrode systems. The electrochemical properties of MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn), pristine WSe2/NF, and NF were studied and compared by using the CV technique, as shown in Fig. 8A, wherein the electrodes were employed at a constant scan rate of 10 mV s1 and a fixed potential window in the range from 0.4 to 0.6 V. The redox behavior of the proposed electrodes can be observed from the pair of anodic and cathodic peak current response at 0.5 and 0.2 V, respectively, which strongly suggests that the corresponding electrodes are favorable for the battery-type faradaic charge storage mechanism. Among them, CoWSe2 shows the largest redox current response (Ipa = 0.1154 A and Ipc = 0.09 A) with an integrated area of 0.0393 AV, which is comparatively 0.553, 1.311, 1.367, 2.63, 2.89, and 42.6-fold higher than that obtained for NiWSe2 (0.0253 AV), CuWSe2 (0.0170 AV), ZnWSe2 (0.0166 AV), MnWSe2 (0.0108 AV), WSe2 (0.0101 AV), and NF (0.0009 AV). The obtained redox current response and the integrated area of the CV curves suggest that CoWSe2/NF is relatively better than other modified electrodes and is able to produce a higher specific capacitance. In metal chalcogenides, the filling of the eg orbital by the 3d electron of Co induces higher density of states in the conduction band and improves both the intrinsic electronic conductivity and the electrocatalytic activity. Besides, the CD technique was carried out for MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn), WSe2, and NF electrodes to understand the chargedischarge behavior of the electrodes. At the beginning of the experiment, the optimized potential was confirmed by varying the potential window in the order of 0.4, 0.45, 0.5, 0.55, and 0.6 V at 1.35 A g1. The resultant GCD curves of CoWSe2/NF are shown in Fig. S5A; the corresponding specific capacitance was measured and is demonstrated in the bar diagram (Fig. S5B). It can be observed that CoWSe2/NF showed perfect non-symmetrical triangular GCD curves with gradually increasing specific capacitance in the potential window from 0.4 to 0.55 V. In contrast, the GCD curve changed into an imperfect non-symmetrical triangular shape with lower specific capacitance when the potential increased to 0.6 V. Therefore, the potential window of 00.55 V was confirmed as the optimum potential window for application in all CD electrochemical studies. 4. Conclusions

In conclusion, the MWSe2 (M = Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, and Mn)-coated NFs were successfully synthesized by using the hydrothermal method and applied as the battery-type electrode materials for supercapacitor studies. Among these bimetallic chalcogenides, the CoWSe2-coated NF exhibited superior Csp (3309.58 F g1 at 1.35 A g1), lower Rct (10.83 ), and better cycle stability (82.10% up to 5000 cycles) as compared to other proposed electrode materials. For further development, the asymmetric device containing CoWSe2 (positive electrode) and AC (negative electrode) also exhibited a feasible energy density (E) of 182.54 Wh kg1 and a power density of 2810.81 W kg1, which are comparatively higher than those reported in previously reported literature. The higher electrochemical performance of CoWSe2 can be related to the layered nanostructure with interconnected voids, which offers a higher surface area for both electrolyte uptake and ion diffusion during the chargedischarge faradaic redox reaction. In addition, the incorporated transition metals share their intrinsic electronic properties; thus, the proposed bimetallic chalcogenides can exhibit multiple electrochemical properties.
