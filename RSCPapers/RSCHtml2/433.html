Abstract

Hole transport material (HTM)-free carbon-based perovskite solar cells (C-PSCs) have shown much promise because of their excellent stability and low cost. However, the most commonly used three-dimensional (3D) MAPbI3 photoabsorber is ambient-unstable and incompatible with the low-cost mass-production of C-PSCs. Considering the proven operational stability of two-dimensional (2D) perovskites, we herein attempt to use a series of new 2Dâ€“3D hybrid (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 perovskites in C-PSCs. We find that the fabricated (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films (n = 20, 10, and 6) exhibit extremely improved ambient and photo-stability under 60 day-ambient conditions. The HTM-free C-PSCs with a structure of ITO/C60/(EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1/C retain outstanding power conversion efficiency over 11.88%. Particularly, by tuning the stoichiometry of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 to n = 6, the n6-2D device maintains a long-term stability of 93% under ambient conditions after 2160 hours, a thermal stability of 80% after heating at 80 Â°C over 100 hours, and a photo-stability of 92% under continuous 1 sun illumination over 300 hours, which are apparently superior to those of the MAPbI3 device (i.e. ambient stability of 73%; thermal stability of 9%; photo-stability of 67% after 83 hours). To the best of our knowledge, our fabricated C-PSC with the 2Dâ€“3D halide photoabsorber exhibits the best ambient-air-stable performance among all low-temperature carbon electrode-based PSCs reported so far.

Introduction

Despite the impressive photovoltaic performances with power conversion efficiency (PCE) beyond 23%,1,2 perovskite solar cells (PSCs) are poorly stable under operation, failing by far the market requirements.3â€“5 Various technological approaches have been proposed to overcome the instability problem,6â€“14 among which carbon-based perovskite solar cells (C-PSCs) without hole transport materials (HTMs) are arguably the most promising for commercialization applications because of their excellent stability, low cost, high performance and simple manufacturing process.7,15,16 Initial work was performed by Han and colleagues who fabricated HTM-free C-PSCs by infiltrating methylammonium lead iodide (MAPbI3) into a three-layer film configuration of TiO2/ZrO2/C with a PCE of 6.6%.17 Then they achieved an improved PCE of 15.6% by adding ammonium chloride into the perovskite precursor solution.18 Most recently, Wang et al. fabricated HTM-free C-PSCs with a complex mesoporous-structured configuration of TiO2/Al2O3/NiO/C, yielding a highest PCE of 17.02% thus far.19 However, all these TiO2/ZrO2/C or TiO2/Al2O3/NiO/C-based devices require a high-temperature annealing procedure (>400 Â°C) for the purpose of removing the template in the space layer and carbon layer,9,17â€“25 which will inevitably increase energy consumption and limit mass production on a flexible or plastic substrate.

Commercial carbon paste exhibits the advantages of high conductivity, low cost, good stability, and a low-temperature process (100 Â°C), which make it an excellent candidate for the cathode of C-PSCs.26 The photovoltaic performance of paintable C-PSCs with the low-temperature-processed carbon paste as the electrode has rapidly developed from 8.31% in 2014 to 14.38% in 2016, but few C-PSCs exhibited highly efficient and systematic stability matching with that of the stable carbon electrode.26â€“35 Very recently, we employed C60 as the electron transport layer (ETL) to construct an all-carbon-based PSC with a structure of FTO/C60/MAPbI3/C, achieving a champion PCE of 15.38% and an improved stability (5% of efficiency loss after 480 hours in air and under continuous 1 sun illumination (cutoff UV filter, >420 nm) for 180 hours).36 Note that the existing efficiency decay mainly originates from the degradation of the MAPbI3 photoabsorber itself upon exposure to moisture and/or light.37 Two-dimensional (2D) perovskites such as n-butylammonium lead iodide (BA2PbI4) as promising light absorbers have been introduced into photovoltaics due to their higher moisture resistance far above that of the conventional three-dimensional (3D) counterpart i.e. MAPbI3.8,38â€“47 The Quasi-2D perovskite device with an inverted structure of FTO/PEDOT:PSS/(BA)2(MA)3Pb4I13/PCBM/Al was reported to achieve an efficiency of 12.52% (PEDOT:PSS = poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene)polystyrene sulfonate).40 A Cs-2D perovskite device achieved an enhanced PCE of 13.68% with a regular structure of FTO/TiO2/(BA)2Cs3x(MA)3âˆ’3xPb4I13/Spiro-OMeTAD/Au.45 However, such unencapsulated Quasi-2D perovskite devices cannot avoid suffering from ambient-instability owing to the hygroscopic nature of the used organic HTM layer i.e. PEDOT:PSS or Spiro-OMeTAD,48â€“51 which are incompatible with the stability requirement of PSCs.

In this research context, there is an excellent route to comprehensively enhance the stability of PSCs by introducing moisture-resistant 2Dâ€“3D hybrid perovskites into stable HTM-free C-PSCs. Herein we for the first time use 2Dâ€“3D perovskites in a stable HTM-free C-PSC architecture to accommodate all practical requirements including outstanding ambient stability and efficiency. A short carbon-chain cation i.e. EA (EA = ethylammonium, C2H5NH3) is introduced into 3D MAPbI3 to create a 2D component and consequently form a series of new 2Dâ€“3D hybrid perovskites with the general formula (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1.52,53 It is found that the fabricated (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films (n = 20, 10, and 6) exhibit extremely improved ambient and photo-stability as characterized by XRD, UV-Vis spectroscopy, SEM and image measurements under 60 day-ambient conditions. Further, the HTM-free C-PSCs with the structure of ITO/C60/(EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1/C retain outstanding PCE over 11.88%, which is higher than those of the C-PSCs with 2Dâ€“3D hybrid perovskites prepared using longer carbon-chain cations i.e. BA (BA = n-butylammonium, C4H9NH3) and PEA (PEA = phenylethylammonium, C8H9NH3). By tuning the stoichiometry of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 to n = 6, the resultant n6-2D C-PSC device retains a long-term stability of 93% under ambient conditions after 2160 hours, a thermal stability of 80% after continued heating at 80 Â°C over 100 hours, and a photo-stability of 92% under continuous 1 sun illumination over 300 hours, in which all C-PSCs devices are without any encapsulation. Such outstanding stability of n6-2D C-PSC is apparently superior to that of MAPbI3 C-PSC (ambient stability of 73%; thermal stability of 9%; photo-stability of 67% for 83 hours), which is the best ambient-air-stable performance among those of all low-temperature carbon electrode-based PSCs.26â€“36

Results and discussion

The pure 2D perovskite (n = 1), EA2PbI4, was deposited by spin coating the precursor solution containing a stoichiometric ratio of lead iodide (PbI2) and EAI in mixture solutions of DMF and DMSO. Generally, the orientation of 2D materials on flat substrates strongly favors growth where the layers orient parallel to the substrate.54 Fig. S1aâ€  shows the top view SEM image of the EA2PbI4 film, which illustrates the structure of the 2D perovskite tending to crystallize in a direction parallel to the substrate. In the case of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1, this trend from X-ray diffraction appears to be true only for the n = 1 compound without MA, where preferential growth along the (110) direction occurs, thus exclusively revealing the (00l) reflections.38 A new XRD peak at 2Î¸ lower than 10Â° representing the crystallographic planes (002) appears in EA2PbI4 perovskite (Fig. S1bâ€ ), which had been observed in 2D (BA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 perovskites recently reported by Stoumpos et al.55 The observed crystallographic planes (002), (004), (006), and (008) in the EA2PbI4 film similar to the BA2PbI4 film reported by Kanatzidis et al. demonstrate the formation of the pure 2D perovskite.38 The additional low-angle XRD peak as well as the morphology of the EA2PbI4 film demonstrate that the EA cation like BA can facilitate the formation of a 2D perovskite structure. Thus, we add the EA cation into MAPbI3 perovskite with the aim of tuning the dimensions (2Dâ€“3D) of perovskites and investigating their thermal, photo- and ambient stability together with photovoltaic performance. Four different hybrid perovskites, i.e. (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = âˆž, 20, 10 and 6) were prepared by varying the addition amount of EA into the MAPbI3 precursor solution.

XRD measurements were performed for the mixed dimensional (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films to investigate the crystallinity and dimension of the perovskite nanostructure.56 A similar XRD peak at 2Î¸ lower than 10Â° can still be observed in the perovskite films i.e. (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = 6 and 10) (Fig. 1a), demonstrating the 2D component existing in (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 (n = 6 and 10) perovskites.38 Furthermore, the n6 film with more EA content exhibits a stronger intensity XRD peak below 10Â° than the n10 film, indicating a higher amount of 2D component existing in the n6 film. Note that no signal lower than 10Â° in the n20 film appears due to the low trace amount of EA. Besides, three other dominant planes are observed at diffraction angles (2Î¸, Cu KÎ±) of around 14Â°, 28Â° and 32Â° (Fig. 1a and b), representing the (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 crystallographic planes (110), (220) and (310), respectively.57 From zoomed-in XRD patterns in the region of 13Â°â€“33Â°, one can see that the three XRD peaks all shift towards lower angles with the increase of EA content doped in MAPbI3 (from n = âˆž to n = 6). This is because that EA (CH3CH2NH3+) is larger than MA (CH3NH3+),58 and the unit cell expands when MA is replaced by EA. According to Bragg's law, a larger d-spacing would lead to the shift of the diffraction peaks to smaller diffraction angles.57

The ambient stability of the involved four different hybrid (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films (n = âˆž, 20, 10, and 6) is investigated using SEM, images, XRD and UV-Vis absorption spectra measurements under ambient laboratory conditions (30â€“80% humidity) for up to 60 days. The SEM images of the as-prepared and air-aged (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films are presented in Fig. 3 and S8.â€  All four fresh films display a flat surface morphology. Note that many obvious pin-holes are discerned in the MAPbI3 perovskite film (see red circles labelled in Fig. S8aâ€ ). The moisture easily enters the interior perovskite through pin-hole sites, which will lead to inferior moisture-tolerance of the MAPbI3 perovskite film. As expected, upon exposure to ambient conditions for 60 days, a great number of grains can be observed, spreading from the original grain boundaries to the whole MAPbI3 film (n = âˆž), leading to a rough surface. It is worth noting that the grain boundaries disappear completely. The new emerging little grains originate from the decomposition of MAPbI3 perovskite by moisture erosion, which is verified by the strong PbI2 peak in the XRD patterns of the (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 film (n = âˆž) after exposure to ambient conditions for 60 days (Fig. 4). In contrast, other fresh 2Dâ€“3D hybrid (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 perovskite films (n = 20, 10, and 6) all show a compact and well-covered crystal morphology (Fig. S8bâ€“dâ€ ), which can efficiently prevent the erosion from moisture. Obviously, the intensity of the (110) diffraction peak of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films (n = 20, 10 and 6) at about 14Â° also increased dramatically compared to the (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 film without EA (n = âˆž) i.e. 3D MAPbI3 (Fig. S9â€ ), indicating the higher crystallinity of 2Dâ€“3D hybrid perovskites than 3D MAPbI3.66,67 On further comparison of n6-2D, n10-2D and n20-2D films, we found that the intensity of the (110) diffraction peak of the n6-2D film is apparently higher than that of the n20-2D film, but slightly lower than that of the n10-2D film. Herein, it is worth noting that a new diffraction peak of the 2D perovskite at below 10Â° appears. Such 2D crystallographic orientation existing in the perovskite film facilitates the enhancement of film and cell stability.38,46 For the (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 film (n = 20), the larger crystal perovskites are eroded by moisture, giving rise to smaller crystal particles with more grain boundaries, and the original grain boundaries are still discerned after exposure to ambient conditions for 60 days. The XRD patterns of the n20-2D film also demonstrate the maintenance of the pure perovskite composition without PbI2, indicating that this n20-2D film does not decompose in air after storage for 60 days. Moisture erosion is alleviated in the n10-2D film, and the surface still becomes slightly coarser in air, which is in agreement with XRD patterns. As for the n6-2D film, no obvious changes can be observed after 60 day storage, and it still maintains its initial crystal structure and grain boundaries, which indicates its extremely outstanding film stability. In order to unravel the possible degradation pathways of perovskite films, the XRD patterns of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films as a function of storage time under ambient conditions are displayed in Fig. 4.8 The MAPbI3 film gradually decomposed and started to give rise to a slight signal of PbI2 after 4 day storage in air, and then the intensity of the PbI2 peak increased from 4 to 60 days, which illustrates evident degradation of the MAPbI3 film consistent with the SEM images. However, the XRD patterns of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 films (n = 20, 10, and 6) remained unchanged in air up to 60 days, in which no PbI2 peak is observed. These results verify that the introduction of a 2D component into a 3D perovskite effectively prevents the 3D perovskite decomposition and improves its film stability.

Conclusions

In conclusion, we have demonstrated the excellent photovoltaic performance with PCEs over 11.88% and superior ambient stability, thermal stability and photo-stability of 2Dâ€“3D hybrid (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 C-PSCs. By tuning the stoichiometry of (EA)2(MA)nâˆ’1PbnI3n+1 to n = 6, the surface quality as well as the ambient and photo-stability of n6-2D (EA)2(MA)5Pb6I19 films are extremely improved. As a consequence, the n6-2D device maintains a long-term stability of 93% under ambient conditions after 90 days, a thermal stability of 80% after continuously heating at 80 Â°C over 100 hours, and a photo-stability of 92% under continuous 1 sun illumination over 300 hours, far superior to those of the MAPbI3 device. To the best of our knowledge, our fabricated C-PSC with the 2Dâ€“3D lead halide photoabsorber exhibits the best ambient-air-stable performance among all low-temperature carbon electrode-based PSCs reported so far. Our work provides a simple and operational avenue to advance perovskite devices toward commercial applications.
