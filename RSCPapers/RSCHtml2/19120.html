Abstract

Ferric oxide (Fe2O3) is an attractive anode material for lithium-ion batteries (LIBs) with a high theoretical capacity of 1005 mA h gâˆ’1. However, its practical application is greatly restrained by the rapid capacity fading caused by the large volume expansion upon lithiation. To address this issue, we have designed and synthesized a unique yolkâ€“shell Fe2O3/N-doped carbon hybrid structure (YS-Fe2O3@NC) with rich oxygen vacancies for robust lithium storage. The obtained results show that YS-Fe2O3@NC delivers a high reversible capacity of 578 mA h gâˆ’1 after 300 cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1, about 11 times that (53.7 mA h gâˆ’1) of pristine Fe2O3. Furthermore, a high specific capacity of 300.5 mA h gâˆ’1 even at 10 A gâˆ’1 is achieved. The high reversible capacities, excellent rate capability and cycle stability of YS-Fe2O3@NC might be attributed to the elaborate yolkâ€“shell nanoarchitecture. Moreover, electron percolation and a local built-in electric field induced by oxygen vacancies in the Fe2O3 matrix could also enhance the kinetics of Li+ insertion/deinsertion.

1. Introduction

For decades, LIBs with high energy and power density have attracted increasing attention owing to their potential applications in large-scale energy storage, backup power devices and hybrid electric vehicles.1â€“3 Nanostructured transition metal oxides are considered as promising candidates as anode materials for high energy and power density LIBs because of their multiple chemical valence states and various morphological characteristics.4,5 Among numerous anode materials, low-cost and eco-friendly Fe2O3 has been regarded as a promising anode material for LIBs with a high theoretical capacity of 1005 mA h gâˆ’1.6,7 Unfortunately, the intrinsically low Li+ mobility, poor electrical conductivity and drastic volume change still remain as challenges that limit their high-rate electrochemical performance in practical applications.8,9 In order to solve these problems, lots of effective strategies have been developed including tuning the structure and morphology at the nanoscale or using carbon-based materials as the buffer layer.10â€“12 One effective strategy is to synthesize and design yolkâ€“shell nanostructured Fe2O3-based nanomaterials.13,14 With meticulous care design, the hollow interiors can provide enough space to accommodate the intense volume expansion upon lithiation. In addition, the cavity wall provides a short diffusion path, which also enhances the transport of lithium ions. For example, Gu15et al. reported hierarchical nanotubes of Î±-Fe2O3 as a high performance anode for LIBs, exhibiting a stable capacity of 764.2 mA h gâˆ’1 after 500 cycles at 0.5 A gâˆ’1. Zhang16et al. proposed triple-shell hollow Î±-Fe2O3 microspheres as anodes for LIBs, which retained a stable capacity of 903.6 mA h gâˆ’1 after 1000 cycles at a current density of 0.5 A gâˆ’1. Although these strategies could help to facilitate ion transport and release the volume expansion for a partly improved electrochemical performance for LIBs, their lithium storage still suffers from the drawback of poor conductivity.

Vacancies usually impact the physicochemical properties of many semiconducting materials.17,18 Vacancies, which could effectively regulate the electronic structure without fundamentally changing the pristine lattice, would unlock the potential for optimizing the performance of LIBs, especially high-rate capability.19,20 Oxygen vacancies in transition metal oxides are significant owing to their role as electron donors.21 For example, Ma et al.22 reported a yolk@shell structure of TiO2@Fe2O3 with oxygen vacancies for LIBs. These oxygen vacancies in Fe2O3 served as shallow donors, and effectively improved the electronic properties of Fe2O3 nanostructures, leading to excellent rate capability and superior cycling stability. The oxygen-vacancies in the Fe2O3 matrix seem to be a non-negligible factor for improving Li+ kinetics in terms of percolation charge transfer and the local built-in electric field. It is believed that the synergetic design of the yolkâ€“shell structure and lattice defects in metal oxides would be a new avenue for improving the electrochemical performances of rechargeable ion batteries in terms of rate capability and cycling stability.

Based on the above analysis, we have designed and synthesized a unique yolkâ€“shell Fe2O3/N-doped carbon with rich oxygen vacancies for robust lithium storage. The as-prepared samples exhibited high reversible capacity, excellent rate capability and ultralong cyclic stability as anode materials for LIBs. When evaluated as an anode for LIBs, the composites delivered a high specific capacity of 300.5 mA h gâˆ’1 at 10 A gâˆ’1 after 1000 cycles with coulombic efficiency more than 99%. The as-prepared YS-Fe2O3@NC can be a promising candidate for Li+ storage.

2.5 Cell fabrication and characterization

The electrochemical performances are evaluated using CR2025 half-cells with the Fe2O3 or YS-Fe2O3@NC anode electrode coated on copper foil and lithium foil as a counter electrode. The anode slurry is prepared by mixing 70 wt% active materials with 15 wt% CMC (sodium carboxymethyl cellulose) and 15 wt% super-P in a solvent (deionized water). The slurry is then cast onto a copper foil and subsequently dried at 110 Â°C in vacuum for 12 hours. The resulted laminate is punched into a 12 mm diameter disc and pressed under a pressure of 2.5 MPa. The loading density of Fe2O3 or YS-Fe2O3@NC is âˆ¼1.3 mg cmâˆ’2. Electrochemical cells are assembled in an Ar-filled glove box, in which O2 and H2O contents are below 0.01 ppm, using a microporous polypropylene membrane as the separator. The electrolyte is 1 M LiPF6 in a mixture of diethyl carbonate membrane (DEC)â€“dimethyl carbonate (DMC)â€“ethylene carbonate (EC) (1:1:1 in vol. ratio) as the electrolyte. The galvanostatic charge/discharge measurements are carried out on a LAND test system (LAND CT2001A 1U). The cyclic voltammograms (CV) and electrochemical impedance spectra (EIS) for the prepared anodes are measured in a voltage window of 0.01â€“3.0 V on a Zahner Zennium IM6 electrochemical workstation.

The cycling stability of the Fe2O3 and YS-Fe2O3@NC electrodes was first explored by galvanostatic charging/discharging in a voltage window of 0.01â€“3.0 V for the 10th, 50th, 100th and 200th cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1. Work function (WF) defined as the minimum energy required for the loss of an electron from the inside of a bulk material to the vacuum, plays a fundamental role in most of the electronic properties of a material.37 Therefore, the smaller the WF, the easier the loss of an electron from the material. The WF of the as-prepared composites are further characterized by KPFM and the work electrode including Fe2O3 and YS-Fe2O3@NC particles is given in Fig. 7(a). Fig. 7(bâ€“d) present the surface potential images over a scan area of 200 nm Ã— 200 nm of Fe2O3, YS-Fe2O3@NC and Au foil, respectively. According to our previous work,38 the WFs of Fe2O3 and YS-Fe2O3@NC are calculated and the corresponding results are presented in Fig. 7(e). It is found that YS-Fe2O3@NC has a smaller WF (5.35 eV) than Fe2O3 (5.64 eV). The measured WF of Fe2O3 is close to the reported value (5.6 eV).39 The lowed WF induced by the N-doped carbon shell could be explained phenomenologically based on the energy-band model (Fig. 8).30 Smaller WF requires less energy for electrons to escape from the materials. Therefore, the N-doped carbon shell is expected to facilitate electron transfer in the composite, which is consistent with the analysis of EIS measurements. The excellent electrochemical performance in terms of rate capability and cycling stability can be ascribed to the synergistic effects of the morphology, size, structural stability, specific surface area and chemical compositions. First, the nanosize (300 nm) shortens the Li+ diffusion paths, provides many active sites for Li+ storage, reduces the electrode polarization and therefore enhances the kinetics of the Li+ uptake/release. Second, the N-doped carbon layer offers a high-conductivity electron transport pathway and decreases the internal resistance among Fe2O3 particles. Third, the N-doped carbon shell, which serves as a self-supporting framework, can allow for the remission of the aggregation of Fe2O3 particles. Moreover, the large void space could relieve the volume change and prevent the Fe2O3 spindles from aggregation, sustaining voids for facile penetration of the electrolyte and improving cycling stability. This N-doped carbon shell is well maintained even when Fe2O3 expands during the lithiation processes, thanks to the enough void space of the yolkâ€“shell structure.

On the other hand, oxygen-vacancies in the Fe2O3 lattices also play crucial roles in enhancing Li-ion kinetics based on percolation charge transfer and the localized built-in electric field. Due to the imbalance in charge distribution induced by oxygen vacancies, a positively charged area (Î´+) in the oxygen-vacancy center and an equivalent negatively charged electron area surrounding the oxygen vacancies are correspondingly formed, as demonstrated in Fig. 9(a). When oxygen-vacancy concentration exceeds the percolation threshold, the negatively-charged electron area surrounding the oxygen vacancies would overlap to form a charge transfer percolation-channel in the Fe2O3 matrix (Fig. 9(b)), facilitating electron transfer via the electron-jump model and consequently improving rate capability. It is expected that the lopsided charge distribution leads to the central region of oxygen vacancies being positively-charged and the region around oxygen vacancies being negatively-charged.40 Therefore, the localized built-in electric field correspondingly forms pointing from the defect-free region to the negatively-charged overlapped-chain, which accelerates Li-ion migration towards to the oxygen-vacancy region because of the coulombic forces shown in Fig. 9(c). In deep lithiation, the negatively-charged region became electrically neutral. During the delithiation process, the secondary electric field, pointing from the positively-charged oxygen-vacancy to the electroneutral lithiation layer, would also facilitate Li-ion extraction in the Fe2O3 matrix and across heterojunction interfaces, as shown in Fig. 9(d). So, the oxygen vacancies in the Fe2O3 matrix indeed facilitate Li-ion diffusion kinetics during the charge/discharge process. As a result, the obtained YS-Fe2O3@NC reveals excellent lithium storage properties.

4. Conclusions

In summary, we have rationally designed a yolkâ€“shell Fe2O3@carbon (YS-Fe2O3@NC) with rich oxygen vacancies for robust lithium storage. Benefiting from its unique structural features and the existence of oxygen vacancies, YS-Fe2O3@NC shows remarkable rate capability and ultra-long cycling stability. In particular, it delivers a reversible capacity of 578 mA h gâˆ’1 after 300 cycles at a current density of 5 A gâˆ’1, and a satisfactory ultra-long cycle life with a capacity of 300.5 mA h gâˆ’1 after 1000 cycles at 10 A gâˆ’1. This research may open a new avenue for the design and synthesis of Fe2O3-based nanocomposites for high-performance LIBs.
