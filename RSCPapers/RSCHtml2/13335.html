Abstract

Ternary donor (D)â€“acceptor (A)â€“acceptor (A) molecules are commonly considered as low triplet (T1) energy systems for specific applications. In this work, exception to this behavior was observed in a triangle-shaped Dâ€“Aâ€“A molecule PCImbPO with unusually high triplet energy of 3.0 eV. Profiting from the enhanced Dâ€“A electronic coupling, electron injecting and transporting ability of PCImbPO was dramatically improved with negligible influences on its highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) characteristics. 1. Introduction

Optoelectronic organic semiconductors have a bright prospect in information technology1â€“6 and energy conversion fields,7â€“9 owing to their unique superiority in functional design and integration at a molecular level.10 Nevertheless, demanding requirements on optoelectronic performance for practical and commercial applications motivate continuous efforts towards judicious and accurate molecular design with simultaneous consideration of functional groups, linkage modes and intra-/inter-molecular interactions. Commonly, optical properties of organic molecules are mainly determined by excited state characteristics, especially the first singlet and triplet states (S1 and T1),11â€“16 while electrical properties primarily originate from frontier molecular orbitals (FMO) and molecular topological structures.17 These seemingly independent factors are actually correlative, since (i) excited-state energy strongly depends on FMO energy gap and distributions, e.g. low-energy charge-transfer (CT) excited states form in donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) type molecules with separated FMOs; (ii) molecular configuration exerts an important impact on excited state energy and location.18 These correlations reflect the contradiction between optical and electrical properties, which gradually become predominant under increasingly stringent requirements on material performance and has aroused extensive interest in selective photoelectronic modulation approaches.19

Ternary Dâ€“A structures are superior in carrier injection and transportation balance and absorption bathochromic shift, owing to independent and tunable charge affinities of D and A moieties and the formation of low-energy CT excited states,20 which are harmonized for photoactive dyes in organic solar cells.21,22 However, ambipolar characteristics and low-energy triplet excited states become an obstacle for host materials in blue phosphorescent OLEDs (PHOLEDs) and thermally activated delay fluorescence (TADF) devices, since in most cases the low triplet (3CT or 3Ï€Ï€*) energy of the ternary Dâ€“A system can not support efficient triplet energy transfer to blue phosphorescent and TADF dyes such as bis(4,6-difluorophenylpyridinato-N,C2â€²)(picolinato)iridium(III) (FIrpic, T1 = 2.65 eV)23 and bis[4-(9,9-dimethyl-9,10-dihydroacridine)phenyl]sulfone (DMAC-DPS, T1 = 2.90 eV)24 (Fig. 1a, II).23 This leads to a perception that ternary Dâ€“A systems with effective intramolecular electronic coupling can hardly realize high triplet energy approaching 3.0 eV.24â€“34 Nevertheless, owing to their superior electrical properties, efficient phosphorescent Dâ€“A hosts have emerged in recent years,32,35â€“49 even though their intramolecular electronic coupling is commonly suppressed by meta-, insulating or twisted linkages; while, only few Dâ€“A hosts are reported for blue TADF devices.50 Nevertheless, there are very few reports about high-energy-gap ternary Dâ€“A type hosts without any spacers or linkers involved. Actually, if high triplet energy is less of an issue (Fig. 1a, III), ternary Dâ€“A systems are almost ideal as host materials with the independent highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO), lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) and T1 locations, which is conducive to suppressing polarization-induced exciton decomposition (PIED)51 and tripletâ€“polaron quenching (TPQ) effects.52,53 Recently, we reported a Dâ€“Aâ€“D host with a structure of two carbazole donors linked by a phenylphosphine oxide acceptor, which endowed its FIrpic-based blue PHOLEDs with favorable performance owing to its unique dynamically adaptive electrical characteristics.54 Nevertheless, a more in-depth understanding on universal determinants of excited state and FMO properties for ternary Dâ€“A molecules is still imperative.

In this contribution, two triangle-shaped ternary Dâ€“A type carbazoleâ€“benzimidazole hybrids (Fig. 1b), namely PCImbCz and PCImbPO, were constructed with unusual optoelectronic properties. 2.3 Density function theory (DFT) calculations

DFT computations were carried out with different parameters for structure optimizations and vibration analyses. The ground states and triplet states of molecules in vacuum were optimized without any assistance of experimental data by the restricted and unrestricted formalism of Becke's three-parameter hybrid exchange functional56 and Lee, and Yang and Parr correlation functional57 (B3LYP)/6-31G(d), respectively. The fully optimized stationary points were further characterized by harmonic vibrational frequency analysis to ensure that real local minima had been found without imaginary vibrational frequency. The total energies were also corrected by zero-point energy both for the ground state and triplet state. The spin density distributions were visualized with Gaussview 3.0. All computations were performed using the Gaussian 03 package.58

3.2 Optical properties

Particular optical properties of PCImG were investigated with their electronic absorption and emission spectra shown in CH2Cl2 (Fig. 3). PCImG indicated similar absorption bands, among which the bands corresponding to intramolecular CT (ICT) transitions of PCImbCz and PCImbPO at 300â€“350 nm were remarkably stronger than those of their Ïƒ-blocked congeners, owing to more effective Dâ€“A electronic coupling. Consequently, according to absorption edges, band gaps of PCImbCz and PCImbPO are smaller than those of PCImpCz and PCImpPO. Directly linked Dâ€“Aâ€“A structure endowed PCImbPO with the lowest 1CT energy of 3.2 eV, which was coincident to the longest emission wavelength of its fluorescence (FL) spectrum (Fig. 3 and Table 1). The improved ambipolar characteristic of PCImbPO was further affirmed by the largest bathochromic shift of its emissions in polar solvents, compared to other derivatives (Fig. S2, ESIâ€ ). Simultaneously, the asymmetric triangle configurations of PCImG suppressed the intermolecular interactions, which gave rise to their almost unchanged FL spectra in thin films (Fig. S3, ESIâ€ ).

3.3 Electrical properties

Density function theory (DFT) simulation on ground and triplet states of PCImG was performed to quantify the nature of selective modulation effect of their secondary donor or acceptor groups on optoelectronic properties (Fig. 4 and Fig. S5, ESIâ€ ). For PCImpCz and PCImpPO, their Ïƒ-linkers effectively block electronic communication between PCIm and the pendent carbazole and triphenylphosphine oxide (TPPO). Their 3Ï€Ï€*-attributive T1 states, HOMOs and LUMOs are mainly localized and almost completely overlapped on carbazole of PCIm without any contributions from their secondary donor or acceptor. PCImbCz and PCImbPO reveal identical HOMO locations to those of their Ïƒ-blocked analogues. However, for PCImbCz, its LUMO shifts to the N-phenylimidazole moiety, accompanied with minor contribution from one phenyl of carbazole in PCIm. The involvement of active secondary acceptor in PCImbPO further induces its LUMO to be completely localized in the imidazoleâ€“TPPO segment, which is consistent with the strongest electronic coupling in the Dâ€“Aâ€“A structure as proved by optical analysis of PCImbPO. Simultaneously, steric hindrance of rigid pendent groups in PCImbCz and PCImbPO gives rise to approximately planar PCIm at the T1 state with remarkably reduced dihedral angle of its carbazole and benzimidazole, which enhances conjugation in PCIm to generate the 3CT-type triplet state. Therefore, different with their Ïƒ-blocked congeners, T1 states of PCImbCz and PCImbPO are mainly localized on benzimidazoles rather than carbazoles of PCIm. It is convincing that as designed, compared with all of carbazoleâ€“imidazole based Dâ€“Aâ€“D and Dâ€“Aâ€“A congeners (Fig. S6, ESIâ€ ), PCImbPO achieves the lowest LUMO and the most separated FMO and triplet state locations, establishing independent charge and energy transfer channels for PIED and TPQ suppression.

The superiority of ternary Dâ€“A structures in modulating charge injection and transportation was experimentally verified with cyclic voltammetric analysis and the nominal single-carrier transporting devices of PCImG (Fig. 5). 4. Conclusions

Selective optoelectronic modulation of ternary Dâ€“A systems through manipulating T1 configuration and controlling Dâ€“A electronic coupling was demonstrated by a TPPO substituted carbazoleâ€“benzimidazole hybrid PCImbPO. Its unique triangle-shaped Dâ€“Aâ€“A configuration results in an unusually high T1 value of 3.0 eV, identical to its Ïƒ-blocked congeners. The enhanced electronic coupling in the Dâ€“Aâ€“A structure gives rise to notable electron-dominant characteristics of PCImbPO, verifying the inherent advantage of Dâ€“A systems in electrical property tunability, which further renders separated FMO and T1 locations for quenching effect suppression. Therefore, an effective selective optimization approach for ternary Dâ€“A hosts was established to enhance electrical performance without any negative effects on optical properties. Consequently, PCImbPO endowed its blue phosphorescent and TADF OLEDs with the favorable performances, e.g. maximum EQE of >20% and 12%, respectively. With persuasive evidence of achievement of high triplet energy and facile control of triplet state and electronic properties through ternary Dâ€“A systems, this work not only establishes a new understanding about the determining characteristics for optimization of optoelectronic characteristics of Dâ€“A systems, but also provides a pathway to develop high-energy-gap ternary or even more complicated Dâ€“A organic semiconductors for potential applications.
