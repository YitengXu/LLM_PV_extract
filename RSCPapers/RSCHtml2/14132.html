Abstract

Excellent performance and multifunctional electrocatalysts are of great importance for the development of renewable energy. Here, a multifunctional carbon cloth supported cobalt phosphide (CoP@CC) electrocatalyst was synthesized via a simple electrochemical deposition followed by subsequent phosphorization. This self-standing CoP@CC presents superior HER performance in both acid and alkaline media and excellent OER activity in an alkaline environment. When applied as both anode and cathode catalyst to overall water splitting, CoP@CC only required a low cell voltage of 1.68 V to drive a current density of 10 mA cmâˆ’2, making this catalyst a potential candidate in industrial application. Moreover, inspired by its satisfying ORR activity, CoP@CC was first used as the air-electrode in zincâ€“air batteries, which outputted an open-circuit potential of 0.6 V and a power density of âˆ¼30 mW cmâˆ’2.

Introduction

Faced with the global energy crisis, researchers have devoted great efforts to investigate excellent systems for energy conversion and storage.1,2 Electrocatalysis is a convenient and environmentally friendly approach to develop renewable and sustainable energy.3â€“5 For example, the electrochemical hydrogen evolution reaction (HER) is an important reaction for hydrogen generation, while electrochemical oxygen evolution reaction (OER) and oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) are the two indispensable reactions to rechargeable metalâ€“air batteries.6,7

The progress of electrocatalysis, however, generally suffers from sluggish reaction kinetics due to the lack of superior non-Pt electrocatalysts. To combat this problem, great efforts have been made to exploit high-performance noble-metal-free electrocatalysts.8,9 Transition metal dichalcogenides and their corresponding composites have been widely investigated for HER.10,11 MoS2/CoSe2 obtained by the hydrothermal method, for instance, exhibits an excellent HER performance with a low onset potential of âˆ’11 mV and a low Tafel slope of 36 mV decâˆ’1, which is comparable to the commercial Pt/C catalyst.12 As for OER and ORR, most attention is now focused on transition metal oxides or doped carbon materials. The gelled FeCoW oxy-hydroxide shows the lowest overpotential of 191 mV reported at 10 mA cmâˆ’2 and robust stability for OER.13 Dong et al. synthesized Fe and N co-doped carbon frameworks derived from self-assembled Fe3O4 nanocube superlattices. This Feâ€“Nâ€“C electrocatalyst transcends commercial Pt/C and many previous doped carbon materials for ORR.14

Although an abundance of electrocatalysts with excellent performance has been found and designed for applications in the area of electrocatalysis, most of them just possess a single functional property, which can hardly satisfy the needs for the development of sustainable energy systems.15,16 Searching for electrocatalysts with multifunctional characters that can be applied to various fields, such as HER, OER and ORR, seems to be the trend for the future.17,18 Recently, transition metal phosphides have attracted intense attention because of their superior electrocatalytic performance and multifunctional properties.19,20

In this paper, a multifunctional electrocatalyst, carbon cloth supported cobalt phosphide (CoP@CC), was synthesized via a simple electrochemical deposition followed by a subsequent in situ thermal phosphorization. With the support of a carbon cloth, the CoP@CC can be used directly as electrodes. Compared with the hydrothermal method that usually costs several hours for growing CoP precursors on carbon cloth, electrochemical deposition is easy to operate within tens of minutes.

The as-synthesized CoP@CC shows superior HER performance in both acid and alkaline media with low overpotentials, small Tafel slopes and high stabilities. Meanwhile, it also exhibits an excellent OER activity in alkaline media. Inspired by its good electrocatalytic performances for both HER and OER in an alkaline environment, the integrated CoP@CC was directly used as the electrodes for overall water splitting. As expected, the CoP@CC shows a satisfactory activity for water splitting. Moreover, here we first applied this self-standing CoP@CC electrode as the air-electrode to zincâ€“air batteries and investigated its corresponding performance.

Experimental

Materials

CoCl2Â·6H2O was purchased from Sinopharm Chemical Reagent Co., Ltd. Commercial carbon cloth (CC) was bought from PHYCHEMi Co. Ltd. NaH2PO2Â·H2O was purchased from Sinopharm Chemical Reagent Co., Ltd. The commercial Pt/C catalysts were purchased from Alfa Aesar Co. (platinum, nominally 20 wt% on carbon black) and the commercial RuO2 was supplied by Aladdin Industrial Co. Other corresponding materials were of analytical reagent grade and used without any further purification. Redistilled water was used during the whole experiment.

Synthesis of Co(OH)2@CC

Co(OH)2@CC was prepared via an electrochemical-deposition method in a three electrode cell, in which saturated calomel electrode (SCE), carbon rod and CC were used as the reference electrode, the counter electrode and the working electrode, respectively. Prior to electrochemical-deposition, a piece of CC (1 Ã— 3 cm2) was rinsed by acetone, ethanol and redistilled water under ultrasonic treatment each for 15 min, respectively. Afterwards, the piece of CC was hydrophilically treated by nitric acid and washed with redistilled water. Then the piece of CC was immersed in 25 mL CoCl2Â·6H2O (0.05 M) electrolyte acting as the working electrode with an immersed area of 1 Ã— 2 cm2. Finally, Co(OH)2 grew on the CC through electrochemical deposition under a potential of âˆ’1.0 V vs. SCE for 20 min.

Synthesis of CoP@CC

CoP@CC was prepared by a thermal phosphorization. In brief, NaH2PO2Â·H2O (300 mg) was located at the upstream side of a tube furnace and a piece of CC grown with Co(OH)2 was placed at the downstream side. Then the whole samples were heated to 300 Â°C for 1 h at the rate of 5 Â°C minâˆ’1 with Ar gas flowing at a constant rate of 200 s.c.c.m. (cubic centimeters per minute at a standard temperature and pressure). The mass of CoP was determined to be âˆ¼3 mg by comparing the weight of pure carbon cloth and the corresponding carbon cloth after phosphorization. For Pt/C@CC, 3 mg 20 wt% commercial Pt/C was dispersed in ethanol and loaded on the carbon cloth with the area of 1 Ã— 2 cm2. RuO2@CC was prepared using the same method as for Pt/C@CC. Co3O4@CC was synthesized by directly annealing Co(OH)2 in air at 400 Â°C for 2 h.

Synthesis of pure CoP powder

The CoP powder was synthesized via a simple method. In brief, firstly, Co(OH)2 was prepared by dropping 1 M NaOH into CoCl2Â·6H2O solution. Then the Co(OH)2 precipitate was washed with redistilled water several times and dried at 60 Â°C for further use. Finally, the CoP was obtained via subsequent phosphorization which is the same procedure as that of the synthesis of CoP@CC except that the molar ratio for Co:P is controlled to be at 1:10.

Characterization

X-ray powder diffraction (XRD, Philips X'pert PRO MPD diffractometer) equipped with Cu KÎ± radiation (Î» = 0.15406 nm) was used to study the structure of the as-prepared samples. The morphology and element distribution of the samples were obtained by using a scanning electron microscope (Zeiss, Supra 55) equipped with energy-dispersive X-ray spectrometry (EDS) with an acceleration voltage of 30 kV. Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) and high resolution transmission electron microscopy (HRTEM) measurements were carried out via the TEM (FEI Tecnai G2 F20 S-TWIN) under an accelerating voltage of 200 kV. Electrochemical measurements

Electrochemical measurements were carried out on a CHI 760 D electrochemical workstation with a conventional three-electrode glass cell. A saturated calomel electrode (SCE, in acidic media) or mercuric oxide electrode (in alkaline media) was used as the reference electrode and a carbon rod as the counter electrode. CoP@CC was directly used as the working electrode with a geometrical area of 1 Ã— 2 cm2. For the control experiment, the electrocatalytic performance of pure CoP powder was also determined. In brief, 4 mg of pure CoP powder were dispersed in 1 mL of isopropanolâ€“water (v:v = 1:5) mixed solution including 100 Î¼L Nafion solution (0.5 wt%) and ultrasonically treated for at least 2 h to obtain a homogeneous suspension. Then, 5 Î¼L of the homogeneous suspension was extracted and added onto a glassy carbon electrode (GC) with a diameter of 3 mm (âˆ¼0.258 mg cmâˆ’2).

All the electrochemical activities were studied at room temperature. The HER performance was tested in 0.5 M H2SO4 and 1 M KOH electrolytes. The OER performance was tested in a 1 M KOH electrolyte. Prior to measurements, the electrolytes were purged with Ar gas for 10 min to remove oxygen. The electrocatalytic performances of each catalyst were investigated using linear sweep voltammetry (LSV) at a scan rate of 5 mV sâˆ’1. The stability of the catalysts was tested by chronopotentiometry. The ORR performance was evaluated on a rotating disk electrode (RDE) at a rotation speed of 1600 rpm in 0.1 M KOH. Prior to the test, the electrolyte was bubbled with O2 for 30 min and O2 gas was maintained to flow over the electrolyte during the test. The zincâ€“air battery test was performed in 6 M KOH with CoP@CC and a piece of Zn foil (30 Ã— 30 Ã— 0.1 mm3) as two electrodes, respectively. To facilitate comparison, the onset potential in this study is measured at a current density of âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2 (for HER) or 1 mA cmâˆ’2 (for OER).21

The HER activity

The electrocatalytic activities of CoP@CC were carefully investigated. Fig. 2aâ€“c exhibit the HER performance of CoP@CC operated with a standard three-electrode electrochemical cell in acid media (0.5 M H2SO4), together with pure CC and Pt/C@CC for comparison. As expected, the carbon cloth displays a negligible HER activity, while Pt/C@CC undeniably exhibits state-of-the-art performance (Fig. 2a). Compared with Pt/C@CC, the as-synthesized CoP@CC represents a comparative HER performance with an onset potential of âˆ’44 mV at a current density of âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2 which is close to that of Pt/C@CC (âˆ’37 mV@âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2). Besides, it also requires a low overpotential of 131 mV to drive the current density of âˆ’10 mA cmâˆ’2, the significant value for driving solar fuel conversion. Such excellent HER performance of CoP@CC can rival most other non-Pt based HER catalysts.26â€“28

The Tafel slope is widely used to study the intrinsic properties of an electrocatalyst in HER. Fig. 2b displays the Tafel plots derived from Fig. 2a. Pt/C@CC shows a Tafel slope of 30 mV decâˆ’1 which is similar to that of commercial Pt/C, illustrating a Volmerâ€“Tafel mechanism. The electrochemical stability is also a key factor that appraises the HER performance of a catalyst. Fig. 2c shows the Vâ€“t curves of CoP@CC in acidic electrolytes. Even after long-term testing (100000 s) at the current density of âˆ’10 mA cmâˆ’2, the CoP@CC can retain a high efficiency with negligible changes.

A highly efficient HER performance in alkaline media is of importance because alkaline water electrolysis has been widely operated in industrial application recently. The HER performances of pure CC, Co3O4@CC, Co(OH)2@CC, CoP@CC and Pt/C@CC were also studied in 1 M KOH. As shown in Fig. 2d, Pt/C@CC still represents an excellent activity in alkaline media with an onset potential of âˆ’33 mV at the current density of âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2. Likewise, the as-prepared CoP@CC also demonstrates a superior HER activity in 1 M KOH which is almost the same as that in 0.5 M H2SO4. The onset potential of CoP@CC is as small as âˆ’86 mV@âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2, much lower than that of Co3O4@CC (âˆ’252 mV@âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2) and Co(OH)2@CC (âˆ’249 mV@âˆ’1 mA cmâˆ’2). According to Fig. 2f, CoP@CC can work efficiently for 100000 s (>27 h) in alkaline media at the current density of âˆ’10 mA cmâˆ’2. To further identify the superior HER activity of CoP@CC, the HER activity of pure CoP powder was also studied (as shown in Fig. S5â€ ). Based on the above investigations, it is concluded that the as-synthesized CoP@CC exhibits a superior HER activity in both acidic and alkaline electrolytes. Moreover, this self-standing electrode is easy to operate and thus may have great prospects in industrial hydrogen evolution.

The OER activity

The OER performance of CoP@CC was further investigated with a standard three-electrode electrochemical cell in 1 M KOH. Fig. 3a illustrates the LSV curves of pure CC, Co3O4@CC, Co(OH)2@CC, CoP@CC and RuO2@CC. Both the OER performances of Co3O4@CC and Co(OH)2@CC are barely satisfactory in alkaline media. CoP@CC exhibits an excellent OER activity, which is better than that of RuO2@CC. As shown in Fig. 3b, the onset potential of CoP@CC is as low as 1.35 V at 1 mA cmâˆ’2, which is much lower than that of RuO2@CC (1.56 V@1 mA cmâˆ’2). As the OER polarization curve of CoP/CC might possess high capacitive current density and lead to low onset potential, the curve was also recorded at a low scan rate of 1 mV sâˆ’1 for comparison, as shown in Fig. S6.â€  The onset potential of CoP@CC is then determined to be 1.41 V (@1 mA cmâˆ’2), also showing a small value.

The excellent electrocatalytic activity of CoP@CC is perhaps due to the following reasons: (1) the crisscrossing nanosheets make a closer contact with the electrolyte which enables the efficient utilization of activity sites; (2) the direct growth of CoP on carbon cloth without any binders guarantees its good electrical conductivity, ensuring the high charge transfer, namely the primary factor for electrocatalysis; and (3) the carbon cloth supporter avoids the aggregation of CoP nanosheets which provides sufficient activity sites.

Conclusions

In summary, a multifunctional carbon cloth supported CoP was prepared via a simple and fast electrochemical-deposition combined with subsequent phosphorization. This method could serve as a universal route to synthesize various transition metal phosphides supported on versatile conductive carriers. The prepared CoP@CC achieved an excellent electrocatalytic performance for both HER and OER, and thus could be used directly as the electrodes for overall water splitting in alkaline media. Besides, the CoP@CC self-standing electrode was first used as the air electrode in zincâ€“air batteries and exhibited good performance. Our investigation may provide a simple and universal strategy to fabricate multifunctional electrocatalysts, which may have potential applications in industry.
