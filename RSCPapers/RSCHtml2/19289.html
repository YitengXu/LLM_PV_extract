Abstract

A series of two-dimension-conjugated Dâ€“Ï€â€“A copolymers based on benzodithiophene (BDT) or benzodifuran (BDF) as the donor unit, benzothiadiazole (BT) as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the Ï€-bridge, were designed and synthesized for application as donor materials in polymer solar cells (PSCs). The polymers include PBDTT-BT based on BDT with a thiophene side chain, PBDTF-BT based on BDT with a furan side chain, PBDFT-BT based on BDF with a thiophene side chain and PBDFF-BT based on BDF with a furan side chain. The replacement of some of the thiophene rings in the donor unit with furan rings influences the UV-vis absorption spectra, electronic energy levels and band gaps of the copolymers. The HOMO levels of the copolymers based on BDF are ca. 0.1 eV up-shifted in comparison with that of the polymers based on BDT. While the furan side chain makes the LUMO level shift downwards a little. Power conversion efficiency (PCE) of the PSCs based on the polymers as the donor and [6,6]-phenyl C71-butyric acid methyl ester (PC71BM) as the acceptor reached 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT. The results indicate that the replacement of thiophene rings by furan rings in the 2-D-conjugated BDT unit significantly influences the photovoltaic properties of the copolymers.

Introduction

Bulk heterojunction (BHJ) polymer solar cells (PSCs) have been intensively studied in recent years, due to their potential applications in large area, lightweight and flexible photovoltaic panels. Great effort has been devoted to the design and synthesis of new conjugated polymer donor materials1â€“9 and new fullerene derivative acceptor materials10â€“13 for improving the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of PSCs, and over 7% power conversion efficiency (PCE) values have been reported by several research groups.2â€“9,11b

Broad absorption with narrower bandgap, relatively lower HOMO energy levels and higher hole mobility are pursued in the molecular design of high efficiency polymer donor materials.1a Dâ€“A copolymerization has been proven to be an efficient way to meet the requirement. Many donor and acceptor building blocks have been used in the design and synthesis of efficient Dâ€“A copolymer photovoltaic materials. Among various donor units in the Dâ€“A copolymers, benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT), with a symmetric and planar conjugated structure, is the most important donor unit in constructing high efficiency polymer donor materials.2,3,5,7,8,14â€“17 Benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]difuran (BDF), which is similar to BDT with its two thiophene rings replaced by two furan rings, was also introduced to polymer donor materials recently.18 In addition, the two-dimension-conjugated (2-D-conjugated) BDT unit with thiophene conjugated side chains is superior to the BDT unit with alkoxy side chains.5,8 For the acceptor units, benzothiadiazole (BT) has been widely used in high efficiency Dâ€“A copolymer photovoltaic materials in PSCs.19â€“21

In this study, we designed and synthesized four 2-D-conjugated Dâ€“Ï€â€“A copolymers based on BDT or BDF with thiophene (T) or furan (F) conjugated side-chains as donor units, BT as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the Ï€-bridge, for investigating the effect of replacing thiophene units with furan units in the BDT donor unit on the photovoltaic properties of the copolymers. The four polymers include PBDTT-BT based on BDT with a thiophene side chain, PBDTF-BT based on BDT with a furan side chain, PBDFT-BT based on BDF with a thiophene side chain and PBDFF-BT based on BDF with a furan side chain, as shown in Fig. 1. The PSCs based on the 2-D-conjugated copolymers as the donor and [6,6]-phenyl C71-butyric acid methyl ester (PC71BM) as the acceptor demonstrated the power conversion efficiencies of 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT, indicating the significant influence of the replacement of thiophene rings by furan rings in the 2-D-conjugated BDT unit.

Synthesis and characterization of the copolymers

The synthesis route of the monomers and corresponding polymers are outlined in Scheme 1. It is important to note that the alkyl side chain of the polymers plays a critical role in determining the solubility and photovoltaic performance of the polymers.16b In this work, the thiophene Ï€-bridge in the main chain of the polymers with different alkyl (hexyl and 2-ethylhexyl) chains was used in the synthesis of the Dâ€“Ï€â€“A copolymers. It was found that the thiophene Ï€-bridge with a hexyl chain led to poor solubility of the copolymers. The influence of the replacement of the thiophene ring in the BDT unit with furan on the HOMO energy levels of the copolymers could be explained as follows. The five-member ring of furan shows less steric hindrance to adjacent units, because the diameter of the oxygen atom is smaller than that of the sulfur atom.30 The copolymers based on the BDF unit possess an even better planar structure and thus a better conjugated backbone than that of the copolymers based on the BDT unit. Therefore, the bandgap of the copolymers based on BDF is decreased and their HOMO energy level could be up-shifted.

Conclusion

In summary, we designed and synthesized four 2-D-conjugated Dâ€“Ï€â€“A copolymers PBDTT-BT, PBDTF-BT, PBDFT-BT and PBDFF-BT based on BDT or BDF with thiophene (T) or furan (F) conjugated side-chain as the donor units, BT as the acceptor unit and thiophene as the Ï€-bridge. These polymers showed lower energy bandgaps, good solubility in common organic solvents and good thermal stability. The copolymers based on BDF possess a lower bandgap and broader absorption, but a higher HOMO energy level. The PCE of the PSCs based on the polymers as the donor and PC71BM as acceptor with a donorâ€“acceptor weight ratio of 1:1 reached 1.85% for PBDTT-BT, 2.88% for PBDTF-BT, 4.42% for PBDFT-BT and 2.60% for PBDFF-BT, respectively. Obviously, PBDFT-BT based on the BDF unit with thiophene conjugated side chains shows the best photovoltaic performance, which could be as a benefit of the broad absorption and higher hole mobility (9.0 Ã— 10âˆ’3 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1) of PBDFT-BT. These results indicate that BDFT (BDF with thiophene conjugated side chains) is a promising new donor unit in constructing high efficiency Dâ€“Ï€â€“A copolymer donor materials for PSCs.
