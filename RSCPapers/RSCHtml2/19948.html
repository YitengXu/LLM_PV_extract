Abstract

Perylene-based orthogonal triads were synthesized by attaching two identical electron donor units, i.e., phenothiazine (PTZ, Pery-2PTZ) and phenoxazine (PXZ, Pery-2PXZ), to investigate symmetry breaking spinâ€“orbit charge transfer intersystem crossing (SOCT-ISC). The ISC efficiency was highly dependent on the solvent polarity, which was the maximum in solvents with medium polarity (singlet oxygen quantum yield Î¦Î” = 46â€“70%) and higher than that of the previously reported Pery-PTZ/Pery-PXZ dyads (Î¦Î” = 20â€“60% in DCM). Femtosecond transient absorption spectroscopy confirmed the symmetry-breaking charge transfer (SBCT) process in these compact electron donorâ€“acceptorâ€“donor (Dâ€“Aâ€“D) triads, where charge separation (CS) occurred in ca. 0.17â€“0.27 ps, followed by SOCT-ISC within 1.0â€“2.6 ns, which are faster than those in the previously reported analogue dyads (CS, 0.25â€“0.49 ps and ISC, 0.99â€“5.98 ns). An exceptionally long-lived triplet state was observed for the triads in DCM solution (Ï„T = 359 Î¼s), in glycerol triacetate (Ï„T = 1.62â€“1.83 ms) and in polymer films (Ï„T = 4.03â€“5.0 ms). The low-lying long-lived 3CT state of the triad in polar solvent was confirmed by an intermolecular photosensitizing method. Solvent-dependent, tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (TTA)-based p-type delayed fluorescence was observed for the triads (luminescence lifetime = 114 Î¼s under the specific experimental conditions). The triads were also used as efficient triplet photosensitizers for the generation of delayed fluorescence with solvent Green-5 as the triplet acceptor and a long luminescence lifetime up to 94.8 Î¼s was observed.

Introduction

Triplet photosensitizers (PSs) are compounds that generate triplet excited state efficiently upon photoexcitation. These compounds have important applications in photocatalysis,1â€“4 photovoltaics,5,6 tripletâ€“triplet annihilation (TTA) upconversion,7â€“10 photodynamic therapy (PDT)11â€“14 and artificial photosynthesis.15,16 Regarding these applications, strong light absorption, efficient intersystem crossing (ISC), and long-lived triplet state are the desirable photophysical properties for triplet PSs. Their ISC ability can be enhanced through various approaches, including the heavy atom effect (Br, I, Pt, Ru, etc.),11,17â€“19 exciton coupling20,21 and use of an electron spin converter.22,23 However, for the conventional heavy atom effect-based triplet PSs, their triplet state lifetimes is usually shortened, whereas longer triplet state lifetimes are beneficial for applications of triplet PSs.24â€“27 Furthermore, all these strategies suffer from some drawbacks, i.e., high cost, toxicity and complicated synthesis.

Considering this, triplet population via charge recombination (CR) in electron donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) systems has attracted considerable attention.28â€“31 However, in these molecules, the electron donors and acceptors are usually connected with long linkers, and hence the electronic coupling between the donor and acceptor is small, resulting in a small electron exchange integral (J < 0.01 cmâˆ’1), i.e., there is a small energy gap between the 1CT and 3CT states. In these systems, the 3CT state can be populated by the radical pair ISC (RP-ISC) mechanism, and then via internal conversion, the locally excited (3LE) state is populated, given that it has lower energy than the 3CT state. The main driving force in RP-ISC systems is the hyperfine coupling interaction (HFI) and the corresponding slow ISC (usually on the nanosecond timescale) makes this molecular structural motif inappropriate for the design of heavy atom-free triplet PSs. Due to the long spacer between the donor and acceptor, the synthesis of these molecules is also tricky. Therefore, it is still a challenge to develop heavy atom-free triplet PSs possessing simple molecular structures and high ISC efficiency.

Alternatively, in compact electron Dâ€“A dyads/triads, RP-ISC is inhibited due to the large electronic coupling between the donor and acceptor moieties, i.e., larger J value and 1CT/3CT state energy gap. However, the alignment of the electron donor and acceptor in an orthogonal geometry will enhance the population of the triplet excited state through the spinâ€“orbit charge transfer ISC (SOCT-ISC) mechanism.32â€“37 In this process, the CR between the donor and acceptor produces a large change in the molecular orbit angular momentum. This change in momentum produces a torque, which causes electron spin flipping/rephasing, and the molecular orbital angular momentum offsets the electron spin angular momentum change, hence enhancing the ISC. The effective ISC makes compact electron Dâ€“A systems efficient triplet PSs. Thus, chromophores such as anthracene,33,36 Bodipy,32,38,39 naphthalimide and naphthalenediimide37,40â€“42 have been used for the design of compact Dâ€“A dyads undergoing SOCT-ISC as triplet PSs. However, to understand the relationship between the SOCT-ISC efficiency and molecular structures, more examples are needed.

Considering this, symmetry-breaking charge transfer (SBCT) is a promising approach for the preparation of heavy atom-free triplet PSs because of the fast charge separation (CS). SBCT is crucial for CS in the photosynthetic reaction center of bacteria.43â€“45 Upon photoexcitation, SBCT can occur when the excited chromophore is surrounded by several identical units (electron donor or acceptor).45â€“49 SBCT has faster CS kinetics than the hetero Dâ€“A dyads. Usually, the SBCT is more favorable in polar solvents, where the CT state can be stabilized. SBCT-based molecular systems have various applications, such as in artificial photosynthesis,45,50 photonics51 and solar cells.52 Photoinduced SBCT has also been observed in multi-chromophoric systems containing two or more identical molecules, e.g., anthracene,53â€“55 perylene,56â€“59 Bodipy60,61 and metallodipyrrins.62,63 In contrast, examples of SBCT systems containing two identical and strong donors are limited.

Using the concept of SBCT together with the orthogonal conformation will be a fascinating approach to design heavy atom-free triplet PSs to exploit SOCT-ISC, which can enhance the CS process and lead to efficient ISC. Recently, numerous studies have been conducted on the photoinduced SOCT-ISC mechanism, which was confirmed by time-resolved electron paramagnetic resonance (TREPR) spectroscopy.35,64â€“66 However, studies on the use of the SBCT approach in compact systems to achieve a longer-lived triplet state has rarely been reported. Thus, new molecular structures are needed to study the role or advantage of introducing SBCT in SOCT-ISC dyads or triads.

Previously, we prepared an orthogonal perylene-phenothiazine (Pery-PTZ) dyad, which showed efficient ISC (Î¦Î” = 60%), and long-lived triplet excited state (Ï„ = 156 Î¼s).35 Similarly, a perylene-phenoxazine dyad (Pery-PXZ) was reported with high ISC efficiency (Î¦Î” = 84%) and long-lived triplet state (Ï„ = 242 Î¼s).67 Taking advantage of these approaches, herein, we investigated the possibility of enhancing the SOCT-ISC with SBCT in compact electron Dâ€“Aâ€“D triads. For this purpose, we prepared two different perylene triads with a connection of two phenothiazines (PTZ) units in Pery-2PTZ and two phenoxazines (PXZ) units in Pery-2PXZ (Scheme 1). We studied the SBCT process in the triads using steady-state and femtosecond/nanosecond transient absorption spectroscopy and theoretical computations. Upon photoexcitation, efficient CR-induced ISC was observed, especially in medium polarity solvents. Interestingly, in Pery-2PTZ, a higher singlet oxygen quantum yield and longer-lived triplet state were observed than in the dyad (Pery-PTZ). Alternatively, one-atom substitution in the electron donor unit in Pery-2PXZ resulted in different optical spectral properties compared to that of Pery-2PTZ.

Results and discussion

Molecular design rationale of the triads and synthesis

We designed Pery-2PTZ and Pery-2PXZ orthogonal triads (Scheme 1), in which PTZ and PXZ moieties were used as two identical electron donors attached at the 3- and 9-positions of the perylene moiety for both triads, respectively. Substitution at the 3,10-positions of perylene is also possible. PTZ is a well-known electron donor (EOX = +0.18 V, Fc/Fc+), but it has a unique puckered structure, while the PXZ moiety (EOX = +0.20 V, Fc/Fc+) has a planar structure. Previous studies showed more significant electronic coupling between the donor and acceptor in Pery-PXZ than Pery-PTZ, and thus we expected the same trend for the triads Pery-2PXZ and Pery-2PTZ.

Most importantly, in these triads, there are two-electron donor units; therefore electron transfer should be faster than in the previously reported dyads.45,58 It has already been proven that the ISC mechanism in Pery-PTZ and Pery-PXZ dyads is SOCT-ISC,35,67 and thus for the current triads a similar ISC mechanism is expected. Pery-PTZ and Pery-PXZ were used as references for the comparison of the photophysical and photochemical study of triads. 3,9-Dibromoperylene (Pery-2Br) was used as an intermediate for the preparation of the triads and as a reference compound to investigate the ISC process (Scheme 1). The synthesis is based on the Buchwaldâ€“Hartwig coupling reaction, the compounds were obtained in satisfactory yield. The molecular structures of all the compounds were verified via1H NMR, 13C NMR and HR-MS spectroscopy (see Experimental section and ESIâ€ ).

This suggests that the charge separation is faster in the triad than in the dyad due to the larger driving force. In addition, these results indicate that the formation of the triplet state through the CR process is thermodynamically allowed in medium polar solvents; however, the driving force for the formation of the triplet state decreases in highly polar solvents due to the lower lying CT state than the 3LE state or the faster CR process towards the ground state. Femtosecond transient absorption spectroscopy (fs-TA): charge separation (CS) and charge recombination (CR) processes

To investigate the excited-state dynamics and confirm the SBCT process in the triads, the femtosecond transient absorption (fs-TA) spectra were measured in solvents with different polarity, including n-HEX, DCM and ACN, at two different excitation wavelengths (400 and 450 nm). Firstly, we measured the spectra of the reference compounds perylene and Pery-2Br (Fig. S15 and S16, respectively, ESIâ€ ), where upon photoexcitation, a strong positive excited state absorption (ESA) band (S1 â†’ Sn transition) centered at about 700 nm and the characteristic ground state bleach (GSB) of perylene at around 440 nm were observed. Moreover, a stimulated emission (SE) band in the range of 460â€“510 nm was observed. No ISC process was observed for perylene, which is in agreement with its strong fluorescence (Î¦F = 94%) and its long fluorescence lifetime (4.6 ns). A triplet ESA band at 500 nm was observed for Pery-2Br due to the heavy atom effect, but its triplet state signal was weak due to the slow ISC.

The fs-TA spectra of Pery-2PTZ measured in non-polar (n-HEX), medium polar (DCM) and high polar solvents (ACN) are presented in Fig. 6. The excitation wavelength was 450 nm, preferably exciting the CT absorption band. We performed transient emission spectroscopy and observed solvent-dependent delayed fluorescence (Fig. 9). It has been previously demonstrated that the delayed fluorescence of compounds can be directly detected using an ns-TA spectrometer in emission mode for compounds with 2ET > ES1.75,80 In the case of Pery-2PTZ, after excitation at 445 nm under an N2 atmosphere, a long-lived emission band was observed at 560 nm (lifetime Ï„DF = 25.8 Î¼s, Fig. 9a and b). This lifetime was 2000-times longer than the prompt fluorescence lifetime measured in n-HEX under deaerated conditions (12.8 ns). Interestingly, in TOL, Pery-2-PTZ showed a red-shifted delayed fluorescence at 630 nm (similar to the CT emission band) with a delayed fluorescence lifetime of 89.7 Î¼s (Fig. 9c and d).

Similar results were observed for Pery-2PXZ, where in n-HEX, its delayed fluorescence band appeared at 550 nm with a lifetime of Ï„DF = 40.2 Î¼s (Fig. S33, ESIâ€ ). In TOL, red-shifted delayed fluorescence was observed at 610 nm with a lifetime of 114 Î¼s. The triads did not show any delayed fluorescence in the polar solvents due to the poor ISC. The observation of solvent-dependent delayed fluorescence with the perylene chromophore has rarely been reported.

Generally, two different mechanisms may lead to delayed fluorescence in organic molecules, i.e., E-type delayed fluorescence and p-type delayed fluorescence. The E-type delayed fluorescence, also known as thermally-induced delayed fluorescence, requires a minimum singlet/triplet energy gap, and thus reverse intersystem crossing (T1 â†’ S1 or T1 â†’ 1CT) is possible. Alternatively, TTA-based delayed fluorescence requires the intermolecular collision of two triplets to form the S1 state. Given that the 1CT/T1 state energy gap is large (0.62â€“0.65 eV), for the triads, the observed delayed fluorescence is most likely via the TTA up-conversion mechanism (P-type delayed fluorescence), which was confirmed by using a viscous solvent (please see the following section). This solvent-dependent P-type delayed fluorescence has not been reported previously for the Pery-PTZ/PXZ dyads.35,67

The efficient ISC and the long triplet lifetimes of the triads make these compounds suitable for various applications. Herein, we applied the triads (Pery-2PTZ and Pery-2PXZ) as triplet PSs for the generation of TTET/TTA-induced delayed fluorescence, with Solvent Green 5 (SG-5) as the energy acceptor/emitter (Fig. 10). TTET and TTA are intermolecular diffusion control processes and should occur on the microsecond timescale in fluid solution at room temperature. For a mixture of Pery-2PTZ with SG-5, upon pulsed laser excitation, the long-lived fluorescence of SG-5 was observed. The kinetics monitored at 510 nm exhibited a biphasic decay feature. The trace of the luminescence contained a growth phase taking 18.3 Î¼s and a decay phase taking 98.4 Î¼s. The rising phase is mainly attributed to the TTET between Pery-2PTZ and SG-5 via the Dexter mechanism, i.e., the accumulation of the SG-5 triplet state. After reaching the maximum concentration, the decay process is dominant, showing an apparent lifetime of 98.4 Î¼s, which is related to the depletion of the triplet state of acceptor (SG-5) due to the TTA process (Fig. 10a). It should be noted that for Pery-2PTZ alone, no growth phase was observed for the kinetic trace of the delayed fluorescence because there is no TTET process. In contrast, upon the addition of an acceptor, a rise phase for the fluorescence was detected (Fig. 10b), indicating the TTET process (Fig. 8b, note that no delayed fluorescence was observed upon direct photoexcitation in polar solvents). Based on the intrinsic triplet state lifetimes and quenched triplet state lifetime of Pery-2PTZ, the TTET efficiency was calculated as 85.5%. Similar results were observed for the Pery-2PXZ triad, with a delayed fluorescence lifetime of 82.7 Î¼s (Fig. S34 and S35, ESIâ€ ).

Conclusion

In summary, we prepared perylene-based orthogonal triads by attaching two identical electron donor units of phenothiazine (PTZ) or phenoxazine (PXZ). Their photophysical properties were studied by steady-state and time-resolved transient spectroscopy and DFT calculations. We found that the intersystem crossing (ISC) is highly dependent on the solvent polarity and the ISC efficiency is maximum in medium polar solvents (singlet oxygen quantum yield Î¦Î” = 17% in n-hexane, and Î¦Î” = 70% in chloroform). The electrochemical study showed that the two electron donors in each triad are simultaneously oxidized in the cyclovoltammetry. The electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) spectra of the radical cation of the triads showed that the electron spinâ€“spin interaction between the two radical cations (PXZË™+) is negligible. The femtosecond transient absorption study confirmed the symmetry-breaking charge transfer process, where charge separation occurred in 0.17 ps, followed by spinâ€“orbit charge transfer ISC within 1.0 ns (in DCM), which is faster than that of the previously reported dyads (0.25â€“0.49 ps for CS and 0.99â€“5.98 ns for ISC). According to the nanosecond transient absorption spectra, an exceptionally long-lived triplet excited state was observed in different media (216â€“359 Î¼s in DCM, 1.62â€“1.83 ms in glycerol triacetate, and 4.03â€“5.0 ms in doped polymer films), which is advantageous for various applications. In acetonitrile, with diiodo-BDP as the triplet energy donor and Pery-2PXZ triad as the acceptor, a long-lived low-lying CT state was formed. Solvent polarity-dependent P-type delayed fluorescence was observed for the triads (luminescence lifetime = 114 Î¼s). We also used these triads for the generation of delayed fluorescence with SG-5 as an acceptor (Ï„ = 94.8 Î¼s). Thus, this study will be helpful for the design of heavy atom-free triplet photosensitizers.

The data was stored in a three-dimensional wave-time-absorbance matrix and exported for use with fitting software. Chirp correction was performed for all data shown here.
