Abstract

Three new alkylthienyl substituted benzodithiophene (BDT)-based polymers, poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethylhexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-5â€²,6â€²-dioctyloxy-benzo[c][1,2,5]oxadiazole)}(PBDTTDTBO), poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethylhexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-5â€²,6â€²-dioctyloxy-2â€²,1â€²,3â€²-benzothiadiazole)}(PBDTTDTBT) and poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethyl hexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-2-octyl-2â€²,1â€²,3â€²-benzotriazole)} (PBDTTDTBTz), were synthesized by Stille coupling polymerization reactions. All of the polymers were found to be soluble in common organic solvents such as chloroform, tetrahydrofuran and chlorobenzene with excellent film forming properties. Their structures were verified by elemental analysis and NMR spectroscopy, the molecular weights were determined by gel permeation chromatography (GPC) and the thermal properties were investigated by thermogravimetric analysis (TGA). The polymers exhibited tunable absorptions and energy levels on incorporation of different electron accepting units. All the copolymers showed high field hole mobility up to 10âˆ’2 order, and their blends with PCBM exhibited mobility as high as 10âˆ’1 order by the space-charge-limited current (SCLC) method. Preliminary photovoltaic cells based on the device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/PBDTTDTBO:PC71BM (1:1.5, w/w)/Ca/Al showed a power conversion efficiency of 5.9% with a high open-circuit voltage (Voc) of 0.84 V and a short circuit current density (Jsc) of 11.45 mA cmâˆ’2. To the best of our knowledge, this is the highest efficiency for dithienyl benzooxadiazole (DTBO)-based polymer solar cells.

1. Introduction

In the past decade, polymer solar cells (PSCs) have intrigued much interest because they allow the fabrication of light-weight, large-area, flexible devices by low-cost solution processing methods, etc.1â€“3 To achieve highly efficient PSCs, conjugated polymers with relatively broad absorption, suitable energy levels, and good solubility in organic solvents are important prerequisites.4,5 Power conversion efficiencies up to 8% have now been achieved.6â€“8 The design and synthesis of new conjugated polymers play a crucial role during the development of PSCs. Conjugated polymers containing alternating donor (D) and acceptor (A) units in their main or side chains have been widely used in PSCs because of their easily tunable electronic properties.9â€“11 It is important to choose suitable D and A units while designing Dâ€“A copolymers with high photovoltaic performance.

To improve the polymer solubility while maintaining the polymer's coplanarity, we choose three typical acceptors with alkyl or alkoxyl groups: (4,7-di(5-bromothien-2-yl)-5,6-dioctyloxy benzo[c][1,2,5]oxadiazole (M1), 4,7-di(5-bromo-thiophen-2-yl)-5,6-dioctyloxybenzo[c][1,2,5]-thiadiazole (M2), and 2-octyl-4,7-di(5-bromo-thiophen-2-yl)-2H-benzo[d][1,2,3]triazole (M3). Our research group has reported that the copolymers from copolymerization of M1, M2 or M3 with alkoxyl substituted benzodifuran (BDF) or benzodithiophene (BDT) units exhibit good photovoltaic properties with a PCE reaching 4.5%.12â€“15 From the studies on polymer photovoltaic materials, it is obvious that the change of donors can affect greatly device performance by well tuning of absorption, energy levels, morphologies, etc.

During the process of searching excellent donors, some recent reports attract us. Some groups have reported alkylthienyl substituted BDT (BDTT)-based copolymers showing great potential in PSCs with PCEs over 6%.16,17 For example, Hou and coworkers synthesized a BDTT based polymer-PBDTTT-C-T, indicating a PCE of up to 7.6% with a high short circuit current density (Jsc) of 17.48 mA cmâˆ’2,16 furthermore, they synthesized another BDTT based polymer-PBDTDTTT-S-T with a PCE over 7%.18 BDTT seems an excellent donor and BDTT based polymers show better photovoltaic properties than those of their corresponding BDT or BDF based copolymers. Conjugated alkylthienyl unit replacing alkoxy group as side chain has proven to be a good strategy to improve the absorbance and mobility of BDT-based polymers.19 However, it is still necessary to do further research on the influence of the BDTT unit on photovoltaic properties of different Dâ€“A polymer systems.

To further explore BDTT based polymers towards highly efficient photovoltaic applications, we designed and synthesized three new BDTT based copolymers with M1, M2 and M3 as electron accepting units, namely poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethylhexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-5â€²,6â€²-dioctyloxy-benzo[c][1,2,5]oxadiazole)} (PBDTTDTBO), poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethylhexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-5â€²,6â€²-dioctyloxy-2â€²,1â€²,3â€²-benzothiadiazole)} (PBDTTDTBT) and poly{4,8-bis(2â€²-ethyl hexylthiophene)benzo[1,2-b;3,4-bâ€²]dithiophene-alt-5,5-(4â€²,7â€²-di-2-thienyl-2-octyl-2â€²,1â€²,3â€²-benzotriazole)} (PBDTTDTBTz). All the polymers were found to be soluble in common organic solvents with good film forming properties. Their thermal, absorption, and electrochemical properties and hole mobilities were well characterized. Preliminary photovoltaic cells based on the device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/PBDTTDTBO:PC71BM (1:1, w/w)/Ca/Al showed a power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 5.9% with an open-circuit voltage (Voc) of 0.84 V and a short circuit current density (Jsc) of 11.45 mA cmâˆ’2. These investigations indicate that the photovoltaic properties can be well tuned by combining with different electron accepting units. More importantly, this work also provides some guidance about the molecular design towards high performance optoelectronic materials.

2.3 Fabrication of OFET devices

Thin-film OFETs were fabricated with top-contact configuration. Briefly, the substrates were soaked in a mixture of deionized water, 25% ammonium hydroxide and 30% H2O2 (5:1:1 by volumetric ratio) for 20 min at 80 Â°C, then rinsed with deionized water and dried with a nitrogen flow. Octadecyltrimethoxysilane (OTMOS) was self-assembled on the surface of hydrophilic-treated substrates according to the reported method. Thin polymer films were prepared by spin coating of the copolymers in chlorobenzene onto the OTMOS modified SiO2/Si substrates at a speed of 3000 rpm (revolutions per minute) for 40 s at room temperature. After annealing at 150 Â°C under N2 for half an hour, the gold film (50 nm) was deposited on the organic layer to form the drain and source electrodes, for a typical device, the drainâ€“source channel length (L) and width (W) are 80 Î¼m and 8800 Î¼m, respectively. 3.4 Electrochemical properties

Electrochemical cyclic voltammetry (CV) was used to determine the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) energy levels of PBDTTDTBO, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz (see Fig. 3). We calculated the HOMO and LUMO energy levels of the polymers according to the equations:22 HOMO = âˆ’e(Eoxon + 4.71) (eV) and LUMO = âˆ’e(Eredon + 4.71) (eV). The onset oxidation potentials of PBDTTDTBO, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz are 0.54 V, 0.44 V and 0.21 V, respectively. The corresponding HOMO levels of the polymers are âˆ’5.25 eV, âˆ’5.15 eV and âˆ’4.92 eV, respectively. As well known, Voc is limited by the difference between HOMO of the donor and LUMO of the acceptor (usually PCBM as the acceptor). Therefore, different HOMO levels lead to different Voc while blending with PCBM. The onset reduction potentials of PBDTTDTBO, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz are âˆ’1.43 V, âˆ’1.41 V and âˆ’1.70 V, respectively. The corresponding LUMO levels of the polymers are âˆ’3.28 eV, âˆ’3.30 eV and âˆ’3.01 eV, respectively. From the typical CV curves, the energy levels of the copolymers can be well tuned by altering the electron withdrawing units. PBDTTDTBO has the deepest HOMO level due to the stronger electron accepting ability of benzooxadiazole compared to other two electron accepting units, which ensured good air stability and a high Voc in the polymer solar cell applications. As expected from the structures of the polymers, polymers with different electron withdrawing units have different HOMO values, which is proved by our earlier work.13 The electrochemical bandgaps, estimated from the difference between the HOMO and LUMO energy levels, were 1.87 eV, 1.85 eV and 1.91 eV for PBDTTDTBO, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz, respectively. As listed in Table S1,â€  compared with their alkoxyl substituted BDT or BDF based copolymers, PBDTTDTBO shows a deeper HOMO level, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz exhibit comparable or lower-lying HOMO levels. Electrochemical bandgaps are larger than optical bandgaps, this discrepancy might have been induced by the presence of an energy barrier at the interface between the polymer film and the electrode surface.23 For a clear comparison, we have summarized the electrochemical data in Table 2.

3.5 Hole mobility

3.6 Photovoltaic properties

The bulk heterojunction PSCs were fabricated with a device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PCBM/Ca/Al to investigate the photovoltaic properties of the polymers. Fig. 5a shows the typical Jâ€“V curves of the devices under the illumination of AM 1.5G, 100 mW cmâˆ’2 and the corresponding Voc, Jsc, FF and PCE are also summarized in Table 3. The blends of the polymers and PCBM at different weight ratios of 1:1, 1:1.5, 1:2, respectively, were used to optimize the device performances. Fig. S3â€  shows the current density versus voltage (Jâ€“V), external quantum efficiency (EQE) curves of the devices and the detailed device results are summarized in Table S3.â€ PBDTTDTBO demonstrates much higher photovoltaic performance over PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz. When the donor (D)/acceptor (A) weight ratio of the PBDTTDTBO and PC61BM blend is 1:1, the PCE reaches 5.83% with a high Voc of 0.89 V, a Jsc of 10.68 mA cmâˆ’2 and a FF of 0.61, when the D/A weight ratio of PBDTTDTBO and PC61BM increases to 1:1.5, the PCE decreases to 5.75%. For PBDTTDTBT based solar cells, the PCEs are above 4% at different weight ratios, and the PCE of PBDTTDTBT reaches up to 4.76% when the D/A weight ratio of PBDTTDTBT and PC61BM is 1:1. When PBDTTDTBTz is blended with PC61BM at a D/A weight ratio of 1:1.5, the best device efficiency is just 2.45%, and the large decrease of the PCE of PBDTTDTBTz compared to PBDTTDTBO and PBDTTDTBT is mainly originated from the reduction of Voc and Jsc. We replaced PC61BM with PC71BM to improve the Jsc of the devices because PC71BM has better absorption than PC61BM with a similar energy level. Compared with PC61BM as an acceptor, in all cases, PCEs of the devices based on PBDTTDTBO and PC71BM blends of above 5.0% are obtained. When the copolymers are blended with PC71BM, the best D/A weight ratio is 1:1.5. Under these conditions, PBDTTDTBO exhibits a PCE of 5.9% with a Voc of 0.84 V, a Jsc of 11.45 mA cmâˆ’2 and a FF of 0.61. Among the three polymers, PBDTTDTBO shows the best photovoltaic properties because of high Jsc and Voc, although the absorption of PBDTTDTBO and PBDTTDTBT is similar, PBDTTDTBO has a relatively higher Jsc due to higher hole mobility and better morphology as shown in Fig. 6. PBDTTDTBO exhibits the highest Voc due to the deepest HOMO level among the three BDTT based polymers used in this work. For the PBDTTDTBT/PC71BM blend, the best PCE of ca. 5% with a Voc of 0.75 V, a Jsc of 10.29 mA cmâˆ’2 and a FF of 0.64 is obtained. PBDTTDTBTz has the lowest PCE of 3.1% among the three copolymers when blended with PC71BM, mainly due to a very low Voc of ca. 0.54 V from a high HOMO level and relatively lower Jsc from a relatively broad bandgap of 1.78 eV. PBDTTDTBTz has the lowest Jsc, resulting from the compromise of higher hole mobility and blueshifted absorption compared to the other two polymers. All of three copolymers demonstrate very high FF above 0.60, which indicates that the devices have relatively balanced charge transport and low serial resistance. As listed in Table S2,â€  the polymers used in this work show better photovoltaic properties than those of the corresponding BDT or BDF based copolymers,12â€“15 which is mostly from higher Jsc. For PBDTTDTBO and PBDTTDTBTz based devices, they exhibit two times higher PCE than those of their corresponding BDT or BDF based polymers, due to their greatly improved Jsc although of comparable or a little higher Voc and FF. Compared to its counterparts, the enhanced Jsc for PBDTTDTBO is concerned with a large increase of the hole mobilities with the similarity of their absorption. The increased Jsc of PBDTTDTBTz is related to the red-shifted absorption and much higher hole mobility. For PBDTTDTBT, compared to its analogues, the devices show a little higher PCE, because the hole mobilities are not much improved compared to those of PBDTTDTBO or PBDTTDTBTz, which led to the little difference in Jsc between PBDTTDTBT and its counterparts. In one word, improved photovoltaic properties of the polymers used in this work are mainly from higher Jsc mostly due to the greatly increased hole mobilities compared to their corresponding BDT or BDF based polymers. The accuracy of the photovoltaic measurements can be confirmed by the external quantum efficiency (EQE) of the devices. Fig. 5b shows the typical EQE plots of the PSC devices fabricated under the same conditions as those for the Jâ€“V measurements. The higher values of EQE are consistent with the higher Jsc measured in the solar cells. The EQE results indicate that the photovoltaic results are reliable. The EQE values above 40% from 350 nm to 650 nm of PBDTTDTBO led to higher Jsc up to ca. 11.45 mA cmâˆ’2. The maximum EQE plateau reached 58% from 500â€“600 nm. From the photovoltaic measurements, the difference of PCEs used in this work is mainly from Voc due to varying HOMO levels of the copolymers as mentioned in the electrochemical discussion part, the change in the trend of Voc is similar to the corresponding BDF based polymers reported by us.13

3.7 Morphology

Besides the above parameters such as absorption, energy levels and charge mobility, the morphology of the photoactive layer is very important for PSCs. To better explain different photovoltaic properties and determine if the devices were fully optimized, we investigated the morphologies of the three polymers and PC71BM blends spin-coated from their o-dichlorobenzene (ODCB) solution by using a tapping-mode atomic-force microscope (AFM). Fig. 6 shows the height and phase images of polymer:PC71BM (1:1.5, w/w) films. From the height image (Fig. 6aâ€“c), the root-mean-square (RMS) surface roughness values of the blend films for PBDTTDTBO, PBDTTDTBT and PBDTTDTBTz are 1.04 nm, 1.24 nm and 0.77 nm, respectively. All the blends form very smooth high quality films when spin-cast from o-dichlorobenzene. The films are very homogenous with little apparent phase separation and low surface roughness. From the phase images of the copolymer and PCBM blends (Fig. 6dâ€“f), the connectivity is sensitive to the structures of the polymers. The fine and nanoscale phase separation can be seen more clearly in the PBDTTDTBO/PC71BM blend and the PBDTTDTBO blend nanostructure is most pronounced among the three polymer blends, implying that long and good connected pathways have been formed, which further explains the better performance of PBDTTDTBO based solar cells. For BHJ PSCs, the nanoscale phase separation is a practical way to form a bicontinuous donorâ€“acceptor network.27,28 AFM images indicate that all the BDTT based polymers possess good morphology for PSCs. It is believed that the photovoltaic performance of this type of copolymers can be further improved by the optimization of film morphology and device fabrication conditions.

4. Conclusions

In conclusion, we have synthesized three new BDTT based copolymers. The copolymers have been well characterized by TGA, UV-vis absorption and cyclic voltammetry. The opto-electronic properties of the polymers can be finely tuned by differing the electron accepting units. The polymers exhibit surprisingly high hole mobilities (10âˆ’1 to 10âˆ’2 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1). The energy levels, absorption, morphology, etc., have been greatly affected by the introduction of different electron accepting units, resulting in different photovoltaic performances. The PCE of 5.9% from PBDTTDTBO based polymer solar cells with Voc = 0.84 V, Jsc = 11.45 mA cmâˆ’2 and FF = 61.3% was recorded without any device modifications or post-treatment. These results indicate that PBDTTDTBO is a promising candidate for high-efficiency polymer solar cells.
