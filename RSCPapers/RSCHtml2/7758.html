Abstract

Three thiazole-bridged DPP polymers with deep lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) levels were designed for field-effect transistors (FETs) and polymerâ€“polymer solar cells. By introducing thiazoleâ€“thiazole coupled segments, perfluoroalkyl side chains or strong electron-deficient naphthalenediimide units into the conjugated backbone the three thiazole-bridged DPP polymers have LUMO levels of âˆ’4.0 to âˆ’4.4 eV. The three DPP polymers exhibit optical absorption in the near-infrared region, crystallinity and an electron mobility of around 0.01 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 in bottom contact FETs. The polymers were applied as electron acceptors in polymerâ€“polymer solar cells to provide PCEs of around 0.4%. The low PCEs are mainly due to low short-circuit currents (Jsc) and attributed to large phase separation. Our results demonstrate several efficient strategies to lower the energy levels of conjugated polymers in order to be used as universal acceptors for photovoltaic cells.

Introduction

Bulk-heterojunction polymer solar cells comprising conjugated polymers as electron donors and fullerene derivatives as electron acceptors have successfully achieved power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) above 10% in recent years.1â€“3 The application of conjugated polymers as electron acceptors to replace fullerene derivatives has also attracted increasing attention,4 even though polymerâ€“polymer solar cells provide lower maximum PCEs of around 6% than polymerâ€“fullerene solar cells.5â€“8 Compared to the small-molecule fullerene derivatives, extended conjugated polymers provide better thermal stability of the morphology in photovoltaic devices. In addition, the chemical structure of polymer acceptors can be intentionally modified to tune their absorption spectra, energy levels, charge transport properties and crystallinity. It can be expected that the PCE of polymerâ€“polymer solar cells will approach the PCE of polymerâ€“fullerene solar cells when new polymer acceptors are systematically explored together with morphology and device optimization.

The success of fullerene derivatives such as [6,6]-phenyl-C71-butyric acid methyl ester ([70]PCBM) in high efficient photovoltaic cells is mainly attributed to their high electron mobility, high crystallinity and suitable energy level for charge separation and transportation.9 PCBM possesses a deep lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) level below âˆ’4.0 eV and a highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) level below âˆ’6.0 eV10 and can be applied as an almost universal electron acceptor in combination with numerous conjugated polymers as electron donors. Polymer acceptors with a LUMO below âˆ’4.0 eV have also been reported and are generally based on perylenediimide (PDI)11â€“13 and naphthalenediimide (NDI) derivatives.8,14â€“16Other building blocks, such as benzothiadiazole (BT),17 isoindigo18 and diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP),7,19â€“22 have also been used in electron accepting polymers for polymerâ€“polymer solar cells, but they usually possess higher-lying LUMO levels. While most studies have focused on designing polymer acceptors with high-lying LUMO to afford a high open-circuit voltage (Voc) in polymerâ€“polymer solar cells, it is also useful to explore polymer acceptors with lower lying LUMO levels similar to PCBM.

In this work, we attempt to develop conjugated polymers as electron acceptors based on electron-deficient DPP units, aiming at obtaining a deep LUMO level. Several DPP-based conjugated polymers exhibit high hole and electron mobilities of around 10 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1,23â€“25 and high PCEs above 8% as electron donors.26â€“28 Upon introducing thiazole (Tz) into the conjugated backbone of DPP polymers, the LUMO of the resulting PDPP2TzT polymer is reduced âˆ’4.0 eV due to the electronegative imine nitrogens (CNâ€“C) and an electron mobility of 0.13 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 has been achieved.21 As a consequence, PDPP2TzT can be successfully applied as an electron acceptor to yield a PCE of 2.9% in polymerâ€“polymer solar cells.21 The high-lying LUMO of thiazole-bridged DPP polymers can be realized by introducing electron-rich copolymerized units into the conjugated backbone.22

In order to achieve a low-lying LUMO in DPP polymers, three thiazole-bridged DPP polymers were designed and synthesized, as shown in Fig. 1. We show below that PDPP2Tz with thiazoleâ€“thiazole coupled segments has a LUMO of âˆ’4.03 eV. In PDPP2TzFBDT, when Tzâ€“DPPâ€“Tz alternates with perfluoroalkyl thiophene substituted benzodithiophene (FBDT) the LUMO is lowered to âˆ’4.16 eV. Further deepening of the LUMO to âˆ’4.4 eV is realized in PDPP2TzNDI, an acceptorâ€“acceptor polymer consisting of Tzâ€“DPPâ€“Tz and NDI. These three polymers possess electron mobilities of around 0.01 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 in bottom contact field-effect transistors (FETs). The polymers were applied in polymer solar cells as electron acceptors, but provided low PCEs of 0.4%. Our results pave an efficient way to design polymer acceptors with deep LUMO levels that can be used as universal electron acceptors for polymerâ€“polymer solar cells.

Optical and electrochemical properties

The UV-vis absorption spectra of the DPP polymers in chloroform solution and as thin films are shown in Fig. S2 (ESIâ€ ) and Fig. 2a, and the data are summarized in Table 1. All polymers exhibit red-shifted absorption spectra in thin films compared to solution spectra, indicating aggregation in the solid state. PDPP2Tz has an optical band gap of Eg = 1.37 eV, which is lower than that of PDPP2TzT which has an additional thiophene as a co-unit (Eg = 1.47 eV).21 PDPP2TzFBDT has an Eg = 1.58 eV, which is slightly higher than that of PDPP2TzBDT with benzodithiophene (BDT) (Eg = 1.53 eV).22 PDPP2TzNDI with an electron-deficient DPP core and NDI give an Eg of 1.53 eV, which is much larger than that of its analogue PNDI-DPP (Eg = 1.15 eV) with thiophene as bridges,34 indicating the strong influence of thiazole on the optical properties.

The frontier orbital energy levels of the polymers were determined by cyclic voltammetry (CV) on thin films (Fig. 2b, Table 1 and Fig. S3, ESIâ€ ). For comparison, [70]PCBM gave a LUMO of âˆ’4.16 eV and a HOMO of âˆ’6.15 eV in our measurements (Fig. S3b, ESIâ€ ). PDPP2Tz has a LUMO of âˆ’4.03 eV and a HOMO of âˆ’5.40 eV. Upon introducing perfluorohexyl units, PDPP2TzFBDT shows a similar LUMO as [70]PCBM of âˆ’4.16 eV and a HOMO of âˆ’5.74 eV. It is interesting to note that PDPP2TzBDT, without perfluoroalkyl side groups, has a LUMO of âˆ’4.0 eV,22 indicating that the side chains can effectively tailor the energy levels. Further lowering of the LUMO and HOMO was realized by incorporating two electron-deficient units, DPP and NDI. PDPP2TzNDI has a LUMO of âˆ’4.40 eV and a HOMO of âˆ’5.93 eV. The deep LUMO of PDPP2TzNDI compared to that of PCBM should provide a larger driving force for exciton dissociation into free charges in polymerâ€“polymer solar cells, but of course a concomitant loss in Voc.

The delocalization of the frontier orbitals over the conjugated backbone and the expected energy levels were further studied by density functional theory (DFT) calculations on the B3LYP/6-31G level (Fig. 3). In the DFT calculations, methyl and trifluoromethyl units were used to replace long alkyl chains and perfluorohexyl units in order to reduce the calculation time. For PDPP2Tz and PDPP2TzFBDT, both HOMO and LUMO levels are delocalized along the conjugated backbone. However, PDPP2TzNDI has a more localized HOMO on the DPP-segment and a LUMO on the NDI-segment. In addition, the calculated HOMO and LUMO levels show similar trends as in electro-chemical measurement that increase from PDPP2Tz to PDPP2TzFBDT and PDPP2TzNDI.

Charge carrier mobility

The electron mobility of the conjugated polymers is important for the efficient polymerâ€“polymer solar cells. High electron mobility assists charge transport to the electrodes and reduces recombination. The mobilities of the polymers were determined in a bottom gateâ€“bottom contact FET configuration, where a highly n-doped Si wafer with a SiO2 layer, which contained strip gold electrodes, was passivated with octadecyltrichlorosilane (OTS) self-assembled monolayers. DPP polymers were dissolved in chloroform and spin-coated on the substrates. The devices were thermally annealed at 140â€“160 Â°C (Table 2) and measured under an inert atmosphere. Typical transistor transfer and output characteristics are shown in Fig. 5 and Fig. S4 (ESIâ€ ). OFETs based on the as cast polymer thin films were also characterized and showed much lower charge carrier mobilities (Table S1, ESIâ€ ) than those of the devices after thermal annealing (Table 2). The improved mobilities after thermal annealing can be attributed to the enhanced crystallinity, which was also observed in other conjugated polymer FETs.35 AFM revealed that the roughness of the polymer thin films was increased after thermal annealing, indicating better aggregation (Fig. S5, ESIâ€ ). PDPP2Tz and PDPP2TzFBDT exhibit ambipolar transfer characteristics with high electron mobility compared to their hole mobility (Fig. 5 and Table 2). PDPP2TzNDI only shows an electron mobility of 1.7 Ã— 10âˆ’2 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1. The electron mobilities of these DPP polymers are comparable to those of other electron acceptors such as P(NDI2OD-T2) in the same device configuration.36

Polymerâ€“polymer solar cells performance

The DPP polymers in this work show optical absorption in the near-infrared region, deep LUMOs, crystallinity and sufficiently high electron mobility, making them of interest for application as electron acceptors for polymerâ€“polymer solar cells. In this work, we selected another DPP polymer PDPP5T as an electron donor to construct photovoltaic devices.21 PDPP5T with DPP as the core alternating with five thiophene rings as repeating units has been successfully applied into polymerâ€“polymer solar cells combined with PDPP2TzT as an electron acceptor.21 By applying cyclic voltammetry as performed for the three acceptor DPP polymers, the HOMO and LUMO levels of PDPP5T were determined to be âˆ’4.89 eV and âˆ’3.45 eV (Fig. 2b and Fig. S3a, ESIâ€ ). Therefore, both the HOMO and LUMO offsets between PDPP5T and the DPP polymers in this work are larger than 0.3 eV, which ensure enough driving force for exciton dissociation into free charges.37

Polymerâ€“polymer solar cells were fabricated by using ITO/PEDOT:PSS and Ca/Al as electrodes. The photo-active layers, consisting of PDPP5T blended with the thiazole-bridged DPP polymers, were processed from chloroform solution, in which the processing additive, the donor to acceptor ratio and the thickness of the active layer were optimized. Different high boiling point additives such as DIO, 1-CN and o-DCB were examined, but 1-CN provided the best performance for all cells (Table S2, ESIâ€ ). The Jâ€“V characteristics are shown in Fig. 6a and the photovoltaic parameters are summarized in Table 3. All cells provide a moderate Voc of 0.53â€“0.61 V, where PDPP2TzNDI with the deepest LUMO gave a Voc of 0.53 V. In comparison, PDPP5T:PCBM cells have been reported to give a Voc of 0.57 V.21 Therefore, the Voc in these polymerâ€“polymer solar cells confirm the similar or even lower LUMO levels compared to that of PCBM. However, all these cells show a low short circuit current density (Jsc) of 0.77â€“2.2 mA cmâˆ’2 and a poor fill factor (FF) <0.4. The low Jsc is reflected in a low external quantum efficiency (EQE) (Fig. 6b). The three cells display EQEs below 0.1 in their absorption region. As a result, the PCE based on these cells ranged from 0.19 to 0.42%.

The low PCE in these cells needs to be discussed. Several parameters can influence the device performance, including photon absorption, exciton diffusion, charge separation, and charge transport into electrodes. Since in these donorâ€“acceptor polymerâ€“polymer systems, the HOMOâ€“HOMO and LUMOâ€“LUMO energy differences are higher than 0.3 eV, efficient charge generation via exciton dissociation is expected. Therefore, we presume that exciton diffusion or charge transport and recombination limit the efficiency. While studying the morphology of the blend films by AFM (Fig. 7), large domains were found in all three systems, indicating that photo-generated excitons are prone to intrinsic recombination before they can diffuse to the interface between the donor and acceptor, where charges can be formed. Especially, for PDPP5T:PDPP2TzFBDT with perfluoroalkyl side chains, fibril-like structures were found that can be attributed to the demixed thin films between PDPP5T and PDPP2TzFBDT. It has been also reported that conjugated polymers bearing perfluoroalkyl chains had the tendency to self-aggregate into fibril structures via the fluorophobic effect.38 Therefore, further improvement of PCE based on these polymer acceptors requires selecting suitable donor polymers and device optimization to achieve better phase separation.

Conclusions

In this work, three thiazole-bridged DPP polymers with deep LUMO levels were designed and synthesized via Stille polymerization for application in FETs and polymer solar cells as electron acceptors. By applying thiazoleâ€“thiazole coupled segments, perfluoroalkyl side chains or strong electron-deficient moieties in the main chain as the design strategy, the LUMO levels of DPP polymers could be adjusted below âˆ’4.0 eV. The polymers also show near-infrared absorption and are semi-crystalline. The polymers were applied in bottom-contact FETs where they showed an electron mobility of around 0.01 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1. The polymers were tested as electron acceptors for polymerâ€“polymer solar cells in combination with another DPP polymer as a donor to give a PCE of 0.4%. The low PCE was found to mainly originate from the large phase separation between the donor and acceptor, leading to a reduced Jsc. From this point, much effort is still needed to further improve the PCE based on these DPP polymers acceptors, such as the selection of donor materials and optimization of the solution process technique.
