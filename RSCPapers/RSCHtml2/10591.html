Abstract

We have found that 9,9â€²BF can be used as an electron acceptor for P3HT-based OPVs while similar devices using 4,4â€²BP do not show any photovoltaic effect. This can be related to the respective aromaticity and antiaromaticity of the reduced forms of 9,9â€²BF or 4,4â€²BP. The OPV device fabricated using P3HT and 9,9â€²BF exhibited a PCE of 2.28% with a Voc of 1.07 V, a Jsc of 5.04 mA cmâˆ’2, and a FF of 0.42.

Fullerene derivatives are currently widely used as n-type semiconductors in solution-processed bulk-heterojunction (BHJ) solar cells because they have high electron affinity and good electron transporting ability.1 However, they also have some drawbacks as electron acceptors for organic photovoltaic cells (OPVs) such as weak absorption in the visible region, and difficult synthesis and purification. To solve these problems, new electron accepting materials with high absorbance in the solar spectrum, facile synthesis, and controllable frontier orbital energy levels are needed for OPV applications. Recently some non-fullerene type electron accepting materials have been reported for BHJ PSCs including fluoranthene-fused imide,2 diketopyrrolopyrrole,3 benzothiadiazole,4 bifluorenylidene derivatives,5 and others.6 Among the reported non-fullerene acceptors, 9,9â€²-bifluorenylidene (9,9â€²BF) derivatives have attracted much scientific attention since the first report by Wudl and co-workers. They conjectured that 9,9â€²BF derivatives could easily accept one electron from a donor polymer because the reduced form would acquire more aromaticity by fulfilling HÃ¼ckel's requirements.5,7â€“10 Several 9,9â€²BF derivatives have been reported as electron acceptors for OPVs, but to date only one, 12-(3,6-dimethoxy-fluoren-9-ylidene)-12H-dibenzo[b,h]fluorene (D99â€²BF), has shown a reasonable photovoltaic effect (1.7% PCE).5,11 Moreover, the photo-physical properties of 9,9â€²BF and its application to OPVs have not been reported yet.

In this study, we chose to compare the photo-physical properties of 9,9â€²BF and 4,4â€²-bicyclopenta[def]phen-anthrenylidene (4,4â€²BP) as electron acceptors for OPVs because 9,9â€²BF forms a 4n + 2 aromatic system while 4,4â€²BP forms a 4n anti-aromatic system when they accept one electron. The UV-visible absorption spectra of 9,9â€²BF and 4,4â€²BP solutions in chloroform and solid thin films are shown in Fig. S2 (ESIâ€ ). Both 9,9â€²BF and 4,4â€²BP showed three main absorption bands.

To investigate the charge transfer characteristics between poly(3-hexylthiophene) (P3HT) and the acceptors (9,9â€²BF and 4,4â€²BP), the photoluminescence (PL) quenching experiment was performed. The PL spectra of the P3HT solution in chloroform were measured at different acceptor concentrations. Especially the PL intensity was dramatically decreased by adding very low concentration of 9,9â€²BF. Photovoltaic devices were fabricated using P3HT as the donor and 9,9â€²BF or 4,4â€²BP as the electron acceptors with a device configuration of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/P3HT:acceptor/LiF/Al. To find the optimum blend ratio we used four different donor to acceptor composite ratios (1:0.5, 1:0.7, 1:1, and 1:1.2) for each active layer, and the spin-coated active layers were annealed at different temperatures (70, 90, and 150 Â°C) for 10 min. The devices fabricated using 4,4â€²BP did not show any detectable photovoltaic effect. The most efficient devices fabricated using 9,9â€²BF were achieved at a blend ratio of 1:0.7 and film thickness of 100 nm. The power conversion efficiency (PCEs) of the optimized device was 1.07% with an open circuit voltage (Voc) of 1.05 V, a short circuit current density (Jsc) of 3.28 mA cmâˆ’2, and a fill factor (FF) of 0.31, respectively. Fig. S8 (ESIâ€ ) shows AFM images of pristine P3HT:9,9â€²BF films (1:0.7, w/w) and after thermal annealing. In the AFM images, the brighter islands correspond to the P3HT domains and darker valleys to the 9,9â€²BF segment. Better heterojunction morphology and phase separation was readily observable after thermal annealing. The surface root-mean-square (rms) roughness of the pristine P3HT:9,9â€²BF active layers, annealed at 70, 90, and 150 Â°C were 6.00, 7.45, 7.93, and 9.77 nm, respectively. In general, annealed P3HT films show a rougher surface than the corresponding pristine films due to a higher degree of P3HT self-organization.15 Among the AFM images, P3HT:9,9â€²BF blend films annealed at 70 Â°C showed better phase and aggregation than that of the pristine film. However, the same film annealed at 90 Â°C exhibited somewhat enlarged 9,9â€²BF domains and phase separation. In addition, it could be observed that the active layer annealed at 150 Â°C exhibited much larger phase separation and domain size than those of the other annealed films. The large phase domains would lead to a smaller interfacial area, which is unfavorable for efficient exciton dissociation and charge transport.

In conclusion, we have investigated 9,9â€²BF and 4,4â€²BP as electron acceptors for P3HT-based bulk-heterojunction polymer solar cells. 9,9â€²BF effectively quenched PL of P3HT. Furthermore, 9,9â€²BF showed two orders of magnitude higher electron mobility than 4,4â€²BP. The OPV device fabricated using 9,9â€²BF showed 2.28% of PCE which is the highest value reported so far for devices fabricated using 9,9â€²BF derivatives while the device fabricated using 4,4â€²BP did not show any photovoltaic effect. We conclude that the aromaticity of the reduced form of 9,9â€²BF derivatives appears to be important in determining their electron-accepting and also charge-transporting abilities, which is consistent with the original hypothesis of Wudl.
