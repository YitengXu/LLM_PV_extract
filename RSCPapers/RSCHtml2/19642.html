Abstract

In this article, we report a series of diketopyrrolopyrrole based donorâ€“acceptor small molecules, PDPP-OC8, PDPP-EC8, PDPP-EG and PDPP-CNEC8, with different phenyl containing flexible terminal side chains such as alkoxy (â€“OC8H17), ester ((â€“CO)OC8H17), ester with glycol ((â€“CO)(O(C2H4O)3â€“CH3)) and cyano acrylate with an octyl side chain ((â€“CN)(CO)OC8H17), respectively, via direct arylation. A comparative analysis has been done on their opto-electronic properties such as absorption, HOMO, LUMO, band gap, thermal properties, packing nature and hole mobility. The physical properties of the four small molecules were studied using absorption spectroscopy, thermal gravimetric analysis, cyclic voltammetry, differential scanning calorimetry and charge carrier mobility measurements. In comparison to molecules with alkoxy terminal side chains, those with ester and cyano acrylate terminal side chains exhibit (i) a lowered optical band gap from 1.65 eV (alkoxy) to 1.43 eV (ester) and (ii) a broad absorption covering the solar spectrum from 500 to 800 nm. From the results we are able to conclude that the terminal side chains significantly alter their optical and electronic properties. Density functional theory calculations have been carried out to substantiate the opto-electronic characteristics and further understand the effects of terminal alkyl chains.

1. Introduction

Over the last two decades organic photovoltaics (OPVs) based on solution processed bulk heterojunction (BHJ) systems comprised of polymer/fullerene blends have been of much attraction to the research community because of their unique advantages such as low cost, light weight, flexibility and large area device fabrication using various printing methodologies.1 Even though impressive power conversion efficiencies (PCE) of over 10% in single junction and 11% in tandem devices have been realized in polymer based donor materials, their batch to batch variations in molecular weight, polydisperse nature and purity can lead to variations in their processing properties and device performances.2 In contrast to polymers, small molecular donor materials are highly attractive owing to their monodisperse nature, definite molecular structure, specific molecular weight, excellent purity and less batch to batch variation.3 Inspiring success in their power conversion efficiencies of over 10% in a single layer has been reported based on small molecules as donor materials.4 Among various small molecular organic semiconductors, thiophene flanked diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP) based materials have received more interest for organic solar cells. Their well conjugated fused aromatic framework favors strong Ï€â€“Ï€ interactions, good absorption in the visible range and their electron deficient lactam groups make DPP a strong acceptor. Combining DPP with a variety of donor materials offers the development of a wide range of pushâ€“pull or donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) systems. These systems exhibit a low band gap due to intramolecular charge transfer between the donor and the acceptor.5 In general, such types of Dâ€“A systems can be effectively built using traditional cross coupling methodologies like Suzuki, Negishi, Stille, etc. The main drawback of cross coupling is that it needs an organometallic precursor such as organoboron or organotin components which require a multi-step monomer synthesis and expensive purification and release toxic byproducts during coupling. Recently a new technique named direct arylation, an alternate to traditional cross coupling methodologies, has emerged as a convenient tool for synthesizing organic conjugated materials. The advantages of direct arylation are the simplicity of the reactant and the absence of an organometallic precursor which makes it cheap, time effective and environmentally benign.6 Recently Welch and coworkers reported a series of DPP based small molecules with different end groups and demonstrated that Î±-Câ€“H bonds of thiopheneâ€“DPP can be subjected to direct arylation using palladium catalysis.7,8 Thus, a DPP flanked thiophene segment should be the perfect candidate for the study of direct arylation.9 However, in contrast to polymers, small molecule based semiconductors suffer from unsatisfactory film formation and poor morphology because of their inherent crystalline nature which results in low device performance. This can be overcome by elegant molecular design and by controlling side chain engineering.10

Selecting a rigid conjugated backbone helps in enhancing Ï€â€“Ï€ interactions and also favors solid packing. Apart from using side chains for solubility purposes, a recent study reveals that it has an ability to control absorption, emission, HOMO, LUMO, band gap, morphology and molecular packing. On the other hand, there is no appropriate protocol for selecting this flexible side chain.11 Therefore, designing a donor material with the appropriate side chain with an appropriate length and functional group are the key factors for controlling their crystalline nature, solubility, and miscibility and the orientation of small molecules in thin films which in turn control the charge separation and charge transport properties. Herein, we report four different solution processed small molecules as donor materials via direct arylation, with a chromophoric thiophene flanked DPP moiety bonded to a phenyl group containing various flexible terminal side chains. The effect of different functional flexible side chains in the optical property, band gap, packing nature, and HOMO and LUMO energy levels of the four small molecules has been investigated in detail. To gain an in-depth understanding of the opto-electronic profiles of the four small molecules, theoretical calculations have been carried out using Density Functional Theory (DFT).12

2. Experimental section

2.1 Materials

2,5-Dioctyl-3,6-di(thiophen-2-yl)pyrrolo[3,4-c]pyrrole-1,4(2H,5H)-dione was prepared, according to the literature procedure.13 4-Bromophenol, 4-bromobenzaldehyde, octyl cyanoacetate, piperidine, 4-bromobenzoic acid, 1-octanol, potassium carbonate, tetrabutylammonium hexafluorophosphate, triethylene glycol monomethyl ether, N,Nâ€²-dicyclohexylcarbodiimide, 4-(dimethylamino)pyridine, N,N-dimethylacetamide, Pd(OAc)2 and pivalic acid were purchased from Sigma Aldrich and used without further purification.

2.2 Instrumentation

1H and 13C NMR spectra were recorded using a Bruker NB 400 MHz using CDCl3 as the solvent and tetramethylsilane (TMS, d = 0) as the internal standard. Molecular weights of the small molecules were confirmed using Matrix-assisted laser desorption and ionization (MALDI) mass spectra were recorded using a Bruker ultraflextreme MALDI-mass spectrometer using a 4-HCCA (Î±-hydroxycinnamic acid) matrix. Thermal stability was analyzed using a Mettler TOLEDO TGA/SDTA 851e at a heating rate of 10 Â°C minâˆ’1. Electrochemical analyses were performed using cyclic voltammetry using CH instruments, a CHI 600D electrochemical work station with platinum disc as the working electrode, Ag/AgCl as a reference electrode and platinum wire as a counter electrode. The experiments were carried out by coating a thin layer of small molecules in a platinum disc electrode and measurements were taken in 0.1 M tetrabutylammonium hexafluorophosphate as the supporting electrolyte. UV-vis-NIR absorption spectra were recorded using a Varian Carey 50 Bio UV-visible spectrophotometer. For the solid state, spin coating was done on quartz from solution in chloroform with a concentration of 1 mg mlâˆ’1.

2.3 Theoretical calculations

Density functional theory (DFT) calculations were performed using the Gaussian 09 ab initio quantum chemical software package.14 The ground state geometries of all of the molecules were optimized in the gas phase using the hybrid Becke's three-parameter gradient-corrected functional (B3LYP)15 along with the 6-31G(d) basis set. The B3LYP functional has been proven to be reliable for its optimum prediction of properties for DPP based systems.16 Frequency calculations were performed at the same level of theory to check for consistency in the optimized geometries. The excited state properties of the systems were calculated using the time dependent-DFT theory (TD-DFT) using the hybrid PBE exchange correlational functional (PBE0)17 in combination with the 6-31G(d) basis set. Single-point TD-DFT calculations for the first 20 singletâ€“singlet vertical transitions were carried out using the optimized geometries of the systems. The solvent environment was incorporated using the polarizable continuum solvation model (PCM) with chloroform as solvent.18

2.4 Mobility measurements

3.3 Optical properties

The ultraviolet/visible (UV/vis) absorption spectra for the four small molecules in dilute chloroform solution and spin coated thin film are shown in Fig. 3. Their corresponding optical parameters are listed in Table 1. All the molecules exhibit two distinct absorption bands at shorter and longer wavelength regions attributed to Ï€â€“Ï€* and internal charge transfer. Such an absorption profile reflects the typical donorâ€“acceptor Ï€-conjugated system.20 Compared to solution, all the molecules exhibit red shifts in both shorter and long wavelength regions in thin film. This is due to strong intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ stacking in the solid state. The optical band gap in the solid state derived from the onset of absorption spectra decreases in the order of PDPP-OC8 > PDPP-EC8 â‰ˆ PDPP-EG > PDPP-CNEC8, with a decrease in Eg (by ca. 0.1â€“0.2 eV). It is noteworthy that PDPP-CNEC8, PDPP-EG and PDPP-EC8 exhibit broad absorption covering the solar spectrum in the range of 500 to 800 nm. Specifically âˆ¼120 nm red shift is observed in the onset of absorption (Î»onset) when replacing the alkoxy side chain with an electron-withdrawing ester substituent side chain, their optical band gap in thin film is reduced from 1.65 to 1.43 eV. The absorption maxima (Î»max) also show a red shift in both solution and thin film when replacing alkoxy with ester. This may be due to strong Ï€-intermolecular interactions in the solid state. Particularly electron withdrawing side chain substituted molecules PDPP-EG, PDPP-CNEC8, and PDPP-EC8 show a discernible vibronic shoulder attributed to molecular rigidity and an aggregating nature in the solid state7,21,22 whereas PDPP-OC8 with an alkoxy side chain exhibits narrower absorption and a sharp onset. The TD-DFT results substantiate the experimental results and the absorption profiles of the molecules extend over a broad range from â‰ˆ300 to 700 nm. The dominant lower energy band (â‰ˆ600 to 700 nm) transitions of the systems are the intra-molecular charge transfer absorption peaks which mainly arise from HOMO â†’ LUMO transitions. Each system exhibits another important peak at the high energy band (â‰ˆ300 to 400 nm) of a Ï€â€“Ï€* nature with HOMOâˆ’2 â†’ LUMO transitions. The simulated absorption spectra of the systems show that changing the terminal end groups from PDPP-OC8 to PDPP-CNEC8 results in bathochromic shift of the absorption maxima from â‰ˆ600 to 690 nm. It is important to note that the molecule PDPP-CNEC8 exhibits a maximum red-shifted absorption at about â‰ˆ690 nm. Analysing the electron density contours of the small molecular systems is significant in understanding the nature of their transitions. In all the molecules, the HOMOs are delocalized mainly on the central thienyl DPP unit whereas the LUMOs extend from DPP to the phenyl fragment except for PDPP-OC8. This is because of the electron withdrawing nature of the side chain.

4. Conclusion

In conclusion, we successfully synthesized a series of DPP based small molecules using direct arylation methodologies. The four small molecules were confirmed by NMR and MALDI-TOF MS. A comprehensive study has been performed using absorption spectroscopy, cyclic voltammetry, XRD and hole mobility in order to analyse how the functional side chain substitution impacted on the HOMO, LUMO, band gap, and the optical and device performance. We find that a simple replacement of the alkoxy group with ester leads to a drastic change in their absorption profile (by â‰ˆ0.2 eV and 120 nm red shift on onset) highlighting the effect of the electron withdrawing nature of the terminal position. Of all of the four molecules PDPP-OC8 exhibits the highest carrier mobility, due to highly ordered structure and strong intermolecular interactions which were clearly supported by XRD data. The theoretical calculations provide more insights regarding the nature of the small molecules. The simulated absorption spectra and the charge density plots reveal the effect of the side chains on the optical properties of the molecules. We have also identified a coupling defect occurring in thienyl DPP, conveniently identified by MALDI-mass. Thus a basic understanding on the selectivity of the Câ€“H monomer in direct arylation has yet to be carefully understood and proper optimization of the reaction conditions is an essential tool for assuring the reaction yield and purity.
