Abstract

Anthocephalus cadamba shaped carbon nanostructures comprising FeNi encapsulated in radially grown nitrogen-doped carbon nanotubes (NCNTs) are produced by controlled pyrolysis which exhibit advanced electrochemical oxygen evolution reaction (OER) performance with a low overpotential of âˆ¼290 mV at 10 mA cmâˆ’2, small Tafel slope (37 mV decâˆ’1) and endurance in an accelerated stability test. In addition to better surface electron transfer kinetics, the radial growth of NCNTs offers plenty of electrochemically active sites (high roughness factor âˆ¼340) in tiny spaces, better accessibility to reactant species and appropriate gas outlets to prevent bubble accumulation during OER. Besides OER, the preferential 4eâˆ’ pathways for the oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) and excellent overall oxygen electrochemistry Î”E (Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) = 0.71 V) suggest excellent bifunctionality and advocate the practical importance of Anthocephalus cadamba shaped carbon nanostructures as a possible unitary oxygen electrocatalyst for metalâ€“air battery systems.

Introduction

Emergence of sustainable, eco-friendly electrochemical energy storage and conversion devices like unified regenerative fuel cells and metalâ€“air batteries demand electrocatalysts to achieve the desirable oxygen evolution/reduction reaction (OER/ORR) rate to overcome their sluggish kinetics owing to the multiple sequential electron transfers involved.1â€“4 Precious metal based electrocatalysts such as Pt-group based nanostructures are widely studied/explored for ORR, while the Ru/Ir based oxides are for OER.1â€“9 Besides limited reserves, high cost and operational instability of these benchmark electrocatalysts, their mono-functionality (e.g. Pt-based nanostructures which are excellent for ORR, have a much inferior OER activity and the Ru/Ir based electrocatalysts which are best used for OER show very poor ORR activity) is a major obstacle in the commercial domain and technological scalability.1â€“4,10 Importantly, unlike ORR which is a gas consuming reaction, OER is a more aggressive process, due to gaseous product evolution, and requires appropriate gas outlets to prevent bubble accumulation.4,10 Recently, tremendous efforts have been devoted to exploring non-precious transition metal based electrocatalysts through sensible designing for next generation electrochemical devices.10â€“19 Among the various available selections, studies suggest that NiFe based nanomaterials, in particular, are promising OER electrocatalysts with good activity and stability in an alkaline medium due to the synergy between Ni and Fe which modifies the electronic structure and consequently optimizes the adsorption energy of reaction intermediates.20â€“29 Nevertheless, these FeNi based nanostructures also suffer from poor ORR activity and consequently, are practically limited to electrolyzers only and are not suitable for devices like metalâ€“air batteries and fuel cells where ORR also plays the decisive role in performance evaluation.21 The recent literature survey suggests that the presence of hetero-atoms (e.g. N, B, S, P etc.) in carbon nanostructures (CNS) like carbon nanotubes (CNTs), graphene, etc. activates these CNS electrochemically towards ORR.10,11,30â€“39 Moreover, the hetero-atoms like N, in particular, easily co-ordinates with transition metals to form the Mâ€“NxC complexes during the course of the synthesis and are considered as highly efficient active centres for oxygen electrochemistry.10,11,15,17,18 Though recent scientific contributions suggest that transition metalâ€“carbon based nanoelectrocatalysts have potential to perform bifunctional oxygen electrochemistry (ORR/OER),10â€“19 the cycling loss caused by the combined large overpotentials (Î·ORR + Î·OER, for oxygen electrochemistry) in systems like a metalâ€“air battery diminishes the cycling efficiency and power output by 70% and needs to be minimized for efficient utilization and practical realization of these devices.3 The matrix which provides a measure of the combined overpotentials for oxygen electrochemistry and a better reversible oxygen electrode behaviour is represented by Î”E (= Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) where Ej=10 mA is the potential at which the OER current density attained a value of 10 mA cmâˆ’2 and E1/2 is the half-wave potential of ORR on electrocatalysts under study) and its magnitude should be as small as possible for minimizing the cycling loss.3,33 Therefore, electrochemically, the most active catalysts must be rationally designed and implemented to reduce the magnitude of the combined overpotentials in order to make the process (ORR/OER) energy-efficient. Consequently, rational designing of highly active, economical, earth-abundant transition metal based resilient electrocatalysts is of paramount importance in the attempt to assimilate the bifunctionality (ORR/OER) for efficient overall oxygen electrochemistry.

Herein, we report a single-step facile approach to obtain Anthocephalus cadamba shaped carbon nanostructures comprising FeNi nanoparticles encapsulated in radially grown nitrogen-doped CNTs (NCNTs) produced by controlled pyrolysis of ferrocene, nickelocene and melamine. The radially grown and spatially separated NCNTs are expected to accommodate plenty of electrochemically active sites in tiny spaces, thereby, offering better accessibility of electrochemically active sites to the reactant species, enhanced electrochemical active surface area (ECSA), appropriate gas outlets to prevent bubble accumulation in OER and consequently smaller Î”E (=Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR)) = 0.71 V as compared to the various recently reported transition metalâ€“carbon based state-of-the-art nanoelectrocatalysts for overall oxygen electrochemistry.

Oxygen electrochemistry

In an attempt to access the electrocatalytic activity of the as-synthesized FNMR (M = 3, 10, 25, and 40), the OER study has been performed in an alkaline medium (1 M NaOH) using a standard three electrode system with Pt-ring as the counter, KCl saturated Ag/AgCl as the reference and FNMR (M = 3, 10, 25, and 40) modified glassy carbon as the working electrodes, respectively. The linear sweep voltammetry (LSV) OER polarization curves recorded at 1200 rpm and at a scan speed of 10 mV sâˆ’1 using a rotating disk electrode (RDE) system with FNMR (M = 3, 10, 25, and 40) are shown in Fig. 4a. For a comparative study, the LSV-OER polarization curves are recorded on commercially available state-of-the-art Ptâ€“C and RuO2 electrocatalysts too, under similar experimental conditions. Interestingly, the greatly enhanced OER current is observed on FN25R (94 mA cmâˆ’2), largely exceeding that of Ptâ€“C (15 mA cmâˆ’2), RuO2 (30 mA cmâˆ’2) in addition to FN3R (25 mA cmâˆ’2), FN10R (46 mA cmâˆ’2) and FN40R (17 mA cmâˆ’2) at 1.7 V vs. RHE, and is shown in the inset of Fig. 4a. Conclusions

In conclusion, the study suggests that the temperature ramping rate during the pyrolysis of ferrocene, nickelocene and melamine, influences the final morphology, in addition to the total nitrogen and metallic (Fe + Ni) content in the structure. A ramping rate of 25 Â°C (FN25R) results in Anthocephalus cadamba shaped carbon nanostructures comprising FeNi encapsulated in radially grown NCNTs. The diverging and spatially separated NCNTs accommodate plenty of electrochemically active sites in tiny spaces offering better accessibility of electrochemically active sites to the reactant species. In addition, the overall structure offers an enhanced electrochemical active surface area (ECSA), higher specific BET surface area, appropriate gas outlets to prevent bubble accumulation in OER, and better surface electron transfer kinetics. Besides promising OER, the preferential 4eâˆ’ ORR process ensures the bifunctionality in an alkaline medium with a smaller Î”E (=Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR)) âˆ¼ 0.71 V as compared to the various recently reported transition metalâ€“carbon based state-of-the-art nanoelectrocatalysts for overall oxygen electrochemistry. The smaller Î”E suggests that the OER and ORR processes are more energy-efficient on FN25R as evident by the smaller Tafel slope and high current density for the respective electrochemical processes and may be of practical importance.
