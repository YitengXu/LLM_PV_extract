Abstract

Herein we prepare four novel Dâ€“Ï€â€“A dyes based on triphenylamine (ZHG1, ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4) by modifying the Ï€-bridges. Compared with ZHG1, the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of ZHG2 is improved to 6.1% after the introduction of ethynyl. But further extension of the conjugation of the Ï€-bridges by introducing the chromophore 4,8-bis(n-octyloxy)-benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT) into ZHG3 conversely decreases the PCE to 4.6%. Improving the coplanarity by replacing cyclobenzene with thiophene in ZHG4 after introducing BDT further decreases the PCE of ZHG4 to 4.3%. Theoretical calculations indicate that the LUMOs of ZHG3 and ZHG4 were mainly delocalized over benzothiadiazole which is far from the anchoring groups. Cyclic voltammetry experiments indicate that the LUMO energy levels of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are lower than those of ZHG1 and ZHG2. Both of these results affect the ability to inject electrons into the TiO2 conduction band. X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) analysis shows that the mean thickness of the dye coverage for ZHG1, ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4 is 16 Ã…, 18 Ã…, 27 Ã… and 24 Ã…, respectively. So the tilt angle of the dye backbone anchored on the TiO2 film is in the order of ZHG1 > ZHG2 > ZHG4 > ZHG3, which is consistent with the dye coverage of the outermost TiO2 surfaces. This result indicates that the intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ aggregation in ZHG3 and ZHG4 with overlong Ï€-bridges is more serious compared with that in ZHG1 and ZHG2. Perhaps the above two factors are the reason that the PCEs of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are lower than those of ZHG1 and ZHG2. So it is very important to find a balance point between the electron injection ability, intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ aggregation and the expansion of the light absorption range.

Introduction

Dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs) have attracted immense interest since the epoch-making breakthrough made by GrÃ¤tzel and co-workers in 1991.1 During the past twenty years, the power conversion efficiency (PCE) of DSSCs has made significant progress via numerous efforts. To date, the highest efficiency of DSSCs based on ruthenium-complexes and Zn porphyrins is 11.9% and 13%, respectively.2 But compared to these two kinds of dyes, metal-free pure organic dyes have attracted more and more attention in recent years due to their simple synthesis, flexible structural modulation and high molar extinction coefficients.3 In general, a typical pushâ€“pull organic dye comprises Dâ€“Ï€â€“A structures. Donors in DSSCs include carbazole,4 triarylamine,5 diphenylamine,6 coumarin,7 phenoxazine,8 and indoline9 and acceptors anchored on TiO2 film are carboxylic acid,10 cyanoacrylic acid,11 pyridine,12 phosphonic acid,13 2-hydroxybenzonitrile,14 8-hydroxyquinoline15 and pyridine-N-oxide.16 Donors and acceptors are connected by Ï€-bridges which are the key factor for efficient intra-molecular charge transfer (ICT),17 so modulation of the linkers is helpful for reducing the molecular energy gap and extending the absorption region to the near-infrared to improve the performance of DSSCs. General strategies for modifying the linkers include changing the Ï€-conjugated extension18 and enhancing the rigidification of the Ï€-bridges to reduce the rotational disorder.19

In this study, we have synthesized ZHG1, ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4 (Fig. 1), four novel dyes based on modifying the Ï€-bridges. We introduce long chains to noncoplanar triphenylamine because the long chains can suppress dye aggregation and conduction band charge recombination with its blocking effect and therefore improve the electron lifetime.20 Modification of the linkers is mainly based on the following considerations: (1) benzothiadiazole (BT) as an assistant electron-withdrawing unit in all four dyes is thought to facilitate intramolecular charge transfer (ICT) and therefore improve the light-harvesting ability;21 (2) ethynyl is introduced to improve the rigidification of the conjugate linkers to reduce rotational disorder; (3) the excellent chromophore 4,8-bis(n-octyloxy)-benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT) is adopted in the Ï€-bridges to improve the conjugated extension for reducing the energy gap and therefore expanding the light absorption range; (4) heterocycles are generally thought to possess better conjugacy, so we employ thiophene in ZHG4 to replace the cyclobenzene of the dye ZHG3 adjoining anchor to improve its conjugacy.22 The different device performances caused by modification of the linkers are investigated with regards to the spectral and physicochemical properties of the sensitizers. The influence of dye coverage on the nanocrystalline TiO2-surface with different Ï€-conjugated dyes is investigated by X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS).

Electrochemical properties

For evaluation of the possibility of electron transfer from the excited dye molecule to the conduction band (CB) of TiO2 and the ability for dye regeneration, we use cyclic voltammetry to determine the redox potential of dyes in THF as shown in Fig. 3 and the corresponding data are summarized in Table 1. The ground state oxidation potentials of ZHG1, ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4 are 1.23, 1.25, 1.22 and 1.22 V vs. NHE, respectively, which are efficient driving forces for regeneration in Iâˆ’/I3âˆ’ electrolyte (0.45 V vs. NHE). The excited-state oxidation potentials deriving from the energy gap E0â€“0 and ground state oxidation potentials are âˆ’1.03, âˆ’0.91, âˆ’0.84 and âˆ’0.84 V vs. NHE, respectively.25 As shown in Fig. S1 (ESIâ€ ), these calculated values are all sufficiently more negative than the energy level of the TiO2 conduction band (âˆ’0.5 V vs. NHE), ensuring enough power to inject the electron to the TiO2 conduction band. It can be observed that the elevated rigidification and the extension linkers have more influence on the LUMOs of sensitizers. This kind of phenomenon gives us more enlightenment on tuning the LUMO levels in future novel organic dyes.

Theoretical calculations

In order to gain insight into the distribution of the frontier molecular orbitals and the dye molecular structures, density functional theory (DFT) studies are carried out at the DFT-B3LYP/6-31G(d,p) level with the Gaussian 09 suite of programs. From Fig. S2 (ESIâ€ ), it can be observed that the HOMO of ZHG1 is mainly delocalized between triphenylamine and benzothiadiazole. The HOMOs of ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4 extend to ethynyl and the chromophore BDT moiety, respectively. The LUMOs of the dyes are all delocalized between benzothiadiazole and the acceptors. In general, the excellent overlap of the HOMOs and the LUMOs in all the dyes is helpful for transferring the electron from the donors to the acceptors,26 but at the same time it will prompt recombination of the excited electrons back to the donor subunits. So this kind of excellent overlap of the HOMOs and the LUMOs is actually a double-edged sword for the device performance. This phenomenon means that finding a balance point between excellent overlap of the HOMOs and the LUMOs and good charge-separation states will be very important in the future designing of new dyes. Compared with ZHG1 and ZHG2, the LUMOs of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are mainly delocalized over benzothiadiazole which is far from the anchoring groups, and it may be harmful for electron injection into the TiO2 conduction band and may affect the device performances based on ZHG3 and ZHG4.

Photovoltaic performance

Photocurrent densityâ€“voltage (Jâ€“V) curves and incident photon-to-current conversion efficiencies (IPCE) based on ZHG dyes with Iâˆ’/I3âˆ’ electrolyte under AM 1.5 full sunlight (100 mW cmâˆ’2) illumination are shown in Fig. 4. From the corresponding data summarized in Table 2, it can be observed that tuning the Ï€-bridges can produce an apparent influence on the DSSC performances. Compared with ZHG1, the power conversion efficiency of ZHG2 is improved to 6.1% after the introduction of ethynyl. The Voc of ZHG2 is similar to that of ZHG1, but the Jsc of ZHG2 is obviously larger than that of ZHG1. Thus the introduction of ethynyl to ZHG2 leads to a broader wavelength range of IPCE (Fig. 4a) and a higher Jsc value which eventually improves the overall efficiency. The superior performance of the dye ZHG2 prompts us to further extend the conjugation of the Ï€-bridge by introducing the chromophore BDT in order to gain better results, but unfortunately the cell performance reduces to 4.68% when the BDT is introduced to the dye ZHG3. We speculate that although the introduction of BDT can further extend the absorption range, the dihedral angle of up to 23.83Â° between BDT and cyclobenzene might suppress the intramolecular charge transfer effects because of their overlong linkers.27 The Jsc of ZHG1, ZHG2, ZHG3 and ZHG4 is 11.4, 12.0, 9.85 and 9.89 mA cmâˆ’2, respectively. ZHG2 has a higher Jsc than ZHG1 due to its broader wavelength range of IPCE as shown in Fig. 4a. The IPCE wavelength range of ZHG2 is extended to 650 nm which is higher compared with that of ZHG1 (625 nm). Their maximum IPCE values both reach 80%. The IPCE wavelength ranges of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are further extended to 665 nm due to the introduction of BDT. Their maximum IPCE values just reach 68%, much lower than those of ZHG2 from 300 nm to 560 nm, so they exhibit relatively lower current density. The Jsc values from the IPCE curves are 9.3, 10.1, 8.5, and 9.0 mA cmâˆ’2, respectively. The differences from the experimental results are probably due to the slight spectral mismatch but the order is in line with that from the Jâ€“V curves.

In order to gain insight into the open circuit voltage properties, we investigate the electron recombination effect of the cell devices using electrochemical impedance measurements in the dark under a bias (âˆ’0.7 V).28 The equivalent circuit, and the fitted and experimental results are shown in Fig. 5a.29 The larger semicircle of the Nyquist plot in the high frequency region corresponds to the charge recombination resistance (Rrec) at the photoanode/dye/electrolyte interface. In order to gain insight into this kind of adsorption property, X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) is used to study the TiO2 surfaces. XPS is a powerful technique to study surface properties because it can provide molecular structures, atomic valence state information, elemental composition, the content of various compounds and chemical bond information. Conclusions

Herein we prepare four new Dâ€“Ï€â€“A dyes by modifying the Ï€-bridges. We discover that the appropriate extension of linkers can effectively improve the PCE of dye ZHG2 compared with ZHG1, but overlong linkers in ZHG3 and ZHG4 will conversely reduce the PCE of the DSSCs. Theoretical calculations indicate that the LUMOs of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are mainly delocalized over benzothiadiazole which is far from the anchoring groups. Cyclic voltammetry experiments indicate that the LUMO energy levels of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are lower than those of ZHG1 and ZHG2. These results both affect the ability for injection of electrons into the TiO2 conduction band. X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) shows that the tilt angles of the dye backbones anchored on the TiO2 film are in the order of ZHG1 > ZHG2 > ZHG4 > ZHG3, which is consistent with the dye coverage of the outermost TiO2 surfaces. This result indicates that the intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ aggregation in ZHG3 and ZHG4 with overlong Ï€-bridges is more serious compared with that in ZHG1 and ZHG2. Perhaps the above two factors are the reason that the PCEs of ZHG3 and ZHG4 are lower than those of ZHG1 and ZHG2. So finding a balance point between the electron injection ability, intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ aggregation and the expansion of the light absorption range will be very important. More important is that this result can provide some enlightenment for the future research of the structureâ€“activity relationship of dyes.
