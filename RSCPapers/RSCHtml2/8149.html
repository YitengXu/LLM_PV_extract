Enhanced photoelectric conversion efficiency of dye-sensitized solar cells by the incorporation of dual-mode luminescent NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+

Ying Li , Kai Pan , Guofeng Wang *, Baojiang Jiang , Chungui Tian , Wei Zhou , Yang Qu , Shuai Liu , Li Feng and Honggang Fu *
Key Laboratory of Functional Inorganic Material Chemistry, Ministry of Education, School of Chemistry and Materials Science, Heilongjiang University, Harbin, 150080, P. R. China. E-mail: wanggf75@gmail.com; fuhg@vip.sina.com

Received 10th December 2012 , Accepted 22nd January 2013

First published on 1st March 2013

Abstract

This work focuses on the design of composite photoanodes with dual-mode luminescent function as well as the effects of luminescent phosphors on the photoelectric properties of dye-sensitized solar cells. Specifically, hexagonal phase NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ microcrystals were prepared by a hydrothermal method and added to the TiO2 photoanodes of dye-sensitized solar cells. The results indicated that the TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanodes can emit visible light under 495 or 980 nm excitation, and then the visible light can be absorbed by dye N719 to improve light harvesting and thereby the efficiency of the solar cell. Under simulated solar radiation in the wavelength range of 400 nm, the photoelectric conversion efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was increased by 10% compared to pure TiO2 cell. For the electrodes with the same thickness, the amount of dye adsorption of the photoanodes decreased a little after adding NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+, which was attributed to the decrease of TiO2 in the photoanodes. The electron transport and interfacial recombination kinetics were investigated by the electrochemical impedance spectroscopy and intensity-modulated photocurrent/photovoltage spectroscopy. The TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell has longer electron recombination time as well as electron transport time than pure TiO2 cell. The charge collection efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was little lower than that of pure TiO2 cell. In addition, the interfacial resistance of the TiO2-dye|I3/I electrolyte interface of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was much bigger than that of pure TiO2 cell. All these results indicated that the charge transport cannot be improved by adding NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+. And thus, the enhanced photoelectric conversion efficiencies of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells were closely related to the dual-mode luminescent function of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+.

1. Introduction

The dye-sensitized solar cell (DSSC) has become one of the most promising solar cells in the renewable energy research and development field for its potentially low fabrication cost and relatively good efficiency.1 Generally, DSSCs are comprised of a nanocrystalline semi-conductor oxide photoelectrode, a dye sensitiser, a redox shuttle and a counter electrode. An electric current can be generated when a photon absorbed by a dye molecule gives rise to electron injection into the conduction band of the semiconductor.2 To complete the circuit, the dye must be regenerated by electron transfer from a redox species in solution, which is then reduced at the counter electrode.3 The amount of dye adsorption, light harvesting efficiency, and the ability of charge separation and transport are three important factors to improve the photoelectric conversion efficiency of DSSCs. Among nanocrystalline semiconductor oxide electrodes, the anatase phase TiO2 with a band gap of 3.2 eV has been one of the best choices for DSSCs due to its high dye adsorption and fast electron transport.4 So far, numerous methods have been adopted to modify TiO2 and improve photoelectric conversion performance of DSSCs, such as doping of metal or nonmetal elements, surface modifications, and combination with narrow band gap semiconductors.5 Ru(II) complexes, such as N3 and N719, are known as the best sensitizers in DSSCs. However, the main absorption wavelength ranges for dyes N3 and N719 are 290700 nm, which is very low compared to the near-infrared (NIR) light.6 About 43% of the radiant energy from the sun in the infrared region is not absorbed, which limits the sun energy conversion efficiency of DSSCs. Therefore, it is significant for transferring infrared light to visible light which can be absorbed by dyes to enhance the photoelectric conversion efficiency of DSSCs.

Rare earth (RE) ions doped luminescent materials usually show unique down-conversion (DC) and up-conversion (UC) properties, which offer the opportunity to improve light harvesting and thereby the efficiency of the solar cells. Especially, the UC of RE ions brought a new opportunity for the utilization of long-wavelength sunlight.79 However, the effects of DC luminescence of Er3+ ions on the photoelectric properties of DSSCs have never been reported.

The purpose of this work was to design composite DSSCs with dual-mode luminescent function as well as deeply investigate the effects of luminescent phosphors on the photoelectric properties. It is well known that the luminescence of RE ions mainly originates from the electron transitions within the 4f shell. Many transitions are initiated from an excited level with a small energy gap to the next-lower level, and therefore, materials with lower phonon energy are required as the luminescent host to minimize nonradiative loss.10 Among various materials, fluoride nanocrystals, such as NaYF4, LaF3, YF3, and CaF2 have aroused great interest due to their low phonon energies and optical transparency over a wide wavelength range.11 In particular, NaYF4 is acknowledged as the most efficient DC and UC host material at present.12

Here, NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ microcrystals with DC as well as UC function were chosen as upconverting phosphors to design TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanodes. Under simulated solar radiation in the wavelength range of 400 nm, the photoelectric conversion efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was increased by 10% compared to pure TiO2 cell. We carried out a large number of experiments and proved that the utilization of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ phosphors is an effective way to enhance the photoelectric conversion efficiency of the solar cells.

2. Experimental section

2.1 Preparation of samples

All of the chemicals used in this paper were analytical-grade and used without further purification. For the synthesis of NaYF4:Er3+/Yb3+: In a typical synthesis, 2 mL of 0.5 M Ln(NO3)3 (Ln = Y, Yb, and Er) aqueous solution and 1 mmol of EDTA were dispensed into 10 mL of distilled water and magnetically stirred for 30 min, forming a chelated LnEDTA complex. After that, an aqueous solution of NaF was added into the complex and vigorously stirred for 30 min. The mixture was then transferred into a 50 mL autoclave and heated at 160 C for 24 h. After cooling to room temperature, the final products were collected by means of centrifugation, washed with distilled water, and finally dried in vacuum at 80 C.

Fabrication of photoelectrode and the assembly of DSSCs: several pastes, from homogeneously mixing NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ and TiO2 (Degussa P25) into 3 mL of TiO2 colloid, were prepared with different mass concentrations of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+. The TiO2 colloid was prepared following the previously published synthesis procedure.13 A screen-printed double layer of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ was used as the photoanode. A layer of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ was prepared by a doctor-blade method on the FTO substrate and then sintered at 450 C for 1 h. Subsequently, the second layer of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ was covered on the first TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ film and then sintered at 450 C for 30 min again. The thicknesses of all electrodes were determined to be around 10 m. The sensitization of the photoelectrodes was achieved by immersing them into 0.5 mM ((C4H9)4N)2[Ru(4-carboxy-4-carboxylate-2,2 bipyridine)2(NCS)2] dye (N719, Solaronix SA, Switzerland) in acetonitrile and tert-butanol (volume ratio, 1:1) for 48 h at room temperature. The Pt counter electrodes were prepared following the previous literature.14 The dye-sensitized photoelectrode and Pt counter electrode were adhered together with epoxy resin. The space between the electrodes was filled with the electrolyte, which comprised 0.6 M 1-propyl-2,3-dimethyl-imidazolium iodide, 0.05 M I2, 0.1 M LiI, and 0.5 M tert-butylpyridine (TBP) in 3-methoxypropionitrile (3-MPN), by capillary action.

2.2 Characterization

The compositions of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoelectrodes were analyzed by a Bruker D8 diffractionmeter equipped with graphite monochromatized Cu K radiation ( = 1.5406 ). The size and morphology of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanodes were investigated by transmission electron microscopy (TEM, JEM 2010 with operating voltage of 200 KV) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM, Hitachi S-4800 with operating voltage of 15 kV). The DC luminescence spectra were recorded with a Hitachi F-4500 fluorescence spectrophotometer at room temperature. The UC luminescence spectra were recorded using the spectrophotometer with an adjustable laser (980 nm, Beijing Hi-Tech Optoelectronic Co., China) as the excitation source with a fiber-optic accessory. Photocurrentphotovoltage curves were recorded by a BAS100B Electrochemical Analyzer. A Xe lamp with an AM1.5 radiation filter was used as the light source. A mask with an aperture was placed on the cell, giving an effective irradiation area of the working electrode of about 0.12 cm2. The EIS was performed with a computer-controlled IM6 impedance measurement unit (Zahner IM6&Zennium, Germany). EIS were obtained by applying sinusoidal perturbations of 0.8 mV over the Voc at the frequency range from 0.05 to 100 kHz. The obtained spectra were fitted with ZsimpWin software in terms of appropriate equivalent circuits. Intensity-modulated photocurrent spectroscopy (IMPS) under short-circuit conditions and intensity-modulated photovoltage spectroscopy (IMVS) under open-circuit conditions were carried out using the same electrochemical workstation (Zahner IM6&Zennium, Germany) with a frequency response analyzer under an intensity modulated (30150 W m2) white light emitting diode driven by a Zahner (XPot) source supply. Illumination was always incident on the working electrode side of the solar cells. The modulated light intensity was 10% or less than the base light intensity. The frequency range was set from 1000 Hz to 0.1 Hz for IMPS and IMVS.

3.2 Dual-mode luminescence of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ microcrystals and TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanodes

Fig. 3 shows the UC luminescence spectrum of the as-prepared NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ microcrystals under 980 nm excitation. The spectral peaks correspond to the following transitions: 2H9/2 4I15/2 (410 nm), 2H11/2 4I15/2 (524 nm), 4S3/2 4I15/2 (541 nm), and 4F9/2 4I15/2 (654 nm). It is obvious that the dominant emission is located in the green luminescence range.
Fig. 3 Upconversion emission spectrum of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ microcrystals under 980 nm excitation.

The UC luminescence mechanism and population processes in Yb3+/Er3+-codoped systems are presented in Scheme 2. The Er3+ ion is excited from the ground state 4I15/2 to the excited state 4I11/2via energy transfer of neighboring Yb3+ and Er3+. Subsequent nonradiative relaxation of 4I11/2 4I13/2 also populates the 4I13/2 level. Then, the same laser pumps the excited-state atoms from the 4I11/2 to the 4F7/2 levels via energy transfer and excited-state absorption, or from the 4I13/2 to 4F9/2 states via phonon-assisted energy transfer. The populated 4F7/2 may mostly nonradiatively relax to two lower levels: 2H11/2 and 4S3/2. In addition, the electron in 4F9/2 level may be excited to the 2H9/2 level via phonon-assisted energy transfer by the third photon.


Scheme 2 Mechanism of NIR sunlight harvesting in TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells (ET: energy transfer).

Under the excitation of a 980 nm laser, the UC luminescence spectra of the TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ photoanodes before and after sensitization with N719 dye were recorded to evaluate the influence of N719 dye on the light emissions from the NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+, as shown in Fig. 4. For naked TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ photoanode, violet (2H9/2 4I15/2), green (2H11/2 4I15/2 and 4S3/2 4I15/2), and red (4F9/2 4I15/2) UC emissions were observed. However, for N719-sensitized TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ photoanode, the violet/green emissions almost vanished, meaning that dye N719 has absorbed the violet/green emissions from NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+. Then the violet/green light excites dye N719 molecules to generate electronhole pairs. Thus, the near infrared (980 nm) can be utilized via the UC function of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanode. 3.4 Measurements of interfacial charge transfer for TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite DSSCs

As mentioned above, the photoelectric conversion efficiencies of DSSCs are related to the amount of dye adsorption, light harvesting efficiency, and charge transport. To further prove the measurements of enhanced photoelectric conversion efficiencies of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells, the influence of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ on photoelectric conversion performance of DSSCs was investigated from two aspects: dye adsorption and charge transport.

The UV-vis absorption spectra of desorption dye from N719-sensitized photoanodes are shown in Fig. S4 in the ESI. According to the BeerLambert law,15 the molar concentration of dye N719 loading from pure TiO2 and TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ photoanodes was 1.09 105 and 0.97 105 mol L1, respectively. Obviously, the amount of dye adsorption of photoanodes decreased a little after adding NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+, which was attributed to the decrease of TiO2 in the photoanodes. Usually, a higher amount of dye adsorption is expected to enhance the performance of DSSCs. Consequently, we can conclude that the enhancement of photoelectric properties is unrelated to the amount of dye adsorption.

EIS is a powerful method to investigate internal resistance that is attributed to the charge-transfer properties of DSSCs. Usually, the impedance at low frequency (0.051 Hz) corresponded to the Nernst diffusion of I3/I within the electrolyte. The impedance at high frequency (1k100k Hz) in the impedance spectra corresponded to the capacitance and charge-transfer resistance at the Pt|I3/I electrolyte interface. The medium-frequency response at 1100 Hz was associated with the TiO2-dye|I3/I electrolyte interface, where an accumulation of photoelectrons and redox shuttles are expected. For liquid electrolyte, the fast diffusion of electrolyte can result in low resistance, and thus, the semicircle at high frequency often overlaps with that at middle frequency. Fig. 9 shows the EIS of pure TiO2 cell and TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell by applying sinusoidal perturbations of 0.8 mV. It can be clearly seen that the interfacial resistance of the TiO2-dye|I3/I electrolyte interface of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell is much bigger than that of pure TiO2 cell.

IMPS and IMVS are conventional methods to investigate the electron transfer and recombination process. The IMPS response plot and IMVS response plot of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell and pure TiO2 cell are shown in Fig. 10. The electron transport time (d) and the electron recombination time (n) can be estimated from the IMPS and IMVS plot. In comparison with pure TiO2 cell, the TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell has longer electron recombination time and longer electron transport time. It noted that longer transport time can result in poorer photoelectric properties, while longer recombination time is beneficial for enhancing photoelectric properties. Thus, the photoelectric properties are closely related to the result of competition between charge transport and charge recombination in DSSCs. The charge collection efficiency (cc) of DSSC is determined by the relation: cc = 1 d/n.16 Where, d is a charge transport time and n is a charge recombination lifetime. Fig. 11 shows the charge collection efficiencies of pure TiO2 cell and TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell. It was found that the charge collection efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell is little lower than that of pure TiO2 cell. The electron diffusion coefficient (Dn) and diffusion length (Ln) can be determined on the basis of film thickness (x) and d, as shown in Fig. S5 and S6 in the ESI. All these results indicated that the charge transport and interfacial recombination cannot be improved by adding NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+. Thus, the enhanced photoelectric conversion efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell must be closely related to the UC function of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+.

4. Conclusions

NaYF4:Yb3+(20%)/Er3+(2%) microcrystals were prepared by a hydrothermal method and subsequently mixed with TiO2 powder. The TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ mixture was further used as a photoanode material to fabricate DSSCs. The photoelectrical properties have been systematically studied. The results indicated that the TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ composite photoanodes had the ability to improve light harvesting and thereby the efficiency of the solar cells. Under simulated solar radiation in the wavelength range of 400 nm, the photoelectric conversion efficiencies of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells were higher than that of pure TiO2 cell. The photoelectric conversion efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was obviously increased compared to pure TiO2 cell.

To further prove the measurements of enhanced photoelectric conversion efficiencies of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells, the influence of NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ on photoelectric conversion performance of DSSCs was investigated from two aspects: dye adsorption and charge transport. The results indicated that the amount of dye adsorption of photoanodes decreased a little after adding NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+, which was attributed to the decrease of TiO2 in the photoanodes. And thus, the enhancement of photoelectric properties is unrelated to the amount of dye adsorption. The electron transport and interfacial recombination kinetics were investigated by EIS, IMPS, and IMVS. EIS analysis revealed that the interfacial resistance of the TiO2-dye|I3/I electrolyte interface of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell is much bigger than that of pure TiO2 cell. In addition, the TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell exhibited longer electron recombination time and longer electron transport time than pure TiO2 cell. The charge collection efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cell was a little lower than that of pure TiO2 cell. All these results indicated that the charge transport cannot be improved by doping NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+. And thus, we concluded that the NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ with dual-mode luminescent function that can enhance the photoelectric conversion efficiency of TiO2NaYF4:Yb3+/Er3+ cells. In addition, the enhancement of photoelectric properties is also related to the scattering effect.
