Abstract

Oxygen reduction and evolution reactions are of immense importance in electrochemical conversion/storage devices like regenerative/alcohol/hydrogen based fuel cells and metalâ€“air batteries. Here, a rational facile synthesis methodology has been developed to design N-doped carbon nanotubes (N-CNTs) and Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures co-embedded in B-doped mesoporous carbon nanostructures (BFNCNTs) as a noble metal-free superior bi-functional electrocatalyst for oxygen evolution/reduction reactions. The incorporation of N and B with negligible/undetectable Bâ€“N formation and Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures leads to the superior performance by introducing plenty of defects, local heterogeneity and a high specific surface area (âˆ¼272 m2 gâˆ’1). Besides, mesoporous boron-doped carbon acts as a host material for NCNTs and Feâ€“Fe3C, and it offers good connectivity as well as a protective coating for durable catalysis. Remarkably, more positive onset (âˆ’30 mV) and half-wave potentials (âˆ’225 mV) with âˆ¼94% current retention under accelerated stability test and fuel tolerance for the ORR, in combination with lower onset (422 mV) and Ej=10(OER) (562 mV) potentials with high current density (190 mA cmâˆ’2 @ 0.8 V vs. Ag/AgCl) for the OER as compared to commercial state-of-the-art electrocatalysts suggest superior bifunctional behavior of BFNCNTs. The complete oxygen electrochemical activity Î”E = Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) = 0.788 V for BFNCNTs is lower than recently reported various state-of-the-art bifunctional catalysts. Besides, a prototype battery fabrication using BFNCNTs as the cathode electrode for driving a light emitting diode has been demonstrated. Overall, BFNCNTs have potential to serve as a non-precious electrocatalyst for electrochemical energy devices.

Introduction

Electro-reduction of oxygen besides the oxygen evolution reaction in electrochemical conversion/storage devices like regenerative/hydrogen/alcohol based fuel cells and metal air batteries plays a pivotal role in deciding their operational efficiency.1â€“6 The large over potential and sluggish oxygen reduction reaction (ORR) kinetics due to the involvement of the multi electron transfer process (O2 + 2H2O + 4eâˆ’ â†’ 4OHâˆ’, alkaline media) requires a catalyst to facilitate the ORR.7 Traditionally, Pt-/Pd-based mono-/multi-component novel metal nanostructures are well-studied as electro-catalysts for the ORR.8â€“13 However, the potential driven leaching of metals, agglomeration due to high surface energy and aggressive experimental conditions with limited availability accompanied by high price limit the commercial adoption of these materials in electrochemical devices.12 In an attempt to address these issues, hetero-atom doped/co-doped carbon nanostructures (CNSs) have recently emerged as a potential class of electro-catalysts for the ORR in alkaline media.14â€“39 The presence of hetero-atoms with different atomic sizes and electronegativities introduces the structural defects and electronic polarization that drive the doped CNS electrochemically active for the ORR.24â€“26,29 In this regard, N-doped carbon nanotubes (NCNTs) have shown potential for the ORR as a Pt-free catalyst.22,24â€“26,30â€“32 Interestingly, the co-doping further offers the enhanced ORR activity by synergistic effects.24â€“26,28â€“32,40 The recent study on the volcano-shaped relationship between ORR activity and binding energies of ORR intermediates for various hetero-atom doped graphene-based electrocatalysts suggests that N-/B-doped graphene-based electrocatalysts have moderate binding energies of ORR intermediates and thus may offer better ORR activity over S-/P-/O-doped CNSs.25 However, in the boron (B) and nitrogen (N) co-doped CNS, the Bâ€“N bond formations are highly probable which are electrochemically inactive due to the neutralization effect and does not contribute significantly in the ORR process.24 The effect of Bâ€“N co-doping on the electronic transport study suggests that the bonded Bâ€“N species are transparent to the conduction electrons of carbon nanotubes.26 As a result, the bonded Bâ€“N species hardly contribute to the activation/conjugation of carbon Ï€ electrons. Therefore, B and N co-doping with least/negligible Bâ€“N species formation or effective B and N separation in order to prevent the neutralization effect for the efficient utilization of these co-doped heteroatoms is the essential requirement for the electro-reduction of oxygen by the B and N co-doped CNS. Besides, these heteroatoms in the CNS with the presence of iron moieties further promote the ORR process and support the 4eâˆ’ ORR pathways as the presence of iron incorporates additional ORR active centres in the form of Feâ€“NxC complexes as well as through Feâ€“Fe3C centres.35â€“38

Likewise, the oxygen evolution reaction (OER) is an electrochemical reaction of immense importance (4OHâˆ’ â†’ O2 + 2H2O + 4eâˆ’, alkaline media) and equally complex in nature.3â€“5,34 In this context, it is worthy to note that ORR and OER reactions collectively play a decisive role in regenerative fuel cells and metal air batteries. Literature surveys suggest that oxides and compounds of some noble metals (specially, Ir and Ru) as well as iron-based catalysts like FeNi are well suited for the OER but not promising for the ORR.41â€“44 Moreover, these precious metal (Ir and Ru) based oxide electrocatalysts for the OER are unstable under high anodic potentials cause dissolution and leaching of these catalysts.4,5 Similarly, Pt-based materials which are widely considered as the state-of-the-art ORR catalysts exhibit inferior performance towards the OER and suffer serious stability issues.41,44â€“46 Recently, bi-functional catalysts have drawn considerable interest that can overcome the cycling loss during charging and discharging process in rechargeable metalâ€“air batteries.3â€“5 In this regard, a cost effective, noble metal-free, efficient and robust catalyst for the ORR and OER is of paramount importance, and the quest is to develop bi-functional robust electrocatalysts. Recently, ironâ€“iron carbide (Fe/Fe3C) carbon nano-composites have shown their potential as an efficient ORR electro-catalyst which unveils the usefulness of iron incorporation in CNSs.20,21,47â€“49 They also offer a good OER electrocatalytic activity.48,49 As both NCNTs and Fe/Fe3C nanostructures can be used for the ORR and OER, we intend to design NCNTs and Fe/Fe3C nanostructures co-embedded in B-doped mesoporous carbon (BFNCNTs) in an attempt to make defect enriched heterogeneous nanoelectrocatalysts in order to assimilate the individual active behaviour and explore the maximum possible electrocatalytic activity towards the ORR and OER in alkaline media.

In this regard, we have made an effort to integrate the efficacy of B and N co-doping in CNSs with maximum possible separation of B and N species by sequential pyrolysis of ferroceneâ€“melamine followed by ferroceneâ€“triphenyl borane (TPB) to obtain nitrogen-doped carbon nanotubes (NCNTs) and Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures co-embedded in boron containing mesoporous carbon nanostructures (named BFNCNTs) as shown in Scheme 1 (Fig. S1â€ ) and studied the ORR and OER bifunctional activities in alkaline media. In the present study, apart from being the host for NCNTs and Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures, boron-doped mesoporous carbon provides good connectivity as well as a protective coating to NCNTs and Feâ€“Fe3C which enhances the operational durability. Furthermore, to elucidate the synergistic behaviour of boron & Feâ€“Fe3C containing carbon coated on NCNTs (BFNCNTs), we synthesized the boron-doped carbon coated NCNTs (named BNCNTs) and Feâ€“Fe3C containing carbon coated NCNTs (named FNCNTs) separately and subjected to the electrochemical study. Interestingly, the electrochemical activities of BFNCNTs are better than the commercially available state-of-the-art catalysts like Ptâ€“C (20 wt% Pt loading for the ORR) and RuO2 (for the OER) under similar experimental conditions as well as superior to BNCNTs and FNCNTs. Furthermore, the accelerated stability test suggests âˆ¼94% current retention in both the cases even after the 1000th cycle indicating good stability. Besides the electrochemical studies, in an attempt to study the viability for practical application of BFNCNTs as a cathode material, we fabricated a proto-type metalâ€“air battery system using aluminium foil (a common house hold) as the anode material and BFNCNT coated carbon paper as the cathode material to power light emitting diodes.

Overall oxygen electroactivity

In general, the overall oxygen electro-catalytic activity of an electrocatalyst is appraised by the difference between potentials where the current density is 10 mA cmâˆ’2 for the OER and half-wave potential for the ORR (Î”E = Ej=10 âˆ’ E1/2).30 The smaller is the magnitude of Î”E, better is the electro-catalyst for oxygen electrochemistry in electrochemical storage devices like metalâ€“air batteries.30 As one of the reasons for measure loss in metalâ€“air batteries which diminishes the power output and cyclic efficiency by 70% is the combined over-potentials in the ORR (Î· discharge) and OER (Î· charge).6â€“9 In the present study, BFNCNTs display a Î”E of 0.788 V (Fig. 6a) which is much smaller than those of B-MWCNTs (Î”E = 1.00 V),23 NS-CN (Î”E âˆ¼ 0.88 V),30 MWCNTs@Sâ€“Nâ€“C (Î”E = 1.25 V),32 nitrogen, phosphorous co-doped carbon paper (Î”E = 0.96 V),33 nitrogen-doped graphene-CNT composites (Î”E = 1.00 V)34 as well as some noble/transition-metal based electro-catalysts e.g. Fe/N/C@BMZIF (Î”E âˆ¼ 0.79 V)31 Fe/Fe3C@NGL-NCNT (Î”E = 1.29 V),47 Fe/Fe3C@N-graphitic layer (Î”E = 0.97 V),48 NiCo2O4/graphene (Î”E = 0.915 V),59 CoFe2O4/rGO (Î”E = 0.98 V),60 CoFe2O4/Nâ€“P-biocarbon (Î”E = 0.98 V),63 Ir@C (Î”E = 0.92 V), CaMn4Ox (Î”E = 1.04 V), Pt@C (Î”E = 0.94 V), and MnxOy/N-carbon (Î”E = 0.93 V),63,64 as shown in Fig. 6b (Table S4â€ ). The superior bifunctional behaviour of BFNCNTs can be attributed to the presence of hetero-atoms like Fe, N, and B in the carbon structure. It is known that the encapsulated Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures activate the carbon layers towards the ORR and the carbon layers protect the Feâ€“Fe3C nanostructures which improve the stability.15c Furthermore, the presence of N is beneficial in many ways. The pyridinic and pyrrolic N coordinates with Fe to form Feâ€“Nx/C centres which has been considered as the active electrochemical site.16c In addition, the graphitic N modifies the Câ€“N bond length to the Oâ€“O bond length and facilitates the oxygen interaction and hence, the ORR activity.17c Similar to N, B also interacts with carbon resulting in a variety of BCx moieties which can serve as active ORR centres due to the electronegativity difference between C (âˆ¼2.5) and B (âˆ¼2).25,54 Thus, the presence of all these heteroatoms in BFNCNTs is highly beneficial for oxygen electrochemistry. The improved stability of BFNCNTs may be attributed to the presence of protective layers and the mesoporosity in the structure (as evident from Fig. 3h) that provide the facile transportation pathways and accessibility of electrochemical active centres to the reactant species. In the case of oxygen evolution, the porous structure is highly beneficial as it offers an appropriate gas outlet to prevent the gas-bubble accumulation that blocks the electrochemical active centres1b and trims down the activity with time. Overall, the presence of different active centres and the protective layers makes BFNCNTs as excellent bifunctional oxygen electrocatalysts both in terms of activity and stability.

Conclusions

In summary, a rational facile methodology has been developed to fabricate N-enriched carbon nanotubes (N-CNTs) and Feâ€“Fe3C co-embedded nanostructures in B-doped mesoporous carbon nanostructures (BFNCNTs) as a noble metal-free superior bi-functional electrocatalyst for oxygen evolution/reduction reactions. The co-embedded electrocatalyst obtained by sequential pyrolysis provides better opportunity to control the incorporation of N and B with negligible/undetectable Bâ€“N formation which is otherwise electrochemically inactive due to the compensating nature of B and N entities. The resultant mesoporous CNS with a high specific BET surface area (272 m2 gâˆ’1), enhanced defects and heterogeneity in a single structure ensures the increased electrochemical active centres, better exposure and accessibility of active sites to the reactant species. Besides the better OER/ORR activities as compared to those of commercial RuO2/Ptâ€“C catalysts and various recently reported state-of-the-art bi-functional electrocatalysts, BFNCNTs exhibit a nearly 94% current retention even after the accelerated stability test for the OER and ORR. In addition, the fuel tolerant behaviour in combination with preferable 4eâˆ’ ORR pathways warrants maximum energy harvesting. Interestingly, the overall oxygen electroactivity on BFNCNTs as appraised by Î”E (=Ej(OER)=10 âˆ’ E1/2(ORR) = 0.788 V) is much lower than several hetero-atom doped/co-doped carbon nanostructure based metal-free/transition metal incorporated state-of-the-art bi-functional electrocatalysts developed recently. In addition, we tested the viability of BFNCNTs as the cathode catalyst by fabricating a prototype battery system for powering a light emitting diode. Overall, our study suggests co-embedding may offer a new approach to revisit and modify the synthesis/design technique for carbon nanostructures in order to develop promising bi-functional electro-catalysts for both the ORR and OER. Moreover, the methodology applied here, is believed to pave the way for the design of multi-functional catalysts to assimilate the individual properties of each catalyst.

Experimental section

Materials

For synthesis, ferrocene (Merck, 98% purity), melamine (C3H6N6, S.D. Fine chemicals, 97.5% purity) and TPB ((C6H5)3B, Sigma Aldrich) are used. Milli-Q water (conductivity âˆ¼5 Ã— 10âˆ’8 S) and sodium hydroxide pellets (NaOH) are used to make the solution alkaline.

Synthesis procedure for NCNTs, BFNCNTs, BNCNTs and FNCNTs

NCNTs are synthesized by pyrolysing22,65 ferrocene (20 mg) and melamine (100 mg) at 950 Â°C (temperature ramp rate is 10 Â°C per min) for 3 h duration using a Lenton tube furnace (LTF 14/â€”/180) in a quartz tube (1 cm in diameter and 100 cm in length) with one end closed and the other end connected to a rubber bladder (Fig. S1â€ ). After the synthesis of NCNTs, the furnace was allowed to cool to attain the ambient temperature. Furthermore, 100 mg of TPB and 100 mg of ferrocene were introduced in the same quartz tube and kept in the furnace for 3 h at 850 Â°C (temperature ramp rate is 10 Â°C per min) to obtain BFNCNTs. To synthesize BNCNTs/FNCNTs, TPB/ferrocene is added in the same quartz tube (after the synthesis of NCNTs as discussed above) and subjected to 850 Â°C (ramp rate of 10 Â°C per min). The as-synthesized catalysts (BFNCNTs, BNCNTs & FNCNTs) were subjected to acid wash followed by Milli-Q water washing prior to characterization and electrochemical studies to remove the residual metal ions, if any.

It may be noted that the synthesis temperature plays a crucial role in the synthesis of hetero-atom doped carbon nanostructures. In the first step, we have chosen the pyrolysis temperature as 950 Â°C to synthesize the nitrogen-doped CNTs.
