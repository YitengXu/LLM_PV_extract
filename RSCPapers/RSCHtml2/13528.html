Abstract

Recently, perylene diimide (PDI)-based all-polymer solar cells (All-PSCs) have gained increasing attention due to molecular structural diversity, facile chemical modification, and mechanical and morphological stability. However, acceptorâ€“acceptor (Aâ€“A)-structured PDI-based polymeric acceptors (PAs) exhibit inferior photovoltaic performance to donorâ€“acceptor type and/or fused-PDI-based ones. Herein, two Aâ€“A type thermo- and photo-stable PAs, namely, PPDI-DTBT and PPDI-DTFBT, by choosing a long 2-octyldodecyl-substituted PDI unit and 4,7-dithienylbenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (DTBT) and/or partially fluorinated 4,7-dithienyl-5-fluorobenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (DTFBT) subunits to construct a polymer backbone, were developed. Both of them possess a complementary absorption profile and a similar ELUMO to donor PTB7-Th. Not only a slightly descended LUMO energy level, better molecular planarity and aggregation, and reduced exciton binding energy (Eb), but also enhanced exciton dissociation efficiency, more closed and ordered stacking, higher and balanced charge mobility and a desired microstructural morphology structure were achieved in partially fluorinated PPDI-DTFBT. Moreover, femtosecond transient absorption (fs-TA) spectra suggested faster and more efficient exciton dissociation and transfer characteristics in the PTB7-Th:PPDI-DTFBT system. These changes enabled the PPDI-DTFBT-based device to acquire a PCE of 6.04%, which was 51.76% higher than that (3.98%) of its counterpart. This increase was mainly profited from 32.70% increased JSC from 11.10 to 14.73 mA cmâˆ’2 and 13.92% enhanced FF from 46.54% to 53.02%. The current results demonstrate that precisely incorporating fluorine into an Aâ€“A type PDI-based polymer backbone can synergistically adjust the molecular aggregation and improve the photophysical process, with the aim of gaining an inspirational device efficiency in all-PSCs.

Introduction

Recently, organic solar cells (OSCs) have gained growing attention and been regarded as an environment friendly alternative to directly convert clean and sustainable solar energy into electricity, due to their richly varied chemical structure, flexibility, semitransparency and large scale roll-to-roll fabrication.1â€“6 The insurmountable downsides including weak light-harvesting ability in the visible region, fossilized electronic energy levels, morphological instability and high cost of fullerene-like electron acceptors curtailed the further development and the relevant devices exhibited a record power conversion efficiency (PCE) of 11.7%.7 To surmount this obstacle, all-polymer solar cells (all-PSCs), whose photoactive layers consist of a binary blend of polymeric donors (PDs) and polymeric acceptors (PAs), have been particularly focused on and they underwent swift and violent development, because of the unparalleled superiorities such as versatile and easily-tuned molecular structures, excellent mechanical properties, good miscibility with polymeric donors and morphological stability.8â€“10 Assisted by exploiting the efficient so-called polymerized small molecule acceptors (PSMAs) and reasonably selecting matched polymeric donors,11â€“13 all-PSCs achieved a comparable photovoltaic (PV) performance to small molecular acceptor Y6 derivative systems,14 and the state-of-the-art efficiency has also outstripped 17%.15,16

Apart from the aforementioned PSMAs, rylene diimides (RDIs), mainly embodying 1,4,5,8-naphthalenediimide (NDI) and perylene-3,4,9,10-tetracarboxylic acid diimide (perylene diimide for short, PDI) building blocks were demonstrated to be the most efficient building blocks to build sterling electron acceptors, because of the following features including, (i) strong and variable sunlight-harvesting ability, (ii) remarkable electron affinity originating from the strong electron-withdrawing diimide group, (iii) high electron transfer capability induced by quasi-planar conjugated structures, (iv) easy chemical tailoring at different positions, and (v) outstanding thermal-, chemical-, and photo-stability.17,18 Thanks to the excellent field-effect transistor (FET) mobility as high as 0.85 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 and a space-charge-limited-current (SCLC) mobility of up to 5 Ã— 10âˆ’3 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1,19,20 the NDI-based star polymer N2200 obtained via a polycondensation reaction between 2-octyldecyl substituted NDI-based dibromide and bithiophene-containing bitin exhibited a record PCE of 11.0% using green processing solvent cyclopentyl-methylether (CPME) when paired with heptamethyltrisiloxane-terminal-group-functionalized 6-octyl[1,2,3]triazole[4,5-f]isoindole-5,7(2H,6H)-dione-containing electron donor PTzBi-Si.21 Following this, the efficiency was further up-shifted to 11.76% after optimizing the morphology by utilizing the method of combining the 2-methyl-tetrahydrofuran volatile solvent and solvent vapor annealing.22 In contrast, the PDI moiety has an extended conjugated system and undergoes stronger intermolecular interaction, which is conducive to acquiring a high crystallinity, an elevated electron mobility and thus anticipated PV properties.23,24 Nevertheless, the PDI-based PAs displayed a relatively inferior device performance (6.58%,25 7.57%26 and 8.59%27 for the corresponding unfused PPDIODT, PDI-V, and fused NDP-V) to NDI-based counterparts. The first PDI-containing polymer PPDIDTT made of bis(2-decyltetradecyl)-side-chain-substituted PDI and dithienothiophene (DTT) was exploited by Zhan and his coworkers in 2007 and exhibited a PCE of 1.03% when paired with bi(thienylenevinylene)-bonded polythiphene.28 Subsequently, the PCE was increased to 3.04% when choosing PBDTTT-C-T as the donor in 2014 and then was continuously maximized to 3.49% when matched with donor BDDT in 2017.29,30 Assisted by the design idea of the well-developed PDs, substantial efforts devoted to constructing donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) structured alternating conjugated polymers (CPs) via coupling with numerous electron-rich units, i.e., vinylene (V),26,27,31 fluorene (F),31â€“33 carbazole (Cz),31,32 benzo[1,2-b:4,5-bâ€²]dithiophene (BDT),33â€“36 naphtho[1,2-b:5,6-bâ€²]dithiophene (NDT),36 thiophene (T),25,31,37,38 selenophene (Se),37,39 tellurophene (Te),40 bithiophene (2T),41â€“43 thieno[3,2-b]thiophene (TT),36â€“38 thienylenevinylene (TVT),44 cyclopenta[1,2-b:5,4-bâ€²]dithiophene (CPDT),45 dithieno[3,2-b:2â€²,3â€²-d]pyrrole (DTP),31 and so on. Apparently, among these PDI-based Dâ€“A type CPs, thiophene, tellurophene and vinylene building blocks possessing relatively small volume cubage exhibited an excellent PCE of over 6% (Table S1, ESIâ€ ). Besides the aforementioned Dâ€“A CPs, another combination scenario was the selection of two electron-deficient units to build up acceptorâ€“acceptor (Aâ€“A) type PAs (Chart 1), due to their sterling air-stability and outstanding electron transfer properties.46â€“50 Actually, there were limited Aâ€“A type PDI-based CPs to be focused on.48â€“53 Early in 2012, Zhan and his coworkers developed an Aâ€“A type PA with excellent air stability by combining PDI and fluorenone.48 Following this, Li et al. introduced the electro-deficient benzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (BT) moiety into a PDI-based polymer backbone, and found that 2-octyl-dodecyl (OD) substituted PDI-based P1-C20 with an optical bandgap (Eoptg) of 2.01 eV acquired a PCE of 0.31%.49 In 2018, Zhou et al. incorporated 4,7-dithienylbenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (DTBT) into a polymer backbone to develop the PPDI-DTBT, exhibiting a remarkably reduced Eoptg of 1.59 eV, increased EHOMO and ELUMO, and thus the markedly enhanced PCE of 4.07% resulting from the increased short circuit current density (JSC) and fill factor (FF) regardless of the reduced open circuit voltage (VOC); meanwhile, after performing annulation to obtain fused PDI units, the PCE of the corresponding polymer PFPDI-DTBT increased to 6.23%.51 Besides BT, isoindiago (IIG)-based PIIG-PDI(OD),50 thieno[2â€²,3â€²:5,6]-pyrido[3,4-g]thieno[3,2-c]isoquinoline-5,11-dione (TPTI)-based PIP,52 TPTI's isomer thieno[2â€²,3â€²:4,5]-pyrido[2,3-g]thieno[3,2-c]quinoline-4,10-dione (TPTQ)-based PQP and its difluorinated PFP,52 and diketopyrrolopyrrole (DPP)-based PDPP-PDI53 were developed, displaying unsatisfactory PV performance with the PCEs spanning from 0.97% to 3.52% (Table S1, ESIâ€ ). Therefore, it is of great significance and challenging to explore new efficient Aâ€“A type PAs and to delineate the structureâ€“propertyâ€“performance relationship, aimed at enhancing the PV performance for all-PSCs.

Inspired by the merits of electron-deficient PDI7,26,51 and the advantages of BT,54,55 herein, we selected the partially-fluorinated 4,7-dithienyl-5-fluorobenzo[c][1,2,5]thiadiazole (DTFBT) moiety to construct the Aâ€“A type PDI-based polymeric acceptor PPDI-DTFBT. For comparison, the unfluorinated PPDI-DTBT was prepared for better comparison (Scheme 1). The effects of partially fluorinating BT on absorption, energy levels, molecular configuration, electrostatic potential (ESP), exciton binding energy (Eb), charge mobility, morphology, photophysical procedure and thus PV performance were investigated comprehensively. It was found that partial fluorination of BT not only led to a reduced Eb, enhanced aggregation, and desirable morphology, but also led to improved photophysical properties. Therefore, the PPDI-DTFBT-based device achieved a winning efficiency of 6.04% over that of its counterpart PPDI-DTBT when paired with PTB7-Th.

Photovoltaic properties

To find out the impact of partially fluorinating the BT core on the photovoltaic performance, devices with the configuration of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/PTBT-Th:polymeric acceptors/PDINO/Al were fabricated. The device test process included selecting the donor:acceptor (D:A) ratio using the solvent additive. As shown in Fig. S17 (ESIâ€ ), when the D:A ratio was varied from 1:1.5 to 1:1.2, to 1:1, then to 1.2:1, the PCE of PPDI-DTBT-based devices first increased from 2.34% to 2.77% then decreased to 2.27% and then to 1.89%, and in contrast, the PCE of the PPDI-DTFBT-based device first increased from 2.27% to 2.59% and then decreased to 2.42% and then to 1.77% (Table S4, ESIâ€ ). Therefore, the best weight ratio for both these polymer acceptors was 1:1.2. Subsequently, the solvent additives 1,8-diiodooctane (DIO) and 1-chloronaphthalene (CN) were selected to adjust the microstructural morphology of the photoactive layer and further maximize the device efficiency.55,67,68 When utilizing 3% DIO (volume content) as the additive, an increase of 14.08% in the PCE from 2.77% to 3.16% was achieved in the PPDI-DTBT-based device, mainly benefiting from an 21.25% elevated FF. In the meantime, an increase in 9.65% in the PCE from 2.59% to 2.84% was achieved after partially fluorinating the BT, chiefly profiting from collectively 5.38% increased JSC and 3.19% ameliorated FF (Fig. S18 and Table S4, ESIâ€ ). Following this, after utilizing 0.5% CN (volume content), an increase of 33.41% in the JSC from 8.32 to 11.10 mA cmâˆ’2 contributed to an increase of 25.95% in the PCE from 3.16% to 3.98% in the PPDI-DTBT-based device in spite of the marginally decreased VOC and FF. In contrast, an increase of 87.88% in the JSC from 7.84 to 14.73 mA cmâˆ’2 and an increase of 12.93% in the FF from 46.95% to 53.02% jointly contributed to an increase of 112.68% in PCE from 2.84% to 6.04% in the PPDI-DTFBT-based device (Fig. S18 and Table S4, ESIâ€ ). We can see that the optimal PTB7-Th:PPDI-DTBT-based device achieved a PCE of 3.98%, with a VOC of 0.76 V, a JSC of 11.10 mA cmâˆ’2 and an FF of 46.54%, which was comparable to the previously reported value measured from Zhou's group.51 Similarly, the best PTB7-Th:PPDI-DTFBT-based device exhibited a PCE of 6.04%, with a VOC of 0.77 V, a JSC of 14.73 mA cmâˆ’2 and an FF of 53.02% (Fig. 5(a) and Table 4). Conclusions

To conclude, via condensation polymerization between PDI-based dibromide and 4,7-dithienylbenzothiadiazole (DTBT)- and/or 4,7-dithienyl-5-fluorobenzothiadiazole (DTFBT)-containing bistins, two Aâ€“A type polymeric acceptors PPDI-DTBT and PPDI-DTFBT were synthesized. Both of them exhibited wide absorption spanning from 300 to 780 nm, sterling thermo- and photo-stability, and an ELUMO of about âˆ’3.87 eV, exhibiting good compatibility with PTB7-Th. The partial fluorination barely influenced spectral complementarity regardless of an increased optical bandgap. And the gradually down-shifted EHOMO, reduced Eb, and better molecular planarity were also observed. Further fs-TA analysis exhibited that there exist efficient exciton dissociation and a transfer procedure in PTB7-Th:PPDI-DTFBT, which led to a higher photocurrent and fill factor. As a result, the conjointly 32.70% increased JSC and 13.92% enhanced FF were achieved, resulting in an 51.76% increased PCE from 3.98% to 6.04% after partially fluorinating the BT core. This improvement was benefited from the increased exciton dissociation efficiency, strengthened molecular aggregation and concomitant elevated and more balanced charge mobility, as well as improved microstructural morphology. Our experimental results proved that precisely choosing the fluorination can effectively adjust the molecular aggregation and influence the photophysical properties, giving rise to boosted device performance in PDI-based all-PSCs.
