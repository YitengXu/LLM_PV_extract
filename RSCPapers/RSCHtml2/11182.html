Abstract

Two dimensional (2D) organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskites have attracted great interest due to their tunable band gap and structural stability. In this study, biaxial strain behavior and carrier mobility of monolayers (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 are investigated by first principles calculations. (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 still retain their structural stability at Îµ = 13% and Îµ = 15%, respectively. Ab initio molecular dynamics (AIMD) simulation has confirmed that the system at 300 K is still thermodynamically stable at a biaxial strain of Îµ = 8%. The band gaps of (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 calculated from the HSE06 functional are increased from 2.427 and 1.953 eV at zero strain to 3.002 and 2.626 eV at Îµ = 8%. Deformation potential (DP) models based on longitudinal acoustic phonon (LAP) and optical phonon (OP) scattering are used to investigate mobility. The mobility of (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 is lower than that of (C4H9NH3)2SnI4. It is mainly determined by the scattering from OP with lower energy and decreases sharply with an increase in biaxial strain. Compared with Pb based perovskites, (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 exhibits high carrier mobility and thermodynamic stability.

1. Introduction

Pure perovskite compounds usually have a crystal structure similar to that of CaTiO3, in which an O anion is used to combine Ca and Ti cations.1 An organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskite can be regarded as a derivative of a perovskite oxide, in which organic molecules, metal cations and anions replace Ca, Ti and O atoms, respectively.2,3 Halide perovskites have attracted much attention due to their unique optical properties in photovoltaic and optoelectronic devices.4â€“6 Their chemical formula is of the form ABX3, where A is an organic cation (CH3NH3+, NH2CHNH2+, C2H5NH3+, etc.), B is a divalent metal cation (Pb2+, Cu2+, Sn2+, etc.), and X is a halogen anion (Iâˆ’, Brâˆ’ and Clâˆ’). The inorganic atomic layer is composed of six halogen anions forming a perfect octahedral structure with one divalent metal cation at its center. Organic molecules are filled between the inorganic atomic layers. In recent years, a hybrid halide perovskite has been widely studied as a promising photoelectric material.7â€“9 It is found that the power conversion efficiency of solar cells based on the perovskite CH3NH3PbI3 film has reached 21%â€“22%,10,11 and this record may be exceeded soon. However, the application of the compound is still facing challenges due to its poor environmental stability and toxicity.3

In contrast, a two-dimensional (2D) organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskite is regarded as a promising alternative material due to its low fabrication cost and excellent environmental stability.12,13 Recent studies have found that the photoelectric conversion efficiency of solar cells based on the 2D perovskite has reached 12.5%.14 Compared with the bulk CH3NH3PbI3, the 2D material has a smooth surface, and the layers are bonded by van der Waals interactions.15â€“28 So, impurities, defects and dangling bonds are more easily eliminated in them. Carrier mobility is a physical quantity that describes the speed of carrier movement in semiconductors under electric field, including electron and hole mobilities.29,30 It plays an important role in electronic devices.31â€“33 The high carrier mobility of organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskites is still a controversial issue.12,34â€“36 It is well known that the physical properties of some materials can be improved by strain engineering.37â€“43 The hole mobility of widely used silicon is about 400 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1, which seriously limits its application. However, the value is increased to 50% using strain engineering.44 2D materials can bear more strain than bulk ones, and some of them can maintain a good elastic behavior with a strain of over 20%.45 Strain behavior can be used to tune the photoelectric properties of materials, which are widely used in optoelectronic devices, such as field-effect transistors, light-emitting diodes and photodetectors.46â€“50 Recently, 2D hybrid perovskites, (C4H9NH3)2MI4 (M = Ge, Sn), have been successfully synthesised.51 Compared with Pb based perovskites, they may be promising materials due to their non-toxicity, stability and low effective mass.12 Previously, research on them mainly focused on their structure, optical properties and electronic structure. However, their strain behavior and carrier mobility have not been investigated. This encourages us to do further research on them.

In this study, we perform a detailed first-principles investigation on the biaxial strain behavior of (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4. First, the materials are applied with a biaxial strain in the range from zero to 20% with an interval of 1%. The elastic range is predicted accurately. Then, the carrier mobility is calculated using the deformation potential (DP) models based on longitudinal acoustic phonon (LAP) and optical phonon (OP) scattering. The effect of strain on carrier mobility is studied. The upper limit of carrier mobility is predicted. This study provides some valuable knowledge for insight into the novel organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskites.

2. First principles calculations

The main calculations are performed using the Vienna ab initio simulation package (VASP 5.4).52â€“54 The exchangeâ€“correlation functional is treated within the generalized gradient approximation (GGA) of Perdew, Burke, and Ernzerhof (PBE). The projector-augmented wave (PAW) method is used to describe the interaction between the core and electron. The (C4H9NH3)2MI4 (M = Ge or Sn) unit cell exhibits two possible configurations with space groups C222 (no. 21)13 and PBCA (no. 61)12,51 and is composed of 156 atoms, including 16 I atoms, 4 Ge or Sn atoms, 8 N atoms, 32 C atoms, and 96 H atoms. The lattice and atomic position optimizations are performed with an energy cutoff of 500 eV and a 3 Ã— 3 Ã— 1 k-point sampling. All atoms are fully relaxed during geometric optimization until the maximum force is less than 4 Ã— 10âˆ’3 eV Ã…âˆ’1 and the total energy change is less than 10âˆ’6 eV. In this study, the van der Waals (VdW) interaction between layers is corrected by the DFT-D3 method. Monolayers (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 are obtained by separation from the bulk structure with the space group PBCA (no. 61). A 30 Ã… vacuum region is used to completely eliminate the interaction between layers.55 The GGAâ€“PBE functional underestimates the band gaps of semiconductors or oxides. So, a hybrid Heydâ€“Scuseriaâ€“Ernzerhof functional (HSE06) is used to investigate electronic band structures.56 Furthermore, the spin orbit coupling (SOC) effect is also considered during the calculation, which is more reasonable to predict the effective mass. Carrier mobility is calculated using LAP and OP models, which will be explained in the Results and discussion section.

4. Conclusions

In summary, biaxial strain behavior and carrier mobility of (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 are investigated using density functional theory. The first principles calculations reveal that (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 can retain their structure stable when the biaxial strain is less than 13% and 15%. Molecular dynamics simulations show that they maintain thermodynamic stability at room temperature as the strain is less than 8%. The band gap increases with the biaxial strain changing from zero to 8%. The values for (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 are 1.680â€“2.156 eV in PBEâ€“SOC, 1.811â€“2.299 eV in GGAâ€“PBE and 2.427â€“3.002 eV in HSE06, while the values for (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 are 1.165â€“1.807 eV in PBEâ€“SOC, 1.410â€“1.984 eV in GGAâ€“PBE and 1.953â€“2.626 eV in HSE06. The variation of electron affinity and ionization energy with strain is also analyzed. The electron affinity calculated from HSE06 is in the range from 2.514 to 2.778 eV for (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and 2.686 to 2.769 eV for (C4H9NH3)2SnI4. The ionization energies are 5.205â€“5.516 eV and 4.720â€“5.312 eV, respectively.

The semiempirical models based on LAP and OP scattering are used to investigate mobility. The values from OP scattering are lower than those from the LAP model. Compared with that of inorganic materials, the mobility of the organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskite is mainly determined by the scattering from optical phonons with lower energy. At 300 K, the phonon limited mobility is estimated to be Î¼e,x = 131/Î¼e,y = 86 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 and Î¼h,x = 67/Î¼h,y = 47 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 for (C4H9NH3)2GeI4 and Î¼e,x = 527/Î¼e,y = 589 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 and Î¼h,x = 192/Î¼h,y = 187 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 for (C4H9NH3)2SnI4. High carrier mobility can be obtained by replacing Pb with Sn in organicâ€“inorganic hybrid perovskites. The effective mass increases with the biaxial strain, which leads to the rapid decrease in mobility. Compared with Pb-based perovskites, (C4H9NH3)2SnI4 exhibits good mechanical properties, thermodynamic stability, moderate band gap and high carrier mobility, so it will become a promising material in nanoelectronic devices.
