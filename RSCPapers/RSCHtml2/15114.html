Abstract

Fossil fuel alternatives, such as solar energy, are moving to the forefront in a variety of research fields. Polymer solar cells (PSCs) hold promise for their potential to be used as low-cost and efficient solar energy converters. PSCs have been commonly made from bicontinuous polymer:fullerene composites or so-called bulk heterojunctions. The conjugated polymer donors and the fullerene derivative acceptors are the key materials for high performance PSCs. In the present study, we have performed density functional theory calculations to investigate the electronic structures and magnetic properties of several representative C60 fullerene derivatives, seeking ways to improve their efficiency as acceptors of photovoltaic devices. In our survey, we have successfully correlated the LUMO energy level as well as chemical hardness, hyper-hardness, nucleus-independent chemical shift, and static dipole polarizability of PC60BM-like fullerene derivative acceptors with the experimental open circuit voltage of the photovoltaic device based on the P3HT:fullerene blend. The obtained structureâ€“property correlations allow finding the best fullerene acceptor match for the P3HT donor. For this purpose, four new fullerene derivatives are proposed and the output parameters for the corresponding P3HT-based devices are predicted. It is found that the proposed fullerene derivatives exhibit better photovoltaic properties than the traditional PC60BM acceptor. The present study opens the way for manipulating fullerene derivatives and developing promising acceptors for solar cell applications.

1. Introduction

The development of clean and renewable energy is vital to meet the ever-increasing global energy demands arising from rapid economic expansion and increasing world population, while minimizing fossil-fuel depletion, pollution and global warming.1 Accordingly, new technologies for energy conversion e.g. solar cells and fuel cells are under intensive research.2 Solar cells are ideal alternatives to the traditional energy sources such as coal and oil. Bulk heterojunction (BHJ) polymer solar cells (PSCs) have drawn broad attention since their first report in 19953â€“5 because of their advantages of easy fabrication, low cost, light weight and the possibility of fabricating flexible devices in comparison with traditional inorganic semiconductor photovoltaics.6 Combining an electron-deficient compound (acceptor) and an electron-rich conjugated polymer (donor) leads to the formation of film which contains high donorâ€“acceptor interfacial areas. When the film is placed between appropriate electrodes it demonstrates the photovoltaic effect and the resulting valuable device is identified as the BHJ solar cell.3,7 When light irradiates on the PSCs, the semiconducting donor and acceptor materials in the photoactive layer absorb photons to form excitons (bounded electronâ€“hole pairs), then the excitons diffuse to the donor/acceptor interfaces where they dissociate into electrons at the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) level of the acceptor and holes at the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) level of the donor. The dissociated electrons and holes then move to and are collected by negative and positive electrodes, respectively, to realize the photon-to-electron conversion.6

In recent years, efforts for developing and optimizing materials used in PSCs have increased8,9 and various materials have been designed and synthesized in order to achieve high performance PSCs.10,11 In this way, the electron acceptors are of the same importance as the electron donors for increasing the efficiency of PSCs. However the research efforts devoted to the acceptors are much less than those to the polymer donors. Among various acceptor substances, fullerenes with inherent high electron affinity and being able to transport charges effectively have been introduced as intriguing acceptors for BHJ solar cells.7 Recently significant research efforts have already been put into developing large-area technologies for polymer:fullerene BHJ solar cells with record power conversion efficiency (PCE) approaching 6%.11â€“14 Different combinations of donor and acceptor materials have been used to make the active layer of polymer:fullerene BHJ solar cells. One of the most efficient BHJ devices is a blend of poly(3-hexylthiophene) (P3HT) as an electron donor and a soluble C60 derivative, [6,6]-phenyl-C61-butyric acid methyl ester (PC60BM), as an electron acceptor that showed a PCE of up to 5.5% for single layer devices.15 To further improve the PCE, the design and synthesis of new fullerene acceptors have been the priority of many research surveys.7,15â€“20 Although some n-type conjugated polymers with stronger visible absorption have been applied as acceptors in the PSCs,21â€“25 the fullerene derivatives PC60BM still dominate the acceptors in the PSCs for their high efficiency.6

In a polymer:fullerene solar cell, merely a little improvement of the ISC value is possible since the highest reachable value of ISC is very close to its maximum feasible value27 while there are still several considerable paths for improving VOC. It is also well known that the VOC of the PSCs is proportional to the difference between the HOMO of the electron donor and the LUMO of the electron acceptor.7,27 Moreover, there is a crucial threshold value in the energy gap of the donor LUMO and the acceptor LUMO of about 0.5 eV for an efficient charge transfer from the donor polymer to the acceptor fullerene.28 So, the VOC can be increased by preserving this donorâ€“acceptor LUMOâ€“LUMO gap and increasing the LUMO level of the electron acceptor molecule revealing that it is necessary to find the appropriate acceptor for each donor polymer.7 Therefore, the LUMO energy level of the fullerene derivatives is a key parameter for the application as an acceptor to match a polymer donor.6 Recently, new fullerene derivatives with up-shifted LUMO energy levels were developed and the PSCs based on P3HT as a donor and the new fullerene derivatives as an acceptor show higher VOC and improved PSC efficiency.7,18,29,30

In view of recent research progress on the new fullerene derivative acceptors, in the present study, we set out to understand the effect of different substitutions on the acceptor strength of these materials. We have employed various quantum chemical properties belonging to different categories to establish several correlation relationships for the prediction of VOC of fullerene based PSCs. Accordingly, some efficient descriptors such as LUMO energy, chemical hardness, and hyper-hardness are considered for the reproduction of experimental VOC. Moreover, owing to the important role of static dipole polarizability in governing various physical and chemical phenomena, the relationship between this quantity and experimental VOC is also checked. On the other hand, the unique physical and chemical properties of fullerenes are due to the existence of an extended Ï€-electron system. For this reason, the study of aromaticity of fullerene derivatives can elucidate various aspects regarding their structure and reactivity. Accordingly, we also investigate the magnetic properties of the interior space of fullerene derivatives by computing the nucleus-independent chemical shift (NICS).31â€“33 This quantity not only measures electron delocalization inside the cage but also is relevant to the reactivity of fullerene derivatives based on its connection with chemical hardness. However, the obtained structureâ€“property relationships enable us to foresee the VOC of polymer:fullerene solar cells once we know their electronic and magnetic properties of the corresponding acceptors. Thus, in the final part of the present study, we propose several new fullerene-based acceptors which have the desired properties for increasing the efficiency of PSCs.

2. Computational details

Modern density functional theory (DFT) provides a valuable tool for calculating a variety of molecular properties and electronic structures. Nonetheless, the accuracy of density functional methods depends upon the applied exchangeâ€“correlation (XC) functionals. In this study, four density functional methods belonging to different categories of DFT methods have been tested. In particular, one functional of generalized gradient approximation (GGA) type, PBE,34 one meta-GGA functional, TPSS,35 and two hybrid-GGA functionals, B3LYP as well as HSE,36 have been assessed against the experimental LUMO energy (ÎµLUMO) and HOMOâ€“LUMO energy gap (Eg) of PC60BM. All calculations were performed by using the 6-31G(d) basis set with the help of GAUSSIAN09 suite of programs.37 In Fig. 1, we compared the density of states (DOSs) of the PC60BM calculated by HSE, PBE, TPSS and B3LYP methods. The figure demonstrates diverse predictive ability of the tested functionals. The numerical values of the calculated ÎµLUMO and Eg of PC60BM as well as the corresponding experimental data38,39 are given in the table below Fig. 1. While the PBE and TPSS functionals underestimate the band gap by about 30â€“40%, the two hybrid functionals (B3LYP and HSE) give better estimations of ÎµLUMO and Eg. In particular HSE remarkably outperforms other functionals and its results for ÎµLUMO and Eg are in better agreement with the experimental values. This outcome is well supported by previous investigations where the hybrid functionals have been suggested as efficient methods for band gap calculations.40,41 In fact hybrid functionals improve the predicted band gaps by combining the overestimation of band gaps from Hartreeâ€“Fock with the underestimation from GGA.41 On the other hand, the selection of a suitable basis set is another important task which should be carried out with care in order to advance safely to the prediction of reliable properties. Therefore, to verify the compatibility of the 6-31G(d) basis set, we have also examined a larger 6-31 + G(d,p) basis set for computing various properties of PC60BM. A comparison between the results of two basis sets, given in Table S1 of the ESI,â€  indicates that there is no significant difference between the magnitudes of various properties. Accordingly, we rely on the 6-31G(d) basis set as a good compromise between performance and computational cost. In fact, our main focus is on the trends that are obtained for the different properties due to change in the fullerene structure. A larger basis set may change the quantitative value of the calculated properties but do not change the trends in the data. However, all quantum chemical calculations of this study have been carried out using the HSE functional together with the 6-31G(d) basis set. After geometry optimization and solving vibrational modes (to prove that all the stationary points are minima of the potential energy surfaces) all electronic and magnetic properties are calculated at this level of theory.

3. 1. 4 The static dipole polarizability. Static dipole polarizability is another important property which defines many physical and chemical processes such as intermolecular interactions, optical properties, chemical reactions, and many others.41 Thus, investigating the polarizability of fullerenes and their derivatives is highly informative for designing novel materials with advanced properties. In a recent review article, Sabirov55 covered experimental and main theoretical studies in the field of polarizability of fullerenes and their derivatives from 1990s to 2014.

3. 1. 5 NICS characterization. The study of aromaticity of fullerenes and their precursors provides valuable insight into their structure and reactivity. Aromaticity is defined as the ability of a compound to sustain an induced ring current, which causes extra stabilization/destabilization in the case of aromatic/antiaromatic compounds. Probably, the most widely employed methods among several ones to evaluate aromaticity are based on magnetic properties.60 The NICS index as a simple and efficient aromaticity probe is proposed by Chen et al.32,33 and corresponds to the negative of the magnetic shielding computed at the geometric center of a ring system. The negative NICS value in interior positions of the molecules (magnetically shielded) signifies the presence of induced diatropic ring currents or aromaticity, while the positive value at each point (magnetically deshielded) is a proof of paratropic ring currents or antiaromaticity.33,60

In order to evaluate the magnetic field due to electron mobility inside the fullerene derivatives, we have calculated isotropic NICS, NICSIso, at the geometric center of considered fullerenes (F1â€“F9). As evident from Table 1, monoadduct derivatives (F1â€“F6) have almost similar NICSIso values while the bis and trisadducts (F7â€“F9) exhibit larger NICSIso indicating greater electron mobility inside these cages. The enhancement in the aromatic character of F7â€“F9 derivatives is in line with their greater chemical hardness, that is, the stability of these derivatives is supported by both electronic and magnetic evidences. Moreover, existence of a correlation between magnetic-based indices of aromaticity and reactivity-based criteria provides an opportunity to consider NICSIso values as another descriptor for estimating VOC. The variation of NICSIso values for our objective fullerene derivatives versus the experimentally determined VOC is illustrated in Fig. 6. A linear relationship with a high correlation coefficient (R = 0.933) indicates the reliability of the NICSIso index for the prediction of VOC. We have also checked the correlation between NICSIso as a local magnetic index and ÎµLUMO as a global electronic descriptor for the studied fullerene derivatives. The remarkable correlation (R = 0.971) shown in Fig. 7 infers that the shift at the LUMO level to higher energies and consequently the enhancement in the efficiency of the fullerene based PSCs can be predicted by isotropic magnetic shielding inside the fullerene cage.

3. 2 Designing new fullerene-based acceptors for the P3HT:fullerene solar cell

The final section of the present article is devoted to design fullerene derivatives with superior performance as acceptors of a P3HT:fullerene solar cell. In fact, the results of this work with the aid of previous findings enable us to propose new fullerene structures and to foresee the output parameters for the PSCs made with these fullerenes as acceptors and P3HT as a donor. Traditionally, the attention of such studies is directed to the use of frontier orbitals. Levels of frontier orbitals provide a good orienteer to screen the HOMO and LUMO levels of a candidate fullerene acceptor in a highly automated manner.61 Herein, we focus our attention on several descriptors derived not only from frontier orbitals but also from electric response properties. Moreover, the use of magnetic properties for the estimation of VOC is also investigated. It should be noted that the VOC of polymer:fullerene solar cells depends on several factors such as the position of the LUMO level of the acceptor, the injection barriers, the ideality factor, the recombination, the mobility and the morphology of the blend.62 For this reason, we restricted our search to the structures with the molecular dimension close to the fullerene derivatives studied in the present work (F1â€“F9). First we refer to the points that are taken into account for designing new fullerene acceptors. (i) Altering the aliphatic chain length as well as changing the alkoxyl substituent on the aliphatic tile affect the LUMO level of fullerene derivatives. (ii) Replacement of fullerene monoadducts by bis- and trisadducts of C60 enhances the acceptor efficiency. (iii) Attaching the heteroatoms to the external surface of fullerenes (exohedral doping) increases the LUMO level. In this context, Akasaka and co-workers have reported the synthesis of stable fullerene silirane derivatives.63 Moreover, quantum chemical calculations show that fullerene silirane derivatives with increased LUMO levels are useful to increase the VOC of a PSC.64

Considering F1, F4, F6 and F7 as the parent structures and utilizing the above points, we suggest four new fullerene derivatives as presented in Fig. 8. P1 is designed on the basis of the reduction of the aliphatic chain in F1 and increasing the number of adducts to three. The two proposed P4 and P6 derivatives have similar structures to F4 and F6 unless the attached atom to the C60 cage is silicon instead of carbon. The last structure, P7, is suggested so that doping with the silicon atom is accompanied by increasing the number of adducts to three. However, for all the proposed fullerene derivatives, the geometry optimization were performed at the same level of theory as the F1â€“F9 derivatives, that is HSE/6-31g(d). The calculated properties including ÎµLUMO, hardness, hyper-hardness, mean polarizability, and NICSIso for proposed fullerenes are collected in Table 2. Compared to the primary building block (PC60BM), the LUMO levels of the proposed fullerenes increase by about 0.10â€“0.33 eV. To verify the effect of silicon doping and trisadduct on the electronic structure of C60, the DOS plots of proposed fullerene derivatives are presented in Fig. 9. To make the comparison easier, the DOSs of the corresponding parent structures are also included. The plots show that the LUMO levels of the new fullerene derivatives shift toward higher energies when they are compared with their parent structures. The amounts of up-shift in LUMO levels are about 0.27, 0.06, 0.04 and 0.20 eV, respectively, for P1, P4, P6, and P7. Table 2 reveals that other considered properties such as mean dipole polarizability and NICSIso vary in a consistent fashion with LUMO energy. In P1 and P7 structures, the maximum up-shifts in ÎµLUMO are accompanied by maximum mean polarizability and larger NICSIso values. We have used these properties for predicting the VOC of constructed P3HT-based PSCs. Table 3 collects the VOC values obtained from the correlation equations with ÎµLUMO, Î±, and NICSIso. Moreover, the amount of increase in VOC with respect to the parent structures is also included in this table. Obviously, increases up to 200 mV are predicted for VOC of the devices fabricated with these model fullerenes. Although the predicted VOC obtained by various descriptors have different values, similar trends are observed for the VOC variation from P1 to P7. The results demonstrate that in comparison to the presence of heteroatoms, the increasing in the number of adducts has more impact on the overall photovoltaic performance of the PSCs. Among four model fullerenes, the structures P1 and P7 are suggested as the most promising acceptors for application in the P3HT:fullerene solar cell.

Lastly, it is worth mentioning that the other two quantities, chemical hardness and hyper-hardness, fail to predict the correct trend for VOC of the proposed fullerene derivatives. This may be due to the approximations used for estimating these quantities. In fact, there is no way to obtain correct analytical derivatives of energy with respect to the number of electrons. Hence, both Î· and Î³ are estimated by approximate approaches which are mainly based on electron affinity and ionization potentials. On the other hand, no experimental measurements of electron affinity and ionization potentials are available. Thus, as demonstrated by the poor correlations between VOC and Î· or Î³ (Fig. 4), chemical hardness and hyper-hardness are not appropriate descriptors for the prediction of the VOC of polymer:fullerene solar cells. In contrast, the LUMO energy level, mean dipole polarizability, and NICSIso are found to be reliable properties for predicting the acceptor strength of the deigned fullerene acceptors.

4. Conclusion and perspectives

Improving the efficiency of fullerene-based organic solar cells is a rapidly developing interdisciplinary field between the photovoltaics and fullerene materials science. The present study assesses the feasibility of theoretical prediction of the open circuit voltage for a PSC based on the quantum chemical properties of its fullerene acceptor. For this purpose, we first investigated the electronic and magnetic properties of nine fullerene derivatives already used in the fabrication of P3HT:fullerene solar cells. We have developed several structureâ€“property relationships based on the correlations between VOC with ÎµLUMO, Î±, Î·, Î³, and NICSIso. According to the correlations found, the most efficient fullerene-based acceptors are characterized by the highest LUMO energy level, maximum hardness, maximum dipole polarizability, and a larger magnetic field inside the cage. Despite the fact that the discussed data are mainly computational, they well correlate with the known photovoltaic performance of fullerene derivatives. However, these correlations give us the opportunity to search for new fullerene derivatives and increase the efficiency of P3HT-based solar cells. Accordingly, in the second part of the present work we proposed four new fullerene derivatives and predicted the output parameters for the corresponding P3HT-based photovoltaic devices. The results demonstrate that increasing the number of adducts in the fullerene structure is most influential factor on the overall photovoltaic performance of the PSCs. Compared to PC60BM, the proposed fullerenes increase the VOC of P3HT-based PSC by about 40%. Important finding to be emphasized is that the predicted VOC by the use of different descriptors exhibit similar trends. It is worth mentioning that we have performed our calculations in the vacuum in order to find a simple approach for the theoretical prediction of VOC of a solar cell. Due to the slight dependence of the considered properties on the solvent effect,65 we expect that the obtained correlations can also be applicable in the presence of solvent. However, this idea is in its infancy and further efforts such as simulating the real media by considering the solvent model can improve the results. Indeed, the present study might serve as a modest spur to induce extensive efforts in this field. We hope that our investigations on fullerene derivatives will be useful for further theoretical and especially experimental studies dealing with applications.
