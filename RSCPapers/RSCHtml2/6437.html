Enhanced performance of ZnO microwire/PEDOT:PSS heterojunction ultraviolet photodetectors via carbon nanohorns and DMSO treatment

Yang Liu , Mingming Jiang *, Peng Wan , Tong Xu , Daning Shi and Caixia Kan *
College of Physics, MIIT Key Laboratory of Aerospace Information Materials and Physics, Key Laboratory for Intelligent Nano Materials and Devices, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics, No. 29 Jiangjun Road, Nanjing 211106, P. R. China. E-mail: mmjiang@nuaa.edu.cn; cxkan@nuaa.edu.cn

First published on 8th August 2022

Abstract

Conducting polymers with stability, flexibility and transparency are highly desired as the crucial component of multifunctional wearable electronic devices due to the growing requirements but suffer from poor electrical characteristics. In this work, carbon nanohorns (CNHs) and dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) were employed to modulate the electrical performances of poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene):polystyrene sulfonate (PEDOT:PSS) samples. Combining a Ga-doped ZnO microwire (ZnO:Ga MW), the treated PEDOT:PSS sample was used to fabricate an inorganicorganic heterojunction photodetector. The device exhibited excellent photoresponse properties, containing a rectification ratio of 105, a responsivity of 0.273 A W1, a specific detectivity of 1.17 1012 Jones and an external quantum efficiency (EQE) of about 91.5% under 370 nm light illumination in a self-driving operation manner. Compared with the detector that uses untreated PEDOT:PSS, the performance features of the rectification ratio, photoresponsivity, specific detectivity and EQE have been distinctly increased over 9.0, 10.9, 9.1 and 10.9 times, respectively. In particular, the response times were greatly reduced from 1.53/16.9 ms to 152 s/13.5 ms. The enhanced photoresponse characteristics were ascribed to the increased photoelectrical properties of the treated PEDOT:PSS via the CNHs&DMSO co-additive. The electrical conductivity of the DMSO treated PEDOT:PSS was remarkably increased, thus leading to an enhanced rectification ratio of the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS heterojunction while the improved photoresponse performances mainly originated from the incorporated CNHs, supplying a high-efficiency transport channel for the photogenerated electronholes. This work can provide a potential candidate for developing high-performance flexible optoelectronic devices.

1 Introduction

Conducting polymers have attracted tremendous attention for their potential applications in photodetectors, solar cells, organic light emitting diodes, flexible optoelectronic devices and so on.17 Compared with inorganic materials, conducting polymers demonstrate significant advantages of light-weight, low-temperature processing, low-cost fabrication, excellent electrical performances and mechanical flexibility, providing potential applications in power-efficient, miniaturized, integrated and wearable optoelectronic devices.813 Currently, a large number of conducting polymers (like polypyrrole, polyaniline, poly(3-hexylthiophene), poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene):polystyrene sulfonate (PEDOT:PSS), etc.) are being investigated to develop flexible optoelectronic devices.8,1417 Due to the excellent conductivity, high hole affinity, chemically stability and optical properties, the PEDOT:PSS polymer has been treated as a desired candidate to fabricate organic light emitting devices, photodetectors, biosensors, etc.1821 However, there are still great challenges that need to be resolved for the commercial applications, including inadequate conductivity, reliability issues and lower carrier mobility, which would lead to high power wastage and low operational speed.19,22,23

Recently, various methods have been used to modulate the electrical properties of PEDOT:PSS samples, like rapid thermal annealing, structure engineering, post treatment using acids, etc.2428 For example, Yong et al. employed a PEDOT:PSS film as the electrode to prepare solar cells. By introducing thermal post-treatment on the PEDOT:PSS electrode, the power conversion efficiency increased from 2% to 2.54%, and the series resistance decreased from 30.4 to 16.4 ohm cm2.29 Deng et al. fabricated PEDOT:PSS/TiO2 heterojunction fibrous photodetectors through concentrated H2SO4 treatment. The photodetection performances were significantly improved, including a 3000% responsivity enhancement and 50 times photocurrent increment.25 Wang et al. synthesized PEDOT using an oxidative chemical vapor deposition method and applied hydrobromic acid treatment to treat the film. The conductivity reached up to a record-high value of 6259 S cm1 after the engineering process.28 Considering the complex treatment processes and the cost, polar organic solvent treatment, such as by dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO), is supposed to be an effective method to modulate the conductivity of PEDOT:PSS samples.22,30,31 Additionally, carbon nanostructures are considered as promising candidates to improve the photoelectrical, thermal and mechanical strength performances of conducting polymers.3236 Benefitting from low acute toxicities, molecular sieving effects and photo-thermal conversion characteristics, carbon nanohorns (CNHs) have been used as drug carriers, biosensors, catalyst supports and electrodes.3739 However, searching for appropriate additives, which can be utilized to modulate the electronic and photoelectrical properties of conducting polymers, remains a mighty task to be achieved to develop high-performance, flexible, and wearable optoelectronic devices.

In the present research, we proposed and demonstrated a facile scheme to fabricate a kind of one-dimensional organicinorganic hybrid heterojunction photodetector, which is made of a single ZnO microwire doped by the Ga-element (ZnO:Ga MW) and a p-type PEDOT:PSS polymer film. For the enhancement of the photodetection performances, the electrical properties of the PEDOT:PSS polymer were increased by adding CNHs and DMSO as co-additives. Photoresponse characteristics of the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/PEDOT:PSS heterojunction were studied under 370 nm illumination in a self-powering operation manner. Compared with the device that uses untreated PEDOT:PSS as a p-type conducting material, the photoresponse performances, including the rectification ratio, responsivity, ultraviolet/visible rejection and specific detectivity, have been greatly increased up to 105, 0.273 A W1, 883 and 1.17 1012 Jones, respectively. In particular, the response speed (rising/decaying times 152 s/13.5 ms) was obtained from a CNHs&DMSO treated detector, which is much faster than that of the untreated detector (1.53/16.9 ms). The influences of CNHs and DMSO on the photodetection performances were investigated respectively. That is, using DMSO treatment, the conductivity of the PEDOT:PSS polymer was significantly improved, thus resulting in an enhanced rectification ratio of the fabricated single wire based heterojunction. Meanwhile, the incorporated CNHs could enhance the carrier mobility of the PEDOT:PSS film, thus providing a high-efficiency carrier transport channel for the photogenerated electronholes in the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/PEDOT:PSS heterojunction detector. This study offers an alternative candidate to improve the electrical performances of PEDOT:PSS samples, as well as a facile approach to fabricate high-performance flexible optoelectronic devices.

2 Experimental section

2.1 Sample preparation

The CNHs powder (20 mg) was added in DMSO organic solution (2 mL), and then ultrasonic dispersion for 10 min. Next, different amounts of CNHs&DMSO colloidal solution (5, 10, 20, 30 and 40 L) was added into PEDOT:PSS colloidal solution (1 mL), respectively. Thus, the PEDOT:PSS polymers treated by CNHs&DMSO with different treating concentrations were prepared successfully.

2.2 Device fabrication

The individual ZnO:Ga samples were prepared using a simple chemical vapor deposition (CVD).40,41 The photodetection device, which was composed of a single ZnO:Ga MW and PEDOT:PSS film, was fabricated. And the device fabrication procedure is as follows: (1) an individual ZnO:Ga wire was immersed in the PEDOT:PSS colloidal solution for about 3 h, and then dried in a vacuum drying oven for 1 h at 50 C; (2) the used polyethylene terephthalate (PET) films were cleaned through trichloro ethylene, ethyl alcohol and ultrapure water for 20 min, respectively. And the cleaned PET films were dried for 1 h in a vacuum drying oven; (3) a PET film was treated by a plasma cleaner to increase its wettability and remove organic contaminants; (4) a single ZnO:Ga MW covered by PEDOT:PSS film was mechanically transferred onto the PET substrate, and then fixed the end of the bare wire using an In particle, in which the In particle was functioned as negative electrode; (5) the PEDOT:PSS solution was deposited on the other end of the PEDOT:PSS covered ZnO:Ga MW. The deposited PEDOT:PSS solution will turn into a film, which was used to fix the MW on the PET substrate; and (6) Ag paste serving as the positive electrode was deposited on the PEDOT:PSS film.42,43 Besides, a schematic diagram of the device fabrication is further illustrated in Fig. S7 in the ESI. By tuning the treating volume of CNHs&DMSO colloidal solution mixed into PEDOT:PSS, the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS organicinorganic heterojunction detectors were donated as Device-1 (0 L), Device-2 (5 L), Device-3 (10 L), Device-4 (20 L), Device-5 (30 L) and Device-6 (40 L), respectively.

2.3 Analysis methods

The structures and surface morphological features of the CVD-synthesized ZnO:Ga MWs, PEDOT:PSS film and n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS hybrid structure were characterized using a scanning electron microscopy (SEM). The surface morphological and the thickness of the PEDOT:PSS films were characterized using Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM). The morphological characteristics of CNHs were examined by a transmission electron microscopy (TEM, Hitachi TEM system). The crystal structure of ZnO:Ga MW was tested using X-ray diffraction (XRD), with the Cu-K1 radiation source ( = 1.540598 ). The element distribution of an individual ZnO:Ga wire was measured using energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS). Optical characteristics of CNHs, PEDOT:PSS films untreated and treated by CNHs&DMSO were measured by Raman spectroscopy and ultraviolet-visible (UV-vis) spectrophotometry (Shimadzu 3600 plus). The electrical properties and photoresponse performances of the heterojunction device were characterized using a detecting system composed of a Xe lamp, a monochromator and a Keysight semiconductor device analyzer (Keithley, B1500A). The response times of the detector were measured using a testing system, containing a pulse laser of 370 nm and an oscilloscope.

3 Results and discussion

Fig. 1(a) reveals a typical TEM image of the CNHs, showing that the CNH polymerides are assembled from thousands of individual CNHs. The transmittance spectra are plotted in Fig. 1(b). It is clearly illustrated that the introduction of CNHs&DMSO has negligible influence on the transmittance of the prepared PEDOT:PSS samples. The transmittances are measured to about 75% in the UV-Vis region. Besides, the as-synthesized CNH sample exhibits little absorbability in the optical wavelengths. The bond vibration modes of the CNHs, the PEDOT:PSS untreated and treated by CNHs&DMSO, were investigated using Raman spectroscopy (the laser wavelength is 532 nm). The Raman spectra of CNHs, the PEDOT:PSS untreated and treated by CNHs&DMSO, and the glass substrate are displayed in Fig. 1(c). The Raman spectrum of CNHs (the violet line) exhibits two dominant peaks. The 1331 cm1 peak matches the D-band of CNHs, which reflects the defect density and can be applied to monitor the functionalization process of the transformation from sp2 to sp3 states. Meanwhile, the other peak positioning at around 1584 cm1 is assigned to the oscillation of sp2-hybridized carbon atoms in a two-dimension hexagonal lattice.37,38 The much weaker Raman peak at 1095 cm1 could be derived from the glass substrate. The Raman spectrum of the untreated PEDOT:PSS film (the red line) displayed two peaks. The wavenumber at 1435 cm1 represents the symmetric CC stretching of the PEDOT, while the peak wavenumber at 1578 cm1 suggests the vibration modes of PSS.42,43 As the concentration of CNHs treated in PEDOT:PSS lower than a certain value, the Raman result of CNHs in the treated PEDOT:PSS samples is nearly undetectable (the blue line).

The photoresponse mechanism of the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS heterojunction detector was researched. The IV characteristics of CNHs&DMSO treated PEDOT:PSS film in the dark and illuminated with various irradiated lights are shown in Fig. 3(e). Obviously, the as-prepared film shows nearly negligible photoresponse in the wavelength range of 300500 nm. Thus, the PEDOT:PSS sample can only act as a hole transport material in the n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS heterojunction detector. The large band gap of ZnO:Ga MW (3.37 eV) makes it competitive to prepare an ultraviolet photodetector. And the photoresponse wavelength of the detector is matched well with the band gap of ZnO:Ga samples. Therefore, the photo-induced current originated from the ZnO:Ga MW active layer. The band structure of the n-ZnO:Ga/p-PEDOT:PSS heterojunction device in the absence of driven voltage is illustrated in Fig. 3(f). In this case, a typical type-2 band structure is formed around the interface between ZnO:Ga MW and PEDOT:PSS film, which has the advantage for the transportation of photogenerated charge carriers. When the energy of the illuminated photons is higher than the bandgap of ZnO:Ga MW, the electronhole pairs will be generated. Driven by the built-in electric field, the photo-generated electrons will move to the negative electrode (In particle) from ZnO:Ga MW while the holes will transport along the valence band from the ZnO:Ga MW to the PEDOT:PSS film, and then captured by the positive electrode (Ag paste).42,56

The dark current in the forward-biased regime can be increased by incorporating DMSO, leading to an increase of the rectification ratio. And the great improvement of rectification ratios for the fabricated devices can be assigned to the enhanced conductivity of PEDOT:PSS film via DMSO treatment. It has been reported that PEDOT:PSS treated by DMSO exhibits lower work function than that of the untreated samples.61 Thus, the built-in potential of the n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS would be reduced by introducing DMSO using the formula

eVD = WF(PEDOT:PSS) WF(ZnO:Ga),(8)
where eVD is the built-in potential, WF(PEDOT:PSS) and WF(ZnO:Ga) are the work functions of PEDOT:PSS and ZnO:Ga, respectively. The reduced built-in potential may have a negative effect on the photoresponse performances of the fabricated detectors. Consequently, the conductivity of PEDOT:PSS samples can be significantly increased by introducing DMSO treatment, but giving rise to little enhancement of the measured photocurrent in the fabricated n-ZnO:Ga MW/p-PEDOT:PSS organicinorganic heterojunction photodetectors.

4 Conclusions

In summary, a high-performance and flexible ultraviolet photodetector, which was constructed using an individual ZnO:Ga MW and PEDOT:PSS via CNHs&DMSO co-treatment, was fabricated. The detector can operate in a self-biasing mode, and exhibits high-performance features, including a rectification ratio of 105, a responsivity of 0.273 A W1, a specific detectivity 1.17 1012 Jones, an EQE of 91.5%, a fast response time of 152 s/13.5 ms under 370 nm illumination without an external power unit. In addition, the detector exhibits good bending stability and long-time stability. The excellent device properties could be attributed to CNHs&DMSO co-treatment in the as-prepared PEDOT:PSS samples. The conductivity of PEDOT:PSS samples enhanced by incorporating DMSO yields significantly increased forward-biased current and large rectification ratio. The photocurrent can be observably enhanced by further introducing CNH treatment, which would provide a high-efficiency transport channel for the photogenerated electronholes. This work provides a workable and meaningful approach to modulate the electrical properties of PEDOT:PSS synthetically organic polymers, and developing high-performance organicinorganic heterojunction flexible optoelectronic devices.
