Abstract

A series of p-type copolymers based on oligothiophene and benzothiadiazole units was synthesised via Stille coupling and their physical, electrochemical and photovoltaic properties were evaluated. The polymers varied in the number and type of oligothiophene units as well as in the position of the solubilizing alkyl chains relative to the benzothiadiazole unit. These differences in the polymer structure had a significant impact on the thin-film transistor mobility and the solar cell power conversion efficiency, thus illustrating the importance of these design factors for polymers in optoelectronic applications.

1 Introduction

Organic bulk heterojunction solar cells, which utilize interpenetrating networks of a polymeric donor and a soluble fullerene-based acceptor,1,2 have attracted considerable attention in recent years due to their potential for extremely low cost fabrication. A major hurdle to the large-scale commercialization of organic photovoltaics (OPVs) is the relatively low power conversion efficiency (PCE) of current material systems. One of the strategies for improving the PCE of OPVs is to design donor polymers with small band-gaps to improve light absorption, high hole carrier mobilities for efficient charge extraction, optimally positioned frontier orbitals to ensure efficient exciton dissociation while minimising energy losses in the charge transfer step, and good solubility for easy processing.3â€“5 It has been predicted that PCEs of above 10% are possible if a donor material with appropriate electronic properties can be found.3

The use of alternating donor (D) and acceptor (A) units on a polymer chain is a widely used design concept for small band-gap polymers.6â€“8 In recent years, Dâ€“A type polymers have been successfully applied in the field of OPVs,9â€“12 achieving PCEs of up to 8%.13â€“20 Due to the large number of possible combinations of the D and A building blocks, judicious selection of these units is necessary to achieve polymers with suitable properties for OPV applications.

The thiophene building block has been used successfully to create polymers for organic thin-film transistor (OTFT) applications. Oligothiophene-based polymers such as poly(3,3â€²â€²â€²-dialkyl-quaterthiophene) (PQT) and poly(2,5-bis(3-alkylthiophen-2-yl)thieno[3,2-b]thiophene) (PBTTT) have delivered excellent hole transport mobilities of between 0.2 and 0.6 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 when used in OTFT applications.21,22 However, these polymers have not performed well when used as donor polymers in organic solar cells, due largely to the limited absorption spectra of these polymers as well as their tendency to aggregate and form large domains when blended with [6,6]-phenyl-C61-butyric acid methyl ester.23â€“26 The incorporation of acceptor moieties such as 2,1,3-benzothiadiazole into the polythiophene backbone has resulted in polymers with lower band-gap and improved absorption covering the entire visible spectrum.27â€“31 However, solar cells associated with these polymers are yet to deliver high power conversion efficiencies, with the highest reported efficiency being 2.23%.31 Further fine-tuning of the polymer structure is thus needed for this class of polymers.

Apart from choosing the appropriate type of donor and acceptor moieties in the polymer structure, the positioning and type of solubilizing alkyl groups is also an important design consideration as they can affect not just the solubility but also the optical and electronic properties of the polymer.32â€“35 In our previous communication, we reported our work on an oligothiopheneâ€“benzothiadiazole polymer POD2T-DTBT. By incorporating long, branched alkyl chains into the structure, a polymer with high molecular weight and good processability was achieved.36 OPV cells fabricated using POD2T-DTBT:PC71BM blends gave PCEs of up to 6.2% for spin-coated films and 6.7% for blade-coated films, thus demonstrating the potential of this family of polymers in high efficiency OPVs.36,37 In this work, we explore the structureâ€“property relationships of a number of benzothiadiazoleâ€“oligothiophene polymers which differ from each other by the position of the alkyl substituents and the number and type of oligothiophene units in the backbone. We demonstrate that these factors affect the charge transport properties and solar cell performance significantly and are therefore critical in the design of high performance polymers in this family.

3 Characterisation

3.1 Thermal characterisation

Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) and differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) were performed on the four polymers. The polymers exhibited excellent thermal stability, with all the polymers having 5% weight loss temperatures (Td) of above 300 Â°C (Table 1). POD2T-DTBT and P2T-ODTBT had the best thermal stability, with their 5% weight loss temperatures being over 400 Â°C.

The DSC scans of the polymers are shown in Fig. 2. Glass transition temperatures were not detected for all of the polymers. Each polymer exhibited an endothermic peak upon heating and an exothermic peak upon cooling, which can be attributed to the melting and crystallisation of the polymer backbone respectively. POD2T-DTBT and POD4T-DTBT, which differ in their structures by two thiophene rings, had similar transition temperatures. POD2T-DTBT had a higher melting transition than P2T-ODDTBT, which could be due to its higher molecular weight and better packing of polymer chains. PDTT-ODDTBT had the highest melting point of 279 Â°C.

Unlike their parent polymer PQT,22P2T-ODDTBT and POD2T-DTBT did not exhibit any liquid crystalline behaviour. This could be because the branched alkyl chains used in these polymers do not facilitate the side-chain interdigitation which is seen in PQT and hence do not self-organise as readily as PQT.

3.3 Electrochemical characterisation

Cyclic voltammetry was performed on thin films of the polymers and the results are shown in Fig. 4. Clear oxidation peaks were observed for all of the materials and the HOMO levels of the polymers were determined from the onsets of these peaks (see Experimental section). The calculated values of the HOMO levels were between âˆ’5.11 eV and âˆ’5.27 eV. Under the same conditions, the HOMO level of P3HT was determined to be âˆ’5.00 eV. The new polymers thus have lower lying HOMOs than P3HT, implying improved air stability and potentially higher open-circuit voltage in solar cell applications. Interestingly, the HOMO levels of POD2T-DTBT and P2T-ODDTBT differed by almost 0.1 eV despite their structures differing only in the position of the alkyl chains. Density functional theory calculations (see Section 3.4) were made on these two structures to investigate the reasons for this difference.

3.4 Density functional theory calculations

It has previously been shown that the positioning of the alkyl chains within the repeat unit of a thiophene-based polymer can affect the dihedral angles between the aromatic rings significantly.34,35 The structures of two repeat units for POD2T-DTBT and P2T-ODDTBT (Fig. 5) were optimized using density functional theory (DFT) software DMol,3,42,43 at the level of theory GGA-PW91-DSPP/DNP (cutoff 4.0 Ã…). All the side chains were represented as â€“CH2CH(CH3)2 to reduce the time needed for the calculations. Dihedral angles and HOMO and LUMO levels were calculated at the optimized geometry of the ground state. The dihedral angles were taken from the central portion of the structure to reduce end effects. The isosurfaces of the HOMO and LUMO (Fig. S2 and S3 in the ESIâ€ ) were plotted with isovalues Â±0.03 (â€˜+â€™ green and â€˜âˆ’â€™ orange).
Fig. 5 The molecular structure used for the computational study. The positions of the alkyl chains are represented as A (analogous to POD2T-DTBT) and B (analogous to P2T-ODDTBT).

The results of our calculations show that placing the alkyl units at the B position instead of the A position significantly increased the dihedral angle R2 (Table 3), while having little effect on R1. This increased steric twisting, which is possibly due to the proximity of the thiadiazole rings and the alkyl chains in the B position (Fig. S2 and S3â€ ), led to reduced Ï€-orbital overlapping between the thiophene and benzothiazole rings and therefore lowered the HOMO level and increased the band gap. This is consistent with our observations from cyclic voltammetry and UV-Vis measurements. The positioning of the alkyl chains on the polymer backbone relative to the benzothiadiazole units can thus be a powerful tool for controlling the electronic properties of the polymer.

5 OPV characterisation

To evaluate the performance of the polymers as donor materials, solar cells with the structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PC71BM/Al were fabricated. A detailed description of the fabrication and testing procedures can be found in the Experimental section. Variables such as the weight ratio of polymer to PC71BM and the spin-rate of the spin-coating process were investigated and a 1:1 weight ratio of polymer to PC71BM was generally found to give the best results. Cells prepared using POD2T-DTBT gave the best performances with an average PCE of 6.12%, a high average short-circuit current density (Jsc) of 12.43 mA cmâˆ’2, an open-circuit voltage (Voc) of 0.720 V and a fill factor (FF) of 68.4%. The Jsc values of these cells were much higher than those of the other polymers, and were a key factor behind the excellent performance of the cells. The high Jsc was likely due to the wider absorbance spectrum of the polymer compared with the other polymers which allowed comparatively more photons to be absorbed, as well as its high charge carrier mobility, which contributed to efficient charge collection. Indeed, a typical EQE spectrum for POD2T-DTBT:PC71BM cells (Fig. 7b) shows photocurrent generation up to longer wavelengths than cells based on the other polymers, as well as a higher EQE across the entire spectral range.

Besides the absorption spectra and carrier mobilities of the polymers, device performance is also highly dependent on the morphology of the blend film. The atomic force microscope height images (Fig. 8) of the polymer:PC71BM films showed a clear difference in morphology. POD2T-DTBT:PC71BM films contained many long fibres which are believed to correspond to the polymer phase. In contrast, the domains in the other films were large and granular, and appeared to be uneven. The nanofibrous network in the POD2T-DTBT:PC71BM films likely facilitated efficient charge transport and provided a large donorâ€“acceptor interfacial area for exciton dissociation, contributing to higher FF and Jsc, respectively, and thus an improved PCE compared to cells based on the other polymers. A combination of good absorbance, favourable morphology and good charge carrier mobility thus contribute to the good solar cell performance of this system.

P2T-ODDTBT cells had a Voc of 0.760 V, which was 0.040 V higher than POD2T-DTBT. This could be due in part to the difference in HOMO energy levels of these polymers â€“ P2T-ODDTBT had a HOMO level that was 0.09 eV lower than that of POD2T-DTBT as measured by cyclic voltammetry. The Jsc values of these cells were however much poorer than those of POD2T-DTBT.

During the preparation of this manuscript, we noted that a polymer with the same structure as P2T-ODDTBT was published by another group.44 Solar cells fabricated from blends of this polymer with PC61BM achieved a Jsc of 5.6 mA cmâˆ’2, Voc of 0.74 V, FF of 63% and PCE of about 2.6%. These results are quite consistent with ours, except for the lower Jsc which is likely due to the lower absorbance of the PC61BM compared to PC71BM.

Although the fine morphology of PDTT-ODDTBT:PC71BM appeared favourable for efficient bulk heterojunction solar cells, the device performance was relatively poor, with an average PCE of only 1.59%. This polymer had poor solubility in 1,2-dichlorobenzene due to the presence of rigid bridged dithienothiophene units (PDTT-ODDTBT). High solution temperatures of 80â€“90 Â°C coupled with a reduction in the concentration of the polymer to 5 mg mLâˆ’1 were therefore necessary to ensure solubility. As a result of the reduction in concentration, the films were much thinner (65 nm) than those of the other polymers (105â€“110 nm). Attempts to increase the film thickness by increasing the concentration of the polymer in the solution were unsuccessful as they resulted in poor quality films which gave even poorer solar cell performance. The much poorer solar cell performance of PDTT-ODDTBT despite its comparable charge carrier mobility with POD2T-DTBT and fine film morphology illustrates the importance of solubility and processability in solar cell performance.

Due to the high molecular weight of POD4T-DTBT and thus the high viscosity of the POD4T-DTBT:PC71BM blend solutions, the films which were spin-coated at 500 rpm were much thicker (about 240 nm) than films spin-coated from the other polymer blends, which led to a low fill factor of 49.5% and a low PCE of 1.37%. Spinning the films at a higher spin rate resulted in reduced film thicknesses of around 110 nm, hence resulting in an improved fill factor of 59.1% and a better solar cell performance of 3.00%. Compared with the POD2T-DTBT cells, the POD4T-DTBT cells had significantly lower Voc, which could be due partly to the shallower HOMO level of POD4T-DTBT compared to POD2T-DTBT.

6 Conclusions

A series of low band-gap donor polymers based on oligothiophene and benzothiadiazole has been synthesised and characterised. The combination of the benzothiadiazole moiety and the high mobility oligothiophene unit resulted in reduced band-gaps compared with typical polythiophenes. The use of long, branched alkyl chains ensured that the polymers could be solution-processed.

All the polymers exhibited hole mobilities of at least 10âˆ’2 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1, and up to 0.25 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 when applied in TFT devices. When applied in organic solar cell devices, average PCEs ranging from 1.59% to 6.12% were obtained. The positioning of the alkyl chains relative to the benzothiadiazole units, the introduction of bridged thiophene rings and the number of thiophene rings in the polymer structure have thus been shown to influence the electronic properties, TFT mobility and solar cell performance of the polymers.

General procedure for Stille-copolymerisation

The dibromo and bis-stannylated monomers (1 equivalent each) were added to a reaction vial equipped with a magnetic stirrer. Cyclic voltammetry

Cyclic voltammetry experiments were performed using an Autolab potentiostat (model PGSTAT30) from Echochimie. The experiments were performed at room temperature in a glovebox with a conventional three electrode configuration consisting of a glass carbon working electrode, a platinum counter electrode, and a silver wire as a quasi-reference electrode. The electrolyte was 0.1 M tetrabutylammonium hexafluorophosphate in acetonitrile. The polymer sample was drop cast onto the working electrode from chlorobenzene. Ferrocene was used as an internal standard for calculating the redox potentials of the samples following each batch of samples, and the ferrocene/ferrocenium redox couple was assumed to have an absolute value of 4.8 eV below the vacuum level.45 The onset of the first oxidation peak was used to calculate the value of the HOMO.

OTFT device fabrication and characterization
