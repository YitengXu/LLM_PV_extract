Abstract

A novel asymmetrical squaraine derivative bearing a cyano-substituted indoline end-capping group, namely ASQ-5-CN, was designed and synthesized. In comparison with the noncyano-substituted ASQ-5, ASQ-5-CN showed an analogous absorption band-gap in the thin solid film state, but a 0.11 eV lowered HOMO energy level, which led to a higher Voc. Density functional theory calculation results revealed that the dipole moment of ASQ-5-CN was over double that of ASQ-5. Hence the stronger dipoleâ€“dipole interactions of ASQ-5-CN might trigger more intense intermolecular packing in ASQ-5-CN, which should account for the higher hole mobility of ASQ-5-CN than that of ASQ-5 (4.00 Ã— 10âˆ’5vs. 1.67 Ã— 10âˆ’5 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1). Accordingly, solution-processed bulk-heterojunction small molecular organic solar cells using ASQ-5-CN as the electron donor exhibited a much higher PCE (5.24%) than that of the reference compound ASQ-5-based device (4.22%) due to its simultaneously enhanced Voc (0.92 vs. 0.82 V), Jsc (11.38 vs. 10.94 mA cmâˆ’2) and FF (0.50 vs. 0.47). Additionally, the PCE of the ASQ-5-CN-based device could be improved to be as high as 6.11% when measured at 80 Â°C, which is the record PCE among the hitherto reported squaraine-based solution-processed bulk-heterojunction organic solar cells.

1. Introduction

Owing to the well-defined molecular structure and molecular weight, high purity and good batch to batch reproducibility of small molecular compounds, solution-processed bulk-heterojunction small molecular organic solar cells (BHJ-SMOSCs) have been demonstrated to be a competitive alternative to their polymeric counterparts recently.1â€“7 Among the numerous kinds of small molecular OSC materials, squaraine dyes have drawn much attention because they not only possess high molar extinction coefficients, but also show intense and broad absorption in the Vis-NIR spectral regions.8â€“20 Nevertheless, although many efforts have been devoted to the exploitation of novel squaraines for OSC applications, the performance of photovoltaic squaraines is still unsatisfactory. For example, the record power conversion efficiency (PCE) of solution-processed squaraine-based BHJ-OSCs is only 5.50%, note that the squaraine compound used just possesses a symmetrical molecular structure with a Dâ€“Aâ€“D skeleton.21

In comparison with symmetrical squaraines (SSQs), asymmetrical squaraines (ASQs) bearing a Dâ€“Aâ€“Dâ€² rather than a Dâ€“Aâ€“D molecular skeleton should be more promising due to their better structural tunability.22,23 Consequently, recently, we have been devoted to the development of novel ASQ materials for BHJ-OSC applications, in which 1,1,2-trimethyl-1H-benzo[e] indole acts as the D subunit, a squaric acid core acts as the A subunit, and a 2,6-dihydroxyphenyl group acts as the Dâ€² subunit.24â€“27 Through delicate modulation on the end-capping group of the Dâ€² segment, such as diisobutylamino, 9-carbazyl, diarylamino, tetrahydroquinoline and indoline, a series of ASQ materials was achieved, and one of them, bearing an indoline end-capping subunit (named ASQ-5, molecular structure shown in Fig. 1), displayed the highest photovoltaic performance (PCE = 4.29%). These encouraging results indicate that ASQ derivatives could act as prospective OSC materials, and the indoline unit could serve as a quite promising end-capping unit to construct high performance photovoltaic ASQ materials. However, the performance of the ASQ-5-based BHJ-SMOSC was still inferior to that of the device using a SSQ compound as the photovoltaic material,21 which should be ascribed to its relatively low open-circuit voltage (Voc, 0.81 V) stemming from the relatively high HOMO energy level of ASQ-5 (âˆ’5.09 eV).25 Therefore, we aimed to exploit ASQ-5 derivatives with a lower HOMO energy level, so that an enhanced Voc could be expected.


Fig. 1 Molecular structures of ASQ-5 and ASQ-5-CN.

Owing to the strong electron-withdrawing nature and linear geometry of the cyano groups, cyano-substitution is a well-adopted strategy with regard to the lowering of the HOMO energy levels of OSC electron donor materials.28,29 Moreover, according to Cha's report, the cyano-modified conjugated polymer shows higher crystallinity than its unmodified counterpart, resulting in an OSC with a higher fill factor (FF) and PCE.30 However, most of the current studies have been focused on cyano-substitution on the vinylic linkage of the molecular skeleton of photovoltaic materials and the dicyanovinyl-substituted compounds,31â€“36 yet never has concern been paid to the effect of cyano-substitution on the end-capping groups in OSCs. Consequently, a new asymmetrical squaraine (named ASQ-5-CN, molecular structure shown in Fig. 1) bearing a cyano-substitution on the indoline end-capping unit of ASQ-5 was designed and synthesized, and the substitution effect of the cyano group on the end-capping segment of ASQ compounds has been investigated.

3.2 Optical properties

The UV-Vis absorption spectra of the two ASQs in dilute chloroform solution and in thin film states are shown in Fig. 3, and the data are summarized in Table 1. Similar to ASQ-5, the cyano-substituted compound ASQ-5-CN also showed intense absorption at 550â€“700 nm in dilute solution, with a considerably high molar extinction coefficient of >105 Mâˆ’1 cmâˆ’1. In dilute solution, the maximum absorption wavelength of ASQ-5-CN was blue-shifted by 14 nm from that of ASQ-5, which might arise from the reduced electron-donating capability of the end-capping group due to the presence of an electron-deficient cyano group in this compound. In comparison with their dilute solution samples, both ASQ-5-CN and ASQ-5 showed much bathochromic-shifted and broadened absorption spectra in the thin solid film state, but ASQ-5-CN exhibited a more bathochromic-shifted (55 vs. 41 nm) and broadened (full width at half maxima, FWHM: 3791 vs. 3336 cmâˆ’1) absorption spectrum than that of ASQ-5, which should be propitious to the harvesting of solar irradiation.10 In fact, although in solution state, the Î»absmax of ASQ-5-CN was blue-shifted by 14 nm to that of ASQ-5 and the FWHM of the two compounds were identical (âˆ¼900 cmâˆ’1), the solid film samples of both ASQ-5-CN and ASQ-5 displayed an analogous absorption maximum of 718 nm. Consequently, there may exist more intense intermolecular Ï€â€“Ï€ stacking interactions in ASQ-5-CN than in ASQ-5.38 Determined from the onset position of the absorption spectra of the two ASQs in the thin films, the optical band-gaps of ASQ-5-CN and ASQ-5 were both 1.43 eV.

3.3 Electrochemical properties

To estimate the energy level of ASQ-5-CN, its electrochemical property was investigated by cyclic voltammetry. As shown in Fig. 4 and Table 2, during the anodic scan, a quasi reversible oxidation process could be observed in ASQ-5-CN, and its Eonsetox was determined to be 0.40 V relative to Fc/Fc+. Hence, by comparison with the Fc/Fc+ redox couple whose energy level is âˆ’4.80 eV in a vacuum, the HOMO energy level of ASQ-5-CN was calculated to be âˆ’5.20 eV, which is 0.11 eV lower than that of ASQ-5 (âˆ’5.09 eV). Consequently, the introduction of a cyano-substitution on the indoline end-capping segment could endow ASQ-5 derivatives with a lower HOMO energy level, which could be beneficial to the enhancement of Voc in their corresponding OSC device.39 Moreover, deduced from the HOMO level and the corresponding optical band-gap, the LUMO energy level of ASQ-5-CN was calculated to be âˆ’3.77 eV, which was also 0.11 eV lower than that of ASQ-5, hence a lower energy loss could be expected during the electron transfer processes from the ASQ donors to the PC71BM acceptor.39

3.4 DFT calculation

To gain further insights into the effects of cyano-substitution on the electronic properties of ASQ-5-CN, DFT calculations were performed on ASQ-5-CN and ASQ-5, the computational results are shown in Table 2, and the electron density distribution of the HOMO and LUMO of the two ASQ compounds are shown in Fig. S1 (ESIâ€ ). According to the calculation results, the HOMO energy levels of ASQ-5 and ASQ-5-CN were âˆ’5.17 eV and âˆ’5.33 eV, respectively, while the LUMO energy levels of ASQ-5 and ASQ-5-CN were âˆ’2.94 eV and âˆ’3.06 eV, respectively. These data well reproduce their corresponding electrochemical characterization results, indicative of the reliability of the calculation results. More importantly, as shown in Fig. 5, both of the two ASQ derivatives showed a similar direction of the dipole moment, however, ASQ-5-CN exhibited a dipole moment more than double that of ASQ-5 (11.26 vs. 3.73 D), indicating that significantly stronger dipoleâ€“dipole interactions should exist in ASQ-5-CN, which may endow the compound with a more enhanced higher hole mobility.40â€“42 To the best of our knowledge, this is the first time to reveal that cyano-substitution would result in an increased dipole moment of the OSC materials. This finding should be of great importance for the recognition of the role that cyano-substitution might play in the rational molecular design of photovoltaic materials.

For comparison, reference devices with ASQ-5 as electron donor material have also been fabricated under the same conditions. Firstly, the effect of different blend ratios of ASQ-5-CN/PC71BM on the photovoltaic properties of the devices were investigated. Consequently, a reference ASQ-5 device with a similar D/A blend ratio of 1:3 was fabricated, and the PCE, Voc, Jsc, and FF of this device were 3.95%, 0.83 V, 10.35 mA cmâˆ’2, and 0.46 respectively, and each was inferior to that of the ASQ-5-CN device (5.00%, 0.93 V, 10.77 mA cmâˆ’2, and 0.50, respectively). The 0.10 V higher Voc of the ASQ-5-CN device relative to the reference device was consistent with the 0.11 eV decreased HOMO energy level of ASQ-5-CN than that of ASQ-5. Moreover, the ASQ-5-CN-based device also displayed a higher Jsc than that of the reference device, as the Jsc should correlate highly with the light-harvesting capability, carrier mobility, and morphology of the active layer.44â€“46 To elucidate the origin of the enhanced Jsc in the ASQ-5-CN device, the EQE curves of the two devices were recorded. As shown in Fig. 7d, in comparison with the reference device, the ASQ-5-CN device showed a narrower spectral response, but its EQE values at 300â€“700 nm were much higher. And the EQE data were in line with the absorption of the corresponding active layer (shown in Fig. S2, ESIâ€ ). Additionally, AFM characterization results revealed that both the two blend films showed similar morphologies and smooth surfaces (Fig. 6). Thus, the increased Jsc of the ASQ-5-CN device should chiefly originate from the higher hole mobility of its active layer. Additionally, the FF of the ASQ-5-CN-based device was also higher than that of ASQ-5 (0.50 vs. 0.46), which is attributed to its higher hole mobility and better balanced hole and electron mobilities.20 It should be pointed out that the high FF of 0.50 is, to the best of our knowledge, one of the highest FF values of solution-processed BHJ-OSC with squaraine derivatives as electron donor materials.

Upon thermal annealing, the performances of both the two devices were enhanced, with the best PCE of 4.22% for the reference device, and 5.24% for the ASQ-5-CN device. The enhanced PCE should be mainly attributed to the improved Jsc upon thermal annealing. Because the outdoor working temperature of solar cells is generally higher than the ambient conditions, these devices were also measured at 80 Â°C.47 Excitingly, for the ASQ-5-CN device, although its Voc (0.92 vs. 0.85 V) was found to drop slightly, both its Jsc (12.40 vs. 11.38 mA cmâˆ’2) and FF (0.58 vs. 0.50) were observed to be significantly enhanced (Table 4), hence the PCE is as high as 6.11%, which is about 17% enhanced over that of the as-prepared device (5.24%), which should be attributed to the significantly enhanced hole mobility and decreased Î¼e/Î¼h ratio.48 After being recooled down to room temperature, the device showed a similar performance (PCE: 5.23% vs. 5.24%) to that of the annealed one (Table 4), which shows that they are quite promising candidates for practical applications.

4. Conclusion

In conclusion, with cyano-substitution on the electron-donating indoline end-capper of ASQ-5, the resulting asymmetrical squaraine ASQ-5-CN possessed comparable absorption characteristics to ASQ-5 in thin film states, but a lower HOMO energy level relative to ASQ-5. In addition to its 0.11 eV lowered HOMO energy level, ASQ-5-CN also showed a much enhanced dipoleâ€“dipole interaction than that of ASQ-5, hence a higher hole mobility could be acquired in ASQ-5-CN. Consequently, in comparison with that of the ASQ-5-based reference device, the solution-processed ASQ-5-CN-based BHJ-OSC showed a simultaneously enhanced Voc, Jsc and FF, and a high PCE of 6.11% could be achieved, which is the best PCE of solution-processed BHJ-OSC based on squaraine materials reported so far. Our results show the importance of molecular tailoring for achieving high performance photovoltaic materials, and the cyano-substitution on the end-capper of conjugated small molecules may be an important strategy for achieving high performance photovoltaic devices with a greatly improved Voc, Jsc and FF.
