Abstract

Solar thermal water evaporation is a clean, sustainable, and energy-saving approach to fresh water generation compared with traditional methods. Electroplated coating, such as nickel black, have been commercialized in solar water heaters. Inspired by this, we developed a nanostructured Niâ€“NiOx/Ni foam (NF) composed of Ni metal nanoparticles and NiOx with defects. The Niâ€“NiOx/NF exhibited high solar absorbance due to the plasmonic effect of metal Ni nanoparticles and defects. Niâ€“NiOx/NF also showed a low thermal conductivity, which prevented the loss of thermal energy and thermal transfer, which will be beneficial to the generation of localized high temperatures. The as-prepared Niâ€“NiOx/NF can self-float on the water surface without any other materials owing to a high contact angle. The average water evaporation rate of the as-prepared Niâ€“NiOx/NF reached 1.41 kg mâˆ’2 hâˆ’1 and the average solar conversion efficiency was about 94%. The best sample exhibited a water evaporation rate of 1.45 kg mâˆ’2 hâˆ’1 and 96% solar conversion efficiency. This report highlights potential semiconductor materials as highly efficient solar water evaporation materials.

Introduction

Solar energy is a promising sustainable clean energy to support various human activities. Converting solar energy into energy sources that can be directly applied in daily life is a great opportunity and challenge. For example, solar cells convert solar energy into power, while solar water heaters convert solar energy into thermal energy. Solar steam generation is an increasingly popular technique for seawater desalination, and water purification has attracted tremendous attention.1,2 To efficiently convert solar energy to thermal energy, various photothermal materials have been employed as light absorbers to increase absorptance. Plasmonic metal nanostructures3â€“12 and carbonaceous materials,13,14 including graphene,15â€“23 carbon nanotubes,24,25 carbonized biomass,26â€“28 conductive polymers,29 and semiconductors with narrow band gap,30â€“33 have been developed as broadband solar absorption materials. Meanwhile, 2D and 3D porous nanostructures have been used as matrixes for efficient water transportation to construct high-performance solar water evaporation devices.13,14,28,34 Coating the light absorber on a porous matrix is a complicated process. Therefore, developing low-cost and scalable materials to achieve optimal light absorption, thermal management, and water transportation is highly challenging.

Generally, photothermal materials must have the following properties:30 (i) a wide absorption capability covering the full solar spectrum; (ii) a low emissivity, ensuring the largest photo-to-heat conversion efficiency, and (iii) a lower thermal conductivity than surrounding media to achieve localized high temperatures. In commercial solar-to-heat systems, electroplated coating, such as nickel black and chrome black, have commonly been employed as highly efficient light-absorbing materials owing to their high solar absorptance. Nickel-based nanomaterials are potential low-cost light-absorbing materials. Liu and coworkers showed that plasmonic Ni nanochains in Al2O3 or SiOx as a selective solar thermal absorber have a high solar absorptance (>90%) and low thermal emittance loss (<10%).35,36 Nickel has better chemical stability than Al or Fe. Recently, Ni-based nanomaterials have been extensively explored as electrocatalysts. Although nickel black is a commercialized light-adsorbing material used in solar water heaters, it has rarely been investigated as a solar thermal material for water evaporation. Herein, we developed a nanostructured Niâ€“NiOx composite on Ni foam (Niâ€“NiOx/NF) through a solution process. This composite exhibited high light absorptance and low thermal conductivity. This material can also self-float on the water surface owing to its surface nanostructure, and can therefore be fabricated as a self-floating photothermal device for water desalination and purification. The Niâ€“NiOx/NF exhibited a high photothermal conversion efficiency of âˆ¼94% due to the plasmonic effect of Ni metal particles and the rich defects of NiOx, resulting in broad light absorbance. Thermal conductivity prevented the loss of thermal energy from the generation site. The localized high temperature promoted the water evaporation rate, leading to a high photothermal conversion efficiency.

Experimental

Materials

Ni(NO3)2Â·6H2O and hexamethylenetetramine (HMT) were purchased from Beijing Chemical Corporation. Ni foam (NF) with a pore size of 110 PPI, surface density of 250 g mâˆ’2, and thickness of 1 mm was purchased from Suzhou Christie's Foam Metal Co., Ltd. Synthesis of Niâ€“NiOx/NF

NF was washed with diluted hydrochloric acid, ethanol, and deionized water several times to ensure that the NF surface was clean. First, Ni(NO3)2Â·6H2O (1.45 g, 5 mmol), HMT (1.40 g, 10 mmol), and water (40 mL) were added into a beaker, and then stirred well to give a uniform transparent solution. The above solution and NF were transferred to a 100 mL Teflon-lined stainless-steel autoclave, heated at 100 Â°C for 10 h, and allowed to cool. The obtained NF was washed with water several times and dried at 60 Â°C in the oven. Subsequently, the treated NF was calcined in air at different temperatures (300â€“550 Â°C) for different times (1â€“3 h) to obtain NiO/NF. Finally, the obtained NiO/NF was reduced using different amounts of hydrazine hydrate (0.05â€“0.50 mL) for different periods (0.5â€“5.5 h) to afford Niâ€“NiOx/NF.

Characterization

To achieve a highly efficient photothermal material, the first requirement is a wide light adsorption capability covering the full solar spectrum range from 250 to 2500 nm. To determine the absorption capability, the absorbance and reflectance of the samples were measured. As shown in Fig. 3a, Ni foam as a skeleton exhibited absorbance in the UV light region and a long decay in the visible light and IR regions. The absorbance decreased in the IR region (wavelength, >1500 nm). The Ni/NiOx composites showed excellent absorbance over the whole solar spectrum. To determine why Niâ€“NiOx/NF had the best photothermal effect, the structureâ€“performance relationships of this material must be understood. SEM and TEM images showed that two types of Ni nanoparticles were present, the first type being a few nanometers in size and dispersed on the NiOx nanosheets and the second type being large Ni particles a few hundred nanometers in size. These Ni nanoparticles had a strong surface plasmonic effect, which promoted photothermal conversion.35,45 Furthermore, the Niâ€“NiOx/NF had the lowest thermal conductivity among the samples in this study, while Ni foam had the highest. From the temperature curve (Fig. 4a), the Ni foam reached the balance temperature within the shortest time (âˆ¼5 min). However, Niâ€“NiOx/NF took about 30 min to reach the balance temperature owing to its low thermal conductivity. This low thermal conductivity also resulted in a low thermal transfer rate, which further explained why Niâ€“NiOx/NF took a longer time to reach the balance temperature. This also increased the localized temperature, allowing the temperature to reach 48 Â°C when using the Niâ€“NiOx/NF sample, thus promoting a high water evaporation rate at a relatively high temperature. In summary, the nanostructured Niâ€“NiOx/NF composite possessed high light absorbance and low conductivity, which led to a high photothermal conversion efficiency and low thermal loss. This resulted in Niâ€“NiOx/NF exhibiting the best photothermal effect in this study.

Conclusions

We successfully fabricated nanostructured Niâ€“NiOx/NF materials for application to solar water evaporation. The surface of these nanostructured materials had a high contact angle, allowing them to self-float on the water surface. The Niâ€“NiOx/NF exhibited broad light absorption in the visible and IR regions due to the plasmonic resonance effect of Ni particles. The solar light absorbance reached âˆ¼90%. In water evaporation, the Niâ€“NiOx/NF had a low thermal conductivity of 0.243 W mâˆ’1 Kâˆ’1. Under 1-sun irradiation (100 mW cmâˆ’2), the water evaporation rate reached 1.41 kg mâˆ’2 hâˆ’1 and the photothermal conversion efficiency was âˆ¼94%. The materials prepared herein also exhibited improved stability and water evaporation rates and efficiencies when applied to Bohai seawater. This work provides a potential method for fabricating low-cost photothermal water evaporation materials for fresh water production.
