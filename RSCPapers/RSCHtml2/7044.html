Enhanced performance of dye-sensitized solar cell using triazole based phenothiazine dendrimers as additives

Abstract

Phenothiazine dendritic architectures with 1,2,3-triazole as a bridging unit are synthesized through a convergent approach using click chemistry and when used as additives in dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs) show increased efficiency in solar energy harvesting systems. The presence of a greater number of phenothiazine and triazole units increased the molar absorption coefficient and fluorescence quantum yields. In DSSCs, the redox couple doped with first generation triazole based phenothiazine dendrimers showed a higher Voc voltage through the suppression of back electron transfer and exhibited a better power conversion efficiency (η) of 8.6% under 70 mW cm−2 irradiation than the zeroth generation dendrimers.

Introduction

Dendrimers1 have highly branched and symmetrical structures and have attracted great attention in the past few decades due to their various applications including optical data storage,2 solar cells3 and medical applications.4 Research in the field of dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs) has gained considerable attention during recent years because of the need for developing low cost and efficient solar energy harvesting systems. As the energy obtained from fossil fuels is limited, a possible alternative, solar energy, appears to be very attractive. In 1991 Grätzel introduced DSSCs which have attracted a great deal of interest due to their low cost and relatively high power conversion efficiency.5 The DSSC is a molecular system which consist of a wide band gap nanocrystalline semiconductor photo anode, typically TiO2, an anchored molecular sensitizer such as cis-dithiocynato-N,N-bis(2,2′ bipyridyl-4,4′ dicarboxylic acid)ruthenium(II)dehydrate (N3 dye), a redox electrolyte and a platinized counter electrode. To enhance the power conversion efficiency as well as the stability, the DSSC system needs to be optimized with further substantial improvement. Among the components of DSSCs, the electrolyte plays a key role in energy conversion.6 The precise composition of the (I−/I3−) electrolyte affects the performance of the dye-sensitized solar cell significantly due to the sublimation of iodine. The use of liquid electrolyte suffers from drawbacks such as leakage, evaporation of solvent, and reduced long term stability which decreases the performance of DSSCs.7 Most research groups focus on electrolyte modification in DSSCs.8,9 Hence, DSSCs fabricated with electron rich imine dendrimers as additives could exhibit a high open-circuit voltage through the suppression of back electron transfer.10

Recently, Cu(I)-catalyzed11 Huisgen-type 1,3 dipolar cycloaddition, otherwise called a click reaction, has become a very popular approach to perform quantitative ligation of various functional units into different types of dendrimeric architectures.12 Phenothiazine is a well known heterocyclic compound with electron-rich sulfur and nitrogen heteroatoms. It has been used as the electron donor component due to its low reversible oxidation potentials due to the sulfur atom, which can facilitate the hole transport of the carrier.13 Phenothiazine and its derivatives have recently attracted much research interest because of their unique electro-optical properties, resulting in diverse applications such as in light-emitting diodes,14 photovoltaic devices15 and chemiluminescence.16 Moreover, the non-planar phenothiazine ring structure of the dendrimer restricts π-stacking aggregation, which prevents the detrimental pure singlet exciton recombination process.17

Recently, we have used dendrimers with pyrene,18 dimethyl isophthalate19 and benzothiazole20 surface groups as additives to improve the open-circuit voltage (Voc). In order to find suitable and efficient dendrimers as additives in the redox couple (I−/I3−) for dye-sensitized solar cells, we report herein the synthesis of phenothiazine dendrimers 1–12 (Fig. 1) with triazole bridging units through a click chemistry approach and their photophysical properties are also investigated.

Results and discussion

Synthesis

The reaction of one equiv. of phenothiazine 13 with 1.2 equiv. of propargyl bromide in the presence of NaH in DMF for 2 h gave 10-(prop-2-ynyl)-phenothiazine 14 in 72% yield (Scheme 1). Further reaction of 2.0 equiv. of propargylated phenothiazine 14 with 3,5-bis(azidomethyl)phenol under click chemistry conditions using CuSO4·5H2O (5 mol%) and sodium ascorbate (10 mol%) in a mixture of water and THF (1:1) at room temperature for 10 h gave the phenolic dendron 15 in good yield. The phenolic dendron 15 undergoes O-alkylation with 1.2 equiv. propargyl bromide in dry DMF at 60 °C to give acetylenic dendron 16 in 87% yield (Scheme 1). The 1H NMR spectrum of alkyne dendron 16 displayed a triplet at δ 2.36 (J = 1.8 Hz), a doublet at δ 4.41 (J = 1.8 Hz), and two singlets at δ 5.13 and 5.26 for acetylene, benzylic and N-methylene protons, respectively. The aromatic protons appeared in the region of δ 6.39 to 7.22. The 13C NMR spectrum of 16 showed benzylic, N-methylene and propargyl carbons at δ 44.7, 53.6, 55.9, 76.3 and 77.6 in addition to the signals for aromatic carbons. The mass spectrum (ESI) of dendron 16 showed a molecular ion peak at m/z 717.2 (M + H)+.

In order to investigate the effect of phenothiazine dendritic architecture on the photophysical and solar cell properties, we proposed the synthesis of a zeroth generation phenothiazine dendrimer. Hence, the propargylated phenothiazine 14 was reacted with di, tri and hexa azides 17–22 under Cu(I)-catalysed Huisgen click reaction conditions to give zeroth generation phenothiazine dendrimers 1–6, respectively, in good yields (Scheme 2). In the 1H NMR spectrum, dendrimer 6 showed a set of twelve proton singlets at δ 4.96 and 5.81 for benzylic and N-methylene protons. The traizole proton appeared at δ 8.24 in addition to the aromatic protons. The 13C NMR spectrum of 6 showed two signals at δ 49.1 and 52.7 for benzylic and N-methylene carbons in addition to ten aromatic carbon signals. The mass spectrum (MALDI-TOF) of 6 showed a peak at m/z 1830 (M+) corresponding to the molecular ion. The elemental composition of 6 was further supported from CHN analysis values. Similarly, spectroscopic and analytical methods were also used for the confirmation of the structure of the other phenothiazine–triazole dendrimers 1–5.

First generation dendrimers 7–12 were also obtained in good yields by the ‘click’ reaction. The treatment of azides 17–22 with alkyne dendron 16 in the presence of 5 mol% CuSO4·5H2O and 10 mol% sodium ascorbate in THF/H2O (1:1) for 10 h at room temperature gave the phenothiazine dendrimers 7–12 (Scheme 3). In the 1H NMR spectrum, dendrimer 12 displayed four singlets at δ 4.77, 5.02, 5.34 and 5.99 for different types of benzylic, N-methylene and O-methylene protons, a singlet corresponding to twelve protons at δ 7.89 for the protons of the outer triazole moiety adjacent to the phenothiazine unit and a singlet corresponding to six protons at δ 7.84 for the protons of the inner trizole moiety near to the core unit in addition to the signals for the aromatic protons. In the 13C NMR spectrum, dendrimer 12 displayed four signals at δ 43.6, 52.3, 60.9 and δ 78.9 for three types of N-methylene and O-methylene carbons in addition to fifteen aromatic carbon signals. The mass spectrum (QTOF) of 12 showed the molecular ion peak at m/z 4707.4. The elemental composition analysis also further supported the molecular formula of the dendrimer 12.

Solar energy studies

The efficiency of a dye-sensitized solar cell is related to the TiO2–dye–iodide/triiodide–Pt components. Among these, the redox electrolyte system (I−/I3−) occupies an important role in the power conversion efficiency of a DSSC. The redox couple without organic additives shows a decreased open-circuit voltage (Voc) and short-circuit current (Jsc). After adding the additive the sublimation of iodine is decreased which in turn increases the Voc and Jsc. In general, amine derivatives form stable molecular complexes with the (I−/I3−) electrolyte system and drastically alter the Voc due to the electron donating properties of the nitrogen lone pair.21 Various organic molecules such as aminopyridine, pyrimidine, pyrrolidinopyridine and pyrrolidinoethylpyridine have been used as additives to increase Voc which eventually increases the solar energy harvesting efficiency (η) of the DSSC.22 The addition of such organic nitrogenous compounds in the redox electrolyte shifts the negative band of TiO2 and enhances the Voc and further results in an increased electron lifetime.23,24

Hence, we have designed and synthesized a new series of electron rich phenothiazine dendrimers 1–12 and employed them as additives for DSSCs. Fig. 4a and b show the current–voltage (I–V) curves for the undoped electrolyte system and the doped electrolyte system with the phenothiazine dendrimers 1–12. The photovoltaic performance data of the short-circuit current (Jsc), the open-circuit voltage (Voc), the fill factor and the electric energy efficiency (η) are summarized in Table 2. In the present investigation the highest power conversion efficiency of 8.6% was observed when the phenothiazine dendrimer 12 was used as an additive in the DSSC. From the results it is clear that as the dendritic generation increases the power conversion efficiency (η) also increases. Dendrimer 12 has the most highly packed structure among the other zeroth and first generation dendrimers and hence could capture I3− effectively25 and improve the lifetime of the redox couple. Hence, a further improved design of the dendritic generation is possible, and dendrimer 12 as an additive in DSSCs would be potentially better than the other materials.

Conclusions

In conclusion, dendrimers with phenothiazine as the surface and triazole as the bridging unit were synthesized in good yields by a convergent synthetic strategy using a click chemistry approach. Optical and dye-sensitized solar cell studies indicate better performance for higher generation dendrimers. DSSC studies clearly reveal that as the number of phenothiazine and triazole units increases in the zeroth and first generation dendrimers, the DSSC performance also increases. Hence, dendrimers of type 12 could be further developed as an excellent additive in DSSCs to further increase the efficiency of the solar energy harvesting system.

Experimental

Materials and methods

All the melting points reported were uncorrected and were determined using Toshniwal melting point apparatus by the open capillary tube method. The UV-Vis spectra were recorded on an Agilent 8453 spectrophotometer. The emission spectra were recorded on a HORIBA JOBIN YVON Fluoromax-4 spectrofluorometer. The IR spectra were recorded on an FTIR-8300 spectrophotometer. The 1H and 13C NMR spectra were recorded on a Bruker 300 MHz spectrometer. The chemical shifts are reported in ppm (δ) with TMS as an internal standard and the coupling constants (J) are expressed in Hz. MALDI-TOF mass spectra were recorded on a Voyager-DE PRO mass spectrometer using an α-cyano-4-hydroxy cinnamic acid (CHCA) matrix and ESI-MS spectra on a Jeol DX-303 mass spectrometer. The QTOF-MS spectra were recorded on a Xevo G2-S QT mass spectrometer. Elemental analyses were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 240B elemental analyzer. TLC was performed either on glass plates coated with silica gel-G (ACME) of about 0.25 mm thickness and visualized with iodine or on pre-coated plastic sheets (POLYGRAM® SIL G/U254) and detected under UV light. Column chromatography was carried out with silica gel (ACME, 60–120 and 100–200 mesh).

General procedure for the synthesis of 1,2,3-triazole using click chemistry

A mixture of azide 17–19 (0.5 equiv.)/20 or 21 (0.33 equiv.)/22 (0.17 equiv.), alkyne 14 or 16 (1.0 equiv.), CuSO4·5H2O (5 mol%) and sodium ascorbate (10 mol%) in a mixture of THF and water (1:1) was stirred for 10 h at room temperature. The residue obtained after evaporation of the solvent was washed thoroughly with water and dissolved in CHCl3 (150 mL). The organic layer was washed with water (2 × 100 mL) and brine (1 × 150 mL) and dried (Na2SO4), and evaporated to give the crude triazole, which was purified by column chromatography (SiO2).

General procedure for dye-sensitized solar cell studies

The nanocrystalline TiO2 photoelectrode was prepared as described earlier.26 The prepared TiO2 electrodes were immersed in a 5 × 10−5 M solution of the sensitized dye cis-dithiocynato-N,N-bis(2,2′ bipyridyl-4,4′ dicarboxylic acid)ruthenium(II) (N3 dye) in absolute ethanol for one day. The dye coated photoelectrode was washed, dried and immediately used for the measurement of solar cell performance. A sandwich type photoelectrochemical cell was used, which was composed of a dye-coated TiO2 photoanode. Platinum coated fluorinated tin oxide (FTO) acted as a counter electrode. The electrolyte solution was injected into the space between photoanode and counter electrode. The electrolyte solution was composed of potassium iodide (KI, 2.2 × 10−5 M); iodine (I2, 3 × 10−6 M), and dendrimer (7.5 × 10−6 M) additives in 10 mL DMF solvent. The photovoltaic properties were measured under simulated solar light at 70 mW cm−2. The photo current–photovoltage (I–V curve) was measured using a BAS 100A electrochemical analyzer. The apparent cell area of the TiO2 photoelectrode was 1 cm2 (1 cm × 1 cm).
