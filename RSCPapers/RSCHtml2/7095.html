Abstract

With increasing demand for energy storage, obtaining aqueous electrolytes that are incombustible, low cost, and conveniently assembled in air with high ionic conductivity has become a dominant focus. Herein, we report the successful synthesis of ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets in a controllable way for 2.0 V aqueous electrolyte-based electrochemical capacitors that involve activated carbon. From electrochemical performance tests, the electrode results in a large increase in the performance of an asymmetric supercapacitor, which involves activated carbon, in an operating voltage window from 0 V to 2.0 V in aqueous electrolyte, with a high specific capacitance of 340 F gâ1 at a current density of 1.0 A gâ1, and cycling stability for 6000 cycles with only a 5% drop in the initial capacitance. The superior performance during the chargingâdischarging process is attributed to the existence of a film, which can prevent electronic conduction while allowing ionic conduction.

1. Introduction

As a source of life, water always has the advantages of environmental friendliness and recyclability, especially in aqueous electrolyte-based electrochemical capacitors (ECs).1â3 For ECs, commonly used electrolytes include aqueous solutions, organic solutions and solid electrolytes. Recently, aqueous electrolytes have become more dominant due to their low cost, incombustibility, environmental-friendliness, convenient assembly in air and high ionic conductivity.4 Aqueous electrolytes show enhanced contact with the micropores of electrodes.5,6 In addition, higher ionic conductivity can allow aqueous-based ECs to achieve higher power density than non-aqueous electrolyte-based ECs.7 However, the temperature range from the freezing point to the boiling point is narrow, which limits the development of these ECs at very low or high temperatures. As we know, the decomposition voltage of water is only 1.23 V.8,9 So widening the stable potential window of aqueous electrolytes has become an urgent research focus.

To solve the limitations of the narrow electrochemical stability potential window, it is necessary to find appropriate strategies. So far, many methods have been developed, such as adjusting the pH of the electrolyte, adding redox-active additives, using water-in-salt electrolyte, and so on.10 According to Luo et al., one common practice is to eliminate O2 and adjust the pH value of the electrolyte.11 Meanwhile, another work reported the use of a room temperature Li salt hydrate melt as the aqueous electrolyte, which could realize a high voltage (â¼2.3â3.1 V) in aqueous Li-ion batteries.9 What's more, according to an excellent study, researchers reported a highly concentrated aqueous electrolyte, namely a âwater-in-saltâ electrolyte, which enabled a full Li-ion battery to operate at 2.3 V for more than 1000 cycles with a coulombic efficiency of nearly 100% at both 0.15C and 4.5C.12 These excellent studies provide more opportunities to improve the potential window, especially for pseudocapacitive storage applications.

In addition to widening the electrochemical stability potential window, the selection of electrode materials is also essential, since using appropriate capacitance materials can improve the energy density.13â15 In recent years, the incorporation of nanomaterials and metal organic frameworks (MOFs) has become attractive.16 These new types of materials exhibit novel chemical and physical properties, such as crystalline structures and large internal surface areas from MOFs as well as surface, size and microscopic quantum tunneling effects from nanomaterials.17,18 To date, there have been some excellent examples in this research field. Yan et al. reported work involving the fabrication of Fe2O3-NP@MOF composites, in which they concluded that the as-obtained composites enabled the integration of the characteristics of both materials.19 In 2014, Vinogradov et al. synthesized a MIL-125@TiO2 composite and found that, when applied in solar cells, it demonstrated a 6.4% power conversion efficiency (PCE).20 Similarly, to achieve high energy density in supercapacitors, combining MOFs and nanomaterials to form hybrid nanocomposites could be a successful strategy.21

MnO2, a promising pseudocapacitive material with the advantages of environmental-friendliness and low cost, can be used in neutral aqueous electrolytes in electrochemical supercapacitors.22â25 Very recently, Liu's team and Xia's team created high-voltage aqueous supercapacitors established from Mn-based materials.26,27 Liu's team reported a 2.4 V hydrogel supercapacitor that used polyvinyl alcohol (PVA)âLiCl as the electrolyte. This device demonstrated superior coulombic efficiency and excellent rate capability, as well as remarkable cycle life (>5000 cycles; 95.5% capacity retention). According to Xia, they firstly synthesized a 2.6 V aqueous Na0.5MnO2//Fe3O4@C asymmetric supercapacitor that has an energy density reaching 81 W h kgâ1. Such a high voltage is comparable even to those of organic electrolyte supercapacitor systems.

Herein, we report a high-voltage (2.0 V) supercapacitor, with ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets as the electrode material, in neutral environments. Meanwhile, during charging and discharging in aqueous Na2SO4 electrolyte with a stable potential window from 0 V to 2.0 V in a two-electrode system that involves activated carbon, a film formed, suppressing the electrolysis of water. The corresponding electrochemical tests demonstrated that the as-prepared ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheet electrode exhibited high capacitance (340 F gâ1 at a current density of 1 A gâ1) and excellent cycle performance (approximately 95% capacitance retention over 6000 cycles).

2. Experimental section

Material preparation

Ultrathin Î´-MnO2 nanosheets. A synthesized potassium permanganate solution (0.002 M, 150 mL) was mixed with ethyl acetate (99.8%, 40 mL) uniformly in a boiling flask. Then, the solution was stirred magnetically under condensation reflux at a temperature of 85 Â°C. This process was continued until a large quantity of brown particles formed, revealing the end of the reaction. Eventually, the Î´-MnO2 material was obtained after washing with deionized water and ethanol several times. Later, to obtain a 10 g Lâ1 Î´-MnO2 standard suspension, 15 mL of ethanol was added into a centrifugal tube containing all of the prepared Î´-MnO2 material.
Ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets. First 1.0 mL of the standard Î´-MnO2 suspension and 30 mL of ethanol were placed in a beaker under magnetic stirring at room temperature. After dropping in 3.0 mL of Cu(NO3)2 solution (0.1 M), the solution was ultrasonicated (10 minutes) to mix it well. In addition, 2 mL of sodium trimesate solution (0.1 M) was added dropwise. The stirring process lasted for one hour, and Cu-MOF successfully grew on the sheet of Î´-MnO2. After alternately washing with deionized water and ethanol at least three times, Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 material was eventually obtained.

Î´-MnO2 nanosheets with a layered spacing of 0.71 nm may exhibit better intercalation/deintercalation of Na+ into and out of the activated material, and offer promising properties for enhancing capacitance. Firstly, the emerged film acts as a protection layer. To further verify the electrochemical performance, comparisons with previously reported MnO2-based materials from other supercapacitors are provided in Tables S1 and S2 in the ESI.â  Obviously, the materials in this work show improvements (specific capacitance and maximum energy density) over most of those materials. The ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets benefit from synergistic effects between the Î´-MnO2 nanosheets and Cu-MOF nanoparticles to exhibit enhanced capacitance behavior. We can easily find that the Î´-MnO2 nanosheets have good electrochemical performance, and the folds of the Î´-MnO2 nanosheets can provide sufficient interfacial area to facilitate charge transport during the charging-discharging process. At the same time, the existing porous Cu-MOF nanoparticles can not only offer a porous structure to provide more diffusion paths for ions but can also strengthen the folds to increase the active surfaces of Î´-MnO2 for the absorption and desorption of electrolyte ions. Other ions or molecules can easily insert into the interlamination spaces of Î´-MnO2, leading to good ion exchange capacity.

4. Conclusions

In conclusion, ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets have been successfully prepared in a controllable way. The as-prepared nanoparticle/nanosheet composite combines the advantages of the porous character of Cu-MOF together with the functional characteristics of ultrathin Î´-MnO2 nanosheets. We investigated its electrochemical performance through a traditional three-electrode system and asymmetrical supercapacitor device. The test results indicate that the ultrathin Cu-MOF@Î´-MnO2 nanosheets can be successfully applied in supercapacitors. The MOF@metal oxide composite is a promising material for electrochemical energy storage devices with aqueous electrolytes. When chargingâdischarging tests were performed, the as-assembled device, which involves activated carbon, showed a wide operating voltage window from 0 V to 2.0 V in aqueous electrolyte, which shows potential for applications in high-energy density supercapacitors. The present work opens an avenue to use MOF@metal oxide composites as promising electrode materials for electrochemical energy storage.
