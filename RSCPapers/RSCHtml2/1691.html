Synthesis of a new 1,3,4-thiadiazole-substituted phenanthroimidazole derivative, its growth on glass/ITO as a thin film and analysis of some surface and optoelectronic properties

Abstract

A new phenanthroimidazole derivative (PMItz) bearing a 1,3,4-thiadiazole moiety was prepared and deposited as a thin film on a previously prepared glass/ITO substrate via the physical vapor deposition (PVD) method. The optical and surface morphological properties of the fabricated thin film, glass/ITO/PMItz, were investigated via UV-Vis spectroscopy, atomic force microscopy (AFM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM). Subsequently, metal/ITO/PMItz/metal (metal: In and Ag) devices were fabricated and their electrical properties were investigated. The optical measurements revealed the reasonably high optical conductivities of the thin films. The energy band gap was calculated to be 2.96 eV, the absorption coefficient was calculated to be 1.3 106 m1 and the optical conductivity was calculated to be 3.14 1014. The typical diode characteristics of the devices were demonstrated by electrical measurements. The currentvoltage (IV) measurements revealed that the devices exhibited photo-sensitivity, the photovoltaic effect and strong negative differential resistance (NDR) effect at certain voltage values. The ideality factors in the dark were calculated to be 2.4 and 2.54 for the In/ITO/PMItz/In and Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag devices, respectively. According to the Hall effect measurements, the conductivity of PMItz was found to be p-type with high carrier mobility and concentration and low specific resistance.

1. Introduction

Studies concerning the design and applications of organic heterojunction semiconductors (OSCs) is attracting increasing attention from researchers owing to their miscellaneous applications in the fabrication of modern optoelectronic devices such as organic light emitting diodes (OLED), organic field-effect transistors (OFETs), organic photovoltaics (OPV), organic photodetectors (OPDs), and organic solar cells (OSC). Although inorganic semiconductors have dark/illuminated conductivity, high carrier mobility and long life span, they are difficult to commercialize on the industrial scale owing to their limited variety, relatively high production costs and rigid film structure. However, organic semiconductors are easily accessible with wide diversity, solution processable, possess mechanical flexibility, have low production cost and their electronic properties are tunable via molecular modification.15 Phenanthroimidazoles are widely acknowledged as a class of molecules exhibiting outstanding photophysical-optoelectronic properties, and thus have been exploited for the fabrication of modern organic optoelectronics612 and their electrical performances can be tuned rationally via molecular modification.1316 In this context, the optimization of parameters such as charge carrier mobility, band gap, power conversion efficiency (PCE), and threshold voltage is mandatory for the optoelectronic performance.2,17,18 Conversely, the negative differential resistance (NDR), as an intriguing phenomenon, which is accompanied by a decrease in electrical current with an increase in bias voltage, is pivotal and utilized for the design of diverse advanced devices such as oscillators working in the terahertz frequency domain, resonant tunnelling diodes, static random access memories, semiconductor quantum wells, and logic circuit devices.1922 Therefore, research based on the design of devices displaying the NDR effect has recently gained momentum.1921 In our study, as part of our ongoing research, for the first time, we synthesized the 5-(4-(1H-phenanthro[9,10-d]imidazol-2-yl)phenyl)-N-phenethyl-1,3,4-thiadiazol-2-amine (PMItz) compound in which the strong electron-withdrawing thiadiazole group was secondarily substituted to widen the band gap and modulate the electrical performance of the phenanthroimidazole scaffold, and a peripheral ethylbenzene group was attached to ensure the good solubility of the compound. Subsequently, a new heterojunction thin film with PMItz as the organic layer was deposited on a pre-prepared glass/ITO bottom layer via the PVD method. Accordingly, our aim in this study was to introduce a new alternative semiconductor device to contribute to the development of the aforementioned modern optoelectronic applications. The surface morphology and optical and IV characteristics of the film were investigated and it was demonstrated that the film exhibited significant rectifying and NDR properties under illumination and low bias conditions, while acted as a photodiode under reverse bias conditions. Also, the induction of the NDR effect under illumination demonstrated that it can be potential candidate for application in devices especially optoelectronic information function devices.

2.2 Deposition of thin films

A glass/ITO substrate with dimensions of 1 2 cm and resistance of about 10 sq1 was successively immersed and left in deionized water, acetone and propanol in an ultrasonic bath for 10 min. The cleaned electrode was dried with nitrogen gas. van der Waals epitaxial growth of PMItz powder on the glass/ITO was carried out via thermal evaporation using a molybdenum pot, which was pre-cleaned with propanol. The deposition was performed at a chamber temperature of 26 C and 106 torr pressure.

3. Materials, measurements and computations

All chemicals used in the synthesis were analytical reagent-grade (Merck, Darmstadt, Germany and Sigma-Aldrich, USA). Thin-layer chromatography was used to determine the progress of the reaction and the degree of purity of the product obtained. The melting point of the compound was determined using a Stuart SMP30 instrument. NMR analyses were performed on a Bruker Ultrashield 400 MHz NMR spectrometer. Elemental analysis was carried out on a EuroVector Brand EA3000-Single model. IR spectra were recorded using a Bruker Alpha instrument. Mass analyses were carried out on an LCMS-MS (Shimadzu, LCMS-8030 Plus). The absorption spectra of the compound were measured using a Shimadzu UV Pharmaspec 1700. Fluorescence emission data were obtained using a Horiba Fluoromax-4.

The thickness of the PMItz thin film on which organic PMItz and inorganic ITO layers interacted with each other through attractive van der Waals forces was measured with a profilometer and determined to be 580 nm. Surface analyses were performed using a NANOSURF C3000 atomic force microscope (AFM) at room temperature and using a Tap190-AlG cantilever in scan areas of 25 25, 10 10, 5 5 and 1 1 m. Optical measurements were performed with a Thermo SCIENTIFIC Thermo Scientific Evolution Array model UV-VIS spectrometer.

Indium and silver metals were contacted with the fabricated glass/ITO/PMItz device to investigate some electrical properties, as introduced in Fig. 1. IV characterization was performed using a SCIENCETECH AAA model Solar Simulator System in the dark and 190290420 W m2 illuminated media.


Fig. 1 Configurations of (a) In/ITO/PMItz/In and (b) Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag devices.

As shown in Fig. 1, the anodes in direct bias were connected to indium and silver on PMItz, while the cathodes were connected to them on ITO. In reverse bias, the connections were replaced. Hall effect measurements were conducted using an Ecopia HMS-5000 Hall effect measurement system at 300 K, with a current of 1 105 A, under a 0.55 T magnetic field using the four-probe method.

Density functional theory (DFT) calculations were carried out using the Gaussian 09W program suit. The hybrid B3LYP functional with basis set combinations of 6-31G(d,p) for C, H, and N atoms and 6-311G(d,p) for S atoms was used for the gas-phase geometry optimization. The pure B1LYP density functional together with the cc-PVTZ basis set was used for the single-point energy calculation.24

4.2 DFT studies

The frontier molecular orbitals (FMOs), HOMO and LUMO isosurfaces of PMItz extracted from SPE calculation at the optimized structure are depicted in Fig. 4. Its HOMO is significantly localized over the phenanthroimidazole terminal, while its LUMO mostly resides on the phenyl and thiadiazole moieties. This change in molecular orbital distribution clearly indicates the contribution of the thiadiazole group to electronic excitation by conjugative delocalization through a strong inductive electron-withdrawing effect.

The Rs (series) and Rsh (Shunt) resistances are pivotal parameters that substantially affect the performance and reliability of any diode.

Thus, the Rs and Rsh values of the In/ITO/PMItz/In and Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag diodes were obtained from the graphs in Fig. 12b, c and 13b, c, respectively, which were plotted as bias voltage vs. junction resistance . Generally, a low Rs value denotes a higher current flow through the junction, while an RSH value near infinity shows a small leakage current through the junction. At a sufficient forward bias, the Rj value in the bottom region of the RjV plot gives Rs, while at a sufficient reverse bias, the maximum peak resistance is estimated to be Rsh.28 The Rs effect occurs under the interface conditions between ITO and PMItz, while Rsh is associated with the conditions between the contacts (In and Ag) and thin films.29Tables 1 and 2 list the Rs and Rsh values for the In/ITO/PMItz/In and Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag devices, respectively, which were calculated from the RjV plot, and the n and Rs values were also calculated using the Cheung function given in eqn (7).

In both heterostructures, the NDR effect was obviously observed. In the In/ITO/PMItz/In and Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag heterostructures, the NDR effect is very high at some voltage values, while it is very low at others. The effect of light enhances the NDR effect at low voltage values, while it reduces it at high voltage values. The band overlaps occurring between the junctions decreased or partly disappeared depending on the external electrical field arising from the applied potential. Namely, the number of electrons quantum mechanically contributing to the current decreased. In relation to this observation, X. Wang et al. associated the enhancement of the NDR effect under light with strong photo-resistance,31 while K. W. Lee et al. associated the decrement in the NDR effect with a decrement in the overlap of wavefunctions at valley voltage values in the contact region.32

5. Concluding remarks

A new phenanthroimidazole derivative, PMItz, which was prepared using 9,10-phenanthrenequinone, 4-carboxybenzaldehyde and 2-phenylethyl thiosemicarbazide precursors, showed high light sensitivity and quantum yield in solution. Then based on these properties, the compound was grown as a thin film on a conducting substrate, and AFM images indicated that the fine structure of thin film is very smooth. The energy band gap of the film, which was quite transparent in the near IR and visible region, was calculated to be 2.96 eV and its absorption coefficient was found to be 1.3 106 m1. Sharp peaks at around 400 nm for the n and k values were also observed. Besides, the optical conductivity value in the high absorption region was estimated to be around 1014, which is a reasonably high value. The Hall effect analysis demonstrated that PMItz is a p-type conductor with high carrier mobility and concentration, which is pivotal in terms of high conductivity. The electrical analysis data revealed that the devices exhibited typical diode behaviour and the ideality factors estimated in the dark were 2.4 and 2.54 for the In/ITO/PMItz/In and Ag/ITO/PMItz/Ag devices, respectively. It was observed from the IV characterization at various light intensities that the devices were photo-sensitive, showing a photovoltaic effect and having a strong NDR effect at some voltage values. Therefore, they are potential candidates for advanced optoelectronic applications such as tunnel diodes, high frequency oscillators, static random access memories, and switching gates. Alternatively, the optoelectronic performance of the fabricated devices can be enhanced by using different contact materials (Au, Al, etc.), changing the film thickness and constructing new heterojunctions using well-known semiconductors such as GaAs, CIGS, and CIS.
