Abstract

Utilization of the fascinating properties of Î²-oxodithioesters as building blocks in heterocyclic frameworks was started a few decades ago. Its similarity and dissimilarity with Î²-ketoesters in terms of its synthesis or as a substrate towards synthesis makes it an interesting and emerging field of synthetic organic chemistry. Tuning of catalysts and counter substrates towards selective utilization of its multiple reacting sites was summarized in the following discussion. A diverse range of monocyclic and polycyclic heterocycles with several different heteroatoms is plotted in an easy and reader friendly manner.

1. Introduction

Organic synthesis being a creative science attracts particular attention because of its immense importance to life and society since from drugs to polymers to synthetic fuels there are few areas of life today where one does not feel the impact of organic synthesis. The strategies regarding construction and cleavage of bonds represent the central theme in organic synthesis. A major challenge of modern synthesis is to design not only efficient and selective cascades, but also to be eco-compatible that provides maximum structural diversity and complexity with a minimum number of synthetic steps. Therefore, new protocols in terms of efficiency, minimal environmental hazards, operational simplicity, and high selectivity are still demanding and would be of great relevance to synthetic, material, and medicinal chemists.

Î²-Oxodithioesters were first envisaged in 1968 by Beer and co-workers1a and they opened up a new dynamic field in synthetic organic chemistry which may be assumed as a parallel to the chemistry of 1,3-dicarbonyl synthons. From the last few decades, Î²-oxodithioesters are widely used as synthons in the synthesis of several organosulfur moieties where the sulfur atom is present in the ring entity or as an external substituent. Due to their extensive presence in cysteine, methionine, and ferredoxins (the sulfur containing amino acids and proteins) as well as in pentathiepin molecules, which have antifungal, antitumor, and antimicrobial properties. Sulfur is considered as a very important element in bio-molecular systems. Further, their applications as functional materials in rubber industry, polymer chemistry, pharmaceutical industry, as anti-oxidant and anti-radiation agents, the industrial interest can hardly be overstated.1

Synthesis and application of Î²-ketoesters2 have been known for many decades and are discussed in reviews and in several books. Î²-Ketoesters act as initial synthon in the synthesis of several different heterocycles. Some examples are Claisen isoxazole synthesis, Hantzsh pyrrole synthesis, Pechmann coumarin synthesis, Biginelli pyrimidone synthesis, Beirut reaction towards synthesis of quinoxaline-1,4-dioxides from benzofurazan oxide, Feist-Benary furan synthesis, Combs, Conrad-Limpach, Friedlander quinoline synthesis, Fischer, JappâKlingemann indole synthesis, Knorr pyrrazole synthesis and many more. Despite structural similarities, due to difference in electronic configuration of the sulfur atom in Î²-oxodithioesters i.e., the presence of vacant d-orbital, chemistry of Î²-oxodithioesters shows a distinctive feature from Î²-ketoesters in terms of their synthesis and applications in various organic transformations. Î²-Oxodithioesters exist in their enolic form and henceforth are very susceptible towards complex formation with several different metal salts which make Î²-oxodithioesters a hit in inorganic chemistry.3

Because of the significance of this scaffold both from theoretical and synthetic points of view, tremendous progress has been achieved in this field during the past two decades. The continual renewal of interest in the chemistry of this framework is mainly due to its two electrophilic and three nucleophilic centres (Fig. 1) coupled with its versatile reactivity, making it a very useful building block with a valuable impact in modern organic synthesis. Even though Î²-oxodithioesters have several promising prospects in synthetic organic chemistry, only a little has been done so far. The diverse chemical nature and broad synthetic utility of Î²-oxodithioesters have made chemists busily explore its chemistry over the last few decades. To explore this new field of research, documentation of information regarding its synthesis and scope of utility is very helpful. The goal of this review is to cover the efforts of the chemical community in the development of new methodologies and novel scaffolds utilizing Î²-oxodithioesters and their wide applications in various transformations since this field has not been reviewed so far. The objective of the present contribution is to cover comprehensively the abundant literature from the early beginning up to December 2012, giving a special emphasis on the development of new synthetic approaches for their preparation and their reactivity as versatile intermediates.

2. Reaction profiles of Î²-oxodithioesters

The chemical properties of the Î²-oxodithioester can be determined by the presence of three nucleophilic and two electrophilic centres as shown in Fig. 1. Two nucleophilic centres are localized on the oxygen and sulfur atom associated with the carbonyl and the thiocarbonyl groups. The third one is present on the carbon atom, bounded by the carbonyl and the thiocarbonyl group as shown below. The two electrophilic centres are present on two different carbon atoms bearing carbonyl and thiocarbonyl groups. Due to the presence of those active centres, the reactions of Î²-oxodithioesters with various dielectrophilic reagents may lead, depending on the structure and nature of the starting dielectrophile, and the reaction conditions, to the formation of various heterocyclic systems where the sulfur atom is present in the ring system or as an external substituent.

5. Synthetic applications of Î²-oxodithioesters

Synthetic organic chemistry (SOC) occupying a central role in every area of our increasingly technological society, retains its importance as it is an integral part of new drug discovery, and is the basis of the bulk of the chemical industry. Synthesis of heterocycles is a key to organic synthesis, since heterocycles are indispensable materials in the functioning of any developed society. Simple polyfunctional molecules are ideal starting materials in diversity-oriented synthesis (DOS), which aims at providing quick access to libraries of molecules. Synthons containing both electrophilic and nucleophilic sites have great potential in developing new reaction pathways. One such synthon is Î²-oxodithioester, a thio-analogue of the normal Î²-ketoester with general formula 1 (Fig. 1).

5.1. Synthesis of monocyclic heteroaromatics

The interest in synthesizing monocyclic heteroaromatics has always been enormous. Among heterocycles, the six- and five-membered O-, N- and S-heterocycles are probably one of the most common structural motifs spread across natural products, from simple to structurally complex metabolites present in the structure of several biologically interesting compounds. Although particularly attractive, direct annulation reactions onto the heterocyclic framework from acyclic precursors still constitute an important synthetic challenge. However, some interesting approaches involving Î²-oxodithioesters have been recently proposed from various research groups and are presented here.
5.1.1. With one heteroatom. Thiophene derivatives have attracted considerable interest due to their presence in a broad spectrum of natural and synthetic organic molecules.23 Thiophene derivatives are identified as the core structure of various drugs like Plavix, Articaine and Patrine. 6. Summary and outlook

In this review, we presented the power and diversity of domino/cascade reactions based on the use of Î²-oxodithioesters as key substrates, which have quickly become powerful, fascinating, and highly efficient intermediates in organic synthesis. The concept of domino sequences has allowed easily reaching high molecular complexity with very often excellent levels of selectivity with simple operational procedures, as well as advantages of savings in solvent, time, energy, and costs. These domino reactions proceed under relatively mild reaction conditions and tolerate a wide variety of functional groups, thus avoiding protection group chemistry. The resulting functionalized heterocycles can undergo a variety of very useful subsequent transformations to give highly substituted heterocycles. The cases cited in this review have been selected to highlight the most promising applications of Î²-oxodithioesters in organic synthesis. It can be seen that many well-established different methodologies were successfully applied to Î²-oxodithioesters by worldwide laboratories to more environmentally benign approaches. In fact, many efforts and developments have transformed heterocyclic chemistry into a very broad and exciting field with many opportunities for research and development of applications. Definitely, much more remains to be done in this field, and in the next few years, we will see many new, exciting findings in Î²-oxodithioester chemistry. Thus, we hope with this review to have provided appropriate background for such developments and the encouragement to synthetic organic chemists to employ this valuable intermediate in important new heterocyclic and medicinal chemistry. On the other hand, some new synthetic approaches were developed for the specific aim of achieving a more sustainable transformation. Currently, it is expected that more developments will emerge not only in line with the previous ones reported but also in the following more fascinating specific areas: (i) asymmetric and non-asymmetric bio- and organocatalysis, (ii) efficient organometallic catalysis with minimal contamination of toxic metals, (iii) more efficient or new one-pot multicomponent couplings, and (iv) application of other efficient technologies such as microreactors and membrane separation processes.
