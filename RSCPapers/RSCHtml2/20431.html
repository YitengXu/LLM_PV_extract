Abstract

Two soluble isomeric acceptor molecules based on a triptycene core, which is connected to three aroylenimidazole units are described. Due to the inherent threefold axis, the molecules are soluble and thus could be fully photophysically characterized in solution and film. Additionally, the preliminary results of these acceptors in organic photovoltaic devices with two different donor materials are reported.

The power conversion efficiency of bulk heterojunction organic solar cells (BHJ OSCs) has dramatically increased in recent years mainly due to the ongoing development of new donor polymers and oligomers. These donor materials exhibit photophysical and electrochemical properties tailored to fit those of fullerene derivatives (PC61BM and PC71BM) which are used as electron acceptors.1 Recently, research efforts to replace fullerene acceptors have increased due to the existence of a large number of compounds that show promise as alternative electron acceptors because of their spectroscopic and electrochemical properties.2,3 In particular, rylenediimide and aroylenimidazole derivatives exhibit excellent molecular properties as electron acceptors such as appropriate energetics, large extinction coefficients and good thermal and photostabilities.4â€“6 Despite these attractive properties, no reports exist concerning the application of aroylenimidazoles as electron acceptors in BHJ OSCs, mainly because the majority of these Ï€-systems are planar and self-assemble in a fashion that is disadvantageous for the formation of an ideal donor:acceptor morphology.7 By incorporating acceptor units in molecules with a nonplanar geometry, a number of non-fullerene acceptors have been successfully processed in BHJ OSCs with efficiencies of up to 8.3%.8 In 2012, Liu and Troisi gave a structural suggestion for the design of new non-fullerene acceptors based on two acceptor units, such as naphthalene diimides (NDIs), or perylenediimides (PDIs), which are connected via a rigid methine unit.9 Due to the fact that in triptycene three aromatic benzene units are connected via two methine bridgeheads, it is an ideally suited molecular scaffold to place three electron acceptor units in a fashion similar to the one proposed by Liu and Troisi. Furthermore, photoelectron spectroscopy studies suggest that there is an electronic communication between the three benzene units of triptycene by homoconjugation.10 Although triptycenes with extended Ï€-planes have been reported,11 to the best of our knowledge, none of these compounds have been used as acceptors in BHJ OSCs to date. We were interested in the synthesis of triptycenes with aroylenimidazole units, which have nearly ideal LUMO levels and high extinction coefficients.5 Additionally, due to the inherent â€˜three-bladedâ€™ molecular geometry, a better phase separation of these new electron-acceptors in BHJ OSCs is expected in comparison to their planar congeners. Here, we present the synthesis of two isomeric rigid Ï€-extended triptycenes with three aroylenimidazole units and first tests of these compounds as non-fullerene acceptors in BHJ OSCs with poly-3-hexylthiophene (P3HT) and poly({4,8-bis[(2-ethylhexyl)oxy]benzo[1,2-b:4,5-b0]dithiophene-2,6-diyl}{3-fluoro-2-[(2-ethylhexyl)carbonyl]thieno[3,4-b]thiophenediyl}) (PTB7) as donor materials.

The synthesis of the C3v-symmetric isomer 7a (Scheme 1) starts from triptycene triamine 1a,12 which was converted in three steps into the triaminotrinitro triptycene 4a according to the protocol described earlier by MacLachlan et al. for the Cs-symmetric isomer 4b.13 In contrast to the procedure described by MacLachlan, the intermediate N-acylated triptycene 2a was isolated in almost quantitative yields simply by filtration of the resulting precipitate obtained after 3 h of stirring the reaction mixture at room temperature. Subsequent nitration within 10 min with fuming nitric acid results in 3a with yields of 79%. In comparison to the previously described procedure, the overall reaction time was significantly reduced and the combined yield for the two-step method is even higher than that for the one-pot method. Hydrolysis of amide 3a was performed using a mixture of aqueous conc. hydrochloric acid and ethanol under reflux conditions for 12 h giving triaminotrinitro triptycene 4a in 62% yield after column chromatography.

To conclude, we have synthesized three-dimensional molecules consisting of aroylenimidazole units, which has been fully photophysically characterized in solution. To the best of our knowledge, this has been difficult for other congeners of this substrate class. The energy levels of the compounds 7a/7b reveal that this new class of compounds can act as potential non-fullerene acceptor material in BHJ OSCs. Preliminary tests to incorporate the compounds into solar cell devices showed for the first time that these materials could be used as acceptors, giving PCEs of about 1% for both isomers. Further directions in the investigation will include the use of other donor materials as well as increasing the solubility of the aroylenimidazole acceptor compounds to be able to tune the microstructure of the BHJ towards higher efficiencies.
