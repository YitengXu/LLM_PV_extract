Abstract

The development of sustainable polymeric photo-catalytic systems for the conversion of solar energy is an important issue. In this perspective, efficient photo-catalytic systems have been developed by many research groups; however, their long-term stability or sustainability is still challenging and need to be resolved. In order to realize sustainable polymeric photoactive systems, we developed terpyridine (tpy)-based polymers and their ruthenium complexes, which were reversibly assembled and disassembled through metal ionâ€“ligand interactions. In the presence of metal ions, Ru-based photoactive polymers were assembled on tpy-coated ITO substrates, generating visible-light induced photocurrents. Subsequently, when disassembled by the introduction of tetra-n-butylammonium fluoride, the assembled polymeric complex decomposed, decreasing the photocurrent. However, the photocurrent efficiency of this system was recovered through re-assembly and repeated several times without a significant decrease in its efficiency. In addition, a sustainable photo-catalyst system was achieved by photo-oxidative coupling with benzylamine. The initial active photo-catalytic system became inactive after disassembly; however, its activity was restored through assembly again, and this process was repeated successfully several times. The self-assembly via reversible metal ionâ€“ligand interaction would enable photo-catalytic systems to be self-repairable and sustainable.

1. Introduction

Plants in nature constantly obtain chemical energy from solar energy (i.e., photosynthesis) for maintaining their in vivo metabolism.1 Because solar energy is considered to be a key component in utilizing environment-friendly energy conversion, many researchers have developed artificial photo-active materials (polymers, nanostructures, and small molecules),2,3 exhibiting high solar energy conversion and catalytic activity, and these photo-active materials have been rapidly applied in pollutant removal,4,5 green chemistry,6,7 solar cells,8 and solar fuels.9 Although significant development of photo-active materials has been achieved, their commercialization still remains a problem and needs to be solved, especially in organic photo-active materials. The organic photo-active complexes generally have low stability and short lifetime, because of their decomposition by oxygen, water, and other conditions under illumination.10,11 Enhancement in stability is one of the important issues to be addressed for commercialization; however, it still remains a challenge in spite of significant efforts.12â€“14

Nature's photosystems, consisting of â€œorganicâ€ materials (i.e., proteins and coenzymes), carry out photosynthesis without failure even under very high illumination of UV light. Interestingly, protein complexes in photosystems undergo oxidative damage because of excess light. However, the photo-inactivated protein complexes or photo-damaged components (D1 protein) in the reaction center are rapidly degraded and re-assembled,15,16 and this self-repair mechanism of photo-active proteins enables constant energy harvesting under excess light. If such biomimetic self-repair mechanism can be introduced in artificial photo-active materials, their stability can be significantly improved.

Terpyridine (tpy) ligands have been used to fabricate self-healing materials with metal ionâ€“ligand interaction.17,18 They can reversibly bind to metal ions (e.g., Fe2+, Zn2+, Co2+, Mg2+, etc.), and these metals are easily removed by the addition of stronger ligands such as ethylenediamine tetraacetates (EDTAs) and halides.19â€“21 Tpy-functionalized polymers, nanostructures, and small molecules have been developed and applied as catalysts,22 self-repairing materials,17 and optoelectronic materials.23 For example, Collomb and coworkers synthesized bimetallic self-assembled coordination polymers based on [Ru(bpy)3]2+ units connected to bis-tpy ligands.24 Interestingly, the bimetallic films with Ru(II)â€“Fe(II) exhibited a photo-induced electron transfer cycling process over Fe2+/Fe3+ and Ru2+/Ru3+ oxidations.24,25 Li and coworkers synthesized self-healing polypyrrole/Znâ€“tpy hybrid gels by incorporating Znâ€“tpy supramolecules, which are mechanically and electrically self-healing at room temperature without external stimulus due to assembly or disassembly of metalâ€“tpy supramolecules.26 Tieke and coworkers also synthesized organized double-electrochromic films consisting of electrochromic tpy-substituted polyiminofluorene bound to Zn forming a Znâ€“bis-tpy complex, and thus improved the electrochromic properties.27 In addition, they synthesized organized electrochromic films of tpy-substituted polyaniline derivatives by layer-by-layer (LbL) assembly with metal-ion complexes.28 Burnworth and coworkers synthesized photohealable metallosupramolecular polymers and they attributed their photothermally induced healing to metalâ€“ligand complex formation. Those supramolecular healing systems seem to be applicable to any supramolecular polymer.29 In this study, we envisioned that the reversible metal ionâ€“tpy interaction is suitable for developing biomimetic repairable polymer systems through programmed assembly and disassembly. Many research groups have developed separately efficient photo-catalytic systems or self-healing materials. In our systems, we envisaged that regenerative photoactive polymer systems could be successfully achieved through metalâ€“tpy interactions, resulting in biomimetic, sustainable photoactive systems.

Previously in our research, self-assembled polymeric thin films were fabricated via tpy-based LbL assembly, and visible-light-induced photocurrents were generated in such polymeric films.30,31 In addition, the photocurrent efficiency was enhanced with hybrid systems using tpy-functionalized multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWNTs). Herein, we designed and synthesized tpy-functionalized polymers by ring-opening metathesis polymerization (ROMP) using various functionalized norbornene derivatives. To endow photo-activity, tpy-functionalized ruthenium(II) complex (Ru(bpy)2(bpyâ€“tpy)Cl2),32 in which the tpy moiety of the Ru(II) complex can bind to tpy-functionalized polymers through metal ions, was synthesized. Using the tpy-functionalized polymers and Ru(II) complex, reversible self-assembly of the polymeric systems was demonstrated and applied to â€œregenerativeâ€ photocurrent generation and photo-catalysis (Fig. 1). The photocurrent efficiency of our polymeric system recovered through disassembly and re-assembly and was repeated several times without a significant decrease in its efficiency. In addition, the assembled photo-active polymers were used as photo-catalysts promoting the photo-oxidative coupling of benzylamine to N-benzylbenzaldimine. The photocatalytic system produced N-benzylbenzaldimine repeatedly after assembly with Mg2+; however, when disassembled by Fâˆ’, the system showed almost no reaction due to the removal of the ruthenium complex. We believe that regenerative photo-active polymeric systems could be possible through metalâ€“ligand interactions, and such reversible assembly can be applied to repair photo-active materials.

3. Results and discussion

To realize self-repairing or regenerative properties, a polymer and a photosensitizer (e.g., a Ru complex) should possess a reversible binding ability for â€œprogrammableâ€ disassembly and assembly. If the photoactive system is photo-damaged and its efficiency decreases, the malfunctioned components are disassembled and â€œfreshâ€ parts are re-assembled, restoring its efficiency. Since tpy has been known as a metal-binding ligand enabling reversible assembly,34,35 we designed tpy-functionalized polymers and Ru complexes (Scheme 1 and Fig. 1). The tpy-functionalized Ru (tpyâ€“Ru) complex could be assembled on the tpy-functionalized polymer (P1 and P2), operating as a photoactive state. Once damaged photochemically (or by other reasons), the photo-inactive state could be repaired by disassembly and subsequent assembly with fresh components, regenerating the photoactive state. The tpy-functionalized Ru complex was prepared from tpy-attached bipyridine according to the literature.32

For the preparation of tpy-functionalized polymers, norbornene derivatives were chosen as monomers, as norbornene is very versatile for functional-group modifications and can be easily polymerized in a controlled fashion by ring-opening metathesis polymerization (ROMP) using Grubb's catalysts.36

In this study, a tpy-functionalized linear polymer (P1) was synthesized to fabricate photoactive films. The crosslinked polymer (P2) was also synthesized for physically more stable photoactive polymer structures. First of all, ethylhexyl- (1), carboxyl- (2), and bis-norbornene (3) were synthesized from cis-5-norbornene-exo-2,3-dicarboxylic anhydride by condensation reactions with 2-ethylhexylamine, 3-aminopropionic acid, and hexyl-1,6-diamine, respectively. In order to attach the tpy ligand, carboxyl-norbornene (2) was further coupled to amine-functionalized tpy,33 promoted by a guanidinium-type reagent (1-[bis(dimethylamino)methylene]-1H-1,2,3-triazolo[4,5-b]pyridinium 3-oxide hexafluorophosphate, HATU) and N,N-diisopropylethylamine (DIEA) to furnish the desired tpy-norbornene (4) (Scheme 1). Subsequently, the linear polymer P1 was synthesized by ROMP with a monomer mixture (4:1) of ethylhexyl-norbornene (1) and tpy-norbornene (4) in a decent yield. Interestingly, the tpy ligand, which could bind the Ru catalyst, did not significantly affect the efficiency of ROMP, and the molecular weight of Mn = 25200 g molâˆ’1 and polydispersity index (PDI) of 1.3 were obtained. To fabricate stable gels (see below), a relatively-large amount of free tpy was required in the mixture of P1 and tpyâ€“Ru. In the lower content of tpy, we were not able to generate a stable, photoactive gel. When the tpy ratio was higher than 20 mol% in P1, it gave rise to P1's low solubility and thus we confirmed that 20 mol% terpyridine seems appropriate for the fabrication of photoactive gels. The synthesized P1 was characterized by 1H NMR, FTIR, and UVâ€“vis spectroscopy, confirming its structure. The presence of tpy could be easily observed by the addition of divalent metal ions, such as Fe2+ and Mg2+, resulting in rapid gelation (Fig. 2b).

In order to perform repeated assembly and disassembly for sustainability, a platform by using a statically crosslinked polymer with tpy ligands is necessary. The crosslinked polymer P2 was easily synthesized using crosslinkable bis-norbornene (3) as well as ethylhexyl-norbornene (1) and tpy-norbornene (4) in a ratio of 3:6:1. Crosslinked P2 was characterized by FTIR, confirming the presence of a tpy moiety (Fig. S9â€ ). Crosslinked P2 was insoluble, but physically stable with swelling properties (% of wswollen âˆ’ wdry/wdry) ranging from 1200% in THF and 4100% in dichloromethane.

The reversible binding ability of tpyâ€“polymer P1 and tpyâ€“Ru complex for assembly and disassembly was demonstrated by using Mg2+ and Fâˆ’ ions, respectively (Fig. 2). When Mg2+ (20 nmol) was introduced into a THF solution of P1 (227 mg mLâˆ’1), the solution immediately formed a gel (Fig. 2b), because of the formation of a (tpy)2Mg2+ complex (Fig. 2a).34,37 The gel-forming, assembled P1 was decomposed again with the addition of excess Fâˆ’ ions, since Mg2+ binds stronger with Fâˆ’, than with tpy.20 To confirm the metalâ€“ligand interaction in the self-assembly of P1, UVâ€“vis absorption spectra were recorded with the addition of metal ions (Fig. 2c and e). The binding of tpy to a metal ion is well known to induce the planarization of pyridine moieties (i.e., dihedral angles between pyridine close to 0Â°), resulting in the red-shifts in the UVâ€“vis absorption.38â€“40 Indeed, the addition of a 5 Î¼L Mg2+ solution (1 mM, 5 nmol) at regular intervals to a THF solution (3 mL) of P1 (0.1 mg mLâˆ’1, âˆ¼185 nmol of tpy) clearly showed the red-shifted absorptions of P1 from âˆ¼310 nm to âˆ¼330 nm due to tpy ligand planarization induced by metal-binding (Fig. 2c).40,41 Interestingly, when tetra-n-butylammonium fluoride (TBAF, 20 nmol each 4 times) was added to the assembled P1 solution, the red-shifted absorption (âˆ¼330 nm) gradually returned to the pristine absorption (âˆ¼310 nm) (Fig. 2d), indicating that tpy became â€œun-boundedâ€. This result is in good accordance with the fact that Fâˆ’ ions bind stronger to Mg2+ than tpy, removing Mg2+ ions from the complexes. We also observed that although the amounts of Fâˆ’ were not enough (i.e., less than 2 equiv. of total Mg2+), the tpyâ€“Mg2+ in P1 was completely dissociated. When the Fe2+ ion was used instead of Mg2+, similar gel formation and red-shifts in the tpy's UVâ€“vis absorption were also observed (Fig. 2b and e). Interestingly in the case of Fe2+, the immediate color change to violet occurred because of the increased absorption at âˆ¼570 nm, which can be assigned to the metalâ€“ligand charge transfer (MLCT) band of the Fe(tpy)2 complex.31,38

We successfully implemented the regenerative properties in photoactive systems (photo-catalyst and photocurrent) through metalâ€“ligand interaction; the photocatalytic activity and photocurrent efficiency can be restored via disassembly and re-assembly of active components. First of all, we confirmed that the photoactive ruthenium complex with tpy (Ru(bpy)2(bpyâ€“tpy) or tpyâ€“Ru) can be well-assembled to tpy-functionalized polymers (P1) by Mg2+ by red-shifts of ligand absorption (planarization) along with the existence of the Ru complex's MLCT band (âˆ¼450 nm) in the UVâ€“vis spectra (Fig. S1â€ ).42 In addition, when Fe2+ was applied instead of Mg2+, the MLCT band of Fe(tpy)2 was evident at âˆ¼570 nm (Fig. S1â€ ). The assembled complex P1/tpyâ€“Ru was disassembled with the addition of TBAF (Fâˆ’) as evidenced by the blue-shifted ligand absorptions (Fig. S1bâ€ ). The above results together indicate that tpyâ€“Ru, the photocatalyst or photosensitizer for photocurrent generation, can be successfully assembled and disassembled on demand via metalâ€“tpy interaction.

Utilizing the tpyâ€“Ru photocatalyst capable of on-demand assembly, visible-light-induced photocatalytic oxidation of benzylamine to imine was conducted43 and found to be regenerative and repairable (Fig. 3a). 2 mM of tpyâ€“Ru and 1 mM of Mg2+ were introduced on tpy-functionalized network polymer P2 (20 mg mLâˆ’1) in THF for assembly into the photoactive state. It is well known that by irradiation at 455 nm, photoexcited Ru(II)* under an oxygen atmosphere oxidizes benzylamine to benzaldimine via a benzylamine radical cation, regenerating Ru(II).44 During the process, superoxide (O2Ë™âˆ’) seems generated either by the direct electron transfer from Ru(II)* to oxygen (redox pathway),45 or by oxidation of benzylamine with singlet oxygen (1O2) (by energy transfer from Ru(II)*, singlet-oxygen pathway).46 Either way, the generated superoxide reacts with the benzylamine radical cation, furnishing benzaldimine and hydrogen peroxide via a peroxy-amine intermediate. Subsequently, benzaldimine is hydrolyzed to benzaldehyde, which couples to another benzylamine forming N-benzyl benzaldimine (Fig. 3b). We confirmed that superoxide (O2Ë™âˆ’) and hydrogen peroxide were generated under our conditions by a control experiment with superoxide dismutase (SOD, O2Ë™âˆ’ scavenger) and a redox titration with KI/starch solution, respectively (Fig. S5 and S6â€ ).47 After assembly, 1H NMR spectroscopy investigations showed that the irradiation of visible light (455 nm) furnished N-benzyl benzaldimine, and the conversion increased with irradiation time (Fig. 3c and S3â€ ). Although the reaction rate was rather slow, it is important to note that by addition of TBAF for disassembly, the photocatalytic efficiency (i.e., conversion) significantly decreased due to the removal of the Ru complex. Notably, the system became photoactive again by the process of re-assembly with â€œfreshâ€ tpyâ€“Ru and Mg2+, which could be repeated several times. We envision that this reversible, on-demand self-assembly system would be applicable to other photocatalytic reactions (â€œsolar-fuelâ€ generation) through repeatable assembly and disassembly, resulting in regenerative, sustainable systems.

Interestingly, the conversion rates of benzylamine to imine were found to be different between self-assembled photoactive P1 with Mg2+ and Fe2+ (Fig. S3â€ ). For P1 with Mg2+ under similar conditions (455 nm), the conversion was âˆ¼72% after 6 h, while in that with Fe2+, the conversion was only 35%. Our hypothesis is that the reaction follows a redox pathway and the electron transfer rate is different depending on the metalâ€“tpy linkages. In P1 with Mg2+, the Mg(II)(tpy)2 does not seem to take part in the photo-induced reaction, thus following the mechanism described above. However, in P1 with Fe2+, the oxidized Ru(III) complex appears to obtain an electron from Fe(II)(tpy)2 faster than from benzylamine, furnishing Fe(III)(tpy)2 (Fig. S4â€ ). If the photoinduced oxidative coupling of benzylamine to imine proceeds, Fe(III)(tpy)2 should be able to oxidize benzylamine, which seems to occur; however, the overall conversion rate was slower (âˆ¼52% reduction). The slower conversion rate with Fe2+ was attributed to the retarded single-electron transfer from benzylamine to Fe(III)(tpy)2 when compared to those with the Ru(III) complex following the above mechanism since the diminished excitation of the Ru(II) dye (âˆ¼20%) by Fe(tpy)2 alone cannot fully explain the retarded photoreaction rate.

The different electron-transfer pathways between network polymers with Mg2+ and Fe2+ were also observed in the photoreduction of an irreversible electron acceptor, 4-bromophenyl diazonium salt (Fig. S7â€ ).48â€“50 In the case of the network polymer with Fe2+, initially the MLCT absorptions of Fe(II)(tpy)2 at âˆ¼570 nm and Ru(II)(bpy)3 at âˆ¼450 nm were observed. During the irradiation of visible light at 455 nm, the MLCT band of Fe(II)(tpy)2 slowly decreased, while that of Ru(II)(bpy)3 remained almost the same. Because the photoexcited Ru complex (Ru(II)(bpy)3*) should be oxidized first to Ru(III)(bpy)3 by 4-bromophenyl diazonium salt, we can safely assume that Ru(II)(bpy)3 was restored by single-electron transfer from Fe(II)(tpy)2. As a result, the MLCT absorption of Fe(II)(tpy)2 (âˆ¼570 nm) decreased due to its transformation to Fe(III)(tpy)2.24,49 In contrast, in the case of the network polymer with Mg2+ under similar conditions, the MLCT absorption of Ru(II)(bpy)3 (âˆ¼450 nm) slowly decreased, suggesting that the restoration of Ru(II)(bpy)3 was not achieved, and the Ru complex remained as the oxidized state (Ru(III)(bpy)3).24 Notably, the metalâ€“tpy bondings can not only act as the linkers for on-demand assembly, but also participate in the electron transfer pathways, altering the reaction mechanism.

We also successfully developed a regenerative photocurrent system using self-assembled composites of tpy-functionalized polymer P1, tpy-functionalized multi-walled carbon nanotubes (tpyâ€“MWNTs),30 and tpyâ€“Ru by metalâ€“ligand interaction (Fig. 4). First of all, a P1/tpyâ€“MWNT composite film was fabricated by filtration of a DMF mixture (5 mL) of P1 (0.06 mg mLâˆ’1) and tpyâ€“MWNT (0.02 mg mLâˆ’1) over an anodized aluminum oxide (AAO) membrane, followed by transfer on an indium-tin-oxide (ITO)-coated glass electrode. Then, a few drops of acetonitrile solution of Mg2+ (1 mM) were cast on the composite film of P1/tpyâ€“MWNT for assembly and after 5 min, extra Mg2+ ions were removed by rinsing with acetonitrile. Subsequently, a few drops of acetonitrile solution of tpyâ€“Ru (2 mM) were also applied over the composite film bound with Mg2+ for assembly to complete the â€œphotoactiveâ€ state. The immobilization of tpyâ€“Ru was confirmed by the increased absorption of the MLCT band of Ru(bpy)3 (450 nm) (Fig. S8â€ ). The prepared photoactive film was irradiated under visible light (455 nm), and the photocurrents generated were measured. The photocurrent with the self-assembled photoactive composite film increased to âˆ¼30 nA cmâˆ’2 upon irradiation (Fig. 4b). When TBAF (Fâˆ’) was introduced over the assembled composite film, the assembled tpy-complexes underwent decomposition, rendering the composite â€œphotoinactiveâ€; the photocurrent from the photoinactive composite film decreased from âˆ¼30 to âˆ¼2 nA cmâˆ’2. The MLCT band absorption of Ru(bpy)3 was also confirmed to decrease after disassembly with Fâˆ’ (Fig. S8â€ ). It is important to note that the photocurrent efficiency of this system could be recovered through re-assembly with â€œfreshâ€ tpyâ€“Ru and Mg2+ (Fig. 4b), and the assembly/disassembly process was repeated several times without a significant decrease in its efficiency (Fig. 4b inset), highlighting the regenerative or repairable properties enabled by metalâ€“tpy interaction.

4. Conclusion

In this study, reversible self-assembled photoactive polymers were successfully developed and applied to regenerative or repairable photocatalysis and photocurrent generation. Reversible metal ionâ€“tpy interaction was utilized for on-demand assembly and disassembly of a photoactive component (Ru complex) onto polymers (P1 and P2) or MWNT composites, all of which were functionalized with a tpy moiety. P1 and P2 were synthesized from tpy-functionalized norbornene derivatives by ROMP. Assembly was achieved by addition of Mg2+, as confirmed by red-shifted ligand absorptions (âˆ¼330 nm) due to the planarization of pyridine groups by binding to Mg2+. Disassembly was accomplished by using TBAF due to a higher binding affinity of Fâˆ’ to Mg2+ than tpy, returning the tpy ligands to the pristine, free state. In addition, a photocatalytic polymer complex was successfully fabricated with tpy-functionalized network polymer (P2) and tpyâ€“Ru, promoting visible-light-induced photo-oxidative coupling of benzylamine to N-benzyl benzaldimine. More importantly, the photocatalytic activity of the system ceased upon disassembly by Fâˆ’ and restored upon assembly with â€œfreshâ€ tpyâ€“Ru and Mg2+ and was repeated several times. A similar regenerative or repairable property was demonstrated with photocurrent generation; a photocurrent system using tpy-functionalized composite films with P1, tpyâ€“MWNT, and tpyâ€“Ru via self-assembly by Mg2+â€“tpy interaction was successfully fabricated. The photoactive composite films showed a photocurrent generation of âˆ¼30 nA cmâˆ’2 after assembly; however, the photocurrent efficiency decreased after disassembly by Fâˆ’, which was restored repeatedly by re-assembly. We believe that the metalâ€“tpy interaction can be applied to the development of other regenerative or repairable photoactive systems via on-demand assembly/disassembly, which may ensure sustainability in organic photoactive systems.
