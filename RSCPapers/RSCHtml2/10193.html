Abstract

A universal synthetic strategy toward thieno[3,2-b]indole (TI) derivatives was developed. Three conjugated polymers (PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT) containing N-alkyl-TI as the donor units, 2,1,3-benzodiathiazole (BT) as the acceptor units and thiophene as the spacers were synthesized. The thiophene spacers have a dramatic impact on the physical and electrochemical properties of these copolymers. These polymer donors were used for the fabrication of bulk heterojunction polymer solar cells (PSCs). Preliminarily, power conversion efficiencies (PCEs) based on the device structure of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/polymer:PC71BM/Ca/Al exhibit a large distinction (1.61% for PTIBT, 5.83% for PTITBT and 1.79% for PTIDTBT) at optimal device fabrication conditions. The device based on PTITBT:PC71BM (1:3, w/w) shows the best PCE of 5.83% (Voc = 0.69 V, Jsc = 13.92 mA cmâˆ’2, FF = 61.8%), which represents one of the best performances among PCDTBT analogues. In addition, the Jsc of 13.92 mA cmâˆ’2 is also among the highest Jsc values of all PCDTBT analogues. On the basis of our results, one can conclude that incorporating TI and its derivatives into donorâ€“acceptor conjugated polymers is a feasible and effective way to develop novel donor materials for high efficiency PSC applications.

Introduction

Polymer solar cells (PSCs) have attracted an increasing amount of attention due to their potential as a low-cost clean means to convert solar energy into electricity, as well as applications in flexible, lightweight, transparent and large-area devices.1â€“7 With the discovery of efficient photo-induced electron transfer from a conjugated polymer to a fullerene derivative, the bulk heterojunction (BHJ) PSC has become the most successful device geometry for high power conversion efficiency (PCE) to date.8â€“15 The PCE of PSCs can be improved by synthesizing novel donor and acceptor materials, increasing the light absorption and optimizing the morphology of the active layer. With great endeavors devoted to molecular engineering of conjugated donor materials, assisted by limited successes with fullerene derivatives, the PCE of BHJ PSCs has been improved from below 1% to over 11% rapidly during the past few decades.5,17â€“27 However, they still cannot achieve the targeted 15% PCEs and satisfactory lifetime, which are crucial for future commercialization.20 Despite the development of PSC device fabrication techniques, more promising candidate donor conjugated polymers are still much in demand.

Main chain donorâ€“acceptor (Dâ€“A) conjugated polymers have dominated the library of donor materials for PSCs because of their intrinsic optical and electronic properties, which can be readily tuned by controlling the intramolecular charge transfer (ICT) from donor units to acceptor units.20,28,29 Among the reported Dâ€“A copolymer donor materials, poly(2,7-carbazole-alt-dithienylbenzothiadiazole) (PCDTBT) (as shown in Scheme 1),30 based on 2,7-carbazole and 4,7-bis(2-thienyl)-2,1,3-benzothiadiazole (DTBT) units, is an excellent parent structure with a low-lying highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) of âˆ’5.45 eV, showing outstanding stability against oxidation at high temperatures and feasible synthesis.30â€“33 Since PSCs based on PCDTBT were first reported by Leclerc et al. in 2007,34 the initial PCE of 3.6% in a typical single junction BHJ device has been subsequently improved from 6.0% to 7.5% by BHJ layer thickness optimization, nanomorphology control using mixed solvents or electric contact optimization with electron-transporting layer.5,35â€“37 In addition, tandem and triple-junction PSCs with PCEs of 8.9% and 9.6% containing PCDTBT-based layer have been reported by Janssen et al.38 Yet, one must be aware that the polymer PCDTBT possesses a limited absorption range, which results in a medium Jsc.5,34â€“38 The bandgap of PCDTBT (1.85 eV) is still larger than the value (1.5â€“1.7 eV) of the ideal polymers for PCE values exceeding 10%.20 Recently, to further lower the bandgap of PCDTBT, many efforts have been made to develop PCDTBT analogues. For example, Hsu et al.39,40 and Yang et al.41,42 independently reported PCDTBT-based ladder type polymers with impressive PCEs of up to 5.9%. However, the multiple synthetic steps for ladder-type PCDTBT analogues have restricted the realization for their usage in BHJ devices.39,40 Bo et al. have reported systematic investigations of the molecular engineering on both carbazole units and BT units, such as incorporating alkoxy groups on BT units or F-atoms on carbazole units, leading to another series of PCDTBT analogues with higher PCEs of 7.39%.43â€“49 Nevertheless, most of those PCDTBTs also suffer the difficulties of monomer synthesis. A novel strategy for designing PCDTBT analogues is still in demand.

It was reported that poly{N-[1-(2â€²-ethylhexyl)-3-ethylheptanyl]dithieno[3,2-b:2â€²,3â€²-d]pyrrole-2,6-diyl-alt-4,7-di(2-thienyl)-2,1,3-benzothiadiazole-5â€²,5â€²â€²-diyl} (PDTPDTBT), based on dithienopyrrole (DTP), a carbazole-like unit with N-atom bridged dithiophene, showed a high-lying HOMO (âˆ’5.0 eV), a narrow bandgap of 1.46 eV and strong absorption from 300 nm to 850 nm with low Voc (0.52 V) in similar PSC devices.50a The extended-DTP unit of pentacyclic pyrroloindacenodithiophene (NIDT) has emerged as an electron-rich building block for conjugated polymers used in OFETs and OPVs.50bâ€“d For example, Donaghey and Cheng reported that NIDT-based copolymers showed a hole mobility as high as 0.07 cm2 Vâˆ’1 sâˆ’1 for OFETs and BHJ solar cells with PC71BM as acceptor affording a PCE close to 3.8%.50bâ€“d Both DTP- and NIDT-based copolymers possess relatively high planarity, light-harvesting property and high mobility, due to the rigid and planar feature of thiophene-fused indolo unit.51 Inspired by the advantages of carbazole- and DTP-based copolymers, copolymers based on the tricyclic donor unit thieno[3,2-b]indole (TI), which is formed by merging DTP and carbazole, are expected to show high performance as donor materials for PSCs. The TI unit has been rarely explored after it was first reported in 1977.51 TI was first introduced into an organic dye for dye sensitized solar cells (DSSCs) which give a relatively high PCE of 7.8% and medium Voc of 0.7 V as reported in 2010.52 Interestingly, very recently, Suh et al. reported PTTITBT-h with a PCE of 3.35% and a Voc of 0.81 V.53 Both works indicate significant efforts should be made to exploit the application of TI in Dâ€“A conjugated semiconducting materials.

Herein, a universal synthetic strategy was developed to synthesize TI and its derivatives. A series of PCDTBT analogues, namely PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT, by introducing none, single and double thiophene spacers in TIâ€“BT repeat units were designed and synthesized (see Scheme 1). Among various Dâ€“A conjugated polymers, 2,1,3-benzothiadiazole (BT) has been proven to be an excellent acceptor for high-performance PSC donor materials owing to its stability, moderate electron affinity and planar structure.19â€“27,37,54â€“56 So, the BT unit was chosen as the acceptor unit. To ensure good solubility in organic solvents, we attached large branched 2-decyltetradecyl (C24) substituents on TI units. We expected the thiophene spacers to tune the energy levels, bandgap and ICT effect from TI to BT to further improve the photovoltaic performance.28,29 The resulting copolymers were synthesized, fully characterized and used as donor materials in PSC devices. The PCE of the device based on PTITBT reached 5.83% with a high Jsc of 13.92 mA cmâˆ’2 in a conventional single junction PSC, which is 30.1% higher than that obtained from a PCDTBT-based reference device (PCE of 4.48% in our previous report).35

Optical properties

The optical properties of PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT were investigated by ultraviolet-visible (UV-vis) absorption spectroscopy. In chloroform solution, all copolymers exhibit similar profiles with two distinct absorption bands at around 400 nm (weak) and 630 nm (broad, strong) (Fig. 2a). The shorter wavelength absorption comes from the localized Ï€â€“Ï€* transitions of TI units, while the lower energy band (from 550 to 750 nm) is attributed to the strong ICT between TI and BT segments. Compared to the absorption spectra in the solution state, all polymers showed not only a bathochromic shift but also broadening of the ICT bands in the solid state (Fig. 2b) due to the main chain aggregation and strong interchain electronic interactions in films.12â€“18 Their optical band-gaps (Eoptg) deduced from the absorption edges of the thin film spectra are in the following order: PTIBT (1.66 eV) > PTITBT (1.60 eV) > PTIDTBT (1.56 eV) (Table 2).30 Obviously, the band-gaps are much narrower than those of PCDTBT (1.85 eV).31,32,35 The difference of the Î»max as well as Eoptg points out that the donor strength of TI unit is larger than that of carbazole, which is in agreement with earlier studies in that the replacement of benzene ring with thiophene results in a reduction of Eoptg due to the increase of ICT transition in the polymer backbone.22,23,28,29 Besides, the deep blue color of PTITBT and PTIDTBT solutions is observed unlike the red color of PCDTBT in solution. Compared to PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT with one and two additional thiophene rings in repeat unit, respectively, exhibited the red-shifted Ï€â€“Ï€* transition band due to longer conjugated length. However, this modification of incorporation of thiophene spacers also results in a blue-shift of the ICT band of PTIDTBT due to the weaker donorâ€“acceptor interaction.39,40 The absorption coefficients deduced from the solution absorption spectra are in the following order: PTITBT > PTIBT > PTIDTBT (see Table 2). This order indicates that PTITBT possesses better light absorbing capability in the visible light region, which gives PTITBT the potential for higher performance for PSC application. These results apparently suggest that the conjugation length and ICT transition both can be finely tuned by incorporating thiophene spacers in the repeat unit of TI-based copolymers.

Electrochemical properties

The electrochemical properties of the copolymers were investigated by cyclic voltammetry (CV) using a standard three-electrode electrochemical cell in order to evaluate the HOMO and LUMO levels of the polymers (Fig. 3, Table 3). The polymers showed stable and reversible process both in oxidative and reductive scans, which are important prerequisites for p-type semiconductors. The HOMO and LUMO energy levels of these copolymers were calculated from onset oxidation potential (Eoxonset) and the onset reduction potential (Eredonset), respectively.58 The oxidation onsets of the polymers PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT are estimated to be 0.54, 0.49 and 0.41 V, which correspond to HOMO levels of âˆ’5.27, âˆ’5.22 and âˆ’5.14 eV, respectively. The LUMO levels of PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT are estimated to âˆ’3.66, âˆ’3.65 and âˆ’3.63 eV, respectively. The LUMO energy levels are higher than that of PC71BM acceptor (âˆ’4.30 eV) to ensure energetically favorable electron transfer. According to the HOMO and LUMO energy levels of the copolymers, the electronic bandgaps were calculated to be 1.61, 1.57 and 1.51 eV for PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT, indicating that the bandgaps are becoming narrower with the introduction of the thiophene spacers. Compared to the bandgap of PCDTBT at 1.85 eV,30,35 all three copolymers show narrower bandgaps, indicating they can harvest more solar photons and provide remarkable short-circuit current densities (Jsc). In addition, the LUMO energy levels of these copolymers are around âˆ’3.65 eV, which is closer to the LUMO level of PC71BM (âˆ’4.3 eV) than that of PCDTBT (âˆ’3.5 eV).5,24,35 The material-design rules described by Scharber et al. suggested that the PCEs of a BHJ PSC should be much more sensitive to changes of the donor LUMO level compared to variations of the donor bandgap.59 On the basis of these rules, the estimated PCE from the Eoptg and the LUMO value of TI-containing copolymers should be higher than that of PCDTBT (6.5%),34â€“37 indicating that TI-containing copolymers are highly promising materials for PSCs.

Conclusions

In summary, a universal synthetic strategy towards the electron rich, N-bridged building block of thieno[3,2-b]indole (TI) has been presented. The synthesis of three novel analogues of PCDTBT, namely PTIBT, PTITBT and PTIDTBT, by incorporating TI, BT and thiophene spacers has been described, aiming to render energy levels as well as the light absorption properties toward further improving the photovoltaic parameters. Both experimental results and theoretical simulation have revealed that the incorporation of thiophene spacers can have a dramatic impact on the resulting polymers, such as optical and electrochemical properties, energy levels, charge-carrier transporting capability and molecular packing. The resulting copolymers show the desired narrower band gaps (âˆ¼1.6 eV, with HOMO levels of âˆ’5.14 to âˆ’5.27 eV) than PCDTBT (1.85 eV, with HOMO level of âˆ’5.45 eV), possibly allowing higher Jsc.

The photovoltaic performances of the PSCs using blends of the resulting copolymers with PC71BM have been investigated and are consistent with their chemical and electronic structures. Under similar device fabrication conditions, the PTITBT-based devices showed a much higher efficiency than the corresponding PTIBT- and PTIDTBT-based devices (5.83% vs. 1.61% and 1.79%). According to the EQE evaluation, the improvement is mainly the result of much enhanced Jsc, which is in good agreement with the largest absorption coefficient of PTITBT and its strongest absorption properties in thin-film form among all three polymers. The optimized device based on PTITBT:PC71BM (1:3, w/w, 150 Â°C thermal annealing for 15 min) demonstrates a PCE of 5.83% (Voc = 0.69 V, Jsc = 13.92 mA cmâˆ’2, FF = 61.8%) under AM 1.5 (100 mW cmâˆ’2), indicating PTITBT is a promising donor material for PSC application. The high Jsc obtained from the PTITBT-based device is among the highest Jsc values for PSCs based on PCDTBT analogues. This work provides a feasible strategy for a novel family of TI-based copolymers for high-performance PSCs and reports useful insights into a new electron-rich unit for Dâ€“A conjugated copolymers. Further exploration of PTITBT-based PSCs is still warranted for high-performance PCEs by means of controlling the film morphology with other additives such as DMSO and improving the electric contacts.
